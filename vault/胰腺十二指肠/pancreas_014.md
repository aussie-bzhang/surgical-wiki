## Overview
Pancreaticoduodenectomy (Child reconstruction) for obstructive jaundice due to duodenal papillary cancer.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Obstructive jaundice
**Intraoperative:** Obstructive jaundice, duodenal papillary cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
A hard mass measuring approximately 2.0 cm in diameter was found at the duodenal papilla, presenting as cauliflower-like. The common bile duct had a diameter of 1.5 cm. The liver was slightly hard, no mass was palpated. The intestines, pelvis, and ascites were explored and were negative.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia achieved, patient placed in supine position, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline upper abdominal incision made, peritoneum entered as described.
3. Detailed dissection of the胆囊三角, ligation and division of the cystic artery and cystic duct, subserosal dissection of the gallbladder with cautery for hemostasis, gallbladder removed.
4. Biliary decompression confirmed by puncture.
5. Dissection and mobilization of the second and third segments of the duodenum, incision of the duodenal wall, exploration of the papilla.
6. Resection of the pre-colic ligament of the transverse colon and mobilization of the left gastrocolic ligament to the splenic flexure and the right gastrocolic ligament to the hepatogastric region, ligation and division of the right gastroepiploic vessels and greater curvature vessels, preservation of the left gastroepiploic vessels, division of the gastropancreatic fold along the pancreatic capsule, mobilization of the posterior gastric wall.
7. Dissection of the hepatoduodenal ligament, mobilization of the common bile duct, hepatic artery, and portal vein, dissection of the 12th group of lymph nodes, division of the common bile duct below the confluence of the right and left hepatic ducts, hemostasis at the proximal断面 for anastomosis.
8. Mobilization and dissection of the common hepatic artery, dissection of the NO.8 lymph nodes, clarification of the common hepatic artery, left gastric artery, and splenic artery, ligation and division of the gastroduodenal artery in two layers.
9. Palpation of the pancreatic head tumor on the left side, ligation and division of the right gastric vessels and lesser curvature vessels, suture and ligature at the root, mobilization of the gastric body upwards to the superior margin of the pancreas, preservation of about 1/3 of the gastric body and division with a 55mm side-to-side stapler for anastomosis.
10. Continued mobilization of the duodenum, mobilization of the superior mesenteric vein at the inferior margin of the pancreas, careful mobilization of the pancreas along the anterior wall of the SMV, convergence with the portal vein, ligation and suture at both superior and inferior margins of the pancreas for traction, transection of the pancreatic neck.
11. Transection of the jejunum 15 cm distal to the Treitz ligament, the proximal jejunum moved to the superior mesenteric artery lateral margin, complete resection of the pancreatic tail along the superior mesenteric artery, specimen removed.
12. The jejunum segment was pulled to the superior mesenteric artery lateral margin from the middle of the mesentery, mobilization of the pancreatic segment about 3.0 cm (careful ligation of small vessels between the pancreas and splenic vein to avoid bleeding), a scalp needle catheter left in the main pancreatic duct, completion of pancreaticojejunal end-to-end anastomosis.
13. Opening the jejunum wall 15 cm from the pancreaticojejunal anastomosis, diameter slightly larger than CBD diameter, 5-0 PDS continuous full-thickness inverted suture for biliary-enteric end-to-side anastomosis.
14. 28.5# tube stapler for gastrojejunal side-to-side anastomosis (in front of the colon), full-thickness interrupted reinforcement, gastric tube left at the distal end of the gastrojejunal anastomosis (input loop), completion of biliary-enteric reconstruction according to the Child method.
15. Hemostasis completed, two negative pressure drains placed at the inferior margin of the liver through the biliary-enteric anastomosis to the pancreaticojejunal anastomosis, extorted from the upper right abdomen.
16. Abdominal cavity flushed with large amounts of normal saline until clean, no bleeding or bile leakage confirmed, instruments and gauze counted correctly, abdomen closed in layers.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: common bile duct (胆总管), portal vein (门静脉), gallbladder (胆囊), gastrocolic ligament (胃结肠韧带). Use English with Chinese in parentheses where helpful.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included careful mobilization of the pancreas and splenic vein to avoid bleeding, and precise anastomosis of the bile duct and pancreas.

## Teaching Points
- Careful mobilization of the pancreas and splenic vein to avoid bleeding.
- Precise anastomosis of the bile duct and pancreas.
- Proper placement of drains.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis during dissection.
- Proper anastomosis technique to prevent leaks.

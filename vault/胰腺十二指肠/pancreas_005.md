## Overview
Pancreaticoduodenectomy for periampullary tumor, post-T-tube drainage, with significant adhesions in the right upper quadrant.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Periampullary tumor
**Intraoperative:** Periampullary tumor, T-tube drainage, significant adhesions
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a periampullary tumor approximately 3*4cm, involving the duodenum and pancreatic head. Lymph nodes in groups 12 and 13 were enlarged. CBD was dilated to 2cm. Exploration of liver and omentum was negative. Severe adhesions were present in the right upper quadrant, making surgery difficult.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Upper midline incision for abdominal access, exploration as described, and removal of T-tube.
2. Koch's incision to open the lateral abdominal wall of the duodenum, mobilization of the second segment of the duodenum, and initial tumor exploration.
3. Mobilization of the gastrocolic ligament from the hepatic flexure to the splenic flexure, division of the greater curvature of the stomach, preservation of part of the left gastroepiploic and short gastric vessels, division of the right gastroepiploic vessel, mobilization of the bulb of the duodenum, and entry into the lesser omentum from the pancreatic surface, division of the hepatogastric ligament.
4. Dissection of the hepatoduodenal ligament, exploration of the original T-tube drainage opening of the CBD, dissection along the hepatic artery to the celiac trunk, division of the left gastric artery at its root, division of the right gastric and gastroduodenal arteries, mobilization of the portal vein.
5. Careful dissection of the superior mesenteric vein at the inferior margin of the pancreas, and careful separation of the pancreas from the portal vein, division of the pancreas posteriorly at the level of the superior mesenteric vein.
6. Opening of the Treitz ligament, division of the mesentery of the small intestine at the root of the mesentery close to the superior mesenteric artery, division of the small intestine below the Treitz ligament approximately 20cm, mobilization of the third and fourth segments of the duodenum.
7. Careful separation of the pancreas from the superior mesenteric vein, stripping the pancreas from the venous surface, and ligating and dividing the caudate process of the pancreas one by one, while protecting the superior mesenteric artery, and removing the specimen.
8. Performing the Child procedure, including pancreaticojejunal end-to-end anastomosis with捆绑式anastomosis, biliary-enteric anastomosis with 5-0 PDS continuous suture, and side-to-side anastomosis with a 25# circular stapler in the antimesenteric position.
9. Perfect hemostasis, closure of the mesenteric orifice, placement of two negative pressure tubes above the pancreaticojejunal anastomosis at the superior margin of the liver and leading them out through the right upper quadrant below the biliary-enteric anastomosis, verification of instruments and sponges, repair of the skin and subcutaneous tissue at the original T-tube drainage site, and layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: pancreas, duodenum, common bile duct, portal vein, superior mesenteric vein. (胆总管, 胰腺, 十二指肠, 门静脉, 肠系膜上静脉)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included significant adhesions in the right upper quadrant, which made surgery challenging.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of careful mobilization and dissection around the critical structures.
- The technique of performing the Child procedure with attention to hemostasis and anastomotic technique.
- The management of significant adhesions and their impact on surgical access and dissection.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper anastomotic technique to prevent leaks and strictures.

## Overview
Pancreatectomy with biliary-enteric and gastrojejunal anastomosis for pancreatic cystic tumor and cholecystolithiasis, involving extensive dissection and complex anastomotic techniques.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Pancreatic cystic tumor, cholecystolithiasis
**Intraoperative:** Pancreatic cystic tumor, cholecystolithiasis
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Multiple cystic lesions in the pancreas, adherent to the lateral wall of the portal vein by about 2cm. Gallbladder size approximately 8*4*3cm with two stones of 1cm diameter. Exploration revealed no liver, pelvic, or ascites.

## Operative Steps
1. Patient placed in supine position after successful general anesthesia. Abdominal exploration via a midline incision.
2. Detailed dissection of the gallbladder triangle, ligation and division of the cystic artery and cystic duct, and subserosal dissection of the gallbladder with cautery.
3. Resection of the transverse mesocolon and division of the left gastrocolic ligament to the splenic flexure and the right gastrocolic ligament to the hepatocolic region.
4. Dissection of the hepatoduodenal ligament, freeing the common bile duct, hepatic artery, and portal vein.
5. Dissection of the celiac trunk, identifying the common hepatic artery, left gastric artery, and splenic artery.
6. Division of the gastroduodenal artery with double ligation and division, and division of the confluence of the right and left hepatic ducts to expose the common bile duct.
7. Ligation and division of the splenic artery and vein at the superior margin of the pancreas.
8. Dissection and division of the splenorenal, splenohyoidal, and gastrosplenic ligaments.
9. Dissection and ligation of the small branches of the portal vein to the pancreas, and complete mobilization of the pancreatic tissue.
10. Kocher incision, mobilization of the descending duodenum, and ligation and division of the right gastric and lesser curvature vessels.
11. Mobilization of the stomach to the superior margin of the pancreas, with a 55mm side-to-side stapler for anastomosis.
12. Mobilization of the superior mesenteric vein and careful mobilization of the pancreas along the SMV anterior wall.
13. Resection of the portal vein segment involved by the tumor, with suture approximation of the portal vein.
14. Resection of the pancreatic head and duodenum, and resection of the entire pancreas and duodenum.
15. Completion of the biliary-enteric anastomosis and gastrojejunal anastomosis.
16. Hemostasis, placement of drains, and closure of the abdominal wall.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Common bile duct (胆总管), portal vein (门静脉), pancreas (胰腺), splenic artery and vein (脾动脉和静脉), gastroduodenal artery (胃十二指肠动脉)

## Technical Challenges
Difficult dissection around the portal vein, complex anastomotic techniques, and careful hemostasis.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of careful dissection around the portal vein to avoid injury.
- Mastery of complex anastomotic techniques.
- Proper management of hemostasis during resection and anastomosis.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis during resection and anastomosis.
- Adequate drainage to prevent postoperative complications.

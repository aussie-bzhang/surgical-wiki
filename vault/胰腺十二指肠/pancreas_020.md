## Overview
Pancreaticoduodenectomy with Roux-en-Y anastomosis is a complex surgical procedure performed for pancreatic head tumors. It involves resection of the head of the pancreas, duodenum, gallbladder, and part of the bile duct, followed by reconstruction with a Roux-en-Y anastomosis.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Pancreatic head mass
**Intraoperative:** Pancreatic head mass
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a 8.0cm hard mass in the pancreatic head, encircling the superior mesenteric vessels. The middle colonic vein was thickened, the main pancreatic duct was significantly dilated with a diameter of 5cm. The gallbladder was distended, measuring approximately 12*6*4cm. The common bile duct was dilated with a diameter of 5cm, containing a large amount of dark green bile without stones. The abdominal viscera were jaundiced with cholestatic liver changes. No obvious mass was found in the liver, stomach, small intestine, and colon upon exploration.

## Operative Steps
1. The patient was placed in a supine position under general anesthesia. The abdomen was entered via a midline upper abdominal incision.
2. The gallbladder triangle was dissected, the cystic artery and cystic duct were separated, ligated, and divided. The gallbladder was excised, and the common bile duct was longitudinally incised.
3. The gastrocolic ligament was divided to expose the pancreas. The main pancreatic duct was longitudinally incised, and stones were removed.
4. The jejunum was divided 15cm distal to the Treitz ligament. The distal jejunum and main pancreatic duct were anastomosed in a side-to-side fashion (full-thickness continuous suture). Ten cm distal to the pancreatojejunal anastomosis, the jejunum and common bile duct were anastomosed in a side-to-side fashion (full-thickness continuous suture). Fifteen cm distal to the choledochojejunal anastomosis, the jejunum and greater curvature of the stomach were anastomosed in a side-to-side fashion (full-thickness interrupted suture, muscular layer suture for reinforcement). The proximal jejunum was anastomosed to the jejunum 40cm distal to the gastrojejunal anastomosis (retrocolic). The mesenteric gap was closed.
5. Hemostasis was achieved, and two negative pressure drains were placed above the pancreatojejunal anastomosis along the inferior margin of the liver and extended from the right upper quadrant. Instruments and sponges were counted and found to be correct. The abdomen was closed in layers.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included encasement of the superior mesenteric vessels by the mass, significant dilatation of the main pancreatic duct and common bile duct, and the need for meticulous dissection and anastomosis.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of identifying and preserving the superior mesenteric vessels during dissection.
- The technique of performing a Roux-en-Y anastomosis with attention to proper orientation and tension-free anastomosis.
- The need for meticulous hemostasis and drain placement to prevent postoperative complications.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper drain placement to prevent bile leak and infection.

## Overview
Pancreaticoduodenectomy for a pancreatic head mass, with significant involvement of the common bile duct and portal vein.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Pancreatic head mass
**Intraoperative:** Suspected pancreatic head cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located in the head of the pancreas, approximately 32cm in size, hard consistency. Tumor compresses 1/2 circumference of the superior mesenteric vein. Common bile duct diameter 1.0cm. Gallbladder 7.5cm in size, containing multiple mixed stones. No abnormalities found in liver, stomach, small intestine, or pelvis.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Midline upper abdominal incision, enter peritoneum as described.
2. Incise the gastrocolic ligament along the transverse colon margin, to the right to the pyloric area, and to the left to the inferior pole of the spleen.
3. Perform Kocher incision, mobilize the lateral peritoneum and loose connective tissue of the descending duodenum; mobilize the second, third, and fourth segments of the duodenum; open the hepatoduodenal ligament, dissect the胆囊三角, separate, ligate, and transect the cystic artery and cystic duct, remove the gallbladder, and transect the common bile duct.
4. Mobilize the hepatic artery and portal vein, dissect the 12th group of lymph nodes; transect the right gastric vessels, and continue along the hepatic artery to the common hepatic artery, transect the gastroduodenal artery, and transect the lesser curvature of the stomach.
5. Find the superior mesenteric vein inferior to the uncinate process, identify the relationship between the tumor and the superior mesenteric vein-portal vein, transect the pancreas on the left side of the vessels, carefully dissect the tumor from the attachment of the superior mesenteric vein-portal vein, and repair the vascular puncture.
6. Transect the jejunum 15cm from Treitz ligament, open the Treitz ligament, remove the specimen.
7. Perform Child's reconstruction of the gastrointestinal tract, pancreaticojejunal end-to-end anastomosis, choledochojejunostomy, and perform an end-to-side anastomosis with a 25# tube anastomosis stapler at the anterior aspect of the colon.
8. Perfect hemostasis, place three negative pressure drainage tubes superior and posterior to the pancreaticojejunal anastomosis along the inferior margin of the liver, and lead them out from the superior right abdomen through the posterior aspect of the choledochojejunal anastomosis. Count instruments and sponges, ensure accuracy, and close the abdomen in layers.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: common bile duct (胆总管), portal vein (门静脉), superior mesenteric vein (肠系膜上静脉). Structures requiring protection: superior mesenteric vein-portal vein junction.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in mobilizing the superior mesenteric vein-portal vein junction and in performing the pancreaticojejunal and choledochojejunal anastomoses.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of identifying and preserving the superior mesenteric vein-portal vein junction.
- Technique of performing pancreaticojejunal and choledochojejunal anastomoses.
- The need for meticulous hemostasis and drainage placement.

## Complication Prevention
- Preventing bile leak by ensuring proper anastomosis technique.
- Preventing pancreatic fistula by ensuring adequate pancreaticojejunal anastomosis.

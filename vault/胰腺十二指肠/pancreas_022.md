## Operative Steps

Lay the patient in a supine position, perform routine disinfection and draping under general anesthesia; enter the abdomen via a midline upper abdominal incision; separate the gastrocolic ligament, open the omental bursa, and expose the pancreas; probe as described above, discuss with the family after the decision on the surgical approach; dissect the hepatoduodenal ligament, transect the common hepatic artery, mobilize the superior margin of the pancreas and the portal vein; transect the splenogastric ligament superiorly and the splenocolic ligament inferiorly. Dissect the superior mesenteric vein, splenic vein at 3.0 cm from the tumor, and transect the pancreas; suture the distal stump of the pancreas with a locking suture and ligate it; clamp the splenic vein on the left side of the SMV, transect the splenic vein, ligate the proximal end with 5-0 prolene suture, and tie it off. Ligate the splenic artery.

Dissect and transect the splenorenal ligament, splenodiaphragmatic ligament, mobilize the enlarged retroperitoneal lymph nodes, pancreatic tail, and spleen, and remove the specimen; thoroughly hemostasis, repeatedly irrigate the abdominal cavity, suture the retroperitoneum, spray hemostatic powder on the wound surface, pack with hemostatic gauze. Confirm no bleeding. Place two negative pressure drains at the pancreatic stump and splenic fossa, puncture the incision and bring them out, count instruments and sponges, close the abdomen in layers.

Operation completed.

The operation was successful, with moderate bleeding of about 400ml, and the patient was safely returned to the ward after surgery.

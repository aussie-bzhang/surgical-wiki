## Overview
Pancreaticoduodenectomy is a complex surgical procedure performed for pancreatic head tumors. It involves resection of the head of the pancreas, duodenum, gallbladder, and parts of the bile duct and stomach. The procedure is typically indicated for pancreatic adenocarcinoma.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Pancreatic head mass
**Intraoperative:** Suspected pancreatic head cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
The tumor was located in the caudate process of the pancreatic head, approximately 32cm in size, hard in consistency. The tumor compressed 1/2 circumference of the superior mesenteric vein. The common bile duct had a diameter of 1.0cm. The gallbladder was 7.5cm in size, containing multiple mixed stones. No abnormalities were found in the liver, stomach, small intestine, or pelvis.

## Operative Steps
1. The patient was placed in a supine position under general anesthesia. The abdomen was accessed via a midline upper abdominal incision.
2. The gastrocolic ligament was transected along the margin of the transverse colon, extending to the subpyloric area. The right gastroepiploic vessels were transected and ligated, and the ligament was extended to the inferior pole of the spleen.
3. Kocher's incision was performed, freeing the lateral peritoneum and loose connective tissue of the descending duodenum.
4. The second, third, and fourth segments of the duodenum were freed. The hepatoduodenal ligament was opened, the胆囊三角 was dissected, and the cystic artery and cystic duct were separated, ligated, and transected. The gallbladder was removed, and the common bile duct was transected.
5. The hepatic artery and portal vein were freed, and the 12th group of lymph nodes were dissected.
6. The right gastric vessels were transected, and the right gastric artery was followed to the common hepatic artery, where the gastroduodenal artery was transected, and the lesser curvature of the stomach was transected.
7. The superior mesenteric vein was identified below the caudate process, and its relationship with the portal vein was confirmed. The pancreas was transected on the left side of the vessels, and the tumor was carefully dissected from the superior mesenteric vein-portal vein attachment site, with the vascular break being repaired.
8. The jejunum was transected 15cm from the Treitz ligament, and the ligament was opened. The specimen was removed.
9. The Child method was used for intestinal reconstruction, with pancreaticojejunal end-to-end anastomosis, choledochojejunostomy, and a 25# tube anastomotic stapler was used for the colonic anterior-lateral anastomosis.
10. Hemostasis was achieved, and three negative pressure drainage tubes were placed above and behind the pancreaticojejunal anastomosis along the inferior margin of the liver. They were led out from the superior right abdomen. The instruments and sponges were counted and found to be correct. The abdomen was closed in layers.

## Anatomical Key Points
> The common bile duct (胆总管), portal vein (门静脉), superior mesenteric vein (肠系膜上静脉), and the relationship between the tumor and these structures are critical. Careful dissection and protection of these structures are essential.

## Technical Challenges
The complex dissection and anastomosis, especially the reconstruction of the biliary and pancreatic ducts, can be challenging.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of identifying and protecting critical structures such as the common bile duct, portal vein, and superior mesenteric vein.
- The technique of pancreaticojejunal and choledochojejunal anastomosis.
- The management of vascular injuries during the procedure.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful dissection to avoid injury to the bile duct, portal vein, and superior mesenteric vein.
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.

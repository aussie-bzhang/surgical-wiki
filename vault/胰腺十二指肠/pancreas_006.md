## Overview
A complex surgical procedure involving partial pancreatectomy, splenectomy, complex small bowel adhesiolysis, and jejunal feeding jejunostomy for a pancreatic neck and body mass.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Pancreatic mass
**Intraoperative:** Intraductal papillary mucinous neoplasm of the pancreatic neck and body
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
A large, hard mass measuring approximately 9*6cm was found in the neck and body of the pancreas. The mass involved the head and tail of the pancreas and was densely adherent to the superior mesenteric vein and portal vein. The splenic vein was occluded. Severe adhesions were present in the upper right abdomen. No abnormalities were found in the liver, biliary tract, small intestine, or colon during exploration.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping, midline upper abdominal incision.
2. Dissection of the gastrocolic ligament, opening of the omental bursa, and exposure of the pancreas.
3. Careful dissection of adhesions in the upper right abdomen, anatomization of the hepatoduodenal ligament, transection of the common hepatic artery, and mobilization of the superior mesenteric vein and portal vein.
4. Transection and ligation of the gastro splenic ligament superiorly and the splenic colonic ligament inferiorly. Anatomization of the mesenteric root and mobilization of the splenic vein.
5. Ligation and transection of the splenic renal ligament, splenic diaphragmatic ligament, and gastro splenic ligament. Mobilization of the body and tail of the pancreas superiorly.
6. Careful dissection of the tumor from the portal vein and superior mesenteric vein, securing and transecting the small branches of the portal vein to the pancreas, and mobilization of the pancreatic tissue to the right of the portal vein and superior mesenteric vein. Protection of the pancreaticoduodenal vessels.
7. Transection of the pancreas to the right of the portal vein and superior mesenteric vein, removal of the specimen. Retention of the pancreatic hook and part of the pancreatic head, and dense suture ligation of the pancreatic断面.
8. Mobilization of a jejunal segment 15cm below the Treitz ligament, placement of a 12F urinary catheter for jejunal jejunostomy, suture of the serosal layer of the intestinal wall around the jejunostomy tube for about 5cm, and suture fixation to the left lateral abdominal wall. The jejunostomy tube is brought out through the left upper abdominal wall.
9. Thorough hemostasis, repeated lavage of the abdominal cavity, closure of the peritoneum, application of hemostatic powder, packing with hemostatic gauze. No bleeding confirmed. Placement of two negative pressure drains in the pancreatic stump and one in the splenic fossa, puncture drainage, and confirmation of no bleeding. Layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen.
10. Completion of the procedure.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: pancreatic neck and body, splenic vein, portal vein, jejunal segment. (胆总管, 脾静脉, 门静脉, 空肠段)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in mobilizing the pancreas due to dense adhesions and protecting the pancreaticoduodenal vessels.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of careful mobilization around the pancreas to avoid injury to the pancreaticoduodenal vessels.
- Technique of performing a complex small bowel adhesiolysis.
- Proper management of the pancreatic stump and splenic vein.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Adequate lavage of the abdominal cavity to reduce the risk of infection.

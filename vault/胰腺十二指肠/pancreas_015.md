## Overview
Pancreatic tail tumor resection and splenectomy, with emphasis on the dissection of the pancreatic tail and spleen, and the management of the splenic hilum.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Pancreatic tail tumor
**Intraoperative:** Pancreatic tail tumor
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a 3.0 cm hard mass in the pancreatic tail, adherent to the splenic artery and vein. The spleen measured 5*3*2 cm. No significant abnormalities were found in the liver, stomach, and small intestine during exploration.

## Operative Steps
1. Position the patient in supine position, under general anesthesia, and perform routine disinfection and draping. Make a midline upper abdominal incision to enter the abdomen and explore as described above.
2. Dissect the gastrocolic ligament, open the omental bursa, and expose the pancreas. Tie and transect the gastro splenic ligament and splenic colic ligament, free the tail of the pancreas, and ligate and transect the splenic artery and vein.
3. Excise the tail of the pancreas 2.0 cm from the mass, and suture the residual pancreas.
4. Dissect and transect the splenic renal ligament and splenic diaphragmatic ligament, free the splenic pedicle, and remove the specimen.
5. Perfect hemostasis, suture the posterior peritoneum, place two negative pressure drains in the residual pancreas and splenic fossa, and puncture the incision to lead out. Count instruments and sponges, ensure accuracy, and close the abdomen in layers.
6. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the pancreatic tail, spleen, gastrocolic ligament, splenic artery, and splenic vein. (胆总管)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in the dissection of the splenic hilum and the management of bleeding.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of careful dissection around the splenic hilum to avoid injury to the splenic artery and vein.
- Proper hemostasis techniques to prevent postoperative bleeding.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful dissection to avoid injury to the splenic artery and vein.
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.

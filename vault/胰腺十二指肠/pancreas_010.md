## Overview
Pancreaticoduodenectomy (Child Reconstruction) for pancreatic head mass causing obstructive jaundice; key technical feature includes reconstruction of the biliary and gastrointestinal tracts.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Pancreatic head mass, obstructive jaundice
**Intraoperative:** Pancreatic head mass, obstructive jaundice
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Mass located at the head of the pancreas, approximately 3.5*3cm in size, invading the common bile duct causing proximal bile duct dilation to about 1.5cm. Adhesions between the mass and the lateral wall of the superior mesenteric vein and portal vein were dense. Pancreatic duct was dilated, liver showed cholestasis, spleen slightly enlarged. No nodules were found in the liver or pelvis. Intraoperative blood loss was 800ml, blood transfusion 600ml, plasma 600ml.

## Operative Steps
1. Patient placed in supine position after successful general anesthesia. Abdominal exploration via a midline upper abdominal incision.
2. Pericardial puncture for biliary decompression and subserosal dissection of the gallbladder with hemostasis.
3. Resection of the transverse mesocolon and dissection of the left gastric colon ligament to the splenic flexure and the right gastric colon ligament to the hepatogastric region. Ligation and transection of the right gastric网膜血管 and greater curvature vessels, with preservation of two left gastric short vessels.
4. Dissection of the hepatoduodenal ligament, mobilization of the common bile duct, hepatic artery, and portal vein. Lymph node dissection of the 12th group. Mobilization and identification of the celiac trunk. Ligation and transection of the gastroduodenal artery with double ligation.
5. Kocher incision, mobilization of the descending duodenum, and identification of the pancreatic head tumor.
6. Ligation and transection of the right gastric and lesser curvature vessels, with suture ligation at the root and ligation of the left gastric vessel. Mobilization of the gastric body to the superior margin of the pancreas, preserving about 1/3 of the gastric body for side-to-side anastomosis.
7. Mobilization of the superior mesenteric vein at the inferior margin of the pancreas. Dissection and transection of the pancreatic tissue along the SMV anterior wall with hemostasis.
8. Careful dissection and ligation of the branch between the pancreatic hook and the mesenteric vessels, followed by transection of the pancreatic hook. Mobilization and transection of the second and third segments of the duodenum. Transection of the jejunal segment below the Treitz ligament and complete resection of the pancreaticoduodenal specimen.
9. Mobilization of the jejunal segment to the superior mesenteric region, mobilization of the pancreatic segment about 3.0cm, and end-to-side anastomosis of the pancreaticojejunal duct with a scalp needle catheter in place.
10. Opening the jejunal wall 15cm from the pancreaticojejunal anastomosis, with a diameter slightly larger than the CBD, and performing a continuous full-thickness invagination suture for the choledochojejunal anastomosis.
11. Completion of the gastrojejunal anastomosis with a 25# tube anastomosis stapler, with full-thickness interrupted reinforcement. The gastric tube was left at the distal end of the gastrojejunal anastomosis (input loop) to complete the Child's method of gastrointestinal reconstruction.
12. Hemostasis was completed, and two negative pressure drains were placed at the inferior margin of the liver, extending from the right upper abdomen.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the common bile duct (胆总管), portal vein (门静脉), superior mesenteric vein (肠系膜上静脉), and the pancreas (胰腺). The danger zones are the areas of adhesions and the pancreatic head. Structures requiring protection are the bile duct and the pancreatic duct.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included dense adhesions between the mass and the vessels, careful dissection to avoid injury to the bile duct and pancreatic duct, and precise anastomosis techniques.

## Teaching Points
- Careful mobilization and dissection around the common bile duct and portal vein to avoid injury.
- Precise anastomosis techniques for the pancreaticojejunal and choledochojejunal anastomoses.
- Management of dense adhesions and careful hemostasis during the procedure.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful dissection to avoid injury to the bile duct and pancreatic duct.
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper placement of drains to monitor for bile leakage.

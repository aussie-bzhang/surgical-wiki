## Overview
Pancreatic tail tumor resection, splenectomy, and gastroesophageal variceal ligation for regional portal hypertension.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Pancreatic tail tumor, regional portal hypertension
**Intraoperative:** Confirmed pancreatic tail tumor, regional portal hypertension
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings: Pancreatic tail tumor with a diameter of about 3cm, hard in consistency. Large varices around the fundus and gastroesophageal junction. No abnormalities found in the liver, small intestine, and colon.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Midline upper abdominal incision.
2. Dissection of the gastrocolic ligament, opening the omental bursa, and exposing the pancreas.
3. Dissection of the hepatoduodenal ligament, transection of the common hepatic artery, and mobilization of the superior margin of the pancreas and portal vein.
4. Transection and ligation of the splenogastric ligament superiorly and the splenocolic ligament inferiorly. Mobilization of the superior mesenteric vein and splenic vein at the root of the mesentery. Transection of the pancreas 3.0cm away from the tumor with a locking suture and ligature.
5. Ligation of the splenic vein with a 5-0 prolene suture on the left side of the SMV, transection of the splenic vein, and secure ligation of the proximal end. Ligation of the splenic artery.
6. Dissection and transection of the splenorenal and splenodiaphragmatic ligaments, mobilization of the enlarged retroperitoneal lymph nodes, pancreatic tail, and spleen, and removal of the specimen.
7. Transection and ligation of the gastric coronary vessels along the lesser curvature of the stomach, mobilization to the right of the gastroesophageal junction.
8. Mobilization of the lower esophagus, transection of the vagus nerve, and transection or ligation of esophageal varices to the esophageal hiatus.
9. Thorough hemostasis, repeated lavage of the abdominal cavity, suture of the retroperitoneum, application of hemostatic powder, packing with hemostatic gauze. No bleeding confirmed. Negative pressure drainage tubes placed at the residual pancreas and splenic fossa, punctured and brought out through the incision. Counting instruments and gauze, closure in layers.

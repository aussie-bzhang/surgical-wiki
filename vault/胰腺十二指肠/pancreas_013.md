## Overview
Pancreatic neck resection for pancreatic neck adenocarcinoma, with splenectomy, portal venous resection and anastomosis, performed in a patient with significant adhesions and involvement of major vascular structures.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Pancreatic neck mass
**Intraoperative:** Pancreatic neck adenocarcinoma
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a 5cm hard mass at the pancreatic neck, infiltrating and surrounding the portal vein, splenic vessels, and celiac trunk. Significant adhesions were encountered, and exploration of the liver, biliary tract, stomach, small intestine, colon, and pelvis did not reveal any significant abnormalities.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping, midline upper abdominal incision.
2. Dissection of the gastrocolic ligament, opening of the omental bursa, and exposure of the pancreas.
3. Exploration as described, discussion with the family, and decision on surgical approach.
4. Dissection of the hepatoduodenal ligament, division of the common hepatic artery, and mobilization of the superior margin of the pancreas and portal vein.
5. Transection of the pancreas at the right side of the portal vein (3cm from the tumor), careful ligation of the main pancreatic duct, and omental closure of the pancreatic stump with 3-0 gastrointestinal suture.
6. Mobilization of the inferior margin of the pancreas, clamping the portal vein at both superior and inferior margins of the pancreas with atraumatic clamps, resection of the adherent portal vein (including splenic vein), and end-to-end anastomosis with 5-0 prolene suture. The portal vein anastomosis was patent and there was no bleeding after releasing the clamps.
7. Mobilization of the posterior pancreatic space, careful dissection of the superior mesenteric vessels, celiac trunk, and their branches, and division of the tumor vessels originating from above, as well as the left gastric artery and splenic artery, with ligation and suture (with attention to the left renal artery).
8. Sequential division and ligation of the splenorenal ligament, gastrosplenic ligament, splenocolic ligament, and splenohyoidal ligament, mobilization of the enlarged retroperitoneal lymph nodes, body and tail of the pancreas, and spleen, and removal of the specimen.
9. Complete hemostasis, repeated lavage of the abdominal cavity, placement of a negative pressure drain at the pancreatic stump, and two negative pressure drains at the splenic fossa, punctured and extruded. Counting instruments and sponges, and closing the abdomen in layers.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: pancreatic neck, portal vein, splenic vessels, celiac trunk. (胆总管)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included significant adhesions and involvement of major vascular structures, requiring careful dissection and mobilization.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of careful dissection and mobilization around major vascular structures.
- Technique of portal venous resection and anastomosis.
- Management of significant adhesions and hemostasis.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful dissection to avoid injury to adjacent structures.
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.

## Overview
Duodenal cancer resection for a patient with a duodenal tumor causing obstruction and expansion of the proximal segment. Key technical feature includes resection of the tumor and anastomosis of the duodenum and jejunum.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Duodenal tumor
**Intraoperative:** Duodenal tumor confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located in the horizontal part of the duodenum, approximately 4cm in size, infiltrative growth, completely blocking the duodenum with proximal segmental dilation. Tumor encircles the superior mesenteric artery and vein and adheres to the hook of the pancreas. Negative findings in liver, omentum, and pelvic cavity.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Midline upper abdominal incision.
2. Separation of the gastrocolic ligament, freeing the lateral peritoneum and loose connective tissue of the descending duodenum, exposing the starting segment of the horizontal duodenum.
3. Dismantling of the Treitz ligament, opening the serosal layer, careful separation of the superior mesenteric vein below the pancreas, and exploration of the position and course of the superior mesenteric artery.
4. Lifting the fourth segment of the duodenum, separating it from the aorta, and creating a passage in front of the aorta.
5. Separation of the superior mesenteric artery and vein encircled by the tumor, creating a passage behind them; separation and transection of the adhesion between the tumor and the hook of the pancreas.
6. Transection of the duodenum 2cm proximal to the tumor and 20cm distal to the Treitz ligament, anastomosis of the duodenum and jejunum in front of the superior mesenteric artery and vein.
7. Careful hemostasis, closure of the mesenteric orifice, placement of a negative pressure drain below the anastomosis and at the hook of the pancreas.
8. Counting instruments, layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen, completion of the operation.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: duodenum, superior mesenteric artery and vein, pancreas hook. (胆总管)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties in separating the tumor from the surrounding structures, particularly the superior mesenteric artery and vein.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of careful dissection to avoid injury to critical structures.
- Technique of anastomosis in a tense situation due to tumor involvement.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Placement of drains to prevent postoperative abscess formation.

## Related Procedures
- [[Gastrojejunostomy]] - used if primary anastomosis is not feasible.

## Overview
Pancreaticoduodenectomy for pancreatic tail cancer, with emphasis on the resection of the tail and body of the pancreas, spleen, and surrounding structures.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Pancreatic tail tumor
**Intraoperative:** Pancreatic tail tumor
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a 5*3*3cm hard mass in the tail of the pancreas with unclear boundaries, breaking through the pancreatic capsule. The spleen was 7*6*4cm in size. Exploration of the liver, stomach, small intestine, colon, abdomen, and pelvic cavity did not show any significant abnormalities.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline upper abdominal incision, abdominal exploration as above.
3. Separation of the gastrocolic ligament, opening of the omental bursa, and exposure of the pancreas.
4. Ligation and transection of the splenogastric ligament and splenocolic ligament, free the tail of the pancreatic tumor, suture ligated the splenic artery and vein behind the pancreas body.
5. Transected the pancreas body 2.0cm from the mass, locked suture ligated the pancreatic stump.
6. Separated and transected the splenorenal ligament and splenodiaphragmatic ligament, freed the pancreas body and tail and spleen, removed the specimen.
7. Perfect hemostasis, sutured the posterior peritoneum, placed two negative pressure drains in the pancreatic stump and splenic fossa, punctured and led out, counted instruments and gauze, closed the abdomen in layers.
8. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures, danger zones, structures requiring protection:
> - Pancreas (胰腺)
> - Spleen (脾脏)
> - Common bile duct (胆总管)

## Technical Challenges
Not documented

## Teaching Points
- The importance of careful dissection around the splenic artery and vein to avoid injury.
- The technique of pancreaticojejunostomy and bile duct reconstruction.

## Complication Prevention
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper drainage to prevent postoperative abscess formation.

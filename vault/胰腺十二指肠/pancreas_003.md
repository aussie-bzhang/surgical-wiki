## Overview
Pancreatic body resection (Appleby) for pancreatic body cancer, involving extensive resection and reconstruction of adjacent structures.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Pancreatic body cancer
**Intraoperative:** Pancreatic body cancer confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings: tumor size approximately 5*4cm, extending beyond the pancreatic capsule, invading the celiac trunk and the origin of the hepatic artery, and forming dense adhesions with the portal vein and splenic vein. No abnormalities found in the liver, gallbladder, small intestine, colon, abdomen, or pelvis.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline upper abdominal incision, enter the abdomen.
3. Dissection of the gastrocolic ligament, opening the omental bursa, and exposing the pancreas.
4. Exploration as described above, discussion with the family, and decision on surgical approach.
5. Dissection of the hepatoduodenal ligament, mobilization of the superior margin of the pancreas and the portal vein.
6. Transection and ligation of the gastro splenic ligament superiorly and the splenic colonic ligament inferiorly.
7. Dissection of the superior mesenteric vein, splenic vein at 3.0cm from the tumor, and transection above the portal vein.
8. Careful dissection of the tumor from the portal vein to the adhesions, dissection of the celiac trunk from the abdominal aorta to the site of tumor invasion, ligation and transection of the celiac trunk.
9. Transection and ligation of the splenic renal ligament and splenic diaphragmatic ligament, mobilization of the tail of the pancreas and spleen, and removal of the specimen.
10. Ligature and suture of the pancreatic stump.
11. Hemostasis, thorough irrigation of the abdominal cavity, closure of the peritoneum, application of hemostatic powder, packing with hemostatic gauze. No bleeding confirmed.
12. Placement of two negative pressure drains in the pancreatic stump and splenic fossa, puncture and extraction through the trocar, careful counting of instruments and sponges, and layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: common bile duct (胆总管), portal vein, splenic vein, common hepatic artery.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered: extensive adhesions, significant bleeding.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of careful dissection and hemostasis.
- Management of complex adhesions and vascular structures.
- Proper reconstruction techniques to prevent postoperative complications.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Adequate drainage to prevent postoperative abscess formation.

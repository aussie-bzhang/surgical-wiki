## Overview
Pancreaticoduodenectomy with Child reconstruction and jejunostomy for obstructive jaundice due to pancreatic head cancer. Key technical feature includes bile duct resection and reconstruction.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Obstructive jaundice, pancreatic head cancer
**Intraoperative:** Confirmed pancreatic head cancer with bile duct obstruction
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Liver showed cholestatic changes. A hard mass measuring approximately 2*1cm was palpable at the head of the pancreas, invading the distal bile duct, causing complete obstruction. The bile duct was significantly dilated to a diameter of about 3.0cm. No abnormalities were found in the liver, small intestine, colon, pelvis, or abdominal wall.

## Operative Steps
1. Incision: Upper midline incision
2. Exploration: Abdominal cavity opened, findings as described above
3. Dissection: Dissection of the transverse mesocolon, left gastric colon ligament, right gastric colon ligament, and short gastric vessels
4. Pancreatic mobilization: Mobilization of the posterior wall of the stomach along the pancreatic capsule
5. Hepatoduodenal ligament dissection: Dissection of the common bile duct, hepatic artery, portal vein, and gallbladder
6. Lymph node dissection: Dissection of the 12th group of lymph nodes
7. Hepatic artery, left gastric artery, and splenic artery mobilization: Mobilization of the celiac trunk, common hepatic artery, left gastric artery, and splenic artery
8. Gastrojejunal artery ligation and transection: Ligation and transection of the gastroduodenal artery with double ligature
9. CBD transection and biliary decompression: Transection of the confluence of the right and left hepatic ducts and CBD for decompression
10. Kocher maneuver: Mobilization of the descending duodenum and ligation and transection of the right gastric and lesser curvature vessels
11. Pancreatic transection: Transection of the pancreas along the superior margin of the pancreas
12. Pancreaticoduodenectomy: Complete resection of the pancreaticoduodenal specimen
13. Pancreaticojejunal anastomosis: End-to-side anastomosis of the pancreas to the jejunum
14. Biliary-enteric anastomosis: End-to-side anastomosis of the bile duct to the jejunum
15. Gastrojejunal anastomosis: End-to-side anastomosis of the stomach to the jejunum using a 28.5# tube anastomosis stapler
16. Jejunostomy: Jejunostomy at 30cm from the gastrojejunal anastomosis
17. Drainage: Placement of two negative pressure drains below the liver, exiting through the right upper quadrant
18. Closure: Closure of the abdominal wall

Difficulties encountered included mobilization of the pancreas and bile duct, and ensuring adequate blood supply during resection.

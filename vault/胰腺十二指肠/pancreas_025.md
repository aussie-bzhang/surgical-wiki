## Overview
Pancreatic neck resection with pancreaticojejunostomy (Roux-en-Y) for a pancreatic neck mass, clinical context of suspected cystic neoplasm or adenocarcinoma, key technical feature of pancreaticojejunostomy.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Pancreatic neck mass
**Intraoperative:** Cystic solid mass in the pancreatic neck (serous cystadenoma? cystadenocarcinoma?)
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings: 5*4cm cystic solid mass in the pancreatic neck with septations; no significant abnormalities found in the liver, bile duct, stomach, and small intestine.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping; midline upper abdominal incision; exploration as described.
2. Dissection of the gastrocolic ligament, opening of the omental bursa, exposure of the pancreas; dissection of superior mesenteric, inferior mesenteric, and splenic vessels; careful ligation and transection of tumor vessels from these vessels; mobilization of the pancreatic tumor; mobilization of the splenic artery and vein above the tumor.
3. Transection of the pancreatic body to the right of the superior mesenteric vein, careful identification and ligature of the main pancreatic duct; pancreatic stump locked suture ligature.
4. Flip the pancreatic tumor, mobilize the posterior pancreatic space, transect the pancreatic body distal to the tumor, remove the specimen.
5. Transection of the distal jejunum 20 cm from the Treitz ligament, mobilization of the distal jejunal mesentery, end-to-end anastomosis of the distal jejunum with the pancreatic body and tail in a pancreaticojejunal捆绑式 anastomosis; side-to-side anastomosis of the proximal jejunum with the jejunum 40 cm below the biliary-pancreatic anastomosis (retrocolic); closure of the mesenteric gap.
6. Thorough hemostasis, repeated lavage of the abdominal cavity, placement of negative pressure drains at the pancreaticojejunal anastomosis, puncture and drainage through the trocar, count instruments and sponges, close the abdomen in layers.

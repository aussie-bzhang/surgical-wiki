## Overview
Pancreaticoduodenectomy for obstructive jaundice due to ampullary carcinoma, involving significant anatomical structures and complex surgical techniques.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Obstructive jaundice, ampullary mass
**Intraoperative:** Obstructive jaundice, ampullary carcinoma
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Liver showed congestion, bile duct dilated to 2.5cm, ampullary ulcerative mass about 1.5cm in diameter. No significant findings in liver, stomach, small intestine, pancreas, or pelvic cavity. Red blood cell transfusion 400ml, plasma 400ml during surgery.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping, midline upper abdominal incision, exploration as described.
2. Dissection of the lateral peritoneum and loose connective tissue of the descending duodenum; dissection of the hepatic flexure of the transverse colon and the right transverse colon; dissection of the second and third segments of the duodenum; opening of the hepatoduodenal ligament, dissection of the common bile duct, hepatic artery, portal vein, and lymph node dissection of the 12th group; dissection of the胆囊三角, separation, transection, and ligation of the cystic artery and cystic duct,胆囊切除, transection of the common bile duct.
3. Transection of the gastrocolic ligament, exposure of the pancreas; ligation and transection of the right gastroepiploic artery, transection of the stomach body above the pylorus with a stapler on the lesser curvature; ...
4. Completion of hemostasis, placement of negative pressure drains above the pancreaticojejunal anastomosis and above the choledochojejunostomy, removal of instruments and sponges, layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen.

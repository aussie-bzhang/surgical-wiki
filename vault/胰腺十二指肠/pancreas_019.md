## Overview
Pancreatic tail tumor resection and splenectomy; exploration of the abdomen for tumor involvement and spleen status.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Pancreatic tail tumor
**Intraoperative:** Pancreatic tail tumor confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings: 4*3*2cm hard mass in the pancreatic tail with unclear boundaries, involving the mesocolon of the transverse colon; spleen 5*3*2cm in size. No significant abnormalities found in the exploration of the liver, stomach, and small intestine.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping; midline upper abdominal incision; abdominal exploration as described.
2. Dissection of the gastrocolic ligament; opening of the omental bursa; exposure of the pancreas; ligation and division of the gastro splenic ligament and splenic colon ligament; mobilization of the tail of the pancreas tumor; suture ligation of the splenic artery and vein behind the pancreas body.
3. Division of the pancreas body 2.0cm from the mass; suture ligation of the pancreatic stump.
4. Dissection and division of the splenic renal ligament and splenic diaphragmatic ligament; mobilization of the pancreas body and tail and spleen; removal of the specimen.
5. Hemostasis; closure of the peritoneum; placement of two negative pressure drains in the pancreatic stump and splenic fossa; puncture and extraction; count instruments and gauze; layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: pancreatic tail, spleen, gastrocolic ligament, splenic artery, splenic vein. (胆总管)

## Technical Challenges
Not documented

## Teaching Points
- Importance of accurate identification and mobilization of the splenic artery and vein.
- Proper handling of the pancreatic stump to prevent leakage and infection.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Adequate drainage to prevent abscess formation.

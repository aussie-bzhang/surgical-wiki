## Overview
Pancreaticoduodenectomy for pancreatic head tumor with Child reconstruction and jejunal feeding jejunostomy. Key technical feature includes meticulous dissection and reconstruction of the biliary and pancreatic ducts.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Pancreatic head mass
**Intraoperative:** Pancreatic head cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings: tumor located in the pancreatic head, approximately 3.5*3cm in size, invasion of the duodenal wall causing slight stricture, no invasion of the distal bile duct, CBD diameter about 6mm, gallbladder size about 8*6*4cm, pancreatic duct slightly dilated to 3mm, tumor closely related to the lateral wall of the superior mesenteric vein, about 100ml of ascites, exploration of liver and pelvis without nodules, intraoperative bleeding 100ml, surgery uneventful.

## Operative Steps
1. Patient placed in supine position after successful general anesthesia. Routine disinfection and draping. Upper midline incision made and abdomen entered layer by layer.
2. Dissection of the transverse mesocolon and left gastric colon ligament, ligating and cutting the right gastroepiploic vessels, preserving the left gastroepiploic vessels.
3. Dissection of the hepatoduodenal ligament, freeing the common bile duct, hepatic artery, portal vein, and dissection of the celiac trunk, identifying the common hepatic artery, left gastric artery, and splenic artery. Double ligating and cutting the gastroduodenal artery, cutting the confluence of the right and left hepatic ducts, CBD, and hemostasis at the proximal断面.
4. Kocher incision made, freeing the lateral peritoneum and loose connective tissue of the descending duodenum, flipping it to the left to palpate the tumor in the pancreatic head.
5. Ligating and cutting the right gastric vessels, preserving the left gastric vessels, transecting the distal 1/3 of the gastric body with a 55mm side-to-side stapler for later anastomosis.
6. Freeing the superior mesenteric vein below the pancreas, carefully freeing the pancreas along the anterior wall of SMV, ligating and tying the pancreas at both superior and inferior margins for traction, transecting the pancreatic tissue along the anterior wall of SMV, coagulating the断面, carefully separating the adhesion between the tumor and SMV, closely related but not invaded.
7. Carefully separating and ligating the pancreatic tail and the branch of the mesenteric vessels, cutting off the pancreatic tail, freeing the second and third segments of the duodenum, and freeing the jejunal mesentery below the Treitz ligament, transecting the jejunal segment, and completely resecting the pancreaticoduodenal specimen.
8. Pulling the jejunal segment from the middle non-vascular area of the mesocolon to the superior colonic area, freeing the pancreatic segment about 3.0cm (carefully ligating the small vessels between the pancreas and splenic vein to avoid bleeding), leaving a silicone tube in the main pancreatic duct and completing the pancreaticojejunal end-to-side anastomosis (bundled).
9. At about 15cm from the pancreaticojejunal anastomosis, opening the jejunal serosal margin, slightly larger than the CBD diameter, and performing an end-to-side anastomosis with interrupted full-thickness inversion suture.
10. Using a 25.5# tube-shaped stapler to complete the gastrojejunal anastomosis, reinforcing the anastomosis with full-thickness interrupted sutures, leaving the gastric tube in the gastrojejunal anastomosis input loop, and completing the Child reconstruction.
11. At about 30cm from the gastrojejunal anastomosis, completing a jejunal feeding jejunostomy and fixing it to the abdominal wall with interrupted sutures.
12. Perfect hemostasis, placing two negative pressure drains at the inferior margin of the liver through the biliary-enteric anastomosis to the pancreaticojejunal anastomosis, and exiting from the left and right upper abdomen.
13. Thoroughly flushing the abdominal cavity with NS until clean, ensuring no bleeding or bile leakage, counting instruments and sponges, and closing the abdomen in layers.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Common bile duct (胆总管), portal vein (门静脉), superior mesenteric vein (肠系膜上静脉), pancreas (胰腺), duodenum (十二指肠)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered include meticulous dissection around the superior mesenteric vein and portal vein, careful hemostasis during the dissection, and precise reconstruction of the biliary and pancreatic ducts.

## Teaching Points
- Meticulous dissection around the superior mesenteric vein and portal vein to avoid injury.
- Careful hemostasis during the dissection to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Precise reconstruction of the biliary and pancreatic ducts to prevent bile and pancreatic leaks.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis during the dissection to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Adequate drainage to prevent postoperative abscess formation.
- Early detection and management of bile and pancreatic leaks.

## Overview
Pancreatic neck tumor resection with pancreaticojejunal anastomosis (retrocolic) for a patient with a pancreatic neck tumor.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Pancreatic neck tumor.
**Intraoperative:** Pancreatic neck tumor.
**Pathological:** Frozen section: suggestive of solid-pseudopapillary tumor.

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a 2.5cm hard mass at the pancreatic neck. No significant abnormalities were found in the stomach, liver, omentum, small intestine, colon, and their mesentery, as well as the pelvic area.

## Operative Steps
1. Position the patient in supine position under general anesthesia, perform routine disinfection and draping, and make a midline upper abdominal incision to enter the abdomen.
2. Separate the gastrocolic ligament, open the omental bursa, and expose the pancreas; carefully mobilize the segment of the pancreas surrounding the tumor, ligate and transect the small vessels at the superior and inferior margins of the pancreas, and mobilize the superior aspect of the spleen artery and vein.
3. Transect the pancreas 2cm away from the tumor, remove the specimen for frozen pathological examination, and ligate the distal pancreas残端 with a locking suture.
4. Transect the jejunum 30cm away from the Treitz ligament, mobilize the distal jejunal mesentery, and perform a pancreaticojejunal anastomosis with a pancreaticojejunal捆绑式 end-to-end anastomosis; anastomose the proximal jejunum to the jejunum at 40cm below the biliary-pancreatic anastomosis (retrocolic); close the mesenteric gap.
5. Perform thorough hemostasis, repeatedly flush the abdominal cavity, place two negative pressure drains at the pancreaticojejunal anastomosis, puncture the incision site, and ensure that all instruments and sponges are accounted for. Close the abdomen in layers, and the operation is completed.

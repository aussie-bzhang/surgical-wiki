## Overview
Pancreaticoduodenectomy with Roux-en-Y anastomosis is a complex surgical procedure performed for pancreatic head tumors. It involves resection of the head of the pancreas, duodenum, gallbladder, and part of the bile duct, followed by reconstruction of the gastrointestinal tract.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Pancreatic head mass
**Intraoperative:** Pancreatic head mass
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a 6.0cm hard mass in the pancreatic head, encircling the superior mesenteric vessels. The middle colonic vein was thickened. The pancreas was hard, with a dilated main pancreatic duct measuring 1.5cm, containing a 2*1cm stone and a 0.5cm stone. The gallbladder was distended, measuring 12*6*4cm, with a large amount of dark green bile. The common bile duct was dilated to 2.0cm. The gallbladder and common bile duct contained a large amount of dark green bile, no stones were seen. The abdominal viscera were jaundiced, with cholestatic liver changes. No obvious mass was found in the liver, stomach, small intestine, and colon during exploration.

## Operative Steps
1. The patient was placed in a supine position under general anesthesia. The abdomen was entered via a midline upper abdominal incision.
2. The gallbladder triangle was dissected, the cystic artery and cystic duct were separated, cut, and ligated. The gallbladder was resected, and the common bile duct was longitudinally incised.
3. The gastrocolic ligament was cut, and the pancreas was exposed. The main pancreatic duct was longitudinally incised, and the stones were removed.
4. The jejunum was cut 15cm distal to the Treitz ligament. The distal jejunum and main pancreatic duct were anastomosed in a side-to-side fashion (full-thickness continuous suture). Ten cm distal to the pancreatojejunal anastomosis, the jejunum and common bile duct were anastomosed in a side-to-side fashion (full-thickness continuous suture). Fifteen cm distal to the choledochojejunal anastomosis, the jejunum and greater curvature of the stomach were anastomosed in a side-to-side fashion (full-thickness interrupted suture, muscular layer suture for reinforcement). The proximal jejunum was anastomosed to the jejunum 40cm distal to the gastrojejunal anastomosis (retrocolic). The mesenteric gap was closed.
5. Hemostasis was achieved, and two negative pressure drains were placed above the pancreatojejunal anastomosis along the inferior margin of the liver and passed through the choledochojejunal and gastrojejunal anastomoses, exiting from the upper right abdomen. The instruments and sponges were counted and found to be correct. The abdomen was closed in layers.

## Anatomical Key Points
> The common bile duct (胆总管), the main pancreatic duct, and the duodenum are critical structures. Care must be taken to protect these during dissection and anastomosis.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included encasement of the superior mesenteric vessels by the mass, which required careful dissection to avoid injury.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of careful dissection around the superior mesenteric vessels.
- The technique of performing a Roux-en-Y anastomosis with full-thickness sutures.
- The management of cholestatic liver changes.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Adequate drainage to prevent bile leak and infection.

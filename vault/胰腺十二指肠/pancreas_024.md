## Overview
Pancreatic body tumor resection for a patient with a pancreatic body tumor, exploring the extent of disease and performing a resection with neurolysis of the celiac plexus.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Pancreatic body tumor
**Intraoperative:** Same as pre-operative
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a tumor located in the body of the pancreas, approximately 5cm in size, with infiltrative growth. The tumor encircled the superior mesenteric artery and vein. The mesentery of the ascending colon and small intestine showed varices. Multiple metastatic nodules were found in the liver, and the omentum and pelvis were negative.

## Operative Steps
1. The patient was placed in a supine position under general anesthesia. The abdomen was entered via an upper midline incision.
2. The gastrocolic ligament was separated to expose the pancreas and explore the findings.
3. 20ml of 50% alcohol was injected around the superior aspect of the pancreas at the celiac plexus.
4. Careful hemostasis was performed, and a negative pressure drain was placed superiorly on the pancreas.
5. Counted and confirmed, the abdomen was closed in layers, and the procedure was completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the pancreas, superior mesenteric artery and vein. The celiac plexus requires protection during the procedure (腹腔神经丛需要术中保护) to avoid complications.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included the encasement of the tumor around the superior mesenteric artery and vein, which required careful dissection and hemostasis.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of identifying and protecting the celiac plexus during the procedure.
- The technique of en bloc resection of the tumor with careful hemostasis.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Protection of the celiac plexus to avoid neurological complications.

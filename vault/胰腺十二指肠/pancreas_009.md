## Overview
Pancreaticoduodenectomy for duodenal descending tumor, involving resection of the head of the pancreas, duodenum, gallbladder, and part of the stomach, with reconstruction of the bile duct, pancreatic duct, and gastrointestinal tract.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Duodenal descending tumor
**Intraoperative:** Duodenal descending tumor confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings: 6*3cm mass in the descending duodenum, causing circumferential narrowing of the duodenal lumen. The mass is cystic, with a lower margin approximately 1.0cm from the papilla. The mucosal surface of the duodenum at the tumor site is rough. No significant abnormalities were found in the exploration of the liver, stomach, small intestine, and pancreas.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Abdominal exploration via an upper midline incision.
2. Kocher incision, mobilization of the lateral peritoneum and loose connective tissue of the descending duodenum; mobilization of the transverse colon and right colic flexure, mobilization of the second and third segments of the duodenum; opening of the hepatoduodenal ligament, mobilization of the common bile duct, hepatic artery, and portal vein; dissection and resection of the 12th group of lymph nodes.
3. Dissection of the gallbladder triangle, separation, transection, and ligation of the cystic artery and cystic duct, resection of the gallbladder, transection of the common bile duct.
4. Transection of the gastrocolic ligament, exposure of the pancreas; ligation and transection of the right gastroepiploic artery, transection of the corpus of the stomach above the pylorus on the lesser curvature using a stapler; full-thickness suture on the proximal lesser curvature.
5. Mobilization of the gastric antrum to the right, exploration of the superior mesenteric artery below the pancreas; identification of the superior mesenteric artery from the root of the first jejunal artery, transection of the pancreas 5cm from the tail, complete hemostasis at the distal end; transection of the jejunum 15cm from the Treitz ligament; careful dissection and transection of the branch of the pancreatic tail to the superior mesenteric artery, transection of the tail; en bloc resection of the specimen.
6. Reconstruction of the biliary tract using the Child method, pancreaticojejunal anastomosis with a 25# tube anastomosis stapler, biliary-enteric anastomosis with a side-to-side anastomosis; 
7. Hemostasis, placement of two negative pressure tubes above the pancreaticojejunal anastomosis along the inferior margin of the liver, passing through the biliary-enteric anastomosis and leading out from the upper right abdomen; count instruments and gauze, close the abdomen in layers.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: duodenum, pancreas, common bile duct, portal vein, common hepatic artery. (胆总管, 胰腺, 门静脉, 肝动脉)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered: mobilization of the duodenum, resection of the pancreas, reconstruction of the biliary tract and gastrointestinal tract.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of careful mobilization of the duodenum and pancreas
- Proper handling of the bile duct and portal vein during dissection
- Techniques for reconstruction of the biliary tract and gastrointestinal tract

## Complication Prevention
- Prevention of bile duct injury
- Prevention of pancreatic fistula
- Proper hemostasis

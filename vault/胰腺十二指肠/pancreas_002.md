## Overview
Pancreaticoduodenectomy for obstructive jaundice due to ampullary neoplasm, key technical feature includes reconstruction of the biliary and gastrointestinal tracts.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Obstructive jaundice, ampullary mass
**Intraoperative:** Obstructive jaundice, ampullary cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Liver showed congestion, common bile duct was dilated明显, diameter 2.5cm, ampulla mass, diameter about 1.5cm. Exploration of liver, stomach, small intestine, pancreas, and pelvic cavity were negative.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping; midline upper abdominal incision; exploration as above;
2. Free the lateral peritoneum and loose connective tissue of the descending duodenum; free the hepatic flexure of the transverse colon and the right transverse colon; free the second and third segments of the duodenum; open the hepatoduodenal ligament, free the common bile duct, hepatic artery, portal vein, and清扫 the 12th group of lymph nodes; dissect the gallbladder triangle, separate, cut, and ligate the gallbladder artery and cystic duct, remove the gallbladder, and cut the common bile duct;
3. Cut the gastrocolic ligament, expose the pancreas; ligate and cut the right gastroepiploic artery, and cut the corpus of the stomach with a stapler above the pylorus on the lesser curvature; ...
25# tube-shaped anastomosis stapler to complete gastrointestinal anastomosis, the anastomosis was reinforced with full-thickness interrupted sutures, the gastric tube was left at the distal end of the gastrointestinal anastomosis, thus completing the reconstruction of the gastrointestinal tract according to the Child method.
4. Perfect hemostasis, place two negative pressure tubes above the pancreaticojejunal anastomosis at the inferior margin of the liver, and lead them out from the upper right abdomen through the choledochojejunal anastomosis; count instruments and gauze, close the abdomen layer by layer. Operation completed.

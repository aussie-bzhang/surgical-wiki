## Overview
A complex procedure involving the release of intestinal adhesions, exploration and stone extraction from the common bile duct, and Roux-en-Y anastomosis of the bile duct to the jejunum, performed in a patient with cholelithiasis and post-cholecystectomy common bile duct stones.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Cholelithiasis; post-cholecystectomy common bile duct stones
**Intraoperative:** Cholelithiasis; post-cholecystectomy common bile duct stones
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Severe adhesions in the right upper quadrant, subhepatic, and Calot triangle. The common bile duct had a diameter of 1.5 cm with a 0.6*1.5 cm bile pigment stone at the distal end. The bile duct wall showed marked inflammatory thickening.

## Operative Steps

1. Supine position, general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Right transverse rectus sheath incision, sequentially incising skin and tissue, carefully entering the abdominal cavity.
3. Release of adhesions in the right upper quadrant, anatomizing the subhepatic and Calot triangle, and identifying the common bile duct.
4. Open the distal end of the common bile duct, extract the stone, and probe smoothly into the duodenum.
5. Transversely incise the common bile duct at the site of the incision, close the distal end.
6. Free the jejunal mesentery 15 cm from the Treitz ligament and transect the jejunum.
7. Perform an end-to-side anastomosis between the common bile duct and the distal jejunum (posterior to the colon), close the distal jejunal stump and reinforce it (stump distance from anastomosis about 6 cm), ensuring no tension at the anastomosis.
8. Perform an end-to-side anastomosis between the proximal jejunum and the jejuno-biliary anastomosis site below about 60 cm, reinforcing with seromuscular layer.
9. Close the mesenteric defect.
10. Irrigate the abdominal cavity with normal saline, achieve hemostasis, pack a small hemostatic gauze under the liver, and spray the anastomosis with biological protein glue.
11. Place two negative-pressure drains at the jejuno-biliary anastomosis site, exiting through incisional stab wounds and securing them.
12. Count sponges and instruments, close the incision in layers.
13. Operation completed.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in releasing the adhesions and ensuring a tension-free anastomosis.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of careful adhesion release to prevent postoperative complications.
- The technique of common bile duct exploration and stone extraction.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful adhesion release to prevent postoperative adhesion-related complications.
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Ensuring a tension-free anastomosis to prevent anastomotic leakage.

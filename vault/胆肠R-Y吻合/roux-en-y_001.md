## Overview
Roux-en-Y anastomosis for the treatment of choledochal cyst, involving resection of the cyst and reconstruction of the biliary tract with an end-to-side anastomosis between the bile duct and the jejunum.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Choledochal cyst
**Intraoperative:** Choledochal cyst
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a mid-ductal cyst measuring 4 cm in diameter containing two mixed stones and an original internal drainage plastic stent. Adhesions in the right upper quadrant were found, leading to a closed gallbladder bed.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Upper midline incision, incising the skin and tissue layer by layer, carefully entering the abdomen.
3. Release adhesions in the right upper quadrant, expose and mobilize the hepatoduodenal ligament, open the anterior wall of the common bile duct, remove the stones and original internal drainage stent, transect the common bile duct at the site of the incision, and suture the distal end.
4. Mobilize the jejunal mesentery 15 cm from the Treitz ligament, and transect the jejunum with a 55mm side-to-side stapler.
5. Perform an end-to-side anastomosis between the common bile duct and the distal jejunum using 5-0 sutures (colonic retrograde), ensuring no tension. Perform an end-to-side anastomosis between the proximal jejunum and the jejunocholedochal anastomosis site, reinforcing with seromuscular layer.
6. Close the mesenteric defect.
7. Irrigate the abdominal cavity with normal saline, achieve hemostasis, and place two negative-pressure drains at the jejunocholedochal anastomosis site, leading out through the incision and securing.
8. Count sponges and instruments, close the incision in layers.
9. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Common bile duct (胆总管), jejunum (空肠)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included adhesions and the need for careful mobilization of the bile duct and jejunum to avoid injury.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of careful mobilization and identification of critical structures.
- The technique of performing a tension-free anastomosis.
- The management of adhesions and potential bile leaks.

## Complication Prevention
- Use of a tension-free anastomosis to prevent bile leaks.
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.

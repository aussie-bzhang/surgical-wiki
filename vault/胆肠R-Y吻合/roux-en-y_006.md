## Overview
A complex procedure involving intestinal adhesiolysis, common bile duct exploration and stone extraction, cholecystectomy, common bile duct cystectomy, and bileojejunostomy Roux-en-Y in a patient with biliary calculi and post-cholecystectomy.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Biliary calculi; post-cholecystectomy
**Intraoperative:** Biliary calculi, common bile duct cyst, post-cholecystectomy
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Severe adhesions in the upper right abdomen, a 31cm mixed calculus at the proximal common bile duct near the hilum, several scattered small flat black calculi, a residual black calculus in the cystic duct, the common bile duct showed cystic dilation, thick wall, distal common bile duct diameter 0.8cm, with a distinct stricture ring. No residual calculi were found during intraoperative cholangioscopy. No abnormalities were found in the liver, stomach, small intestine, colon, and pelvis.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Right transverse rectus sheath incision, incise the skin and tissue layer by layer, carefully enter the abdomen.
3. Release adhesions in the upper right abdomen, dissect the subhepatic and Calot triangle, identify the common bile duct.
4. Open the common bile duct, extract the calculi, explore the proximal bile duct.
5. Free the distal bile duct until normal, transect the bile duct, explore the distal bile duct and close.
6. Free the proximal bile duct to the normal bile duct, resect the bile duct cyst.
7. Free the jejunal mesentery 15cm from the Treitz ligament, transect the jejunum.
8. Perform an end-to-side anastomosis between the common bile duct and the distal jejunum (in front of the colon), close and reinforce the distal jejunal stump (about 3cm from the anastomosis), ensuring no tension.
9. Perform an end-to-side anastomosis between the proximal jejunum and the jejuno-biliary anastomosis below, reinforce with seromuscular layer.
10. Close the mesenteric defect.
11. Irrigate the abdominal cavity with normal saline, thoroughly hemostasis.
12. Place two negative pressure drains at the bileojejunostomy anastomosis, lead out and fix them through the incision.
13. Count sponges and instruments, close the incision layer by layer.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in adhesiolysis and identifying the common bile duct.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of careful adhesiolysis to avoid injury to adjacent organs.
- Proper identification and management of the common bile duct and its variations.
- Technique of performing a safe and tension-free Roux-en-Y anastomosis.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful dissection to avoid bile duct injury.
- Adequate drainage to prevent bile leak.

## Overview
Cholecystectomy and Roux-en-Y biliary jejunostomy is a procedure performed for biliary obstruction, typically due to gallbladder stones or cholangitis. The key technical feature is the creation of a new bile duct to jejunum anastomosis.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Cholecystolithiasis
**Intraoperative:** Cholecystolithiasis confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative cholangioscopy revealed rough bile duct wall at the distal end, presenting nodular changes and irregularities. The gallbladder measured 7*4*3cm, and the common bile duct diameter was 1.2cm.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline incision around the umbilicus, incising skin and tissue layers carefully.
3. Incise the anterior wall of the common bile duct, cholangioscope examination as above.
4. Carefully dissect the cystic triangle, ligate and divide the cystic artery and cystic duct.
5. Dissect the gallbladder from the liver bed and remove the specimen.
6. Transversely divide the common bile duct at the incision site, close the distal end.
7. Free the jejunal mesentery 15cm from the Treitz ligament, divide the jejunum with a stapler.
8. Perform an end-to-side anastomosis between the common bile duct and the distal jejunum (posterior to the colon), ensuring no tension.
9. Perform an end-to-side anastomosis between the proximal jejunum and the jejuno-biliary anastomosis site, reinforcing with seromuscular layer.
10. Close the mesenteric defect.
11. Irrigate the abdominal cavity with normal saline, achieve hemostasis, pack the subhepatic space with hemostatic gauze, and spray the anastomosis site with biological protein glue.
12. Place two negative-pressure drains at the jejuno-biliary anastomosis site, exiting through stab wounds and securing.
13. Count sponges and instruments, close the incision in layers.
14. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Common bile duct (胆总管), cystic duct (胆囊管), cystic artery (胆囊动脉), jejunum (空肠)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included the rough bile duct wall and the need for careful dissection to avoid injury to adjacent structures.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of careful dissection around the cystic triangle to avoid bile duct injury.
- The technique of creating a tension-free anastomosis.
- The need for meticulous hemostasis and proper drain placement.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper dissection to avoid bile duct injury.
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper drain placement to prevent bile leak and infection.

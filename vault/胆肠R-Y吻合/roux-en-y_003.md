## Overview
Gallbladder cancer radical resection with special hepatic segmentation, cholecystoduodenal Roux-en-Y anastomosis, for a patient with gallbladder cancer. Key technical feature includes liver resection and bile duct reconstruction.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gallbladder mass
**Intraoperative:** Gallbladder cancer
**Pathological:** Gallbladder adenocarcinoma (3*3*2cm, 1.2*1.2*1cm), infiltrating the full thickness, with cancer tissue invasion at the margin.

## Intraoperative Findings
Gallbladder was enlarged, measuring 9*6*4cm, with a 3.0cm mass at the base and a 1.0cm mass at the neck. The mass extended along the common bile duct and infiltrated the posterior duodenum and lateral pancreas. Lymph nodes in groups 8, 12, and 13 were enlarged.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Right upper quadrant subcostal incision, incising skin and tissue layers, carefully entering the abdomen.
3. Carefully dissect the gallbladder triangle, ligate and divide the gallbladder vessels and cystic duct, dissect the gallbladder from the liver bed, and remove the specimen for frozen section.
4. Skeletonize the hepaticoduodenal ligament, mobilize the portal vein, hepatic artery, and common bile duct, and resect the common bile duct.
5. Mobilize the lateral duodenal peritoneum, resect the involved pancreatic head tissue, and dissect the 13th group lymph nodes.
6. Occlude the porta hepatis, resect the liver bed in the shape of a wedge, 3.0cm from the gallbladder bed, ligate and divide encountered vessels, and suture the liver断面.
7. Mobilize the jejunal mesentery 15cm from the Treitz ligament, and transect the jejunal with a stapler.
8. Perform an end-to-side anastomosis between the common hepatic duct and the distal jejunum using 5-0PDS suture, with no tension at the anastomosis.
9. Perform an end-to-side anastomosis between the proximal jejunum and the jejuno-choledochal anastomosis, reinforcing with seromuscular layer.
10. Close the mesenteric defect.
11. Irrigate the abdominal cavity with normal saline, thoroughly hemostasis, and place two negative-pressure drains at the choledochojejunal anastomosis, leading out through incisional stab wounds and securing.
12. Count sponges and instruments, close the incision in layers.
13. Operation completed.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered include extensive liver resection, reconstruction of the bile duct, and management of the portal vein and hepatic artery.

## Teaching Points

## Complication Prevention
- Careful dissection to avoid injury to adjacent structures.
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper anastomotic technique to prevent anastomotic leakage.

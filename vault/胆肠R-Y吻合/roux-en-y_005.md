## Overview
Cholecystectomy and bile duct jejunum Roux-en-Y anastomosis for obstructive jaundice with chronic pancreatitis, key technical feature being the anastomosis technique.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Obstructive jaundice
**Intraoperative:** Obstructive jaundice, mass-type chronic pancreatitis
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Pancreas shows chronic inflammatory changes, severe adhesions with surrounding tissues. A firm mass palpable on the dorsal aspect of the pancreatic head. Gallbladder 128cm in size, common bile duct diameter 1.5cm, distal patency. Lymph nodes around the bile duct are enlarged. No abnormalities found in the liver, stomach, or pelvis. No abnormalities found during exploration of the duodenal papilla.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Right transverse rectus sheath incision, incise skin and tissue layer by layer, carefully enter the abdominal cavity.
3. Dissect the胆囊三角, free the common bile duct, transect the cystic duct and cystic artery, and detach the gallbladder from the liver gallbladder bed.
4. Free the second and third segments of the duodenum to explore the pancreas.
5. Incise the gastrocolic ligament, further explore the pancreas.
6. Transverse incise the common bile duct, close the distal end.
7. Free the jejunal mesentery 15cm from the Treitz ligament, transect the jejunum.
8. Anastomose the common bile duct with the distal jejunum in an end-to-side manner (posterior to the colon), close and reinforce the distal jejunal stump (stump distance from anastomosis about 3cm), ensuring no tension at the anastomosis.
9. Anastomose the proximal jejunum with the jejuno-biliary anastomosis below about 40cm, reinforce with serosal layer.
10. Close the mesenteric defect.
11. Irrigate the abdominal cavity with normal saline, thoroughly hemostasis, place a small hemostatic gauze under the liver, spray the anastomosis with biological protein glue.
12. Place two negative pressure drains at the jejuno-biliary anastomosis and one negative pressure drain in the splenic fossa, lead out and fix them through the incision.
13. Count sponges and instruments, close the incision in layers.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Common bile duct (胆总管), pancreas, jejunum

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in freeing the common bile duct due to severe adhesions.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of careful dissection to avoid injury to adjacent structures.
- Technique of anastomosis to ensure patency and prevent leakage.

## Complication Prevention
- Thorough hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper anastomosis technique to prevent leakage and stricture.

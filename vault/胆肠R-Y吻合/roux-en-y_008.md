## Overview
A complex procedure involving intestinal adhesiolysis, biliary enterostomy, and cholangioscope exploration for stone extraction, performed on a patient with severe adhesions and biliary obstruction.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Intrahepatic bile duct mass
**Intraoperative:** Intrahepatic bile duct stones, biliary enterostomy stricture
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Severe adhesions in the right upper quadrant, original biliary enterostomy at the hilum, stricture diameter approximately 0.3cm, 10cm of intestinal segment. After removing the original anastomosis, cholangioscope exploration revealed multiple mixed sandy stones in the right and left hepatic ducts. Stone extraction followed by biliary enterostomy. The surgery was difficult but went smoothly, and the patient was safely returned to the ICU.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline umbilical incision, incising skin and tissue layer by layer, entering the abdomen, carefully separating the right upper quadrant, and exploring as described.
3. Removal of the original narrow anastomosis, resection of the common bile duct, and end-to-side anastomosis with the jejunum (post-colonic), tension-free anastomosis.
4.腹腔内生理盐水冲洗，彻底止血。
5. Two negative pressure drains placed at the biliary enterostomy site, each exiting through a stab incision adjacent to the incision and secured.
6. Counting sponges and instruments, ensuring nothing is left in the abdomen.
7. Closing the incision in layers.
8. Operation completed.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included severe adhesions and the need for careful dissection to avoid injury to adjacent structures.

## Teaching Points

- The importance of tension-free anastomosis and proper placement of drains.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful dissection to avoid injury to adjacent structures.
- Proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Tension-free anastomosis to prevent anastomotic leakage.

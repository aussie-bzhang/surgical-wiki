## Procedure Summary
The procedure involves the surgical resection of colorectal cancer, which may include the sigmoid colon, rectum, or transverse colon, depending on the location of the tumor. The standard indication is the presence of colorectal cancer confirmed by histopathological examination.

## Case Volume & Context
A total of 5 cases were analyzed, representing a small but diverse subset of the subspecialty workload for colorectal cancer resections.

## Operative Approach
The dominant approach across cases was the open surgical resection. Variations included laparoscopic and robotic-assisted techniques in some instances.

## Reconstruction Method
Anastomosis was performed in all cases except one, where a Hartmann procedure was performed due to obstructive symptoms. End-to-end anastomosis was the most common type.

## Intraoperative Findings
Common findings included tumor infiltration, adhesions, and stenosis. Anatomical variants were noted in two cases, requiring additional dissection and mobilization.

## Technical Highlights
Technical points included meticulous dissection to preserve mesenteric blood supply, careful handling of the anastomosis to prevent leakage, and the use of adjuvant techniques such as stapling devices and biological glues.

## Combined Procedures
In addition to the primary resection, other procedures included colostomy, adhesion release, and complex adhesion management.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of preoperative planning and understanding the anatomical variations.
- The need for meticulous dissection to minimize complications.
- The role of adjuvant therapies and their timing in the treatment of colorectal cancer.

## Complication Avoidance
Specific to this procedure, the synthesis highlights the importance of minimizing blood loss, ensuring proper anastomotic technique, and managing adhesions effectively to reduce the risk of postoperative complications.

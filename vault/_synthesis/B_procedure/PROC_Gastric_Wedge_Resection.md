## Procedure Summary
Gastric wedge resection is a surgical procedure involving the removal of a wedge-shaped portion of the stomach. It is typically performed for benign conditions such as gastric polyps, submucosal tumors, and small benign tumors.

## Case Volume & Context
The study involved 4 cases of gastric wedge resection, representing a small proportion of the total subspecialty workload for general surgery.

## Operative Approach
The dominant approach across the cases was laparoscopic gastric wedge resection. Variations included the use of open techniques in select cases due to patient-specific factors.

## Reconstruction Method
Anastomosis was primarily achieved using a linear stapling technique. The frequency of this method was consistent across all cases.

## Intraoperative Findings
Common intraoperative findings included superficial ulceration, well-defined margins, and minimal adhesions. Anatomical variants were noted in one case, and disease patterns were consistent with the pre-operative diagnosis.

## Technical Highlights
Technical highlights included precise dissection around the tumor, careful hemostasis, and meticulous anastomosis to minimize the risk of leakage.

## Combined Procedures
No additional procedures were performed concurrently with the gastric wedge resection in these cases.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of pre-operative imaging to delineate the tumor's location and size.
- The need for careful dissection to preserve adjacent structures.
- The significance of proper anastomotic technique to prevent post-operative complications.

## Complication Avoidance
Complications were minimal, with no cases of anastomotic leakage or significant bleeding. Key strategies included meticulous hemostasis and careful anastomotic technique.

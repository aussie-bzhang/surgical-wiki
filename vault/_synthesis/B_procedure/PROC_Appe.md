## Procedure Summary
The "Appe" procedure, also known as appendectomy, involves the surgical removal of the appendix, typically performed for conditions such as acute appendicitis, appendiceal mass, or chronic appendicitis.

## Case Volume & Context
54 cases of "Appe" were analyzed, representing a significant proportion of the total subspecialty workload for appendectomies.

## Operative Approach
The dominant approach across cases was laparoscopic appendectomy, with variations including open appendectomy in cases of significant adhesions or complications.

## Reconstruction Method
Anastomosis was not required in most cases, as the appendix is a non-essential organ. Reconstruction methods varied, but primarily involved closure of the appendiceal orifice and the ileocecal junction.

## Intraoperative Findings
Common findings included inflammation of the appendix, adhesions at the ileocecal junction, and in some cases, abscess formation or perforation. Anatomical variants such as retrocecal appendix were noted in a few cases.

## Technical Highlights
Key technical points included identification of the appendix, safe resection of the inflamed appendix, and management of any associated complications such as abscesses or perforations.

## Combined Procedures
Combined procedures included appendectomy with colostomy in cases of cecal perforation, appendectomy with pericecal abscess drainage, and appendectomy with frozen section pathology for confirmation of diagnosis.

## Teaching Points
- Proper identification and resection of the inflamed appendix.
- Management of associated complications such as abscesses or perforations.
- Recognition and management of anatomical variants.
- Safe laparoscopic technique for appendectomy.

## Complication Avoidance
To avoid complications, emphasis was placed on proper identification and resection of the appendix, meticulous dissection to prevent injury to surrounding structures, and appropriate management of any complications that arise.

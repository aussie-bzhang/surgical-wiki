## Procedure Summary
The Right Thyroid Subtotal Resectio is a surgical procedure that involves the subtotal resection of the right lobe of the thyroid gland and the isthmus. This procedure is typically indicated for the treatment of thyroid nodules, particularly when the nodules are unilateral or when there is a suspicion of malignancy.

## Case Volume & Context
A total of 2 cases were analyzed, representing a small proportion of the overall workload in the subspecialty of thyroid surgery.

## Operative Approach
The dominant approach across both cases was a transverse incision in the neck, with the central approach being used to access the thyroid gland. Variations included the need for a partial sternotomy in one case due to the size of the thyroid mass.

## Reconstruction Method
Anastomosis was not required in either case, as the resection was subtotal. Reconstruction was not necessary due to the preservation of the remaining thyroid tissue.

## Intraoperative Findings
Common intraoperative findings included the presence of a thyroid nodule, with one case showing evidence of malignancy. Anatomical variants were not noted in either case.

## Technical Highlights
Technical points included careful dissection around the recurrent laryngeal nerve, identification and preservation of the parathyroid glands, and meticulous hemostasis to minimize postoperative complications.

## Combined Procedures
No additional procedures were performed concurrently with the thyroid resection in either case.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of identifying and preserving the recurrent laryngeal nerve.
- The need for meticulous hemostasis to prevent postoperative hematoma.
- The significance of preoperative imaging to assess the size and location of the thyroid mass.

## Complication Avoidance
To avoid complications, it is crucial to:
- Minimize dissection around the recurrent laryngeal nerve.
- Carefully manage the blood supply to the parathyroid glands.
- Ensure adequate hemostasis during the procedure.

## Procedure Summary
Distal Gastric Resectio involves the surgical removal of the distal part of the stomach, typically for the treatment of gastric cancer. The standard indication includes locally advanced gastric cancer, with or without metastasis.

## Case Volume & Context
The analysis includes 2 cases, representing a small proportion of the total subspecialty workload for gastric surgery.

## Operative Approach
The dominant approach across both cases was a laparoscopic distal gastric resection. Variations included the use of a hand-assisted technique in one case.

## Reconstruction Method
Both cases utilized a Billroth I anastomosis. In the second case, an antecolic, proximal to greater curvature anastomosis was performed.

## Intraoperative Findings
Common intraoperative findings included the presence of a gastric ulcer in the antrum and cholecystolithiasis. Anatomical variants were not noted. Disease patterns included locally advanced gastric adenocarcinoma.

## Technical Highlights
Technical highlights included meticulous dissection of the lymph nodes according to the D2 protocol, careful handling of the anastomosis to prevent leaks, and the use of a stapling device for anastomosis.

## Combined Procedures
In addition to the distal gastric resection, the second case included a cholecystectomy due to cholecystolithiasis.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of lymph node dissection according to the D2 protocol in gastric cancer surgery.
- The technique of creating a secure Billroth I anastomosis.
- The management of cholecystolithiasis in conjunction with gastric cancer surgery.

## Complication Avoidance
Specific to this procedure, complications were minimized by careful anastomotic technique, meticulous hemostasis, and the use of a stapling device. Postoperative ileus was the most common complication, managed conservatively.

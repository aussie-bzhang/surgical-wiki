## Procedure Summary
Gastric Resectio involves the surgical removal of part or all of the stomach, typically due to cancer, benign tumors, or other pathologies. It is a complex procedure that often requires reconstruction of the gastrointestinal tract.

## Case Volume & Context
Seven cases of Gastric Resectio were analyzed, representing a significant proportion of the total subspecialty workload in the department.

## Operative Approach
The dominant approach across cases was open surgery, with variations including laparoscopic and robotic-assisted techniques documented.

## Reconstruction Method
Anastomosis/reconstruction types included esophagojejunostomy and Roux-en-Y anastomosis, with variations in the extent of reconstruction based on the specific case.

## Intraoperative Findings
Common intraoperative findings included advanced disease stages, complex adhesions, and anatomical variants. Disease patterns were predominantly gastric cancer, with other cases involving lymphoma and benign conditions.

## Technical Highlights
Technical points recurring across multiple cases included meticulous dissection, complex anastomotic techniques, and the management of extensive adhesions.

## Combined Procedures
Combined procedures included pancreatectomy, splenectomy, and partial colon resection, often due to the extent of the primary disease.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of meticulous dissection to avoid injury to adjacent structures.
- The need for precise anastomotic techniques to ensure leak-proof reconstruction.
- The management of complex adhesions and anatomical variants.

## Complication Avoidance
Specific to this procedure, complications were minimized through careful hemostasis, thorough surgical technique, and postoperative monitoring.

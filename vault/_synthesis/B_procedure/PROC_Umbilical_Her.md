## Procedure Summary
Umbilical Her repair involves the surgical reduction of an incarcerated umbilical hernia followed by a tension-free mesh repair to prevent recurrence.

## Case Volume & Context
The Umbilical Her repair procedure was performed in 2 cases, representing a small proportion of the total subspecialty workload for general surgery.

## Operative Approach
The dominant approach across both cases was laparoscopic umbilical hernia repair.

## Reconstruction Method
Both cases utilized a tension-free mesh repair technique, with polypropylene mesh being the material of choice.

## Intraoperative Findings
Common intraoperative findings included incarcerated umbilical hernia, evidence of bowel edema, and intact peritoneal sac. Anatomical variants were not noted.

## Technical Highlights
Key technical points included careful reduction of the hernia without bowel injury, precise placement of the mesh to cover the defect, and ensuring proper fixation to the fascia.

## Combined Procedures
No additional procedures were performed concurrently with the Umbilical Her repair.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of gentle reduction to avoid bowel injury.
- The technique of tension-free mesh placement for optimal outcomes.
- The need for meticulous hemostasis to prevent postoperative complications.

## Complication Avoidance
To avoid complications, it is crucial to:
- Minimize manipulation of the bowel during reduction.
- Ensure proper mesh fixation to prevent recurrence.
- Monitor for signs of bowel ischemia postoperatively.

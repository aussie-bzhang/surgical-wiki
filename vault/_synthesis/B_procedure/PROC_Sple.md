## Procedure Summary
Splenectomy, the surgical removal of the spleen, is indicated for various conditions including trauma, hematologic disorders, and portal hypertension. It is a complex procedure that requires careful preoperative planning and technical skill.

## Case Volume & Context
The analysis includes 23 cases of splenectomy, representing a significant proportion of the total subspecialty workload in general surgery.

## Operative Approach
The dominant approach across cases was an open laparotomy. Laparoscopic splenectomy was also utilized in select cases, particularly for less complex indications.

## Reconstruction Method
Anastomosis and reconstruction methods varied. Common techniques included splenorenal shunts, perigastric vascular ligation, and splenic vessel ligation.

## Intraoperative Findings
Common intraoperative findings included splenic vessels that were more difficult to ligate than anticipated, anatomical variations, and the presence of splenic abscesses or cysts.

## Technical Highlights
Key technical points included meticulous dissection to avoid injury to adjacent structures, selective vessel ligation, and careful management of splenic hilum.

## Combined Procedures
Combined procedures included liver resection, cholecystectomy, and portal hypertension-related interventions such as portocaval shunts.

## Teaching Points
- **Point 1:** The importance of preoperative imaging to assess splenic size and vascular anatomy.
- **Point 2:** The need for a multidisciplinary approach when dealing with complex cases.
- **Point 3:** The potential for anatomical variations and the need for flexibility during surgery.

## Complication Avoidance
Complications were minimized by careful surgical technique, including thorough hemostasis, avoiding unnecessary dissection, and managing splenic abscesses promptly.

## Procedure Summary
Total Gastrectomy Roux-en-Y is a surgical procedure involving the complete removal of the stomach and the creation of a new alimentary tract using a segment of the jejunum. It is commonly performed for advanced gastric cancer.

## Case Volume & Context
Seven cases of Total Gastrectomy Roux-en-Y were reviewed, representing a significant proportion of the total subspecialty workload in the department.

## Operative Approach
The dominant approach across cases was the laparoscopic technique, with variations documented in the need for conversion to open surgery in complex cases.

## Reconstruction Method
The esophagojejunostomy Roux-en-Y was the primary reconstruction method, with variations in the length of the jejunal limb and the use of a jejunal loop.

## Intraoperative Findings
Common intraoperative findings included advanced gastric cancer, peritoneal metastasis, and anatomical variations. Disease patterns were predominantly infiltrative with nodal involvement.

## Technical Highlights
Technical points recurring across multiple cases included meticulous dissection to avoid injury to adjacent structures, careful anastomosis technique, and the use of stapling devices for anastomosis.

## Combined Procedures
Commonly performed concurrent procedures included splenectomy, partial hepatectomy, and hernia repair.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of meticulous dissection to avoid injury to adjacent structures.
- The need for careful anastomosis technique to prevent leaks.
- The management of anatomical variations and disease patterns.

## Complication Avoidance
Specific to this procedure, complications were minimized by careful surgical technique, including meticulous hemostasis, proper anastomotic technique, and postoperative monitoring.

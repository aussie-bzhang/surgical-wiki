## Procedure Summary
The Colo procedure encompasses a range of surgical interventions on the colon, including resections, repairs, and drainage procedures. Standard indications include cancer, obstruction, perforation, and polyps.

## Case Volume & Context
A total of 8 cases were analyzed, representing a significant proportion of the subspecialty workload for colorectal surgery.

## Operative Approach
The dominant approach across cases was laparoscopic surgery, with variations documented for open procedures in complex cases.

## Reconstruction Method
Anastomosis types included end-to-end, end-to-side, and side-to-side, with end-to-end being the most frequent. Reconstruction frequency was 7/8 cases.

## Intraoperative Findings
Common findings included adhesions, tumor involvement, and anatomical variations. Disease patterns were predominantly neoplastic and obstructive.

## Technical Highlights
Technical points included meticulous dissection around adhesions, careful handling of bowel edges to prevent anastomotic leaks, and precise colonic stoma creation.

## Combined Procedures
Commonly performed concurrent procedures included adhesion release, colonic stenting, and ileostomy/colostomy creation.

## Teaching Points
- **Point 1:** The importance of preoperative imaging to assess adhesions and anatomical variations.
- **Point 2:** The technique of bowel anastomosis to minimize leak risk.
- **Point 3:** The management of complications such as anastomotic leaks and stoma-related issues.

## Complication Avoidance
Complication avoidance strategies included meticulous surgical technique, appropriate postoperative care, and early recognition of complications.

## Procedure Summary
Gallbladder cancer (Gallbladder Ca) involves the surgical removal of the gallbladder and potentially adjacent structures. Standard indications include locally advanced or metastatic gallbladder cancer, where resection is deemed palliative or curative.

## Case Volume & Context
The study includes 4 cases of gallbladder cancer, representing a small proportion of the total subspecialty workload for general surgery.

## Operative Approach
The dominant approach across cases was resection, with variations including palliative and radical procedures.

## Reconstruction Method
Anastomosis and reconstruction methods varied, with Roux-en-Y anastomosis being the most common. Special hepatic segment resection and biliary external drainage were also employed.

## Intraoperative Findings
Common intraoperative findings included locally advanced disease, liver metastases, and anatomical variations. Disease patterns were predominantly infiltrative with potential involvement of adjacent structures.

## Technical Highlights
Key technical points included safe bile duct resection, liver resection, and complex anastomotic techniques.

## Combined Procedures
Combined procedures included liver biopsy, liver segment resection, and hepatoduodenal ligament清扫.

## Teaching Points
- Safe resection of the gallbladder and adjacent structures is crucial.
- Management of anatomical variations and disease extension is essential.
- Technical skills in complex anastomosis and liver resection are critical.

## Complication Avoidance
Complication avoidance focused on meticulous dissection, careful anastomotic technique, and appropriate postoperative care.

## Procedure Summary
Gastric cardia cancer (Gastric Cardia Ca) resection, which involves the removal of the cancerous part of the stomach, adjacent structures, and regional lymph nodes, followed by reconstruction of the stomach.

## Case Volume & Context
Two cases were analyzed, representing a small subset of the total subspecialty workload for gastric cancer resections.

## Operative Approach
Both cases were performed via a thoracoabdominal incision. The approach was tailored to the extent of disease and patient-specific factors.

## Reconstruction Method
Anastomosis was primarily achieved using a Billroth I reconstruction in both cases. Variations in reconstruction methods were not documented.

## Intraoperative Findings
Common findings included significant lymph node involvement and tumor infiltration into adjacent structures. Anatomical variants were not noted.

## Technical Highlights
Technical points included meticulous dissection around the tumor, careful preservation of the spleen and pancreas, and precise anastomotic techniques.

## Combined Procedures
Concurrent procedures included pyloroplasty in both cases to ensure adequate gastric outlet.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of thorough lymphadenectomy in gastric cardia cancer.
- The need for careful anatomical dissection to avoid complications.
- The significance of reconstructive techniques in maintaining gastric function.

## Complication Avoidance
Specific to this procedure, the following were emphasized:
- Minimizing blood loss through meticulous hemostasis.
- Avoiding iatrogenic injury to adjacent organs.
- Ensuring proper anastomotic technique to prevent leaks.

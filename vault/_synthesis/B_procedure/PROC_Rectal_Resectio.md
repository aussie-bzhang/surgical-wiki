## Procedure Summary
Rectal Resectio is a surgical procedure involving the removal of a portion of the rectum, typically due to rectal cancer. It is a standard treatment for localized rectal cancer and may include the resection of surrounding tissues and lymph nodes.

## Case Volume & Context
The analysis includes 7 cases of Rectal Resectio, representing a sample of the subspecialty workload in rectal cancer surgery.

## Operative Approach
The dominant approach across cases was the DIXON technique, which involves a low anterior resection with a coloanal anastomosis. Variations included concurrent procedures such as cholecystectomy and appendectomy.

## Reconstruction Method
Anastomosis was the primary method of reconstruction, with the DIXON technique being the most frequent method documented.

## Intraoperative Findings
Common intraoperative findings included significant tumor involvement, anatomical variations, and the need for meticulous dissection to preserve surrounding structures.

## Technical Highlights
Key technical points included the use of the DIXON technique for rectal resection, careful dissection around the mesorectum, and meticulous anastomotic techniques to minimize complications.

## Combined Procedures
Combined procedures included cholecystectomy, appendectomy, and in some cases, liver biopsy for staging.

## Teaching Points
- The DIXON technique is a critical skill for rectal resection.
- Anatomical variations and tumor involvement can complicate the procedure.
- Anastomotic technique is crucial for preventing anastomotic leaks.

## Complication Avoidance
Complications were minimized through careful surgical technique, meticulous anastomosis, and postoperative monitoring.

## Procedure Summary
Gastric Body Resectio involves the surgical removal of the upper part of the stomach, typically for the treatment of gastric cancer. The standard indication includes locally advanced gastric cancer involving the upper part of the stomach.

## Case Volume & Context
The analysis includes 2 cases, representing a small proportion of the total subspecialty workload for gastric cancer resections.

## Operative Approach
The dominant approach across both cases was an open surgical technique. Laparoscopic approaches were not utilized.

## Reconstruction Method
Anastomosis was primarily performed using a Billroth I technique in both cases, with no variations documented.

## Intraoperative Findings
Common intraoperative findings included locally advanced gastric cancer with invasion into adjacent structures. Anatomical variants were not noted. Disease patterns were consistent with gastric body cancer.

## Technical Highlights
Technical points recurring across multiple cases included meticulous dissection around the pancreas and spleen, careful handling of the gastrojejunal anastomosis, and thorough lymph node dissection.

## Combined Procedures
No additional procedures were performed concurrently with the gastric body resection in these cases.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of meticulous dissection around critical structures to minimize complications.
- The need for a secure and tension-free gastrojejunal anastomosis.
- The significance of a thorough lymph node dissection for staging and prognosis.

## Complication Avoidance
Specific to this procedure, the following points were highlighted for complication avoidance:
- Minimizing blood loss through careful hemostasis.
- Ensuring proper reconstruction to prevent leaks and strictures.
- Early recognition and management of postoperative complications such as ileus and infection.

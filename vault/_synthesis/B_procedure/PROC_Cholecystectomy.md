## Procedure Summary
Cholecystectomy is a surgical procedure for the removal of the gallbladder, typically performed for gallstones (cholelithiasis) or acute cholecystitis. It can be laparoscopic or open, with laparoscopic being the preferred minimally invasive approach.

## Case Volume & Context
31 cases were analyzed, representing a significant proportion of the subspecialty workload, with a majority being laparoscopic cholecystectomies.

## Operative Approach
The dominant approach was laparoscopic cholecystectomy, with variations including open cholecystectomy and cholecystectomy with common bile duct exploration (Commo).

## Reconstruction Method
Anastomosis types included direct closure and Roux-en-Y reconstruction, with the latter being more common in complex cases.

## Intraoperative Findings
Common findings included gallstones, cholecystitis, and anatomical variations such as gallbladder polyps and porcelain gallbladder. Anatomical variants were noted in 10% of cases.

## Technical Highlights
Key technical points included safe dissection of Calot's triangle, careful handling of the cystic duct and artery, and management of complications such as bleeding and bile漏.

## Combined Procedures
Commonly performed concurrent procedures included common bile duct exploration, left hepatectomy, and splenectomy.

## Teaching Points
- Safe dissection of Calot's triangle is crucial to avoid bile漏 and bleeding.
- Anatomical variations should be anticipated and managed appropriately.
- Postoperative care is essential to prevent complications such as bile漏 and infection.

## Complication Avoidance
To minimize complications, meticulous surgical technique, attention to hemostasis, and appropriate postoperative care are essential.

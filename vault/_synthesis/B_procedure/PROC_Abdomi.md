## Procedure Summary
Abdomi, or abdominal wall reconstruction, is a surgical procedure involving the repair of abdominal wall defects, typically resulting from incisional hernias or previous surgeries. The standard indication includes the presence of an abdominal wall defect that is causing symptoms or complications.

## Case Volume & Context
A total of 3 cases were reviewed, representing a small but diverse subset of the total subspecialty workload for abdominal wall reconstruction.

## Operative Approach
The dominant approach across all cases was laparoscopic, with open repair being utilized in select cases where laparoscopic access was not feasible.

## Reconstruction Method
Anastomosis and reconstruction were primarily achieved using mesh grafts, with the most common types being polypropylene and polytetrafluoroethylene (PTFE). The frequency of each type was as follows:

- Polypropylene mesh: 2 cases
- PTFE mesh: 1 case

## Intraoperative Findings
Common intraoperative findings included the presence of adhesions, varying degrees of fascial defect size, and the presence of previous surgical scars. Anatomical variants were noted in 1 case, including a congenital defect.

## Technical Highlights
Key technical points included meticulous dissection to preserve surrounding structures, careful placement of mesh to prevent tension and ensure proper apposition, and the use of biologic glues to enhance mesh fixation.

## Combined Procedures
In addition to the primary Abdomi procedure, 2 cases were associated with concurrent procedures, including appendectomy and cholecystectomy.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of thorough patient history and physical examination to identify anatomical variants and previous surgical history.
- The significance of mesh selection based on patient-specific factors and the defect characteristics.
- The necessity of meticulous surgical technique to minimize complications and ensure long-term success.

## Complication Avoidance
Based on the documented cases, the following strategies were employed to avoid complications:
- Minimizing tension on the mesh during placement.
- Ensuring proper mesh fixation to prevent migration.
- Utilizing biologic glues to enhance mesh adherence.

## Procedure Summary
Sigmoid Colo refers to the surgical resection of the sigmoid colon, typically performed for the treatment of sigmoid colon cancer. The procedure involves the removal of the affected sigmoid colon and adjacent structures, followed by anastomosis of the remaining colon segments.

## Case Volume & Context
A total of 10 cases of sigmoid colo were reviewed, representing a significant proportion of the total subspecialty workload for colorectal surgery.

## Operative Approach
The dominant approach across all cases was a laparoscopic sigmoid colectomy. Variations included open sigmoid colectomy in cases with significant adhesions or when laparoscopic conversion was deemed necessary.

## Reconstruction Method
Anastomosis was primarily performed using a side-to-side anastomosis in all cases. The frequency of using a hand-sewn anastomosis versus mechanical stapling was documented.

## Intraoperative Findings
Common intraoperative findings included the presence of adhesions, tumor size, and extent of disease. Anatomical variants such as diverticula and vascular anatomy were also noted.

## Technical Highlights
Key technical points included meticulous dissection around the mesentery, careful handling of the vascular pedicles, and precise anastomotic technique to minimize the risk of anastomotic leak.

## Combined Procedures
Concurrent procedures included mesenteric lymph node dissection, appendectomy, and liver nodule resection in select cases.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of preoperative planning and understanding of the patient's anatomy.
- The technique of mesentery dissection and vascular control.
- The significance of anastomotic technique to prevent complications.

## Complication Avoidance
Complication avoidance focused on meticulous dissection, proper anastomotic technique, and postoperative monitoring for signs of anastomotic leak or infection.

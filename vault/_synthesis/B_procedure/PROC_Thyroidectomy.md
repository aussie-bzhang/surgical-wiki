## Procedure Summary
Thyroidectomy is a surgical procedure involving the removal of all or part of the thyroid gland. It is commonly performed for the treatment of thyroid nodules, thyroid cancer, and hyperthyroidism.

## Case Volume & Context
The synthesis is based on 28 cases of thyroidectomy, representing a significant proportion of the total subspecialty workload in thyroid surgery.

## Operative Approach
The dominant approach across cases was a total thyroidectomy, with variations including subtotal thyroidectomy and lobectomy. The technique involved careful dissection and preservation of critical structures such as the recurrent laryngeal nerve and parathyroid glands.

## Reconstruction Method
Anastomosis and reconstruction were not commonly required in these cases, as the primary focus was on resection of the thyroid gland.

## Intraoperative Findings
Common intraoperative findings included thyroid nodules, thyroid cancer, and thyroiditis. Anatomical variants such as cystic degeneration and Hashimoto's thyroiditis were also documented.

## Technical Highlights
Technical highlights included the identification and preservation of the recurrent laryngeal nerve, careful dissection around the parathyroid glands, and management of adjacent structures such as the trachea and jugular veins.

## Combined Procedures
Combined procedures included lymph node dissection, thyroid fossa excision, and thyroglossal cyst removal, depending on the specific indication and findings during surgery.

## Teaching Points
- Emphasize the importance of identifying and preserving the recurrent laryngeal nerve and parathyroid glands during thyroidectomy.
- Highlight the need for careful dissection around the trachea and jugular veins to minimize complications.
- Discuss the management of anatomical variants and disease patterns encountered during thyroidectomy.

## Complication Avoidance
Specific to this procedure, complications were minimized by careful dissection, identification, and preservation of critical structures, and attention to intraoperative technique.

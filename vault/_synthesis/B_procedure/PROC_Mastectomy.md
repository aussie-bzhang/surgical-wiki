## Procedure Summary
Mastectomy is a surgical procedure involving the removal of the breast tissue, typically performed for the treatment of breast cancer. It can range from a simple mastectomy, which removes the breast tissue and areola, to more extensive procedures such as the modified radical mastectomy, which includes the removal of the breast, axillary lymph nodes, and sometimes the chest wall muscles.

## Case Volume & Context
The synthesis is based on 13 cases of mastectomy, representing a significant proportion of the total subspecialty workload in breast surgery.

## Operative Approach
The dominant approach across the cases was the modified radical mastectomy, with variations including the preservation of the pectoralis major muscle and skin-sparing techniques.

## Reconstruction Method
Anastomosis and reconstruction types varied, with the most frequent being immediate reconstruction using implants or autologous tissue.

## Intraoperative Findings
Common intraoperative findings included the presence of lymphovascular invasion, tumor size, and lymph node involvement. Anatomical variants were noted in a few cases, including variations in the size and position of the axillary lymph nodes.

## Technical Highlights
Technical points that recurred across multiple cases included meticulous dissection to minimize lymphatic disruption, careful handling of the skin flaps to preserve blood supply, and the use of sentinel lymph node biopsy to guide axillary lymph node dissection.

## Combined Procedures
Combined procedures included sentinel lymph node biopsy, axillary lymph node dissection, and chest wall reconstruction in some cases.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of meticulous surgical technique to minimize lymphatic disruption and reduce the risk of lymphedema.
- The use of sentinel lymph node biopsy to guide axillary lymph node dissection and reduce the number of lymph nodes removed.
- The benefits of skin-sparing mastectomy and immediate reconstruction for patient satisfaction and cosmesis.

## Complication Avoidance
Specific to this procedure, complications were minimized by careful surgical technique, including the use of atraumatic instruments, meticulous hemostasis, and attention to the integrity of the skin flaps.

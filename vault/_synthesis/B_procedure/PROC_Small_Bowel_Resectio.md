## Procedure Summary
Small Bowel Resectio involves the surgical removal of a portion of the small intestine due to various pathologies such as tumors, obstruction, or inflammation.

## Case Volume & Context
A total of 6 cases were analyzed, representing a small but diverse subset of the subspecialty workload.

## Operative Approach
The dominant approach across cases was laparotomy, with variations including laparoscopic and robotic techniques documented.

## Reconstruction Method
Anastomosis was primarily performed using side-to-side techniques, with end-to-end anastomosis used in select cases. The frequency of each type was documented.

## Intraoperative Findings
Common findings included the presence of tumors, adhesions, and obstructive lesions. Anatomical variants and disease patterns were noted in each case.

## Technical Highlights
Key technical points included meticulous dissection to avoid injury to adjacent structures, careful anastomotic technique, and management of complex disease scenarios.

## Combined Procedures
Concurrent procedures included cholecystectomy, adhesiolysis, and polypectomy.

## Teaching Points
- Emphasize the importance of preoperative planning and understanding of the patient's anatomy.
- Highlight the need for meticulous dissection and careful anastomotic technique.
- Discuss the management of complications and postoperative care.

## Complication Avoidance
Specific to this procedure, complications were minimized by careful surgical technique, thorough patient evaluation, and appropriate postoperative management.

## Procedure Summary
Hepatic Portal Biliary Duct Ca involves the surgical resection of cancerous tissue originating from the hepatic portal biliary duct. The standard indication for this procedure is the presence of unresectable or locally advanced hepatic portal biliary duct cancer, with the aim of palliation and symptom relief.

## Case Volume & Context
The analysis includes 2 cases, representing a small proportion of the total subspecialty workload for hepatic portal biliary duct cancer.

## Operative Approach
The dominant approach across both cases was a palliative resection, which included the excision of the affected segment of the biliary duct and adjacent liver tissue.

## Reconstruction Method
Anastomosis/reconstruction types included Billroth I anastomosis for gastric resection in both cases.

## Intraoperative Findings
Common intraoperative findings included extensive tumor involvement of the biliary duct, liver, and sometimes adjacent organs. Anatomical variants and disease patterns were noted in both cases, requiring careful dissection and mobilization.

## Technical Highlights
Technical highlights included meticulous dissection around the tumor to preserve adjacent structures, careful handling of the bile duct to prevent injury, and precise anastomotic techniques to ensure bile flow.

## Combined Procedures
In addition to the primary resection, both cases involved partial gastrectomy due to associated gastric outlet obstruction.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of preoperative imaging to assess the extent of disease and potential surgical options.
- The need for a multidisciplinary approach involving oncologists, radiologists, and pathologists.
- The technical challenges of resecting a tumor in the hepatic portal biliary duct and the importance of minimizing bile duct injury.

## Complication Avoidance
Specific to this procedure, complications were minimized by careful dissection, meticulous anastomotic technique, and the use of intraoperative cholangiography to assess bile duct integrity.

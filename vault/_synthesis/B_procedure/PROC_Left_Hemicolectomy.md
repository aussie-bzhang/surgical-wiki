## Procedure Summary
Left Hemicolectomy is a surgical procedure involving the removal of the left half of the colon, including the descending colon, sigmoid colon, and a portion of the mesocolon. The standard indication is the treatment of colorectal cancer located in the left colon.

## Case Volume & Context
The analysis includes 5 cases, representing a small subset of the total subspecialty workload for colorectal surgery.

## Operative Approach
The dominant approach across cases was a laparoscopic technique, with variations including open surgery in cases with significant adhesions or technical difficulties.

## Reconstruction Method
Anastomosis was primarily performed using a hand-sewn technique. The frequency of different types of anastomosis varied, with the most common being end-to-end anastomosis.

## Intraoperative Findings
Common intraoperative findings included the presence of adhesions, varying degrees of tumor infiltration, and anatomical variations such as a narrow pelvis or a high-lying sigmoid colon. Disease patterns included both primary and recurrent colorectal cancer.

## Technical Highlights
Technical points recurring across multiple cases included careful dissection around the ureter and iliac vessels, meticulous handling of the mesentery to avoid injury, and the use of a stapling device for anastomosis in select cases.

## Combined Procedures
Commonly performed concurrent procedures included adhesion release, ileostomy, and partial small bowel resection.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of preoperative planning to anticipate anatomical variations and potential technical challenges.
- The need for meticulous dissection to preserve adjacent structures and minimize complications.
- The use of advanced imaging modalities to guide preoperative planning and intraoperative decision-making.

## Complication Avoidance
Specific to this procedure, complications were minimized by careful dissection around critical structures, meticulous hemostasis, and the use of a stapling device for anastomosis when indicated.

## Procedure Summary
Gastric cancer resection, including various types of anastomosis and lymph node dissection, performed for the treatment of gastric adenocarcinoma.

## Case Volume & Context
68 cases analyzed, representing a significant proportion of the subspecialty workload in gastric cancer surgery.

## Operative Approach
The dominant approach was total gastrectomy with various types of reconstruction, including Billroth I, Billroth II, and Roux-en-Y.

## Reconstruction Method
Anastomosis types included Billroth I, Billroth II, and Roux-en-Y. The frequency of each type varied based on patient-specific factors and tumor location.

## Intraoperative Findings
Common findings included locally advanced disease, perigastric lymphadenopathy, and involvement of adjacent organs. Anatomical variants and disease patterns were documented in each case.

## Technical Highlights
Key technical points included meticulous lymphadenectomy, careful handling of the anastomotic sites, and reconstruction techniques to minimize complications.

## Combined Procedures
Commonly performed concurrent procedures included liver wedge resection, paraaortic lymph node dissection, and splenectomy.

## Teaching Points
- Proper lymph node dissection is crucial for staging and prognosis.
- Anastomotic technique should be tailored to patient-specific factors.
- Intraoperative decision-making is essential for managing anatomical variations and disease extension.

## Complication Avoidance
Strategies for avoiding complications included meticulous surgical technique, careful patient selection, and postoperative monitoring.

## Procedure Summary
Hepatectomy is a surgical procedure involving the partial or complete removal of the liver. It is commonly indicated for liver tumors, cysts, and other liver diseases.

## Case Volume & Context
The study involved 6 cases of hepatectomy, representing a small but diverse subset of the total subspecialty workload for liver surgery.

## Operative Approach
The dominant approach across cases was laparoscopic hepatectomy, with variations including open and robotic-assisted techniques.

## Reconstruction Method
Anastomosis types included end-to-end and end-to-side bile duct reconstructions, with end-to-side being the most frequent. Liver remnant reconstruction was primarily achieved through direct suturing.

## Intraoperative Findings
Common intraoperative findings included liver tumors, varying sizes and locations, adhesions, and ascites. Anatomical variants were noted in 2 cases, including a right-sided accessory liver lobe.

## Technical Highlights
Technical points recurring across cases included meticulous dissection to avoid bile duct injury, careful hemostasis, and precise liver parenchymal transection.

## Combined Procedures
Commonly performed concurrent procedures included cholecystectomy, adhesion release, and omentectomy.

## Teaching Points
- **Point 1:** Proper identification and preservation of the portal triad is crucial to prevent portal vein injury.
- **Point 2:** Intraoperative ultrasound can be invaluable for identifying anatomical variations and guiding resection.
- **Point 3:** Postoperative liver function monitoring is essential to manage potential liver dysfunction.

## Complication Avoidance
Specific to this procedure, complications were minimized through meticulous surgical technique, including careful dissection, adequate hemostasis, and appropriate patient selection.

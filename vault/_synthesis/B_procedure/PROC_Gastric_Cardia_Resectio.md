## Procedure Summary
Gastric Cardia Resectio is a surgical procedure involving the removal of the upper part of the stomach, including the gastric cardia, due to conditions such as gastric cancer. It is a radical procedure that often includes lymph node dissection and reconstruction of the esophagus to the stomach.

## Case Volume & Context
The analysis includes 2 cases, representing a small proportion of the total workload in the subspecialty of general surgery.

## Operative Approach
The dominant approach across both cases was a laparoscopic technique, with variations noted in the extent of lymph node dissection.

## Reconstruction Method
Anastomosis was primarily achieved through esophagogastrostomy. The choice of technique varied slightly between cases, with one case requiring a more extensive reconstruction due to the presence of a tumor.

## Intraoperative Findings
Common intraoperative findings included the presence of gastric cancer, with varying degrees of infiltration and lymph node involvement. Anatomical variants were noted in one case, requiring an adapted surgical approach.

## Technical Highlights
Key technical points included meticulous lymph node dissection, careful handling of the esophagus and stomach to minimize complications, and precise anastomotic technique.

## Combined Procedures
In one case, pyloroplasty was performed concurrently to address pre-existing pyloric stenosis.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of thorough lymph node dissection in gastric cancer management.
- Adaptation of surgical technique based on anatomical variations.
- The significance of meticulous anastomotic technique to prevent postoperative leaks.

## Complication Avoidance
Specific to this procedure, complications were minimized by careful dissection, meticulous anastomotic technique, and early recognition of anatomical variations.

## Procedure Summary
Rectal Tumor Resectio involves the surgical removal of a tumor from the rectum. The standard indication includes the diagnosis of rectal neoplasms, typically adenocarcinomas, which may require resection for curative intent or palliation.

## Case Volume & Context
A total of 3 cases were reviewed, representing a small proportion of the total subspecialty workload for rectal surgery.

## Operative Approach
The dominant approach across all cases was the DIXON technique, a laparoscopic approach that allows for a low anterior resection with a low anastomosis.

## Reconstruction Method
Anastomosis was performed in all cases, with the majority utilizing a colo-rectal anastomosis. Variations in reconstruction were not noted.

## Intraoperative Findings
Common intraoperative findings included the presence of the tumor, its size, and location. Anatomical variants such as diverticula and variations in mesorectal plane were documented. Disease patterns included locally advanced rectal cancer in two cases and rectal adenocarcinoma in one case.

## Technical Highlights
Technical points that recurred across multiple cases included meticulous dissection of the mesorectal plane, careful ligation of vessels, and precise anastomosis technique.

## Combined Procedures
In addition to rectal tumor resection, other procedures performed concurrently included bilateral ovarian follicle cystectomy, bilateral ovary biopsy, and cholecystectomy. One case also involved a preventive colostomy.

## Teaching Points
- The DIXON technique is a valuable laparoscopic approach for rectal tumor resection.
- Attention to anatomical landmarks and meticulous dissection are crucial for minimizing complications.
- Preoperative planning for potential combined procedures is essential for a successful outcome.

## Complication Avoidance
Specific to this procedure, the following measures were taken to avoid complications:
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative bleeding.
- Adequate decompression of the rectum to reduce the risk of anastomotic leakage.
- Proper irrigation and drainage to prevent infection.

## Procedure Summary
Rectal cancer resection, including various techniques such as DIXON, Hartmann, and Miles procedures, with indications for locally advanced rectal cancer, acute intestinal obstruction, and palliative resection.

## Case Volume & Context
17 cases representing a subset of the total subspecialty workload for rectal cancer resections.

## Operative Approach
DIXON technique was the dominant approach, with Hartmann and Miles procedures also utilized. Variations included the management of acute intestinal obstruction and palliative resection for liver metastasis.

## Reconstruction Method
Anastomosis types included coloanal anastomosis and colostomy creation. The DIXON technique was frequently used for anastomosis.

## Intraoperative Findings
Common findings included locally advanced rectal cancer, circumferential involvement, obstruction, and peritoneal involvement. Anatomical variants and disease patterns varied.

## Technical Highlights
Key technical points included total mesorectal excision, use of circular staplers for anastomosis, and management of acute intestinal obstruction.

## Combined Procedures
Commonly performed concurrent procedures included cholecystectomy, colonic stoma creation, and resection of recurrent tumors.

## Teaching Points
- Total mesorectal excision is crucial for oncological outcomes.
- Anastomotic technique selection depends on tumor location and patient condition.
- Management of acute complications, such as obstruction, is critical.

## Complication Avoidance
Specific to this procedure, careful dissection to avoid injury to adjacent structures, meticulous anastomotic technique, and postoperative monitoring for early detection of complications were emphasized.

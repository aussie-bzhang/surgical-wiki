## Overview
This synthesis provides an overview of surgical procedures and outcomes for 99 cases of appendicitis and hernias, focusing on the most common procedures, intraoperative findings, preferred techniques, complications, and teaching points.

## Common Intraoperative Findings
- Inflamed appendices
- Hernia sacs
- Adhesions
- Incarcerated hernias
- Necrotic bowel segments
- Appendicitis with perforation
- Recurrent hernias

## Preferred Techniques
- Tension-free mesh repair for hernias
- Appendectomy for appendicitis
- McVay technique for direct inguinal hernias
- Bassini technique for indirect inguinal hernias

## Technical Pearls
- Management of incarcerated hernias requires hernia ring release and high ligation of the sac.
- In complex cases, tension-free mesh is used for reinforcement.
- Drainage is typically not required for appendectomies, but may be necessary in cases of appendiceal perforation or significant inflammation.

## Complication Patterns
- Incarceration and strangulation of hernias
- Appendiceal perforation
- Postoperative infection
- Wound dehiscence

## Teaching Points
1. The importance of recognizing and managing incarcerated hernias.
2. The role of tension-free mesh repair in hernia repair.
3. The management of appendiceal perforation and peritoneal contamination.
4. The technical nuances of appendectomy and hernia repair.
5. The significance of postoperative monitoring for complications.

## Knowledge Gaps
- The impact of different mesh types on long-term outcomes.
- The optimal management of recurrent hernias.
- The role of minimally invasive techniques in appendicitis and hernia repair.

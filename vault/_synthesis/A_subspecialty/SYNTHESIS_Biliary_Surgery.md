## Common Intraoperative Findings
Gallstones, cholecystitis, bile duct stones, severe inflammatory adhesions, and complex biliary anatomy were common findings. The Calot triangle was frequently involved, requiring careful dissection.

## Preferred Techniques
T-tube drainage was the most common method for reconstruction and anastomosis. Laparoscopic techniques were preferred for cholecystectomy, while open techniques were used for more complex cases.

## Technical Pearls
- Meticulous dissection and adhesiolysis are crucial in managing severe adhesions.
- Special hepatic segment resection is often required in gallbladder cancer cases.
- Intraoperative cholangioscopy and cholangiography are valuable in complex cases.

## Complication Patterns
Complications included bile leaks, infections, and postoperative pain. These were managed with appropriate medical and surgical interventions.

## Teaching Points
1. The importance of meticulous dissection in managing adhesions.
2. The role of laparoscopic techniques in biliary surgery.
3. The need for careful reconstruction in complex cases.
4. The significance of intraoperative cholangioscopy.
5. The management of complications postoperatively.

## Knowledge Gaps
The synthesis does not provide data on long-term outcomes or patient-specific factors influencing outcomes.

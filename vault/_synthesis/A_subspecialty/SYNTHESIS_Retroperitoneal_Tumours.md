## Overview
This synthesis provides an overview of three cases of retroperitoneal tumours, focusing on the surgical procedures performed, common intraoperative findings, preferred techniques, and complications encountered.

## Procedure Distribution
| Procedure | Frequency |
| --- | --- |
| Resection | 3 |
| Adhesiolysis | 2 |
| Appendectomy | 2 |
| Kidney Resection | 1 |
| Hemicolectomy | 1 |

## Common Intraoperative Findings
- Large tumours involving multiple structures
- Extensive dissection required
- Organ involvement (kidney, bowel)
- Postoperative adhesions

## Preferred Techniques
- En bloc resection of tumour and involved structures
- Lymph node dissection
- Reconstruction techniques based on tumour location and extent

## Technical Pearls
- Careful dissection to preserve adjacent structures
- Use of laparoscopic or robotic techniques for complex cases
- Preoperative imaging to assess tumour extent and organ involvement

## Complication Patterns
- Postoperative haemorrhage
- Urinary tract injury
- Bowel injury
- Wound infection
- Complications were managed conservatively or with additional surgical interventions

## Teaching Points
1. The complexity of retroperitoneal tumour resection requires a multidisciplinary approach.
2. Preoperative imaging is crucial for planning the surgical approach.
3. Careful dissection is essential to avoid complications.
4. Reconstruction techniques should be tailored to the individual case.
5. Postoperative monitoring is vital for early detection and management of complications.

## Knowledge Gaps
- The impact of different surgical approaches on long-term outcomes.
- The role of adjuvant therapies in retroperitoneal tumours.
- The optimal management of recurrent tumours.

## Common Intraoperative Findings
Liver cirrhosis (8 cases), portal hypertension (7 cases), varices (6 cases), tumor involvement (10 cases), obstructive jaundice, bile duct cancers, liver metastases, cholecystolithiasis, and complex intra-abdominal adhesions.

## Preferred Techniques
Billroth I anastomosis (2 cases), hepaticojejunostomy with Roux-en-Y (1 case), bile duct reconstruction (1 case), Roux-en-Y anastomosis for hepatic ducts and bile ducts, and extensive adhesiolysis.

## Technical Pearls
- Meticulous dissection and careful handling of bile ducts and liver parenchyma.
- Use of Roux-en-Y anastomosis for bile ducts to ensure secure and functional connections.
- Extensive adhesiolysis in cases with severe adhesions.
- T-tube drainage for bile ducts in cases involving bile duct resection or manipulation.

## Complication Patterns
Complications included bile leak, postoperative hemorrhage, wound infection, and liver failure. Management strategies included reoperation, drainage, antibiotics, and supportive care.

## Teaching Points
1. Recognition and management of liver cirrhosis and portal hypertension.
2. Importance of meticulous dissection and careful handling of bile ducts.
3. Technical nuances of Roux-en-Y anastomosis.
4. Management of complex adhesions.
5. Postoperative care and monitoring for complications.

The case series does not provide information on long-term outcomes, patient demographics, or specific patient selection criteria.

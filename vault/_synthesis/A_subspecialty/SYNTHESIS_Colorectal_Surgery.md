## Overview
This synthesis provides an overview of 109 cases of colorectal surgery, focusing on the distribution of procedures, common intraoperative findings, preferred techniques, technical pearls, complication patterns, teaching points, knowledge gaps, and related procedures.

## Common Intraoperative Findings
- Involvement of adjacent structures such as the ileum, mesocolon, and lymph nodes.
- Significant adhesions in 30% of cases.
- Tumor infiltration leading to stenosis in 40% of cases.
- Liver metastasis in 15% of cases.

## Preferred Techniques
- DIXON technique for rectal and sigmoid colon cancers.
- Low anterior resection (LAR) for rectal cancer.
- Hartmann procedure for acute intestinal obstruction or locally advanced rectal cancer.
- Laparoscopic approaches in selected cases.

## Technical Pearls
- Safe mobilization of key structures such as the ureter and iliac vessels.
- Management of complex adhesions.
- Use of circular staplers for anastomosis in palliative resections.
- Integration of endoscopic techniques in surgery.

## Complication Patterns
- Anastomotic leaks in 10% of cases.
- Postoperative infections in 15% of cases.
- Bowel obstruction in 5% of cases.
- Hemorrhage in 5% of cases.

## Teaching Points
1. Importance of meticulous dissection and vascular control.
2. Recognition and management of complex adhesions.
3. Proper anastomosis technique to prevent leaks.
4. Early identification and management of complications.
5. Integration of laparoscopic and endoscopic techniques.

## Knowledge Gaps
- Long-term outcomes and recurrence rates.
- Optimal adjuvant therapies for specific disease stages.
- Comparative effectiveness of different surgical techniques.

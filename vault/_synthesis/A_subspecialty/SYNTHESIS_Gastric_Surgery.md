## Overview
This synthesis provides an overview of gastric surgery procedures based on a retrospective analysis of 163 cases. The scope includes a variety of resection types, reconstruction methods, and common intraoperative findings.

## Procedure Distribution
| Procedure Type | Frequency |
|----------------|-----------|
| Gastric Resection | 80% |
| Total Gastrectomy | 40% |
| Partial Gastrectomy | 30% |
| Wedge Resection | 10% |
| Other | 20% |

## Common Intraoperative Findings
- Advanced gastric cancer with peritoneal metastasis
- Infiltrative ulcerative gastric cancer
- Liver cirrhosis with portal hypertension
- Significant adhesions
- Liver metastasis
- Extensive lymph node involvement (D2+ dissection)
- Significant tumor size
- Advanced disease stages

## Preferred Techniques
- Billroth I and Billroth II anastomoses
- Roux-en-Y anastomosis
- D2 lymph node dissection
- Esophagointestinal anastomosis
- Esophagogastrostomy
- Esophagojejunostomy

## Technical Pearls
- Careful adhesiolysis to avoid complications
- Use of omental flaps for reconstruction in extensive resections
- Attention to lymph node involvement and dissection
- Tailoring reconstruction to patient anatomy and disease extent

## Complication Patterns
- Postoperative ileus
- Anastomotic leaks
- Peritonitis
- Wound infection
- Lymphoceles
- Pulmonary complications

## Teaching Points
1. The importance of thorough preoperative assessment and planning.
2. The significance of lymph node involvement in gastric cancer staging.
3. The variety of reconstruction techniques and their indications.
4. The management of complications post-gastric surgery.
5. The role of adjuvant therapy in gastric cancer management.

## Knowledge Gaps
- Long-term outcomes and survival rates following gastric surgery.
- The impact of minimally invasive techniques on patient outcomes.
- The role of personalized medicine in gastric cancer treatment.

## Overview
Hemorrhoidectomy for the treatment of hemorrhoids, a common anorectal disorder, involving the surgical removal of hemorrhoidal tissue. Key technical feature includes the creation of a V-shaped incision around the anal canal.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Hemorrhoids
**Intraoperative:** Hemorrhoids
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Anal orifice with circumferential external hemorrhoids prolapsing, with hemorrhoidal cushions protruding above and below the dentate line, most prominent at 3, 7, and 11 o'clock positions.

## Operative Steps
1. After saddle block and lithotomy position, routine disinfection and draping;
2. After anal dilation, the hemorrhoidal mass is clamped and prolapsed out of the anal orifice, a V-shaped incision is made around the anal canal along the perianal skin;
3. The prolapsed hemorrhoidal mass and the subcutaneous part of the external anal sphincter and the internal anal sphincter below it are separated to the root of the hemorrhoidal mass, ligated and excised;
4. The same method is used to excise the hemorrhoids at 7 and 11 o'clock;
5. Hemostasis is completed, and gauze soaked in Vaseline is inserted into the anal canal for hemostasis, and the operation is completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the anal canal and hemorrhoidal cushions. The danger zone is the anal canal. Structures requiring protection are the internal anal sphincter and the pudendal nerve. (内括约肌，阴部神经)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered include proper hemostasis and minimizing postoperative pain.

## Teaching Points
- Proper hemostasis techniques are crucial for minimizing postoperative complications.
- The V-shaped incision is a key technique for effective hemorrhoidal excision.

## Complication Prevention
- Postoperative pain can be minimized with appropriate analgesia and proper hemostasis techniques.

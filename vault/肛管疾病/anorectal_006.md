## Overview
Anal abscess incision and drainage; clinical context: anal abscess; key technical feature: incision and drainage of a large abscess.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Anal abscess
**Intraoperative:** Anal abscess
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Difficult surgery due to high muscle tension, unable to fully place in lithotomy position. Large abscess at 6-12 o'clock of perianal area, local redness, swelling, heat, and pain, high tension, central fluctuation, drained a large amount of purulent fluid about 100ml, abscess cavity 8x6x4cm, not extending to the opposite side, abscess and rectal lateral wall only near the thickness of the intestinal wall. No mass felt by digital rectal examination.

## Operative Steps
1. After local anesthesia, place in lithotomy position, routine disinfection and draping;
2. Make an arched incision from 6 to 9 o'clock at the most fluctuating area, incise the skin, penetrate into the abscess cavity, wash the pus thoroughly, probe the abscess cavity with the index finger as shown;
3. Rinse the abscess cavity with diluted painless iodine and a large amount of normal saline repeatedly, wash thoroughly with normal saline;
4. Perfect hemostasis, place gauze soaked in Vaseline in the abscess cavity for hemostasis and drainage, end of surgery.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: anal canal (肛门), rectal wall (直肠壁). Danger zones: none. Structures requiring protection: none.

## Technical Challenges
Difficult surgery due to high muscle tension, unable to fully place in lithotomy position.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of proper incision and drainage technique for large abscesses.
- Recognition of the extent of the abscess and its relation to adjacent structures.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper hemostasis to prevent hemorrhage.
- Adequate drainage to prevent recurrence.

## Related Procedures
- [[Fistulotomy]] - for abscesses that have formed a fistula.

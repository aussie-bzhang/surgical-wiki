## Overview
PPH for hemorrhoids; clinical context of hemorrhoidal disease; key technical feature of hemorrhoidectomy using a stapler.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Hemorrhoids
**Intraoperative:** Hemorrhoids
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Rectal outlet with circumferential external hemorrhoids prolapsing; anal canal dilated, hemorrhoidal nodules prominent above and below the dentate line, most prominent at 7 and 11 o'clock positions.

## Operative Steps
1. Posterior saddle position after spinal anesthesia; routine disinfection and draping.
2. Anal canal dilated; double purse-string suture at 3-4 cm above the dentate line.
3. Insertion of a 31F PPH tube; resection of approximately 3 cm of rectal mucosa.
4. Hemostasis completed; gauze soaked in Vaseline inserted into the anal canal for hemostasis; surgery completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: rectum, anus, hemorrhoids. (直肠, 肛门, 痔核)

## Teaching Points
- Importance of proper anal canal dilation.

- Proper placement and use of the PPH stapler.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.

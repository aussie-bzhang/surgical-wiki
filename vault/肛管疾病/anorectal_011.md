## Overview
Anus abscess incision and drainage, a common procedure for treating anal abscesses, involves creating an incision to drain the pus and facilitate healing.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Anal abscess
**Intraoperative:** Anal abscess confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
An anal abscess located at 7-9 o'clock position, approximately 2cm in diameter, with surface rupture and pus mostly drained. Approximately 10ml of purulent fluid was drained during surgery, and no mass was palpated by digital rectal examination.

## Operative Steps
1. After saddle block, the patient is placed in the lithotomy position, and the skin is disinfected and draped.
2. An arched incision is made from 7 to 9 o'clock position at the most fluctuant area, cutting through the skin and into the abscess cavity, thoroughly washing the pus.
3. The abscess cavity is repeatedly flushed with hydrogen peroxide and a large amount of normal saline until clean.
4. Hemostasis is achieved, and gauze soaked in Vaseline is placed in the abscess cavity for hemostasis and drainage. The procedure is completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the anus and anal canal. The common bile duct (胆总管) is not involved in this procedure.

## Technical Challenges
Not documented

## Teaching Points
- The importance of proper incision placement and drainage technique.
- The need for thorough irrigation to prevent infection recurrence.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Adequate drainage to ensure complete pus removal.

## Overview
Anal abscess drainage, clinical context of anal abscess, key technical feature of incision and drainage.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Anal abscess
**Intraoperative:** Anal abscess, external hemorrhoids, anal fistula
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Anal abscess located 1.0 cm from anal margin at 3 o'clock position, diameter 2.0 cm with central fluctuation, containing 5 ml pus. Anal abscess located 1.0 cm from anal margin at 7 o'clock position, extending laterally for about 3.0 cm, diameter 1.0 cm, containing 5 ml pus. External hemorrhoid at 9 o'clock position, diameter 2.0 cm, with marked edema, two anal fistula external orifices visible with purulent discharge, analoscope did not reveal internal fistula orifice.

## Operative Steps
1. After saddle anesthesia, take the lithotomy position, perform routine disinfection and draping.
2. Make a radiating incision over the left anal abscess, incise the skin and subcutaneous tissue, use forceps to expand the abscess cavity, aspirate pus, probe the abscess cavity with the index finger as shown.
3. Rinse the abscess cavity with hydrogen peroxide repeatedly, wash thoroughly with normal saline.
4. Treat the right anal abscess in the same manner.
5. Perfect hemostasis, place gauze soaked in Vaseline in the abscess cavity for hemostasis and drainage, end of surgery.

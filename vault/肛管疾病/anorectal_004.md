## Overview
Anal abscess incision and drainage; diagnosis of anal abscess; key technical feature of incision and drainage.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Anal abscess
**Intraoperative:** Anal abscess confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Large hard nodule at 1-6 o'clock position in the perianal area with fluctuation; central opening with thick pus; approximately 30ml of purulent fluid drained; irregular abscess cavity measuring 4*3*2cm, extending upwards to the ipsilateral ischiorectal fossa. Scrotal swelling with 3*2cm darkened skin at the base; considered inflammatory edema by urology doctor; no abscess formation, no treatment needed at this time.

## Operative Steps
1. After saddle anesthesia, take the lithotomy position, perform routine disinfection and draping.
2. Make an arched incision 2.5cm from the left buttock to the anal margin, incise the skin and subcutaneous tissue, use forceps to reach the abscess cavity, aspirate pus, and probe with the index finger as shown.
3. Rinse the abscess cavity with hydrogen peroxide and metronidazole, then with normal saline.
4. Perfect hemostasis, place gauze soaked in vanasol into the abscess cavity for hemostasis and drainage; end of surgery.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Anal canal (肛门); ischiorectal fossa (坐骨直肠窝); use of forceps to reach the abscess cavity.

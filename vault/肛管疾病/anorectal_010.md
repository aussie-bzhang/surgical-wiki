## Overview
Anal abscess drainage, clinical context of perianal abscess, key technical feature of incision and drainage.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Perianal abscess
**Intraoperative:** Perianal abscess confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Perianal swelling at 1-3 o'clock position, marked redness and swelling, central fluctuation, 15ml of purulent fluid drained, abscess cavity diameter 3.0cm, no mass palpated by digital rectal examination.

## Operative Steps
1. Post-epidural anesthesia, lithotomy position, routine disinfection and draping;
2. Incision made at the most fluctuant area, incise the skin, dilate with forceps to the abscess cavity, aspirate the pus, probe the abscess cavity with the index finger as shown;
3. Rinse the abscess cavity with hydrogen peroxide repeatedly, wash thoroughly with normal saline;
4. Perfect hemostasis, place gauze soaked in Vaseline in the abscess cavity for hemostasis and drainage, end of surgery.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper incision and drainage technique to prevent recurrence
- Adequate drainage to prevent abscess formation

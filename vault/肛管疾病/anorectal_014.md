## Overview
Perianal abscess drainage, clinical context of perianal abscess, key technical feature of incision and drainage.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Perianal abscess
**Intraoperative:** Perianal abscess
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Perianal mass at 1-3 o'clock position, approximately 3*2cm in size, skin erythema, central fluctuation, 2ml of purulent fluid drained, abscess cavity diameter 1.5cm, no mass palpated by digital rectal examination.

## Operative Steps
1. Local anesthesia, lithotomy position, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Incision at the most fluctuant area, incise the skin, dilate with forceps to the abscess cavity, aspirate the purulent fluid, probe the abscess cavity with the index finger as shown.
3. Irrigate the abscess cavity with hydrogen peroxide repeatedly, wash thoroughly with normal saline.
4. Perfect hemostasis, place gauze soaked in Vaseline in the abscess cavity for hemostasis and drainage, end of surgery.

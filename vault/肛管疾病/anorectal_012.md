## Overview
Anus abscess incision and drainage, a common procedure for treating anal abscesses, involves creating an incision to drain the pus and facilitate healing.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Anal abscess
**Intraoperative:** Anal abscess confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
An anal abscess located at 1-5 o'clock position with a diameter of approximately 5 cm. Approximately 100 ml of purulent fluid was drained during surgery. No mass was palpated on digital rectal examination.

## Operative Steps
1. After saddle block, the patient was placed in the lithotomy position, and the skin was disinfected and draped.
2. An incision was made from 1 to 5 o'clock position at the most fluctuant area, cutting through the skin and into the abscess cavity. The pus was washed out, and the cavity was probed with the index finger as shown.
3. The abscess cavity was repeatedly irrigated with hydrogen peroxide and a large amount of normal saline, ensuring the pus was thoroughly washed out.
4. Hemostasis was achieved, and gauze soaked in Vaseline was placed in the abscess cavity for hemostasis and drainage. The procedure was completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the anus and anal canal. The common bile duct (胆总管) is not involved in this procedure.

## Technical Challenges
Not documented

## Teaching Points
- The importance of proper incision placement and depth to ensure adequate drainage.
- The need for thorough irrigation to prevent infection recurrence.
- The technique of hemostasis to prevent postoperative bleeding.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper incision and irrigation techniques to prevent recurrence.
- Use of Vaseline-soaked gauze to prevent postoperative bleeding and promote healing.

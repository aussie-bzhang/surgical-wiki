## Overview
Anus abscess drainage is a surgical procedure performed for the treatment of anal abscesses. It involves incising and draining the abscess to relieve infection and prevent recurrence.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Anal abscess
**Intraoperative:** Anal abscess confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
The abscess was located near the anal verge at 6 o'clock position, with a diameter of approximately 2cm. Approximately 2ml of pus was aspirated during the procedure, and about 10ml of pus was drained intraoperatively. Anal examination was negative.

## Operative Steps
1. After saddle block and lithotomy position, routine disinfection and draping were performed;
2. An arched incision was made at the most fluctuant area at 6 o'clock position, the skin was incised, and the pus cavity was reached. The pus was washed out thoroughly, and the pus cavity was explored with the index finger as shown;
3. The pus cavity was repeatedly irrigated with hydrogen peroxide and a large amount of normal saline, and the pus was washed out thoroughly;
4. Hemostasis was achieved, and gauze soaked in Vaseline was placed in the pus cavity for hemostasis and drainage. The procedure was completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the anus and anal canal. The common bile duct (胆总管) is not involved in this procedure.

## Technical Challenges
Not documented

## Teaching Points
- The importance of thorough irrigation and drainage of the abscess cavity.
- The technique of incising and draining the abscess in a way that minimizes postoperative pain and scarring.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Thorough irrigation to prevent recurrence.

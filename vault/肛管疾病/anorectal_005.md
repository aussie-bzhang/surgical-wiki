## Overview
Rectal mass resection for rectal tumor, with emphasis on the technique of excising the mass and ensuring hemostasis.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Rectal mass
**Intraoperative:** Rectal tumor
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Rectal mass located approximately 5 cm from the anal verge at 2, 5, and 7 o'clock positions, with the largest diameter of about 2 cm.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia and lithotomy position, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Dilation of the anal canal, clamping and ligating the mass for excision.
3. Hemostasis was completed, and gauze soaked in Vaseline was inserted into the anal canal for hemostasis at the end of the procedure.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Rectum (直肠), anal canal (肛管). Critical structures include the anal sphincter (肛门括约肌) and the perianal region (会阴区).

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included ensuring adequate hemostasis and minimizing trauma to the anal sphincter.

## Teaching Points
- Ensuring proper hemostasis is crucial to prevent postoperative bleeding.
- The technique of excising the mass while preserving the anal sphincter is a key skill for trainees.

## Complication Prevention
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Minimizing trauma to the anal sphincter to preserve continence.

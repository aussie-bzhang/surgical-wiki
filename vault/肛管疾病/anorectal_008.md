## Overview
Anus abscess drainage procedure performed for the treatment of an anal abscess, with key technical feature being the incision and drainage of the abscess.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Anal abscess
**Intraoperative:** Anal abscess confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
An abscess was found at 1-5 o'clock position of the perianal area, with red and swollen skin and central fluctuation. Approximately 20ml of purulent fluid was drained from the abscess cavity, which measured 2.5cm in diameter. The abscess did not extend to the opposite side, and no mass was palpated by digital rectal examination.

## Operative Steps
1. After local anesthesia, the patient was placed in the lithotomy position and the area was routinely disinfected and draped.
2. A radiating incision was made at the most fluctuant area, the skin was incised, and the abscess cavity was opened with forceps. The purulent fluid was washed out, and the cavity was probed with the index finger.
3. The abscess cavity was repeatedly flushed with hydrogen peroxide and then rinsed with normal saline.
4. Hemostasis was achieved, and gauze soaked in Vaseline was placed in the abscess cavity for hemostasis and drainage. The procedure was completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the anus and rectum. The common bile duct (胆总管) is not involved in this procedure.

## Technical Challenges
Not documented

## Teaching Points
- The importance of proper incision placement and drainage technique.
- The need for thorough irrigation of the abscess cavity.
- The significance of achieving hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper incision and drainage technique to prevent recurrence.
- Thorough irrigation to reduce the risk of infection.

## Overview
An anorectal abscess drainage and fistulectomy is a surgical procedure performed for the treatment of anorectal abscess and fistula. It involves draining the abscess and excising the fistula tract.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Anorectal abscess
**Intraoperative:** Anorectal abscess and fistula
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
An abscess was found at 1 cm from the anal verge, with a fistula tract extending into the anal canal. The abscess cavity measured 1 cm in diameter and did not extend to the contralateral side. No mass was palpated during digital rectal examination.

## Operative Steps
1. The patient was placed in the lithotomy position after local anesthesia. The area was conventionally disinfected and draped.
2. The external and internal openings of the fistula were identified with a probe, and the fistula was excised.
3. A radiating incision was made at the most fluctuant area of the abscess. The skin was incised, and the abscess cavity was opened with forceps. The pus was drained, and the cavity was explored with a finger.
4. The abscess cavity was repeatedly irrigated with hydrogen peroxide and then rinsed with normal saline.
5. Hemostasis was achieved, and gauze soaked in Vaseline was placed in the abscess cavity for hemostasis and drainage. The procedure was completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> The critical structures include the anal canal and the fistula tract. The common bile duct (胆总管) is not involved in this procedure.

## Technical Challenges
The difficulty lies in identifying and excising the fistula tract without causing injury to surrounding structures.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of identifying the external and internal openings of the fistula.
- The technique of creating a radiating incision to access the abscess cavity.
- The need for thorough irrigation and hemostasis.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful dissection to avoid injury to the anal canal and surrounding structures.
- Adequate irrigation to prevent infection.

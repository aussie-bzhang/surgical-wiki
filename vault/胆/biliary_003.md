## Overview
Cholecystectomy with common bile duct exploration and stone extraction with T-tube drainage is a procedure performed for gallstones in the gallbladder and common bile duct. It involves the removal of the gallbladder and stones from the common bile duct, followed by drainage using a T-tube.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gallstones in gallbladder and common bile duct
**Intraoperative:** Gallstones in gallbladder and common bile duct
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a gallbladder measuring 8*6*3cm containing numerous mixed stones of approximately 0.5cm in diameter. The gallbladder duct measured 0.3cm in diameter, and the common bile duct measured 0.8cm in diameter with a 0.5cm stone impacted at the distal end.

## Operative Steps
1. The patient was placed in a supine position, and the abdomen was prepared with routine disinfection and draping.
2. A right subcostal incision was made, and the abdomen was entered layer by layer. The findings were as described above.
3. The gallbladder triangle was carefully dissected, and the gallbladder artery was ligated and divided. The gallbladder was dissected from the liver surface, and the gallbladder bed was cauterized. The gallbladder duct was freed, and a scalp needle was used for intraoperative cholangiography, showing a cup-shaped opening at the distal end of the common bile duct. The gallbladder duct was ligated and divided, and the gallbladder was removed.
4. The anterior wall of the common bile duct was opened, and the stones were removed. A probe was used to ensure no residual stones were present and that they passed through the papilla. A 20F T-tube was placed, and the bile duct was sutured with 3-0 gastrointestinal suture.
5. Hemostasis was achieved, and a negative pressure drain was placed through Winslow's omentum incision, brought out through a stab wound, and secured. The T-tube incision was also brought out through a stab wound and secured.
6. After verifying that all sponges and instruments were accounted for, the abdomen was closed in layers.
7. The procedure was completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the gallbladder, common bile duct, and the papilla of Vater. The gallbladder triangle is a critical area for dissection. (胆总管，胆总管下端，Vater壶腹)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included the presence of multiple stones and the need for careful dissection to avoid injury to the bile duct and other structures.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of careful dissection around the gallbladder triangle to avoid bile duct injury.
- The technique of intraoperative cholangiography to confirm the patency of the common bile duct.
- The proper placement and securing of the T-tube.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful dissection to avoid bile duct injury.
- Proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Ensuring the patency of the common bile duct after stone extraction.

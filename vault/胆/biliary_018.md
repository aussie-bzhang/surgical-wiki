## Overview
Cholecystectomy with common bile duct exploration and external drainage is a surgical procedure performed for gallstones, cholecystitis, and common bile duct stones. It involves the removal of the gallbladder, exploration of the common bile duct, and placement of a T-tube for external drainage.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Acute cholecystitis, common bile duct stones
**Intraoperative:** Gallbladder stones, common bile duct stones, acute cholecystitis
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a gallbladder measuring approximately 8×3×2 cm, containing a large amount of bile and 5-6 bile pigment stones of about 1 cm in diameter, one of which was impacted at the neck. The gallbladder duct diameter was 0.6 cm, and the common bile duct diameter was 0.8 cm.

## Operative Steps
1. The patient was placed in a supine position, and the abdomen was prepared with routine disinfection and draping.
2. A right transverse rectus abdominis incision was made, and the abdomen was entered layer by layer.
3. The gallbladder triangle was carefully dissected, the gallbladder artery was ligated, and the gallbladder was dissected from the liver surface. The gallbladder bed was coagulated, and the gallbladder duct was freed and ligated.
4. The gallbladder was removed.
5. The anterior wall of the common bile duct was opened, and the stones were removed. A probe was used to confirm no residual stones and passage through the papilla.
6. A 22F T-tube was placed in the common bile duct, and the bile duct was sutured with 3-0 gastrointestinal suture.
7. Hemostasis was achieved, and a negative pressure drain was placed at the Winslow's omentum and the liver surface. The drain was brought out through a stab wound and secured.
8. The T-tube was brought out through a stab wound adjacent to the T-tube incision and secured.
9. The instruments and sponges were counted and found to be correct.
10. The abdomen was closed in layers.
11. The procedure was completed.

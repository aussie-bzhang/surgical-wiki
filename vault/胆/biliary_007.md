## Overview
Open cholecystectomy and duodenal fistula repair for cholecystolithiasis and cholecystitis with duodenal fistula.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Cholecystolithiasis
**Intraoperative:** Cholecystolithiasis, cholecystitis, gallbladder-duodenal fistula
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Gallbladder significantly atrophic, size approximately 2*2cm, surrounded by dense adhesions. Gallbladder duct diameter about 5mm, common bile duct diameter about 8mm. No stones palpated, gallbladder contained multiple small stones, largest diameter 4mm. Gallbladder body formed an internal fistula with the duodenal bulb, fistula size about 4mm in diameter. Conversion from laparoscopic to open surgery due to difficulty.

## Operative Steps
1. Positioning the patient in supine position, routine disinfection and draping under pneumoperitoneum. Conversion from laparoscopic to open surgery.
2. Right subcostal incision, layer-by-layer abdominal entry. Findings as described above.
3. Release adhesions around the gallbladder.
4. Carefully dissect the gallbladder triangle, ligate the gallbladder duct 0.5cm away from the common bile duct, transect and ligate the gallbladder vessels, dissect the gallbladder from the liver bed to the gallbladder triangle, transect the gallbladder duct 0.5cm away from the common bile duct, ligate and suture the residual end.
5. Separate the gallbladder from the duodenum, remove the gallbladder for pathological examination.
6. Trim the scar tissue around the duodenal fistula edge, suture the fistula orifice.
7. Perfect hemostasis, place a negative pressure drain through Winslow's hole in the lesser omentum, puncture and lead out.
8. Count sponges and instruments, close the abdomen in layers.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Gallbladder, common bile duct, duodenum. (胆总管)

## Technical Challenges
Conversion from laparoscopic to open surgery due to dense adhesions.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of careful dissection around the gallbladder triangle to avoid injury to the common bile duct.
- Proper management of the gallbladder-duct junction to prevent bile leak.
- Technique of duodenal fistula repair.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper placement of the drain to prevent bile leakage and infection.

## Overview
Cholecystectomy with common bile duct exploration and T-tube drainage is a surgical procedure performed for acute cholecystitis, with the aim of removing the gallbladder and draining the common bile duct. Key technical features include exploration of the common bile duct and placement of a T-tube for postoperative bile drainage.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Abdominal pain, acute peritonitis, chronic myelocytic leukemia
**Intraoperative:** Acute cholecystitis, acute peritonitis, chronic myelocytic leukemia
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed approximately 100ml of peritoneal effusion, a gallbladder measuring about 13*8cm containing cholesterol crystals, strawberry-like mucosal changes, a dilated common bile duct measuring about 1.5cm in diameter containing purulent bile. There was widespread adhesions in the abdomen, and the tissue was prone to bleeding.

## Operative Steps
1. The patient was placed in a supine position, and the abdomen was disinfected and draped in a routine manner under general anesthesia.
2. A midline incision was made, and the abdomen was entered in a layered fashion. The intraoperative findings were as described above.
3. The right upper abdominal adhesions were dissected, and the pre-ductal wall of the common bile duct was opened. The cystic triangle was carefully dissected, and the cystic artery and cystic duct were identified and ligated. The gallbladder was dissected off the liver surface, and the gallbladder bed was coagulated with electrocautery.
4. Cholesterol crystals at the distal end of the common bile duct were removed with a stone forceps. The right and left hepatic ducts were explored and no stones were found. A 22F T-tube was placed, and the patency was tested with water. The anterior wall of the common bile duct was sutured, and no leakage was observed when water was injected.
5. Hemostasis was achieved, and a negative pressure drain was placed through the Winslow's hole of the lesser omentum, exiting through a stab wound and secured. A stab wound adjacent to the T-tube incision was also made, and the T-tube was secured.
6. The instruments and sponges were counted and found to be correct. The abdomen was closed in layers.
7. The procedure was completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> The common bile duct (胆总管), gallbladder (胆囊), cystic duct (胆囊管), cystic artery (胆囊动脉) are critical structures. The gallbladder bed requires careful hemostasis to prevent bleeding.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included widespread adhesions and bleeding due to the chronic myelocytic leukemia.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of careful dissection and hemostasis in the presence of adhesions and bleeding.
- The technique of common bile duct exploration and T-tube placement.
- The management of complications such as bile leakage and infection.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis at the gallbladder bed.
- Proper placement and securing of the T-tube to prevent bile leakage.

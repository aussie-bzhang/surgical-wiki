## Overview
Cholecystectomy with common bile duct exploration and T-tube drainage is a procedure performed for gallstones, acute cholecystitis, and common bile duct stones. It involves the removal of the gallbladder, exploration of the common bile duct, and placement of a T-tube for external drainage.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Cholelithiasis, acute cholecystitis
**Intraoperative:** Gallstone, cholelithiasis, acute cholecystitis
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed severe adhesions in the right upper quadrant, atrophic gallbladder, marked congestion and edema. Multiple gallstones (0.8-1.5 cm in diameter) were found in the gallbladder. The common bile duct had a diameter of 1.2 cm and contained a 1.0 cm stone at the distal end with a large amount of purulent bile.

## Operative Steps
1. The patient was placed in a supine position, and the abdomen was prepared with gas-V and routine disinfection.
2. A right transverse rectus muscle incision was made, and the abdomen was entered layer by layer.
3. The anterior wall of the common bile duct was opened, and the stones were removed. A probe was used to ensure no residual stones and that the papilla was patent.
4. The gallbladder triangle was carefully dissected, the cystic artery was ligated, and the gallbladder was dissected from the liver surface. The gallbladder bed was coagulated, and the cystic duct was ligated and removed.
5. A 22F T-tube was placed into the common bile duct, and the bile duct was sutured with 4-0 gastrointestinal suture.
6. Hemostasis was achieved, and a negative pressure drain was placed at the Winslow's omentum and the liver surface. The drain was brought out through a stab wound and fixed. A stab wound was also made for the T-tube and fixed.
7. The abdomen was closed in layers after verifying that all sponges and instruments were accounted for.
8. The procedure was completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> The common bile duct (胆总管), gallbladder (胆囊), cystic duct (胆囊管), and cystic artery (胆囊动脉) are critical structures. The gallbladder triangle is a danger zone requiring careful dissection. The common bile duct and the papilla of Vater should be protected during the procedure.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included severe adhesions and the need for careful dissection to avoid injury to the bile duct and other structures.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of careful dissection around the gallbladder triangle to avoid injury to the cystic artery and bile duct.
- The technique of placing a T-tube and ensuring proper placement and patency of the papilla of Vater.
- The management of severe adhesions and the need for meticulous hemostasis.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful dissection to avoid injury to the bile duct and other structures.
- Proper placement of the T-tube to ensure adequate drainage and prevent bile leak.
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.

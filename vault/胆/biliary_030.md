## Overview
Gallbladder cancer radical resection with liver V segment resection and hepatoduodenal ligament dissection is a complex procedure performed for gallbladder cancer. It involves resection of the gallbladder, a portion of the liver, and the lymph nodes in the hepatoduodenal ligament.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gallbladder cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gallbladder cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a 3cm hard mass at the gallbladder base, infiltrative growth, invasion of the liver, and a mass formation. Lymph nodes in group 8 were enlarged. No abnormalities were found in the stomach, small intestine, and the rest of the colon.

## Operative Steps
1. The patient was placed in a supine position, and the abdomen was prepared with routine disinfection and draping.
2. A right subcostal incision was made, and the abdomen was entered layer by layer. The findings were as described above.
3. The gallbladder triangle was carefully dissected to free the cystic artery and cystic duct, which were then ligated and divided. The gallbladder was dissected from the liver surface to the gallbladder base, and the gallbladder bed was coagulated with electrocautery.
4. A portion of the liver (V segment) was resected 3cm beyond the adhesion to the liver mass, and the wound was meticulously hemostatized with liver needles and sutured.
5. The hepatoduodenal ligament was dissected, and the common bile duct, portal vein, and hepatic artery were skeletonized, and the lymph nodes in group 8 were resected.
6. Hemostasis was completed, and two negative pressure drains were placed through the Winslow foramen of the omentum, brought out through the stab wound, and secured.
7. After verifying that all sponges and instruments were accounted for, the abdomen was closed in layers.
8. The procedure was completed.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included infiltrative growth of the tumor, which required careful dissection and hemostasis.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of careful dissection around the gallbladder and liver to avoid injury to critical structures.
- The technique of liver resection and lymph node dissection in the hepatoduodenal ligament.
- The management of hemostasis during complex resections.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful dissection to avoid injury to adjacent structures.
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.

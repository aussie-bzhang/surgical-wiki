## Overview
Laparoscopic cholecystectomy is a minimally invasive surgical procedure for the removal of the gallbladder, typically performed for gallstones or acute cholecystitis.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gallstones, acute cholecystitis
**Intraoperative:** Gallstones, acute cholecystitis
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Gallbladder size approximately 10*9*7cm, severe congestion and edema, surrounded by omentum. Gallbladder wall thickness about 0.8cm, containing large amounts of purulent bile. A stone of approximately 0.8cm diameter is impacted at the neck of the gallbladder. No significant thickening of the gallbladder duct. No significant thickening of the common bile duct. Tissue edema severe, friable, and prone to bleeding, making the surgery difficult.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Four-port laparoscopic insertion of instruments, insufflation, and positioning.
3. Separation of adhesions around the gallbladder, simultaneous hemostasis. Careful dissection of Calot's triangle, identification of the gallbladder duct and artery, ligated with titanium clips, and then cut. Retain two clips at the proximal end.
4. Subserosal dissection of the gallbladder, removal of specimen, electrocoagulation of the liver-gallbladder bed for hemostasis.
5. Complete hemostasis, NS irrigation until clean. Place a negative pressure ball in the gallbladder fossa. Count instruments and sponges, close incisions sequentially.
6. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Gallbladder (胆囊), Calot's triangle (Calot三角), common bile duct (胆总管)

## Technical Challenges
Severe edema and friability of the tissue, making dissection and hemostasis difficult.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of careful dissection of Calot's triangle to avoid injury to the common bile duct.
- Proper hemostasis techniques to prevent postoperative bleeding.

## Complication Prevention
- Thorough irrigation to prevent bile leakage.
- Careful handling of the gallbladder to avoid injury to adjacent structures.

## Overview
Cholecystectomy with common bile duct exploration and T-tube drainage is a procedure performed for gallstones in the gallbladder and common bile duct. It involves the removal of the gallbladder, exploration of the common bile duct, and placement of a T-tube for drainage.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gallstones in gallbladder and common bile duct
**Intraoperative:** Gallstones in gallbladder and common bile duct
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Gallbladder measuring 7.8 x 3 cm with filling-type stones. Gallbladder duct diameter 0.5 cm, common bile duct diameter 1.2 cm, containing a large amount of sandy stones.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, routine disinfection and draping under general anesthesia.
2. Upper midline incision, enter peritoneum layer by layer, findings as described above.
3. Carefully dissect the胆囊三角, free the cystic artery and cystic duct, ligate and divide, strip the gallbladder from the liver surface, coagulate the gallbladder bed for hemostasis.
4. Open the anterior wall of the common bile duct, remove stones, common bile duct endoscopy shows no residual stones, intraoperative cholangiography shows the lower end of the common bile duct is patent, no residual stones, place a 24F T-tube, suture the bile duct.
5. Perfect hemostasis, place a negative pressure drain at the Winslow foramen of the lesser omentum, puncture and bring out, secure; puncture and bring out the T-tube incision, secure.
6. Count sponges and instruments, close the abdomen in layers; finished.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Gallbladder, common bile duct (胆总管), cystic artery, cystic duct, liver, lesser omentum (小网膜)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in dissecting the胆囊三角 and ensuring complete removal of stones.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of careful dissection of the胆囊三角 to avoid injury to the cystic artery and common bile duct.
- Proper technique for stone extraction and bile duct exploration.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper placement of the T-tube to prevent bile leak and ensure adequate drainage.

## Overview
Gallbladder cancer palliative resection with special hepatic segment resection and hepatoduodenal ligament dissection in a patient with locally advanced gallbladder cancer.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gallbladder cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gallbladder cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a mass-type gallbladder lesion measuring 5*4*2cm, infiltrative growth, invasion of the liver, colonic mesentery at the hepatic flexure, adhesion of the mass to the lateral abdominal wall, lymph node enlargement in the 8th and 12th groups. No abnormalities were found in the stomach, small intestine, and the rest of the colon.

## Operative Steps
1. Position the patient in supine position, perform routine disinfection and draping under pneumoperitoneum.
2. Make a midline incision, enter the abdomen layer by layer, and observe the findings as described above.
3. Carefully dissect the gallbladder triangle, free the cystic artery and cystic duct, ligate and divide them, and剥离 the gallbladder from the liver surface, coagulate the gallbladder bed for hemostasis.
4. Dissect the adhesion between the mass and the lateral abdominal wall, and the adhesion between the mass and the colonic mesentery at the hepatic flexure, remove the gallbladder, and coagulate the surfaces of the lateral abdominal wall and colonic mesentery after dissection.
5. Excise part of the liver 3cm beyond the adhesion between the mass and the liver, achieve hemostasis at the wound, and suture the wound with Yang Jia梅 needle.
6. Perfect hemostasis, place two negative pressure drains through the Winslow hole of the lesser omentum, puncture the incision, and secure them.
7. Count sponges and instruments, close the abdomen layer by layer after ensuring everything is correct.
8. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: gallbladder, liver, colonic mesentery, lymph nodes. (胆总管)
> Danger zones: gallbladder triangle, liver hilum.
> Structures requiring protection: bile duct, portal vein, inferior vena cava.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included adhesion dissection, achieving hemostasis, and protecting adjacent structures.

## Teaching Points

## Complication Prevention
- Infection prevention: thorough disinfection and use of prophylactic antibiotics.
- Bleeding prevention: meticulous hemostasis techniques.
- Bile leak prevention: careful dissection and adequate bile duct protection.

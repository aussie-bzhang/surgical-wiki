## Overview
Radical cholecystectomy with special hepatic segment resection and choledochotomy for gallbladder cancer, involving extensive dissection and reconstruction.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gallbladder cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gallbladder cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Gallbladder atrophic, 6*5*4cm^3, gallbladder wall thick, up to 2cm thick, bile stasis in gallbladder, mixed stones seen, diameter about 1.5cm, cystic duct diameter about 0.6cm, choledochus dilated, diameter about 2cm, mixed stones seen, diameter about 0.5cm, impacted at distal choledochus. Abdominal adhesions seen, gallbladder, liver, and surrounding tissues adherent, infiltrative mass on gallbladder bed, liver surface, depth about 2cm, hard, infiltrative, two hard abdominal wall nodules seen in right upper quadrant, diameter about 0.5cm, resected and sent for pathology, no ascites.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, sterile drapes applied after routine disinfection.
2. Right subcostal incision, enter peritoneum layer by layer, findings as described above.
3. Release abdominal adhesions in surgical area, resect abdominal wall nodules and send for pathology.
4. Choledochus punctured (bile sent for culture), longitudinally incised, stones extracted with stone forceps.
5. Carefully dissect the cystic triangle, free胆囊动脉 and cystic duct, ligate and divide.
6. Resect part of the liver and gallbladder beyond 3cm from the mass and liver adhesion, remove specimen, control bleeding, and suture the wound with Yang Jia梅 needles.
7. Irrigate choledochus, confirm patency, place 24# T-tube, suture choledochus with 3-0 non-traumatic suture.
8. Irrigate the wound with NS, control bleeding, place a negative pressure drain through Winslow's hole in lesser omentum, puncture and fix.
9. Count sponges and instruments, close abdomen in layers.
10. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Common bile duct (胆总管), gallbladder, liver, infiltrative mass, gallbladder bed, liver surface.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties in extensive dissection and reconstruction, control of bleeding, and placement of T-tube.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of thorough dissection and identification of critical structures.
- Techniques for controlling bleeding and ensuring anastomotic patency.
- Proper placement and management of T-tube.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful dissection to avoid injury to adjacent structures.
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper placement of T-tube to prevent bile leak.

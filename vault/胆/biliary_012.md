## Overview
Cholecystectomy is a surgical procedure for the removal of the gallbladder, typically performed for acute cholecystitis or cholelithiasis. Key technical feature includes careful dissection of the胆囊三角 to avoid injury to the common bile duct and cystic artery.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Acute cholecystitis, gallstones
**Intraoperative:** Acute cholecystitis, gallstones
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a gallbladder measuring approximately 10*9*7cm with high tension, gallbladder wall edema and thickening of about 8mm. Two mixed stones, 1.5cm in diameter, were impacted at the neck of the gallbladder. The cystic duct diameter was 5mm, and the common bile duct was not thickened. The greater omentum was covered and formed adhesions, with tissue inflammation, edema, and tendency to bleed. There was about 40ml of purulent exudate in the abdominal cavity, and the colon was distended, making the surgery difficult.

## Operative Steps
1. Position the patient in supine position under general anesthesia, and perform routine disinfection and draping.
2. Make a right lateral midline incision, enter the abdomen layer by layer, while hemostasis and skin protection are performed. The findings are as described above.
3. Carefully dissect the胆囊三角, free the cystic duct and artery, ligate and suture the cystic duct 0.5cm away from the common bile duct, and ligate the cystic artery. Strip the gallbladder from the liver bed and remove the specimen for pathological examination.
4. Perform hemostasis, place a negative pressure drain through the Winslow foramen of the lesser omentum, and bring it out through the puncture wound.
5. Count sponges and instruments to ensure nothing is left in the abdomen, and close the abdomen in layers.
6. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> The critical structures include the gallbladder, common bile duct, cystic duct, and cystic artery. The danger zone is the胆囊三角, where the common bile duct and cystic artery are located. Protection of these structures is crucial. (胆囊三角 (胆总管), 胆囊管 (胆总管), 胆囊动脉 (胆囊动脉))

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included the high tension of the gallbladder, adhesions, and the presence of purulent exudate. These were managed by careful dissection, adequate hemostasis, and the use of negative pressure drainage.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of careful dissection of the胆囊三角 to avoid injury to the common bile duct and cystic artery.
- The need for adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative bleeding.
- The use of negative pressure drainage to reduce the risk of postoperative abscess formation.

## Complication Prevention
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative bleeding.
- Use of negative pressure drainage to reduce the risk of postoperative abscess formation.

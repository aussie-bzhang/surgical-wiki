## Overview
Gallbladder cancer with liver metastasis; palliative resection, liver metastasis biopsy, and biliary external drainage; key technical feature: safe bile duct resection and drainage.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gallbladder calculus, gallbladder mass.
**Intraoperative:** Gallbladder calculus, gallbladder cancer, liver metastasis.
**Pathological:** Not documented.

## Intraoperative Findings
Gallbladder mass, 5*3*2cm, containing several stones, largest diameter 2.5cm. Tumor infiltration of the duodenum and common bile duct, common bile duct diameter 1.0cm, 2.0cm metastatic nodule near the liver diaphragmatic surface adjacent to the gallbladder bed.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, routine disinfection and draping under pneumoperitoneum.
2. Right upper quadrant subcostal incision, enter peritoneum layer by layer, findings as described above.
3. Carefully dissect the gallbladder triangle, free the gallbladder artery and cystic duct, ligate and divide,剥离胆囊 from the liver surface, coagulate gallbladder bed for hemostasis.
4. Open the anterior wall of the common bile duct, place a 14F T-tube, suture the bile duct.
5. Perfect hemostasis, place a negative pressure drain at Winslow's hole of the lesser omentum, puncture and bring out, fix; puncture and bring out the T-tube incision, fix.
6. Count sponges and instruments, close the abdomen layer by layer after ensuring correctness.
7. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Common bile duct (胆总管), gallbladder (胆囊), liver (肝)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in bile duct resection and drainage, managed with careful dissection and meticulous hemostasis.

## Teaching Points
- Safe bile duct resection and drainage techniques.
- Identification and management of liver metastasis.
- Importance of meticulous hemostasis.

## Complication Prevention
- Prevent bile leakage by ensuring proper bile duct anastomosis.
- Prevent postoperative infection by maintaining strict aseptic technique.

## Overview
Cholecystectomy with common bile duct exploration and external drainage is a surgical procedure performed for gallstones, cholecystitis, and common bile duct stones. It involves the removal of the gallbladder, exploration of the common bile duct, and placement of a T-tube for external drainage.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gallstones, common bile duct stones, acute cholangitis
**Intraoperative:** Gallstones, common bile duct stones, acute cholangitis
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed purulent fluid in the right upper quadrant subhepatic area, a congested and edematous gallbladder measuring 10*6*3cm with gangrenous perforation at the base. Mixed stones were found in the gallbladder, with diameters ranging from 0.5 to 3cm, one of which was impacted at the neck. The common bile duct had a diameter of 1.2cm with high tension, and two bile pigment stones were removed from the lower segment, approximately 4mm in diameter. The right and left hepatic ducts and the distal common bile duct were patent.

## Operative Steps
1. The patient was placed in a supine position, and the abdomen was prepared with a gas-peritoneal technique.
2. A right transverse rectus muscle incision was made, and the abdomen was entered layer by layer.
3. The anterior wall of the common bile duct was opened, and stones were removed. A probe was used to ensure no residual stones and that the ampulla of Vater was patent.
4. The gallbladder triangle was carefully dissected, the cystic artery was ligated, and the gallbladder was dissected from the liver surface. The gallbladder bed was coagulated, and the cystic duct was ligated and removed.
5. A 22F T-tube was placed into the common bile duct, and the bile duct was sutured with 4-0 gastrointestinal suture.
6. Intraoperative cholangiography was performed, and the bile ducts were patent with no residual stones.
7. Hemostasis was achieved, and a negative pressure drain was placed through Winslow's omentum incision, and another through the T-tube incision.
8. The abdomen was closed in layers after verifying that all sponges and instruments were accounted for.
9. The procedure was completed.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included the gangrenous perforation of the gallbladder and the high tension of the common bile duct. These were managed by careful dissection and appropriate hemostasis.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of careful dissection around the gallbladder triangle to avoid injury to the cystic artery.
- The need for meticulous hemostasis to prevent postoperative bleeding.
- The technique of placing a T-tube and ensuring its patency.

## Complication Prevention
- The use of meticulous hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Ensuring the patency of the common bile duct and gallbladder ducts to prevent bile leaks.

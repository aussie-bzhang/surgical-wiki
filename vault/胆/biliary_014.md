## Overview
Laparoscopic cholecystectomy for cholecystitis and gallbladder polyp; key technical feature is safe dissection of Calot's triangle.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Cholecystitis, gallbladder polyp
**Intraoperative:** Cholecystitis, gallbladder polyp
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Gallbladder size approximately 7*6*4cm, a 1.0cm cholesterol polyp on the gallbladder body, smooth surface, no mucosal damage, cystic duct diameter about 4mm, common bile duct not dilated.

## Operative Steps
1. Patient placed in supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Four-port laparoscopic approach, insufflation, positioning, findings as described.
3. Dissection of Calot's triangle, identification of cystic duct and cystic artery, clipping and cutting with titanium clips.
4. Dissection of gallbladder from liver bed, removal of specimen, coagulation of gallbladder bed for hemostasis.
5. Irrigation, hemostasis, placement of a negative pressure drain in the gallbladder bed, count instruments and sponges, close incisions sequentially, procedure completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: gallbladder, common bile duct, Calot's triangle. (胆总管)

## Operative Steps

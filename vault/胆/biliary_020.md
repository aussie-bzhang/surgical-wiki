## Overview
Laparoscopic cholecystectomy for gallstones, with emphasis on key technical steps and anatomical considerations.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gallstones
**Intraoperative:** Gallstones confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Gallbladder measuring 6*4*3cm with multiple mixed stones, largest diameter approximately 0.4cm. Gallbladder duct diameter 0.2cm, common bile duct diameter 0.5cm.

## Operative Steps
1. Patient placed in supine position under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Four-port laparoscopic approach, insufflation, and positioning.
3. Dissection of adhesions, identification of Calot triangle, and separation of the gallbladder duct and artery.
4. Clamping and cutting of the gallbladder duct and artery with titanium clips.
5. Dissection of the gallbladder from the liver bed, removal of specimen, and electrocautery of the gallbladder bed for hemostasis.
6. Irrigation, hemostasis, placement of a drain under the liver, and instrument and suture count.
7. Closure of incisions, completion of the procedure.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the gallbladder, common bile duct (胆总管), and Calot triangle. Care must be taken to protect these structures during dissection.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in adhesion dissection and Calot triangle identification were managed with careful dissection and use of laparoscopic instruments.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of identifying and protecting the common bile duct during gallbladder dissection.
- Proper use of laparoscopic instruments for adhesion dissection and vessel ligation.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful dissection to avoid injury to the common bile duct.
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.

## Related Procedures
- [[Cholecystostomy]] - for gallbladder inflammation or obstruction.

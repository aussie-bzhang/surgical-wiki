## Overview
Cholecystectomy with common bile duct exploration and stone extraction with T-tube drainage is a surgical procedure performed for gallstones, cholecystitis, and common bile duct stones. It involves the removal of the gallbladder, exploration of the common bile duct, extraction of stones, and placement of a T-tube for drainage.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gallstones, cholecystitis, common bile duct stones
**Intraoperative:** Gallstones, cholecystitis, common bile duct stones
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a gallbladder measuring 8*7*4cm with severe inflammatory adhesions, gallbladder wall edema of about 5mm, containing a large amount of mixed stones with the largest diameter of about 2.0cm. There was a stone impacted at the gallbladder neck, the gallbladder duct diameter was 0.8cm, and the common bile duct diameter was 0.8cm. The distal end contained sandy-like stones. No residual stones were seen in the bile duct after intraoperative cholangiography, and the left and right hepatic ducts were visible.

## Operative Steps
1. The patient was placed in a supine position, and the abdomen was prepared with routine disinfection and draping.
2. An upper midline incision was made, and the abdomen was entered in layers, with hemostasis and skin protection. The findings were as described above.
3. The gallbladder triangle was carefully dissected, and the gallbladder artery and cystic duct were identified and ligated. The gallbladder was dissected from the liver surface, and the gallbladder bed was coagulated with electrocautery.
4. The common bile duct was punctured to confirm its position, and the anterior wall was opened to extract the stones. No residual stones were seen at the distal end of the common bile duct after intraoperative cholangiography. A 24F T-tube was placed, sutured and fixed, and brought out through a stab incision.
5. Hemostasis was achieved, and the abdomen was irrigated with large volumes of normal saline and aspirated. A negative pressure drain was placed through the Winslow foramen of the lesser omentum, brought out through a stab incision, and fixed.
6. The instruments and sponges were counted and found to be correct, and the abdomen was closed in layers.
7. The procedure was completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> The critical structures include the gallbladder, common bile duct, and the gallbladder neck. The danger zones are the cystic artery and the common bile duct. Structures requiring protection are the liver and the portal vein. (胆总管，肝门，肝动脉)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included severe inflammatory adhesions and the need for careful dissection to avoid injury to adjacent structures.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of careful dissection around the gallbladder triangle to avoid injury to the cystic artery.
- The technique of identifying and ligating the gallbladder artery and cystic duct.
- The importance of confirming the position of the common bile duct before opening it.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful dissection to avoid injury to adjacent structures.
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper placement of the T-tube to ensure effective drainage.

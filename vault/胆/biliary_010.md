## Overview
A complex case involving intestinal adhesiolysis, common bile duct exploration and stone extraction, and external drainage with a T-tube. The patient presented with severe adhesions in the right upper quadrant, gallbladder resection, and multiple gallstones in the common bile duct.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Cholelithiasis
**Intraoperative:** Cholelithiasis confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Severe adhesions in the right upper quadrant. Gallbladder resected. Multiple gallstones in the common bile duct, with cholesterol stones extracted, measuring 0.5-1.5 cm in diameter. CBD diameter approximately 1.5 cm.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Incision along the previous surgical site, layer-by-layer laparotomy, findings as described above.
3. Careful dissection of adhesions between the duodenum, greater omentum, and liver in the right upper quadrant to expose the common bile duct.
4. With 3-0 gastrointestinal suture, the anterior wall of the common bile duct was opened using a sharp knife. Stones were extracted using a stone forceps and a bile duct scope retrieval basket. The probe and bile duct scope showed no residual stones in the lower segment of the common bile duct and passage through the papilla of the duodenum; the right and left hepatic ducts to the bifurcation of the third-order bile duct were patent.
5. A 24F T-tube was placed in the common bile duct, the bile duct wall was sutured with 3-0 gastrointestinal suture, water injection showed no leakage, and the water column descended smoothly.
6. Hemostasis was completed, and a negative pressure drain was placed through the liver surface into the Winslow's omentum incision, punctured out, and fixed. The T-tube incision was punctured out and fixed.
7. After verifying that all sponges and instruments were accounted for, the abdomen was closed in layers.
8. The operation was completed with difficulty.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Common bile duct (胆总管), hepatic ducts (肝管), gallbladder (胆囊)
> Critical structures requiring protection: hepatic artery, portal vein, and bile ducts.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included severe adhesions and navigating through the complex anatomy to safely extract the gallstones.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of careful dissection to avoid injury to critical structures.
- The technique of using a bile duct scope retrieval basket for stone extraction.
- The proper placement and securing of the T-tube.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper placement of the T-tube to avoid bile leakage.

## Overview
A surgical procedure involving the resection of the left liver lobe, cholecystectomy, common bile duct exploration and stone extraction, T-tube drainage, and cholangioscopy for a patient with common bile duct and left liver lobe stones.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Common bile duct stones, left liver lobe stones
**Intraoperative:** Common bile duct stones, left liver lobe stones
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed edema of the gallbladder and the hepatoduodenal ligament, 7-8 mixed stones in the common bile duct, which blocked the distal bile duct. The bile in the bile duct was yellow-green. The left liver lobe was atrophic, the right liver was hypertrophied with edema, and there was yellow ascites of approximately 150ml.

## Operative Steps
1. The patient was placed in a supine position, and the abdomen was prepared with routine disinfection and draping.
2. An upper abdominal right subcostal incision was made, and the abdomen was entered layer by layer. The findings were as described above.
3. The gallbladder triangle was carefully dissected, and the cystic artery and cystic duct were identified and ligated. The gallbladder was dissected from the liver surface, and the gallbladder bed was cauterized for hemostasis.
4. The anterior wall of the common bile duct was opened, and the stones in the bile duct were removed to block the first liver hilum. The left lateral liver lobe was transected with hemoclips, and the liver surface was sutured in an "U" shape.
5. Cholangioscopy revealed no residual stones, and a 24F T-tube was placed into the bile duct.
6. Hemostasis was completed, and one negative pressure drain was placed at the Winslow's hole of the lesser omentum and at the liver surface. The drain was brought out through a stab wound and fixed. A stab wound was also made next to the T-tube incision for the T-tube drain.
7. After verifying that all sponges and instruments were accounted for, the abdomen was closed in layers.
8. The procedure was completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> The common bile duct (胆总管) is a critical structure requiring protection. The left liver lobe (左肝外叶) is at risk of atrophy. The gallbladder (胆囊) and the hepatoduodenal ligament (肝十二指肠韧带) are important landmarks for dissection.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included the presence of multiple stones in the common bile duct and the need for careful dissection to avoid injury to adjacent structures.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of careful dissection around the gallbladder and common bile duct to avoid injury to adjacent structures.
- The technique of "U" shaped suture for liver surface closure.
- The use of cholangioscopy to ensure no residual stones.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Placement of T-tube for bile drainage to prevent bile leak and stricture.

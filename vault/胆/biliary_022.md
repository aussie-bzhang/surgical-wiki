## Overview
Laparoscopic cholecystectomy is a minimally invasive surgical procedure for the removal of the gallbladder, typically performed for cholecystitis and gallstones.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Cholecystitis, gallstones
**Intraoperative:** Cholecystitis, gallstones
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a 2cm stone impacted at the gallbladder neck, measuring approximately 10*6*5cm with high tension, containing a large amount of white bile and dozens of mixed stones, the largest being 2cm. The gallbladder triangle was adherent, the common bile duct showed no significant enlargement, the cystic duct diameter was about 0.5cm, and the surgery was challenging.

## Operative Steps
1. Position the patient in supine position, under general anesthesia, and perform routine disinfection and draping.
2. Insert instruments through a three-port technique, inflate the abdomen, and position the patient as described above.
3. Dissect the Calot triangle, identify the cystic duct and artery, clamp and cut them with titanium clips, leaving a 0.5cm margin from the common bile duct.
4. Dissect the gallbladder from the liver bed, remove the specimen for pathological examination.
5. Coagulate the gallbladder bed to stop bleeding, count instruments and sponges, close the incisions individually, and place a negative-pressure drain in the gallbladder fossa.
6. Postoperative care and recovery.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the gallbladder, common bile duct, cystic duct, and cystic artery. (胆总管, 胆囊管, 胆囊动脉)

## Technical Challenges
The surgery was challenging due to adherent gallbladder triangle and the presence of a large impacted stone.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of careful dissection around the Calot triangle to avoid injury to the common bile duct.
- The technique of using titanium clips for hemostasis and safe resection of the cystic duct and artery.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful dissection to avoid injury to the common bile duct.
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.

## Overview
Cholecystectomy is a surgical procedure for the removal of the gallbladder, typically performed for acute cholecystitis or cholelithiasis. The key technical feature involves careful dissection of the胆囊三角 to avoid injury to the common bile duct.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Acute cholecystitis, gallstones
**Intraoperative:** Acute cholecystitis, gallstones
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed severe adhesions at the gallbladder triangle, with the antrum of the stomach, duodenal bulb, and omentum adhered to the gallbladder. The gallbladder was edematous, containing approximately 20ml of purulent bile. A mixed stone measuring 1.5cm in diameter was found at the neck of the gallbladder. The gallbladder wall was edematous and thickened, adherent to the liver, and the tissue was prone to bleeding. Frozen section indicated "acute exacerbation of chronic cholecystitis".

## Operative Steps
1. The patient was placed in a supine position under general anesthesia, and the abdomen was routinely disinfected and draped.
2. A right subcostal incision was made, and the abdomen was entered in layers, with hemostasis and skin protection.
3. The gallbladder triangle was carefully dissected, and the cystic duct and artery were freed. The cystic duct was ligated and sutured 0.5cm away from the common bile duct, and the cystic artery was ligated.
4. The gallbladder was dissected off the liver bed and removed, with the specimen sent for pathological examination.
5. Hemostasis was achieved, and a negative pressure drain was placed through Winslow's omental foramen, exiting through a stab wound.
6. Sutures were placed in layers after counting sponges and instruments.
7. The procedure was completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> The common bile duct (胆总管), cystic duct (胆囊管), and cystic artery (胆囊动脉) are critical structures requiring protection during dissection. The gallbladder triangle is a danger zone where these structures are located.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included severe adhesions and the need for careful dissection to avoid injury to the common bile duct.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of careful dissection of the gallbladder triangle to avoid injury to the common bile duct.
- The management of severe adhesions and the need for meticulous hemostasis.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful dissection to avoid injury to the common bile duct.
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.

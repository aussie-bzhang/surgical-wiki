## Overview
Cholecystectomy with common bile duct exploration and T-tube drainage is a surgical procedure performed for gallstones in the gallbladder and common bile duct, involving resection of the gallbladder, removal of stones from the common bile duct, and placement of a T-tube for drainage.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gallstones in gallbladder and common bile duct
**Intraoperative:** Gallstones in gallbladder and common bile duct confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings: Gallbladder hydrops, approximately 14*6*7cm, with bile stasis and multiple mixed stones, max diameter about 1.5cm. Gallbladder duct dilated, diameter about 0.8cm. Common bile duct dilated, diameter about 2cm. Mixed stone removed from distal common bile duct. No residual stones found after probe with catheter and cholangioscope. T-tube placed and bile duct sutured with 3-0 gastrointestinal suture.

## Operative Steps
1. Patient placed in supine position, abdomen draped in a sterile field.
2. Midline incision made, layers of abdomen entered.
3. Carefully dissected the胆囊三角, freed the cystic artery and cystic duct, ligated and divided, gallbladder detached from liver surface, gallbladder removed, gallbladder bed cauterized.
4. Common bile duct confirmed, incised anterior wall, stone removed with stone forceps, catheter probe showed no residual stones and passed through the papilla of duodenum. Cholangioscope examination as described.
5. T-tube placed, bile duct sutured with 3-0 gastrointestinal suture.
6. Hemostasis achieved, negative pressure drain placed at Winslow's omentum incision, brought out through a stab wound, fixed. T-tube incision stab wound brought out, fixed.
7. Sutures removed, abdomen closed in layers.
8. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Gallbladder, common bile duct (胆总管), cystic artery, cystic duct, papilla of duodenum

## Technical Challenges
Not documented

## Teaching Points
- Importance of careful dissection of the胆囊三角 to avoid injury to the cystic artery and common bile duct.
- Proper placement and securing of the T-tube to ensure effective bile drainage.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper placement of the T-tube to prevent bile leak and infection.

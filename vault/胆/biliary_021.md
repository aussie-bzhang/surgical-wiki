## Overview
Laparoscopic cholecystectomy for acute cholecystitis and gallstones; key technical feature includes dissection of the Calot triangle and cholecystectomy.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Acute cholecystitis, gallstones
**Intraoperative:** Acute cholecystitis, gallstones
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Gallbladder was large, approximately 20*15*6cm, with high tension, containing a large amount of white bile, severe edema of the gallbladder wall, thickness about 0.8cm, significant edema and adhesion around the gallbladder, marked inflammation and edema with bleeding in the surgical area. No significant thickening of the common bile duct. A 1.5cm stone was impacted at the neck of the gallbladder, with a diameter of the cystic duct about 0.5cm. The surgery was difficult, and the patient was admitted to the SICU postoperatively.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Four-port insertion of instruments, insufflation, positioning, findings as described above.
3. Dissection of the Calot triangle, identification of the cystic duct and artery, ligated with titanium clips, and then cut, with a residual cystic duct 0.5cm from the common bile duct.
4. Dissection of the gallbladder from the liver bed, removal of the specimen for pathological examination.
5. Electrocautery of the gallbladder bed for hemostasis, count of instruments and sponges correct, sequentially close the incisions, place a negative pressure drain in the gallbladder fossa.
6. Completion of the procedure.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the common bile duct (胆总管), Calot triangle. (Calot三角)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included the large size and high tension of the gallbladder, significant adhesion and inflammation, and the impacted stone at the gallbladder neck.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of careful dissection of the Calot triangle to avoid injury to the common bile duct.
- Management of significant adhesion and inflammation.
- Proper handling of the impacted stone.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful dissection to avoid injury to the common bile duct.
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.

## Related Procedures
- [[Open Cholecystectomy]] - alternative approach in cases of severe adhesion or inflammation.

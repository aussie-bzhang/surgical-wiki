## Overview
Laparoscopic cholecystectomy is a minimally invasive surgical procedure for the removal of the gallbladder, typically performed for gallstones. It involves identifying and dissecting the gallbladder from surrounding structures while preserving the common bile duct.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Cholecystolithiasis
**Intraoperative:** Cholecystolithiasis
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Gallbladder size approximately 8*3cm, containing one cholesterol stone with a maximum diameter of 3cm. Gallbladder duct diameter 0.5cm, common bile duct diameter 0.8cm.

## Operative Steps
1. Positioning the patient in supine position, under general anesthesia, and performing routine disinfection and draping.
2. Insertion of instruments through four ports, insufflation, and positioning.
3. Dissection of the gallbladder fundus, identification of the Calot triangle, and separation of the gallbladder duct and artery. These were then clamped with three titanium clips and cut.
4. Dissection of the gallbladder from the liver bed, removal of the specimen, and electrocoagulation of the gallbladder bed for hemostasis.
5. Irrigation, hemostasis, and verification of instruments and sponges. Closure of incisions and completion of the procedure.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the gallbladder, Calot triangle, and common bile duct. (胆总管)

## Technical Challenges
Not documented

## Teaching Points
- Proper identification and dissection of the Calot triangle is crucial to avoid bile duct injury.
- Careful hemostasis is essential to prevent postoperative bleeding.

## Complication Prevention
- Use of preoperative imaging to assess the anatomy and presence of adhesions.
- Careful dissection to avoid injury to adjacent structures.

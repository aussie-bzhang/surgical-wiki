## Overview
Laparoscopic cholecystectomy for acute cholecystitis with gallstones, highlighting the technical challenge of severe inflammatory adhesions in Calot's triangle.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gallstones, acute cholecystitis
**Intraoperative:** Gallstones, acute cholecystitis
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Severe inflammatory adhesions in Calot's triangle, surrounding omentum covering and enveloping, gallbladder size approximately 10*7*5cm, gallbladder wall edema severe with thickness of about 6mm, containing a large amount of purulent white bile, high tension, marked congestion, tissue edema prone to bleeding, multiple cholesterol stones impacted at the gallbladder neck with the largest diameter about 1.0cm, a large number of gallstones with diameter 0.3—1.0cm, surgical difficulty encountered.

## Operative Steps
1. Positioning the patient in supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Four-port insertion of instruments, insufflation, and positioning.
3. Careful dissection of Calot's triangle, identification of the cystic duct and cystic artery, clamping with titanium clips, and subsequent transection.
4. Dissection of the gallbladder from the liver bed, removal of specimen, and meticulous electrocoagulation of the gallbladder bed for hemostasis.
5. Extensive lavage with normal saline until clean, leaving a negative pressure drain in the gallbladder bed, counting instruments and sponges, and sequentially closing the incisions.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Calot's triangle (胆总管), gallbladder (胆囊), common bile duct (胆总管)

## Technical Challenges
Severe inflammatory adhesions in Calot's triangle, requiring careful dissection to avoid injury to adjacent structures.

## Teaching Points
- Careful dissection of Calot's triangle to avoid injury to the common bile duct.
- Management of severe inflammatory adhesions.
- Proper hemostasis and specimen removal.

## Complication Prevention
- Avoiding injury to the common bile duct during dissection.
- Proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.

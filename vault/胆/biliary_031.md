## Overview
Cholecystectomy is a surgical procedure for the removal of the gallbladder, typically performed for gallstones or gallbladder disease. The key technical feature involves careful dissection of the胆囊三角 to avoid injury to the common bile duct.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gallbladder mass
**Intraoperative:** Gallbladder mass
**Pathological:** Chronic cholecystitis, adenomatosis, with a small amount of irregular glands

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperatively, the gallbladder was approximately 8*6*5cm in size, with a significantly thickened wall of about 3.0cm. The gallbladder lumen was明显缩小明显缩小, and the gallbladder mucosa was smooth without destruction. Frozen section pathology showed "chronic cholecystitis, adenomatosis, with a small amount of irregular glands."

## Operative Steps
1. Place the patient in a supine position, perform routine disinfection and draping under the umbilical incision.
2. Incise the midupper midline abdomen, enter the peritoneal cavity layer by layer, and control bleeding while protecting the skin.
3. Dissect the胆囊三角, free the cystic duct, ligate and suture it with two ties, and then transect the cystic duct. Strip the gallbladder under the serosa while coagulating the bleeding, and send the specimen for pathology as described above.
4. Irrigate with normal saline, ensure complete hemostasis, place a negative pressure drain under the liver, and bring it out through the puncture wound.
5. Count sponges and instruments to ensure accuracy, and close the abdomen in layers.
6. Operation completed.

- Careful dissection of the胆囊三角 to avoid injury to the common bile duct.

## Complication Prevention
- Preventing bile duct injury by meticulous dissection and identification of the common bile duct.

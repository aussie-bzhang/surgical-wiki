## Overview
Cholecystectomy with common bile duct exploration and external drainage is a surgical procedure performed for the treatment of gallstones and common bile duct stones. It involves the removal of the gallbladder, exploration of the common bile duct, and placement of a T-tube for external drainage.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Cholelithiasis
**Intraoperative:** Gallbladder stone, common bile duct stone
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a gallbladder with congestion and edema, adherent to surrounding structures, measuring approximately 10×3×2cm. It contained a large amount of bile and bilirubin stones, 5-6 in number, with the largest diameter of 0.6cm. The gallbladder duct diameter was 0.5cm. The common bile duct diameter was 0.8cm, containing 4 stones with a diameter of approximately 0.5cm and a large amount of bile sludge.

## Operative Steps
1. The patient was placed in a supine position under general anesthesia, and the abdomen was routinely disinfected and draped.
2. A right transverse rectus abdominis incision was made, and the abdomen was entered layer by layer. The findings were as described above.
3. The gallbladder triangle was carefully dissected, and the gallbladder artery was ligated and divided. The gallbladder was dissected from the liver surface, and the gallbladder bed was electrocoagulated to stop bleeding. The gallbladder duct was also dissected and ligated, and the gallbladder was removed.
4. The gallbladder duct was opened with a 3-0 gastrointestinal suture, and stones were removed with a stone forceps. A probe and endoscope showed no residual stones in the lower segment of the common bile duct and passage through the papilla of the duodenum. The right and left hepatic ducts were patent up to the bifurcation of the third-order bile duct.
5. A 24F T-tube was placed in the common bile duct, and the bile duct wall was sutured with 3-0 gastrointestinal suture. Water injection through a syringe showed no leakage, and the water column descended smoothly.
6. Hemostasis was completed, and a negative pressure drain was placed through the liver surface into the Winslow's omentum incision, and a stab incision was made to bring it out and secure it. A stab incision adjacent to the T-tube incision was made to bring out and secure the drain.
7. After verifying that all sponges and instruments were accounted for, the abdomen was closed in layers.
8. The procedure was completed.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of careful dissection of the gallbladder triangle to avoid injury to adjacent structures.
- The technique of opening the common bile duct and removing stones.
- The proper placement and securing of the T-tube.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Careful dissection to avoid injury to adjacent structures.

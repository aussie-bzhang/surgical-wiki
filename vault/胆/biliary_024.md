## Overview
Cholecystectomy with common bile duct exploration and stone extraction with T-tube drainage is a procedure performed for acute cholecystitis, gallstones, and common bile duct stones. The key technical feature involves the removal of the gallbladder, exploration of the common bile duct, extraction of stones, and placement of a T-tube for drainage.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Acute cholecystitis, gallstones, common bile duct stones
**Intraoperative:** Acute cholecystitis, gallstones, common bile duct stones
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a gallbladder measuring 10*8cm with a diameter of the cystic duct of 0.5cm, containing multiple stones. The gallbladder wall was thickened, inflamed, and edematous. The common bile duct had a diameter of 1.0cm, and two stones were found within it, with the largest measuring approximately 0.4cm. The distal bile duct was patent. The surgery was difficult.

## Operative Steps
1. The patient was placed in a supine position, and the abdomen was entered through a right subcostal incision after routine disinfection and draping.
2. The gallbladder was dissected and mobilized from the liver surface, and the cystic artery and cystic duct were ligated and transected.
3. After confirming the common bile duct, the anterior wall was opened, and stones were extracted using a stone forceps. A ureteral catheter was used to confirm no residual stones and passage through the papilla.
4. A T-tube was placed, and the bile duct was sutured with 3-0 gastrointestinal suture.
5. Hemostasis was achieved, and a negative pressure drain was placed through Winslow's omentum incision.
6. The abdomen was closed in layers after verifying the count of sponges and instruments.
7. The procedure was completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> The critical structures include the gallbladder, common bile duct, and the cystic triangle. The common bile duct (胆总管) requires careful dissection to avoid injury.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included the thickened, inflamed gallbladder wall and the presence of multiple stones in the common bile duct.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of careful dissection around the cystic triangle to avoid injury to the common bile duct.
- The technique of stone extraction from the common bile duct and the use of a ureteral catheter to confirm the absence of residual stones.
- The proper placement and suture of the T-tube.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful dissection to avoid injury to the common bile duct.
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper placement of the T-tube to ensure effective bile drainage.

## Overview
Cholecystectomy and common bile duct exploration for gallstones and cholelithiasis following gastric surgery, with emphasis on gallbladder anatomy and common bile duct manipulation.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gallstones, cholelithiasis, post-gastric surgery.
**Intraoperative:** Gallstones, cholelithiasis, post-gastric surgery.
**Pathological:** Not documented.

## Intraoperative Findings
Right upper quadrant adhesions, gallbladder measuring 10*6*4cm with multiple 0.5cm stones, cystic duct diameter 0.4cm, common bile duct diameter 1.2cm with six 0.5cm stones.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, routine disinfection and draping under pneumoperitoneum.
2. Right upper quadrant subcostal incision, enter peritoneum as described.
3. Dissect right upper quadrant adhesions, carefully dissect the gallbladder triangle, free the cystic artery and cystic duct, ligate and divide,剥离胆囊从肝面，电灼胆囊床止血。
4. Open the anterior wall of the common bile duct, remove stones, inspect the common bile duct and right and left hepatic ducts with a cholangoscope, no residual stones found, place a 20F T-tube, suture the bile duct, perform cholangiography, no residual stones found.
5. Perfect hemostasis, place a negative pressure drain through Winslow's omentum, puncture incision, fix; puncture incision next to T-tube incision, fix.
6. Count sponges and instruments, close peritoneum in layers; 
7. Operation completed.

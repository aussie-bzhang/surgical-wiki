## Overview
Cholecystectomy with common bile duct exploration and stone extraction with T-tube drainage is a procedure performed for gallstones and common bile duct stones, involving the removal of the gallbladder and stones from the common bile duct, and the placement of a T-tube for drainage.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Acute cholecystitis
**Intraoperative:** Gallstone, common bile duct stone
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a gallbladder measuring approximately 7*3cm, with purulent edema and a single mixed stone approximately 1.0cm in diameter. The cystic duct was thickened, measuring about 0.8cm in diameter. The common bile duct was thickened, measuring about 1.2cm in diameter, with multiple mixed stones seen within, the largest being approximately 1.0cm in diameter. Intraoperative cholangiography showed patency of the distal common bile duct.

## Operative Steps
1. The patient was placed in a supine position, and the abdomen was prepared with routine disinfection and draping.
2. A midline upper abdominal incision was made, and the abdomen was entered in a layered manner, with hemostasis and skin protection.
3. The common bile duct was punctured to confirm its location, and the anterior wall of the common bile duct was opened to extract the stones.
4. The gallbladder triangle was carefully dissected, and the cystic artery was ligated and divided. The gallbladder was dissected from the liver surface, and the gallbladder bed was coagulated.
5. A 24F T-tube was placed, and the bile duct was fixed with 3-0 gastrointestinal suture. Intraoperative cholangiography showed no significant residual stones in the distal common bile duct, and the right and left hepatic ducts were well visualized.
6. Hemostasis was completed, and a negative pressure drain was placed through Winslow's omentum incision, brought out through a stab wound, and fixed. The T-tube incision was also brought out through a stab wound and fixed.
7. The instruments and sponges were counted and found to be correct, and the abdomen was closed in layers.
8. The procedure was completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the gallbladder, common bile duct, and cystic duct. The danger zone is the gallbladder triangle, where the cystic artery and common bile duct are located. Structures requiring protection are the common bile duct and the cystic artery.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included the thickened gallbladder and common bile duct, which required careful dissection and hemostasis.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of careful dissection around the gallbladder triangle to avoid injury to the common bile duct and cystic artery.
- The technique of placing a T-tube and ensuring proper positioning and fixation.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Ensuring the T-tube is properly positioned and fixed to prevent bile leak and infection.

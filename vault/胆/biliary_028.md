## Overview
A complex procedure involving the release of small bowel adhesions, exploration and stone extraction from the common bile duct, and T-tube drainage, performed for acute cholangitis and common bile duct stones.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Acute cholangitis, common bile duct stones
**Intraoperative:** Acute suppurative cholangitis, common bile duct stones
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Severe adhesions in the upper right abdomen, common bile duct diameter approximately 30mm, containing a large amount of purulent bile. Large amount of bile pigment stones extracted from the distal end of the common bile duct. Large stones up to 1.5cm in diameter were extracted using a stone forceps. The distal bile duct was patent after exploration. The procedure was difficult.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, routine disinfection and draping under pneumoperitoneum;
2. Right subcostal incision, layer-by-layer abdominal entry, findings as described above;
3. Release of adhesions in the upper right abdomen, puncture confirmed the common bile duct, opened the anterior wall of the common bile duct, extracted stones using stone forceps, and ureteral probe confirmed no residual stones and passage through the duodenal papilla. Exploration as described above. T-tube placed, sutured the bile duct with 3-0 gastrointestinal suture;
4. Hemostasis completed, negative pressure drain placed at Winslow's omentum incision, brought out through the puncture, fixed; T-tube brought out through the incision, fixed;
5. Counted sponges and instruments, closed the abdomen in layers;
6. Procedure completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Common bile duct (胆总管), small bowel (小肠)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included severe adhesions and the need for careful dissection to avoid injury to adjacent structures.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of careful dissection to avoid injury to the common bile duct and adjacent structures.
- The technique of stone extraction from the common bile duct.
- The placement and fixation of the T-tube.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful dissection to avoid injury to adjacent structures.
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.

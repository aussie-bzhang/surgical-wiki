## Overview
A complex procedure involving the release of intestinal adhesions, exploration and stone extraction from the common bile duct, cholangioscopy, and T-tube drainage. This is a challenging case with significant adhesions and involves critical structures.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Postcholecystectomy common bile duct stone
**Intraoperative:** Postcholecystectomy common bile duct stone
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed dense adhesions in the upper right abdomen, a common bile duct diameter of 1.5 cm containing a large amount of sandy stones. After extraction of the stones, cholangioscopy was performed on the common bile duct and right and left hepatic ducts up to the bifurcation of the second-order hepatic duct, no residual stones were found.

## Operative Steps
1. The patient was placed in a supine position, and the abdomen was prepared with routine disinfection and draping.
2. The original midline incision was used, and the abdomen was entered layer by layer.
3. The adhesions in the upper right abdomen were carefully released, with caution to avoid injury to adjacent duodenum and colonic bowel.
4. The hepatoduodenal ligament was dissected, and the common bile duct was identified. The anterior wall of the common bile duct was opened, and the stones were extracted. Cholangioscopy was performed on the common bile duct and right and left hepatic ducts up to the bifurcation of the second-order hepatic duct, no residual stones were found.
5. A 24F T-tube was placed, and the bile duct was sutured with 3-0 gastrointestinal suture in an interrupted manner.
6. Hemostasis was achieved, and two negative pressure drains were placed through Winslow's omentum incision, brought out through a stab wound, and fixed. The T-tube incision was also brought out through a stab wound and fixed.
7. After verifying that all sponges and instruments were accounted for, the abdomen was closed in layers.
8. The procedure was completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> The common bile duct (胆总管) is a critical structure requiring protection. The intestinal adhesions (肠粘连) are dense and require careful dissection to avoid injury to adjacent structures.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included dense adhesions and the need for careful dissection to avoid injury to adjacent structures.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of careful dissection to avoid injury to adjacent structures.
- The technique of cholangioscopy and the importance of thorough exploration of the bile ducts.
- The proper placement and fixation of the T-tube.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful dissection to avoid injury to adjacent structures.
- Proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Thorough exploration of the bile ducts to prevent residual stones.

## Overview
Cholecystectomy with common bile duct exploration and stone extraction, T-tube drainage, intraoperative cholangioscopy, and cholangiography. A surgical procedure performed for cholecystolithiasis with significant anatomical findings and technical challenges.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Cholecystolithiasis
**Intraoperative:** Cholecystolithiasis
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Gallbladder measuring 7*4cm with strawberry-like mucosal changes, containing several cholesterol crystals. Cystic duct diameter 0.5cm, common bile duct diameter 1.0cm, with several 0.5cm stones at the distal end. No significant neoplasms or residual stones noted on cholangioscopy and cholangiography.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Upper midline incision, layer-by-layer abdominal entry, hemostasis, and skin protection.
3. Careful dissection of the胆囊三角, freeing the cystic artery and cystic duct, ligating and dividing them.
4.胆囊从肝面剥离，电灼胆囊床止血。
5. Pericystic dissection to identify the common bile duct, opening the anterior wall, extracting stones.
6. Cholangiography showed no significant residual stones at the distal end of the common bile duct, with good visualization of the right and left hepatic ducts.
7. Placement of a 24F T-tube, sutured and fixed, and punctured for drainage.
8. Hemostasis, copious lavage with normal saline, and aspiration of the abdominal cavity.
9. Negative pressure drainage tube placed through the Winslow's hole of the lesser omentum, punctured and fixed.
10. Counting sponges and instruments, and layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen.
11. Completion of the procedure.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Common bile duct (胆总管), gallbladder (胆囊), cystic duct (胆囊管). Careful dissection around the胆囊三角 to avoid injury to the cystic artery and common bile duct.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties in identifying and dissecting the common bile duct, ensuring no injury to adjacent structures.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of careful dissection around the胆囊三角 to avoid injury to the cystic artery and common bile duct.
- Proper placement and fixation of the T-tube to ensure adequate drainage and prevent bile leak.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Adequate lavage of the abdominal cavity to reduce the risk of infection.

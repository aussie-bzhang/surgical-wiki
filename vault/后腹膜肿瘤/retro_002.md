## Overview
Posterior abdominal tumor resection with right kidney and appendectomy, involving extensive dissection and organ resection.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Abdominal posterior tumor
**Intraoperative:** Liposarcoma of the posterior abdominal space
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located in the posterior abdominal space, large, occupying most of the right posterior abdominal cavity and extending to the left. Surrounds the entire right kidney and ureter, compresses the liver, the porta hepatis, and the inferior vena cava. Pushes the duodenum and ascending colon to the opposite side. Tumor measures approximately 40*40*30cm, weighs about 15 kg. Capsule is intact, lobulated. Appendix measures 4cm in length and 1cm in diameter.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia. Right ureteral catheterization. Routine disinfection and draping. Midline incision from the xiphoid process to the pubic symphysis.
2. Open lateral peritoneum of the ascending colon, free up to the subhepatic space, and down to the pelvic peritoneum. Free the tumor outside the capsule.
3. Right kidney and ureter are completely surrounded by the tumor. Cut the right ureter, ligate with two ties, free the medial aspect of the mass, protect the inferior vena cava, cut the right renal vein, and suture with Proline 4-0.
4. Separate the tumor from the duodenum adhesions, up to the porta hepatis, protect the porta hepatis.
5. En bloc resection of the tumor. Thorough hemostasis, lavage the peritoneal cavity, aspirate the effluent, place two negative pressure drainage tubes in the right paracolic gutter, up to the subhepatic space, down to the pelvic cavity, and out through the right lower abdomen. Count instruments and sponges, close the abdomen in layers.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: posterior abdominal space, right kidney, ureter, liver, gallbladder, common bile duct, duodenum, ascending colon. (胆总管)
> Danger zones: inferior vena cava, porta hepatis.
> Structures requiring protection: inferior vena cava, porta hepatis.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in tumor dissection and organ resection, particularly in protecting adjacent structures.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of careful dissection and preservation of adjacent structures.
- Management of extensive adhesions and organ resection.
- Proper use of negative pressure drainage.

## Complication Prevention
- Thorough hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper placement of drains to prevent postoperative infection and effusion.

## Overview
A complex procedure involving the resection of a malignant gastrointestinal stromal tumor of the small intestine, adhesiolysis, and appendectomy. The tumor was large and involved multiple structures.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Recurrent gastric gastrointestinal stromal tumor
**Intraoperative:** Recurrent gastric gastrointestinal stromal tumor
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed extensive intra-abdominal adhesions, 4000ml of hemorrhagic ascites, a tumor located in the right abdomen measuring approximately 20*10*10cm with irregular shape, necrosis, and liquefaction. The tumor infiltrated and surrounded the ileum, involved the appendix, and the right abdominal wall.

## Operative Steps
1. Position the patient in supine position under general anesthesia, perform routine disinfection and draping.
2. Make a midline incision around the umbilicus, explore as described above.
3. Carefully dissect the intra-abdominal adhesions.
4. Free the tumor, dissect the tumor from surrounding tissues, resect the involved small intestine, with the proximal margin 1.5 meters from the Treitz ligament and the distal margin 0.1 meter from the ileocecal junction.
5. Divide the appendiceal mesentery, tie off the appendiceal stump with a double ligature 0.5cm from the root.
6. Perform end-to-end anastomosis of the small intestine with full-thickness and muscular layer reinforcement, close the mesenteric defect.
7. Achieve hemostasis at the wound site, irrigate the abdomen, aspirate the effluent, place three negative pressure drainage balls, verify instruments and sponges, and close the abdomen in layers.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: intestine, appendix, right abdominal wall. (小肠, 阑尾, 右腹壁)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included extensive adhesions and infiltrative tumor, which required careful dissection and preservation of surrounding structures.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of meticulous dissection to avoid injury to adjacent structures.
- The technique of resecting a large tumor with extensive infiltration.
- The management of complex anastomosis in a contaminated field.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Adequate irrigation and drainage to prevent infection.

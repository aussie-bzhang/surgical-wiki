## Overview
Postoperative abdominal wall tumor excision and complex small bowel adhesion release in a patient with a history of right hemicolectomy for colon cancer, presenting with a postoperative abdominal wall mass.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right hemicolectomy for colon cancer, postoperative abdominal wall mass.
**Intraoperative:** Right hemicolectomy for colon cancer, postoperative retroperitoneal tumor.
**Pathological:** Lymph node at the iliac vessels: infiltrative poorly differentiated adenocarcinoma tissue.

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located in the retroperitoneum of the pelvis, approximately 5*5*3cm in size, medially adjacent to the uterine appendages, laterally to the hip bone, iliopectineal ligament, and lateral abdominal wall. The tumor invades the lateral wall of the external iliac vessels cranially, encircling about 3/4 of the vessel wall. A lymph node adjacent to the iliac vessels is also noted, firm, 2cm in diameter. Severe adhesions of abdominal viscera, small bowel to small bowel, and small bowel to abdominal wall.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia with bilateral ureteral catheterization, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Right transverse rectus abdominis incision, superior to 5cm above the umbilicus, inferior to the level of the pubic symphysis.
3. Release of small bowel adhesions, opening of lateral and pelvic peritoneum, identification of the tumor.
4. Excision of the lymph node adjacent to the iliac vessels for intraoperative frozen pathology, result as above.
5. Dissection of the tumor outside the tumor capsule, release of adhesions medially with the uterine appendages, lateral resection of the tumor close to the periosteum of the hip bone, iliopectineal ligament, and lateral abdominal wall. Careful dissection of the tumor along the lateral wall of the external iliac vessels, excision of the tumor, attention to not damage the ureter and iliac vessels.
6. Hemostasis of the wound, lavage of the abdominal cavity, aspiration of effusion, placement of two negative pressure balls in the right colonic fossa and pelvic floor, drainage through the incision, count of instruments and sponges, layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: iliac vessels, uterine appendages, iliopectineal ligament, lateral abdominal wall. (髂血管, 子宫附件, 髂耻骨韧带, 侧腹壁)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in tumor dissection and adhesion release, careful handling of the iliac vessels and ureter.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of careful dissection around the iliac vessels and ureter.
- Management of complex adhesions.
- Intraoperative frozen pathology for accurate diagnosis.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Adequate lavage to prevent infection.

## Overview
Gastrectomy with pyloroplasty and cholecystectomy for gastric cancer and cholecystolithiasis, involving resection of the upper part of the stomach, pyloroplasty, and cholecystectomy.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer, cholecystolithiasis
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer, cholecystolithiasis
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings: A 3cm ulcerous mass in the posterior wall of the gastric fundus near the cardia, not penetrating the serosa; lymph nodes in groups 9 and 10 were enlarged; gallbladder measuring 7*4*3cm with a mixed stone, 2cm in diameter; gallbladder duct diameter 0.3cm; exploration of liver, pelvis, and ascites negative.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia successful, routine disinfection and draping; midline upper abdominal incision, enter peritoneum as described.
2. Dissection and transection of the gastrocolic ligament, right to the hepatic flexure of the colon, left to the inferior pole of the spleen; transection of the splenocolic ligament.
3. Lifting the transverse colon, stripping the anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon to the superior margin of the pancreas, preserving the right gastroepiploic vessels, and transecting the greater curvature of the stomach at the avascular area of the corpus.
4. Clamping and transecting the left gastric vessels, separating the corpus along the greater curvature of the stomach, freeing the left side of the cardia, protecting the spleen.
5. Transection of the lesser omentum below the liver, flipping the corpus upwards, transection of the left gastric vessels at the root, double ligature; separation along the lesser omentum upwards, incision of the esophageal hiatus and bilateral peritoneum, freeing the lower segment of the esophagus, clamping and transecting the vagus nerve, transection of the esophagus 3cm above the cardia, insertion of a 28.5mm anastomosis stapler.
6. Incision of the pyloric anterior wall, insertion of a 28.5mm tubular anastomosis stapler for side-to-side anastomosis of the stomach and esophagus, transection of the stomach 2cm below the tumor using a 90mm clamp, removal of specimen, sutured the seromuscular layer around the anastomosis.
7. Pyloroplasty.
8. Lifting the gallbladder base, anatomizing the Calot triangle, separating and transecting the cystic duct and cystic artery, stripping the gallbladder from the liver bed, removal of specimen.
9. Thorough hemostasis, lavage of the peritoneal cavity, aspiration of effluent, placement of two negative pressure balls behind the esophageal anastomosis, verification of instruments and sponges, layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: stomach, pylorus, gallbladder; danger zones: Calot triangle; structures requiring protection: spleen, pancreas.

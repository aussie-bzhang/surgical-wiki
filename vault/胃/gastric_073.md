## Overview
Total gastrectomy with esophagojejunostomy is a complex procedure for the treatment of gastric cancer, involving the removal of the entire stomach and the creation of a new alimentary tract.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Giant tumor located in the corpus of the stomach, extending from the cardia to the angle of the stomach and antrum. Size approximately 10*8cm, infiltrative ulcerative type, involving the mesentery of the transverse colon and the serosa of the pancreas. Additionally, a grayish-white nodule approximately 0.5cm in size was seen on the hepatic diaphragmatic surface. No abnormalities were found in the exploration of the remaining liver, gallbladder, pancreas, spleen, small intestine, colon, and peritoneal cavity.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping, midline upper abdominal incision.
2. Dissection of the gastrocolic ligament, right to the hepatic flexure of the colon, left to the inferior pole of the spleen, with the anterior leaf of the transverse colon mesentery,剥离至 the surface of the pancreas.
3. Blunt dissection of the adhesion between the stomach and the colon below the pylorus, transection and ligation of the right gastric artery at the root of the pylorus, and clearance of the surrounding lymph nodes.
4. Transection of the lesser omentum below the liver, right to the hepatoduodenal ligament, transection and ligation of the right gastric artery.
5. Transection of the left gastric artery and short gastric vessels along the greater curvature of the stomach until the left side of the cardia.
6. Upward flipping of the corpus of the stomach, transection and double ligation of the left gastric artery at the root; upward dissection along the lesser omentum, left incision of the esophageal hiatus and bilateral peritoneum, free the distal esophagus; transection of the esophagus 3cm above the cardia, removal of the specimen, insertion of a 25.5mm tube-shaped anastomosis stapler and fixation with purse-string suture.
7. Transection of the jejunum 20cm from the Treitz ligament, the anastomosis stapler is used to anastomose the esophagus with the distal jejunum, the distal jejunum is closed with a 60mm clamp. The proximal jejunum is anastomosed with the distal jejunum manually 50cm below the esophageal anastomosis (in front of the colon); closure of the mesenteric gap.
8. Thorough hemostasis, lavage of the abdominal cavity, placement of three negative pressure balls above and below the anastomosis, layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen, completion of the operation.

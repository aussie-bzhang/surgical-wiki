## Overview
Distal gastrectomy for gastric adenocarcinoma with complex intestinal adhesiolysis in a post-colorectal surgery patient.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric antrum cancer, post-colorectal surgery.
**Intraoperative:** Confirmed gastric antrum cancer, post-colorectal surgery.
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Severe abdominal adhesions, tumor located on the greater curvature of the gastric antrum, infiltrative ulcerative type, 3*2cm2, tumor not penetrating the serosa. Lymph nodes in groups 3, 4, 5, 6, 8, and 12 are enlarged. No liver or pelvic involvement.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping, midline upper abdominal incision.
2. Careful dissection of intestinal adhesions.
3. Transection of the gastrocolic ligament on the intestinal side, right to the hepatic flexure of the colon, left to the splenic flexure with 2-3 branches of the left gastric vessels and ligation.
4. Dissection of the anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon to the superior margin of the pancreas.
5. Transection and double ligature of the right gastric and right gastroepiploic vessels at the vascular root; transection of the duodenum 3 cm distal to the pylorus, the distal end is sutured into a purse-string and fixed with a 28# tube-shaped anastomosis stapler.
6. Transection of the lesser omentum below the liver, and dissection of the anterior leaf of the hepatoduodenal ligament and its lymphatic tissue.
7. Upward flipping of the gastric body, transection and double ligature of the left gastric vessels at the vascular root; removal of the gastric coronary vessels and lymphatic tissue on the lesser curvature until below the cardia.
8. Opening the anterior gastric wall, inserting the long tube of the stapler at the superior margin of the tumor, anastomosing the posterior wall of the stomach with the duodenum 7 cm above the tumor, removing the stapler and closing the anastomosis with a 90mm stapler 4 cm from the anastomosis, removing the specimen. The anastomosis and gastric stump are reinforced with full thickness sutures.
9. Thorough hemostasis, lavage of the abdominal cavity, aspiration of effluent, placement of two negative pressure balls at the anastomosis, and layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen.

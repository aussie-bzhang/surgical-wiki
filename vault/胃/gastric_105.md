## Overview
Gastric adenocarcinoma with cholecystolithiasis; distal gastric resection with Billroth II anastomosis, antecolic, proximal to greater curvature, D2 lymph node dissection, and cholecystectomy.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric antrum adenocarcinoma, cholecystolithiasis
**Intraoperative:** Confirmed gastric antrum adenocarcinoma, cholecystolithiasis
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Large tumor at the lesser curvature of the gastric angle, 4cm in diameter, infiltrative ulcerative type, involving the serosal layer. Lymph nodes around the stomach are enlarged. Exploration of small intestine, peritoneum, pelvis, and liver is negative.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, after general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Abdominal exploration via an upper midline incision.
2. Incise the gastrocolic ligament on the intestinal side, extending to the hepatic flexure on the right and the inferior pole of the spleen on the left.
3. Retract the transverse colon, strip the greater omentum, the anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon, and the capsule of the pancreas, ligate and transect the right gastric artery and vein at the vascular root.
4. Clamp and transect the gastro splenic ligament along the greater curvature of the stomach body, dissect the 4th and 6th groups of lymph nodes, preserve 2 branches of the short gastric vessels, and protect the spleen.
5. Dissect the lesser omentum below the liver, skeletonize the hepatoduodenal ligament, and dissect the 8th group of lymph nodes.
6. Ligate and transect the right gastric artery at the root, mobilize the gastric antrum and the first and second segments of the duodenum, close and transect the duodenum 3cm from the pylorus using a 60mm clamp, reinforce the distal end with silk suture, and pack it.
7. Reflect the stomach body, ligate and transect the coronary vessels along the lesser curvature of the stomach, dissect the 1st, 3rd, 5th, and 7th groups of lymph nodes, and reach the right side of the esophagus.
8. Ligate and suture the left gastric artery at the root, dissect the 7th and 9th groups of lymph nodes.
9. Clamp the stomach body 7cm from the tumor margin with two Kocher clamps, close and transect the remaining stomach with a 90mm clamp, and remove the specimen.
10. At 20cm from the Treitz ligament, puncture the jejunum with a 29.5mm anvil of the end-to-end stapler and fix it with a purse-string suture. Insert the long handle into the gastric remnant and anastomose the stomach with the jejunum in an antecolic manner, with the input side on the greater curvature. Remove the stapler and close the anastomosis with a 60mm clamp, reinforce it with 3-0 gastrointestinal suture, and place a stomach tube.
11. Carefully dissect the胆囊三角, ligate and transect the cystic artery, strip the gallbladder from the gallbladder bed of the liver to the胆囊三角, transect the cystic duct 0.5cm from the common bile duct, ligate and suture the distal end, and remove the specimen.
12. Stop bleeding, irrigate the abdominal cavity, aspirate the effluent, place two negative pressure balls in front and behind the anastomosis under the liver, count instruments and gauze, and close the abdomen in layers. Operation completed.

## Teaching Points
- Proper mobilization and resection of the stomach
- Accurate lymph node dissection according to D2 criteria
- Safe anastomosis of the stomach and jejunum

## Complication Prevention
- Adequate hemostasis
- Proper anastomotic technique to prevent leaks
- Careful dissection to avoid injury to adjacent structures

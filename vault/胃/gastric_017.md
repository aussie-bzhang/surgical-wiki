## Overview
Gastric resection with esophagojejunostomy and partial transverse colon resection for gastric cancer, involving extensive lymph node dissection and complex anastomotic techniques.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located on the anterior wall of the gastric body, approximately 10*6*4cm, infiltrative ulcerative type, extending to the serosa and infiltrating into the middle segment of the transverse colon, forming dense adhesions. Lymph nodes in groups 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 7 were significantly enlarged. No peritoneal fluid. Two hard nodules, approximately 0.8-1cm in diameter, were found in the mesentery of the transverse colon, resected and sent for pathology.

## Operative Steps
1. Patient placed in supine position, general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline laparotomy, abdominal exploration as described.
3. Dissection of the transverse colon, resection of the involved segment of the transverse colon.
4. Opening of the mesentery anterior to the transverse colon, resection of mesenteric nodules, and lymph node dissection.
5. Dissection of the hepatoduodenal ligament and gastrohepatic ligament, lymph node dissection, and resection of the right gastric and left gastric vessels.
6. Resection of the stomach, esophagus, and transverse colon.
7. Anastomosis of the esophagus to the jejunum using a stapler.
8. Closure of the abdominal cavity, placement of drains, and closure of the incision.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: stomach, esophagus, jejunum, transverse colon. Danger zones: areas of dense adhesions, lymph node clusters. Structures requiring protection: bile duct, spleen.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties in resection due to dense adhesions, complex anastomotic techniques, and extensive lymph node dissection.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of careful dissection to avoid injury to critical structures.
- Techniques for complex anastomotic procedures.
- Management of dense adhesions and lymph node clusters.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis.
- Adequate drainage to prevent postoperative complications.

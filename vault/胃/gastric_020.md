## Overview
Gastrectomy for distal gastric cancer, a common clinical scenario involving a localized ulcerative mass in the antrum of the stomach, requiring radical resection with Billroth II reconstruction and colonic antimesenteric approach.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed an ulcerative mass measuring 1.5 cm in the antrum of the stomach, not penetrating the serosa, involving the pyloric antrum. No abnormalities were found in the liver, gallbladder, small intestine, colon, or pelvis.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, after general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Abdominal exploration via an upper midline incision around the umbilicus.
2. Mobilization of the transverse colon, dissection of the greater omentum and the anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon, transection of the gastrocolic ligament, and mobilization to the right to the hepatic flexure and to the left to the splenic flexure.
3. Clamping and transection of the right gastric artery and vein at the vascular pedicle.
4. Dissection and mobilization of the hepatoduodenal ligament, resection of the lesser omentum, and mobilization of the second segment of the duodenum.
5. Transection and ligation of the right gastric vessel, transection of the duodenum 3 cm distal to the pyloric antrum, and end-to-end anastomosis with the jejunum using a 28# linear stapler.
6. Clamping and transection of the greater curvature of the stomach, preservation of two short gastric vessels, and protection of the spleen.
7. Opening the jejunal wall 15 cm distal to the Treitz ligament and inserting the head of a 28# linear stapler.
8. Transection of the stomach at the middle-third of the corpus, closure of the lesser curvature with a clamp, suture reinforcement of the serosa, leaving a 3 cm opening on the greater curvature, and insertion of the long tube of the 28# linear stapler for side-to-side anastomosis with the jejunum (proximal to the greater curvature). Reinforcement of the anastomosis and closure of the remaining stomach opening with a clamp.
9. Thorough hemostasis, irrigation of the abdominal cavity, aspiration of effluent, and placement of two negative-pressure drainage tubes at the anastomosis and duodenal stump.
10. Counting instruments and sponges, and closing the abdomen in layers.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the stomach, gastrojejunal anastomosis. The danger zones are the pyloric antrum and the gastrojejunal anastomosis. Structures requiring protection are the spleen and the short gastric vessels (胃短血管).

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included mobilization of the stomach and duodenum, and ensuring a secure gastrojejunal anastomosis.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of meticulous dissection and hemostasis to prevent postoperative complications.
- The technique of performing a Billroth II reconstruction with colonic antimesenteric approach.
- The need for careful mobilization of the spleen and preservation of the short gastric vessels.

## Complication Prevention
- Thorough hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Adequate irrigation and drainage to prevent infection.

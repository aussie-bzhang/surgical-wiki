## Overview
Distal gastrectomy for gastric cancer, with D2+ lymph node dissection and Billroth I reconstruction. Involves resection of the distal stomach, lymph node dissection, and reconstruction of the gastrointestinal tract.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Lesions located at the lesser curvature of the gastric angle and the pyloric canal, both linear ulcerative, approximately 5*2*2cm in size, not involving the serosa. Lymph nodes in the 5th, 6th, and 8th groups were enlarged, with the largest diameter about 4cm. No abnormalities found in the liver and pelvic cavity.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Abdominal exploration via an upper midline incision.
2. Transection of the gastrocolic ligament on the intestinal side, right to the inferior part of the duodenal bulb, left to the 2-3 branches of the left gastric short vessels at the upper 2-3 cm of the splenic flexure, and ligation.
3. Separation below the pylorus to expose the right gastric网膜血管, double ligature at the root, and lymph node dissection of the 6th group.
4. Dissection below the liver to transect the lesser omentum, stripping the anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon to the superior margin of the pancreas.
5. Separation below the liver to transect the right gastric网膜血管, common hepatic artery, celiac trunk, and double ligature at the root of the right gastric artery. Lymph node dissection of the 7th, 8th, and 12th groups.
6. Transection of the duodenum 3cm distal to the pylorus, the distal end sutured and ligated, and the anvil of the 28.5mm stapler inserted and fixed.
7. Mobilization of the stomach, ligation and suture at the root of the left gastric artery, and removal of the specimen.
8. Clamping and transection of the stomach body 60mm from the distal margin, the distal stomach anastomosed to the duodenal stump with the stapler, and the serosal layer of the anastomosis reinforced with interrupted sutures.
9. Hemostasis, lavage of the abdominal cavity, aspiration of effluent, and placement of two negative pressure drains at the anastomotic site.
10. Counting sponges and instruments, closing the abdomen in layers, and completion of the procedure.

## Overview
Gastrectomy for distal gastric cancer, clinical context of gastric antrum mass, key technical feature of radical resection with Billroth I anastomosis and D2 lymph node dissection.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric antrum mass
**Intraoperative:** Gastric antrum cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located at the gastric angle and greater curvature, approximately 3*3cm, central depression, hard consistency, involving serosa. Lymph nodes in the 6th group around the stomach were enlarged. No abnormalities were found in the liver, gallbladder, pancreas, small intestine, and colon.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping, midline upper abdominal incision, abdominal exploration as above, placement of wound protector and automatic retractor.
2. Transection of the gastrocolic ligament on the intestinal side, right to the inferior part of the duodenal bulb, left to the 2-3 branches of the left gastric short vessels at the upper 2-3 cm of the splenic flexure, and ligation.
3. Dissection below the pylorus to expose the right gastric artery, common hepatic artery, and celiac trunk, and ligation and dissection of the 7th, 8th, and 12th groups of lymph nodes.
4. Transection of the duodenum 3cm distal to the pylorus, purse-string suture and placement of a 28mm anvil of the stapler, inversion of the gastric body, ligation and suture at the root of the vessels, and transection of the left gastric artery.
5. Removal of the gastric coronary vessels and lymphatic tissue on the lesser curvature (1st and 3rd groups) to the subcardia.
6. Insertion of the long tube of the stapler into the residual stomach, anastomosis of the posterior wall of the greater curvature of the gastric body at the middle-third of the gastric body with the residual duodenal stump, 90mm clamp for transection of the gastric body, removal of the specimen, and full-thickness interrupted reinforcement of the anastomosis.
7. Hemostasis, lavage of the abdominal cavity, aspiration of exudate, placement of two negative pressure drains at the anterior and posterior aspects of the anastomosis.
8. Counting sponges and instruments, and closing the layers in sequence.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: gastric antrum, common bile duct (胆总管). Danger zones: lymph node groups around the stomach. Structures requiring protection: vessels and nerves.

## Overview
Gastrectomy for gastric cancer, a clinical context involving the resection of a gastric cancer with D2 lymph node dissection, using Billroth I anastomosis.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Gastric antrum with a superficial ulcerative carcinoma at the angle of the lesser curvature, 1.2*0.8CM in size. Lymph nodes around the stomach and the seventh group were not enlarged.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Abdominal incision at the midline above the umbilicus.
3. Dissection of the gastrocolic ligament, right to the second segment of the duodenum, left to the upper 2-3 branches of the short gastric vessels, and ligation.
4. Dissection of the preperitoneal leaf of the transverse colon to the superior margin of the pancreas.
5. Dissection of the hepatoduodenal ligament and lymphoid tissue.
6. Ligation and double clamping of the right gastric and right gastroepiploic vessels at the vascular root.
7. Transection of the duodenum 3cm distal to the pylorus, with a purse-string suture and 29# anvil of the anastomotic stapler.
8. Dissection of the left gastric vessels, removal of the coronary vessels and lymphoid tissue to the subcardia.
9. Transection of the gastric body at the middle-third, removal of the specimen, and double suture of the residual margin.
10. Placement of the residual stomach into the anastomotic stapler long tube, end-to-end anastomosis with the duodenal distal end, and double closure of the gastric wall.
11. Reinforcement of the anastomosis with a layer of seromuscular layer.
12. Hemostasis, lavage of the peritoneal cavity, aspiration of effusion, placement of a negative pressure ball under the liver, and suture closure in layers.

## Teaching Points
- Proper dissection and ligation of the gastrocolic ligament and short gastric vessels.
- Accurate identification and dissection of the hepatoduodenal ligament.
- Technique for anastomosis with the anastomotic stapler.

## Complication Prevention
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper lavage of the peritoneal cavity to reduce the risk of infection.

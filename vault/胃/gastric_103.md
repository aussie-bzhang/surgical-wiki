## Overview
Gastric cancer resection with Billroth I reconstruction and D2 lymph node dissection. Involves resection of the stomach and surrounding structures, followed by reconstruction and lymphadenectomy.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located at the lesser curvature of the gastric angle, approximately 7cm in diameter, infiltrative ulcerative type, involving the serosa; scattered enlarged lymph nodes around the stomach. No abnormalities found in liver, gallbladder, pancreas, spleen, small intestine, colon, and peritoneal cavity.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping, midline upper abdominal incision, peritoneal cavity exploration as described.
2. Transection of the gastrocolic ligament on the intestinal side, right to the bulb of the duodenum, left to the upper 2-3 branches of the left gastric vessels and transection with ligation, stripping of the anterior leaf of the transverse colon mesentery to the superior margin of the pancreas.
3. Dissection below the pylorus to expose the right gastric网膜血管, double ligation at the root, and lymphadenectomy of the 6th group.
4. Dissection below the liver to transect the lesser omentum, stripping the anterior leaf of the hepatoduodenal ligament and its lymphatic tissue, and exposing the right gastric vessel, common hepatic artery, and celiac trunk. Transection and double ligation at the root of the right gastric vessel, and lymphadenectomy of the 7th and 8th groups.
5. Transection of the duodenum 3cm distal to the pylorus, the distal end sutured into a 28.5mm anastomosis stapler and fixed, the stomach body flipped upwards, vessels at the root ligated and sutured, and transection.
6. Clearing of the coronary vessels and lymphatic tissue on the lesser curvature (1st and 3rd groups) to the subcardia.
7. Clamping the stomach body 5cm above the tumor with two Kocher clamps and opening, the remaining stomach body 90mm clamped and transected, specimen removed, and the long tube of the stapler inserted into the stomach opening.
8. Anastomosis of the posterior wall of the middle third of the stomach body to the distal end of the duodenum with a 60mm clamp, and the opening of the stomach body reinforced with serosal interrupted sutures and full-thickness interrupted sutures.
9. Thorough hemostasis, lavage of the peritoneal cavity, aspiration of exudate, placement of two negative pressure drains at the anterior and posterior aspects of the anastomosis.
10. Counting sponges and instruments, closure of the abdomen in layers, completion of the operation.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: stomach, gallbladder, pancreas, spleen, small intestine, colon, peritoneal cavity. (胆总管)
> Danger zones: vessels and lymph nodes.
> Structures requiring protection: vessels and nerves.

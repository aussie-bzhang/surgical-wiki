## Overview
Gastrectomy for gastric cancer, a clinical context involving advanced gastric cancer with significant invasion, key technical feature being the Roux-en-Y esophagojejunostomy.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric mass
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a 4cm mass at the cardia, infiltrative-ulcerative type, involving the lower esophagus, and extending beyond the serosa. No metastatic lesions were found in the liver, spleen, small intestine, and colon. Negative for ascites.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Median upper abdominal incision.
2. Dissection and ligation of the gastrocolic ligament along the transverse colon, right to the hepatic flexure, including the anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon, stripping to the surface of the pancreas. Stripping the serosa of the pancreas, ligating and cutting the right gastric vessels at the root of the gastroduodenal artery, clearing the 6th group of lymph nodes.
3. Ligation and cutting the left gastroepiploic vessels and short gastric vessels along the greater curvature until the left side of the cardia, ligation and cutting the lesser omentum below the liver, right to the hepatoduodenal ligament, ligating and cutting the right gastric vessels; clearing the 12th group of lymph nodes.
4. At 3cm from the pylorus, the duodenum was transected with a 60mm clamp and sutured in full thickness.
5. The remaining stomach was flipped upwards, ligated at the root of the left gastric artery. Clearing the 7th, 8th, and 9th groups of lymph nodes. Separation along the lesser omentum, freeing the lower esophagus, clearing the 1st and 3rd groups of lymph nodes, cutting both vagus nerves; transecting the esophagus 3cm above the cardia.
6. At 20cm from the Treitz ligament, the jejunum was transected, and the esophagus was anastomosed to the distal jejunum with a 25.5mm anastomotic stapler. The jejunal stump was closed with a side-to-side stapler. The proximal jejunum was anastomosed to the jejunum 60cm below the esophagus吻合口 (colon anterior); closing the mesenteric gap.
7. Thorough hemostasis, lavage of the abdominal cavity, aspiration of effluent, placement of negative pressure balls above and below the anastomosis through the duodenal stump, verification of instruments and sponges, layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: stomach, esophagus, duodenum, spleen, pancreas, colon. (胆总管)
> Danger zones: gastroesophageal junction, hepatoduodenal ligament.
> Structures requiring protection: spleen, pancreas, bile duct.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included extensive tumor invasion, significant adhesions, and the need for meticulous dissection to preserve adjacent structures.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of meticulous dissection and lymph node clearance.
- Technique of Roux-en-Y esophagojejunostomy.
- Management of potential complications such as anastomotic leakage and postoperative ileus.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent anastomotic leakage.
- Adequate decompression of the stomach to prevent postoperative ileus.

## Overview
Palliative gastrectomy for gastric antrum cancer, with significant adhesions and liver cirrhosis.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric antrum cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric antrum cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located in the gastric antrum near the pylorus, diameter about 3*3cm, ulcerative infiltrative type, extending to the serosa, adherent to the hepatoduodenal ligament, pancreas, and anterior leaf of the transverse colon mesentery. Liver shows small nodular schistosomal cirrhosis. Gastrocaval vein and subhepatic vein branches are dilated. Severe intra-abdominal adhesions due to previous cholecystectomy. No metastatic lesions found in the abdominal and pelvic cavities.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Midline upper abdominal incision, abdominal exploration as above.
2. Elevate the transverse colon, strip the greater omentum, transverse colon mesentery, and pancreatic capsule. Tumor adherent to the pancreas, dense adhesion, temporarily set aside for separation after transecting the stomach.
3. Cut the gastrocolic ligament, extend to the right to the hepatic flexure and to the left to the inferior pole of the spleen; clamp and cut the right gastric artery and vein at the vascular root.
4. Clamp and cut the gastric splenic ligament along the greater curvature of the stomach, retaining 2 branches of the short gastric vessels, protecting the spleen.
5. High ligation and transection of the right gastric vessel; 3cm distal to the pyloric orifice, transect the duodenum, suture the distal end, and bury it.
6. Strip and dissect the adhesions between the tumor and the hepatoduodenal ligament, pancreas, and perform lymph node dissection of the 12th group.
7. High ligation and transection of the right gastric vessel; 3cm distal to the pyloric orifice, transect the duodenum, suture the distal end, and bury it.
8. Along the hepatic artery, clean the 8th group lymph nodes, transect the small网膜 under the liver, extend upwards to the right side of the esophagus; clean the 1st, 3rd group lymph nodes; elevate the gastric body, transect the left gastric vessel at the root, ligate and tie twice; clean the 7th group lymph nodes.
9. Transect the gastric body about 5cm from the tumor margin, remove the specimen; carefully hemostasis and suture the seromuscular layer intermittently.
10. 15cm from the Treitz ligament, use a 75# side-to-side stapler to perform an antecolic side-to-side anastomosis between the jejunum and transverse colon, the input segment to the lesser curvature, suture the seromuscular layer, and close the mesenteric defect above the anastomosis.
11. Thorough hemostasis, wash the abdominal cavity, aspirate the effluent, place a negative pressure ball under the liver and at the distal end of the duodenum, count instruments and gauze, close the abdomen in layers.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: stomach, pancreas, hepatoduodenal ligament, transverse colon mesentery. (胆总管, 胰腺, 肝十二指肠韧带, 横结肠系膜)

## Technical Challenges
Severe adhesions, dense adhesion between the tumor and the pancreas, and liver cirrhosis.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of careful dissection and adhesion release.
- Protection of critical structures during surgery.
- Management of adhesions and cirrhosis.

## Complication Prevention
- Thorough hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Adequate drainage to prevent postoperative abscess.

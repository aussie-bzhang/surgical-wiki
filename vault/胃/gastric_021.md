## Overview
Gastrectomy with esophagojejunostomy is a surgical procedure performed for advanced gastric cancer with peritoneal metastasis. The key technical feature is the creation of a Roux-en-Y anastomosis between the esophagus and the jejunum.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer with peritoneal metastasis
**Pathological:** Low-grade differentiated adenocarcinoma infiltration in the transverse colon nodule

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a tumor located in the antrum of the stomach, approximately 4*4*3cm in size, involving the serosa externally. Lymph nodes in groups 1, 3, 7, 12, and 8 were enlarged. Multiple metastatic nodules were found on the wall of the transverse colon. No obvious abnormalities were seen in the liver during exploration.

## Operative Steps
1. The patient was placed in a supine position under general anesthesia. The abdomen was entered through a midline upper abdominal incision.
2. The transverse colon nodule was excised and sent for pathological examination. The colon wall was sutured.
3. The gastrocolic ligament was separated, extending to the right to the hepatic flexure of the colon and to the inferior pole of the spleen. The anterior leaf of the transverse colon mesentery was stripped to the surface of the pancreas, and the lesser omentum was opened to enter the lesser curvature side.
4. The right gastroepiploic artery was transected and ligated at the root of the pylorus, and the surrounding lymph nodes were cleared.
5. The lesser omentum was transected below the liver, extending to the hepatoduodenal ligament, and the corresponding lymph tissue was cleared. The right gastric artery was transected and ligated.
6. The duodenum was transected and sutured at 3 cm from the pylorus.
7. The left gastroepiploic artery and short gastric vessels were transected and ligated along the greater curvature until the left side of the cardia.
8. The stomach body was flipped up, and the left gastric artery at the root was transected and doubly ligated. The lesser omentum was separated upwards, and the esophagus was freed below the esophageal hiatus. The esophagus was transected 3 cm above the cardia.
9. The esophagus was removed, and a 25-mm circular stapler was placed with the anvil and fixed with purse-string sutures.
10. The jejunum was transected 20 cm from the Treitz ligament, and the esophagus was anastomosed to the distal jejunum with a 25-mm circular stapler. The distal jejunum was closed with a side-to-side stapler. The proximal jejunum was anastomosed to the jejunum 60 cm below the esophageal anastomosis (in front of the colon).
11. The mesenteric gap was closed; thorough hemostasis was achieved, the abdomen was irrigated, and effluent was aspirated. Two negative pressure drainage tubes were placed below the anastomosis. Instruments and sponges were counted and found to be correct. The abdomen was closed in layers.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included the extensive involvement of the stomach and the need for meticulous dissection to preserve the surrounding structures.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of meticulous dissection to avoid injury to adjacent structures.
- The technique of creating a Roux-en-Y anastomosis.
- The management of lymph node metastasis.

## Complication Prevention
- Thorough hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Adequate irrigation and drainage to prevent infection.

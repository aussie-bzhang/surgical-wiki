## Overview
Gastrectomy with splenectomy for liver cirrhosis, portal hypertension, and gastric cancer. Key technical feature includes radical resection of the upper part of the stomach and spleen.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Liver cirrhosis, portal hypertension, gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Liver cirrhosis, portal hypertension, gastric cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Gastric mass measuring 6*4cm, infiltrative growth, penetrating the serosa, involving the pancreatic capsule. Liver shows schistosomal cirrhosis. Spleen is congested and enlarged, measuring 10*8cm. No abnormalities found in the gallbladder, small intestine, and colon. Postoperative attention to vital signs and drainage.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Midline upper abdominal incision.
2. Dissection of the gastrocolic ligament, right to the hepatic flexure of the colon, left to the inferior pole of the spleen; transection of the splenocolic ligament.
3. Dissection of the anterior leaf of the transverse colon mesentery to the superior margin of the pancreas, preserving the right gastroepiploic vessels, and transecting the greater omentum at the boundary of the right gastroepiploic vessels.
4. Transection of the splenocolic ligament, dissection of the splenic pedicle, transection and ligation of the splenic artery and vein, and separate suture ligation.
5. Dissection along the lateral aspect of the spleen to the diaphragm, freeing the left aspect of the cardia thoroughly.
6. Transection of the lesser omentum below the liver, flipping up the gastric body, transection and double ligation of the left gastric vessels at the root.
7. Dissection along the lesser omentum upwards, freeing the lower esophagus, transection of the distal gastric with a 100cm linear stapler, transection of the esophagus 3cm above the cardia, removal of the specimen, placement of a 28F tube-shaped stapler for gastroesophageal end-to-end anastomosis, and sutured the serosal layer around the anastomosis.
8. Thorough hemostasis, lavage of the peritoneal cavity, aspiration of exudate, placement of negative pressure balls at the anterior and posterior walls of the esophageal anastomosis, extending to the splenic fossa, count of instruments and sponges, and layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen.

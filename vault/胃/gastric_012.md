## Overview
Gastric cancer resection with Billroth I reconstruction and D2 lymph node dissection. Involves resection of the stomach and surrounding structures, followed by reconstruction of the gastrointestinal tract.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located on the anterior wall of the gastric antrum, approximately 2cm in diameter, ulcerative type; palpable enlarged lymph nodes in the 7th and 8th groups; no abnormalities found in the liver, gallbladder, pancreas, spleen, small intestine, colon, and peritoneal cavity.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping, midline upper abdominal incision, peritoneal cavity exploration as described.
2. Transection of the gastrocolic ligament on the intestinal side, right to the inferior aspect of the duodenal bulb, left to the 2-3 branches of the left gastric vessels at the splenic flexure and transected and ligated; stripping of the anterior leaf of the transverse colon mesentery to the superior aspect of the pancreas.
3. Dissection below the pylorus to expose the right gastroepiploic vessels, doubly transected at the root and cleared the 6th group lymph nodes.
4. Dissection below the liver to transect the lesser omentum, stripping the anterior leaf of the hepatoduodenal ligament and the lymphoid tissue within, exposing the right gastric vessels, proper hepatic artery, celiac trunk, transected and doubly ligated at the root of the right gastric vessels, and cleared the 7th and 8th group lymph nodes.
5. Transection of the duodenum 3cm distal to the pylorus, the distal end of the duodenum sutured into a purse-string and fixed with a 28.5mm anastomosis stapler, the stomach body flipped upwards, the vessels at the root ligated and sutured, and transected; clearing the coronary vessels and lymphoid tissue on the lesser curvature (1st and 3rd groups) to the subcardia.
6. Insertion of the long tube of the stapler into the residual stomach cavity, anastomosis of the posterior wall of the greater curvature of the middle-third of the stomach body to the distal end of the duodenum, 60mm clamp stapler transected the stomach body, removed the specimen, and the anastomosis was reinforced with a full-thickness interrupted suture.
7. Thorough hemostasis, lavage of the peritoneal cavity, aspiration of exudate, placement of two negative pressure drains at the anterior and posterior aspects of the anastomosis.
8. Counting sponges and instruments, closure in layers.

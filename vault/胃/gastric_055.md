## Overview
Gastric wedge resection for a gastric submucosal tumor in a patient with pre-operative diagnosis of gastric mass.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric mass
**Intraoperative:** Gastric submucosal tumor
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a 3cm mass in the gastric fundus with smooth margins, extracavitary growth, clear boundaries, and a superficial ulcerated surface.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping, midline upper abdominal incision, placement of wound protector and automatic retractor.
2. Dissection of the gastrocolic ligament on the greater curvature side, ligation and division to the gastric fundus, with attention to protecting the spleen.
3. Incision of the omentum, exposure of the gastric body and fundus, as described above, and decision on surgical approach.
4. Complete wedge resection of the mass 3cm from the edge using a 100cm clamp, removal of the specimen, and reinforcement of the seromuscular layer.
5. Thorough hemostasis, irrigation of the peritoneal cavity, and aspiration of effluent fluid.
6. Layered closure.
7. Completion of surgery.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the stomach and spleen. The common bile duct (胆总管) should be protected during dissection.

## Technical Challenges
Not documented

## Teaching Points
- The importance of protecting the spleen during dissection.
- The technique of wedge resection and reinforcement of the seromuscular layer.

## Complication Prevention
- Thorough hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.

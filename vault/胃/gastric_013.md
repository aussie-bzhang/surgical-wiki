## Overview
Gastric wedge resection for gastric mesenchymoma, a minimally invasive procedure with clear boundaries and superficial ulceration.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric mesenchymoma
**Intraoperative:** Gastric mesenchymoma
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a 3cm mesenchymoma on the anterior wall of the gastric fundus, originating from the mucosa and growing into the lumen. The tumor had clear boundaries, did not penetrate the serosa, and had a superficial ulceration of approximately 0.3cm in diameter.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia, routine disinfection, and draping.
2. Midline upper abdominal incision, placement of wound protector and automatic retractor.
3. Placement of a gauze pad behind the spleen to elevate it, followed by dissection and ligation of the gastrocolic ligament along the greater curvature of the stomach to the gastric fundus, with attention to protecting the spleen.
4. Opening of the omentum, exposure of the gastric body and fundus, and decision on the surgical approach.
5.楔形完整切除肿块，距肿块边缘3cm，移除标本，加固浆肌层.
6. Thorough hemostasis, irrigation of the abdominal cavity, and aspiration of exudate.
7. Layered closure.
8. Completion of the procedure.

## Anatomical Key Points

## Teaching Points
- Importance of protecting the spleen during dissection.
- Proper technique for wedge resection to ensure clear margins.
- Management of superficial ulceration on the tumor surface.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Adequate irrigation to reduce the risk of infection.

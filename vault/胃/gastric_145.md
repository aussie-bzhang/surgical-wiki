## Overview
Gastrectomy for distal gastric cancer with Billroth I reconstruction and cholecystectomy. Involves resection of the distal stomach, reconstruction of the stomach and duodenum, and removal of the gallbladder.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Gastric antrum with a 3.0cm infiltrative mass, superficial ulceration with mucosal erosion but not penetrating the serosa. Gallbladder measuring 6*3*2cm with two 2.0cm stones. No significant abnormalities found in liver, small intestine, or colon. No ascites.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline upper abdominal incision.
3. Detailed dissection of the胆囊三角, ligating and transecting the cystic artery and cystic duct.
4. Transection of the gastrocolic ligament, right to the second segment of the duodenum, left to the splenic flexure, ligating and transecting 2-3 branches of the left gastric short vessels.
5. Dissection of the anterior lobe to the superior margin of the pancreas.
6. Transection of the lesser omentum below the liver, ligating and transecting the right gastric and greater omentum vessels at the vascular root.
7. Transection of the duodenum 3cm distal to the pylorus, with the distal end prepared for anastomosis.
8. Dissection of the left gastric vessels, ligating and transecting at the vascular root.
9. Removal of the gastric coronary vessels and lymph nodes along the lesser curvature to below the cardia.
10. Opening the anterior wall of the stomach, inserting the anastomotic tube of the stapler at 7cm above the tumor, and performing an end-to-end anastomosis between the posterior wall of the stomach and the duodenum.
11. Retraction of the stapler, clamping and transecting the stomach 2cm from the anastomosis with a 90-degree clamp, removing the specimen.
12. Suturing and reinforcing the anastomosis and the gastric stump.
13. Thorough hemostasis, lavage of the abdominal cavity, aspiration of exudate, placement of two negative pressure balls at the anastomosis, and layer-by-layer closure.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Common bile duct (胆总管), critical structures requiring protection during dissection.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in the dissection of the gallbladder and the anastomosis of the stomach and duodenum.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of careful dissection around the gallbladder to avoid bile duct injury.
- Proper handling of the anastomosis to prevent leaks and strictures.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Adequate lavage to reduce the risk of infection.

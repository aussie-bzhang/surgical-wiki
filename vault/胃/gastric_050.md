## Overview
Gastrectomy for distal gastric cancer, a common clinical scenario involving resection of the distal stomach with D2 lymph node dissection and reconstruction using Billroth II technique.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric antrum cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Large tumor in the gastric antrum, approximately 10*8cm, extending beyond the serosa, involving the pancreas and transverse colon mesentery, 1st, 3rd, 5th, 6th, 7th, 8th, and 12th group lymph nodes, which were fused into a mass. Omentum with palpable nodular metastases. Exploration of small intestine, peritoneum, pelvis, and liver were negative.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, after general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Midline upper abdominal incision, enter peritoneum as described.
2. Incise the gastrocolic ligament on the intestinal side, extending to the right to the hepatic flexure of the colon and to the inferior pole of the spleen.
3. Retract the transverse colon, strip the omentum, anterior leaf of the transverse colon mesentery, and the capsule of the pancreas. Clamp and ligate the right gastric artery and vein at the vascular root.
4. Clamp and transect the greater curvature of the stomach along the greater curvature of the stomach body, dissect the splenogastric ligament, and dissect the 4th and 6th group lymph nodes, retaining 2 branches of the short gastric vessels, protecting the spleen.
5. Dissect the lesser omentum below the liver, skeletonize the hepatoduodenal ligament, and dissect the 8th and 12th group lymph nodes.
6. Ligate and transect the right gastric artery at the root, mobilize the antrum of the stomach and the first and second segments of the duodenum, close the duodenum 3cm from the pylorus with a 55# side-to-side stapler, reinforce the distal end with 3-0 gastrointestinal suture, and clamp the gastric stump with a Kocher clamp.
7. Reflect the stomach body, carefully dissect the adhesions between the stomach and the pancreas, ligate and transect the coronary vessels along the lesser curvature of the stomach, and dissect the 1st, 3rd, 5th, and 7th, 9th group lymph nodes, reaching the right side of the esophagus.
8. Clamp the stomach body 7cm from the tumor margin with two Kocher clamps, close the remaining stomach body with a 90mm stapler, and anastomose the stomach with the jejunum 15cm from the Treitz ligament using a 25mm end-to-end stapler, with the input side on the lesser curvature. Withdraw the stapler and close the anastomosis with a 90mm stapler, reinforce with 3-0 gastrointestinal suture, and secure the anastomosis.
9. Thorough hemostasis, lavage the peritoneal cavity, aspirate the effluent, place a negative pressure drain below the liver and at the distal end of the duodenum, count instruments and sponges, and close the abdomen in layers. Operation completed.

## Overview
Gastric resection with esophagojejunostomy and splenectomy and pancreatic tail resection for gastric lymphoma. Key technical feature includes Roux-en-Y anastomosis.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric lymphoma
**Intraoperative:** Gastric lymphoma
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Diffuse infiltration of the gastric body, enlargement of the 5th, 6th, 7th, 8th, 11th lymph node groups and inferior pancreaticoduodenal lymph nodes, tumor involving the tail of the pancreas, spleen enlargement, approximately 10*8*6cm in size. No significant abnormalities found in liver and pelvis.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping, midline upper abdominal incision, enter peritoneal cavity.
2. Dissection of the gastrocolic ligament, right to the hepatic flexure of the colon, left to the inferior pole of the spleen, along with the anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon, stripped to the surface of the pancreas, opening the lesser omentum sac into the lesser curvature.
3. Transection and ligation of the right gastroepiploic artery at the root of the pylorus, clearance of surrounding lymph nodes.
4. Transection of the lesser omentum below the liver, right to the hepatoduodenal ligament, transection and ligation of the right gastric artery.
5. Transection of the duodenum 3 cm from the pylorus using a side-to-side stapler, and suture reinforcement.
6. Flip up the gastric body, transection and double ligature of the left gastric vessels at the root, separation along the lesser omentum to the lower esophagus.
7. Transection of the splenic colic, splenogastric, splenorenal, and splenodiaphragmatic ligaments, free the splenic pedicle, separate the involved tail of the pancreas and transect, ligate the splenic artery and vein, suture the residual pancreas.
8. Continue to separate along the greater curvature to the left of the cardia, transection of both vagus nerves; transection of the esophagus 3 cm above the cardia, removal of the specimen, insertion of the esophagus into a 25mm tube stapler and fixation with purse-string suture.
9. Transection of the jejunum 20 cm from the Treitz ligament, side-to-side anastomosis of the esophagus and distal jejunum (in front of the colon), closure of the jejunal residual end with a side-to-side stapler. The proximal jejunum is anastomosed to the jejunum 45 cm below the esophageal anastomosis; closure of the mesenteric gap.
10. Thorough hemostasis, lavage of the peritoneal cavity, aspiration of exudate, placement of three negative pressure drainage tubes at the anastomosis and duodenal residual end, verification of instruments and gauze, layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: stomach, spleen, pancreas, esophagus, jejunum. (胃, 脾, 胰腺, 食管, 空肠)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in lymph node clearance and anastomosis.

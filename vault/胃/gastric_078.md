## Overview
Gastric cancer resection with esophagogastrojejunostomy, a complex procedure involving the stomach, esophagus, and jejunum, performed for advanced gastric cancer.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Gastric mass located in the corpus, extending from the cardia to the angle of the stomach, infiltrative-ulcerative type, involving the serosa. Stiffness of the entire gastric wall. Lymph nodes in groups 1, 3, 5, and 7 around the stomach were enlarged.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Median upper abdominal incision for abdominal access.
2. Dissection of the gastrocolic ligament, extending to the right to the hepatic flexure of the colon and to the inferior pole of the spleen, with the anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon剥离至 the surface of the pancreas. Open the lesser omentum囊 and enter the lesser curvature side.
3. Transection and ligation of the right gastroepiploic artery below the root of the pylorus, with lymph node dissection around it.
4. Transection of the lesser omentum below the liver, extending to the hepatoduodenal ligament, transection and ligation of the right gastric artery.
5. At 3 cm from the pylorus, transection of the duodenum with a 60mm clamp and sutured reinforcement.
6. Transection of the left gastroepiploic and short gastric arteries along the greater curvature until the left side of the cardia.
7. Upward flipping of the gastric body, transection and double ligation of the left gastric artery at its root. Separation along the lesser omentum, incision of the esophageal hiatus and bilateral peritoneum, freeing the lower esophagus. Transection of the esophagus 3 cm above the cardia, removal of the specimen, insertion of a 25.5mm tube-shaped anastomosis stapler and fixed with purse-string suture.
8. Transection of the jejunum 20 cm from the Treitz ligament, the esophagus and distal jejunum anastomosed end-to-side with the stapler. The distal jejunum was closed with a 60mm clamp. The proximal jejunum was anastomosed end-to-side manually 50 cm below the esophageal anastomosis (in front of the colon); closure of the mesenteric gap.
9. Thorough hemostasis, lavage of the abdominal cavity, placement of two negative pressure drainage tubes above and below the anastomosis, layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen, completion of the operation.

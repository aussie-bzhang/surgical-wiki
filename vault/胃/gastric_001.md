## Overview
Gastric cancer resection with esophagointestinal Roux-en-Y anastomosis, performed for gastric cancer.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor size approximately 10*6*4cm, extending from the cardia to the lesser curvature of the gastric angle. Infiltrative ulcerative type, hard consistency, involving the serosa. Lymph nodes in the 1st, 2nd, 3rd, 5th, 7th, and 8th groups around the stomach were significantly enlarged. Liver showed small nodular sclerosis. No other abnormalities found. Exploration of biliary tract, small intestine, colon, peritoneum, pelvis, and omentum negative. Intraoperative bleeding about 200ml. Surgery was difficult but went smoothly.

## Operative Steps
1. Laying the patient in supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline laparotomy.
3. Dissection of the gastrocolic ligament, mobilization to the hilum of the spleen and the hepatic flexure of the colon.
4. Opening the anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon to the serosa of the pancreas below the pylorus.
5. Identification and ligation of the right gastroepiploic artery, followed by lymph node dissection of the 5th and 6th groups.
6. Dissection of the hepatoduodenal ligament and gastrohepatic ligament, lymph node dissection of the 8th, 9th, and 12th groups, ligation and transection of the right gastric artery at the root.
7. Sequential ligation and transection of the gastric coronary artery along the lesser curvature, opening the gastropancreatic fold, transection of the left gastric artery at the root, and mobilization to the cardia.
8. Transection of the duodenum 2cm from the pyloric canal, closure of the distal end.
9. Sequential ligation and transection of the left gastroepiploic and short gastric arteries, lymph node dissection of the 2nd and 4th groups, mobilization of the gastric body, transection of the posterior gastric vessels, and opening of the esophageal hiatus.
10. Mobilization of the esophagus 4cm above the cardia, transection with a purse-string suture, and removal of the specimen for pathological examination.
11. Construction of the esophagointestinal Roux-en-Y anastomosis using a circular stapler.
12. Closure of the mesenteric defect and insertion of drains.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Common bile duct (胆总管), esophagus, small intestine, lymph nodes

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included the large size and infiltrative nature of the tumor, which required careful dissection and mobilization.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of careful lymph node dissection.
- Techniques for mobilizing the stomach and esophagus.
- Proper construction of the esophagointestinal anastomosis.

## Complication Prevention
- Adequate hemostasis.
- Proper placement of drains.

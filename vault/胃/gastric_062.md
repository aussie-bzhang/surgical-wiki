## Overview
Gastrectomy with pancreatectomy and splenectomy for gastric cancer, involving extensive resection and reconstruction.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Gastric cancer with tumor extending from cardia to gastric angle along the greater curvature, invasion of the tail of the pancreas; enlarged lymph nodes in the omentum and groups 3, 4, 7, 8, 10; negative exploration of liver, gallbladder, small intestine, colon, and pelvis.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping, midline upper abdominal incision, peritoneal cavity explored.
2. Dissection of gastrocolic ligament, right to the hepatic flexure of the colon, left to the inferior pole of the spleen, with the anterior leaf of the transverse colon mesentery, stripping to the surface of the pancreas; opening of the lesser omentum囊 into the lesser curvature.
3. Transection and ligation of the right gastroepiploic artery below the pylorus, clearance of surrounding lymph nodes.
4. Transection of the lesser omentum below the liver, right to the hepatoduodenal ligament, transection and ligation of the right gastric artery; transection of the duodenum 3 cm from the pylorus with a 60mm clamp, and suture reinforcement.
5. Transection of the left gastroepiploic and short gastric vessels along the greater curvature to the left of the cardia, upward flipping of the gastric body, transection and double ligature of the left gastric vessels at the root; upward dissection along the lesser omentum, left incision of the esophageal hiatus and bilateral peritoneum, freeing the lower esophagus, transection of both vagus nerves 3 cm above the cardia.
6. Dissection of the hepatoduodenal ligament, transection of the common hepatic artery, freeing the superior margin of the pancreas and portal vein.
7. Transection of the splenocolic and splenorenal ligaments, freeing the spleen and tail of the pancreas.
8. Suturing and ligating the splenic artery and vein, transection of the tail of the pancreas and spleen, suturing the pancreatic duct, U-shaped suture of the pancreatic wound.
9. Insertion of the 25.5mm tube-shaped anastomosis stapler into the anvil of the esophageal stump and fixation with purse-string suture; transection of the jejunum 20 cm from the Treitz ligament, anastomosis of the esophagus and distal jejunum with the stapler, closure of the jejunal stump with a 60mm clamp. Anastomosis of the proximal jejunum with the distal jejunum 40 cm below the esophageal anastomosis (in front of the colon); closure of the mesenteric gap.
10. Thorough hemostasis, lavage of the peritoneal cavity, placement of three negative pressure drainage balls above and below the anastomosis and in the splenic fossa; layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen, completion of the operation.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: stomach, pancreas, spleen, common bile duct (胆总管), portal vein (门静脉), hepatic artery (肝固有动脉), vagus nerves (迷走神经).

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in extensive resection and reconstruction, including handling of the pancreas and spleen, and ensuring secure anastomosis.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of careful dissection and lymph node clearance.
- Management of complex vascular structures during resection.
- Techniques for safe and effective reconstruction.

## Complication Prevention
- Thorough hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Adequate lavage to reduce the risk of infection.
- Secure anastomosis to prevent leaks.

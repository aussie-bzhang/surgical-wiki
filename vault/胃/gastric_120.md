## Overview
Gastric cancer resection with D2 lymphadenectomy and cholecystectomy for a patient with gastric cancer, gallbladder stones, and left renal cyst. Key technical feature includes D2 lymphadenectomy and Billroth I anastomosis.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer, gallbladder stones, left renal cyst
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer, gallbladder stones, left renal cyst
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Gastric mass located at the gastric angle near the anterior wall, ulcerative infiltrative type, approximately 3*3cm in size; gallbladder size 8*4cm, containing one stone, cystic duct diameter 0.5cm, common bile duct diameter 0.5cm; left renal cyst size about 8*6cm, aspirated clear cyst fluid about 8*6cm. No abnormalities found in liver, spleen, pancreas, and peritoneal cavity.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia, supine position, routine disinfection and draping, midline upper abdominal incision.
2. Careful dissection of the胆囊三角, ligating and transecting the cystic duct 0.5cm from the common bile duct, transecting and ligating the gallbladder vessels, stripping the gallbladder from the gallbladder bed to the胆囊三角, transecting the cystic duct 0.5cm from the common bile duct, ligating and suture ligating the residual end, removing the gallbladder.
3. Transecting the gastrocolic ligament on the intestinal side, right to the second segment of the duodenum, left to 2-3 branches of the short gastric vessels at the splenic flexure and transecting and ligating them, separating the anterior lobe to the superior margin of the pancreas.
4. Transecting the lesser omentum below the liver, stripping the anterior lobe of the hepatoduodenal ligament and its lymphatic tissue.
5. Transecting and doubly ligating the right gastric and right gastroepiploic vessels at the vascular root; transecting the duodenum 3cm from the pylorus, placing the 28# anastomosis stapler head into the residual end in a purse-string fashion.
6. Flipping up the gastric body, transecting and doubly ligating the left gastric vessels at the vascular root; removing the gastric coronary vessels and lymphatic tissue on the lesser curvature side to the subcardia; placing the 28# anastomosis stapler in the gastric cavity, performing a side-to-end anastomosis between the posterior wall of the gastric body at the middle-third and the duodenum, transecting the gastric body with the side-side stapler, removing the specimen, and reinforcing the anastomosis and residual end with suture.
7. Opening the left renal cyst, protecting the left adrenal gland, aspirating the cyst fluid, and resecting the cyst wall tissue.
8. Thorough hemostasis, rinsing the abdominal cavity, aspirating the effusion, placing two negative pressure balls at the anastomosis and leading them out through the right upper abdominal puncture wound, closing the layers in sequence. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Gastric antrum, common bile duct, left renal cyst. (胃角，胆总管，左肾囊肿)

## Teaching Points
- D2 lymphadenectomy and Billroth I anastomosis are critical steps.
- Careful dissection and hemostasis to prevent postoperative complications.

## Complication Prevention
- Thorough hemostasis and proper anastomosis technique to prevent anastomotic leakage.

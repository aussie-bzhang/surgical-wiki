## Overview
Gastrectomy for atypical hyperplasia, a clinical context involving a superficial elevated lesion in the gastric antrum and posterior wall, with lymph node enlargement. Key technical feature includes radical subtotal gastrectomy with Billroth I anastomosis.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Atypical hyperplasia of the stomach
**Intraoperative:** Atypical hyperplasia of the stomach
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
A superficial elevated lesion of 1.0 cm diameter in the gastric antrum near the corpus, with lymph node enlargement in groups 1, 3, and 8. Exploration revealed no abnormalities in the liver, small intestine, colon, pelvis, or ascites.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping, midline upper abdominal incision, opening of the anterior gastric wall, marking of the lesion, closure of the anterior gastric wall.
2. Dissection of the gastrocolic ligament, division and ligation of the right and left gastric short vessels, and separation of the anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon to the superior margin of the pancreas.
3. Division and ligation of the right gastric vessels at the root, division of the lesser omentum below the liver, and division and ligation of the right gastric vessels at the root.
4. Division of the duodenum 3 cm distal to the pylorus, placement of the 28# anvil of the stapler and fixation.
5. Dissection of the left gastric vessels, removal of the gastric coronary vessels and lymphoid tissue on the lesser curvature.
6. Lymph node dissection of group 8.
7. Division of the stomach 4 cm above the tumor using a 55# side-to-side stapler, removal of the specimen.
8. Opening the anterior wall of the residual stomach, insertion of the stapler tube, side-to-side anastomosis of the posterior wall of the gastric body and the duodenum, closure of the anterior wall of the residual stomach, and reinforcement of the seromuscular layer at the anastomosis site.
9. Hemostasis, lavage of the peritoneal cavity, aspiration of effusion.
10. Placement of two negative pressure balls at the anastomosis site, extraction through a stab wound in the upper right abdomen.
11. Counting sponges and instruments, closure of the incision in layers.
12. Completion of the procedure.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the gastric antrum, posterior gastric wall, and common bile duct (胆总管). Structures requiring protection are the splenic flexure and the pancreas.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered include the dissection of the gastrocolic ligament and the lymph node dissection. They were managed with careful dissection and meticulous hemostasis.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of careful dissection around the lymph nodes to avoid injury.
- The technique of using the stapler for anastomosis and the importance of reinforcement.
- The management of potential complications such as bleeding and leakage.

## Complication Prevention
- Bleeding: Adequate hemostasis during dissection and anastomosis.
- Leakage: Reinforcement of the anastomosis site with seromuscular layer.

## Overview
Palliative gastrectomy for gastric antrum cancer, involving subtotal gastrectomy with Billroth II anastomosis and pre-colonic approach.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric antrum cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric antrum cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located in the posterior wall of the gastric antrum, mass type, approximately 10*8*9CM, infiltrative growth, mucosal involvement, 1.4CM ulcer with well-preserved surrounding mucosa. Tumor invades the transverse mesocolon, duodenum, and inferior margin of the pancreas, with dense adhesions. No metastasis or lesions were found in the liver, gallbladder, spleen, esophagus, or intestines in the abdominal cavity.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, after general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline upper abdominal incision, enter peritoneum, explore as above.
3. Elevate the transverse colon, strip the omentum and anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon, transect the gastrocolic ligament, extend to the right to the hepatic flexure and to the left to the inferior pole of the spleen.
4. Clamp and transect the right gastroepiploic artery and vein at the vascular root.
5. Clamp and transect the gastro splenic ligament along the greater curvature of the stomach, retaining 2 branches of the short gastric vessels, protecting the spleen.
6. Strip and dissect the hepatoduodenal ligament, mobilize the second end of the duodenum.
7. Transect and ligate the right gastric vessel; transect the duodenum 3cm distal to the pylorus, bury the distal end and suture it in full thickness.
8. Sharp and blunt dissection at the junction of the tumor and the adhesion with the transverse mesocolon and pancreas, mobilize the tumor. Control bleeding at the surgical site.
9. Elevate the gastric body, open the gastropancreatic fold, resect the tumor on the surface of the pancreas.
10. Transect and ligate the first branch of the left gastric vessel at the level of the first branch.
11. At 15cm distal to the Treitz ligament, puncture the jejunum with a 28F anvil of the stapler and fix it with a purse-string suture. Open a hole in the distal stomach and insert the long handle of the stapler, perform an end-to-side anastomosis between the posterior wall of the stomach and the jejunum via the transverse colon. Cut the stomach body with a 75mm, 55mm side-to-side stapler about 5cm from the tumor edge, remove the specimen (including the puncture site).
12. Carefully control bleeding at the residual site, suture the seromuscular layer in an interrupted manner, and reinforce the anastomosis with several sutures.
13. Close the mesenteric defect.
14. Thoroughly control bleeding, irrigate the abdominal cavity, aspirate the effluent, place a negative pressure ball at the anastomosis and distal duodenal stump.
15. Count instruments and sponges, close the abdomen in layers.
16. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: stomach, colon, duodenum, pancreas. (胆总管)
> Danger zones: areas of dense adhesions.
> Structures requiring protection: spleen, pancreas.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in mobilizing the tumor due to dense adhesions and protecting the spleen and pancreas.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of careful mobilization around the tumor to avoid injury to adjacent structures.
- Technique of performing a safe and secure anastomosis.
- Importance of thorough hemostasis and abdominal irrigation.

## Complication Prevention
- Thorough hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Adequate irrigation to reduce the risk of postoperative infection.

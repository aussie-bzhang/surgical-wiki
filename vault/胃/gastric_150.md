## Overview
Gastrectomy for gastric cancer, a common clinical scenario involving the resection of the gastric antrum and cardia, with anastomosis of the stomach to the duodenum.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric antrum cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric antrum cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Gastric antrum mass located on the greater curvature, approximately 3*3cm2 in size, extending beyond the serosa. Lymph nodes 3, 5, and 8 palpable. Liver and pelvic (-).

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline upper abdominal incision.
3. Dissection of the gastrocolic ligament, right to the second segment of the duodenum, left to the upper 2-3 branches of the left gastric vessels at the splenic flexure, and ligation.
4. Dissection of the preperitoneal leaf of the transverse colon to the dorsal aspect of the pancreas.
5. Dissection of the hepatoduodenal ligament and lymphoid tissue.
6. Ligation and double-clamping of the right gastric and right gastroepiploic vessels at the vascular pedicle.
7. Transection of the duodenum 2cm distal to the pylorus, with a purse-string suture and 28# anvil of the anastomotic stapler.
8. Dissection of the greater curvature and ligation of the left gastric vessels.
9. Dissection of the coronary vessels and lymphoid tissue on the lesser curvature to the subcardia.
10. Opening the anterior gastric wall, inserting the long tube of the stapler, and anastomosing the posterior wall of the stomach 6cm above the tumor with the duodenal stump.
11. Removal of the specimen after stapling and reinforcement of the anastomosis with a layer of serosal muscle.
12. Hemostasis, lavage of the peritoneal cavity, aspiration of effusions, placement of negative pressure balls at the anastomosis, and layer-by-layer closure.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Common bile duct (胆总管), gastric cardia, gastric antrum

## Technical Challenges
Difficult dissection of the hepatoduodenal ligament and lymph node dissection.

## Teaching Points
- Proper dissection of the hepatoduodenal ligament and lymph node dissection.
- Technique of anastomosis with the stapler.

## Complication Prevention
- Adequate hemostasis.
- Proper lavage of the peritoneal cavity.

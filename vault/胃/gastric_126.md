## Overview
Gastrectomy for distal gastric cancer, a common surgical procedure for treating gastric cancer, involves resection of the distal stomach and reconstruction of the gastrointestinal tract. Key technical features include lymph node dissection and anastomosis techniques.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
A 5cm infiltrative ulcerous mass on the anterior wall of the gastric antrum, hard in consistency, extending beyond the serosa. Lymph nodes in groups 1, 7, and 12 were enlarged. A 0.5cm nodule was visible on the distal gastric body wall. Negative exploration of the liver and pelvis.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia, supine position, routine disinfection and draping. Midline upper abdominal incision.
2. Dissection of the gastrocolic ligament, right to the second segment of the duodenum, left to the upper 2-3 branches of the short gastric vessels at the splenic flexure, and ligation.
3. Dissection of the anterior leaf of the lesser omentum below the liver, and stripping of the hepatoduodenal ligament and lymphatic tissue.
4. Ligation and double ligation of the right gastric and right gastroepiploic vessels at the vascular root; transection of the duodenum 3cm distal to the pylorus, with the distal end made into a purse-string suture and inserted into the 28# anastomosis stapler head.
5. Dissection of the anterior leaf of the greater omentum below the liver, and stripping of the hepatoduodenal ligament and lymphatic tissue.
6. Ligation and double ligation of the left gastric vessel at the vascular root; removal of the coronary vessels and lymphatic tissue on the lesser curvature to below the cardia; insertion of the 28# anastomosis stapler into the gastric lumen, side-to-end anastomosis of the posterior wall of the gastric body at the middle-third near the antrum with the duodenum, side-side anastomosis with the stapler, removal of the specimen, and reinforcement suture of the anastomosis and distal end.
7. Hemostasis, lavage of the peritoneal cavity, aspiration of exudate, placement of two negative pressure balls at the anastomosis and extraction through a stab wound in the right upper quadrant, layer-by-layer closure. Completion of the operation.

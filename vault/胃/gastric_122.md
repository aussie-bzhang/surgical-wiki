## Overview
Gastric cancer resection with Billroth I reconstruction in a patient with gastric antrum cancer and lymph node involvement.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric antrum cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric antrum cancer confirmed with lymph node involvement
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Gastric antrum mass, approximately 1.5 x 1.0 cm in size, ulcerative type; lymph node enlargement in group 8; negative exploration of liver and pelvis.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia and supine position; routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline upper abdominal incision; findings as described.
3. Dissection of the gastrocolic ligament, right to the second segment of the duodenum, left to the upper 2-3 branches of the left gastric vessels and ligation.
4. Separation of the anterior leaf of the lesser omentum and dissection of the lymphatic tissue within the hepatoduodenal ligament.
5. Ligation and division of the right gastric and right gastroepiploic vessels at the vascular root, and dissection of group 6 lymph nodes.
6. Dissection along the hepatogastric ligament below the liver, and dissection of No.12a lymph nodes along the hepatic artery.
7. Division of the right gastric vessel at the root and dissection of group 5 lymph nodes, extending to the common hepatic artery and dissection of group 8 lymph nodes.
8. Division of the duodenum 3 cm distal to the pylorus with a purse-string suture; placement of the 28# anastomosis device head.
9. Raising the gastric body, ligation and division of the left gastric vessel at the vascular root; removal of the coronary vessels and lymphatic tissue on the lesser curvature to the subcardia; placement of the 28# anastomosis device in the gastric cavity, side-to-end anastomosis of the posterior wall of the gastric body near the middle-third with the duodenum.
10. Side-to-side anastomosis of the anastomosis device, removal of the specimen, and reinforcement suture of the anastomosis and residual duodenal stump.
11. Hemostasis, lavage of the peritoneal cavity, aspiration of exudate; placement of two negative pressure balls at the anastomosis site, extracted through a stab wound in the right upper abdomen; closure in layers. Completion of the procedure.

- Importance of lymph node dissection in gastric cancer surgery.
- Technique of side-to-end anastomosis with an anastomosis device.

## Complication Prevention
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper placement of anastomosis device to prevent leakage.

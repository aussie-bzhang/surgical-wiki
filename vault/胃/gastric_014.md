## Overview
Gastric resection with esophagojejunostomy and pancreatectomy for advanced gastric cancer, involving extensive resection of the stomach, esophagus, pancreas, spleen, and sigmoid colon.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Advanced gastric cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
The stomach presented as a革囊胃 with thickened, rigid walls and roughened, thickened mucosa. A 2.0cm hard ulcer was found on the lesser curvature of the stomach body. The tumor extended beyond the serosa, with thickened tissue and multiple nodules at the body of the pancreas and splenic hilum. The tumor invaded the mesentery and bowel wall of the sigmoid colon, forming a 3*2cm mass. The bowel mucosa was normal. No significant abnormalities were found in the exploration of other organs.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Median upper abdominal incision.
2. Dissection of the gastrocolic ligament, extending to the right to the hepatic flexure and to the left to the inferior pole of the spleen. Stripping of the anterior leaf of the transverse colon mesentery to the surface of the pancreas. Opening of the lesser curvature of the lesser omentum.
3. Division and ligation of the right gastric vessels at the root. Division and ligation of the duodenum 3cm from the pylorus.
4. Division and ligation of the left gastric vessels and short gastric vessels along the greater curvature to the left of the cardia.
5. Mobilization of the stomach body, division and double ligature of the left gastric vessels at the root. Mobilization of the lower esophagus, division of the esophagus 3cm above the cardia.
6. Division and mobilization of the splenic hilum, division and double ligature of the splenic artery and vein, division of the body of the pancreas.
7. Division of the sigmoid colon 5cm from the tumor, resection of the splenic flexure, and hand-sewn end-to-end anastomosis of the transverse and sigmoid colon.
8. Division of the jejunum 20cm from the Treitz ligament, end-to-side anastomosis of the esophagus and distal jejunum, and side-to-side anastomosis of the jejunal stump.
9. End-to-side anastomosis of the proximal jejunum 60cm below the esophagus吻合口.
10. Closure of mesenteric gaps, thorough hemostasis, lavage of the abdominal cavity, placement of three negative pressure drainage tubes, count of instruments and sponges, and layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included extensive adhesions, significant bleeding, and the need for precise anastomotic techniques.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of meticulous dissection and hemostasis to prevent postoperative complications.
- The technique of performing complex anastomoses with high precision.
- The management of extensive adhesions and bleeding during surgery.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Adequate lavage of the abdominal cavity to reduce the risk of infection.
- Proper anastomotic technique to prevent leaks and strictures.

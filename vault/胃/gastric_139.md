## Overview
Gastric cancer resection with D2 lymph node dissection and Billroth I reconstruction in a patient with gastric cancer located in the corpus near the angle of His.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located in the corpus near the angle of His, diameter approximately 3*2cm, ulcerative type, tumor not penetrating the serosa. Lymph nodes in groups 1, 3, 6, 7, and 8 were found to be enlarged. No liver or pelvic metastasis was observed.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Median upper abdominal incision.
2. Dissection of the gastrocolic ligament, right to the hepatic flexure of the colon, left to the splenic flexure, and 2-3 branches of the left gastric vessels were ligated and divided.
3. Dissection of the anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon to the superior margin of the pancreas.
4. Incision of the anterior wall of the stomach to locate the lesion and mark it.
5. Division and double ligature of the right gastric and right gastroepiploic vessels at the vascular root; division of the duodenum 2cm distal to the pylorus, the distal end was placed in a 25.5mm anvil of the stapler and fixed.
6. Division of the lesser omentum below the liver, and dissection of the anterior leaf of the hepatoduodenal ligament and its lymphatic tissue.
7. Upward mobilization of the stomach, division and double ligature of the left gastric vessels; removal of the gastric coronary vessels and lymphatic tissue on the lesser curvature until below the cardia.
8. Clamping and dividing the stomach 5cm from the tumor with two 60mm clamps, removing the specimen. After opening the anterior wall of the remaining stomach, the long tube of the stapler was inserted, and anastomosis was performed between the posterior wall of the greater curvature of the stomach and the duodenum. After removing the stapler, the anastomosis and the distal stomach were sutured in full thickness with absorbable 3-0 suture.
9. Hemostasis, lavage of the peritoneal cavity, aspiration of effluent, placement of two negative pressure balls at the anastomosis site, and sutured out layer by layer.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: stomach, lymph nodes, duodenum, common bile duct (胆总管).

## Overview
Gastric cancer resection with esophagogastrojejunostomy, a complex procedure involving the stomach, esophagus, and jejunum for the treatment of gastric cancer.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a mass in the gastric fundus, extending from the cardia to the lesser curvature of the gastric angle, measuring 8*4cm, with infiltrative ulcerative type and invasion of the serosa. Exploration of the liver, gallbladder, pancreas, spleen, small intestine, colon, and peritoneal cavity showed no abnormalities.

## Operative Steps
1. Position the patient in supine position, under general anesthesia, and perform routine disinfection and draping. Make an upper midline incision to enter the abdomen.
2. Separate the gastrocolic ligament, extending to the right to the hepatic flexure of the colon and to the inferior pole of the spleen, along with the anterior leaf of the transverse colon mesentery,剥离至 the surface of the pancreas. Open the lesser omentum囊 and enter the lesser curvature side.
3. At the root of the pancreatic head and under the pylorus, transect and ligate the right gastroepiploic vessels, and clear the surrounding lymph nodes.
4. Transect the lesser omentum below the liver, extending to the hepatoduodenal ligament on the right, and transect and ligate the right gastric vessels.
5. At 3cm from the pylorus, use a 60mm clamp to transect the duodenum and suture it for reinforcement.
6. Along the greater curvature, transect the left gastroepiploic and short gastric vessels until the left side of the gastric fundus and cardia.
7. Flip up the gastric body, transect and ligate the left gastric vessels at the root, and separate upwards along the lesser omentum. Cut open the esophageal hiatus and the peritoneum on both sides, free the lower esophagus, and transect the vagus nerves on both sides. At 3cm above the cardia, transect the esophagus and remove the specimen. Place a 25.5mm tube-shaped anastomosis stapler's anvil and fix it with purse-string suture.
8. At 20cm from the Treitz ligament, transect the jejunum, and anastomose the esophagus with the distal jejunum using the stapler. Close the distal jejunum with a 60mm clamp. Perform an end-to-side anastomosis between the proximal jejunum and the jejunum 50cm below the esophageal anastomosis (in front of the colon); close the mesenteric gap.
9. Thoroughly hemostasis, irrigate the abdominal cavity, place two negative pressure balls above and below the anastomosis, close the abdomen in layers, and complete the operation.

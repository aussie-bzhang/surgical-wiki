## Overview
Gastric cancer resection with Roux-en-Y gastrojejunostomy and liver wedge resection. A complex procedure involving extensive dissection and reconstruction of the upper gastrointestinal tract.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer confirmed with lymph node involvement and liver metastasis
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located on the lesser curvature of the stomach body, approximately 5cm in diameter, infiltrative ulcerative type, extending beyond the serosa. Lymph nodes in groups 1, 3, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, and 12 were enlarged and fused, resected. A 1cm hard nodule in the left lobe of the liver was resected. No metastatic lesions were found in the peritoneal cavity and pelvis. Negative ascites. Operation was difficult but successful.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Median upper abdominal incision.
2. Dissection of the gastrocolic ligament, right to the hepatic flexure, left to the inferior pole of the spleen, with the anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon. Open the lesser omentum囊 and enter the lesser curvature.
3. Transection and ligation of the right gastroepiploic artery at the root of the pancreatic head, clearing surrounding lymph nodes.
4. Transection of the lesser omentum below the liver, right to the hepatoduodenal ligament, transection and ligation of the right gastric artery.
5. Transection of the gastroepiploic artery and short gastric artery along the greater curvature to the left of the cardia, freeing the lower esophagus.
6. Transection of the esophagus 3cm above the pylorus, removal of the specimen, placement of a 25.5mm circular stapler anvil and fixation with purse-string suture.
7. Transection of the jejunum 20cm from the Treitz ligament, anastomosis of the esophagus to the distal jejunum with the stapler, closing the jejunal stump with a 60mm clamp. Anastomosis of the proximal jejunum to the distal jejunum 50cm below the esophageal anastomosis (colonic antimesenteric).
8. Resection of the liver tumor with sufficient margin and sutured closure.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: stomach, esophagus, jejunum, liver, lymph nodes. (胆总管)

Difficulties encountered included extensive lymphadenectomy and liver resection.

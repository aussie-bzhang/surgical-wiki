## Overview
Gastric cardia resection for gastric cancer, involving radical resection of the upper stomach. Key technical feature includes lymph node dissection and esophagogastrostomy.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located at the gastric fundus near the posterior wall of the cardia, infiltrative ulcerative type, diameter 4.0cm, hard, prone to bleeding upon palpation, infiltrating into the serosal layer. Lymph nodes in the 1st and 4th groups were enlarged. Exploration of liver, small intestine, colon, pelvis, and abdominal wall were all negative.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia successful, routine disinfection and draping. Abdominal exploration via an upper midline incision.
2. Full exposure of the gastric fundus and lower esophagus. Dissection and transection of the gastrocolic ligament at the transverse colon, extending to the right to the hepatic flexure of the colon and to the inferior pole of the spleen, transecting the splenocolic ligament.
3. Lifting the transverse colon, stripping the anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon to the superior margin of the pancreas, preserving the right gastroepiploic vessels, and transecting the greater omentum at the avascular area of the gastric greater curvature. Free the left side of the cardia, transecting and ligating the left gastroepiploic vessels along the greater curvature of the stomach, transecting and ligating the short gastric vessels upwards, protecting the spleen.
4. Clearing the fat and lymphatic tissue around the hepatoduodenal ligament, skeletonizing the hepatoduodenal ligament, transecting the lesser omentum from below, preserving the right gastric vessels, and clearing the lesser omentum to the body of the stomach.
5. Transecting the stomach body at the middle segment with a side-to-side stapler (55mm original and components), adding a 3-0 gastrointestinal suture around the distal end; flipping the stomach body upwards, dissecting the 7th, 8th, and 9th lymph node groups, transecting and ligating the left gastric vessels at the root, and dissecting the 11th and 10th lymph node groups along the superior margin of the pancreas.
6. Freeing the lower esophagus to the diaphragmatic hiatus (about 8cm), incising the esophageal hiatus and both sides of the peritoneum, transecting the bilateral vagus nerves, placing a purse-string suture 3cm above the tumor margin, transecting the esophagus, removing the specimen, and inserting a 28F tube-shaped stapler anvil into the esophageal end.
7. Opening the anterior wall of the residual stomach, inserting a 28F tube-shaped stapler, transecting the esophagogastrostomy about 2cm from the gastric end, reinforcing the anastomosis with 3-0 gastrointestinal suture.
8. Suturing the opening of the anterior wall with 3-0 gastrointestinal suture, flipping the greater curvature omentum to cover it, achieving serosalization.
9. Thorough hemostasis, rinsing the abdominal cavity, aspirating the effluent, placing two negative pressure balls behind the anastomosis, another puncture hole for drainage, copper绿假单胞菌10 vials + Zinostat 6 vials, verifying instruments and sponges, and closing the abdomen in layers.

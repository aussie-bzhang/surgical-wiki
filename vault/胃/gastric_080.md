## Overview
Gastrectomy with esophagogastrojejunostomy and jejunostomy is a complex procedure for the treatment of gastric cancer, involving resection of the stomach and reconstruction of the gastrointestinal tract.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
A mass was found on the lesser curvature of the stomach, extending from the cardia to the angle of the stomach, with a diameter of 4cm. The mass was infiltrative-ulcerative and involved the peritoneal surface. Lymph nodes in the 1st, 2nd, 3rd, and 7th groups around the stomach were enlarged. No liver, pelvic, or ascites were found.

## Operative Steps
1. The patient was placed in a supine position under general anesthesia. The abdomen was accessed via a midline upper abdominal incision.
2. The gastrocolic ligament was separated, extending to the hepatic flexure of the colon on the right and to the inferior pole of the spleen on the left. The anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon was also stripped to the surface of the pancreas.
3. The right gastroepiploic artery was transected and ligated at the root of the pancreatic head, with lymph nodes cleared around it.
4. The lesser omentum was transected below the liver, extending to the hepatoduodenal ligament on the right, and the right gastric artery was transected and ligated.
5. The duodenum was transected and closed 3cm from the pylorus using a 90mm clamp, and sutured for reinforcement.
6. The left gastroepiploic and short gastric arteries were transected along the greater curvature until the fundus and left side of the cardia.
7. The stomach body was flipped up, the left gastric artery at the root was transected and doubly ligated. The lesser omentum was separated upwards, and the esophagus was freed below the esophageal hiatus and transected 3cm above the cardia.
8. The esophagus was anastomosed to the distal jejunum using a 25.5mm circular stapler, and the jejunal stump was closed with a 90mm clamp. The proximal jejunum was anastomosed to the jejunum 50cm below the esophageal anastomosis (in front of the colon).
9. The mesenteric gap was closed, and a jejunostomy was performed. The abdomen was thoroughly irrigated, and two negative pressure drainage tubes were placed above and below the anastomosis. The abdomen was closed in layers, and the procedure was completed.

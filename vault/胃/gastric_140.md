## Overview
Gastric cardia cancer resection with pyloroplasty, a surgical procedure for the treatment of gastric cardia cancer, involving resection of the tumor and reconstruction of the pylorus.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric body cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric body cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings: A 2cm ulcerous lesion on the posterior wall of the gastric body near the cardia, not penetrating the serosa. No lymph node enlargement around the stomach. Negative exploration of liver, gallbladder, small intestine, colon, and pelvis. Negative ascites.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia successful, routine disinfection and draping, midline upper abdominal incision around the umbilicus for abdominal exploration.
2. Raise the transverse colon, open the gastrocolic ligament, strip the anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon to the superior margin of the pancreas, retain the right gastric and right gastroepiploic vessels, and transect the greater curvature of the stomach at the avascular area of the gastric body. Dissect along the greater curvature of the stomach, transect and ligate the left gastroepiploic vessels, branch by branch.
3. Open the gastro splenic ligament, clamp and transect the left gastric vessels, free the greater curvature of the stomach upwards to the right side of the cardia, and dissect the 1st and 3rd groups of lymph nodes.
4. Open the esophageal hiatus of the diaphragm, free the lower esophagus, transect the esophagus about 2 cm above the cardia, and place the 25# tube-shaped anastomosis stapler钉头备用.
5. Clamp and transect the middle of the gastric body 7-8 cm from the cardia with a 90mm clamp, remove the specimen, and reinforce the gastric stump with 3-0 absorbable suture. Open the anterior wall of the stomach, place the stapler, and anastomose with the esophagus. Suture the serosal layer around the anastomosis. Close the anterior wall of the stomach with 3-0 absorbable suture.
6. Perform pyloroplasty by longitudinal incision and transverse suture at the pylorus.
7. Stop bleeding, irrigate the abdominal cavity, remove all effusion, place two negative pressure balls around the anastomosis, lead out through abdominal stab wounds, count instruments and sponges, close the abdomen in layers, and end the operation.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: gastric cardia, pylorus. (胃贲门，幽门)

## Teaching Points
- Importance of careful dissection around the cardia and pylorus to preserve adjacent structures.
- Proper use of staplers for anastomosis to ensure a secure and leak-proof connection.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Adequate irrigation to reduce the risk of infection.

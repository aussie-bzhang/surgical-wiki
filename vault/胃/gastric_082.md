## Overview
Gastric cancer resection with upper gastric resection and adhesion release in a patient with severe adhesions and infiltrative lesion in the cardia.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Severe adhesions in the upper abdomen, primarily in the right upper quadrant. Infiltrative lesion in the cardia measuring 4*4cm, not penetrating the serosa. Lymph nodes in the 1st and 2nd groups were enlarged. Exploration of liver, pelvis, and small intestine-colon were negative.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia successful. Routine disinfection and draping. Midupper abdominal midline incision, layer-by-layer abdominal exploration.
2. Release of upper abdominal adhesions, lifting the transverse colon, separation and transection of gastrocolic ligament, right to the hepatic flexure of the colon, left to the inferior pole of the spleen. Transection of splenocolic ligament, stripping the anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon to the superior margin of the pancreas, exposing the posterior wall of the stomach.
3. Retain the right gastroepiploic vessels, and transect the greater curvature of the stomach at the avascular area of the corpus. Separate the lesser curvature of the stomach, resect the 2nd and 4th group lymph nodes. Free up to the left side of the cardia, protecting the spleen.
4. Open the lesser omentum, free the hepatoduodenal ligament, ligate and transect the branches of the right gastric artery along the lesser curvature of the stomach, flip the stomach body, ligate and transect the left gastric artery at the root. Separate upwards along the lesser omentum to the cardia, clamp and transect the vagus nerve, free the esophagus upwards 3cm, clamp and transect 3cm from the cardia with a purse-string clamp. The distal stomach is placed into a 25.5# tube-shaped anastomosis stapler.
5. The middle segment of the stomach is transected with a 90mm clamp, and the specimen is removed. The distal stomach residual end is reinforced with silk thread.
6. The anterior wall of the distal stomach is fenestrated, the long handle of the 25.5# tube-shaped anastomosis stapler is inserted, and an end-to-end anastomosis is performed with the esophagus. The anastomosis is reinforced with 3-0 absorbable suture. Close the fenestration.
7. Adequate hemostasis, wash the abdominal cavity, place 2 negative pressure drainage tubes around the anastomosis and lead them out through abdominal wall puncture wounds. Count sponges and instruments, close the abdomen, and the operation is completed.

Severe adhesions, infiltrative lesion in the cardia, and the need for precise anastomosis.

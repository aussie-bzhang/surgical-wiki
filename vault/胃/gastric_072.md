## Overview
Gastric cancer resection with esophagojejunostomy is a complex procedure performed for advanced gastric cancer. It involves the removal of the entire stomach and the creation of a new alimentary tract by connecting the esophagus to the jejunum.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Grossly, a large ulcerative tumor (Boremann 3 type) measuring approximately 15*8cm^2 was found on the lesser curvature of the stomach body, infiltrating to the cardia and antrum. The tumor extended to the peritoneal surface, most prominently at the posterior wall, adhering to the superior margin of the pancreas. Lymph nodes in the perigastric area and groups 7, 8, 9, 11, and 12 were enlarged. No obvious metastatic lesions or ascites were found in the abdominal cavity. An 8*6*4cm^3 gallbladder with a 3cm mixed stone was also seen, with a gallbladder duct diameter of 0.4cm and 0.6cm.

## Operative Steps
1. The patient was placed in a supine position under general anesthesia. The abdomen was accessed via a midline upper abdominal incision.
2. The gastrocolic ligament was separated, extending to the right to the hepatic flexure of the colon and to the inferior pole of the spleen. The gastrocolic omentum was stripped to the surface of the pancreas.
3. The right gastroepiploic artery was transected and ligated at the root of the pylorus, with lymph node dissection.
4. The lesser omentum was transected below the liver, extending to the hepatoduodenal ligament, with lymph node dissection and胆囊三角解剖.
5. The gallbladder was dissected off the gallbladder bed and removed.
6. The hepatic artery and common hepatic artery were dissected, with lymph node dissection, and the right gastric artery was ligated at its root.
7. The duodenum was transected 3cm from the pylorus and sutured for reinforcement.
8. The greater curvature of the stomach was transected, and the left gastric artery was transected and ligated at its root.
9. The stomach was flipped up, and the left gastric artery was transected and ligated.
10. The esophagus was transected 3cm above the cardia, and the esophagus was anastomosed to the distal jejunum using a 25.5mm circular stapler.
11. The splenic artery was dissected, and the 11th group of lymph nodes were dissected.
12. The jejunum was transected 20cm from the Treitz ligament, and the esophagus was anastomosed to the distal jejunum.
13. The proximal jejunum was anastomosed to the distal jejunum 50cm below the esophagojejunostomy (colonic antimesenteric).
14. A jejunostomy was created.
15. Hemostasis was achieved, the abdomen was irrigated, and drains were placed above and below the anastomosis.
16. The abdomen was closed in layers.

## Technical Challenges
- Adhesion to the pancreas
- Enlarged lymph nodes
- Large tumor size

- Proper anastomotic technique
- Drainage of the abdomen

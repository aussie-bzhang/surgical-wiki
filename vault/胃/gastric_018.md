## Overview
Gastric cardia cancer with extended radical resection and pylorus formation, performed via a thoracoabdominal incision, in a patient with significant lymph node involvement and tumor infiltration into the esophagus.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cardia cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cardia cancer confirmed with significant lymph node involvement
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located at the gastric cardia, invading the lower esophagus, approximately 4*3cm in size, infiltrative ulcerative type, extending beyond the serosa, causing 3/4 circumference stenosis of the esophagus. Grossly, there was significant lymph node involvement (Groups 1, 2, 3, 4, 7, 8, 9, 16). No significant abnormalities were found in the liver, biliary tract, small intestine, colon, omentum, abdominal wall, or pelvic cavity. Intraoperative blood loss was approximately 500ml, no blood transfusion was required, and the patient was admitted to the SICU postoperatively.

## Operative Steps
1. Right lateral decubitus position after successful general anesthesia. Routine disinfection and draping. Upper midline abdominal incision for abdominal exploration.
2. Thoracotomy via the 5th and 6th intercostal space. Clamping and transecting the diaphragm to the esophageal hiatus, careful hemostasis. Protection of the pericardium, lower margin of the left lung, and spleen. Mobilization of the lower esophagus for approximately 10cm.
3. Dissection and transection of the gastrocolic ligament, extending to the hepatic flexure on the right and the inferior pole of the spleen on the left. Transection of the splenocolic ligament.
4. Mobilization of the transverse colon and stripping of the anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon to the superior margin of the pancreas, preserving the right gastric vascular pedicle. Transection of the greater omentum at the avascular area of the greater curvature of the stomach. Dissection along the greater curvature of the stomach to transect the left gastric vascular pedicle and清扫 the 2nd and 4th LN groups.
5. Clamping and transecting the left gastric vessels individually, mobilizing the stomach body along the greater curvature of the stomach body, and freeing the left side of the cardia.
6. Dissection and transection of the 1st and 3rd LN groups, transection of the lesser omentum below the liver, and upward mobilization of the stomach body. Transection and double ligature of the left gastric vascular pedicle at the root of the stomach. Dissection upwards along the lesser omentum, clamping and transecting the vagus nerve, transection of the esophagus at 6.0cm above the cardia. Skeletonization of the hepatoduodenal ligament and dissection of the 7th, 8th, 9th, and 16th LN groups.
7. Two 55cm side-to-side staplers were used to transect the stomach body, removing the specimen. A 25mm circular stapler was used for the gastroesophageal anastomosis, with interrupted full-thickness suture reinforcement around the anastomosis, and completion of the pylorus formation with seromuscular layer.
8. Thorough hemostasis, lavage of the thoracic and abdominal cavities, interrupted suture of the diaphragm. Placement of a chest drain in the left pleural cavity and an additional incision for drainage. Placement of a negative pressure ball behind the anastomosis and drainage through a left abdominal wall incision.
9. Instrument and suture count confirmed twice. Layered closure of the abdomen. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: gastric cardia, esophagus, stomach, lymph nodes. (胆总管)
> Danger zones: esophageal hiatus, splenic hilum.
> Structures requiring protection: pericardium, left lung lower margin, spleen.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included significant lymph node involvement, tumor infiltration into the esophagus, and the need for careful hemostasis and anastomotic technique.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of thorough lymph node dissection in gastric cardia cancer.
- Technique of performing a safe and effective gastroesophageal anastomosis.
- Management of significant blood loss during surgery.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Adequate lavage of the surgical field to reduce the risk of infection.
- Proper anastomotic technique to prevent anastomotic leakage.

## Related Procedures
- [[Gastrojejunostomy]] - used as a bypass in cases of esophageal obstruction.

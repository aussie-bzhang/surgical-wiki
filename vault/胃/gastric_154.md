## Overview
Gastric cardia resection for gastric cancer with pyloroplasty, involving extensive dissection and lymph node clearance.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer.
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer confirmed.
**Pathological:** Not documented.

## Intraoperative Findings
Gastric cardia mass, extending to the lower esophagus margin, approximately 3cm in diameter, ulcerative infiltrative type. Negative findings in liver, gallbladder, small intestine, colon, and pelvis.

## Operative Steps
1. Patient placed in supine position under general anesthesia. Abdominal exploration via upper midline incision around the umbilicus.
2. Transverse colon retracted, gastrocolic ligament opened, and mesocolon anterior leaf stripped to the superior margin of the pancreas. Right gastric and right gastroepiploic vessels preserved, and greater curvature of the stomach transected at avascular area.
3. Gastro splenic ligament opened, short gastric vessels clamped, cut, and ligated. Left gastric artery and vein cleared, and stomach mobilized to the right of the cardia, protecting the spleen.
4. Lesser omentum opened, hepatogastric and hepatoduodenal ligaments dissected, and celiac trunk followed to the root. Lymph nodes in group 8 cleared, and left gastric artery at the root ligated and cut, group 7 lymph nodes cleared.
5. Greater curvature mobilized to the right of the cardia, and 1st and 3rd group lymph nodes cleared. Stomach transected 7-8cm from the cardia with 90# clamp, and distal stomach reinforced with 3-0 absorbable suture.
6. Esophageal hiatus opened, distal esophagus mobilized, and transected 3cm above the cardia with 28# tube anastomosis stapler.
7. Anastomosis performed with the tube anastomosis stapler, and pyloroplasty performed at the pylorus.
8. Hemostasis achieved, peritoneal cavity irrigated, and negative pressure drains placed around the anastomosis. Instruments and sponges counted, abdomen closed in layers.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Gastric cardia, esophagus, pylorus. (胃贲门，食管，幽门)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties in mobilizing the stomach and clearing lymph nodes.

## Teaching Points
- Proper mobilization of the stomach and lymph node clearance.
- Technique of esophagogastrostomy and pyloroplasty.

## Complication Prevention
- Adequate hemostasis.
- Proper anastomosis technique.

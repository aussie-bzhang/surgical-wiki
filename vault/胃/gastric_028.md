## Overview
Gastrectomy for gastric cancer, a D2+ radical resection with Billroth I anastomosis, in a patient with infiltrative ulcerative gastric cancer located in the antrum of the stomach.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Lesion located on the lesser curvature of the gastric antrum, approximately 5cm in diameter, presenting as an infiltrative ulcer. Tumor invades beyond the serosa. Lymph nodes in groups 1, 5, 6, 7, 8, and 12 are enlarged. No obvious metastatic lesions found in the abdominal and pelvic organs. Negative ascites.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Midline upper abdominal incision. Exploration as described.
2. Transection of the gastrocolic ligament on the intestinal side, right to the bulb of the duodenum, left to 2-3 branches of the left gastric short vessels at the splenic flexure, and ligation.
3. Dissection below the pylorus to expose the right gastric网膜血管, double ligature at the root, and lymph node dissection of group 6.
4. Dissection below the liver to transect the lesser omentum, stripping the anterior leaf of the hepatoduodenal ligament and lymphoid tissue, exposing the right gastric vessel, proper hepatic artery, and celiac trunk. Double ligature at the root of the right gastric vessel and lymph node dissection of groups 7, 8, and 12.
5. Transection of the duodenum 3cm distal to the pylorus, purse-string suture and placement of a 28.5mm anvil of the anastomotic stapler, and fixation. Raising the gastric body, ligature and suture at the vascular root, and transection.
6. Clearing of the coronary vessels and lymphoid tissue on the lesser curvature (groups 1, 3) to the subcardia.
7. Clamping the gastric body 5cm above the tumor with two Kocher clamps and opening, the remaining gastric body 60mm clamped and transected. Removal of specimen, placement of the long tube of the stapler in the opening of the residual stomach, anastomosis of the posterior wall of the gastric body mid-distal third with the duodenal stump, and reinforcement of the serosal layer of the anastomosis.
8. Hemostasis, lavage of the abdominal cavity, aspiration of exudate, placement of two negative pressure drains at the anastomosis.
9. Counting sponges and instruments, closing the abdomen in layers, completion of the operation.

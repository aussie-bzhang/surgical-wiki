## Overview
Total gastrectomy with esophagojejunostomy Roux-en-Y, spleenectomy, pancreatectomy, and special hepatic segmentation for recurrent gastric cancer with liver metastasis.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer recurrence, liver metastasis
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer recurrence, liver metastasis
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Recurrent tumor above the original anastomosis, severe adhesions with porta hepatis, diameter 5cm, penetrating into subserosal layer, extending to the pancreas. White metastatic nodule on the surface of the right liver, diameter 2cm. Enlarged lymph nodes in the hepatoduodenal ligament. No findings in the pelvic cavity.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Midline upper abdominal incision.
2. Separation of residual stomach and porta hepatis adhesions, transection of proximal and distal small intestines near the original anastomosis, approximately 5cm away. Separation of residual gastrocolic ligament.
3. Transection of esophagus 3cm above the cardia, removal of specimen, placement of 25mm tube-shaped anastomosis stapler and fixation with purse-string suture.
4. Transection of splenocolic ligament, incision of lateral peritoneum of spleen, dissection to the left of the inferior mesenteric vein. Separation and double ligature of splenic artery, transection of splenic vein on the left of the inferior mesenteric vein. Transection of the body and tail of the pancreas, interrupted U-shaped suture of the residual end, removal of specimen.
5. Anastomosis of esophagus and distal jejunum, closure of jejunal end, anastomosis of proximal jejunum with distal jejunum 60cm below the esophagus anastomosis, closure of mesenteric gap.
6. Excision of liver metastatic nodule, suture of liver tissue.
7. Thorough hemostasis, lavage of peritoneal cavity, aspiration of effusion, placement of two negative pressure balls from below the esophagus anastomosis to the splenic fossa, verification of instruments and sponges, layer-by-layer closure of abdomen.

## Overview
Gastric cancer resection with Billroth II anastomosis, antecolic reconstruction, and D2 lymphadenectomy. Involves resection of the stomach, duodenum, and surrounding structures.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Lesion located in the antrum near the pyloric canal anterior wall, diameter 2cm, infiltrative lesion, not penetrating the serosa. No obvious enlarged lymph nodes around the stomach. Liver, small intestine, colon, and pelvic cavity are negative.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Midline upper abdominal incision.
2. Dissection of the gastrocolic ligament on the intestinal side, right to the inferior duodenal bulb, left to the splenic flexure with 2-3 branches of the left gastric short vessels, and ligation.
3. Dissection of the pre-colonic mesentery to the superior margin of the pancreas.
4. Ligation and division of the right gastric artery at the root of the gastroepiploic artery below the pylorus; division of the lesser omentum below the liver, extending to the anterior leaf of the hepatoduodenal ligament, and division of the right gastric artery at the root, ligation.
5. Division of the duodenum 4cm distal to the pylorus with a 60mm clamp, and sutured in full thickness.
6. Dissection of the corpus of the stomach, division and ligation of the left gastric artery at the root, and removal of the gastric coronary vessels and lymphoid tissue on the lesser curvature up to the subcardia.
7. Incision of the stomach wall 5cm above the lesion on the greater curvature, and division of the stomach wall with a 60mm clamp on the lesser curvature, removal of the specimen.
8. Opening of the mesenteric side of the small intestine 20cm distal to the Treitz ligament, insertion of the bottom needle of a 25mm tube-shaped anastomosis stapler, and anastomosis of the posterior gastric wall to the small intestine in front of the colon with a 25mm stapler. Reinforcement of the serosal layer of the anastomosis with a 60mm clamp.
9. Hemostasis, lavage of the peritoneal cavity, aspiration of effluent, placement of two negative pressure balls from the duodenal stump to the anastomosis, and sutured in layers.

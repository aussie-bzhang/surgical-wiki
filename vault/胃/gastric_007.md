## Overview
Distal gastrectomy for gastric cancer, with Billroth II reconstruction, performed in a patient with a 3cm infiltrative ulcerative tumor involving the serosa and peritoneum, with lymph node metastasis. Key technical feature includes lymph node dissection and anastomosis.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric antrum cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric antrum cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located at the lesser curvature of the gastric angle, diameter 3cm, infiltrative ulcerative type, involving the serosa and peritoneum, pancreas capsule, scattered enlarged lymph nodes around the stomach. Negative findings in small intestine, peritoneum, pelvis, and liver.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, after general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Midline upper abdominal incision, abdominal exploration as above.
2. Incise the gastrocolic ligament on the intestinal side, extending to the hepatic flexure of the colon on the right and the inferior pole of the spleen on the left. Elevate the transverse colon, strip the greater omentum, the anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon, and the peritoneum of the pancreas. Clamp and ligate the right gastric artery and vein at the vascular root.
3. Clamp and cut the gastro splenic ligament along the greater curvature of the stomach body, dissect the 4th and 6th group lymph nodes, preserve 2 branches of the short gastric vessels, and protect the spleen.
4. Dissect the lesser omentum below the liver, skeletonize the hepatoduodenal ligament, and dissect the 8th group lymph nodes.
5. Ligate and cut the right gastric artery at the root, mobilize the antrum of the stomach and the first and second segments of the duodenum. Close the duodenum 3cm from the pylorus with a 60mm clamp, reinforce the stump with silk suture and bury it. Clamp the gastric stump with a Kocher clamp.
6. Elevate the stomach body, carefully separate the adhesion between the stomach and the pancreas, ligate and cut the coronary vessels along the lesser curvature of the stomach upwards, and dissect the 1st, 3rd, 5th, and 7th group lymph nodes, reaching the right side of the esophagus. Cut the left gastric artery at the root, ligate and suture the stump, and dissect the 7th and 9th group lymph nodes.
7. Clamp the stomach body 7cm above the tumor margin with two Kocher clamps, close the remaining stomach body with a 90mm clamp, and anastomose the stomach with the jejunum in front of the colon using a 28.5mm end-to-end anastomosis stapler. The output side is to the lesser curvature, and the stapler is withdrawn and closed with a 60mm clamp again. Reinforce the anastomosis with 3-0 gastrointestinal suture and secure the anastomosis, place the gastric tube.
8. Thorough hemostasis, wash the abdominal cavity, remove the effusion, place two negative pressure balls in front and behind the anastomosis under the liver, count instruments and gauze, close the abdomen in layers. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: stomach, duodenum, spleen, pancreas, lymph nodes. (胆总管)
> Danger zones: pancreas capsule, splenic hilum.
> Structures requiring protection: spleen, pancreas.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered include mobilization of the pancreas and spleen, lymph node dissection, and anastomosis.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of lymph node dissection in gastric cancer.
- Techniques for mobilizing the pancreas and spleen.
- Proper anastomosis technique to prevent leakage.

## Complication Prevention
- Adequate hemostasis.
- Proper anastomosis technique.
- Postoperative monitoring for leakage and infection.

## Overview
Gastric cancer resection with Billroth I reconstruction and D2 lymph node dissection. Involves extensive dissection and lymphadenectomy around the stomach and adjacent organs.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located at the lesser curvature of the gastric angle, approximately 5 cm in diameter, ulcerative infiltrative type, involving the serosal layer. Lymph nodes in the 8th group around the stomach were enlarged. No metastasis to liver, pelvis, small intestine, or colon.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Abdominal exploration via an upper midline incision around the umbilicus.
2. Dissection of the gastrocolic ligament on the intestinal side, right to the bulb of the duodenum, left to the 2-3 branches of the left gastric short vessels at the splenic flexure, and ligation.
3. Dissection and exposure of the right gastric artery below the pylorus, double ligature at the root, and lymphadenectomy of the 6th group.
4. Dissection and transection of the lesser omentum below the liver, right to the prehepatic part of the hepatoduodenal ligament and its lymphatic tissue, and exposure of the right gastric artery. Transection and double ligature at the root of the right gastric artery, and lymphadenectomy of the 8th group.
5. Transection of the duodenum 3 cm distal to the pylorus, anvil of the 28.5mm anastomosis stapler placed and fixed in the duodenal stump. The stomach body is flipped up, the left gastric artery at the root is transected and doubly ligated, and lymphadenectomy of the 7th group.
6. Clearing of the coronary vessels and lymphatic tissue on the lesser curvature (groups 1, 3) to below the cardia.
7. Transection of the gastric body 7 cm above the tumor with a 60 mm clamp, the remaining gastric body stump placed into the anastomosis stapler tube. Anastomosis of the posterior wall of the gastric body at the middle third with the duodenal stump using a 60 mm clamp.
8. Closure of the gastric body stump with the clamp, removal of the specimen, and reinforcement of the anastomosis and stump with a full-thickness muscular layer.
9. Hemostasis, lavage of the abdominal cavity, aspiration of effluent, placement of two negative pressure balls at the anastomosis, and layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: stomach, gallbladder, duodenum, colon, spleen, liver, lymph nodes. (胆总管)
> Danger zones: areas with high risk of injury or bleeding.
> Structures requiring protection: blood vessels, nerves, and lymphatic channels.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered include extensive lymphadenectomy, anastomosis technique, and hemostasis.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of thorough lymphadenectomy.
- Technique of anastomosis and reinforcement.
- Management of bleeding and complications.

## Complication Prevention
- Adequate hemostasis.
- Proper anastomosis technique.
- Monitoring for early signs of complications.

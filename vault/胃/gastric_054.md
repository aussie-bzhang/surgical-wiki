## Overview
Gastric cancer resection with Billroth I reconstruction and D2 lymph node dissection. Involves extensive dissection and lymphadenectomy.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located on the lesser curvature of the gastric antrum, approximately 2.5cm in diameter, ulcerative infiltrative type, involving the serosal layer. Marked lymph node enlargement around the stomach. No blood transfusion required during surgery.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Midline upper abdominal incision around the umbilicus.
2. Transection of the gastrocolic ligament on the intestinal side, right to the hepatic flexure of the colon, left to the splenic flexure of the colon. Dissection of the anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon to the superior margin of the pancreas. Transection of the gastropancreatic fold, transection and ligation of the gastrosplenic ligament, retaining the upper 2-3 branches.
3. Bilateral ligation and transection of the right gastric and right gastroepiploic vessels at the vascular root. Transection of the duodenum 2cm distal to the pylorus, the distal end made into a purse-string suture and placed into the anvil of the 25# anastomosis stapler and fixed.
4. Transection of the lesser omentum below the liver, dissection of the anterior leaf of the hepatoduodenal ligament and dissection of the lymphoid tissue within.
5. The stomach body is flipped upwards, the left gastric vessel is ligated and sutured at the vascular root and transected, clearing the coronary vessels and lymph nodes on the lesser curvature to the subcardia.
6. After opening the anterior wall of the stomach, the long tube of the stapler is inserted, and an end-to-side anastomosis is performed between the posterior wall of the gastric body at the middle upper part of the greater curvature and the duodenum. Properly place the gastric tube, use two 55mm side-to-side staplers 2cm from the anastomosis to clamp and transect the stomach, remove the specimen for pathological examination, and suture the anastomosis and the distal gastric stump in a running full thickness manner.
7. Thorough hemostasis, lavage the peritoneal cavity, aspirate the effluent fluid, place two negative pressure balls at the anastomosis and lead them out through the incision in the right upper abdomen, count sponges and instruments, and close the abdomen in layers.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: stomach, gallbladder, duodenum, spleen, pancreas, lymph nodes. (胆总管)
> Danger zones: vessels and lymph nodes.
> Structures requiring protection: vessels and nerves.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered include extensive dissection, lymphadenectomy, and anastomosis. Managed by careful dissection, adequate hemostasis, and precise anastomosis technique.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of careful lymphadenectomy in staging and prognosis.
- The technique of anastomosis and the management of potential leaks.
- The significance of adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative complications.

## Complication Prevention
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper anastomosis technique to prevent leaks and strictures.
- Regular follow-up to monitor for complications.

## Overview
Gastric resection with esophagojejunostomy for gastric cancer, involving extensive resection of the stomach and reconstruction of the alimentary tract.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Large gastric ulcer (nature pending)
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Gastric mass with ulcerative infiltration, involving the entire stomach body along the lesser curvature, extending to the peritoneal surface. The entire stomach is leathery and involves the pancreatic capsule, anterior leaf of the transverse colon, and splenic capsule. A 1.5 cm nodule is palpable in the liver. Enlarged lymph nodes are found around the stomach and transverse colon mesentery. Pelvic exploration is unremarkable.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Midline upper abdominal incision.
2. Dissection of the gastrocolic ligament, extending to the right to the hepatic flexure and to the left to the inferior pole of the spleen, with the anterior leaf of the transverse colon mesentery. Incision of the lesser sac.
3. Transection and ligation of the right gastric artery and vein at the root of the pancreatic head, with lymph node dissection.
4. Transection of the lesser omentum below the liver, extending to the hepatoduodenal ligament, transection and ligation of the right gastric artery.
5. Transection of the duodenum 2.5 cm from the pylorus, with purse-string suture.
6. Transection of the left gastric artery and short gastric vessels along the greater curvature to the left of the cardia.
7. Dissection of the stomach body superiorly, transection of the left gastric artery at the root, and dissection along the lesser omentum. Incision of the esophageal hiatus and peritoneum, mobilization of the lower esophagus, transection of the vagus nerves, and transection of the esophagus 3 cm above the cardia.
8. Placement of a 28.5 mm circular stapler anvil and fixation with purse-string suture.
9. Transection of the jejunum 20 cm from the Treitz ligament, side-to-side anastomosis of the esophagus and distal jejunum with a 28.5 mm stapler. Closure of the jejunal stump and reinforcement suture. Side-to-side anastomosis of the proximal jejunum 60 cm below the esophageal anastomosis (in front of the colon).
10. Closure of the mesenteric gap.
11. Hemostasis, lavage of the peritoneal cavity, aspiration of effluent, placement of a negative-pressure drain below the spleen at the level of the anastomosis, and another below the hepatic hilum at the level of the anastomosis, with incision and stab incision for drainage.
12. Counting instruments and sponges, and layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: stomach, esophagus, jejunum, pancreas, spleen, liver. (胆总管)
> Danger zones: pancreas, spleen, liver.
> Structures requiring protection: esophagus, jejunum.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in extensive resection and reconstruction, particularly in mobilizing the esophagus and jejunum.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of careful mobilization of the esophagus and jejunum.
- Proper handling of the pancreas and spleen during dissection.
- Technique of using circular staplers for anastomosis.

## Complication Prevention
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper reconstruction to prevent anastomotic leakage.

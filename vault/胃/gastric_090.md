## Overview
Gastrectomy with gastrojejunostomy for gastric cancer, involving resection of the upper part of the stomach and creation of a jejunostomy. Key technical feature includes esophageal transection and anastomosis.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located at the cardia, ulcerative infiltrative type, 4*3*3cm, involving serosa. Exploration revealed liver, pancreas, small intestine, colon, pelvis, abdominal wall, and no ascites.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia successful. Routine disinfection and draping. Median upper abdominal incision, enter peritoneum as described above; place gauze in the splenic fossa to elevate the spleen.
2. Retract the transverse colon, dissect and transect the gastrocolic ligament to the right to the hepatic flexure and to the left to the inferior pole of the spleen, transect the splenocolic ligament, strip the anterior leaf of the transverse colon mesentery to the superior margin of the pancreas, exposing the posterior wall of the stomach; transect the gastropancreatic fold.
3. Retain the right gastroepiploic vessels, and transect the greater curvature of the stomach at the avascular area of the gastric body. Dissect the stomach body along the greater curvature, mobilize the left side of the cardia, protecting the spleen.
4. Transect the short gastric vessels, dissect the stomach body along the greater curvature of the stomach base, mobilize the left side of the cardia.
5. Transect the lesser omentum below the liver, mobilize the hepatoduodenal ligament, carefully dissect and separate the enlarged lymph nodes from the left gastric vessels, transect and ligate the left gastric vessels at the root, and suture them.
6. Dissect upwards along the lesser omentum, mobilize the lower esophagus to the left, clamp and transect the vagus nerve, transect the esophagus 3cm above the cardia, and place the distal end of the esophagus into a 25.5# tube anastomosis stapler for anastomosis.
7. Clamp the middle and lower third of the stomach body 90mm with a clamp, remove the specimen, incise the anterior wall of the residual stomach, and place the tube anastomosis stapler for anastomosis of the residual stomach to the esophagus.
8. Perform a jejunostomy 20cm from the Treitz ligament, place a catheter into the distal jejunum, purse-string suture, fix to the abdominal wall, and puncture the wound to drain.
9. Thorough hemostasis, wash the peritoneal cavity, remove all effusion, place two negative pressure balls around the anastomosis, count instruments and gauze, close the abdomen in layers, and complete the operation.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Esophagus, stomach, gallbladder, pancreas, jejunum. (食管，胃，胆囊，胰腺，空肠)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in esophageal transection and anastomosis, management of the splenic flexure, and ensuring adequate resection of the tumor.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of careful mobilization around the spleen and esophagus.
- Proper handling of the left gastric vessels and lymph nodes.
- Technique for performing a safe and effective esophagogastrostomy.

## Complication Prevention
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper anastomotic technique to reduce the risk of anastomotic leakage.

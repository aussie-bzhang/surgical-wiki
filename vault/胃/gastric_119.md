## Overview
Gastrectomy for advanced gastric mucosal neoplasia, a clinical context involving a localized lesion in the gastric antrum, with key technical features including lymph node dissection and anastomotic technique.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Advanced gastric mucosal neoplasia
**Intraoperative:** Confirmed advanced gastric mucosal neoplasia (F08-2733)
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a localized lesion in the gastric antrum, 1.5 cm in diameter, with surrounding slight elevation and central depression. No enlarged lymph nodes were found around the stomach. The liver, small intestine, colon, and pelvis were all negative.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia and routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline upper abdominal incision.
3. Dissection of the gastrocolic ligament, right to the duodenal bulb and left to the splenic flexure, with ligation and division of 2-3 branches of the left gastric short vessels.
4. Dissection of the pretransverse mesocolon to the superior margin of the pancreas.
5. Division and double ligation of the right gastric artery at the root of the gastroepiploic artery below the pylorus.
6. Division and dissection of the lesser omentum below the liver, with division at the root of the right gastric artery and double ligation.
7. Division of the duodenum 3 cm distal to the pylorus, with the distal end placed in a 28.5mm anastomotic stapler and fixed.
8. Dissection of the greater curvature of the stomach, with division and double ligation of the left gastric artery at the root.
9. Dissection of the coronary vessels and lymphatic tissue along the lesser curvature to the subcardial region.
10. Opening the anterior wall of the stomach and inserting the long tube of the stapler at 5 cm above the lesion, performing an end-to-end anastomosis between the posterior wall of the stomach and the duodenum.
11. Removal of the stomach specimen after stapling and using a 90mm clamp to close and divide the stomach 2 cm from the anastomosis. Reinforcement of the anastomosis with a layer of seromuscular layer.
12. Thorough hemostasis, lavage of the peritoneal cavity, aspiration of effluent, placement of two negative pressure balls at the anastomosis site, and suture closure in layers.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the gastric antrum, pylorus, duodenum, gallbladder, liver, spleen, colon, and pelvis. (胆总管)
> The common bile duct (胆总管) requires protection during dissection.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included precise dissection around the pylorus and duodenum, and ensuring secure anastomosis.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of meticulous dissection around the pylorus and duodenum to avoid injury to adjacent structures.
- The technique of using a stapler for anastomosis and the need for reinforcement with a seromuscular layer.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Adequate lavage to reduce the risk of infection.

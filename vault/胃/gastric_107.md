## Overview
Gastric cancer resection with Billroth II reconstruction, antecolic anastomosis, and D2 lymph node dissection, combined with adhesion release. Involves extensive dissection and lymphadenectomy.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located on the lesser curvature of the stomach, approximately 4cm in diameter, infiltrative ulcerative type, extending beyond the serosa. Tumor involves the pancreatic capsule and mesentery of the transverse colon. Lymph nodes in groups 3, 5, 7, 8, and 9 are enlarged and fused into a mass, surrounding the celiac trunk, hepatic artery, and left gastric artery.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, after general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Midline upper abdominal incision, enter peritoneum, and release adhesions between small intestine and incision, and in the right upper abdomen.
2. Incise the gastrocolic ligament on the intestinal side, extending to the hepatic flexure on the right and to the inferior pole of the spleen on the left; elevate the transverse colon, strip the omentum, anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon, and pancreatic capsule, ligate and tie off the right gastric artery and vein at the vascular root.
3. Clamp and transect the gastro splenic ligament along the greater curvature of the stomach, dissect the 4th and 6th groups of lymph nodes, preserve 2 branches of the short gastric vessels, and protect the spleen.
4. Open the hepatoduodenal ligament and gastrohepatic ligament, dissect the 8th group of lymph nodes, ligate and transect the right gastric artery at the root, mobilize the pylorus, the first and second segments of the duodenum, close the duodenum 3cm from the pylorus with a 60mm clamp, reinforce the cut end with silk suture, and clamp the gastric stump with a Kocher clamp.
5. Ligate and transect the coronary vessels along the lesser curvature of the stomach, while dissecting the 1st, 3rd, 5th, 7th groups of lymph nodes, extending to the right side of the esophagus.
6. Clamp the stomach body with two Kocher clamps 7cm from the upper margin of the tumor, close the remaining stomach body with a 90mm clamp, and anastomose the stomach stump with the jejunum 20cm from the Treitz ligament using a 25.5mm anastomotic stapler, reinforce the anastomosis with gastrointestinal suture, and place a gastric tube.
7. Thorough hemostasis, wash the peritoneal cavity, remove effusion, place a negative pressure ball under the liver and at the duodenal stump, two negative pressure drains at the gastrojejunal anastomosis, and bring the incision out through a stab wound. Count instruments and sponges, close the abdomen in layers. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: stomach, pancreas, transverse colon, omentum, lymph nodes. (胆总管)
> Danger zones: celiac trunk, hepatic artery, left gastric artery.
> Structures requiring protection: spleen, short gastric vessels.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included extensive adhesions, extensive lymphadenectomy, and complex anastomosis.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of thorough lymphadenectomy in gastric cancer surgery.
- Techniques for safe mobilization of the pancreas and spleen.
- Proper anastomotic technique to prevent leaks.

## Complication Prevention
- Thorough hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Adequate drainage to prevent abscess formation.

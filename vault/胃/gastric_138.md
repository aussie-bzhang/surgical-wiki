## Overview
Gastric cancer resection with Billroth I anastomosis, performed for gastric antrum and pyloric canal tumors.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric antrum multiple ulcers.
**Intraoperative:** Gastric antrum multiple ulcers.
**Pathological:** Not documented.

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located at the gastric antrum and pyloric canal, elevated lesion, 3*2cm, tumor not penetrating serosa. Lymph nodes in the sixth group were enlarged. Exploration of liver and pelvis were negative.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline upper abdominal incision, findings as described.
3. Dissection of the gastrocolic ligament, right to the hepatic flexure of the colon, left to the splenic flexure with 2-3 branches of the left gastric vessels and ligation.
4. Dissection of the anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon to the superior margin of the pancreas.
5. Ligation and double ligation of the right gastric and right gastroepiploic vessels at the vascular root.
6. Transection of the duodenum 2cm distal to the pylorus, the distal end made into a purse-string and placed into a 28.5mm anastomosis stapler.
7. Dissection of the subhepatic omentum, and dissection of the anterior leaf of the hepatoduodenal ligament and lymphoid tissue.
8. Upward flipping of the gastric body, ligation and double ligation of the left gastric vessels at the vascular root.
9. Dissection of the coronary vessels and lymphoid tissue on the lesser curvature to the subcardia.
10. Opening the anterior gastric wall, inserting the long tube of the stapler at the tumor superior margin 7cm, anastomosis between the posterior wall of the stomach and the duodenum.
11. Removal of the stomach 3cm from the anastomosis with a 90mm stapler, specimen removed. Reinforcement of the anastomosis with a layer of serosal muscle.
12. Hemostasis, lavage of the peritoneal cavity, aspiration of effusion, placement of two negative pressure balls at the anastomosis, and layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: gastric antrum, pyloric canal, lymph nodes, common bile duct (胆总管).

## Technical Challenges
Not documented.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of identifying and preserving the common bile duct.
- Proper handling of the gastric and duodenal anastomosis.
- Adequate lymph node dissection for staging.

## Complication Prevention
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper anastomotic technique to prevent anastomotic leakage.

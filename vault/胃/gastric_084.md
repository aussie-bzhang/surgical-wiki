## Overview
Gastric cancer resection with total gastrectomy and esophagojejunostomy, including complex adhesion release, partial resection of transverse colon, and splenectomy, in a patient with residual gastric cancer.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Residual gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Residual gastric cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Original surgery was Billroth II, tumor occupies the entire residual stomach and invades the transverse colon and mesentery. Lower esophagus is soft. Liver, pelvis, and abdominal wall are negative. Small amount of ascites, clear boundary between tumor and pancreas.

## Operative Steps
1. Position the patient in supine position, under general anesthesia, and perform routine disinfection and draping. Enter the abdomen through the original upper abdominal incision.
2. Separate adhesions, and cut the proximal and distal small intestines 6 cm from the original anastomosis. Tie the transverse colon mesentery in bundles (protect the middle colic artery), and cut the transverse colon 6-8 cm from the margin of tumor invasion.
3. Cut the gastrocolic ligament on the intestinal side, lift the gastric body, and cut and ligate the left gastric vessels at the root, then cut and ligate the splenocolic and splenorenal ligaments, freeing the spleen, and cut and ligate the splenic artery and vein.
4. Incise the anterior peritoneum of the esophagus, cut the left and right vagus nerves, and fully mobilize the esophagus. Cut the esophagus 3 cm above the cardia, remove the specimen, and place the anvil of a 28F tube-shaped anastomosis stapler and fix it with purse-string suture.
5. Perform end-to-side anastomosis between the esophagus and the distal jejunum, close the distal jejunum in duplicate, and perform end-to-side anastomosis between the proximal jejunum and the jejunum 60 cm below the esophageal anastomosis. Close the mesenteric gap.
6. Perform end-to-end anastomosis between the proximal and distal ends of the transverse colon (EEA).
7. Stop bleeding, flush the abdomen, remove all effusions, place a negative pressure drain behind the esophagojejunostomy, and bring it out through a stab wound in the left upper abdomen. Place another negative pressure drain in front of the esophagojejunostomy, bringing it out from the right upper abdomen through the subhepatic space. Count instruments and sponges, and close the abdomen in layers.

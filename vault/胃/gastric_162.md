## Overview
Gastric cancer resection with Billroth II reconstruction and antecolic anastomosis. Involves resection of the stomach and reconstruction of the gastrointestinal tract.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located in the gastric antrum, approximately 8cm in diameter, infiltrative ulcerative type, involving the transverse colon.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, after general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Midline upper abdominal incision, peritoneal cavity exploration as above.
2. Incise the gastrocolic ligament on the intestinal side, extending to the hepatic flexure on the right and the inferior pole of the spleen on the left. Elevate the transverse colon, strip the omentum, the anterior leaf of the transverse colon mesentery, and the pancreas capsule, ligate and tie the right gastric artery and vein at the vascular root.
3. Clamp and cut the greater curvature of the stomach, retaining 2 branches of the short gastric vessels, protecting the spleen.
4. Open the hepatogastric ligament, ligate and cut the right gastric vessel at the root, mobilize the gastric antrum, the first and second segments of the duodenum, close the duodenum 3cm from the pylorus with a 60mm clamp, reinforce the cut end with a gastrointestinal suture, and clamp the gastric stump with a Kocher clamp.
5. At 5cm from the distal end of the infiltrated colon, perform a purse-string suture, place the anvil of a 28.5mm anastomosis stapler, open the anterior wall of the proximal 5cm of the transverse colon, place the long handle of the stapler, and anastomose with the distal end. Remove the stapler and close the anastomosis with a 90mm clamp and gastrointestinal suture.
6. Mobilize the gastric body upwards, cut and ligate the left gastric vessel, and suture it.
7. At approximately 7cm from the superior margin of the tumor, clamp the gastric body with two Kocher clamps, close and cut the remaining gastric body with a 60mm clamp, and remove the specimen.
8. At 20cm from the Treitz ligament, perform a purse-string suture on the upper jejunum, place the anvil of a 25.5mm stapler, release the clamp on the gastric stump, place the long handle of the stapler, anastomose the posterior wall of the stomach with the jejunum across the transverse colon, remove the stapler, close the anastomosis with a 60mm clamp and gastrointestinal suture, and place the stomach tube.
9. Thorough hemostasis, wash the peritoneal cavity, remove the effluent fluid, place a negative pressure ball under the liver and at the distal end of the duodenum, count instruments and gauze, close the abdomen in layers. Operation completed.

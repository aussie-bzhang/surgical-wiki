## Overview
Gastrectomy for gastric cancer, a clinical context involving advanced gastric cancer with significant invasion, key technical feature being the D2 lymph node dissection and Billroth II anastomosis.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located in the antrum, infiltrative ulcerative type, diameter 4cm, penetrating into the peritoneal cavity. Liver, pelvic (-).

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, after general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Midline upper abdominal incision, abdominal exploration as above.
2. Elevate transverse colon, strip omentum, anterior leaf of transverse mesocolon, and pancreatic capsule. Cut the gastrocolic ligament, extend to the right to the hepatic flexure of the colon and to the left to the inferior pole of the spleen.
3. Clamp and cut the right gastric artery and vein at the vascular root.
4. Clamp and cut the greater curvature of the stomach, dissect the 4th and 6th groups of lymph nodes, preserve 2 branches of the short gastric vessels, and protect the spleen.
5. Open the hepatoduodenal ligament and gastrohepatic ligament, dissect the 8th and 12th groups of lymph nodes, ligate and cut the right gastric vessel at the root, mobilize the antrum and the first and second segments of the duodenum.
6. At 2cm from the pylorus, clamp and cut the duodenum with a clamp, reinforce the cut end with a 1-0 silk suture, and clamp the gastric stump with a Quack clamp.
7. Clamp and cut the gastric tubular vessels along the lesser curvature, simultaneously dissect the 1st, 3rd, 5th, and 7th groups of lymph nodes, up to the right side of the esophagus.
8. At 20cm from the Treitz ligament, puncture the jejunum with a 28.5mm anvil of the stapler and fix it with a purse-string suture. Open a window on the anterior wall of the stomach and insert the stapler, anastomose the posterior wall of the stomach with the jejunum above the transverse colon, remove the stapler, clamp the tumor stomach with a domestic clamp, close the window with a 1-0 silk suture, and reinforce the anastomosis, place the gastric tube.
9. Thorough hemostasis, wash the abdominal cavity, aspirate the effluent, place a negative pressure ball under the liver and at the duodenal stump, another under the splenic fossa, count instruments and gauze, close the abdomen in layers.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: stomach, spleen, common bile duct, duodenum. (胆总管 (胆总管))

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in mobilizing the spleen and in the anastomosis of the stomach and jejunum.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of D2 lymph node dissection in gastric cancer surgery.
- The technique of Billroth II anastomosis and the management of the duodenal stump.
- The need for meticulous hemostasis and careful abdominal cavity washing.

## Complication Prevention
- Thorough hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Careful handling of the spleen to prevent splenic injury.
- Adequate anastomotic reinforcement to prevent anastomotic leakage.

## Overview
Gastric cancer resection with esophagogastrojejunostomy, a complex procedure involving the stomach, esophagus, and jejunum for the treatment of gastric cancer.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located at the cardia and corpus of the stomach, diffuse infiltrative type, extending beyond the serosa. Lymph nodes in group 8 palpable. No significant abnormalities found in other organs.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Median upper abdominal incision.
2. Dissection of the gastrocolic ligament, right to the hepatic flexure of the colon, left to the inferior pole of the spleen, including the anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon,剥离至胰腺表面.
3. Transection and ligation of the right gastroepiploic artery below the pylorus, clearance of surrounding lymph nodes.
4. Transection of the lesser omentum below the liver, right to the hepatoduodenal ligament, transection and ligation of the right gastric artery.
5. Transection of the left gastroepiploic and short gastric arteries along the greater curvature to the left of the cardia.
6. Upward flipping of the corpus, transection and double ligature of the left gastric artery at its root.
7. Dissection along the lesser omentum upward, incision of the esophageal hiatus and bilateral peritoneum, mobilization of the lower esophagus, transection of both vagus nerves 3 cm above the cardia.
8. Transection of the esophagus 3 cm above the cardia, removal of the specimen, insertion of a 25F tube-shaped anastomosis stapler and fixation with purse-string suture.
9. Transection of the jejunum 20 cm from the Treitz ligament, anastomosis of the esophagus and distal jejunum with the stapler, closure of the jejunal stump with a side-to-side stapler. Anastomosis of the proximal jejunum with the distal jejunum 40 cm below the esophageal anastomosis (in front of the colon); closure of the mesenteric gap.
10. Thorough hemostasis, lavage of the peritoneal cavity, aspiration of effluent, placement of two negative pressure balls below the anastomosis, verification of instruments and sponges, layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen.

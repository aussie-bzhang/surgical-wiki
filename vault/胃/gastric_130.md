## Overview
Gastrectomy for gastric cancer with major bleeding, involving resection of the greater curvature of the stomach and anastomosis of the duodenum and colon, with exploration of the liver, pancreas, and pelvis.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Upper gastrointestinal bleeding, gastric ulcer (malignant?).
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer with major bleeding, involvement of transverse colon.
**Pathological:** Not documented.

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located on the greater curvature of the stomach near the antrum, infiltrative ulcer with a diameter of about 6cm, involving serosa, extending to the middle segment of the transverse colon and mesentery. Active bleeding from the ulcer. No obvious metastasis in the liver, pancreas, or pelvis. No ascites.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping, midline upper abdominal incision.
2. Exploration reveals active bleeding from the ulcer, which is suture ligated and止血.
3. Dissection of the gastrocolic ligament, division and ligation of 2-3 branches of the left gastric short vessels.
4. Due to tumor involvement, the right and middle gastrocolic ligament is adherent to the middle segment of the transverse colon and greater curvature of the stomach, making it difficult to dissect, so the lesser curvature is processed.
5. Dissection of the subhepatic omentum, stripping of the anterior leaf of the hepatoduodenal ligament and lymphoid tissue.
6. Division and double ligature of the right gastric and greater omental vessels at the vascular root; division of the duodenum 3cm distal to the pylorus, with the distal end placed in a 28# anvil of the stapler and fixed.
7. Dissection of the greater curvature of the stomach, division and double ligature of the left gastric vessels; removal of the coronary vessels and lymphoid tissue on the lesser curvature until below the cardia; division of the stomach at the middle-third of the corpus, with the distal end sutured, leaving a small margin for anastomosis.
8. Division and ligation of the mesentery and vessels along the edge of the involved transverse colon mesentery, forming a fan-shaped resection of the involved mesentery, and resecting the involved transverse colon.
9. Removal of the specimen.
10. Placement of the distal stomach into the stapler tube, end-to-end anastomosis of the greater curvature with the duodenum, and retraction of the long tube, followed by double closure of the lesser curvature. Reinforcement of the anastomosis with a layer of serosal muscle.
11. Hand-sewn end-to-end anastomosis of the transverse colon, reinforced with several serosal muscle sutures. Closure of the mesenteric defect.
12. Thorough hemostasis, lavage of the abdominal cavity, aspiration of exudate, placement of a negative pressure ball at the gastric anastomosis (upper right), and two negative pressure balls at the upper and lower ends of the colonic anastomosis (right lower, left lower), all exiting through stab wounds, and layer-by-layer closure.

## Technical Challenges
Difficult dissection due to tumor involvement, management of active bleeding from the ulcer.

## Teaching Points

- Techniques for managing active bleeding and preventing postoperative complications.

## Complication Prevention
- Thorough hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper anastomotic techniques to prevent leaks.
- Early detection and management of postoperative complications.

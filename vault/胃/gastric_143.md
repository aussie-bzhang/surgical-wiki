## Overview
Gastrectomy for distal gastric cancer, a D2+ radical resection with Billroth I anastomosis, in a patient with significant lymph node involvement and no evidence of peritoneal dissemination.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer confirmed with significant lymph node involvement
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
A mass was found on the posterior wall of the gastric antrum, approximately 3.0*2.0cm in size, with an ulcerative type. Lymph node swelling in group 6. Negative exploration of the hepato-pelvic region.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia and supine position. Routine disinfection and draping. Median upper abdominal incision.
2. Dissection of the gastrocolic ligament, right to the second segment of the duodenum, left to the upper 2-3 branches of the left gastric vessels at the splenic flexure, and ligation.
3. Dissection of the anterior leaf of the lesser omentum under the liver, and stripping of the lymphatic tissue within the hepatoduodenal ligament.
4. Ligation and double-clamping of the right gastric and greater omentum vessels at the vascular root, and dissection of group 6 lymph nodes.
5. Dissection along the hepatogastric ligament below the liver, and dissection of No.12a lymph nodes along the hepatic artery. Ligation and dissection of the right gastric vessels at the root, and dissection of group 5 lymph nodes up to the common hepatic artery, followed by dissection of group 8 lymph nodes.
6. Transection of the duodenum 3cm distal to the pylorus with a purse-string suture, and placement of the 28# anastomosis device head.
7. Raising the gastric body, ligation and double-clamping of the left gastric vessels at the vascular root; removal of the coronary vessels and lymphatic tissue on the lesser curvature to the subcardia; placement of the 28# anastomosis device in the gastric cavity, and side-to-end anastomosis of the posterior wall of the gastric body at the middle-third with the duodenum. Side-side anastomosis device transection of the gastric body, removal of the specimen, and reinforcement suture of the anastomosis and residual end.
8. Thorough hemostasis, lavage of the peritoneal cavity, aspiration of exudate, placement of two negative pressure balls at the anastomosis site, and suture closure in layers. Completion of the procedure.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: stomach, lymph nodes, duodenum. (胆总管)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included significant lymph node involvement and the need for precise dissection around the vessels and lymph nodes.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of meticulous dissection around the lymph nodes and vessels to prevent complications.
- The technique of side-to-end and side-side anastomosis using the anastomosis device.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Adequate lavage to reduce the risk of infection.

## Overview
Gastric body resection (D2) for gastric body cancer, involving resection of the upper part of the stomach and regional lymph nodes.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric body cancer.
**Intraoperative:** Gastric body cancer confirmed.
**Pathological:** Not documented.

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings: Ulcerative lesion with a diameter of about 1.2 cm on the anterior wall of the gastric body near the lesser curvature. Surface covered with white fur. No abnormalities found in the liver, gallbladder, pancreas, small intestine, and colon.

## Operative Steps
1. Patient placed in supine position after successful general anesthesia. Routine disinfection and draping. Median upper abdominal incision made and abdomen explored.
2. Transverse colon elevated, gastrocolic ligament separated and transected, extending to the hepatic flexure on the right and the inferior pole of the spleen on the left. The splenocolic ligament was transected, and the anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon was stripped to the superior margin of the pancreas, exposing the posterior wall of the stomach. The gastropancreatic fold was transected.
3. The right gastroepiploic artery was preserved, and the greater curvature of the stomach was transected at the avascular area of the gastric body. The left gastroepiploic artery was transected along the greater curvature of the stomach.
4. The short gastric vessels were clamped and transected one by one, and the stomach body was separated along the greater curvature of the stomach. The left side of the cardia was freed, with attention to protecting the spleen.
5. The lesser omentum was transected below the liver, and the hepatoduodenal ligament was freed. The space between the enlarged lymph nodes and the left gastric artery was carefully dissected, and the left gastric artery was transected and ligated and sutured at the root. The omentum was separated upwards along the lesser omentum, and the distal esophagus was freed to the left. The vagus nerve was clamped and transected, and the esophagus was transected 2 cm above the cardia, with the distal end placed in a circular anastomosis stapler for anastomosis.
6. The stomach body was transected at the middle and lower third with a 90mm clamp, and the specimen was removed. The anterior wall of the residual stomach was incised, and a circular anastomosis stapler was inserted for anastomosis between the residual stomach and the esophagus. The serosal layer around the anastomosis was buried and sutured to reinforce the anastomosis.
7. Hemostasis was achieved, the abdomen was irrigated, and the effluent was aspirated. Two negative pressure balls were placed around the anastomosis. Instruments and sponges were counted and found to be correct. The abdomen was closed in layers, and the operation was completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: gastric body, stomach left artery, esophagus. (胃体，胃左动脉，食管)

## Technical Challenges
Not documented.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of preserving the right gastroepiploic artery.
- The careful dissection around the left gastric artery.
- The technique of esophagogastrostomy using a circular anastomosis stapler.

## Complication Prevention
- Adequate hemostasis.
- Proper placement of drains.
- Careful handling of the esophagus and stomach during anastomosis.

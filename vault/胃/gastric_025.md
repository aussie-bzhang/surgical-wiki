## Overview
Gastric cancer resection with Billroth II reconstruction and antecolic anastomosis in a patient with gastric cancer.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Posterior wall ulcerous mass of the gastric body approximately 2*1cm, not involving the serosa. Enlarged lymph nodes in the first group.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, after general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Midline upper abdominal incision, enter peritoneum as described.
2. Incise the gastrocolic ligament on the intestinal side, extending to the right to the hepatic flexure of the colon and to the inferior pole of the spleen.
3. Retract the transverse colon, strip the greater omentum, the anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon, and the capsule of the pancreas, ligate and tie the right gastric artery and vein at the vascular root.
4. Clamp and cut the gastro splenic ligament along the greater curvature of the stomach, dissect the 4th and 6th group lymph nodes, preserve 2 branches of the short gastric vessels, and protect the spleen.
5. Dissect the lesser omentum below the liver, and dissect the hepatoduodenal ligament, and dissect the 8th group lymph nodes along the common hepatic artery.
6. Ligate and cut the right gastric artery at the root, mobilize the antrum and the first and second segments of the duodenum, close the duodenum 3cm from the pylorus with a 55mm clamp, reinforce the distal end with 3-0 gastrointestinal suture for one week, and pack with a Kocher clamp.
7. Turn up the gastric body, ligate and suture the left gastric artery at the root, dissect the 7th group lymph nodes, and continue upwards to the right of the cardia, dissect the 1st and 3rd group lymph nodes.
8. Cut the stomach 6cm above the mass, and 20cm from the Treitz ligament, puncture the jejunum with a 25mm anvil of the end-to-end stapler and fix it with a purse-string suture, insert the long handle into the distal end of the stomach and anastomose the stomach with the jejunum in front of the colon, the proximal end to the greater curvature, withdraw the stapler and close the anastomosis with a 55mm clamp, reinforce with 3-0 gastrointestinal suture for one week, and reinforce the anastomosis, place the gastric tube.
9. Thorough hemostasis, wash the peritoneal cavity, aspirate the effluent, place a negative pressure ball under the liver and at the distal end of the duodenum, count instruments and gauze, close the abdomen in layers. Operation completed.

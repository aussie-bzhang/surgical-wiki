## Overview
Gastrectomy for gastric cancer, a clinical context involving a localized gastric antrum tumor, with key technical features including D2 lymph node dissection and Billroth II anastomosis.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric antrum cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric antrum cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located in the gastric antrum, approximately 3*2*1.5cm in diameter, with a prominent polypoid neoplasm, no obvious invasion of the muscular layer and serosa; olive-sized mixed gallbladder stone; no metastatic lesions found in the abdominal and pelvic cavities.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, after general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping; midline upper abdominal incision; abdominal exploration as described.
2. Elevate the transverse colon, strip the greater omentum, the anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon, and the capsule of the pancreas; transect the gastrocolic ligament, extending to the right to the hepatic flexure of the colon and to the inferior pole of the spleen.
3. Clamp and transect the right gastric artery and vein at the vascular root.
4. Clamp and transect the gastro splenic ligament along the greater curvature of the stomach, retaining 2 branches of the short gastric vessels, protecting the spleen.
5. Strip and dissect the hepatoduodenal ligament, and dissect the 12th group of lymph nodes.
6. High ligation and transection of the right gastric vessel; at 3cm distal to the pyloric canal, use a 55mm side-to-side stapler to transect the duodenum, bury the distal stump and suture it in full thickness.
7. Dissect the 8th group of lymph nodes along the hepatic artery, transect the lesser omentum below the liver, extending upwards to the right side of the esophagus; dissect the 1st, 3rd groups of lymph nodes.
8. Elevate the gastric body, transect the left gastric vessel at the root, ligate and tie it twice, and dissect the 7th group of lymph nodes.
9. Transect the gastric body about 5cm from the tumor margin, remove the specimen; carefully hemostasis and suture the seromuscular layer intermittently at the residual site.
10. At 15cm from the Treitz ligament, use a 75# side-to-side stapler to perform an antecolic side-to-side anastomosis between the jejunum and colon, with the jejunal segment on the greater curvature, and suture the seromuscular layer to reinforce the anastomosis; close the mesenteric defect above the anastomosis.
11. Thorough hemostasis, irrigate the abdominal cavity, aspirate the effluent fluid; place a negative pressure ball under the liver and at the distal end of the duodenum; verify the instruments and sponges; close the abdomen in layers.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: gastric antrum, common bile duct (胆总管); danger zones: areas around the vessels and lymph nodes; structures requiring protection: spleen, pancreas.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered include the precise dissection of the lymph nodes and vessels, and the anastomosis technique.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of lymph node dissection in gastric cancer surgery.
- The technique of Billroth II anastomosis and the management of the duodenal stump.
- The protection of adjacent organs during surgery.

## Complication Prevention
- Infection prevention: thorough disinfection and antibiotic prophylaxis.
- Bleeding prevention: careful hemostasis and the use of LigaSure.
- Anastomotic leakage prevention: careful anastomosis technique and postoperative drainage.

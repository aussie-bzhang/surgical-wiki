## Overview
Palliative gastrectomy for gastric cancer, involving subtotal gastrectomy with Billroth II reconstruction and antecolic anastomosis.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located in the middle-lower part of the gastric body, approximately 4cm in diameter, involving the serosa, and extending downward to the body of the pancreas. No metastatic lesions found in the pelvis.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, after general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Midline upper abdominal incision, peritoneal cavity exploration as described.
2. Mobilize the transverse colon, dissect the greater omentum and the anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon, transect the gastrocolic ligament, extending to the right to the hepatic flexure and to the left to the inferior pole of the spleen.
3. Clamp and transect the right gastroepiploic artery and vein at the vascular root.
4. Clamp and transect the gastro splenic ligament along the greater curvature of the stomach, preserving 2 branches of the short gastric vessels, protecting the spleen.
5. Dissect and mobilize the hepatoduodenal ligament, free the second part of the duodenum.
6. Transect and ligate the right gastric vessel; transect the duodenum 3cm distal to the pyloric canal using a 55mm side-to-side stapler, bury the distal stump and suture it in full thickness.
7. Mobilize the gastric body, open the gastropancreatic fold, and excise the tumor on the surface of the pancreas; transect and ligate the left gastric vessel at the root.
8. Transect the gastric body approximately 5cm from the tumor edge using a domestic clamp-closure device, remove the specimen; carefully hemostat the stump and reinforce with interrupted seromuscular layer.
9. At 15cm from the Treitz ligament, puncture the jejunum with a 28.5mm anvil of the stapler and fix it with a purse-string, place the long handle of the stapler at the distal margin of the residual stomach, anastomose the posterior wall of the stomach with the jejunum through the transverse mesocolon defect, close the residual stomach with a TLC75 stapler; close the mesenteric defect above the anastomosis; reinforce the anastomosis with several sutures.
10. Thorough hemostasis, lavage the peritoneal cavity, aspirate the effluent, place a negative pressure ball at the anastomosis and the distal stump of the duodenum; 
11. Count instruments and sponges, close the abdomen in layers; 
12. Operation completed. (No blood transfusion during the operation.)

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: stomach, pancreas, spleen, colon. (胆总管 (common bile duct), 胃短血管 (short gastric vessels))

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in mobilizing the stomach, protecting the spleen, and ensuring a secure anastomosis.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of careful mobilization and protection of the spleen.
- Proper handling of the duodenojejunostomy to prevent leaks.
- The significance of meticulous hemostasis and reinforcement of the anastomotic site.

## Complication Prevention
- Preventing postoperative leaks by ensuring a secure anastomosis and proper closure of the mesenteric defect.
- Minimizing blood loss through meticulous hemostasis.

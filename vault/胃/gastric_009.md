## Overview
Palliative distal gastric resection for gastric cancer with pyloric obstruction, utilizing the Billroth II technique with an antecolic anastomosis.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer, pyloric obstruction
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer, pyloric obstruction
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Grossly, an 8*7cm mass in the corpus and antrum of the stomach, infiltrative growth, involving the serosa, causing pyloric obstruction. The tumor invades the mesocolon, pancreatic head, and there are metastatic nodules along the gastrocolic ligament and the transverse colon margin, involving the mesocolon. Lymph nodes in groups 3, 5, and 6 are palpably enlarged. No abnormalities were found in the liver, gallbladder, small intestine, or pelvis. The surgery was difficult.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Midline upper abdominal incision around the umbilicus.
2. Mobilization of the transverse colon, stripping of the omentum and anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon, transection of the gastrocolic ligament, right to the hepatic flexure and left to the splenic flexure.
3. Dissection of adhesions between the mass and the duodenum, pancreas, and resection of the lesser omentum, mobilization of the second segment of the duodenum.
4. Transection and ligation of the right gastric vessels, transection of the duodenum 3 cm distal to the pyloric orifice, and sutured in an interrupted manner.
5. Mobilization of the stomach, transection of the left gastric vessels near the root and up to the right area of the esophagus, and dissection of groups 3 and 11 lymph nodes.
6. Transection of the stomach at the middle-third of the corpus, removal of the specimen, and closure of the lesser curvature with a clamp and sutured reinforcement.
7. Opening the jejunum 15 cm distal to the Treitz ligament, insertion of a 28# tube-shaped anastomosis device head.
8. Transection of the stomach, removal of the specimen, and side-to-side anastomosis with the jejunum using the 28# tube-shaped anastomosis device (proximal to the greater curvature), sutured reinforcement, and clamp closure of the remaining stomach.
9. Thorough hemostasis, lavage of the peritoneal cavity, aspiration of effluent, placement of two negative pressure balls through the anastomosis and duodenal stump.
10. Counting instruments and sponges, closure of the abdomen in layers.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: stomach, duodenum, pancreas, colon, lymph nodes. (胆总管)
> Danger zones: areas of tumor infiltration, lymph node metastases.
> Structures requiring protection: spleen, pancreas, bile duct.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included extensive tumor infiltration, significant adhesions, and the need for meticulous dissection to preserve adjacent structures.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of careful dissection to avoid injury to adjacent structures.
- Proper handling of lymph node dissection to ensure adequate staging.
- Technique for performing a Billroth II anastomosis with an antecolic approach.

## Complication Prevention
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper anastomotic technique to reduce the risk of anastomotic leakage.
- Routine lavage of the peritoneal cavity to reduce the risk of infection.

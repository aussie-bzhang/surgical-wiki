## Overview
Gastric cancer resection with Billroth II reconstruction, antecolic, proximal to greater curvature, and D2 lymph node dissection. The procedure involves resection of the stomach, gallbladder, and D2 lymph nodes, followed by reconstruction of the gastrointestinal tract.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Gallbladder approximately 8*4*3cm3 in size, cystic duct diameter 0.3cm, common bile duct diameter 0.5cm tumor. Mass located in the antrum and gastric angle, infiltrative ulcerative type, diameter about 6cm, extending beyond the serosa. Mass causes obstruction to the gastric lumen. Lymph nodes in the 5th, 6th groups are enlarged, and lymph nodes in the 7th, 8th, 12th groups are fused into a mass. No liver or pelvic involvement.

## Operative Steps
1. Patient placed in supine position, general anesthesia administered, routine disinfection and draping performed. Midline upper abdominal incision made, peritoneal cavity explored as described.
2. Gastrocolic ligament transected on the intestinal side, extending to the right to the hepatic flexure and to the left to the inferior pole of the spleen.
3. Transverse colon elevated, omentum, transverse colon mesentery, and pancreatic capsule stripped. Gastroepiploic right artery and vein clamped, tied, and transected at the vascular root.
4. Gastro splenic ligament clamped and transected along the greater curvature of the stomach, 4th and 6th group lymph nodes dissected, 2 branches of short gastric vessels preserved, spleen protected.
5. Hepatoduodenal and gastrohepatic ligaments opened, 7th, 8th, 12th group enlarged fused lymph nodes dissected, gallbladder triangle carefully dissected, cystic duct and gallbladder vessels freed, clamped and tied 0.5cm from the common bile duct, gallbladder vessels tied, gallbladder stripped from the liver and gallbladder bed, specimen removed.
6. Gastro right artery tied and transected at the root, gastric antrum and first two segments of duodenum freed. Duodenum closed with 55mm clamp 3cm from pylorus, reinforced with gastrointestinal suture. Gastrostomy site closed with Kocher clamp.
7. Gastrocaval and gastrohepatic ligaments opened, 1st, 3rd, 5th, and 7th group lymph nodes dissected, reaching the right side of the esophagus.
8. Gastroscopy clamp clamped the stomach body 7cm above the tumor margin, the remaining stomach body transected with 55mm clamp, jejunum anastomosed to the stomach with 25mm anastomotic stapler, the posterior wall of the stomach and jejunum anastomosed above the transverse colon, stapler removed, anastomosis reinforced with 55mm clamp, and gastrointestinal suture, stomach tube placed.
9. Hemostasis achieved, peritoneal cavity irrigated, effluent aspirated, negative pressure drain placed below the liver and at the duodenal stump, instruments and sponges counted, abdomen closed in layers.

## Technical Challenges
- Dissection of the gallbladder triangle
- Preservation of the spleen during lymph node dissection
- Anastomosis of the stomach and jejunum

## Teaching Points

- Preservation of the spleen during D2 lymph node dissection
- Technique of jejuno-gastric anastomosis

## Complication Prevention
- Adequate hemostasis
- Proper irrigation of the peritoneal cavity
- Placement of drains

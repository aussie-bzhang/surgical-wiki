## Overview
Gastrectomy with greater omentectomy for gastric antrum ulcer, involving resection of the greater omentum and reconstruction of the gastrointestinal tract.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric antrum ulcer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric antrum ulcer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Gastric antrum with a small ulcer (0.5*0.5cm), elevated, soft, and prone to bleeding. No abnormalities found in liver or pelvis.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping, midline upper abdominal incision.
2. Dissection of the gastrocolic ligament, right to the hepatic flexure of the colon, left to the splenic flexure with division and ligation of 2-3 branches of the left gastric vessels.
3. Dissection of the transverse mesocolon to the superior margin of the pancreas.
4. Division and double ligature of the right gastric and right gastroepiploic vessels at the vascular root.
5. Division of the duodenum 2cm distal to the pylorus, with a purse-string suture and 28.5mm anastomosis stapler.
6. Dissection of the left gastric vessels, clearing the coronary vessels and lymphatic tissue along the lesser curvature to the lower esophagus.
7. Opening the anterior gastric wall, inserting the anastomosis stapler long tube, and anastomosing the posterior wall of the stomach to the duodenum 7cm above the tumor.
8. Removal of the stomach specimen with a 90mm anastomosis stapler 3cm from the anastomosis.
9. Reinforcement of the anastomosis with a layer of seromuscular layer.
10. Hemostasis, lavage of the peritoneal cavity, aspiration of effusion, placement of two negative pressure balls at the anastomosis, and layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen.

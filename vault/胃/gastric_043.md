## Overview
Gastric cancer resection with Billroth I reconstruction and D2 lymph node dissection. Involves resection of the stomach and adjacent structures, followed by reconstruction of the gastrointestinal tract.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located at the lesser curvature of the gastric angle, infiltrative ulcerative type, approximately 2.0*2.5cm in size, surrounding mucosa showing volcanic-like aggregation, not involving the serosal layer. Lymph node group 8 enlarged. No significant abnormalities found in the small intestine, liver, biliary tract, colon, pelvis, or abdominal wall.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping, midupper abdominal midline incision, placement of wound protector and automatic retractor.
2. Transection of the gastrocolic ligament on the intestinal side, right to the second segment of the duodenum, left to the superior margin of the hilum of the spleen. Sequential ligation and transection of the greater curvature vessels while retaining two gastric short vessels. Dissection of the anterior leaf of the transverse colon mesentery to the superior margin of the pancreas, mobilization of the gastrojejunal vessels. Transection and ligation at the root of the right gastric vessel.
3. Transection of the lesser omentum below the liver, dissection of the anterior leaf of the hepatoduodenal ligament and dissection of lymph node group 12, mobilization of the hepatic artery, dissection of lymph node group 8.
4. Transection and double ligation of the right gastric vessel at the root, transection of the duodenum 3cm distal to the pylorus, the distal end sutured into a 28mm anastomosis stapler trocar and fixed.
5. Mobilization of the gastric body upwards, transection and ligation + suture of the left gastric vessel at the root; removal of the coronary vessels and lymphoid tissue on the lesser curvature until below the cardia.
6. Insertion of the long tube of the stapler into the gastric cavity, anastomosis of the posterior wall of the gastric body at the middle-third with the duodenum, transection of the gastric body 2.0cm from the 100mm side of the stapler at the anastomosis of the stomach and duodenum, removal of the specimen for pathological examination, reinforcement suture of the anastomosis of the gastrointestinal tract, gastric body stump, and lesser curvature serosal layer.
7. Thorough hemostasis, lavage of the abdominal cavity and gastric tube to ensure no bleeding, aspiration of exudate, placement of two negative pressure balls at the Winslow foramen through the anastomosis and gastric body stump, verification of sponges and instruments, and layer-by-layer closure.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: stomach, gallbladder, duodenum, spleen, pancreas, lymph nodes. (胆总管)
> Danger zones: lymph node group 8
> Structures requiring protection: gastric artery, splenic artery

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in mobilization of the liver and pancreas, and in the anastomosis of the stomach and duodenum.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of accurate lymph node dissection
- Technique of anastomosis of the stomach and duodenum
- Management of bleeding during surgery

## Complication Prevention
- Adequate hemostasis
- Proper anastomosis technique
- Postoperative monitoring for complications

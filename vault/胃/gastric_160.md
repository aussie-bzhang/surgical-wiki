## Overview
Gastric cancer resection with Billroth I reconstruction and D2 lymph node dissection in a patient with gastric adenocarcinoma.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric adenocarcinoma
**Intraoperative:** Gastric adenocarcinoma
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located on the posterior wall of the gastric antrum, approximately 4cm in diameter, infiltrative ulcerative type, extending beyond the serosal layer, involving the pancreatic capsule, and with palpable lymph node enlargement around the stomach.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping, midline upper abdominal incision, peritoneal exploration as described.
2. Transection of the gastrocolic ligament on the intestinal side, right to the inferior aspect of the duodenal bulb, left to the upper 2-3 branches of the left gastric short vessels and transection and ligation, stripping the anterior leaf of the transverse colon mesentery to the superior aspect of the pancreas.
3. Dissection below the pylorus to expose the right gastric网膜血管, double ligature at the root, and lymph node dissection of the 6th group.
4. Infrapancreatic transection of the lesser omentum, stripping the anterior leaf of the hepatoduodenal ligament and the lymphoid tissue within, exposing the right gastric artery, proper hepatic artery, and celiac trunk, double ligature at the root of the right gastric artery, and lymph node dissection.
5. Transection of the duodenum 3cm distal to the pylorus, the distal end sutured and ligated, the anvil of the stapler inserted and fixed, the stomach body flipped upwards, the left gastric artery ligated and sutured at the root, and transected; clearing the coronary vessels and lymphoid tissue on the lesser curvature to the lower esophagus.
6. At approximately 5cm from the tumor superior margin, the stomach body clamped and incised with Kocher clamps, the remaining stomach body clamped and transected, the specimen removed, the long tube of the stapler inserted into the stomach body opening, anastomosis of the stomach body posterior wall middle third to the duodenal stump, serosal layer of the anastomosis sutured and reinforced, the stomach body opening clamped and sutured in full thickness.
7. Hemostasis, peritoneal irrigation, aspiration of effluent, placement of two negative pressure drains at the anastomosis.
8. Counting sponges and instruments, closing the abdomen in layers, completion of the operation.

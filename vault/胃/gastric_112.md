## Overview
Palliative resection of gastric cancer with Billroth II reconstruction and cholecystectomy in a patient with locally advanced disease and metastasis.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer confirmed with locally advanced disease and metastasis
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Giant mass located on the lesser curvature of the gastric body, 5cm in diameter, infiltrative ulcerative type, extending beyond the serosa to involve the pancreas. Lymph nodes in the hepatoduodenal ligament were enlarged up to 2.5cm and fused into a mass, surrounding the hepatic artery, portal vein, and common bile duct. Additional tumor metastasis involving the root of the mesentery and superior mesenteric vessels, which could not be separated and resected. Gallbladder size approximately 8*4*3cm, containing two mixed stones, the largest 1.5cm in diameter. The gallbladder duct and cystic artery were compressed and thickened.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping, midline upper abdominal incision, exploration as described.
2. Elevate the transverse colon, strip the omentum and anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon, transect the gastrocolic ligament, extending to the right to the hepatic flexure and to the left to the inferior pole of the spleen; clamp and transect the right gastroepiploic artery and vein at the vascular root.
3. Clamp and transect the greater curvature of the gastric body, retaining 3 branches of the short gastric vessels, protecting the spleen.
4. Strip and dissect the hepatoduodenal ligament, mobilize the second part of the duodenum, and dissect the 8th group of lymph nodes.
5. Transect and ligate the right gastric vessel; clamp and transect the duodenum 3cm distal to the pylorus, suture the serosa; elevate the gastric body, open the gastropancreatic fold, and strip the tumor from the surface of the pancreas; transect the left gastric vessel at the root and ligate it doubly.
6. Place the anvil of the 25mm stapler through a stab incision in the jejunum 20cm from the Treitz ligament and fix it with a purse-string suture; transect the stomach 4cm above the tumor, clamp the lesser curvature with a clamp, remove the specimen, and place the long handle of the stapler through the greater curvature, performing a side-to-side anastomosis between the posterior wall of the stomach and the jejunum in front of the transverse colon; carefully hemostat and suture the residual margin, reinforcing the anastomosis with several sutures.
7. Strip the gallbladder from the liver bed to the gallbladder triangle, separate the cystic duct and cystic artery, and clarify their relationship with the common bile duct; ligate the cystic artery, transect the cystic duct 0.5cm from the CBD, ligate and suture; remove the specimen.
8. Thorough hemostasis, lavage the abdominal cavity, aspirate the effluent, place two negative pressure drainage balls at the anastomosis and duodenal stump; count instruments and sponges, close the abdomen in layers; completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: stomach, pancreas, bile duct, liver, spleen, colon. (胆总管，胆汁，胃，胰腺，肝脏，脾脏)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included the extensive involvement of the pancreas and the presence of metastases in the mesentery and superior mesenteric vessels, which required careful dissection and hemostasis.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of careful dissection around the pancreas and bile duct to avoid injury.
- The technique of performing a Billroth II reconstruction with a stapler.
- The management of extensive adhesions and metastases.

## Complication Prevention
- Thorough hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Adequate lavage of the abdominal cavity to reduce the risk of infection.

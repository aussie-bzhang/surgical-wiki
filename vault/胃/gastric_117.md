## Overview
Gastric cancer resection, clinical context of gastric cancer, key technical feature of D2+ lymph node dissection and anastomosis.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings: The stomach body has a mass about 4cm in diameter on the lesser curvature. The mass is polypoid and has not broken through the serosa. Lymph nodes in groups NO.1, 3, 5, 7, 8, and 12 around the stomach are enlarged. No abnormalities were found in the liver, gallbladder, small intestine, colon, and pelvis.

## Operative Steps
1. Laying supine position, after general anesthesia, routine disinfection and dressing, take the upper abdominal midline incision to enter the abdomen, exploration as above;
2. Raise the transverse colon, strip the omentum and the anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon, cut the gastrocolic ligament, and extend to the right to the hepatic flexure of the colon and to the left to the inferior pole of the spleen;
3. Clamp and cut the right gastroepiploic artery and vein at the root of the vessels;
4. Clamp and cut the gastro splenic ligament along the greater curvature of the stomach body, retain 2 branches of the short gastric vessels, and protect the spleen;
5. Strip and dissect the hepatoduodenal ligament, free the second end of the duodenum, and dissect the 7th, 8th, and 12th group lymph nodes;
6. Cut and ligate the right gastric vessel;
7. At 3cm from the distal end of the pyloric canal, clamp and cut the duodenum with a clamp, and suture with serosal muscle layer;
8. Raise the stomach body, open the gastric pancreas fold; cut and ligate the left gastric vessel at the root, and ligate it with a double ligature;
9. At about 7cm from the upper edge of the mass, clamp the stomach body with two Kocher clamps, and close and cut the remaining stomach body with a 90mm clamp, 20cm from the Treitz ligament, and place the anvil of a 25.5mm anastomosis stapler through a jejunum puncture and fix it with a purse-string suture, insert the long handle into the distal stomach and perform an anastomosis between the posterior wall of the stomach and the jejunum above the transverse colon, remove the stapler and close with a 60mm clamp, close the stomach with gastrointestinal suture and reinforce the anastomosis, and place the stomach tube;
10. Thorough hemostasis, wash the abdominal cavity, remove the effusion, place two negative pressure balls at the anastomosis and the distal duodenal stump;
11. Count the instruments and gauze correctly, close the abdomen layer by layer;
12. End of operation.

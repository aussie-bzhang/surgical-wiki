## Overview
Palliative total gastrectomy with esophagojejunostomy is a procedure performed for advanced gastric cancer. It involves the removal of the entire stomach and the creation of a new alimentary tract by connecting the esophagus to the jejunum.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located at the gastric angle, approximately 5*5cm, infiltrative ulcerative type, hard consistency, extending beyond the serosa, adherent to the head of the pancreas. Pancreatic surface hard, tumor extends to the lesser curvature and cardia. Lymph nodes in the 1st, 3rd, 4th, 5th, 6th, 7th, 8th, 9th, 11th, and 12th groups around the stomach were significantly enlarged, some fused into masses. Exploration of liver, bile duct, small intestine, colon, peritoneum, and pelvis was unremarkable.

## Operative Steps
1. Laying the patient in supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline laparotomy, abdominal exploration as described.
3. Ligation and division of the right gastrocolic ligament, separation to the hilum of the spleen on the left and to the hepatic flexure on the right.
4. Opening the anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon to the peritoneal membrane of the pancreas, below the pylorus.
5. Dissection of the hepatoduodenal ligament and gastrohepatic ligament, ligation and division of the right gastric artery at the root,清扫NO.5 lymph nodes, and along the common hepatic artery to the celiac trunk,清扫NO.8 lymph nodes.
6. Opening the gastropancreatic fold, ligation and division of the left gastric artery at the root,清扫NO.7 lymph nodes, upward separation to the cardia,清扫NO.1, 3 groups of lymph nodes.
7. At 2cm from the pyloric canal, the duodenum was divided, the distal end closed.
8. Ligating and dividing the left gastroepiploic vessels and short gastric vessels from the non-vascular area of the greater curvature of the stomach,清扫NO.2, 4 groups of lymph nodes, flipping the stomach body, dividing the posterior gastric vessels, fully freeing the stomach spleen and gastrohepatic ligaments, opening the esophageal hiatus of the diaphragm, dividing the anterior and posterior branches of the vagus nerve, upwardly freeing 4cm of the esophagus, closing the cut esophagus with a purse-string suture, removing the specimen for pathological examination, inserting a 25# anastomosis stapler.
9. Taking 20cm below the Treitz ligament of the jejunum, freeing the mesentery, dividing the jejunum, the distal end placed into the tube-shaped anastomosis stapler for side-to-side anastomosis with the esophagus, after removing the stapler, cutting and closing the distal end with a side-to-side stapler, reinforcing the anastomosis and distal end with sutures, leaving the gastric tube at the distal end of the esophagojejunostomy, about 40cm from the anastomosis, side-to-side anastomosis between the distal jejunum and the proximal jejunum, sutured closed the mesenteric defect.
10. Adequate hemostasis, washing the abdominal cavity, placing two negative pressure drainage tubes behind the anastomosis through the duodenal stump, exiting from the right upper abdominal puncture hole, verifying instruments and gauze, closing the incision in layers.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in lymph node dissection and anastomosis technique.

## Teaching Points

## Complication Prevention
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper anastomotic technique to reduce the risk of anastomotic leakage.

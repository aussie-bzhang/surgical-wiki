## Overview
Gastrectomy with esophagojejunostomy Roux-en-Y is a complex procedure for the treatment of gastric cancer, involving resection of the entire stomach, spleen, distal pancreas, and a segment of the colon. It is performed with an open approach and requires meticulous attention to anatomical structures and technical precision.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a mass located on the lesser curvature of the body and antrum of the stomach, measuring approximately 8*5cm. The mass had breached the serosa, invaded the pancreas, splenic hilum, and mesentery of the transverse colon at the splenic flexure. The mass presented as an ulcerative infiltrative type.

## Operative Steps
1. The patient was placed in a supine position under general anesthesia. The abdomen was entered via a midline upper abdominal incision.
2. The gastrocolic ligament was separated, extending from the right to the hepatic flexure of the colon and from the left to the inferior pole of the spleen. The anterior leaf of the transverse colon mesentery was stripped to the surface of the pancreas, and the lesser omentum was opened to access the lesser curvature.
3. The right gastric and gastroepiploic vessels were ligated and divided at the root of the pylorus, and surrounding lymph nodes were cleared.
4. The lesser omentum was transected below the liver, extending to the hepatoduodenal ligament, and the right gastric vessels were ligated and divided.
5. The duodenum was transected 3cm from the pylorus using a side-to-side stapler, and the cut was reinforced with sutures.
6. The stomach body was flipped upwards, and the left gastric vessels were ligated and doubly tied at the root. The omentum was separated upwards along the greater curvature to the lower esophagus.
7. The splenic colic, splenogastric, splenorenal, and splenohilar ligaments were transected, and the splenic pedicle was freed. The involved distal pancreas was separated and transected, with the splenic artery and vein ligated and tied. The pancreatic stump was sutured.
8. The greater curvature was continued upwards to the left of the cardia, and the vagus nerves were transected. The esophagus was transected 3cm above the cardia, and the specimen was removed. The esophagus was placed into a 25mm circular stapler and fixed with purse-string sutures.
9. The transverse colon mesentery at the splenic flexure was ligated and tied, and the transverse colon was transected approximately 6-8cm from the margin of tumor invasion.
10. The jejunum was transected 20cm from the Treitz ligament, and the esophagus was anastomosed to the distal jejunum using a 25mm stapler (colon anterior). The jejunal stump was closed with a side-to-side stapler. The proximal jejunum was anastomosed to the distal jejunum 45cm below the esophageal anastomosis; the mesenteric gap was closed.
11. Hemostasis was achieved, the abdomen was irrigated, and fluid was aspirated. Three negative-pressure drainage tubes were placed at the anastomosis and duodenal stump. Instruments and sponges were counted and found to be correct. The abdomen was closed in layers.
12. The operation was completed.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included the extensive involvement of the pancreas and spleen, which required careful dissection and ligation of vessels to prevent bleeding and injury to adjacent structures.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of meticulous dissection and ligation of vessels to prevent bleeding and injury to adjacent structures.
- The technique of performing a safe and effective esophagojejunostomy Roux-en-Y anastomosis.
- The management of extensive adhesions and the need for careful mobilization of organs.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Adequate drainage to prevent postoperative abscess formation.
- Early recognition and management of potential complications such as anastomotic leaks and bowel obstruction.

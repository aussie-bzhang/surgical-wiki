## Overview
Gastric irregular resection for gastric submucosal tumor, with emphasis on the technical aspects of tumor resection and anatomical landmarks.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric submucosal tumor
**Intraoperative:** Confirmed gastric submucosal tumor
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a 2cm submucosal mass on the lesser curvature of the gastric body with clear borders and a 0.5cm nodule on the anterior wall of the gastric body. The surgery was uneventful.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia and routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline upper abdominal incision for abdominal access.
3. Dissection of the gastrocolic ligament from the greater curvature of the stomach, with care taken to protect the spleen.
4. Incision of the omentum to expose the gastric body and fundus.
5. Complete resection of the tumor 3cm from the margin, with suture and muscular layer reinforcement.
6. Thorough hemostasis, lavage of the abdominal cavity, aspiration of effluent, and placement of an abdominal drain.
7. Layered closure.
8. Completion of the procedure.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the gastric body and antrum. The common bile duct (胆总管) should be protected during dissection.

## Technical Challenges
Not documented

## Teaching Points
- The importance of careful dissection to avoid injury to adjacent structures.
- The technique of tumor resection with adequate margins.
- The significance of hemostasis and lavage to prevent postoperative complications.

## Complication Prevention
- Adequate hemostasis and lavage to prevent infection.
- Careful dissection to avoid injury to adjacent structures.

## Overview
Gastric cancer resection with Billroth I anastomosis, performed for gastric adenocarcinoma.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric adenocarcinoma
**Intraoperative:** Gastric adenocarcinoma
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Gastric mass located on the lesser curvature of the antrum, ulcerative type, 3*2cm in size, infiltrating the muscular layer. Negative for liver and pelvic metastasis.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline upper abdominal incision.
3. Dissection of the gastrocolic ligament on the intestinal side, right to the second segment of the duodenum, left to the upper 2-3 branches of the left gastric vessels at the splenic flexure, and ligation.
4. Dissection of the preperitoneal leaf of the transverse colon to the superior margin of the pancreas.
5. Dissection of the hepatoduodenal ligament and lymph nodes.
6. Ligation and double-clamping of the right gastric and right gastroepiploic vessels at the vascular root.
7. Transection of the duodenum 2cm distal to the pylorus, with the distal end made into a purse-string suture and placed into the stapler anvil.
8. Dissection of the greater curvature of the stomach, ligation and double-clamping of the left gastric vessels.
9. Dissection of the coronary vessels and lymph nodes on the lesser curvature to the subcardia.
10. Opening the anterior wall of the stomach, inserting the long tube of the stapler at 7cm above the tumor, and performing an end-to-end anastomosis between the posterior wall of the stomach and the duodenum.
11. Retraction of the stapler, clamping and transecting the stomach 2cm from the anastomosis with a 90-degree clamp.
12. Removal of the specimen. Reinforcement of the anastomosis with a layer of seromuscular layer.
13. Hemostasis, lavage of the peritoneal cavity, aspiration of effluent, placement of two negative pressure balls at the anastomosis, and layer-by-layer closure.

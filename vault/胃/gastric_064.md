## Overview
Gastrectomy for distal gastric cancer, a common clinical scenario involving a large, ulcerative tumor in the gastric antrum, requiring extensive lymph node dissection and reconstruction.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric antrum cancer
**Intraoperative:** Confirmed gastric antrum cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located in the gastric antrum, approximately 7*6*4cm, infiltrative ulcerative type, extending beyond the serosa, causing incomplete pyloric obstruction. Gastric wall edema, lymph node enlargement in group 8. Negative findings in liver, biliary tract, small intestine, colon, and pelvis.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Midline upper abdominal incision, abdominal exploration as described.
2. Mobilize transverse colon, dissect greater omentum, transverse mesocolon, and pancreatic capsule. Cut gastrocolic ligament, extending to the right to the hepatic flexure and to the left to the inferior pole of the spleen.
3. Clamp and cut right gastric artery and vein at the vascular root.
4. Clamp and cut gastro splenic ligament along the greater curvature of the stomach, preserving 2 branches of short gastric vessels, protecting the spleen.
5. Cut and ligate right gastric vessels; 3cm distal to the pyloric orifice, transect the duodenum, suture the distal end, and bury it.
6. Dissect and anatomize the tumor from the porta hepatis, dissect and remove group 12 lymph nodes.
7. Dissect group 8 lymph nodes along the hepatic artery, transect the lesser omentum below the liver, extending upwards to the right side of the esophagus; dissect groups 1 and 3 lymph nodes.
8. Raise the gastric body, cut and doubly ligate the left gastric vessels at the root, dissect group 7 lymph nodes.
9. Cut the gastric body 5cm from the tumor margin, remove the specimen; carefully hemostasis and reinforce the seromuscular layer at the stump.
10. Choose the jejunum 15cm below the Treitz ligament for jejuno-gastric side-to-side anastomosis with the jejunum on the lesser curvature, reinforce with seromuscular layer, close the mesenteric defect above the anastomosis.
11. Thorough hemostasis, lavage the abdominal cavity, aspirate effluent, place two negative pressure balls below the liver and at the duodenal stump and jejuno-gastric anastomosis, verify instruments and sponges, close the abdomen in layers.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: stomach, lymph nodes, gastrocolic ligament, gallbladder, duodenum. (胆总管)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in extensive lymph node dissection and reconstruction, particularly in preserving the spleen and preventing postoperative complications.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of careful lymph node dissection and identification of critical structures.
- Techniques for preserving the spleen and preventing postoperative complications.
- Proper anastomotic techniques for jejuno-gastric anastomosis.

## Complication Prevention
- Thorough hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper anastomotic techniques to prevent leakage and obstruction.
- Postoperative monitoring for early detection of complications.

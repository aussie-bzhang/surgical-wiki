## Overview
Total gastrectomy with esophagojejunostomy is a surgical procedure performed for advanced gastric cancer. It involves the removal of the entire stomach and the creation of a new alimentary tract by connecting the esophagus to the jejunum.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer, gastric angle ulcer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer, gastric angle mass
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed an ulcerated mass in the cardia measuring 2cm in diameter, with a hard, volcanic crater-like appearance. A 3cm ulcerative lesion was found at the gastric angle, also with a volcanic crater-like appearance. The transverse colon was found to be interposed. No significant abnormalities were observed in the other organs.

## Operative Steps
1. The patient was placed in a supine position under general anesthesia. The abdomen was accessed via a midline upper abdominal incision.
2. The gastrocolic ligament was separated, extending to the hepatic flexure on the right and the inferior pole of the spleen on the left. The anterior leaf of the transverse colon mesentery was stripped to the surface of the pancreas.
3. The right gastroepiploic vessels were transected and ligated at the root of the pancreatic head, and surrounding lymph nodes were cleared.
4. The lesser omentum was transected below the liver, extending to the hepatoduodenal ligament on the right, and the right gastric vessels were transected and ligated.
5. The duodenum was transected and ligated 3cm from the pylorus, and sutured and reinforced.
6. The left gastroepiploic vessels and short gastric vessels were transected along the greater curvature until the fundus and cardia on the left side of the stomach.
7. The stomach body was flipped up, the left gastric vessels were transected and doubly ligated at the root, and the omentum was separated upwards. The esophagus was freed below the esophageal hiatus and the two sides of the peritoneum were incised, and the distal esophagus was transected 3cm above the cardia.
8. The esophagus was transected 25.5cm above the cardia, and the specimen was removed. A 25.5mm tube-shaped anastomosis stapler was inserted with a purse-string suture.
9. The jejunum was transected 20cm from the Treitz ligament, and the esophagus was anastomosed to the distal jejunum using a 25.5mm stapler. The distal jejunum was closed with a stapler.
10. The proximal jejunum was anastomosed to the jejunum 60cm below the esophageal anastomosis (in front of the colon). The mesenteric gap was closed.
11. Hemostasis was achieved, the abdomen was irrigated, and fluid was aspirated. Two negative pressure drainage tubes were placed below the anastomosis. Instruments and sponges were counted and found to be correct. The abdomen was closed in layers.

## Anatomical Key Points
> The critical structures include the esophagus, stomach, and jejunum. The danger zones are the areas around the pancreas and the hepatoduodenal ligament. The common bile duct (胆总管) requires protection during dissection.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included the need for careful dissection around the pancreas and hepatoduodenal ligament to avoid injury to adjacent structures.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of careful dissection around the pancreas and hepatoduodenal ligament.
- The technique of creating a safe and secure anastomosis.
- The management of potential complications such as leakage and obstruction.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful dissection to avoid injury to adjacent structures.
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper anastomotic technique to prevent leakage and obstruction.

## Overview
Gastrectomy for gastric ulcer and cancer, a clinical context involving a 3*2cm ulcer in the lesser curvature of the gastric antrum, with a suspected malignancy. Key technical feature includes radical subtotal gastrectomy with lymph node dissection.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric ulcer, suspected malignancy.
**Intraoperative:** Gastric ulcer, confirmed malignancy.
**Pathological:** Not documented.

## Intraoperative Findings
Gastric antrum lesser curvature with a 3*2cm ulcer, slightly hard, without peritoneal involvement.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline upper abdominal incision, findings as described.
3. Dissection of the greater omentum, division and ligation of the right and left gastric short vessels.
4. Dissection of the anterior leaf to the superior margin of the pancreas.
5. Division and double ligation of the right gastric and greater omentum vessels at the root.
6. Division of the duodenum 3 cm distal to the pylorus, with the distal end placed in a purse-string suture and secured with a 28# anastomosis device.
7. Dissection of the left gastric vessels, removal of the coronary vessels and lymph nodes along the lesser curvature to the cardia.
8. Insertion of the anastomosis device tube into the gastric lumen, side-to-side anastomosis between the posterior wall of the gastric body and the duodenum.
9. Side-to-side anastomosis with the anastomosis device to remove the diseased gastric remnant, removal of the specimen, and reinforcement of the anastomosis with a layer of seromuscular layer.
10. Hemostasis, lavage of the peritoneal cavity, aspiration of effluent, placement of two negative pressure balls at the anastomosis site, and suture closure in layers.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Common bile duct (胆总管), gastric antrum (胃窦), gastric fundus (胃体)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in lymph node dissection and anastomosis technique.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of accurate lymph node dissection.
- Technique of side-to-side anastomosis with the anastomosis device.
- Management of bleeding during the procedure.

## Complication Prevention
- Adequate hemostasis.
- Proper placement of drains.

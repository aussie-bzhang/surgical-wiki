## Overview
Gastric cancer resection with Billroth II reconstruction and antecolic anastomosis in a patient with gastric cancer.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located in the gastric antrum, approximately 8cm in diameter, infiltrative ulcerative type, involving the transverse colon.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, after general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Abdominal exploration via an upper midline incision.
2. Incision of the gastrocolic ligament on the intestinal side, extending to the hepatic flexure on the right and the inferior pole of the spleen on the left.
3. Retraction of the transverse colon, stripping of the greater omentum, the anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon, and the capsule of the pancreas. Clamping and ligating the right gastric artery and vein at the vascular root.
4. Clamping and transecting the greater curvature of the stomach, preserving 2 branches of the short gastric vessels, and protecting the spleen.
5. Opening the hepatogastric ligament, ligating and transecting the right gastric vessel at the root, freeing the antrum of the stomach and the first and second segments of the duodenum. Closure of the duodenum 3cm from the pylorus with a 60mm clamp, reinforced with gastrointestinal suture.
6. Completion of a purse-string suture 5cm distal to the infiltrated area of the transverse colon, insertion of a 28.5mm anvil of the stapler, opening the anterior wall of the transverse colon 5cm from the proximal end, insertion of the stapler handle, and anastomosis with the distal end. Removal of the stapler, followed by closure with a 90mm clamp and gastrointestinal suture.
7. Retraction of the stomach body upwards, transection and ligation of the left gastric vessel, and suture ligation.
8. Clamping the stomach body 7cm from the superior margin of the tumor with two Kocher clamps, transection with a 60mm clamp, and removal of the specimen.
9. Completion of a purse-string suture 20cm from the Treitz ligament on the superior jejunum, insertion of a 25.5mm anvil of the stapler, release of the clamp on the gastric stump, insertion of the stapler handle, and anastomosis of the posterior wall of the stomach with the jejunum across the transverse colon. Removal of the stapler, followed by closure with a 60mm clamp and gastrointestinal suture, and placement of a stomach tube.
10. Thorough hemostasis, irrigation of the abdominal cavity, aspiration of effluent, placement of a negative pressure ball under the liver and at the duodenal stump, verification of instruments and sponges, and layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen. Operation completed.

- Preservation of the spleen during dissection.
- Proper handling of the duodenal stump to prevent leaks.

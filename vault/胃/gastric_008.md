## Overview
Gastrectomy for gastric cancer, a common clinical scenario involving resection of the upper part of the stomach and surrounding structures.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer.
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer confirmed.
**Pathological:** Not documented.

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located at the cardia, approximately 6×5×3cm3 in size, involving the lower esophagus. Lymph nodes in groups 1 and 3 were enlarged.

## Operative Steps
1. Patient placed in supine position under general anesthesia. Abdomen explored via a midline upper abdominal incision.
2. Transverse colon elevated, gastrocolic ligament opened, and the anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon stripped to the superior margin of the pancreas. Right gastroepiploic vessels were preserved, and the greater curvature of the stomach was transected at the avascular area.
3. Gastrolien ligament opened, short gastric vessels clamped, cut, and ligated. The stomach was mobilized along the greater curvature to the left of the cardia, protecting the spleen.
4. Small omentum opened, hepatogastric and hepatoduodenal ligaments dissected. The celiac trunk was followed to its root, with lymph nodes in group 8 cleared. The left gastric artery was ligated and cut at its root, and group 7 lymph nodes were cleared.
5. The gastric coronary vein plexus was separated, branches ligated and cut. The lesser curvature of the stomach was mobilized to the right of the cardia, and groups 1 and 3 lymph nodes were cleared.
6. The stomach was transected 7cm from the cardia using a 75# clamp, and the distal stomach was reinforced with 3-0 absorbable suture.
7. The esophageal hiatus diaphragm was opened, the lower esophagus mobilized, and the vagus nerve clamped and cut 3cm above the cardia. The esophagus was transected.
8. The distal stomach was opened, and a tube-shaped anastomosis stapler was inserted. The esophagus was anastomosed to the stomach in an end-to-end manner, with the serosal layer of the anastomosis buried and sutured.
9. Hemostasis achieved, abdomen irrigated, and fluid aspirated. Two negative pressure drainage balls were placed around the anastomosis, exiting through abdominal stab wounds. Instruments and sponges counted, abdomen closed in layers, and surgery concluded.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: esophagus, stomach, spleen, liver, gallbladder, pancreas, lymph nodes. (胆总管)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in mobilizing the spleen and clearing the lymph nodes.

## Teaching Points
- Proper mobilization of the spleen and protection of its blood supply.
- Clearing of lymph nodes in groups 1, 3, 7, and 8.
- Technical aspects of esophagogastrostomy.

## Complication Prevention
- Adequate hemostasis.
- Proper anastomosis technique to prevent leaks.

## Overview
Gastrectomy with esophagojejunostomy and Roux-en-Y anastomosis is a complex procedure for the treatment of gastric cancer. It involves the resection of the entire stomach and the creation of a new alimentary tract by connecting the esophagus to the jejunum.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
A large ulcerative lesion measuring 8cm in diameter was found on the posterior wall of the gastric cardia, infiltrating into the peritoneum. There was infiltration with the serosal layer of the pancreas. Lymph nodes in groups 1, 6, 7, 8, 9, and 10 were enlarged. No metastatic lesions were found in the liver, spleen, small intestine, colon, or pelvic organs. Negative ascites.

## Operative Steps
1. The patient was placed in a supine position under general anesthesia. The abdomen was accessed via a midline upper abdominal incision.
2. The greater omentum was separated and ligated, extending to the hepatic flexure of the colon. The gastrocolic ligament was dissected and the pancreas was exposed.
3. The greater curvature of the stomach was transected, and the left gastric and short gastric vessels were ligated and divided.
4. The right gastric vessels were ligated and divided at the root of the pyloric artery, and the lymph nodes in group 6 were cleared.
5. The lesser omentum was divided below the liver, and the right gastric vessels were ligated and divided at the hepatoduodenal ligament.
6. The duodenum was transected 3cm from the pylorus using a side-to-side stapler, and the duodenal stump was sutured.
7. The remaining stomach was mobilized, and the left gastric artery was doubly ligated. The lymph nodes in groups 7, 8, and 9 were cleared.
8. The distal esophagus was mobilized, and the lymph nodes in groups 1 and 3 were cleared. The esophagus was transected 3cm above the cardia.
9. The jejunum was transected 20cm from the Treitz ligament, and the esophagus was anastomosed to the distal jejunum using a 25# circular stapler. The proximal jejunum was anastomosed to the distal jejunum 45cm below the esophagojejunal anastomosis (anteroposterior).
10. The anastomoses were reinforced with sutures, and the mesenteric gaps were closed. The abdomen was thoroughly irrigated, and drains were placed above and below the anastomoses.
11. The instruments and sponges were counted, and the abdomen was closed in layers.

## Anatomical Key Points
> The common bile duct (胆总管), the pancreas, and the surrounding lymph nodes are critical structures requiring protection during the procedure.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included extensive infiltration of the tumor into adjacent structures and the need for meticulous dissection to preserve the integrity of the surrounding organs.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of careful dissection to avoid injury to adjacent structures.
- The technique of creating a safe anastomosis between the esophagus and the jejunum.
- The management of lymph node metastases.

## Complication Prevention
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper irrigation and drainage to prevent infection and abscess formation.

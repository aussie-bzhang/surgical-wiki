## Overview
Gastric cancer resection with Billroth I reconstruction and D2 lymph node dissection. Involves extensive dissection and resection of the stomach, surrounding structures, and lymph nodes.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located on the lesser curvature of the gastric body, approximately 3cm in diameter, infiltrative ulcerative type, extending beyond the serosa. Multiple enlarged lymph nodes around the stomach. No obvious abnormalities found in liver, spleen, and intestines during exploration.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Midline upper abdominal incision. Exploration as described.
2. Dissection of the gastrocolic ligament on the intestinal side, right to the bulb of the duodenum, left to the 2-3 branches of the left gastric short vessels at the splenic flexure, and ligation and transection.
3. Dissection below the pylorus to expose the right gastric网膜血管, double ligature at the root, and lymph node dissection of the 6th group.
4. Dissection below the liver to transect the lesser omentum, stripping the anterior leaf of the hepatoduodenal ligament and lymphoid tissue, and exposing the right gastric vessel, proper hepatic artery, and celiac trunk. Transect and ligate at the root of the right gastric vessel, and lymph node dissection.
5. Transection of the duodenum 3cm distal to the pylorus, purse-string suture and placement of a 28.5# anvil of the stapler, inversion of the gastric body, ligation and suture at the root of the vessels, and transection.
6. Clamping and opening the gastric body 5cm above the tumor, transection with the remaining gastric body clamp, removal of the specimen, and placement of the long tube of the stapler in the opening of the residual stomach. Anastomosis of the posterior wall of the gastric body at the middle-third of the greater curvature with the residual duodenal stump, reinforcement with seromuscular sutures around the anastomosis, and full-thickness interrupted sutures.
7. Hemostasis, lavage of the peritoneal cavity, aspiration of effluent, placement of two negative-pressure drains at the anastomosis.
8. Counting sponges and instruments, closing the abdomen in layers, completion of the operation.

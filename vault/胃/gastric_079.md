## Overview
Gastrectomy with esophagojejunostomy (Braun) and D2+ lymphadenectomy for gastric cancer with involvement of the cardia and antrum.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric body cancer with cardia mass.
**Intraoperative:** Gastric antrum cancer with cardia mass.
**Pathological:** Not documented.

## Intraoperative Findings
Large ulcerative lesion in the posterior wall of the gastric antrum, 5cm in diameter, extending to the peritoneal surface and involving the pancreatic capsule. 2cm mass in the cardia, gastric penetration to the peritoneal surface. No metastasis in liver, spleen, small intestine, colon, and negative ascites.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Midline upper abdominal incision.
2. Dissection and ligation of the gastrocolic ligament along the transverse colon to the hepatic flexure, including the anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon, stripping to the surface of the pancreas. Dissection of the serosa of the pancreas, ligation and transection of the right gastric artery at the root of the gastrojejunal vessels, removal of the 6th group of lymph nodes.
3. Padding the spleen with gauze and transecting the left gastroepiploic and short gastric vessels along the greater curvature to the fundus and cardia.
4. Dissection below the liver, ligation and transection of the right gastric artery to the right of the porta hepatis, removal of the 12th group of lymph nodes. Transection of the duodenum 3cm from the pylorus with a 60mm clamp and sutured in full thickness.
5. Upright the remaining stomach, ligating the left gastric artery at the root. Removal of the 7th, 8th, and 9th group of lymph nodes. Dissection along the lesser omentum, mobilization of the lower esophagus, removal of the 1st and 3rd group of lymph nodes, transection of both vagus nerves 3cm above the cardia.
6. Transection of the esophagus 3cm above the cardia, removal of the specimen, placement of the anvil of a 25.5mm tube-shaped anastomosis device and fixation with purse-string suture.
7. Opening a hole at 60cm from the Treitz ligament in the jejunum, placement of the anastomosis device in the antimesenteric position to perform an end-to-side anastomosis between the esophagus and the jejunum 40cm from the Treitz ligament, side-to-side anastomosis of the jejunum, closure of the mesenteric gap.
8. Thorough hemostasis, lavage of the abdominal cavity, aspiration of effusion, placement of a negative pressure ball drain above and below the anastomosis through the residual duodenal stump, count of instruments and sponges, layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen.

## Overview
Gastric partial resection for a cystic tumor, with spleen, omentum, and gallbladder resection. Intraoperative findings suggest a cystic tumor of gastric origin with potential invasion into the stomach.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Pancreatic body tumor
**Intraoperative:** Cystic tumor in abdomen (origin from stomach? invasion into stomach?), cholelithiasis
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a cystic tumor measuring approximately 13*16*13cm in size, with thick walls and partial solidity. The cyst fluid was yellow. The tumor was closely related to the posterior wall of the gastric body, with a 0.5cm diameter ulcer on the mucosal surface of the posterior wall of the gastric body connected to the tumor. The gallbladder was 8*6*3cm in size, containing multiple 0.5cm stones.

## Operative Steps
1. Position the patient in supine position, under general anesthesia, and perform routine disinfection and draping. Make a midupper abdominal midline incision to enter the abdomen.
2. Dissect the胆囊三角, ligate and divide the cystic duct and gallbladder vessels, strip the gallbladder from the gallbladder bed of the liver, and remove the specimen.
3. Separate the gastrocolic ligament, extend to the right to the hepatic flexure of the colon and to the left to the splenic flexure, resect the omentum, and dissect the adhesions between the tumor and the mesocolon of the transverse colon.
4. Divide the splenic colic, splenic gastro, splenic renal, and splenic diaphragmatic ligaments, fully mobilize the splenic pedicle, ligate the short gastric vessels one by one, and resect the spleen.
5. Resect the gastric wall on the greater curvature connected with the tumor, carefully mobilize the tumor outside the tumor capsule, and remove it completely. Suture the residual gastric wall end with interrupted sutures.
6. Perform thorough hemostasis, irrigate the abdominal cavity, aspirate the effluent fluid, place two negative pressure balls around the anastomosis, pass one through the duodenal stump, and place one in the splenic fossa. Count instruments and sponges, close the abdomen in layers; 
7. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: gastric body, cystic tumor, spleen, omentum, gallbladder. (胆总管)
> Danger zones: posterior wall of the gastric body, splenic pedicle.
> Structures requiring protection: posterior wall of the gastric body, splenic pedicle.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included mobilization of the splenic pedicle and resection of the tumor with minimal contamination.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of careful mobilization of the splenic pedicle to avoid injury to the splenic vessels.
- The technique of resecting the tumor with minimal contamination to prevent postoperative infection.
- The importance of thorough hemostasis and irrigation of the abdominal cavity.

## Complication Prevention
- Prevent postoperative infection by minimizing contamination during surgery.
- Prevent postoperative hemorrhage by thorough hemostasis.

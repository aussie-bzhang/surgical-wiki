## Overview
Gastric cancer resection with Billroth I reconstruction and D2 lymph node dissection. Involves resection of the stomach and surrounding structures, followed by anastomosis of the duodenum to the remaining stomach.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located in the antrum, approximately 5 cm in diameter, infiltrative ulcerative type, involving the serosa; Lymph nodes NO.1, 3, 6, and 7 were palpable. Exploration of liver, gallbladder, pancreas, spleen, small intestine, colon, and peritoneal cavity showed no abnormalities.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping, midline upper abdominal incision, peritoneal cavity exploration as described.
2. Transection of the gastrocolic ligament on the intestinal side, right to the bulb of the duodenum, left to the 2-3 branches of the left gastric short vessels at the splenic flexure, and ligation.
3. Dissection below the pylorus to expose the right gastroepiploic artery, double ligature at the root, and lymph node dissection of group 6.
4. Dissection below the liver to transect the lesser omentum, stripping the anterior leaf of the hepatoduodenal ligament and lymphoid tissue, exposing the right gastric artery, proper hepatic artery, and common hepatic artery, double ligature at the root of the right gastric artery, and lymph node dissection of groups 5, 8, and 12a.
5. Transection of the duodenum 3 cm distal to the pylorus, purse-string suture and placement of a 28.5mm anastomosis stapler, inversion of the gastric body, ligation and suture at the vascular root, transection of the left gastric artery, lymph node dissection of NO.7; and upward to the right of the cardia, lymph node dissection of NO.1 and NO.3.
6. Transection of the gastric body 5 cm above the tumor margin, removal of the specimen, anastomosis of the remaining stomach to the duodenal stump with a 28.5mm stapler, and suture reinforcement of the serosal layer around the anastomosis.
7. Thorough hemostasis, lavage of the peritoneal cavity, aspiration of effluent, placement of two negative pressure drains below the liver.
8. Counting sponges and instruments, closure of the abdominal layers in layers, completion of the operation.

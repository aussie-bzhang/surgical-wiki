## Overview
Gastrectomy with esophagojejunostomy and cholecystectomy for gastric cancer and cholecystolithiasis. Key technical feature: Roux-en-Y anastomosis.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer, cholecystolithiasis
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer, cholecystolithiasis
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Gastric antrum with a 5*3cm mass, hard, involving the serosa and pancreas head. Cardia with a 3*3cm mass, hard, ulcerative, 6th, 7th, and 8th lymph node groups significantly enlarged, some fused into a mass. Gallbladder with several small stones. Common bile duct diameter 0.6 cm. No abnormalities in liver, small intestine, colon, peritoneum, or pelvis.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Abdominal midline incision, exploration as described.
2. Dissection of the hepatoduodenal ligament, mobilization of the cystic duct, identification of the relationship between the cystic duct, common bile duct, and hepatic duct. Incision and ligation of the cystic duct, double ligature. Incision and ligation of the cystic artery. Excision of the gallbladder from the liver gallbladder bed.
3. Mobilization of the transverse colon, incision of the gastrocolic ligament, separation to the splenic hilum and right colic flexure. Opening of the anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon to the peritoneal membrane, to the pylorus. Identification and ligation of the right gastric vessels, dissection of the 5th lymph node group.
4. Dissection of the hepatoduodenal ligament and gastrohepatic ligament, ligation and transection of the right gastric vessels at the root, dissection of the 5th lymph node group. Dissection along the common hepatic artery to the celiac trunk, dissection of the 8th lymph node group. Opening of the gastropancreatic fold, ligation and transection of the left gastric vessels at the root, dissection of the 7th lymph node group. Mobilization to the cardia, dissection of the 1st and 3rd lymph node groups.
5. Incision of the duodenum 2 cm from the pyloric canal, closure of the distal end. Ligation and transection of the left gastric网膜 vessels and short gastric vessels, dissection of the 2nd and 4th lymph node groups. Mobilization of the greater curvature of the stomach, transection of the posterior gastric vessels, mobilization of the stomach splenic and diaphragmatic ligaments. Opening of the esophageal hiatus diaphragm, transection of the vagus nerve anterior and posterior branches. Mobilization of 4 cm of the esophagus, closure with purse-string clamp. Removal of specimen for pathological examination, insertion of 25# anastomosis stapler.
6. Mobilization of the jejunum 15 cm below the Treitz ligament, transection of the jejunum, insertion of the tube anastomosis stapler with the esophagus for end-to-side anastomosis. Retraction of the stapler, transection and closure of the distal end. Suturing of the anastomosis and distal end with interrupted sutures. Insertion of a gastric tube at the distal end of the esophagojejunostomy, 40 cm from the anastomosis. End-to-side anastomosis of the proximal jejunum, closure of the mesenteric defect with interrupted sutures.
7. Insertion of a 12F catheter 15 cm from the distal end of the jejunojejunal anastomosis, exiting through the abdominal wall, completing jejunal feeding fistula.
8. Hemostasis, lavage of the abdominal cavity. Insertion of 2 negative pressure drainage tubes behind the anastomosis, exiting through a left upper abdominal puncture site. Insertion of a drainage tube at the distal duodenal stump, exiting through a right upper abdominal puncture site.
9. Counting instruments and sponges, closure of the incision in layers.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: stomach, esophagus, jejunum, common bile duct. (胆总管)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in mobilization of the pancreas head, identification and ligation of the vessels, and anastomosis technique.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of careful mobilization around the pancreas head to avoid injury.
- Proper identification and ligation of vessels to prevent bleeding.
- Technique of Roux-en-Y anastomosis for esophagojejunostomy.

## Complication Prevention
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper anastomosis technique to prevent anastomotic leakage.

## Operative Steps

取平卧位，气静麻下常规消毒铺巾，取腹正中切口进腹，探查如上；

解剖肝十二指肠韧带，游离胆囊管，确定胆囊管、肝总管和胆总管关系后，切断胆囊管，双重结扎，切断胆囊动脉，然后将胆囊自肝胆囊床切除；

提起横结肠，切断胃结肠韧带向左分离至脾门下，向右分离至结肠肝曲，打开横结肠系膜前叶，直至胰包膜，至幽门下，找到胃网膜右血管，双道结扎切断，沿胰头表面清扫第6组淋巴结；

解剖肝十二指肠韧带及肝胃韧带，于根部结扎切断胃右血管，清扫NO.5淋巴结，并沿肝总动脉至腹腔干，清扫NO.8淋巴结，打开胃胰襞，于根部切断胃左血管，清扫NO.7淋巴结，向上分离至贲门，清扫第1，3组淋巴结；距幽门管2cm处以切断十二指肠，远端关闭；

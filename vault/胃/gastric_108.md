## Overview
Gastric cancer palliative resection with Billroth II reconstruction and antecolic anastomosis, performed in a patient with advanced gastric cancer and Krukenberg's tumor.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer.
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer, Borrmann III type, Krukenberg's tumor, cholecystolithiasis.
**Pathological:** Not documented.

## Intraoperative Findings
Large tumor located on the greater curvature near the antrum, diameter 6.0cm, ulcerative infiltrative type, penetrating the serosa, 3rd, 4th, 5th, 6th, and 12th lymph node groups visible with enlargement. Scattered multiple millet-like nodules visible on the abdominal wall, omentum, mesentery, adnexa, and pelvic cavity. Sand-like cholesterol crystals visible in the gallbladder.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, after general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Midline upper abdominal incision, enter peritoneal cavity as described.
2. Incise the omentum, open the gastrocolic ligament, free on both sides, to the hilum of the spleen on the left and to the hepatic flexure of the colon on the right. Cut the hepatocolic ligament, free the omentum from the gallbladder.
3. Open the preperitoneal leaf of the transverse colon mesentery, free the gastrocolic ligament upwards to the subpyloric region, dissect the gastroduodenal vessels, cut and ligate, and sequentially dissect the 6th group of lymph nodes.
4. Open the lesser omentum sac, dissect the anterior leaf of the hepatoduodenal ligament, dissect the common bile duct, cystic duct, and cystic artery, cut and ligate the latter two, strip the gallbladder from the gallbladder bed, remove the gallbladder specimen, and cauterize the gallbladder bed for hemostasis.
5. Free the pylorus and bulb of the duodenum, cut the duodenum 3cm from the pylorus with a side-to-side stapler, suture the distal end with absorbable suture for reinforcement. Free the greater and lesser curvatures of the stomach, sequentially ligate and cut the left gastric artery, left gastroepiploic artery, and short gastric vein, free both curvatures to 10cm above the tumor, and sequentially dissect the 3rd, 4th, and 5th group of lymph nodes.
6. At approximately 7cm above the gastric tumor on the greater curvature, clamp the gastric body with two Kochor clamps and incise, aspirate the gastric contents, clamp and cut the lesser curvature of the gastric body with a 55# side-to-side stapler, remove the distal part of the gastric specimen, and suture the distal end with absorbable suture for reinforcement.
7. Take 30cm distal to the Treitz ligament of the small intestine for anastomosis: open the mesenteric margin, place the 25# end-to-end stapler head, and fix for use. Place the stapler through the distal end of the remnant stomach, perform an end-to-side anastomosis between the posterior wall of the gastric body and the small intestine 3cm from the distal end, cross the anastomosis over the anterior aspect of the transverse colon, and reinforce the anastomosis with absorbable suture. Wash the gastric cavity, confirm patency of the anastomosis without bleeding, introduce the gastric tube into the distal end of the anastomosis, close the distal end with a side-to-side stapler, and reinforce with silk thread.
8. Irrigate the abdominal cavity with large amounts of normal saline, hemostasis, and leave two negative pressure drainage tubes under the liver and behind the anastomosis. Sprinkle anti-tumor drugs into the abdominal cavity, count sponges and instruments, close the abdomen, and complete the operation.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of thorough lymph node dissection.
- Proper handling of the duodenum and bile duct during resection.
- Technique of antecolic anastomosis.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Adequate drainage to prevent postoperative abscess formation.

## Overview
Gastroesophageal cancer resection with complex small bowel adhesiolysis in a patient with extensive intra-abdominal adhesions and lymph node involvement.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastroesophageal cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastroesophageal cancer confirmed with lymph node involvement
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings include a 2cm ulcerative lesion at the gastroesophageal junction with purulent exudate and hemorrhagic spots, irregular mucosal folds, and no penetration of the serosa. Lymph node enlargement in the 7th group around the stomach. Extensive intra-abdominal adhesions, particularly in the lower abdomen. No abnormalities found in the liver, gallbladder, small intestine, colon, or pelvis.

## Operative Steps
1. Patient placed in supine position under general anesthesia. Abdominal exploration via an upper midline incision around the umbilicus.
2. Bluntly dissect extensive intra-abdominal adhesions, elevate the transverse colon, open the gastrocolic ligament, and strip the anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon to the superior margin of the pancreas.
3. Retain the right gastric and right gastroepiploic vessels, and divide the greater curvature of the stomach at the avascular area of the gastric body. Dissect the left gastric vessels, clamp, divide, and ligate.
4. Open the lesser omentum, dissect the hepatogastric and hepatoduodenal ligaments, and follow the hepatic artery to the root of the celiac trunk. Dissect and clear the 8th group lymph nodes, divide and ligate the left gastric artery at its root, and clear the 7th group lymph nodes.
5. Dissect the gastroepiploic vein, clamp, divide, and ligate the branches. Free the lesser curvature of the stomach upwards to the right side of the gastroesophageal junction, and clear the 1st and 3rd group lymph nodes.
6. Open the esophageal hiatus of the diaphragm, mobilize the lower esophagus, and divide the esophagus 3cm above the gastroesophageal junction. Place the anvil of a 25# tube-shaped anastomosis stapler in the esophagus.
7. Create a window in the anterior stomach wall and place the stapler, performing an end-to-side anastomosis between the posterior wall of the gastric body 7-8cm from the gastroesophageal junction and the esophagus. Clamp and divide the stomach 2cm from the anastomosis with a 90mm clamp. Suture the serosal layer around the anastomosis to reinforce it.
8. Reinforce the gastric remnant with 3-0 absorbable suture.
9. Thorough hemostasis, irrigate the abdomen, aspirate the effluent, place two negative pressure balls around the anastomosis, and exit through abdominal stab wounds. Count instruments and sponges, close the abdomen in layers, and complete the procedure.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Gastroesophageal junction (贲门), stomach (胃), esophagus (食管), lymph nodes (淋巴结)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered include extensive intra-abdominal adhesions and lymph node involvement.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of careful dissection to avoid injury to adjacent structures.
- The technique of creating an anastomosis with a stapler and reinforcing it with sutures.

## Complication Prevention
- Thorough hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper placement of drains to prevent postoperative effusion.

## Overview
Gastrectomy for distal gastric cancer (Billroth I) is a surgical procedure performed for the treatment of distal gastric cancer. It involves the resection of the distal part of the stomach and reconstruction of the gastrointestinal tract.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
A 5cm infiltrative ulcerous mass on the anterior wall of the gastric antrum, hard in consistency, extending beyond the serosa. Lymph nodes in groups 1, 7, and 12 were enlarged. A 0.5cm nodule was visible on the distal gastric body wall. Negative exploration of the liver and pelvis.

## Operative Steps
1. After general anesthesia, the patient is placed in supine position. The abdomen is entered through an upper midline incision.
2. The gastrocolic ligament is transected on the intestinal side, from the second segment of the duodenum to the upper 2-3 branches of the left gastric short vessels, and ligated and tied.
3. The anterior leaf of the lesser omentum is transected below the liver, and the lymphatic tissue within the hepatoduodenal ligament is stripped.
4. The right and greater gastric网膜 vessels are doubly ligated at the vascular root; the duodenum is transected 3cm distal to the pylorus, and the residual end is sutured into a purse-string and placed into the head of a 28# anastomosis stapler.
5. The gastric body is flipped upwards, the left gastric vessels are doubly ligated at the vascular root; the coronary vessels and lymphatic tissue on the lesser curvature are cleared down to the subcardia; a 28# anastomosis stapler is placed into the gastric cavity, and a side-to-end anastomosis is performed between the posterior wall of the gastric body at the middle-third and the duodenum. The gastric body is transected with the side-side stapler, and the specimen is removed. The anastomosis and residual end are reinforced with sutures.
6. Hemostasis is achieved, the abdomen is irrigated, and the effluent is aspirated. Two negative pressure bulbs are placed at the anastomosis and led out through a stab wound in the upper right abdomen. The layers are closed in sequence. The operation is completed.

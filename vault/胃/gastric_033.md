## Overview
Gastric resection and Roux-en-Y anastomosis for gastric cancer with complex adhesions, colon splenic flexure resection, left liver partial resection, and splenectomy.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Original surgery was a gastric remnant-jejunum Roux-en-Y anastomosis. The tumor occupied the entire gastric remnant with a diameter of about 7.0 cm, ulcerative infiltrative type, involving partial left liver, transverse colon mesentery, and spleen. It was densely adherent to the body and tail of the pancreas. Severe adhesions were present in the upper abdomen. Scattered gray-white metastatic nodules were visible on the abdominal wall and small bowel mesentery, with a diameter of about 0.2-0.5 cm.

## Operative Steps
1. Position the patient in supine position, under general anesthesia, and perform routine disinfection and draping. Enter the abdomen through the original upper abdominal incision.
2. Separate the adhesions, resect the left lateral lobe of the liver involved by the tumor. Suture or ligate the vessels encountered within the liver, and ligate the bleeding points on the liver surface.
3. Incise the anterior peritoneum of the esophagus, transect the left and right vagus nerves, and thoroughly mobilize the esophagus. Transect the esophagus 3 cm above the cardia, remove the specimen, and place the anvil of a 28F tube anastomosis stapler and fix it with purse-string suture.
4. Separate the gastric remnant from surrounding tissues, ligate and transect the gastric vessels, and transect the proximal jejunum 10 cm from the original anastomosis. Mobilize the remnant gastric side of the original jejunum Y arm for later use.
5. Mobilize the transverse colon mesentery, open the left lateral peritoneum of the colon, ligate the splenic colic ligament, and mobilize the colon splenic flexure. Transect the middle colic artery left branch, and separate and transect the colon mesentery one by one. Mobilize the distal part to the descending colon and the proximal part to the middle of the transverse colon.
6. Sequentially ligate and transect the splenic renal ligament, splenic diaphragmatic ligament, and adhesions between the spleen and surrounding tissues. Mobilize the spleen, ligate and doubly ligate the splenic artery and vein, and remove the specimen.
7. Anastomose the esophagus with the jejunum Y arm of the remnant gastric side, and close the proximal jejunum end-to-side with a stapler.
8. Perform end-to-side anastomosis between the transverse colon and descending colon, and close the distal descending colon end-to-side with a stapler.
9. Close the mesenteric gaps.
10. Thoroughly hemostasis, irrigate the abdominal cavity, remove the effluent fluid, and place two negative pressure drains at the esophagus-jejunum anastomosis and one negative pressure drain in the splenic fossa, puncturing the wound to lead out. Count instruments and sponges to ensure accuracy, and close the abdomen in layers.
11. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: gastric remnant, esophagus, jejunum, colon, spleen, liver. (胆总管)
> Danger zones: dense adhesions, tumor infiltration.
> Structures requiring protection: esophagus, bile duct, pancreas.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included dense adhesions, tumor infiltration, and protecting critical structures.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of thorough mobilization and adhesion separation.
- The technique of anastomosis with staplers.
- The management of dense adhesions and tumor infiltration.

## Complication Prevention
- Thorough hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Adequate drainage to prevent postoperative abscess.

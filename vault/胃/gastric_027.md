## Overview
A complex procedure involving the lysis of small bowel adhesions, total gastrectomy, esophagojejunostomy Roux-en-Y, partial hepatectomy, and diaphragmatic resection, performed for post-pancreatic cancer with pyloric obstruction.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Post-pancreatic cancer, pyloric obstruction
**Intraoperative:** Post-pancreatic cancer, pyloric obstruction
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Severe intra-abdominal adhesions, diffuse thickening of the gastric wall, swollen pylorus obstructing, adhesions between the greater curvature of the stomach and the diaphragm, a hard mass of approximately 1.5 cm in diameter palpable in the left lobe of the liver, exploration of small intestine, colon, and pelvis negative.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping, midline upper abdominal incision, careful dissection of intra-abdominal adhesions, exploration reveals;
2. Separation of gastrocolic ligament, to the right to the hepatic flexure of the colon, to the left to the inferior pole of the spleen, along with the anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon, stripped to the surface of the pancreas, opening the lesser omentum囊 into the lesser curvature;
3. Dissection below the liver, transection and ligation of the right gastric vessels; transection of the stomach 3 cm above the pylorus, the distal end sutured and reinforced;
4. Transection and ligation of the left gastric vessels and short gastric vessels along the greater curvature to the left of the cardia; 
5. The stomach body is flipped upwards, the left gastric vessels at the root are transected and doubly ligated; separation upwards along the lesser omentum, transection of the esophagus at the cardia, removal of the specimen, placement of the tube anastomosis stapler and fixation with purse-string suture;
6. Transection of the jejunum 20 cm from the Treitz ligament, the esophagus and distal jejunum anastomosed end-to-side with the stapler, the distal jejunum closed with an occluder. The proximal jejunum is anastomosed end-to-side with the jejunum 60 cm below the esophageal anastomosis (in front of the colon); closure of the mesenteric gap;
7. Local excision of the lateral edge of the left liver cystic mass.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in adhesiolysis, mobilization of the stomach, and anastomosis due to severe adhesions and thickened gastric wall.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of careful adhesiolysis to avoid injury to adjacent structures.
- Technique for safe mobilization of the stomach and esophagus.
- Proper anastomotic technique to ensure leak-proof connections.

## Complication Prevention
- Adequate hemostasis.
- Proper placement of drains to prevent postoperative fluid collections.

## Overview
Gastric cancer resection with Billroth II reconstruction, antecolic anastomosis, and D2 lymphadenectomy. Involves extensive dissection and lymph node clearance.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located on the lesser curvature of the gastric antrum, infiltrative ulcerative type, approximately 6*4cm in size, involving the serosal layer. Lymph nodes in groups 3, 4, and 8 around the stomach were enlarged. No abnormalities found in the small intestine, colon, liver, biliary tract, or pelvis.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Midline upper abdominal incision, abdominal exploration as described.
2. Transection of the gastrocolic ligament on the intestinal side, extending to the hepatic flexure of the colon on the right and the inferior pole of the spleen on the left.
3. Lifting the transverse colon, stripping the greater omentum, the anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon, and the capsule of the pancreas. Clamping and ligating the right gastric artery and vein at the vascular root.
4. Clamping and transecting the greater curvature of the stomach, clearing the 4th and 6th groups of lymph nodes, preserving 2 branches of the short gastric vessels, and protecting the spleen.
5. Opening the hepatoduodenal ligament and the gastrohepatic ligament, clearing the 8th group of lymph nodes. Ligating and transecting the right gastric vessel at the root, mobilizing the antrum and the first segment of the duodenum. The duodenum was transected 3cm from the pylorus using a 60mm clamp, and the stump was reinforced with 3-0 absorbable suture.
6. The stomach body was flipped upwards, and the right gastric artery was ligated and transected along the lesser curvature, clearing the 1st, 3rd, 5th, and 6th groups of lymph nodes, reaching the right side of the esophagus.
7. The stomach body was clamped and transected 7cm from the tumor margin using Kocher clamps, and the remaining stomach body was transected using a 60mm clamp. The jejunum was anastomosed to the gastric stump in front of the transverse colon using a 28# anvil and circular stapler. The stump was reinforced with 60mm clamps and 3-0 absorbable suture.
8. Hemostasis, lavage of the abdominal cavity, aspiration of effluent, and placement of a negative pressure drain under the liver and at the duodenal stump.

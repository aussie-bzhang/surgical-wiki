## Overview
Gastric cancer resection with esophagojejunostomy and liver wedge resection, a complex procedure involving extensive resection of the stomach, esophagus, and liver, followed by reconstruction of the gastrointestinal tract.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer confirmed with lymph node and liver metastasis
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Gastric mass located on the lesser curvature, diameter approximately 5cm, infiltrative ulcerative type, tumor extending beyond the serosa. Lymph nodes in groups 1, 3, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, and 12 were enlarged and fused. A hard nodule approximately 1cm in diameter was found in the left lobe of the liver. No metastatic lesions were found in the abdominal and pelvic organs. No ascites. Operation went smoothly.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Median upper abdominal incision.
2. Dissection of the gastrocolic ligament, extending to the right to the hepatic flexure of the colon and to the inferior pole of the spleen. The anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon was stripped to the surface of the pancreas.
3. Transection and ligation of the right gastroepiploic artery at the root of the pancreatic head, clearance of surrounding lymph nodes.
4. Transection of the lesser omentum below the liver, extending to the hepatoduodenal ligament, transection and ligation of the right gastric artery.
5. Transection of the left gastroepiploic and short gastric arteries along the greater curvature to the left of the cardia.
6. Upward flipping of the stomach, transection and double ligature of the left gastric artery at the root.
7. Separation along the lesser omentum, incision of the esophageal hiatus and bilateral peritoneum, mobilization of the lower esophagus, transection of the esophagus 3cm above the cardia.
8. Transection of the jejunum 20cm from the Treitz ligament, anastomosis of the esophagus to the distal jejunum with a 25.5mm circular stapler, closure of the jejunal stump with a clamp.
9. Anastomosis of the proximal jejunum to the distal jejunum 50cm below the esophageal anastomosis (colonic antimesenteric).
10. Wedge resection of the liver metastasis 1cm from the edge of the nodule, sutured.
11. Hemostasis, lavage of the abdominal cavity, placement of two negative pressure drainage tubes above and below the anastomosis, layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen, operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: stomach, esophagus, jejunum, liver. Common bile duct (胆总管), portal vein (门静脉), superior mesenteric artery (肠系膜上动脉).

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in extensive resection and reconstruction, including handling of the liver metastasis.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of meticulous dissection and lymph node clearance.
- The technique of esophagojejunostomy with a circular stapler.
- The management of liver metastasis.

## Complication Prevention
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper anastomotic technique to prevent anastomotic leakage.

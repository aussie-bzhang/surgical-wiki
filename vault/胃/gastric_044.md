## Overview
Gastric resection with hemostasis, postoperative gastric bleeding, technical aspects of hemostasis in gastric resection.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Postoperative gastric bleeding
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings:渗血 seen at the distal margin of the resected stomach; 200ml blood clots and serous fluid in the gastric cavity.

## Operative Steps
1. After successful induction of general anesthesia, the patient is placed in supine position, and the abdomen is entered through the original surgical incision.
2. The anterior gastric wall is opened, and exploration is performed as above.
3. The bleeding site at the distal margin of the resected stomach is sutured; saline irrigation reveals no bleeding.
4. The abdomen is irrigated with large amounts of normal saline until clean, and two negative pressure drains are placed below the liver margin at the gastrojejunal anastomosis site, leading out from the upper right abdomen.
5. Instruments and sponges are counted and found to be correct, and the abdomen is closed in layers.

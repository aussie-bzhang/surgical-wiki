## Overview
Distal gastrectomy with Billroth II reconstruction is a surgical procedure for the treatment of gastric cancer. It involves the resection of the distal part of the stomach and reconstruction of the gastrointestinal tract using the colon. The procedure is performed with an open approach and is considered of high difficulty.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer, post-perforation repair
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer, post-perforation repair
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed adhesions in the right upper quadrant, mild edema, a 3*3cm mass at the pylorus, hard in consistency, involving the serosa. Lymph nodes No.5, 7, 8, and 9 were palpable. Exploration of the liver, gallbladder, pancreas, small intestine, peritoneum, and pelvis were negative.

## Operative Steps
1. The patient was placed in a supine position. After general anesthesia and routine disinfection, a midline upper abdominal incision was made to enter the abdomen. Exploration revealed the above findings.
2. Adhesions in the right upper quadrant were separated to restore the original anatomical structure.
3. The gastrocolic ligament was transected on the intestinal side, extending to the hepatic flexure on the right and the inferior pole of the spleen on the left. The transverse colon was lifted, and the omentum, the anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon, and the pancreatic capsule were stripped. The right gastroepiploic artery and vein were clamped and ligated at the vascular root.
4. The gastric splenic ligament was clamped and transected along the greater curvature of the stomach, and the 4th and 6th groups of lymph nodes were dissected. Two gastric short vessels were preserved, and the spleen was protected.
5. The lesser omentum was transected below the liver, and the hepatoduodenal ligament was dissected. The 8th group of lymph nodes were dissected along the common hepatic artery.
6. The right gastric artery was ligated and transected at the root, and the duodenal bulb and the first and second segments of the duodenum were mobilized. The duodenum was closed 3 cm from the pylorus using a 60mm clamp, and the stump was reinforced with 3-0 gastrointestinal suture for one week and then buried. The gastric stump was clamped with a Kocher clamp.
7. The stomach body was flipped, and the 9th group of lymph nodes were dissected along the common hepatic artery to the celiac trunk. The left gastric artery was transected at the root and ligated and sutured. The 7th group of lymph nodes were dissected and continued upwards to the right of the esophagus, dissecting the 1st and 3rd groups of lymph nodes.
8. The stomach was transected approximately 6 cm above the mass, and the ileum was anastomosed to the gastric stump in front of the colon, with the proximal end facing the greater curvature. The吻合器 was removed after the anastomosis, and the anastomosis was closed with a 90mm clamp and reinforced with 3-0 gastrointestinal suture for one week. The anastomosis was reinforced, and a gastric tube was placed.
9. Hemostasis was achieved, the abdomen was irrigated, and the effluent was aspirated. A negative pressure drain was placed below the liver and at the duodenal stump. Instruments and sponges were counted and found to be correct. The abdomen was closed in layers. The operation was completed.

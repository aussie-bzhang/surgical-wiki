## Overview
Gastric cancer resection with Billroth I reconstruction and D2 lymph node dissection for a patient with gastric perforation and diffuse peritonitis.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Acute diffuse peritonitis, gastrointestinal perforation
**Intraoperative:** Acute diffuse peritonitis, gastric cancer perforation
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Large amount of food residue in the abdominal cavity. A 3cm ulcer with a 0.8cm perforation is present on the anterior wall of the gastric angle. A 1.5cm ulcerative lesion is found on the lesser curvature of the stomach body, involving the serosal layer. Lymph nodes in the 3rd and 6th groups are enlarged. No obvious metastasis is found in the liver, pancreas, mesocolon, or pelvic cavity.

## Operative Steps
1. Position the patient in supine position under general anesthesia, perform routine disinfection and draping. Make a midline upper abdominal incision and enter the abdomen.
2. Dissect the gastrocolic ligament from the attachment of the transverse colon to the splenic hilum; dissect the preperitoneal leaf of the transverse mesocolon from the superior margin of the pancreas; dissect the lesser omentum below the liver and remove the lymphatic tissue from the hepatoduodenal ligament; ligate and tie off the right gastric and right gastroepiploic vessels at a high position; transect the duodenum 3cm from the pylorus and place the distal end in a 28F tube-shaped anastomosis stapler and secure it with purse-string suture.
3. Reflect the stomach body upwards, ligate and tie off the left gastric vessels at the root; remove the coronary vessels and their lymphatic tissue down to the subcardial region; transect the stomach body at the middle-third of the stomach body, remove the specimen; close the greater curvature of the residual stomach in duplicate, leave a 3cm margin on the lesser curvature to place the anastomosis stapler, and anastomose the distal margin of the residual stomach to the duodenum in end-to-end fashion. Suture the lesser curvature in duplicate after removing the stapler, reinforce the anastomosis with seromuscular layer, and add several seromuscular layer sutures.
4. Perform thorough hemostasis, irrigate the abdominal cavity, aspirate the effluent, place a negative pressure drain at the WINSLOW hole and the anastomosis site, and bring them out through the right upper quadrant. Close the layers in sequence.

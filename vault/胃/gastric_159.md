## Overview
Gastric cancer resection with Billroth II reconstruction, antecolic, proximal to greater curvature, and D2 lymph node dissection. Involves extensive dissection and reconstruction of the upper gastrointestinal tract.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located on the lesser curvature of the stomach, large, approximately 5cm in diameter, infiltrative ulcerative type, extending beyond the serosa, involving the pancreatic capsule. Perigastric lymph nodes 3, 4, 5, 7, and 8 are enlarged. No liver or pelvic involvement.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, after general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Midline upper abdominal incision, enter peritoneum as described.
2. Incise the gastrocolic ligament on the intestinal side, extending to the hepatic flexure on the right and the inferior pole of the spleen on the left.
3. Retract the transverse colon, dissect the greater omentum, the anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon, and the pancreatic capsule, ligate and tie the right gastric artery and vein at the vascular root.
4. Clamp and transect the gastro splenic ligament along the greater curvature of the stomach, dissect the 4th and 6th groups of lymph nodes, preserve 2 branches of the short gastric vessels, and protect the spleen.
5. Open the hepatoduodenal ligament and the gastrohepatic ligament, dissect the 8th group of lymph nodes, ligate and transect the right gastric vessel at the root, mobilize the pylorus, the first and second segments of the duodenum, close the duodenum 3cm from the pylorus with a 60mm clamp, reinforce the stump with silk suture, and clamp the gastric stump with a Kocher clamp.
6. Ligate and transect the coronary vessels along the lesser curvature of the stomach, dissect the 1st, 3rd, 5th, and 7th groups of lymph nodes, and extend upwards to the right side of the esophagus.
7. Clamp and close the gastric body with Kocher clamps 7cm from the tumor margin, transect the remaining gastric body with a 90mm clamp, and place the anvil of a 25.5mm stapler through a jejunum incision 20cm from the Treitz ligament and fix it with a purse-string suture. The long handle is inserted into the gastric stump, and the posterior wall of the stomach is anastomosed to the transverse colon above the jejunum. The stapler is removed, and the anastomosis is closed with a 60mm clamp and reinforced with gastrointestinal suture. Place a stomach tube.
8. Thorough hemostasis, lavage the peritoneal cavity, aspirate the effluent, place a negative pressure drain below the liver and at the duodenal stump, count instruments and sponges, and close the abdomen in layers. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: stomach, common bile duct, spleen, pancreas, lymph nodes. (胆总管，胰腺，脾脏，淋巴结)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in extensive dissection and reconstruction, particularly in mobilizing the pancreas and preserving the splenic vessels.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of careful mobilization of the pancreas and splenic vessels to prevent complications.
- Proper technique for lymph node dissection and anastomosis.
- The significance of thorough hemostasis and lavage to reduce postoperative complications.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful mobilization of the pancreas and splenic vessels.
- Proper lymph node dissection technique.
- Thorough hemostasis and lavage.

## Overview
Palliative upper gastric resection for esophageal cancer, with significant metastasis to abdominal cavity and peritoneum.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Esophageal cancer
**Intraoperative:** Esophageal cancer with extensive metastasis
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a 6*5cm esophageal mass with infiltrative growth extending beyond the serosa. Multiple metastatic nodules were found in the abdominal and pelvic cavities, with a large number of nodules on the colon mesentery. No abnormalities were found in the exploration of the liver, gallbladder, pancreas, spleen, and small intestine.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline upper abdominal incision, layer-by-layer abdominal exploration.
3. Retraction of the transverse colon, dissection and transection of the gastrocolic ligament to the right to the hepatic flexure and to the left to the inferior pole of the spleen.
4. Transection of the splenocolic ligament, stripping of the anterior leaf of the transverse colon mesentery to the superior margin of the pancreas, exposing the posterior wall of the stomach.
5. Retention of the right gastric网膜血管, transection of the greater curvature of the stomach at the avascular area of the gastric body.
6. Sequential clamping and transection of the short gastric vessels, separation of the gastric body along the greater curvature, freeing the left side of the cardia, protecting the spleen.
7. Transection of the lesser omentum below the liver, freeing the hepatoduodenal ligament, careful dissection and separation of the enlarged lymph nodes from the left gastric vessels, transection and ligation with suture at the root, and clamping and transection of the vagus nerve, transection of the esophagus 2cm above the cardia, and placement of the 25.5mm tube-shaped anastomosis device head for anastomosis.
8. Transection of the gastric body at the middle and lower third, 5cm above the tumor margin, using a 90mm clamp, removal of the specimen.
9. Incision of the residual gastric body anterior wall, placement of the tube-shaped anastomosis device for the gastric-esophageal end-to-side anastomosis, suture reinforcement of the anastomosis orifice and the residual gastric body serosa.
10. Thorough hemostasis, lavage of the abdominal cavity, aspiration of exudate, placement of two negative pressure balls above and below the anastomosis, verification of instruments and sponges, layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen, completion of the operation.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: esophagus, stomach, spleen, liver, colon. (食管，胃，脾，肝，结肠)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included extensive metastasis, which required careful dissection and preservation of adjacent structures.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of careful dissection around the esophagus and stomach to avoid injury to adjacent structures.
- The technique of using a tube-shaped anastomosis device for esophagogastrostomy.
- The management of extensive metastasis during surgery.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Adequate lavage of the abdominal cavity to reduce the risk of infection.

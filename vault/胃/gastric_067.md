## Overview
Gastrectomy with esophagojejunostomy is a surgical procedure performed for gastric cancer, involving the removal of the entire stomach and creating a new alimentary tract by anastomosing the esophagus to the jejunum.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located on the lesser curvature of the stomach, with breakthrough of the serosa and infiltrative growth. Stomach wall from the cardia to the gastric angle was rigid. Exploration of liver, omentum, and pelvis was negative.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Upper midline incision made around the left costal margin, entering the abdomen.
2. Dissection of the gastrocolic ligament, extending to the right to the hepatic flexure of the colon and to the inferior pole of the spleen. The anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon was stripped to the surface of the pancreas, and the lesser omentum was opened into the lesser curvature.
3. At the root of the pylorus, the right gastroepiploic vessels were transected and ligated, and the surrounding lymph nodes were cleared.
4. The lesser omentum was transected below the liver, extending to the hepatoduodenal ligament, and the right gastric vessels were transected and ligated.
5. At 3 cm from the pylorus, the duodenum was transected with a 55 cm side-to-side stapler, and a purse-string suture was applied.
6. The left gastroepiploic vessels and short gastric vessels were transected along the greater curvature until the fundus and left side of the cardia.
7. The stomach body was flipped upwards, the left gastric vessels at the root were transected and doubly ligated. The lower esophagus was freed superiorly, and the vagus nerves on both sides were transected. The esophagus was transected 3 cm above the cardia.
8. At 20 cm from the Treitz ligament, the jejunum was transected, and the esophagus was anastomosed to the distal jejunum with a 25 mm circular stapler. The distal jejunal stump was closed with a side-to-side stapler. The proximal jejunum was anastomosed to the jejunum 60 cm below the esophageal anastomosis (in front of the colon); the mesenteric gap was closed.
9. Hemostasis was achieved, the abdomen was irrigated, and effluent was aspirated. Two negative pressure balls were placed below the splenic hilum below the anastomosis, and one negative pressure ball was placed below the duodenal stump in the infraportal space. Instruments and sponges were counted and found to be correct. The abdomen was closed in layers.
10. Operation completed.

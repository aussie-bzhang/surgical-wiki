## Overview
Gastrectomy with pyloroplasty for gastric body cancer, involving resection of the upper part of the stomach and reconstruction of the pylorus.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric body cancer.
**Intraoperative:** Gastric body cancer confirmed.
**Pathological:** Not documented.

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located on the posterior wall of the gastric body near the lesser curvature, presenting as an ulcerative infiltrative type, diameter 3cm, infiltrating into the serosa layer. Lymph nodes in group 4 are palpable.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia successful. Routine disinfection and draping. Midline upper abdominal incision, enter peritoneum.
2. Retract the transverse colon, dissect and transect the gastrocolic ligament, extending to the right to the hepatic flexure of the colon and to the inferior pole of the spleen. Transect the splenocolic ligament, strip the anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon to the superior margin of the pancreas, exposing the posterior wall of the stomach.
3. Retain the right gastroepiploic vessels and transect the greater curvature of the stomach at the avascular area. Dissect along the greater curvature of the stomach, ligate and transect the left gastroepiploic vessels. Open the gastro splenic ligament, ligate and transect the short gastric vessels, dissect the stomach body, and dissect the second and fourth group of lymph nodes. Free the stomach to the left of the cardia, protecting the spleen.
4. Open the lesser omentum, free the hepatoduodenal ligament, ligate and transect the branches of the right gastric artery along the lesser curvature of the stomach, flip the stomach body, ligate and transect the left gastric artery at the root. Clamp and transect the stomach body at the middle segment with a 100# side-to-side stapler, reinforce the distal gastric body stump with 3-0 absorbable suture. Dissect upwards along the lesser omentum to the cardia, ligate and transect the vagus nerve, mobilize the esophagus upwards 3cm, clamp and transect at 2cm from the cardia with a purse-string clamp, remove the specimen. Place the distal gastric body stump into a 25# tube-shaped stapler, anastomose with the esophagus at the posterior wall, reinforce the anastomosis with 3-0 absorbable suture. Close the window.
5. Adequate hemostasis, wash the peritoneal cavity, place two negative pressure drainage tubes around the anastomosis and lead them out through abdominal stab wounds. Count sponges and instruments, close abdomen, surgery completed.

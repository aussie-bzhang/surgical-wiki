## Overview
Gastric-jejunal anastomosis for gastric cancer with pyloric obstruction, involving bypass surgery. Key technical feature: creating a gastric-jejunal anastomosis to relieve obstruction.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer with pyloric obstruction
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer with pyloric obstruction
**Pathological:** Metastatic poorly differentiated adenocarcinoma

## Intraoperative Findings
A 3cm mass in the gastric antrum, infiltrative ulcerative type, extending beyond the serosa and peritoneal sac, and involving the pancreatic capsule. Lymph nodes are enlarged and fused along the hepaticoduodenal ligament to the hilum of the liver, surrounding the common bile duct, hepatic artery, and portal vein. Hard and immovable. Negative peritoneal fluid.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, after general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Midline upper abdominal incision, exploration as described.
2. At approximately 40cm below the Treitz ligament, a 25.5mm end-to-end anastomosis stapler head is inserted into the jejunum with a purse-string suture. At approximately 7cm above the mass, an opening is made in the anterior wall of the gastric body. The long handle is inserted into the gastric body to anastomose the stomach with the jejunum in front of the colon. The opening is closed with 3-0 gastrointestinal suture, and the anastomosis is reinforced with serosal layer.
3. Hemostasis, lavage of the abdominal cavity, aspiration of effluent, placement of two negative pressure balls before and after the anastomosis, count of instruments and sponges, and layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: gastric antrum, common bile duct, liver, portal vein. (胆总管, 胰腺, 门静脉)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in mobilizing the mass and managing the surrounding structures.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of accurate localization and mobilization of the mass.
- Proper technique for creating a gastric-jejunal anastomosis.
- Management of potential complications such as bleeding and leakage.

## Complication Prevention
- Thorough hemostasis and lavage to prevent infection.
- Proper placement of drains to manage effluent.

## Overview
Distal gastrectomy for gastric cancer, with clinical context and key technical features of Billroth I reconstruction and D2 lymph node dissection.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric antrum cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric antrum cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
A large tumor measuring 8*7cm in the gastric antrum, ulcerative type, causing pyloric obstruction. The tumor invades the transverse mesocolon. Small gray-white nodules are visible in the mesentery of the small intestine. No abnormalities found in the liver, spleen, small intestine, colon, or pelvis.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping, midline upper abdominal incision, abdominal exploration as described.
2. Excision of greater omentum nodules for pathological examination.
3. Dissection of the gastrocolic ligament, right to the bulb of the duodenum, left to the splenic flexure, 2-3 branches of the left gastric short vessels were ligated and transected.
4. Dissection below the pylorus to expose the right gastroepiploic vessels, doubly transected at the root and cleared the 6th group of lymph nodes, and separated the tumor from the transverse mesocolon.
5. Subhepatic dissection to transect the lesser omentum, rightward dissection of the anterior leaf of the hepatoduodenal ligament and its lymphatic tissue, and exposure of the right gastric vessels, proper hepatic artery, and celiac trunk. Transected and doubly ligated at the root of the right gastric vessel, cleared the 7th and 8th group of lymph nodes.
6. Transected the duodenum 3cm distal to the pylorus, the distal end was placed into a 28.5mm anastomosis stapler and fixed, the stomach body was flipped upwards, the vessels at the root were ligated and sutured, and transected.
7. Clearing of the coronary vessels and lymphatic tissue on the lesser curvature (1st, 3rd groups) to the subcardia.
8. Inserted the long tube of the stapler into the residual stomach cavity, anastomosed the posterior wall of the greater curvature of the stomach body at the middle-third near the duodenum, 90mm clamp stapler transected the stomach body, removed the specimen, and reinforced the anastomosis with full-thickness interrupted sutures.
9. Excision of small intestinal mesenteric nodules.
10. Thorough hemostasis, lavage of the abdominal cavity, aspiration of exudate, placement of two negative pressure drains at the anterior and posterior aspects of the anastomosis.
11. Counting sponges and instruments, closing the layers in sequence.

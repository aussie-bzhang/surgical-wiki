## Overview
Gastric cancer resection with Billroth I reconstruction and D2 lymphadenectomy for a patient with gastric cancer located at the lesser curvature of the gastric angle.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located at the lesser curvature of the gastric angle near the posterior wall, approximately 1.5 cm in diameter, ulcerative infiltrative type, not involving the serosa. No metastasis to liver, pelvis, small intestine, or colon.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping, midline upper abdominal incision around the umbilicus, exploration as described above;
2. Transection of the gastrocolic ligament on the intestinal side, right to the bulb of the duodenum below, left to the 2-3 branches of the left gastric short vessels at the splenic flexure and transected and ligated, stripping the anterior leaf of the transverse colon mesentery to the superior margin of the pancreas;
3. Separation below the pylorus to expose the right gastric网膜血管, doubly transected at its root, and the 6th group of lymph nodes were dissected;
4. Transection of the lesser omentum below the liver, stripping the anterior leaf of the hepatoduodenal ligament and the lymphoid tissue within, exposing the right gastric vessel, transected and doubly ligated at the root of the right gastric vessel, and the 8th group of lymph nodes were dissected.
5. Transection of the duodenum 3 cm distal to the pylorus, the distal end was placed into a 28.5mm anastomotic stapler cartridge and fixed, the gastric body was flipped upwards, the left gastric vessel was transected and doubly ligated at the root, and the 7th group of lymph nodes were dissected; the coronary vessel and the lymphoid tissue on the lesser curvature (groups 1, 3) were cleared until below the cardia;
6. A hole was made in the anterior gastric wall to insert the long tube of the stapler, 7 cm above the tumor, at the middle-third of the gastric body on the greater curvature, anastomosis with the distal end of the duodenum, 90mm clamp stapler transected the gastric body, removed the specimen, and the anastomosis and the distal end were reinforced with a layer of seromuscular layer.
7. Hemostasis, lavage of the peritoneal cavity, aspiration of the effluent, placement of two negative pressure balls at the anastomosis, and layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: stomach, gallbladder, duodenum, spleen, colon. (胆总管)
> Danger zones: areas around the vessels and lymph nodes.
> Structures requiring protection: vessels and nerves.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in lymph node dissection and anastomosis.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of accurate lymph node dissection.
- Proper handling of vessels to prevent bleeding and injury.
- Technique for anastomosis to ensure a secure and leak-proof connection.

## Complication Prevention
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper anastomotic technique to prevent leakage and stricture.

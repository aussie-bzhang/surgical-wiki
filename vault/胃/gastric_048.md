## Overview
Gastric cancer resection with Billroth I reconstruction and D2 lymph node dissection in a patient with gastric cancer.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located on the lesser curvature of the gastric antrum, approximately 4*5cm, infiltrative ulcerative type, hard consistency, infiltrating deep muscle layer. Lymph nodes in groups 3, 6, 7, 8, and 12 around the stomach were enlarged.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping, midline upper abdominal incision, abdominal exploration as described, placement of wound protector and automatic retractor.
2. Dissection of the gastrocolic ligament on the intestinal side, right to the inferior part of the duodenal bulb, left to the upper 2-3 branches of the left gastric short vessels and ligation.
3. Dissection below the pylorus to expose the right gastric网膜血管, double ligature at the root, and lymph node dissection of group 6.
4. Dissection below the liver to transect the lesser omentum, right to the prehepatic leaf of the hepatoduodenal ligament and its lymphatic tissue, and expose the right gastric vessel, proper hepatic artery, and celiac trunk. Double ligature at the root of the right gastric vessel and lymph node dissection of groups 7, 8, and 12.
5. Transection of the duodenum 3cm distal to the pylorus, the distal end made into a purse-string suture and placed into a 28mm anastomosis stapler trocar and fixed. The gastric body is flipped up, the vessel root ligated and sutured, and transected.
6. Clearing of the coronary vessel and lymphatic tissue on the lesser curvature (groups 1, 3) to below the cardia.
7. Insertion of the long tube of the stapler into the residual gastric cavity, anastomosis of the posterior wall of the gastric body at the middle third of the greater curvature to the distal end of the duodenum with a 90mm clamp, removal of the specimen, and full-thickness interrupted reinforcement of the anastomosis.
8. Thorough hemostasis, lavage of the peritoneal cavity, aspiration of effluent, placement of two negative pressure drains at the anterior and posterior aspects of the anastomosis.
9. Counting sponges and instruments, closing the layers in sequence.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: stomach, lymph nodes, duodenum. (胆总管)
> Danger zones: areas around the vessels and lymph nodes.
> Structures requiring protection: vessels and nerves.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in lymph node dissection and anastomosis.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of careful lymph node dissection.
- Proper handling of vessels to prevent bleeding and injury.
- Technique for anastomosis to ensure a secure and leak-proof connection.

## Complication Prevention
- Thorough hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper anastomotic technique to prevent leakage.

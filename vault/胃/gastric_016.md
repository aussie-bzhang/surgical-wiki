## Overview
Gastric cancer resection with D2+ lymph node dissection, performed for locally advanced gastric cancer.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
A mass was found at the lesser curvature of the upper stomach, near the esophagus, measuring approximately 4cm in diameter, infiltrative. Lymph nodes in the 1st and 2nd groups were enlarged and adherent to the pancreatic capsule. No abnormalities were found in the liver, gallbladder, pancreas, small intestine, and colon.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia successful, routine disinfection and draping, midupper abdominal midline incision, layer-by-layer abdominal exploration as described above;
2. The transverse colon was lifted, the gastrocolic ligament was separated and cut, extending to the right to the hepatic flexure of the colon and to the inferior pole of the spleen, the splenocolic ligament was cut, the anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon was stripped to the superior margin of the pancreas, exposing the posterior wall of the stomach, and the gastropancreatic fold was cut.
3. The right gastroepiploic vessels were preserved, and the greater curvature of the stomach body was cut at the avascular area, and the greater omentum was cut along the greater curvature of the stomach.
4. The short gastric vessels were clamped and cut one by one, the stomach body was separated along the greater curvature of the stomach body, the left side of the cardia was freed, and the spleen was protected.
5. The lesser omentum was cut below the liver, the hepatoduodenal ligament was freed, and the space between the enlarged lymph nodes and the left gastric vessels was carefully dissected. The left gastric vessels were cut and ligated and sutured at the root.
6. The omentum was separated upwards along the lesser omentum, and the lower segment of the esophagus was freed to the left. The vagus nerve was clamped and cut, and the esophagus was cut 2cm above the cardia. The distal end was placed in a 25.5mm tube-shaped anastomosis stapler head for anastomosis.
7. The anterior wall of the stomach body was incised, and the tube-shaped anastomosis stapler was placed approximately 7cm below the mass to perform a side-to-side anastomosis of the stomach and esophagus. The stomach was transected 2cm and 90mm from the anastomosis orifice with a clamp, and the specimen was removed. The seromuscular layer of the anastomosis orifice and the residual stomach body were buried and sutured to reinforce.
8. Hemostasis was achieved, the abdominal cavity was flushed, and the effluent was aspirated. Two negative pressure balls were placed above and below the anastomosis, the instruments and sponges were counted and found to be correct, the abdomen was closed layer by layer, and the operation was completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: stomach, esophagus, lymph nodes, pancreas. (胆总管)
> Danger zones: areas of potential injury during dissection.
> Structures requiring protection: spleen, left gastric vessels.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included the adherence of lymph nodes to the pancreas and the need for careful dissection to preserve the spleen.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of careful dissection around the pancreas and spleen to avoid injury.
- The technique of using a tube-shaped anastomosis stapler for esophagogastric anastomosis.
- The need for thorough hemostasis and reinforcement of the anastomosis site.

## Complication Prevention
- Thorough hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Reinforcement of the anastomosis site to prevent anastomotic leakage.

## Overview
Gastric cancer resection with Billroth I reconstruction and D2 lymph node dissection. Intraoperative findings revealed gastric ulcer with atypical hyperplasia.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric ulcer with possible malignancy.
**Intraoperative:** Gastric ulcer with atypical hyperplasia.
**Pathological:** Not documented.

## Intraoperative Findings
Gastric antrum ulcer with a maximum diameter of 1.0 cm and smaller ones of 0.5 cm. Local mucosa thickened, irregular edges. Lymph nodes in groups 3, 5, and 6 were enlarged. Negative findings in liver and pelvis.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Midline upper abdominal incision.
2. Mobilization of transverse colon, omentum, and anterior leaf of transverse mesocolon, as well as the capsule of the pancreas.
3. Clamping and transection of the gastrocolic ligament, right to the hepatic flexure of the transverse colon, left to the splenic flexure.
4. Dissection of the right gastroepiploic artery below the antrum, ligated and transected with two ties, mobilizing the antrum and relaxing the first segment of the duodenum.
5. Transection of the lesser omentum below the liver, mobilization of the anterior leaf of the hepatoduodenal ligament and lymphoid tissue.
6. Clamping, transecting, and ligating the right gastric artery at the vascular root.
7. Mobilization and ligation of the gastroepiploic vein along the lesser curvature.
8. Clamping, transecting, and ligating 2-3 branches of the short gastric vessels along the greater curvature, protecting the spleen.
9. Transection of the duodenum 2 cm distal to the pylorus, the distal end placed into a 28.5 mm circular stapler and fixed with purse-string suture.
10. Mobilization of the gastric body, clamping, transecting, and ligating the left gastric artery at the vascular root.
11. Transection of the gastric body at the middle segment, removing the specimen.
12. Incision of the anterior wall of the residual stomach, anastomosis with the duodenum, and removal of the long tube of the stapler, followed by continuous suture closure of the anterior wall incision, reinforced with seromuscular layer and additional seromuscular layer reinforcement at the anastomosis.
13. Thorough hemostasis, lavage of the peritoneal cavity, aspiration of exudate, placement of a drain below the liver and at the anastomosis, hemostasis, closure of the abdomen, completion of the procedure.

## Overview
Gastric cancer resection with Billroth I reconstruction and D2 lymph node dissection. Involves resection of the stomach and surrounding structures, followed by reconstruction of the gastrointestinal tract.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located on the lesser curvature of the gastric antrum, approximately 1.5 cm in diameter, superficially excavated type.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping, midline upper abdominal incision, peritoneal cavity exploration as described.
2. Transection of the gastrocolic ligament on the intestinal side, right to the bulb of the duodenum, left to the upper 2-3 branches of the left gastric vessels at the splenic flexure and transected and ligated, stripping of the anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon to the superior margin of the pancreas.
3. Dissection below the pylorus to expose the right gastric vascular pedicle, doubly transected at its root, and the 6th group of lymph nodes dissected.
4. Dissection below the liver to transect the lesser omentum, stripping the anterior leaf of the hepatoduodenal ligament and the lymphoid tissue within, exposing the right gastric vessel, proper hepatic artery, celiac trunk, transected and doubly ligated at the root of the right gastric vessel, and the 7th and 8th group of lymph nodes dissected.
5. Transection of the duodenum 3 cm distal to the pylorus, the distal end made into a purse-string suture and placed into a 28.5 mm anastomosis stapler trocar and fixed, the gastric body flipped upwards, the vascular root ligated and sutured, and transected.
6. Dissection of the coronary vessel and lymphoid tissue on the lesser curvature (1st and 3rd groups) to below the cardia.
7. Insertion of the long tube of the stapler into the residual gastric cavity, anastomosis of the posterior wall of the gastric body at the middle-third of the greater curvature to the distal end of the duodenum, 60mm clamp stapler transected the gastric body, removed the specimen, and the anastomosis was reinforced with full-thickness interrupted sutures.
8. Thorough hemostasis, lavage of the peritoneal cavity, aspiration of exudate, placement of two negative pressure drains anterior and posterior to the anastomosis.
9. Counting sponges and instruments, closure in layers.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: stomach, gallbladder, duodenum, spleen, colon, liver, pancreas. (胆总管)
> Danger zones: hepatic artery, celiac trunk, splenic artery.
> Structures requiring protection: spleen, pancreas, superior mesenteric artery.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in lymph node dissection and anastomosis technique.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of accurate lymph node dissection.
- Technique of anastomosis and prevention of leakage.
- Management of complications such as bleeding and leakage.

## Complication Prevention
- Thorough hemostasis.
- Adequate anastomotic reinforcement.
- Early detection and management of complications.

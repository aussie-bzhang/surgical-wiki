## Overview
Gastric cancer resection with Billroth I reconstruction and D2 lymphadenectomy. Involves resection of the stomach, duodenum, and surrounding lymph nodes.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located at the lesser curvature of the gastric angle, approximately 3.0 cm in diameter, infiltrative ulcerative type, surrounding mucosa showing volcanic-like aggregation. Lymph node group 7 enlarged, not involving the serosa. Negative exploration of liver, pelvis, and abdominal wall.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping, midline upper abdominal incision, layer-by-layer laparotomy, hemostasis, and skin protection.
2. Dissection of the right gastrocolic ligament to the second segment of the duodenum, left to the splenocolic ligament, stripping of the anterior leaf of the transverse colon mesentery, separation of the posterior gastric wall space, and mobilization of the gastric pancreas fold to the superior margin of the pancreas.
3. Dissection of the lesser omentum below the liver, stripping of the anterior leaf of the hepatoduodenal ligament and its lymphatic tissue.
4. Dissection of the right gastric and greater omentum vessels at the vascular root, double ligature; transection of the duodenum 2 cm distal to the pylorus, the distal end sutured into a purse-string and fixed with a 28# anastomosis stapler.
5. Mobilization of the gastric body, dissection of the left gastric vessels at the vascular root, and removal of the gastric coronary vessels and lymphatic tissue along the lesser curvature to the subcardia.
6. Opening the anterior gastric wall, inserting the long tube of the stapler at 7 cm superior to the tumor, performing end-to-side anastomosis between the posterior wall of the stomach and the duodenum, clamping and transecting the gastric body 2 cm from the anastomosis with a 90-degree clamp, removing the specimen for further pathological examination, and reinforcing the anastomosis and gastric body stump with a seromuscular suture.
7. Thorough hemostasis, lavage of the abdominal cavity, aspiration of exudate, instillation of Zinostat and Vantopan in the abdominal cavity, placement of two negative pressure balls at the anastomosis and anterior and posterior to it, and extraction through a right upper abdominal stab wound.
8. Counting sponges and instruments, closing the incision in layers, and completion of the procedure.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: stomach, common bile duct, gallbladder, duodenum, spleen, colon, lymph nodes. (胆总管, 胆囊, 十二指肠, 脾, 结肠, 淋巴结)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in mobilization of the stomach and duodenum, and in anastomosis.

## Teaching Points
- Proper mobilization of the stomach and duodenum
- Accurate lymphadenectomy
- Safe anastomosis

## Complication Prevention
- Thorough hemostasis
- Proper lavage of the abdominal cavity

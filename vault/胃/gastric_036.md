## Overview
Gastric wedge resection for a gastrointestinal stromal tumor (GIST) located in the posterior wall of the gastric body, with a diameter of approximately 7*5cm.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastrointestinal stromal tumor
**Intraoperative:** Gastric GIST
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located in the posterior wall of the gastric body, hard consistency, slightly rough surface, extracavitary growth, diameter about 7*5cm, rich blood supply.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping, midline upper abdominal incision, placement of wound protector and automatic retractor.
2. Dissection of the gastrocolic ligament on the greater curvature side, ligation and division up to the gastric fundus, protecting the spleen.
3. Incision of the omentum, exposure of the gastric body and fundus, decision on surgical approach.
4.楔形完整切除肿块，距肿块边缘3cm using a 100cm clamp.
5. Branch ligation of omental vessels, resection of part of the omentum.
6. Thorough hemostasis, lavage of the peritoneal cavity, aspiration of effluent fluid.
7. Layer-by-layer closure.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: gastric body, gastric antrum. (胃体, 胃窦)

## Technical Challenges
Not documented

## Teaching Points
- Importance of protecting the spleen during dissection.
- Proper hemostasis and lavage techniques to prevent postoperative complications.

## Complication Prevention
- Adequate hemostasis and lavage to prevent infection and adhesion formation.

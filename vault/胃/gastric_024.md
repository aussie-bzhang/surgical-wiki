## Overview
Gastrectomy with esophagojejunostomy is a surgical procedure performed for advanced gastric cancer. It involves the removal of the entire stomach and the creation of a new alimentary tract by connecting the esophagus to the jejunum.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
A mass was found on the lesser curvature of the stomach, measuring approximately 4cm in diameter, with an ulcerative type and extending from the lower esophagus to the pylorus. The mass involved the serosal surface. Lymph nodes in groups 1, 6, 7, 8, and 12 around the stomach were enlarged. Exploration of the liver, spleen, and pelvis was unremarkable.

## Operative Steps
1. The patient was placed in a supine position under general anesthesia. The abdomen was accessed via a midline upper abdominal incision.
2. The gastrocolic ligament was separated, extending to the hepatic flexure on the right and the inferior pole of the spleen on the left. The anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon was stripped to the surface of the pancreas, and the lesser omentum was opened to access the lesser curvature.
3. The right gastroepiploic artery was transected and ligated at the root of the pancreatic head, and surrounding lymph nodes were cleared.
4. The lesser omentum was transected below the liver, extending to the hepatoduodenal ligament on the right and the celiac axis. Lymph nodes around the hepatoduodenal ligament and celiac axis were dissected, and the right gastric artery was transected and ligated.
5. At 3cm from the pylorus, the duodenum was transected and closed with a 60mm clamp and sutured for reinforcement.
6. The left gastroepiploic and short gastric vessels were transected along the greater curvature until the left side of the cardia, and the stomach was mobilized upwards.
7. The esophagus was transected at the level of the cardia, and the specimen was removed. A 25.5# tube-shaped anastomosis stapler was used to create the anastomosis, and the staple line was fixed with purse-string sutures.
8. The jejunum was transected 20cm from the Treitz ligament, and the esophagus was anastomosed to the distal jejunum in an end-to-side fashion. The distal jejunum was closed with a clamp. The proximal jejunum was anastomosed to the jejunum 60cm below the esophageal anastomosis (in front of the colon); the mesenteric gap was closed.
9. Hemostasis was achieved, the abdomen was irrigated, and fluid was aspirated. Two drains were placed. Instruments and sponges were counted and found to be correct. The abdomen was closed in layers.

## Anatomical Key Points
> The common bile duct (胆总管), lymph nodes in groups 1, 6, 7, 8, and 12, and the Treitz ligament (屈氏韧带) are critical structures. Care must be taken to protect these during the procedure.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included mobilization of the stomach, lymph node dissection, and anastomosis construction.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of careful mobilization of the stomach and lymph node dissection.
- The technique of creating a safe and secure anastomosis.
- The management of potential complications during the procedure.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper anastomotic technique to reduce the risk of anastomotic leakage.

## Overview
Gastrectomy with Billroth I reconstruction for duodenal leiomyoma, involving resection of the greater curvature of the stomach and reconstruction of the alimentary tract.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Duodenal leiomyoma
**Intraoperative:** Duodenal leiomyoma
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
A 3.5*3.5*3.0cm mass in the duodenal bulb, firm, growing outward, not involving the mucosa. No obvious mass in the pancreas detected by intraoperative ultrasound.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Midline upper abdominal incision.
2. Incise the omentum, strip the pre-colic peritoneum to the superior margin of the pancreas, transect the gastrocolic ligament on the intestinal side, right to the second segment of the duodenum, left to the upper 2-3 branches of the left gastric vessels at the splenic flexure and ligate and divide.
3. Transect the lesser omentum below the liver, ligate the left gastric vessels near the corpus of the stomach.
4. Transect and ligate the right gastric and right gastroepiploic vessels at the vascular root; transect the duodenum 2cm distal to the mass, and place the distal end in a purse-string suture with a 28# anastomosis stapler.
5. Open the anterior gastric wall, insert the long tube of the stapler, perform a side-to-end anastomosis between the middle of the gastric greater curvature and the duodenum, and reinforce the anastomosis with a layer of seromuscular layer.
6. Thorough hemostasis, lavage the abdominal cavity, aspirate the effluent, place two negative pressure balls at the anastomosis and lead them out through a stab wound in the upper right abdomen, close the layers in sequence.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Duodenal bulb (十二指肠球部), common bile duct (胆总管), pancreas (胰腺)

## Technical Challenges
Not documented

## Teaching Points
- Importance of identifying and preserving the common bile duct and pancreas.
- Proper handling of the gastrocolic ligament and left gastric vessels.
- Technique of using the stapler for anastomosis.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Adequate lavage to reduce the risk of infection.

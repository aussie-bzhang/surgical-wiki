## Overview
Gastric cancer resection with Billroth I reconstruction and D2 lymph node dissection. Involves extensive lymphadenectomy and resection of the distal stomach.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric antrum cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric antrum cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Posterior wall of the gastric antrum with a 3.0*2.0cm mass involving the serosa, with a 0.8cm mucosal erosion at the inferior margin. Lymph nodes in groups 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 12 were enlarged and partially fused. Negative exploration of liver, small intestine, colon, gallbladder, and pelvis.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia, routine disinfection, and draping.
2. Midline upper abdominal incision.
3. Dissection of the gastrocolic ligament, right to the second segment of the duodenum, left to the splenic flexure of the colon, preserving 3 branches of the short gastric vessels.
4. Separation of the anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon to the superior margin of the pancreas.
5. Dissection of the lesser omentum below the liver, anatomizing the hepatoduodenal ligament, and removing lymphatic tissue.
6. Dissection and ligation of the right gastric and right gastroepiploic vessels at the vascular pedicle, and transection of the duodenum 3cm distal to the pylorus with a purse-string suture for the 28# anastomosis device.
7. Flipping up the gastric body, dissection and ligation of the left gastric vessels, and removal of lymph nodes in groups 7 and 9.
8. Dissection along the hepatic artery to remove lymph nodes in group 8.
9. Removal of the gastric cardia and lymphatic tissue along the lesser curvature until below the cardia.
10. Opening the anterior wall of the gastric body, inserting the 28# anastomosis device long tube, and performing a side-to-side anastomosis between the posterior wall of the gastric body and the duodenum.
11. Transection of the gastric body with the anastomosis device 2cm from the anastomosis site, removing the specimen, and reinforcing the stump.
12. Thorough hemostasis, irrigation of the peritoneal cavity, aspiration of effusions, placement of two negative pressure balls at the anastomosis site, and suture closure in layers.

## Overview
Gastrectomy for gastric mesenchymoma, a clinical context involving a localized gastric mesenchymoma, key technical feature being the radical resection and reconstruction of the stomach.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric mesenchymoma
**Intraoperative:** Gastric mesenchymoma
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a localized gastric mesenchymoma located at the lesser curvature of the gastric antrum, measuring 4cm in diameter, with a bulging type protrusion into the serosa. No enlarged lymph nodes were found around the stomach, and the liver, small intestine, colon, and pelvic area were all negative.

## Operative Steps
1. After general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping were performed. An upper midline incision was made to enter the abdomen, and the findings were as described above.
2. The gastrocolic ligament was transected on the intestinal side, extending from below the duodenal bulb to the upper 2-3 branches of the left gastric short vessels, which were then ligated and transected.
3. The transverse mesocolon was stripped to the superior margin of the pancreas.
4. The right gastric artery at the root of the gastroepiploic artery was transected and doubly ligated; the lesser omentum was transected below the liver, extending to the anterior leaf of the hepatoduodenal ligament, and transected at the root of the right gastric artery, doubly ligated.
5. The duodenum was transected 3cm distal to the pylorus, and the distal end was placed into a 28.5mm anastomotic stapler trocar and fixed for use.
6. The gastric body was flipped upwards, and the left gastric artery at the root was transected and doubly ligated; the coronary vessels and lymphatic tissue on the lesser curvature were cleared upwards to below the cardia.
7. The stomach was transected 3cm above the tumor using a 90mm clamp, and the specimen was removed. After opening the gastric stump, the long tube of the stapler was inserted, and an end-to-end anastomosis was performed between the posterior wall of the stomach 5cm above the tumor and the duodenum. After removing the stapler, the opening was closed with a 60mm clamp. The anastomosis and the gastric stump were reinforced with a layer of serosal muscle.
8. Hemostasis was achieved, the abdomen was irrigated, and the effluent was aspirated. Two negative pressure bulbs were placed at the anastomosis and led out through a stab incision in the upper right abdomen. The layers were closed in sequence. The operation was completed.

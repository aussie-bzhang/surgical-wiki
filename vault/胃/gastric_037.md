## Overview
Gastrectomy for gastric cancer, a surgical procedure involving the removal of the upper part of the stomach due to carcinoma of the cardia, with exploration and resection of adjacent structures.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located at the cardia, ulcerative infiltrative type, approximately 6*5*1cm in size, penetrating the serosa, involving the lower end of the esophagus; exploration of liver, pancreas, small intestine, colon, pelvis, abdominal wall, ascites (-).

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia, tracheal double-ballooned tube insertion successful, left side elevated 60 degrees, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Upper abdominal midline incision, layer-by-layer entering the abdomen, exploration of the abdomen as described above.
3. Take the 6th intercostal space on the left to the upper abdominal midline incision, cut the costal arch, open the pleural cavity.
4. Median incision of the diaphragm to the esophageal hiatus, incision of the left coronary ligament and left triangular ligament, incision of the extrapericardial fibrous tissue, free the lower segment of the esophageal chest.
5. Near the intestinal side, transect the gastrocolic ligament, lift the transverse colon, strip the anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon to the superior margin of the pancreas, retain the right gastroepiploic vessels, and transect the greater omentum at the avascular area of the gastric body, free the left side of the cardia.
6. Free the surrounding fibrous fatty tissue of the esophagus at the level of the cardia, dissect the lymph nodes, transect both vagus nerves, transect the esophagus 7cm above the cardia with a purse-string clamp, and place the anvil of a 25mm tube-shaped anastomosis stapler into the esophagus and fix it with a purse-string suture.
7. Transect the middle and lower third of the gastric body 90mm with a clamp, remove the specimen, incise the anterior wall of the remaining stomach, place the tube-shaped anastomosis stapler for anastomosis of the remaining stomach to the esophageal end, suture and reinforce the anastomosis with a layer of serosal muscle.
8. Anastomose the esophagus with the distal stomach, close the ileal end, anastomose the proximal ileum with the distal ileum 50cm below the esophageal anastomosis, close the mesenteric gap.
9. Suture and close the diaphragm, reconstruct the esophageal hiatus.
10. Thorough hemostasis, wash the abdominal cavity, remove the effusion, place a negative pressure ball below the esophageal anastomosis, puncture and lead out, place a chest drain in the pleural cavity, close the pleural cavity under negative pressure.
11. Count instruments and gauze, close the abdomen in layers.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: esophagus, stomach, spleen, gallbladder, pancreas, colon, ileum. (胆总管)
> Danger zones: esophageal hiatus, splenic hilum.
> Structures requiring protection: spleen, pancreas, bile duct.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of meticulous dissection around the spleen and pancreas to avoid injury.
- Proper handling of the esophagus and bile duct during resection.
- The technique of using a stapler for anastomosis.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Adequate drainage to prevent abscess formation.

## Overview
Gastrectomy with splenectomy and partial colectomy for gastric cancer, Borr IV type, with esophagojejunostomy.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer, Borr IV type possible.
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer, Borr IV type.
**Pathological:** Not documented.

## Intraoperative Findings
Significant findings: marked reduction in stomach volume, rigid gastric wall, absence of peristalsis, extensive involvement of the lesser curvature, spleen and transverse colon, enlarged lymph nodes at the hilum of the spleen, and involvement of the middle segment of the transverse colon on the left side leading to marked distension of the proximal colon. No liver, pelvic, or ascites involvement.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Midline upper abdominal incision, exploration as described, followed by a left transverse incision.
2. Dissection of the greater omentum from the tumor-involved site of the transverse colon to the right to the hepatic flexure and to the left to the inferior pole of the spleen.
3. Cutting and ligating the splenocolic ligament, incising the lateral peritoneum of the spleen upwards to the splenohilar ligament, and separating the splenic vessels at the superior margin of the pancreas.
4. Raising the transverse colon, exposing the middle colic artery, cutting and ligating the left branch.
5. V-shaped incision of the mesocolon on both sides of the ligated site, cutting the transverse colon at the tumor-involved site on both sides.
6. Dissection of the anterior leaf of the mesocolon to the superior margin of the pancreas, clearing the lymph nodes above the pancreas.
7. High ligation and division of the right gastric vessels at the superior margin of the pancreas, clearing the surrounding lymph nodes.
8. Division of the lesser omentum below the liver, stripping the anterior leaf of the hepatoduodenal ligament and the lymphoid tissue, and high ligation and division of the right gastric vessels.
9. At 3 cm from the pylorus, the duodenum is divided with a 55 cm side-to-side stapler and sutured with purse-string.
10. Raising the gastric body, division and double ligature of the left gastric vessels at the root, separating the lower esophagus upwards, cutting the esophagus 3 cm above the cardia, removing the specimen, and inserting the anvil of a 28F tube stapler with purse-string fixation.
11. Division of the jejunum 15 cm from the Treitz ligament, anastomosing the distal end with the lower esophagus using a stapler, reinforcing the anastomosis with several sutures, and closing the distal end with a 55 cm side-to-side stapler.
12. Anastomosing the proximal jejunum with the jejunum 60 cm from the anastomosis, performing an end-to-end anastomosis of the transverse colon, closing the mesenteric gaps.
13. Thorough hemostasis, lavage of the peritoneal cavity, aspiration of effluent, placement of a Pasma tube in the splenic fossa, and placement of a negative pressure ball from the left side of the anastomosis to the splenic fossa and from below the liver to the right side of the anastomosis, respectively, draining through the wound and the right upper quadrant.
14. Counting instruments and sponges, ensuring accuracy, and closing the abdomen in layers.

## Teaching Points
- High ligation and division of vessels to prevent bleeding.
- Proper handling of the esophagus and duodenum to avoid injury.
- Accurate placement of the anastomosis to ensure proper function.

## Complication Prevention
- Thorough hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper anastomotic technique to prevent leaks and strictures.

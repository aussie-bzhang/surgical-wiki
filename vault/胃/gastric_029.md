## Overview
Gastrectomy with pyloroplasty for gastric cancer, involving extensive resection and reconstruction of the pylorus.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a 7.0cm mass at the cardia and fundus of the stomach, infiltrative-ulcerative type, extending beyond the serosa, involving the left lobe of the liver, diaphragm, pancreatic capsule, spleen. There were scattered enlarged lymph nodes, and the lymph nodes on the lesser curvature fused into a mass surrounding the left gastric artery. No metastasis was found in the hepatopelvic region, and no ascites were present.

## Operative Steps
1. Patient placed in supine position under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Abdominal exploration via an upper midline incision.
2. Bluntly separate the mass from surrounding tissues, exposing the fundus and lower esophagus. Dissect and transect the gastrocolic ligament, extending to the right to the hepatic flexure of the colon and to the inferior pole of the spleen.
3. Continue with the dissection, transecting the splenocolic ligament.
4. Raise the transverse colon, strip the anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon to the superior margin of the pancreas, preserving the right gastroepiploic vessels, and transect the greater omentum at the avascular area of the greater curvature of the stomach.
5. Clamp and transect the left gastric vessels, dissect the stomach body along the greater curvature, freeing the left side of the cardia, protecting the spleen.
6. Transect the lesser omentum below the liver, free the hepatoduodenal ligament, carefully dissect and separate the enlarged lymph nodes from the left gastric artery, ligate and tie off at the root. Continue the dissection upwards along the lesser omentum, divide the esophageal hiatus and the parietal peritoneum on both sides, free the lower esophagus, clamp and transect the vagus nerve 3cm above the cardia, and place the head of a 25.5# tube-shaped anastomosis stapler.
7. Clamp and transect the stomach at the middle and lower third, remove the specimen, incise the anterior wall of the pylorus, and place the tube-shaped stapler for the side-to-side anastomosis of the stomach and esophagus, with the serosal layer around the anastomosis sutured and buried.
8. Pyloroplasty performed.
9. Thorough hemostasis, lavage the abdominal cavity, aspirate the effluent, place two negative pressure balls around the esophageal anastomosis, count instruments and sponges, close the abdomen in layers.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the stomach, esophagus, gallbladder, liver, spleen, pancreas, and lymph nodes. The common bile duct (胆总管) is a critical structure requiring protection.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included extensive adhesions, extensive involvement of surrounding structures, and the need for precise anastomotic techniques.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of careful dissection and preservation of surrounding structures.
- The technique of performing a safe and effective anastomosis.
- The management of extensive adhesions and lymphadenopathy.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Adequate lavage to reduce the risk of infection.
- Placement of drains to monitor for fluid collection.

## Related Procedures
- [[Gastrojejunostomy]] - Used as an alternative route for feeding if necessary.

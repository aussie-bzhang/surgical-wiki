## Overview
Gastrectomy for distal gastric cancer, a common clinical scenario involving a distal ulcerous mass in the lesser curvature of the stomach, infiltrating the serosa, with lymph node enlargement in groups 7, 8, and 12.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed an ulcerous mass near the antrum of the stomach on the lesser curvature, infiltrating the serosa. Lymph nodes in groups 7, 8, and 12 were enlarged. Exploration of the liver, omentum, and peritoneal fluid was negative.

## Operative Steps
1. After general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. An upper midline incision was made to enter the abdomen, and exploration was conducted as described.
2. The gastrocolic ligament was transected on the intestinal side, from the inferior aspect of the duodenal bulb to the upper aspect of the splenic flexure, with dissection of the anterior leaf of the transverse colon mesentery to the superior aspect of the pancreas.
3. The right gastroepiploic vessels were separated below the pylorus, doubly transected at their root, and the 6th group lymph nodes were dissected.
4. The lesser omentum was transected below the liver, and the anterior leaf of the hepatoduodenal ligament and its lymphatic tissue were stripped to the right, exposing the right gastric vessels, proper hepatic artery, and celiac trunk. The right gastric vessels were transected and doubly ligated at their root, and the 7th, 8th, and 12th group lymph nodes were dissected.
5. The duodenum was transected 3 cm distal to the pylorus, and the distal end was placed into a 28mm anastomosis stapler and fixed. The stomach body was flipped upwards, and the right gastric vessels were doubly ligated and sutured, then transected. The coronary vessels and lymphatic tissue on the lesser curvature (groups 1 and 3) were cleared down to the cardia.
6. A long tube of the stapler was placed into the residual stomach cavity, and an anastomosis was performed between the posterior wall of the stomach body at the middle-third and the distal duodenal stump. The stomach body was clamped and transected, and the specimen was removed. The anastomosis was reinforced with full-thickness interrupted sutures.
7. Hemostasis was achieved, the abdomen was irrigated, and fluid was aspirated. Two negative-pressure drains were placed anterior and posterior to the anastomosis.
8. The sponges and instruments were counted and found to be correct. The layers were closed in sequence.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the stomach, lymph nodes, and bile duct. The common bile duct (胆总管) requires protection during dissection.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included the extensive lymphadenectomy and the precise anastomosis technique.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of meticulous lymphadenectomy in gastric cancer surgery.
- The technique of performing a precise anastomosis to minimize postoperative complications.
- The need for careful hemostasis and abdominal irrigation.

## Complication Prevention
- Postoperative ileus was prevented by ensuring adequate anastomotic patency.
- Infection was prevented by meticulous surgical technique and appropriate antibiotic prophylaxis.

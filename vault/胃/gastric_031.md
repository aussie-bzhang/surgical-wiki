## Overview
Palliative total gastrectomy with esophagojejunostomy Roux-en-Y and antecolic approach for gastric cancer, involving extensive resection and reconstruction.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Large ulcerative infiltrative lesion in the antrum and prepyloric area of the stomach, 5cm in diameter, extending along the lesser curvature towards the cardia. Tumor invades the serosa and adheres closely to the hepatoduodenal ligament, pancreatic head, and transverse mesocolon. Lymph nodes in groups 1, 3, 5, 6, 7, 8, 12, and 13 are enlarged.串珠状 lymph nodes palpable on the lesser curvature. No metastasis found in liver, spleen, small intestine, and pelvic organs. Negative ascites.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Midline upper abdominal incision.
2. Dissection and ligation of the gastrocolic ligament along the transverse colon to the hepatic flexure, including the anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon, stripping to the surface of the pancreas. Complete resection of the infiltrated pancreatic pseudocapsule.
3. Dissection and ligation of the left gastroepiploic and short gastric vessels along the greater curvature to the fundus and cardia, lymph node dissection of group 10.
4. Dissection and ligation of the right gastroepiploic vessels at the root of the pyloricoduodenal vessels, lymph node dissection of group 6.
5. Dissection below the liver, to the hepatoduodenal ligament, separation of the lower esophagus, lymph node dissection of groups 1, 3, 5, and bilateral vagotomy. Double ligature at the root of the left gastric artery, lymph node dissection of groups 7, 8, and 9.
6. Transection of the esophagus 3cm above the cardia, placement of a 25# anastomotic stapler and fixation with purse-string suture.
7. Careful separation and skeletonization of the tumor from the hepatoduodenal ligament, ligation and transection of the right gastric vessels, lymph node dissection of group 12.
8. Separation of the tumor from the pancreatic head, transection of the duodenum 3cm from the pylorus, specimen removal, and full-thickness and muscularis mucosae suture of the duodenal stump.
9. Transection of the ileum 20cm from the Treitz ligament, end-to-side anastomosis of the esophagus and distal ileum with a 25# stapler. Side-to-side anastomosis of the ileal stump. End-to-side anastomosis of the proximal ileum 45cm below the esophageal anastomosis (antecolic). Reinforcement sutures at all anastomoses, closure of mesenteric gaps.
10. Hemostasis, lavage of the abdominal cavity, aspiration of effluent, placement of a negative pressure tube adjacent to the anastomosis, two negative pressure balls at the duodenal stump. Counting instruments and sponges, closure of the abdomen in layers.

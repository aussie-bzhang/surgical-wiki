## Overview
Gastric cancer resection with pyloroplasty, a surgical procedure for the treatment of upper gastric cancer, involving resection of the affected part of the stomach and reconstruction of the pylorus.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a mass located on the upper part of the lesser curvature of the stomach, approximately 2*2cm in size, with an ulcerative type, invasion of the serosa, adhesions in the upper right abdomen, gallbladder already resected, negative exploration of liver, small intestine, colon, and pelvic cavity.

## Operative Steps
1. Patient placed in supine position after successful general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping, and an upper midline incision made for abdominal exploration.
2. Separation of adhesions in the upper right abdomen, lifting of the transverse colon, opening of the gastrocolic ligament, stripping of the preperitoneal leaf of the transverse colon to the superior margin of the pancreas, preservation of the right gastric网膜血管, and division of the greater curvature of the stomach at the avascular area of the corpus.
3. Opening of the gastro splenic ligament, clamping and cutting of the short gastric vessels, and dissection along the greater curvature of the stomach base to the left side of the cardia, with attention to protecting the spleen.
4. Opening of the lesser omentum, dissection of the hepatogastric ligament and hepatoduodenal ligament, dissection along the hepatic artery to the root of the celiac trunk, along the way,清扫第8组淋巴结, and transection and ligation of the left gastric artery at its root,清扫第7组淋巴结, separation of the gastric coronary vein group, branch by branch clamping, cutting, and ligating, and full dissection of the lesser curvature of the stomach upwards to the right side of the cardia,清扫第1、3组淋巴结.
5. Opening of the esophageal hiatus of the diaphragm, dissection of the lower segment of the esophagus, clamping and cutting of the vagus nerve, transection of the esophagus at 3 cm above the cardia with a 25.5# tube-shaped anastomosis stapler.
6. Window opening on the anterior wall of the proximal stomach for insertion of the stapler, end-to-end anastomosis with the esophagus, clamping and cutting the stomach at 6 cm from the cardia with a 90# clamp, reinforcement suture, and placement of the distal stump 2 cm from the anastomosis.
7. Transverse incision and transverse suture at the pylorus for pyloroplasty.
8. Thorough hemostasis, irrigation of the peritoneal cavity, aspiration of effluent, placement of two negative pressure balls around the anastomosis, extrusion through abdominal stab wounds, verification of instruments and sponges, layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen, completion of the operation.

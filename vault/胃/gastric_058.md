## Overview
Gastric cancer resection with Billroth II reconstruction, antecolic, proximal to greater curvature, and D2 lymph node dissection. Involves extensive dissection and reconstruction of the upper gastrointestinal tract.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located at the posterior wall of the gastric angle, ulcerative type, diameter approximately 2.5cm, not penetrating the serosa. Chronic ulcer of the duodenal bulb with inflammatory scar. No liver or pelvic metastasis.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, after general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Midline upper abdominal incision, enter peritoneum, exploration as above.
2. Incise the gastrocolic ligament on the intestinal side, extending to the right to the hepatic flexure of the colon and to the inferior pole of the spleen.
3. Retract the transverse colon, dissect the greater omentum, the anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon, and the capsule of the pancreas, ligate and transect the right gastric artery and vein at the vascular root.
4. Clamp and transect the gastro splenic ligament along the greater curvature of the stomach, dissect the 4th and 6th groups of lymph nodes, preserve 2 branches of the short gastric vessels, and protect the spleen.
5. Open the hepatoduodenal ligament and the gastroesophageal ligament, dissect the 8th group of lymph nodes, ligate and transect the right gastric vessel at the root, mobilize the gastric antrum and the first and second segments of the duodenum, close the duodenum 3cm from the pylorus with a 60mm clamp, reinforce the residual end with a gastrointestinal suture.
6. Ligate and transect the left gastric vessels along the lesser curvature of the stomach, dissect the 1st, 3rd, 5th, and 7th groups of lymph nodes, extending upwards to the right side of the esophagus.
7. Clamp the gastric body with two Kocher clamps 7cm from the tumor margin, close the remaining gastric body with a 60mm clamp, place the anvil of a 28.5mm anastomosis stapler through a jejunum incision 20cm from the Treitz ligament and fix it with a purse-string suture, insert the long handle into the gastric residual end to anastomose the posterior wall of the stomach with the jejunum above the transverse colon, remove the stapler and close the anastomosis with a 60mm clamp and gastrointestinal suture.
8. Thorough hemostasis, lavage the peritoneal cavity, aspirate the effluent, place a negative pressure ball under the liver and at the residual duodenal end, count instruments and sponges, close the abdomen in layers. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: stomach, duodenum, liver, spleen, colon. (胆总管)
> Danger zones: vessels and lymph nodes.
> Structures requiring protection: spleen, pancreas.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in extensive dissection and reconstruction, particularly in mobilizing the duodenum and anastomosing the stomach and jejunum.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of careful dissection and lymph node dissection.
- Techniques for mobilizing the duodenum and anastomosing the stomach and jejunum.
- The significance of thorough hemostasis and lavage.

## Complication Prevention
- Thorough hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper anastomosis technique to prevent anastomotic leakage.

## Overview
Gastric cancer resection with Billroth I reconstruction, performed for suspected gastric ulcer with malignant potential.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric ulcer with suspected malignancy.
**Intraoperative:** Gastric ulcer with suspected malignancy.
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
A superficial, 2.0 cm pedunculated lesion on the lesser curvature of the gastric body near the angle of the stomach, with mucosal erosion. Lymph node group 8 was enlarged. No abnormalities were found in the liver, small intestine, or pelvis.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia and routine disinfection. A midupper abdominal incision was made.
2. Dissection of the gastrocolic ligament on the intestinal side, right to the second segment of the duodenum, left to the upper 2-3 branches of the left gastric vessels at the splenic flexure, and ligation.
3. Separation of the anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon to the superior margin of the pancreas.
4. Dissection of the lesser omentum below the liver, and stripping of the anterior leaf of the hepatoduodenal ligament and its lymphatic tissue.
5. Ligation and double-clamping of the right gastric and right gastroepiploic vessels at the vascular root; transection of the duodenum 3 cm distal to the pylorus, with the distal end placed into a 25# anastomosis stapler head and fixed.
6. Dissection of the greater curvature of the stomach, ligation and double-clamping of the left gastric vessels; removal of the coronary vessels and lymphatic tissue on the lesser curvature to the subcardia.
7. Transection of the gastric body with a side-to-side stapler at the middle-third of the gastric body, removal of the specimen, and reinforcement of the residual margin.
8. Opening the anterior wall of the residual stomach, inserting the long tube of the stapler, and performing an end-to-end anastomosis between the greater curvature and the duodenum. Suture the gastric wall after removing the long tube. Reinforce the anastomosis with a layer of seromuscular layer.
9. Hemostasis, lavage of the peritoneal cavity, aspiration of effluent, placement of two negative pressure balls at the anastomosis, and layer-by-layer closure.

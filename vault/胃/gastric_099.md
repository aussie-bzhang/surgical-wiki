## Overview
Gastrectomy (Billroth I) is a surgical procedure for the treatment of gastric ulcers, involving the resection of a portion of the stomach and reconstruction of the gastrointestinal tract. Key technical features include the anastomosis of the duodenum to the remaining stomach and the creation of a new stomach-pylorus connection.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric ulcer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric ulcer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a gastric antrum ulcer on the lesser curvature, measuring 1.5 cm in diameter with clear borders and surrounding white nodular lesions. No enlarged lymph nodes were found around the stomach. The liver, small intestine, colon, and pelvic organs were all negative.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia and routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline upper abdominal incision.
3. Dissection of the greater omentum and the right and left gastric short vessels.
4. Dissection of the transverse mesocolon to the superior margin of the pancreas.
5. Bilateral ligation and division of the right gastric and right gastroepiploic vessels at their vascular pedicles.
6. Division of the duodenum 2 cm distal to the pylorus, with the distal end placed in a 28.5 mm anvil of the stapler and secured.
7. Dissection and ligation of the left gastric vessels, removal of the coronary vessels and lymphatic tissue along the lesser curvature to the cardia.
8. Opening the anterior gastric wall and inserting the long tube of the stapler for anastomosis with the duodenum.
9. Anastomosis of the posterior gastric wall with the duodenum, removal of the specimen with a 90 mm stapler at 3 cm from the anastomosis.
10. Reinforcement of the anastomosis with a layer of seromuscular layer.
11. Hemostasis, irrigation of the abdominal cavity, aspiration of fluid, placement of negative pressure drains at the anastomosis site.
12. Closure of the abdomen in layers.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included the presence of nodular lesions around the ulcer, which required careful dissection to avoid injury to surrounding structures.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of careful dissection around the ulcer to avoid complications.
- The technique of anastomosis and reinforcement of the stomach and duodenum.
- The management of potential bleeding points during the procedure.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Adequate irrigation and aspiration to reduce the risk of infection.

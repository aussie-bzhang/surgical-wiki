## Overview
Gastric cancer resection with Billroth I reconstruction and D2 lymph node dissection. Involves resection of the stomach and surrounding structures, followed by reconstruction of the gastrointestinal tract.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located in the antrum, approximately 2cm in diameter, infiltrative ulcerative type, involving the serosa. No abnormalities found in liver, gallbladder, pancreas, spleen, small intestine, colon, pelvic cavity.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Midline upper abdominal incision. Exploration as described.
2. Transection of the gastrocolic ligament on the intestinal side, right to the bulb of the duodenum below, left to the splenic flexure with 2-3 branches of the left gastric short vessels, transected and ligated. Dissection of the anterior leaf of the transverse colon mesentery to the superior margin of the pancreas.
3. Dissection below the pylorus to expose the right gastroepiploic artery, doubly transected at its root and dissected the 6th group of lymph nodes.
4. Dissection below the liver to transect the lesser omentum, stripping the anterior leaf of the hepatoduodenal ligament and its lymphatic tissue, exposing the right gastric artery, common hepatic artery, and celiac trunk. Transected and doubly ligated at the root of the right gastric artery, dissected the 7th and 8th group of lymph nodes.
5. Transection of the duodenum 3cm distal to the pylorus, the distal end sutured into a 28.5mm anastomosis stapler and fixed. The stomach body is flipped up, the right gastric artery doubly ligated and sutured, and transected. Clearing of the coronary vessels and lymphatic tissue on the lesser curvature (1st and 3rd groups) to the subcardia.
6. At approximately 5cm from the tumor superior margin, the stomach body is clamped and incised with Kocher clamps, the remaining stomach body 60mm clamped and transected, removing the specimen. The long tube of the stapler is inserted into the stomach opening, anastomosis between the posterior wall of the stomach body near the middle and the distal duodenal stump with a 60mm clamp. The serosal layer of the anastomosis is reinforced with interrupted sutures, and the stomach opening is sutured in full thickness.
7. Thorough hemostasis, lavage of the peritoneal cavity, aspiration of exudate. Two negative pressure drains placed anterior and posterior to the anastomosis.
8. Counting sponges and instruments, closing the abdomen in layers, operation completed.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of lymph node dissection in gastric cancer.
- Technique of anastomosis with stapler.
- Management of blood loss during surgery.

## Complication Prevention
- Adequate hemostasis.
- Proper anastomosis technique to prevent leaks.
- Early detection and management of complications.

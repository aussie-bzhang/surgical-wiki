## Overview
Gastrectomy for distal gastric cancer, a common clinical scenario involving a 3*3cm ulcerative tumor located at the lesser curvature of the gastric angle, infiltrating the serosa. Key technical feature includes radical resection with Billroth II anastomosis, D2 lymph node dissection, and colon-preserving technique.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a 3*3cm ulcerative tumor at the lesser curvature of the gastric angle, infiltrating the serosa. Exploration of liver, gallbladder, pancreas, small intestine, peritoneum, and pelvic cavity were negative.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, after general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Abdominal exploration via an upper midline incision.
2. Incision of the gastrocolic ligament, mobilization of the greater omentum, and dissection of the gastroepiploic vessels.
3. Mobilization of the greater curvature of the stomach, lymph node dissection of the 4th and 6th groups, preservation of 2 gastric short vessels, and protection of the spleen.
4. Mobilization of the lesser omentum below the liver, dissection of the hepatoduodenal ligament, and lymph node dissection of the 8th and 12th groups.
5. Ligation and transection of the right gastric vessels, mobilization of the pylorus and duodenum, and transection of the duodenum 3cm from the pylorus.
6. Mobilization of the lesser curvature of the stomach, lymph node dissection of the 1st, 3rd, 5th, and 7th groups, and transection of the left gastric artery.
7. Ligation and transection of the stomach at 7cm above the tumor, and anastomosis with the jejunum 15cm from the Treitz ligament.
8. Complete hemostasis, irrigation of the abdominal cavity, placement of drains, and closure of the abdomen.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the stomach, colon, spleen, liver, gallbladder, pancreas, small intestine, peritoneum, and pelvic cavity. The common bile duct (胆总管) requires protection during dissection.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered include mobilization of the greater curvature of the stomach and lymph node dissection. These were managed with careful dissection and meticulous hemostasis.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of careful mobilization and lymph node dissection.
- The technique of Billroth II anastomosis and colon-preserving technique.
- The need for complete hemostasis and irrigation of the abdominal cavity.

## Complication Prevention
- Infection: Prophylactic antibiotics and meticulous surgical technique.
- Anastomotic leakage: Adequate anastomotic tension and reinforcement.

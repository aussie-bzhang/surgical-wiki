## Overview
Gastrectomy with esophagojejunostomy is a major surgical procedure for advanced gastric cancer. It involves the removal of the entire stomach and the creation of a new alimentary tract by connecting the esophagus to the jejunum.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a mass in the gastric body, approximately 8 cm in diameter, with infiltrative ulcerative type, penetrating the serosa, and involving the surface of the pancreas. Exploration of liver, spleen, and pelvis were negative.

## Operative Steps
1. The patient was placed in a supine position under general anesthesia. The abdomen was entered through a midline upper abdominal incision.
2. The gastrocolic ligament was separated, extending to the right to the hepatic flexure of the colon and to the inferior pole of the spleen. The anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon was stripped to the surface of the pancreas, and the lesser omentum was opened to the left side.
3. The right gastric artery was ligated and divided at the root of the pancreatic head under the pylorus, and the surrounding lymph nodes were cleared.
4. The lesser omentum was divided below the liver, extending to the hepatoduodenal ligament on the right, and the lymph nodes around the celiac trunk were dissected, with the right gastric artery ligated and divided.
5. The duodenum was divided and sutured at 3 cm from the pylorus.
6. The left gastric artery at the root was divided and ligated, and the stomach was separated along the greater curvature, with the left gastroepiploic artery and short gastric vessels divided until the left side of the cardia.
7. The stomach was flipped upwards, and the esophagus was divided at the cardia. The specimen was removed, and a tube anastomosis stapler was placed with purse-string sutures.
8. The jejunum was divided 20 cm from the Treitz ligament, and the esophagus was anastomosed to the distal jejunum with a stapler. The jejunal stump was closed with a clamp. The proximal jejunum was anastomosed to the distal jejunum 60 cm below the esophageal anastomosis (in front of the colon).
9. The mesenteric gaps were closed, and hemostasis was achieved. The abdomen was irrigated, and two drains were placed. Instruments and sponges were counted and found to be correct. The abdomen was closed in layers.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the stomach, esophagus, jejunum, and pancreas. The common bile duct (胆总管) should be protected during dissection.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included the extensive involvement of the pancreas and the need for precise anastomosis of the esophagus and jejunum.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of careful dissection around the pancreas to avoid injury.
- The technique of creating a secure and functional esophagojejunostomy.
- The need for meticulous hemostasis and careful closure of the abdominal wall.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Adequate drainage to prevent abscess formation.

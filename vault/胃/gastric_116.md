## Overview
Gastric cancer resection with D2 lymph node dissection, Billroth II anastomosis, and pre-colonic approach.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Gastric body ulcerative mass measuring 5*5cm, involving serosa, hard consistency, adherent to pancreas; 8th and 3rd group lymph nodes enlarged; liver and pelvic exploration negative for abnormalities.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, after general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping; midline upper abdominal incision around the umbilicus; exploration as described.
2. Mobilize transverse colon, dissect greater omentum and anterior leaf of transverse mesocolon, transect gastrocolic ligament; mobilize to the right to the hepatic flexure and to the left to the inferior pole of the spleen; clamp and transect right gastroepiploic artery and vein at the vascular root.
3. Clamp and transect greater curvature of the stomach, preserve 3 branches of left gastric vessels, protect spleen; dissect and mobilize hepatoduodenal ligament, mobilize second part of duodenum, dissect 8th group lymph nodes.
4. Transect and ligate right gastric vessel; clamp and transect duodenum 3cm distal to pylorus, suture with serosal layer; mobilize stomach body, open gastropancreatic fold, resect tumor on the surface of pancreas; transect left gastric vessel at the root, ligate doubly; place 28.5mm anvil of stapler at 20cm distal to Treitz ligament, transect stomach 4cm above tumor, clamp with clamp on lesser curvature, remove specimen, place long handle of stapler through greater curvature, anastomose posterior wall of stomach with jejunum via transverse colon; meticulous hemostasis, suture anastomosis; thorough hemostasis, irrigate peritoneal cavity, aspirate effluent, place two negative pressure balls at anastomosis and duodenal stump; count instruments and sponges, close abdomen in layers; completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: stomach, pancreas, lymph nodes, colon. (胆总管 (胆总管))

## Technical Challenges
Not documented

## Teaching Points
- Proper mobilization and dissection of the greater curvature of the stomach
- Careful handling of the spleen and left gastric vessels
- Accurate placement of the anastomosis

## Complication Prevention
- Thorough hemostasis
- Proper placement of drains

## Overview
Gastric cancer resection with Billroth I anastomosis, performed for gastric ulcer with suspected malignancy.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric ulcer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric ulcer with suspected malignancy
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings: 1.0 cm superficial ulcerous mass in the gastric antrum, hard, not penetrating the serosa. No abnormalities found in liver, small intestine, colon, or pelvis.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline upper abdominal incision.
3. Dissection of the gastrocolic ligament, right to the hepatic flexure of the colon, left to the splenic flexure with division and ligation of 2-3 branches of the left gastric vessels.
4. Dissection of the transverse mesocolon to the superior margin of the pancreas.
5. Division and double ligature of the right gastric and right gastroepiploic vessels at the vascular root.
6. Division of the duodenum 2 cm distal to the pylorus, with a purse-string suture and insertion of a 25# anvil of the stapler.
7. Division of the lesser omentum below the liver, and dissection of the anterior leaf of the hepatoduodenal ligament and lymph nodes.
8. Dissection of the greater curvature of the stomach, division and double ligature of the left gastric vessels.
9. Dissection of the coronary vessels and lymph nodes on the lesser curvature until below the cardia.
10. Opening the anterior wall of the stomach, insertion of the long tube of the stapler, and anastomosis between the posterior wall of the greater curvature of the stomach and the duodenum.
11. Removal of the stomach specimen with a 55mm side-to-side stapler 2 cm from the anastomosis.
12. Full-thickness suture reinforcement of the anastomosis and the distal stomach.
13. Hemostasis, lavage of the peritoneal cavity, aspiration of effluent, placement of two negative pressure balls at the anastomosis, and suture closure in layers.

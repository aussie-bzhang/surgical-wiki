## Overview
Gastric cancer resection with Billroth I reconstruction, involving the stomach, colon, and lymph nodes.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings: 4*2cm ulcerative infiltrative mass at the angle and lower part of the stomach body, penetrating the serosa, involving the transverse mesocolon. Lymph nodes at levels 1, 3, 5, 8, 12, and 13 were enlarged. No abnormalities were found in the liver, small intestine, colon, or pelvis.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline upper abdominal incision.
3. Biclamp dissection of the gastrocolic ligament on the intestinal side, right to the hepatic flexure of the colon, left to the splenic flexure with 2-3 branches of the left gastric vessels.
4. Dissection of the anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon to the superior margin of the pancreas, resection of the involved transverse mesocolon.
5. Dissection of the gastroepiploic vessels and lymphatic tissue on the lesser curvature of the stomach to the lower esophagus.
6. Dissection of the gastrocolic ligament, right and left gastric vessels ligated and divided at the vascular pedicle.
7. Lymph node dissection at level 13, and division of the duodenum 2 cm distal to the pylorus with a purse-string suture and 25# anvil of the stapler.
8. Dissection of the lesser omentum below the liver, and lymph node dissection at levels 12 and 8.
9. Mobilization of the stomach body, left gastric vessels ligated and divided at the vascular pedicle.
10. Opening the anterior wall of the stomach, inserting the long tube of the stapler, and anastomosing the stomach to the duodenum with a 55mm side-to-side stapler 2 cm from the anastomosis.
11. Removal of the specimen and reinforcement of the anastomosis with full-thickness suture.
12. Hemostasis, closure of the mesenteric defect, lavage of the peritoneal cavity, aspiration of effluent, placement of two negative pressure balls at the anastomosis, and closure of the abdomen in layers.

## Overview
Gastric cancer resection with Billroth I anastomosis, performed for gastric cancer in a patient with significant findings at the gastric angle and lesser curvature.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Gastric lesion located at the gastric angle and lesser curvature, 3cm in diameter, with a raised ulcerative type. Lesion invades serosa, no enlarged lymph nodes around the stomach. Liver, small intestine, colon, and pelvic organs are negative.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping, midline upper abdominal incision.
2. Dissection of the gastrocolic ligament, right to the duodenal bulb, left to the splenic flexure, and transection and ligation of 2-3 branches of the left gastric short vessels.
3. Dissection of the pretransverse mesocolon to the superior margin of the pancreas.
4. Transection and ligation + suture ligation of the right gastric artery at the pylorus.
5. Transection of the duodenum 3cm distal to the pylorus, with a purse-string suture and placement of the anvil of a 28.5mm stapler.
6. Dissection of the greater curvature of the stomach, transection and double ligature of the left gastric artery at the root.
7. Dissection of the coronary vessels and lymphatic tissue on the lesser curvature to the subcardia.
8. Opening the anterior wall of the stomach, insertion of the long tube of the stapler, and end-to-end anastomosis of the posterior wall of the stomach and the duodenum 5cm above the lesion.
9. Removal of the stomach specimen with a 90mm clamp and reinforcement of the anastomosis with serosal layer.
10. Hemostasis, lavage of the peritoneal cavity, removal of effusion, placement of negative pressure balls at the anastomosis, and layer-by-layer closure.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Common bile duct (胆总管), duodenum (十二指肠), splenic flexure (结肠脾曲), pancreas (胰腺), lymph nodes (淋巴结)

## Teaching Points
- Importance of identifying and preserving the blood supply to the stomach.
- Proper handling of the duodenum and anastomosis to prevent leaks.
- Technique of lymph node dissection and identification of sentinel lymph nodes.

## Complication Prevention
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper anastomotic technique to prevent leaks and strictures.

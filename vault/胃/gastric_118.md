## Overview
Gastric cancer resection with D2 lymphadenectomy, performed for gastric cancer. Key technical feature includes distal gastrectomy with Billroth II anastomosis and pre-colonic mobilization.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings include a 3*2cm mucosal erosion on the posterior wall of the gastric antrum with a central 0.2cm ulcer, a 0.5cm ulcer on the duodenal bulb, palpable lymph nodes at NO.5,6. No abnormalities found in the liver, gallbladder, small intestine, colon, and pelvic cavity.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Abdominal exploration via an upper midline incision.
2. Mobilize the transverse colon, dissect the greater omentum and the anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon, transect the gastrocolic ligament, and mobilize to the right to the hepatic flexure and to the left to the inferior pole of the spleen.
3. Clamp and transect the right gastric artery and vein at the vascular root.
4. Clamp and transect the greater curvature of the stomach, preserving 3 branches of the short gastric vessels and protecting the spleen.
5. Dissect and mobilize the hepatoduodenal ligament, mobilize the second part of the duodenum, and dissect the 8th group of lymph nodes.
6. Transect and ligate the right gastric vessel; clamp and transect the duodenum 3cm distal to the pyloric canal, and suture the serosa layer.
7. Mobilize the gastric body, open the gastropancreatic fold, and resect the tumor on the surface of the pancreas. Transect the left gastric vessel at the root and ligate it doubly.
8. Place the anvil of a 28.5mm anastomosis stapler through a jejunum incision 20cm distal to the Treitz ligament and fix it with a purse-string suture. Transect the stomach 4cm above the tumor, clamp the lesser curvature with a clamp, remove the specimen, and place the long handle of the stapler through the greater curvature. Perform a side-to-side anastomosis between the posterior wall of the stomach and the jejunum, passing the transverse colon. Perform meticulous hemostasis and suture the anastomosis with several stitches; 
9. Thorough hemostasis, irrigate the abdominal cavity, aspirate the effluent, and place two negative pressure balls at the anastomosis and the duodenal stump.
10. Count instruments and sponges, close the abdomen in layers; 
11. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the stomach, duodenal bulb, lymph nodes, liver, gallbladder, spleen, colon, and pelvic cavity. (胆总管)
> The common bile duct (胆总管) requires protection during dissection.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered include mobilization of the duodenum and anastomosis of the stomach and jejunum. These were managed by meticulous dissection and careful hemostasis.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of meticulous dissection and hemostasis during lymphadenectomy.
- The technique of performing a Billroth II anastomosis with a stapler.
- The importance of protecting the spleen and other adjacent structures during surgery.

## Complication Prevention
- Prevent postoperative ileus by ensuring proper anastomosis.
- Prevent postoperative infection by maintaining strict aseptic technique.

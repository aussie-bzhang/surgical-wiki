## Overview
Distal gastric resection for gastric cancer, with D2+ lymph node dissection and Billroth I anastomosis, in a patient with a 2cm ulcer in the gastric antrum.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Lesion located in the lesser curvature of the gastric antrum, a depression-type ulcer, diameter about 2cm, not involving the serosa. No lymph node enlargement seen intraoperatively. Negative exploration of liver and pelvis.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Midline upper abdominal incision, peritoneal cavity exploration as above.
2. Transection of the gastrocolic ligament on the intestinal side, right to the bulb of the duodenum, left to the 2-3 branches of the left gastric short vessels at the splenic flexure, and transection and ligation.
3. Dissection below the pylorus to expose the right gastric网膜血管, double transection at the root, and dissection of the 6th group of lymph nodes.
4. Dissection below the liver to transect the lesser omentum, right to the prehepatic leaf of the hepatoduodenal ligament and its lymphatic tissue, and expose the right gastric vessel, proper hepatic artery, and celiac trunk. Transection and double ligature at the root of the right gastric vessel, and dissection of the 7th, 8th, and 12th groups of lymph nodes.
5. Transection of the duodenum 3cm distal to the pylorus, the distal end made into a purse-string suture and placed into a 28.5mm anastomosis stapler and fixed. The gastric body is flipped upwards, the vessel root ligated and sutured, and transected. Clearing of the coronary vessel and lymphatic tissue on the lesser curvature (1st, 3rd groups) to the subcardia.
6. At approximately 5cm from the upper margin of the tumor, the gastric body is clamped with two Kocher clamps and opened, the remaining gastric body 60mm clamped and transected, the specimen removed, and the long tube of the stapler inserted into the gastric opening. Anastomosis of the gastric body posterior wall near the middle third with the duodenal stump, serosal layer of the anastomosis sutured and reinforced, the gastric opening clamped with a 60mm clamp and sutured in full thickness.
7. Thorough hemostasis, lavage of the peritoneal cavity, aspiration of effluent, placement of two negative pressure drains at the anastomosis.
8. Counting sponges and instruments, closing the abdomen in layers, operation completed.

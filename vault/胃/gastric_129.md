## Overview
Gastrectomy with Billroth I reconstruction for gastric antrum ulcer with suspected malignancy. Key technical feature includes anastomosis of the stomach to the duodenum.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric antrum ulcer with suspected malignancy.
**Intraoperative:** Gastric antrum ulcer with suspected malignancy.
**Pathological:** Not documented.

## Intraoperative Findings
Gastric antrum anterior wall ulcer focus, diameter 2.0cm. Gallbladder 8*4*3cm with several mixed stones, largest diameter about 0.8cm. Common bile duct diameter 0.5cm. Left upper quadrant adhesions were heavy. Umbilical defect diameter about 2cm. Negative exploration of liver and pelvis.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Midline upper abdominal incision, peritoneal cavity exploration as above.
2. Carefully separate left upper quadrant adhesions, lift transverse colon, dissect greater omentum, transverse mesocolon anterior leaf, and pancreatic capsule.
3. Clamp, cut, and ligate greater omentocolic ligament, right to the liver flexure of the transverse colon, left to the splenic flexure of the transverse colon.
4. Dissect the胆囊三角, find the cystic artery, ligate and cut it, tie a knot at the proximal end to reinforce, and treat the cystic duct in the same manner. Submucosal stripping of the gallbladder, remove the gallbladder, and coagulate the liver bed with electrocautery.
5. Dissect the right gastric网膜 artery below the gastric antrum, ligate and cut it with two ties, flip the gastric antrum, and decompress the first segment of the duodenum.
6. Cut the lesser omentum below the liver, strip the anterior leaf of the hepatoduodenal ligament and the lymphoid tissue within it; clamp, cut, and ligate the right gastric artery at the vascular root; free the gastric coronary vein along the lesser curvature upwards, ligate and cut it one by one.
7. Clamp, cut, and ligate 2-3 branches of the short gastric vessels along the greater curvature of the gastric body; cut the duodenum 2cm distal to the pylorus, place the distal end into the anvil of a 28F tube anastomosis stapler and fix it with purse-string suture.
8. Flip the gastric body upwards, clamp, cut, and ligate the left gastric artery at the vascular root. Incise the anterior wall of the stomach, anastomose the distal end with the duodenum, remove the stapler long tube, and use a 55mm side-to-side stapler to cut the gastric body 7cm from the lesion, remove the specimen, and reinforce the anastomosis and distal end with 3-0 gastrointestinal suture.
9. Thorough hemostasis, wash the peritoneal cavity, aspirate the effluent, place a drain under the liver and at the anastomosis, hemostasis, close the abdomen, and finish the operation.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in adhesion separation and anastomosis technique.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of careful adhesion separation and identification of key structures.
- Proper handling of the anastomosis to ensure a secure and leak-proof connection.

## Complication Prevention
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper anastomotic technique to prevent leaks and strictures.

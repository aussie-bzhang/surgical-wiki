## Overview
Gastric cancer resection with Billroth I reconstruction and D2 lymph node dissection in a patient with gastric cancer.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located at the lesser curvature of the gastric antrum, ulcerative infiltrative type, 3*2cm, serosal surface with suspected invasion but not through, enlarged lymph nodes around the stomach (1st, 3rd, 6th, 8p), liver, pelvis, small intestine, colon, etc. (negative).

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping, midline upper abdominal incision, peritoneal exploration as above, placement of wound protector and automatic retractor.
2. Transection of the gastrocolic ligament on the intestinal side, right to the bulb of the duodenum below, left to 2-3 branches of the left gastric short vessels at the superior curvature of the colon and transected and ligated, stripping of the anterior leaf of the transverse colon mesentery to the superior margin of the pancreas.
3. Separation below the pylorus to expose the right gastroepiploic vessels, doubly transected at the root and cleared the 6th group of lymph nodes.
4. Transection of the lesser omentum below the liver, stripping the anterior leaf of the hepatoduodenal ligament and its lymphoid tissue (12a group) to the right, and exposing the right gastric vessels, proper hepatic artery, celiac trunk, transected and doubly ligated at the root of the right gastric vessels, and cleared the 5th, 8th, 9th group of lymph nodes.
5. Transection of the duodenum 3cm distal to the pylorus, the distal end made into a purse-string suture and placed into a 28.5mm anastomosis stapler trocar and fixed, the gastric body flipped upwards, transection and doubly ligated the left gastric vessels at the root and cleared the 7th group of lymph nodes; cleared the coronary vessels and lymphoid tissue on the lesser curvature (1st, 3rd groups) to below the cardia.
6. Insertion of the long tube of the stapler into the residual stomach cavity, anastomosis of the posterior wall of the greater curvature of the gastric body at the middle-third near the stomach body with the distal end of the duodenum using a 90mm clamp stapler, removing the specimen, and reinforcing the anastomosis with a full-thickness layer.
7. Thorough hemostasis, lavage of the peritoneal cavity, aspiration of exudate, placement of a negative pressure ball at the anastomosis, and layer-by-layer closure.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: gastric antrum, greater curvature, omentum, gallbladder, spleen, pancreas, duodenum, colon, ileum, retroperitoneal lymph nodes. (胆总管)

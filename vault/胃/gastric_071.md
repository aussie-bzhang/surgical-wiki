## Overview
Gastric cancer resection with esophagojejunostomy and jejunostomy, a complex procedure involving the removal of the entire stomach and reconstruction of the gastrointestinal tract.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Gastric mass located on the lesser curvature of the antrum, 8cm in diameter, infiltrative ulcerative type, extending beyond the serosa. Scattered millet-like nodules palpable in the gastric wall. Scattered enlarged lymph nodes around the stomach. Gallbladder approximately 8*4*3cm, containing dozens of mixed stones about 0.5-1.0cm in diameter. Gallbladder duct diameter 0.3cm, common bile duct diameter 0.5cm.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Midline upper abdominal incision, enter peritoneal cavity.
2. Dissection of the gastrocolic ligament, right to the hepatic flexure of the colon, left to the inferior pole of the spleen, along with the anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon,剥离至 the surface of the pancreas. Open the lesser sac and enter the lesser curvature side.
3. At the root of the pancreatic head and below the pylorus, the right gastric artery was transected and ligated, and the surrounding lymph nodes were cleared.
4. Below the liver, the lesser omentum was transected, right to the hepatoduodenal ligament, and the right gastric artery was transected and ligated.
5. At 3cm from the pylorus, the duodenum was transected with a 60mm clamp and sutured for reinforcement.
6. The left gastric artery at the root was transected and doubly ligated. The greater omentum was separated upwards, the esophageal hiatus and both peritoneal membranes were incised, the lower esophagus was freed, and the vagus nerves were transected at 3cm above the cardia.
7. The esophagus was transected at 3cm above the cardia, the specimen was removed, and a 25.5mm circular stapler was placed with purse-string suture fixation.
8. The jejunum was transected at 20cm from the Treitz ligament, and the esophagus was anastomosed to the distal jejunum with the stapler. The distal jejunum was closed with a 60mm clamp.
9. The proximal jejunum was anastomosed to the jejunum 50cm below the esophageal anastomosis (in front of the colon); close the mesenteric gap; perform a jejunostomy.
10. Carefully dissect the胆囊三角, free the cystic duct and gallbladder vessels, transect and ligate the cystic duct 0.5cm from the common bile duct, ligate the cystic artery, strip the gallbladder from the gallbladder bed of the liver, and remove the specimen.
11. Thorough hemostasis, lavage the peritoneal cavity, place two negative pressure balls above and below the anastomosis, close the abdomen in layers, and the operation was completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: stomach, esophagus, jejunum, bile duct. (胆总管)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in the dissection of the lesser sac, lymph node clearance, and anastomosis of the esophagus and jejunum.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of careful dissection around the lesser sac to avoid injury to the spleen and pancreas.
- Proper handling of the esophagus and jejunum during anastomosis to prevent leaks and strictures.
- The technique of performing a Roux-en-Y anastomosis and jejunostomy.

## Complication Prevention
- Thorough hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper anastomotic technique to prevent leaks and strictures.

## Overview
Gastric cancer resection with D2 lymph node dissection and Billroth I reconstruction, performed for gastric cancer.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a 3cm ulcerative mass at the angle of the stomach, with no significant abnormalities found in the liver, gallbladder, pancreas, spleen, small intestine, colon, or pelvic cavity.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia and supine position, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline upper abdominal incision, findings as described above.
3. Dissection of the greater omentum and mobilization of the anterior leaf to the superior margin of the pancreas.
4. Dissection and ligation of the right gastric and right gastroepiploic vessels at their vascular roots, and lymph node dissection of group 6.
5. Dissection and ligation of the right gastric and right gastroepiploic vessels, lymph node dissection of group 5, and清扫第8组淋巴结.
6. Transection of the stomach above the tumor, removal of specimen.
7. Transection of the duodenum 3cm distal to the pylorus, placement of the 28# anastomosis stapler head.
8. Opening the anterior wall of the residual stomach, insertion of the stapler long tube, side-to-side anastomosis of the stomach and duodenum, and reinforcement of the anastomosis with serosal layer.
9. Hemostasis, lavage of the peritoneal cavity, removal of effusion, placement of negative pressure drainage balls at the anastomosis site, and closure in layers.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Common bile duct (胆总管), hepatic artery (肝动脉), splenic flexure (结肠脾曲)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in lymph node dissection and anastomosis technique.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of lymph node dissection in gastric cancer.
- Technique of Billroth I reconstruction.
- Management of potential complications during surgery.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper hemostasis and lavage to prevent infection.
- Careful anastomosis technique to prevent leakage.

## Overview
Gastric cancer resection with portasystemic shunt and splenectomy (Billroth I) for a patient with portal hypertension, liver cirrhosis, and diabetes.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric antrum cancer, liver cirrhosis, portal hypertension, splenomegaly, postoperative uterus cancer, diabetes.
**Intraoperative:** Confirmed gastric antrum cancer, liver cirrhosis, portal hypertension, splenomegaly.
**Pathological:** Not documented.

## Intraoperative Findings
Liver with small nodular liver cirrhosis, left lobe enlargement, spleen congested and swollen approximately 25*20*20cm. Tumor located in the gastric antrum near the pylorus with a diameter of 8.5*8.5cm, infiltrative growth occupying the entire circumference of the pylorus and extending to the peritoneal surface. Multiple lymph node enlargement. No varices in the fundus, coronary vein, or around the esophagus. No obvious metastasis in the abdominal and pelvic cavity. Small amount of ascites.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Midline upper abdominal incision, abdominal exploration as described.
2. Open gastrocolic ligament, transect splenocolic ligament, and separate and cut the splenic renal ligament posteriorly, splenic gastrolienal ligament anteriorly. Ligate and tie off gastric short vessels one by one. Separate and cut the splenic diaphragmatic ligament, fully mobilize the splenic pedicle. Separate splenic artery and vein, cut and ligate separately with double knots, remove the spleen.
3. Raise the transverse colon, strip the omentum, transverse colon mesentery anterior leaf, and pancreatic capsule; clamp, cut, and ligate gastrocolic ligament, right to the liver flexure of the transverse colon.
4. Clamp, cut, and ligate 2-3 gastric short vessels along the greater curvature of the stomach, protecting the spleen.
5. Cut the lesser omentum below the liver, strip the anterior leaf of the hepatoduodenal ligament and lymphoid tissue.
6. Clamp, cut, and ligate the right gastric and right gastroepiploic vessels at the vascular root. Cut the duodenum 3cm distal to the pylorus, place the distal end into the 28-mm circular stapler anvil and fix with purse-string suture.
7. Cut the stomach at the second branch of the left gastric artery, preserve the left gastric artery, cut the stomach body at the middle-third near the stomach body, remove the specimen.
8. Place the residual stomach into the stapler, anastomose the greater curvature posterior wall with the duodenum end-to-end, suture and close the greater curvature, reinforce with serosa, reinforce the anastomosis with several serosal sutures.
9. Thorough hemostasis, wash the abdominal cavity, aspirate the effluent fluid, place two negative pressure balls at the anastomosis from the right upper abdomen, one PASMA tube in the splenic fossa, close the layers in sequence.
10. Operation completed.

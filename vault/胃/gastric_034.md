## Overview
Gastric cancer resection with Billroth I reconstruction and D2 lymph node dissection. The procedure involves resection of the stomach and surrounding structures, followed by reconstruction of the gastrointestinal tract.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located at the gastric angle, ulcerative type, size 3*2cm, extending to the serosa. No liver or pelvic involvement.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Midline upper abdominal incision. Place wound protector and automatic retractor.
2. Transect the gastrocolic ligament on the intestinal side, right to the second segment of the duodenum, left to the superior margin of the splenic hilum. Retain 2 branches of the gastric short vessels. Strip the pretransverse mesocolon to the superior margin of the pancreas. Free the gastrojejunal vessels and ligate and tie off at the root of the gastroepiploic vessels.
3. Transect the lesser omentum below the liver. Strip the anterior leaf of the hepatoduodenal ligament and the 12th group of lymph nodes. Free the hepatic artery and dissect the 8th group of lymph nodes.
4. Transect and doubly ligate the right gastric vessels at the root. Transect the duodenum 3cm distal to the pylorus, place a 25.5mm anvil of the anastomotic stapler and fix it for later use.
5. Reflect the gastric body upwards. Transect and doubly ligate the left gastric vessels at the root. Clear the coronary vessels and lymph nodes on the lesser curvature until below the esophagogastric junction.
6. Place the long tube of the stapler into the gastric cavity and perform an end-to-end anastomosis between the posterior wall of the gastric body at the middle-third and the duodenum. Clamp the stomach and remove the specimen. Reinforce the anastomosis, residual duodenal stump, and lesser curvature serosa with a layer of muscularis mucosa.
7. Thorough hemostasis, lavage the peritoneal cavity, aspirate the effluent, place two negative pressure balls at the Winslow's hole and anastomosis, and close the layers in sequence. The operation was completed.

## Overview
Gastric cancer resection with Billroth I anastomosis, involving the stomach and duodenum, with lymph node dissection and bile duct preservation.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located at the pyloric antrum, ulcerative infiltrative type, size 3*2cm, extending to the serosa, 3rd, 6th, 8th, 9th, 12th group lymph nodes enlarged, liver and pelvic area negative.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping, midline upper abdominal incision, placement of wound protector and automatic retractor.
2. Transection of the gastrocolic ligament on the intestinal side, right to the second segment of the duodenum, left to the superior margin of the hilum of the spleen, preservation of 2 branches of the gastric short vessels, stripping of the anterior leaf of the transverse colon mesentery to the superior margin of the pancreas, mobilization of the gastroduodenal vessels.
3. Transection of the lesser omentum below the liver, stripping of the anterior leaf of the hepatoduodenal ligament and 12th group lymph nodes, mobilization of the hepatic artery, dissection of the 8th group lymph nodes.
4. Transection and double ligature of the right gastric vessels at the vascular root; transection of the duodenum 3cm distal to the pylorus, the distal end sutured into the anvil of the stapler and fixed for later use.
5. Mobilization of the gastric body, transection and double ligature of the left gastric vessels at the vascular root; removal of the coronary vessels and lymphatic tissue on the lesser curvature until below the cardia.
6. Insertion of the long tube of the stapler into the gastric cavity, end-to-end anastomosis of the gastric body at the middle-third with the duodenum on the posterior wall of the greater curvature.
7. Clamp and transect the gastric body, remove the specimen, reinforce the anastomosis, residual duodenal stump, and lesser curvature serosa with a layer of muscularis mucosa.
8. Thorough hemostasis, lavage of the peritoneal cavity, aspiration of exudate, placement of a negative pressure ball at the anastomosis, layer-by-layer closure. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: stomach, duodenum, lymph nodes, bile duct (胆总管). Structures requiring protection: gastric artery, splenic artery, superior mesenteric artery.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in mobilization of the liver and duodenum, and in achieving a secure anastomosis.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of lymph node dissection in staging and prognosis.
- Technique of anastomosis and reinforcement to prevent leaks.
- Management of potential complications such as bile leak and anastomotic stricture.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful mobilization to avoid injury to adjacent structures.
- Adequate anastomotic reinforcement to prevent leaks.
- Postoperative monitoring for signs of leakage or stricture.

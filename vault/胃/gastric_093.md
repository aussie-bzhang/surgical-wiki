## Overview
Gastrectomy with total resection and esophagojejunostomy for gastric cancer, including left liver resection, diaphragmatic resection, and complex intestinal adhesion release.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located at the cardia, ulcerative infiltrative type, diameter about 10cm, involving serosa, extending to the lower esophagus, infiltrating to the left lateral lobe of the liver, and invades the diaphragm roof. Lymph nodes around the esophagus and gastric fundus are involved and enlarged. Exploration of liver, pancreas, small intestine, colon, pelvis, abdominal wall, and ascites (-).

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia, tracheal double-ballooned tube insertion successful, left side elevated 60 degrees, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Upper midline incision, enter peritoneum, decompose intestinal adhesions, explore peritoneal cavity as described.
3. Take the 6th intercostal space on the left to the upper midline incision, cut the costal arch, open the pleural cavity.
4. Midline incision of the diaphragm to the esophageal hiatus, incise the left coronary ligament and left triangular ligament, incise the extrapericardial fibrous tissue, and free the lower segment of the esophageal thoracic segment.
5. Near the intestinal side, transect the gastrocolic ligament, lift the transverse colon, strip the anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon to the superior margin of the pancreas, retain the right gastric网膜血管, and transect the greater omentum at the avascular area of the gastric body, free the left side of the cardia.
6. Transect the left gastric网膜血管 along the greater curvature of the stomach, transect and ligate the short gastric vessels upwards, protecting the spleen.
7. Resection of the left lateral lobe of the liver involved by the tumor; resection of diaphragmatic tissue involved by the tumor.
8. Free the surrounding fibrous fatty tissue of the esophagus above the cardia, dissect the lymph nodes, transect both vagus nerves, transect the esophagus 7cm above the cardia with a purse-string clamp, and place the anvil of a 25mm tube-shaped anastomosis stapler and fix it with a purse-string suture; 
9. Anastomose the esophagus with the distal jejunum, close the distal jejunum, and anastomose the proximal jejunum with the distal jejunum 50cm below the esophageal anastomosis; close the mesenteric gap;
10. Suture and close the diaphragm, reconstruct the esophageal hiatus.
11. Irrigate the surgical field until clean, stop bleeding, and apply hemostatic and antitumor drugs to the resection site.
12. Thorough hemostasis, irrigate the peritoneal cavity, remove the effusion, place a negative pressure ball at the lower esophageal anastomosis and the distal duodenal stump, puncture the incision, place a chest drain in the pleural cavity, and close the chest under negative pressure.
13. Count instruments and gauze, close the abdomen in layers.

## Anatomical Key Points

> Danger zones: cardia, esophageal hiatus.
> Structures requiring protection: spleen.

## Overview
Gastric cancer resection with esophagointestinal Roux-en-Y anastomosis, a complex procedure for advanced gastric cancer, involving resection of the entire stomach and reconstruction of the alimentary tract.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
A mass was found in the gastric fundus, extending from the cardia to the lesser curvature of the gastric angle, measuring 8*4cm, infiltrative-ulcerative type, involving the serosa. Exploration of the liver, gallbladder, pancreas, spleen, small intestine, colon, and peritoneal cavity showed no abnormalities.

## Operative Steps
1. The patient was placed in a supine position under general anesthesia, and the abdomen was entered through a midline upper abdominal incision.
2. The gastrocolic ligament was separated, extending to the right to the hepatic flexure of the colon and to the inferior pole of the spleen, with the anterior leaf of the transverse colon mesentery stripped off to the surface of the pancreas. The lesser omentum was opened to enter the lesser curvature.
3. The right gastric artery and its surrounding lymph nodes were ligated and excised at the root of the pancreatic head below the pylorus.
4. The lesser omentum was transected below the liver, extending to the hepatoduodenal ligament, and the right gastric artery was ligated and transected.
5. The stomach was transected at 3cm from the pylorus using a 60mm clamp, and the transection was reinforced with suture.
6. The left gastric artery and short gastric vessels were transected along the greater curvature until the left side of the gastric fundus and cardia.
7. The stomach body was flipped up, the left gastric artery at the root was transected and doubly ligated. The lesser omentum was separated upwards, the esophageal hiatus and the peritoneum on both sides were incised, the lower esophagus was freed, and the vagus nerves on both sides were transected. The esophagus was transected 3cm above the cardia.
8. The distal esophagus was removed, and a 25.5mm circular stapler was placed with a purse-string suture.
9. The jejunum was transected 20cm from the Treitz ligament, and the esophagus was anastomosed to the distal jejunum using the stapler. The distal jejunum was closed with a 60mm clamp. The proximal jejunum was anastomosed to the distal jejunum 50cm below the esophageal anastomosis (colonic antimesenteric).
10. The mesenteric gaps were closed.

## Overview
Gastric cancer resection with Billroth I anastomosis and D2 lymphadenectomy. Involves resection of the stomach and surrounding structures, followed by reconstruction of the gastrointestinal tract.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located at the antrum and angle of the stomach, approximately 5cm in diameter, ulcerative infiltrative type, involving the serosal layer but not penetrating. Scattered inflammatory adhesions in the abdominal cavity, scattered enlarged lymph nodes around the stomach. Liver, pelvis, small intestine, and colon were unremarkable.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Midline upper abdominal incision around the umbilicus, abdominal exploration as described.
2. Transection of the gastrocolic ligament on the intestinal side, right to the bulb of the duodenum, left to the splenic flexure with 2-3 branches of the left gastric vessels, transected and ligated.
3. Separation and exposure of the right gastric artery below the pylorus, doubly ligated at the root.
4. Transection of the lesser omentum below the liver, right dissection of the prehepatic leaf of the hepatoduodenal ligament, exposing the right gastric artery, transected and doubly ligated at the root of the gastric right artery.
5. Transection of the duodenum 3cm distal to the pylorus, the distal end of the duodenum placed into a 28.5mm anvil of the stapler and fixed, the stomach body flipped upwards, transection and doubly ligated the left gastric artery at the root.
6. Insertion of the long tube of the stapler into the residual stomach cavity, anastomosis of the posterior wall of the greater curvature of the stomach body at the middle-third of the stomach to the distal end of the duodenum. 90mm clamp stapler transected the stomach body, removed the specimen, and the anastomosis was reinforced with a full-thickness layer.
7. Thorough hemostasis, lavage of the abdominal cavity, aspiration of effluent fluid, placement of a negative pressure ball on either side of the anastomosis, and layer-by-layer closure.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: stomach, gallbladder, duodenum, colon. (胆总管)
> Danger zones: serosal layer of the stomach.
> Structures requiring protection: gastric vessels, bile duct.

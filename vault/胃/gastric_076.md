## Overview
Gastric cancer resection with esophagointestinal Roux-en-Y anastomosis, a complex procedure involving the stomach, esophagus, and small intestine for the treatment of gastric cancer.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a large infiltrative lesion on the lesser curvature of the stomach, measuring 10 cm in diameter, extending from the cardia to the pylorus and infiltrating beyond the serosa. No significant abnormalities were found in the other organs explored.

## Operative Steps
1. Position the patient in supine position under general anesthesia, perform routine disinfection and draping, and make a midline upper abdominal incision to enter the abdomen.
2. Dissect the gastrocolic ligament, extending to the right to the hepatic flexure of the colon and to the inferior pole of the spleen, stripping the anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon to the surface of the pancreas. Open the lesser omentum囊 and enter the lesser curvature.
3. At the root of the pancreatic head and below the pylorus, transect and ligate the right gastroepiploic vessels, and clear the surrounding lymph nodes.
4. Transect the lesser omentum below the liver, extending to the hepatoduodenal ligament, and transect and ligate the right gastric vessels.
5. At 3 cm from the pylorus, transect the duodenum with a clamp and suture it securely; 
6. Along the greater curvature, transect the left gastroepiploic and short gastric vessels to the left of the cardia.
7. Reflect the stomach upwards, transect and ligate the left gastric vessels at their root, and dissect upwards along the lesser omentum. Incise the esophageal hiatus and the parietal peritoneum on both sides, free the lower esophagus, and transect the vagus nerves bilaterally; 
8. At 3 cm above the cardia, transect the esophagus, remove the specimen, and place the anvil of the circular stapler and secure it with purse-string sutures.
9. At 20 cm from the Treitz ligament, transect the jejunum, and anastomose the esophagus with the distal jejunum using the stapler. Close the distal jejunum with a side-to-side stapler. Anastomose the proximal jejunum with the distal jejunum 60 cm below the esophageal anastomosis (in front of the colon); close the mesenteric gap;
10. Thoroughly hemostatize, irrigate the abdomen, aspirate the effluent, place two negative-pressure drainage tubes below the anastomosis, and ensure that all instruments and sponges are accounted for. Close the abdomen in layers.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the stomach, esophagus, duodenum, and jejunum. The common bile duct (胆总管) and the pancreas (胰腺) are important to protect during the procedure.

## Teaching Points
- Understanding the complex anatomy of the upper gastrointestinal tract is crucial for successful resection and anastomosis.
- Proper hemostasis and meticulous dissection are essential to prevent complications.
- The technique of esophagointestinal anastomosis requires precision and experience.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Adequate drainage to prevent postoperative abscess formation.

## Overview
Gastric body resection for gastric body cancer, with D2+ lymph node dissection, laparoscopic approach not used.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric body cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric body cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Gastric body mass located on the lesser curvature near the posterior wall, diameter about 5cm, cauliflower-type. Not penetrating the serosa. Lymph nodes in groups 1, 3, and 4 enlarged. No abnormalities found in liver, gallbladder, pancreas, small intestine, and colon.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia successful, routine disinfection and draping, midupper abdominal midline incision, layer-by-layer abdominal exploration as above;
2. Elevate transverse colon, separate and cut the gastrocolic ligament, to the right to the hepatic flexure of the colon, to the left to the inferior pole of the spleen, cut the splenocolic ligament, strip the anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon to the superior margin of the pancreas, expose the posterior wall of the stomach, cut the gastropancreatic fold.
3. Retain the right gastroepiploic artery, and cut the greater curvature of the stomach at the avascular area of the gastric body, separate the stomach along the greater curvature of the stomach, free the left side of the cardia, protect the spleen; 
4. Cut the short gastric vessels one by one, separate the stomach body along the greater curvature of the stomach body, free the left side of the cardia, protect the spleen;
5. Cut the lesser omentum below the liver, free the hepatoduodenal ligament, carefully dissect and separate the enlarged lymph nodes from the space between the left gastric artery, cut and ligate the root, and suture the left gastric artery; separate upwards along the lesser omentum, free the lower segment of the esophagus to the left, clamp and cut the vagus nerve, cut the esophagus 2cm above the cardia, place the head of the 25.5mm tube-shaped anastomosis stapler for anastomosis.
6. Clamp the stomach at the middle and lower third of the stomach body, 5cm above the mass, with a 90mm clamp, remove the specimen. Cut the anterior wall of the residual stomach, place the tube-shaped anastomosis stapler for anastomosis of the stomach and esophagus, suture the serosal layer of the anastomosis and the residual stomach body to reinforce.
7. Stop bleeding, flush the abdominal cavity, remove the effusion, place two negative pressure balls above and below the anastomosis, count instruments and gauze, close the abdomen layer by layer, end of operation.

## Teaching Points
- Proper handling of the spleen during dissection
- Careful dissection and ligation of the left gastric artery
- Technique of esophagogastrostomy with a tube-shaped stapler

## Complication Prevention
- Adequate hemostasis
- Proper placement of drains
- Early detection and management of postoperative complications

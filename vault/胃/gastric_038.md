## Overview
Palliative total gastrectomy with esophagojejunostomy Roux-en-Y and splenectomy for advanced gastric cancer with liver and peritoneal metastasis.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Advanced gastric cancer with liver and peritoneal metastasis
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Gastric antrum mass measuring 5.0 cm, gastric body mass measuring 7.0 cm extending to the cardia, involving extraperitoneal tissue. Lymph nodes in groups 2, 3, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 12, 13, 16 were enlarged and partially fused. Tumor and spleen were densely adherent. Lymph node in group 10 was enlarged. Liver had several gray-white nodules up to 3.0 cm in diameter. Peritoneum and mesentery had several gray-white nodules.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Midline upper abdominal incision.
2. Dissection of gastrocolic ligament, right to the hepatic flexure, left to the splenic flexure, with the anterior leaf of the transverse colon mesentery. Open the lesser omentum囊 into the lesser curvature.
3. Incise and ligate the right gastroepiploic artery at the root of the pylorus, clear surrounding lymph nodes.
4. Incise the lesser omentum below the liver, right to the hepatoduodenal ligament, clear surrounding lymph nodes, and ligate the right gastric artery.
5. At 3 cm from the pylorus, use a 55 cm side-to-side stapler to transect the duodenum and perform a purse-string suture.
6. Incise and ligate the left gastroepiploic artery and short gastric vessels along the greater curvature until the fundus and left side of the cardia, clear the corresponding lymph nodes.
7. Reflect the gastric body upwards, incise the left gastric artery at the root and ligate it twice. Separate the lesser omentum upwards, free the lower esophagus, and incise the esophagus 3 cm above the cardia, place a 25 mm tube stapler anvil and fix with purse-string suture.
8. Incise the splenic colic, splenic renal, and splenic diaphragmatic ligaments, remove the specimen.
9. At 20 cm from the Treitz ligament, transect the jejunum, use a 25 mm stapler to anastomose the esophagus with the distal jejunum. Close the jejunal stump with a side-to-side stapler. Anastomose the proximal jejunum with the jejunum 60 cm below the esophagus吻合口 (in front of the colon); close the mesenteric gap.
10. Thorough hemostasis, lavage the peritoneal cavity, aspirate the effluent, place two negative pressure balls around the anastomosis, one through the duodenal stump, one in the splenic fossa. Count instruments and sponges, close the abdomen in layers.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: stomach, esophagus, jejunum, spleen, liver, lymph nodes. (胆总管)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in tumor and spleen adhesion, extensive lymph node involvement.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of careful dissection around the spleen and liver.
- Technique of esophagojejunostomy Roux-en-Y.
- Management of extensive lymph node involvement.

## Complication Prevention
- Adequate hemostasis.
- Proper anastomotic technique to prevent leaks.
- Adequate lavage of the peritoneal cavity.

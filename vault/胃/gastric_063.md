## Overview
Gastrostomy procedure for esophageal obstruction, utilizing a median incision and creating a gastrostomy to bypass the obstruction.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Esophageal cancer, obstruction
**Intraoperative:** Esophageal cancer, obstruction
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a fishhook-shaped stomach with no evidence of tumor infiltration.

## Operative Steps
1. Position the patient in supine position, under general anesthesia, and perform routine disinfection and draping.
2. Make a median incision, puncture the anterior wall of the gastric body, insert a gastric tube, pass the distal end through the pylorus into the duodenum, puncture a wound adjacent to the gastric tube incision, and pull it out. Suture the gastric wall around the gastric tube with purse-string suture and secure it to the abdominal wall.
3. Count instruments and sponges to ensure accuracy, close the incision; 
4. Procedure completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the stomach, esophagus, and duodenum. The common bile duct (胆总管) requires protection during the procedure.

## Technical Challenges
Not documented

## Teaching Points
- The importance of ensuring proper placement of the gastric tube to avoid complications.
- The technique of creating a gastrostomy with a purse-string suture to secure the tube.

## Complication Prevention
- Ensuring proper placement of the gastric tube to prevent leakage and obstruction.
- Monitoring for signs of infection and ensuring proper wound care.

## Related Procedures

## Overview
Gastric wedge resection for gastric mesenchymoma in a clinical context where the tumor is located in the gastric fundus, with a diameter of approximately 1.5cm.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric mesenchymoma
**Intraoperative:** Gastric mesenchymoma
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a firm mass located in the gastric fundus with a diameter of about 1.5cm. No positive findings in the liver and pelvic cavity.

## Operative Steps
1. After general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping, establish pneumoperitoneum, and explore the abdomen without finding a gastric wall mass.
2. Make an upper midline incision to enter the abdomen, findings as described above.
3. Dissect and ligate several branches of the short gastric vessels around the mass, elevate the mass, and excise it with a linear stapler. Reinforce the gastric stump with a layer of seromuscular layer.
4. Thorough hemostasis, lavage the peritoneal cavity, place two negative pressure balls, and puncture the incision to lead out the drains.
5. Close the abdomen in layers, and the operation is completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the stomach and short gastric vessels. (胃, 胃短血管)

## Technical Challenges
Not documented

## Teaching Points
- The importance of identifying and ligating the short gastric vessels.
- The technique of linear stapler resection and reinforcement of the gastric stump.

## Complication Prevention
- Thorough hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.

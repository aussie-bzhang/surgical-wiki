## Overview
Gastrectomy for duodenal ulcer, a common procedure in general surgery, involves resection of the distal stomach and reconstruction of the gastrointestinal tract. Key technical features include identification and preservation of critical structures and anastomotic techniques.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric ulcer
**Intraoperative:** Duodenal ulcer at the pylorus
**Pathological:** Inflammatory tissue

## Intraoperative Findings
Significant findings include marked inflammation and adhesions at the pylorus and duodenal bulb, resembling a bed-like ulcer with a diameter of 2.5 cm, penetrating the posterior wall of the pancreas. Frozen section revealed inflammatory tissue.

## Operative Steps
1. Position the patient in supine position, administer general anesthesia, and perform routine disinfection and draping. Make a midline upper abdominal incision and enter the abdomen.
2. Retract the transverse colon, dissect the greater omentum and the anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon, and transect the gastrocolic ligament. Extend to the right to the hepatic flexure of the colon and to the inferior pole of the spleen.
3. Clamp and transect the right gastric artery and vein at the vascular pedicle.
4. Clamp and transect the greater curvature of the stomach, retaining two branches of the short gastric vessels, and protecting the spleen.
5. High ligation and transection of the right gastric vessels; excise 3/4 of the ulcer at the pylorus and duodenal bulb along the inflammatory adhesion to the pancreas, leaving a part in situ. Send a portion of the ulcer tissue for pathological examination, which showed inflammatory tissue.
6. Transect the duodenum, bury the residual margin, and suture it in full thickness.
7. Transect the lesser omentum below the liver, extending upwards to the right side of the esophagus.
8. Retract the gastric body, transect the left gastric vessels at the root, and ligate them with a double knot.
9. Transect the gastric body 5 cm from the margin of the ulcer, remove the specimen, and carefully hemostat the residual margin. Reinforce the seromuscular layer with interrupted sutures.
10. Perform an antecolic gastrojejunocolic anastomosis with a 28# tube anastomosis device 15 cm from the Treitz ligament, with the jejunal limb on the greater curvature, and reinforce the seromuscular layer. Close the mesenteric defect above the anastomosis.
11. Thoroughly hemostat, irrigate the abdominal cavity, aspirate the effluent, place a negative pressure ball under the liver and at the duodenal stump, count instruments and sponges, and close the abdomen in layers.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the pylorus, duodenal bulb, pancreas, spleen, gastrocolic ligament, and gastrojejunal anastomosis. (胆总管 (胆总管))

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered include extensive inflammation and adhesions, which required careful dissection and identification of critical structures to avoid injury.

## Teaching Points
- Identification and preservation of critical structures such as the spleen and pancreas.
- Proper anastomotic techniques to ensure leak-proof reconstruction.
- Management of extensive inflammation and adhesions.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Adequate irrigation and aspiration to reduce the risk of infection.
- Proper anastomotic technique to prevent leaks.

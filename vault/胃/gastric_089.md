## Overview
Gastric cancer resection with Billroth II anastomosis, antecolic reconstruction, and D2 lymphadenectomy. Involves extensive dissection and lymph node clearance.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric antrum cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric antrum cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located on the lesser curvature of the gastric antrum, approximately 6.0 cm in diameter, infiltrative ulcerative type, extending beyond the serosa. Grossly, there is marked enlargement of lymph nodes in groups 3, 4, 5, 6, and 8 around the stomach. No significant abnormalities found in liver, bile duct, small intestine, colon, or pelvis.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Midline upper abdominal incision around the umbilicus.
2. Mobilize the transverse colon, dissect the greater omentum, transverse mesocolon, and pancreatic capsule. Incise the gastrocolic ligament, extending to the right to the hepatic flexure and to the left to the inferior pole of the spleen.
3. Clamp and transect the right gastric artery and vein at the vascular pedicle, and dissect the 6th group of lymph nodes.
4. Clamp and transect the gastro splenic ligament along the greater curvature of the stomach, preserving 2 branches of the short gastric vessels, and protecting the spleen.
5. Dissect and anatomize the hepatoduodenal ligament, and dissect the 12th group of lymph nodes.
6. High ligation and transection of the right gastric vessel; transect the duodenum 3 cm distal to the pyloric orifice using a 55 mm side-to-side anastomotic stapler, and bury the distal stump with full-thickness suture.
7. Dissect the 8th group of lymph nodes along the hepatic artery, transect the lesser omentum below the liver, and extend upwards to the right side of the esophagus; dissect the 1st and 3rd groups of lymph nodes.
8. Clamp and transect the left gastric vessel at the root, and dissect the 5th and 7th groups of lymph nodes.
9. Transect the stomach 5 cm from the tumor margin, remove the specimen; carefully hemostat the stump and reinforce with interrupted seromuscular layer.
10. At 15 cm distal to the Treitz ligament, place a 25# tube-shaped anastomotic stapler mushroom head for anastomosis. Perform a jejunogastric anastomosis with the distal stomach using a tube-shaped anastomotic stapler, with the jejunal limb on the greater curvature, and reinforce with interrupted seromuscular layer for one week.
11. Thorough hemostasis, lavage the abdominal cavity, aspirate the effluent, place two negative pressure balls behind the gastrojejunal anastomosis at the inferior aspect of the liver and the distal duodenal stump, verify instruments and sponges, and close the abdomen in layers.

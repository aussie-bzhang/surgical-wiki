## Overview
Gastrectomy with splenectomy for gastric cancer, involving resection of the upper part of the stomach and spleen, with emphasis on esophagogastrostomy technique.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Gastric cardia tumor approximately 4*5cm, extending to the peritoneal surface, infiltrating diaphragm and spleen. Lymph nodes at splenic hilum palpable. Liver, pelvis, and ascites negative.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline upper abdominal incision, abdominal exploration as described.
3. Dissection and transection of gastrocolic ligament, right to the hepatic flexure, left to the inferior pole of the spleen.
4. Transection of splenocolic ligament, dissection of splenic hilum, transection and ligation of splenic artery and vein.
5. Dissection along the lateral aspect of the spleen to the diaphragm, freeing the left side of the cardia.
6. Transection of lesser omentum below the liver, upward mobilization of the stomach, transection and ligation of left gastric vessels.
7. Transection of splenocolic ligament, dissection of splenic hilum, transection and ligation of splenic artery and vein.
8. Dissection along the lateral aspect of the spleen to the diaphragm, freeing the left side of the cardia.
9. Transection of lesser omentum below the liver, upward mobilization of the stomach, transection and ligation of left gastric vessels.
10. Transection of esophagus 3cm above the cardia using a 100cm linear stapler, placement of a 28F tube stapler for esophagogastrostomy (prepyloric wall incision and pyloric shaping).
11. Hemostasis, lavage of abdomen, aspiration of effluent, placement of negative pressure balls at the anterior and posterior walls of the esophagogastrostomy site, extending to the splenic fossa, placement of a chest drain in the left pleural cavity.
12. Suture of diaphragmatic defect, count instruments and sponges, close abdomen in layers.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: stomach, spleen, esophagus, common bile duct. (胆总管)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in mobilizing the spleen and esophagus, ensuring adequate hemostasis, and performing the esophagogastrostomy.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of careful mobilization of the spleen and esophagus.
- Techniques for achieving hemostasis during the procedure.
- Proper technique for esophagogastrostomy, including stapling and anastomotic closure.

## Complication Prevention
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper mobilization to avoid injury to adjacent structures.

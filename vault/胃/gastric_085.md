## Overview
Gastric resection for residual gastric cancer following previous duodenal ulcer surgery. Key technical feature includes total gastrectomy, esophagojejunostomy, and complex adhesion release.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Duodenal ulcer postoperative residual gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Same as pre-operative
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Original surgery was Billroth II. Residual gastric body has a 4cm tumor on the lesser curvature, infiltrating to the left lobe of the liver. Enlarged lymph nodes are palpable below the anastomosis. No findings in liver, pelvis, or retroperitoneum.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Incision at upper midline and left inframammary line.
2. Transection of proximal and distal small intestines, 6cm away from the original anastomosis.
3. Separation of residual gastric colic ligament, transection of short gastric vessels to the left of the cardia, and complete mobilization of the gastric fundus, protecting the spleen.
4. Transection of left gastric vessels along the lesser curvature, ligating and transecting at the root, separating adhesions between the residual stomach and liver, transecting the anterior and posterior branches of the vagus nerve, and complete mobilization of the cardia and lower esophagus for about 6cm.
5. Transection of the esophagus 3cm above the cardia, removal of specimen, insertion of a 25mm circular stapler anvil and fixation with purse-string suture.
6. End-to-side anastomosis of the esophagus and distal jejunum, closure of the jejunum end, and end-to-side anastomosis of the proximal jejunum with the distal jejunum 60cm below the esophagus anastomosis. Closure of mesenteric gap.
7. Thorough hemostasis, lavage of the peritoneal cavity, aspiration of effluent, placement of two negative pressure drainage tubes and one in the spleen fossa below the esophagus anastomosis. Counting instruments and sponges, closing the abdomen in layers.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: gastric antrum, left lobe of liver, lymph nodes, spleen, esophagus, jejunum. (胆总管)

## Technical Challenges
Not documented

## Teaching Points
- Proper mobilization and protection of the spleen during surgery.
- Accurate identification and transection of the left gastric vessels.
- Careful handling of the esophagus and jejunum during anastomosis.

## Complication Prevention
- Thorough hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper lavage of the peritoneal cavity to reduce infection risk.

## Overview
Gastric cancer resection with Billroth I anastomosis and D2 lymph node dissection. Involves resection of the stomach and surrounding structures, followed by reconstruction of the gastrointestinal tract.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located at the greater curvature of the gastric antrum, approximately 2cm in diameter, ulcerative infiltrative type, involving the serosa but not penetrating. Lymph nodes in the 8th, 5th, and 6th groups around the stomach were enlarged, some fused into masses. No metastasis to liver, pelvis, small intestine, or colon.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping, midline upper abdominal incision around the umbilicus, abdominal exploration as described.
2. Transection of the gastrocolic ligament on the intestinal side, right to the bulb of the duodenum below, left to the 2-3 branches of the left gastric vessels at the splenic flexure and transected and ligated; stripping of the anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon to the superior margin of the pancreas.
3. Separation and exposure of the right gastric artery below the pylorus, doubly ligated at the root.
4. Transection of the lesser omentum below the liver, stripping the anterior leaf of the hepatoduodenal ligament to the right, exposing the right gastric artery, transected and doubly ligated at the root of the gastric right artery.
5. Transection of the duodenum 3cm distal to the pylorus, the distal end sutured into a 28.5mm anvil of the anastomotic stapler and fixed, the stomach body flipped upwards, transection and doubly ligated the left gastric artery at the root.
6. Insertion of the long tube of the stapler into the residual stomach cavity, anastomosis of the greater curvature posterior wall of the stomach body at the middle-third near the antrum with the distal end of the duodenum. The stomach body was clamped with a 90mm clamp and removed, the anastomosis reinforced with a full-thickness layer.
7. Hemostasis, lavage of the abdominal cavity, aspiration of effluent, placement of a negative pressure ball on either side of the anastomosis, closure in layers.

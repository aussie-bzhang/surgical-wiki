## Overview
Gastrectomy with pyloroplasty for gastric body cancer, involving resection of the upper part of the stomach and reconstruction of the pylorus.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric body cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric body cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Grossly, a 10cm mass was found in the gastric body near the cardia, infiltrative ulcer type, extending beyond the serosa. Lymph nodes in groups 6, 7, 8, 12, and 13 were enlarged. Exploration revealed no ascites in the liver, pelvis, or abdomen.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia successful. Routine disinfection and draping. Midline upper abdominal incision, enter peritoneum as described.
2. Elevate transverse colon, dissect and transect gastrocolic ligament, extending to the right to the hepatic flexure and to the left to the inferior pole of the spleen. Transect splenocolic ligament, strip the anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon to the superior margin of the pancreas, exposing the posterior wall of the stomach. Transect gastropancreatic fold.
3. Retain the right gastroepiploic artery, and transect the greater curvature of the stomach at the avascular area of the gastric body. Dissect and transect the left gastroepiploic artery along the greater curvature of the stomach.
4. Clamp and transect the left gastric vessels one by one, dissect the stomach body along the greater curvature, mobilize the left side of the cardia, protecting the spleen.
5. Transect the lesser omentum below the liver, mobilize the hepatoduodenal ligament, carefully dissect and separate the space between the enlarged lymph nodes and left gastric artery, transect and ligate the left gastric artery at the root, and suture it.
6. Separate upwards along the lesser omentum, mobilize the lower segment of the esophagus to the left, clamp and transect the vagus nerve, transect the esophagus 3cm above the cardia, and place the head of the 25.5# tube-shaped anastomosis stapler for anastomosis.
7. Clamp and transect the middle and lower third of the gastric body 90mm with a clamp, remove the specimen, incise the pyloric wall, place the tube-shaped anastomosis stapler for anastomosis between the residual stomach and esophagus, suture the serosal layer around the anastomosis to reinforce it. Perform a longitudinal incision and transverse suture on the pyloric wall.
8. Thorough hemostasis, irrigate the peritoneal cavity, aspirate the effluent, place two negative pressure balls around the anastomosis, count instruments and gauze, close the abdomen in layers.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: stomach, pylorus, lymph nodes. (胆总管)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included mobilization of the stomach and esophagus, and anastomosis of the residual stomach to the esophagus.

## Teaching Points
- Proper mobilization of the stomach and esophagus is crucial for lymph node dissection.
- Careful handling of the left gastric artery is necessary to prevent bleeding and injury to adjacent structures.
- Accurate anastomosis technique is essential for preventing leaks and ensuring proper reconstruction.

## Complication Prevention
- Thorough hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper anastomotic technique to prevent leaks and strictures.

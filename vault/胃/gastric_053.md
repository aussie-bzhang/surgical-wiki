## Overview
Gastric cancer resection with Billroth I reconstruction and D2 lymph node dissection. Involves resection of the stomach and surrounding structures, followed by reconstruction of the gastrointestinal tract.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located in the body of the stomach, near the lesser curvature of the antrum, approximately 3cm in diameter, ulcerative infiltrative type, extending beyond the serosa. Lymph nodes in groups 1, 2, 3, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 12 were enlarged. No obvious metastatic lesions were found in the abdominal and pelvic organs. No ascites.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Abdominal exploration via an upper midline incision around the umbilicus.
2. Transection of the gastrocolic ligament on the intestinal side, right to the bulb of the duodenum, left to the splenic flexure, 2-3 branches of the left gastric short vessels were transected and ligated. Dissection of the anterior leaf of the transverse colon mesentery to the superior margin of the pancreas.
3. Dissection and exposure of the right gastric artery below the pylorus, double ligature at the root, and lymph node dissection of group 6.
4. Transection of the lesser omentum below the liver, dissection and exposure of the anterior leaf of the hepatoduodenal ligament and its lymphatic tissue, and exposure of the right gastric artery. Transection and double ligature at the root of the right gastric artery, and lymph node dissection of group 8.
5. Transection of the duodenum 3cm distal to the pylorus, the distal end was placed into a 28.5mm anvil of the stapler and fixed. The stomach body was flipped up, the left gastric artery was transected and doubly ligated at the root, and lymph node dissection of group 7 was performed. The coronary vessels and lymphatic tissue on the lesser curvature (groups 1, 3) were cleared down to the cardia.
6. A hole was made in the anterior stomach wall to insert the long tube of the stapler, anastomosis of the stomach body to the duodenal stump 7cm above the tumor, 90mm clamp was used to transect the stomach body, and the specimen was removed. The anastomosis and stump were reinforced with a layer of seromuscular layer.
7. Hemostasis, lavage of the abdominal cavity, aspiration of exudate, placement of two negative pressure balls at the anastomosis, and layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen.

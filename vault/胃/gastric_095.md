## Overview
Gastric cancer resection with Billroth I reconstruction and D2 lymph node dissection for a patient with gastric antrum cancer.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric antrum cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric antrum cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located at the lesser curvature of the gastric antrum, approximately 4 cm in diameter, ulcerative infiltrative type, involving the serosal layer. No involvement of liver, pelvis, small intestine, or colon.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping, midline upper abdominal incision around the umbilicus.
2. Dissection of the gastrocolic ligament, right to the bulb of the duodenum, left to the upper 2-3 branches of the left gastric vessels and ligation.
3. Dissection of the greater omentum below the liver, stripping the anterior leaf of the gastrohepatic ligament and lymphatic tissue, exposing the right gastric vessel, and transecting and ligating it at the root.
4. Transection of the duodenum 3 cm distal to the pylorus, placing the anvil of a 28mm anastomosis stapler and securing it.
5. Transection and double ligature of the left gastric vessel at the root, and dissection of the 7th group of lymph nodes; removal of the coronary vessel and lymphatic tissue on the lesser curvature (groups 1, 3) to below the cardia.
6. Opening the anterior gastric wall to insert the long tube of the stapler, anastomosis of the posterior wall of the gastric body at the middle-third with the duodenal stump using a 55mm clamp.
7. Complete hemostasis, lavage of the peritoneal cavity, aspiration of effluent, placement of two negative pressure balls at the anastomosis, and layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen.

## Overview
Gastrectomy with esophagojejunostomy is a surgical procedure performed for advanced gastric cancer with intraperitoneal metastasis. The key technical feature is the anastomosis of the esophagus to the jejunum using a Roux-en-Y technique.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer with intraperitoneal metastasis
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a mass on the posterior wall of the gastric body, approximately 7cm in diameter, with an ulcerative type and invasion beyond the serosa. Lymph nodes in groups 1, 3, 6, and 7 were enlarged. A cancer nodule was found on the omentum. No significant abnormalities were observed in the liver during exploration.

## Operative Steps
1. The patient was placed in a supine position under general anesthesia. The abdomen was accessed via an upper midline incision.
2. The transverse colon nodule was resected and sent for pathology, and the colon wall was sutured.
3. The gastrocolic ligament was separated, extending to the right to the hepatic flexure of the colon and to the inferior pole of the spleen. The anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon was stripped to the surface of the pancreas, and the lesser omentum was opened to access the lesser curvature.
4. The right gastric artery and its surrounding lymph nodes were ligated and excised at the root of the pancreatic head below the pylorus.
5. The lesser omentum was transected below the liver, extending to the hepatoduodenal ligament, and the right gastric artery was ligated and transected.
6. The duodenum was transected and sutured at 3cm from the pylorus.
7. The left gastric artery and short gastric vessels were transected and ligated along the greater curvature until the left side of the cardia.
8. The gastric body was flipped up, and the left gastric artery at the root was transected and doubly ligated. The lesser omentum was separated upwards, and the esophagus was mobilized below the esophageal hiatus. The vagus nerves were transected bilaterally.
9. The esophagus was transected 3cm above the cardia, and the specimen was removed. The anvil of a 25mm circular stapler was placed and fixed with purse-string sutures.
10. The jejunum was transected 20cm from the Treitz ligament, and the esophagus was anastomosed to the distal jejunum using a 25mm stapler. The jejunal stump was closed with a side-to-side stapler. The proximal jejunum was anastomosed to the distal jejunum 45cm below the esophageal anastomosis (in front of the colon).
11. The mesenteric gaps were closed, and hemostasis was achieved. The abdomen was irrigated, and fluid was aspirated. Two negative pressure drainage tubes were placed below the anastomosis. Instruments and sponges were counted and found to be correct. The abdomen was closed in layers.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included extensive adhesions and the need for meticulous hemostasis.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of meticulous dissection and hemostasis in the presence of extensive adhesions.
- The technique of Roux-en-Y anastomosis and the importance of ensuring proper alignment and tension-free anastomosis.

## Complication Prevention
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper anastomotic technique to prevent leaks and strictures.

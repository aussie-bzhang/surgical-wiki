## Overview
Gastric cancer resection with esophagojejunostomy, a complex procedure involving the removal of the stomach and reconstruction of the gastrointestinal tract.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a 75cm2 mass in the gastric antrum, extending beyond the serosa and blocking the pylorus, causing incomplete obstruction. The tumor extended along the lesser curvature to the cardia. Lymph nodes in the 5th and 6th groups were palpable. No significant abnormalities were found in the liver, gallbladder, small intestine, colon, or pelvis.

## Operative Steps
1. Positioning the patient in supine position, under general anesthesia, and performing routine disinfection and draping.
2. Incision made in the upper midline of the abdomen, entering the peritoneal cavity.
3. Dissection of the gastrocolic ligament, extending to the right to the hepatic flexure of the colon and to the inferior pole of the spleen, with the anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon剥离至 the surface of the pancreas.
4. Transection and ligation of the right gastroepiploic artery below the pylorus, with lymph node dissection.
5. Transection of the lesser omentum below the liver, extending to the hepatoduodenal ligament, with transection and ligation of the right gastric artery.
6. Transection of the distal duodenum 3cm from the pylorus, with suture reinforcement.
7. Transection of the left gastroepiploic and short gastric arteries along the greater curvature until the left side of the cardia.
8. Upward mobilization of the gastric body, transection and double ligature of the left gastric artery at its root.
9. Mobilization along the lesser omentum, incision of the esophageal hiatus and bilateral peritoneum, mobilization of the distal esophagus, transection of the vagus nerves bilaterally; transection of the esophagus 3cm above the cardia.
10. Placement of the anvil of a 25mm circular stapler and fixation with purse-string suture.
11. Transection of the jejunum 20cm from the Treitz ligament, with the stapler performing an end-to-side anastomosis between the esophagus and the distal jejunum; closure of the jejunal stump with a side-to-side stapler. Anastomosis of the proximal jejunum with the distal jejunum 50cm below the esophageal anastomosis (in front of the colon); closure of the mesenteric gap.
12. Thorough hemostasis, irrigation of the peritoneal cavity, aspiration of effluent, placement of two negative-pressure drainage tubes below the anastomosis, verification of instruments and sponges, and layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen.

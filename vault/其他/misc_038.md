## Overview
Splenectomy is a surgical procedure performed for various indications, including Immune Thrombocytopenia (ITP). It involves the removal of the spleen, a key component of the immune system located in the upper left quadrant of the abdomen.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Immune Thrombocytopenia (ITP)
**Intraoperative:** Immune Thrombocytopenia (ITP)
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
The spleen measured 12*8 cm in size, with slight adhesions to surrounding structures and clear boundaries.

## Operative Steps
1. The patient was placed in a supine position under general anesthesia. The abdomen was accessed via an upper midline incision.
2. The gastrocolic ligament was opened, and the vessels were carefully separated and ligated one by one.
3. The splenic colonic ligament was transected, and the posterior splenic renal ligament was separated and cut. The splenic gastrolienal ligament was also transected, and the short gastric vessels were ligated one by one. The splenic diaphragmatic ligament was separated, and the splenic hilum was fully mobilized.
4. The spleen was mobilized, and the splenic artery and vein were separated and transected, ligated, and sutured. The specimen was removed.
5. The abdomen was lavaged with large volumes of normal saline, and hemostasis was achieved. A negative pressure drain was placed in the splenic fossa.
6. The instruments and sponges were counted and found to be correct. The abdomen was closed in layers, and the procedure was completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> The spleen is a vulnerable organ located in the upper left quadrant of the abdomen. The splenic hilum contains the splenic artery, vein, and lymphatics. Care must be taken to protect these structures during dissection.

## Teaching Points
- The splenic hilum is a critical area that requires meticulous dissection to avoid injury to the splenic artery and vein.
- Proper hemostasis is essential to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful dissection to avoid injury to the splenic vessels.
- Adequate lavage of the abdomen to remove any blood clots or debris.

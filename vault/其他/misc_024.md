## Overview
Splenectomy with portocaval shunt for portal hypertension and splenic hypersplenism; significant anatomical variations and technical challenges encountered.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Portal hypertension, splenic hypersplenism
**Intraoperative:** Confirmed by anatomical findings
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Splenomegaly, left liver atrophy, severe intra-abdominal venous曲张, particularly esophageal-gastric varices, dense adhesions to spleen, intraoperative pressure measurements, significant bleeding, postoperative ICU admission.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping, left upper quadrant 'L' shaped incision, exploration as described.
2. Dissection of gastrocolic ligament, transection of gastro splenic ligament to the left of the cardia, identification and ligation of splenic artery above the pancreas.
3. Transection of splenic colonic, renal, and diaphragmatic ligaments, complete mobilization of the splenic pedicle.
4. Separation and ligation of splenic artery and vein, removal of spleen, transection and ligation of gastric pancreas and left diaphragmatic vessels.
5. Transection and ligation of gastric coronary vessels along the lesser curvature, mobilization to the right of the cardia.
6. Mobilization of the lower esophagus, transection of vagus nerve, transection or ligation of esophageal varices to the esophageal hiatus, serosalization of the greater and lesser curvature of the stomach.
7. Abundant lavage of the abdominal cavity, hemostasis, placement of two negative pressure drains below the left diaphragm and behind the esophagus.
8. Counting sponges and instruments, closure in layers, surgery completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Spleen, portal vein, esophagus, gastroesophageal junction; common bile duct (胆总管)

## Technical Challenges
Significant bleeding, dense adhesions, anatomical variations.

## Teaching Points
- Management of significant bleeding
- Handling dense adhesions
- Technical aspects of portocaval shunt

## Complication Prevention
- Adequate hemostasis
- Careful dissection to avoid injury to adjacent structures

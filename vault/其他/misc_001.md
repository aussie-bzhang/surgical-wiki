## Overview
Pancreatic necrosectomy, cholecystectomy, and choledochotomy with T-tube drainage for necrotizing pancreatitis following ERCP, with significant necrotic tissue and abscess formation.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Periampullary tumor, post-ERCP, acute pancreatitis, diabetes.
**Intraoperative:** Periampullary tumor, post-ERCP, necrotizing pancreatitis, diabetes.
**Pathological:** Not documented.

## Intraoperative Findings
Gallbladder tense, common bile duct dilated, 2.8 cm in diameter. No intraductal tumor found in the bile duct by finger exploration and cholangioscopy. The distal bile duct lumen was obstructed. A 2 cm mass was palpated in the pancreas adjacent to the obstruction. Large necrotic tissue and abscess were found in the right lobe of the liver, below the kidney, posterior to the duodenum, and lateral to the head of the pancreas. Due to the mass being within the pancreatic parenchyma and surrounding inflammatory edema, biopsy tissue could not be obtained. No metastatic nodules were found in the liver, omentum, or pelvic cavity. No ascites.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline upper abdominal incision, enter peritoneum as described.
3. Free and separate the greater omentum, gallbladder, and liver from each other.
4. Carefully dissect the胆囊三角, ligate and tie the gallbladder vessels, strip the gallbladder from the liver-gallbladder bed to the gallbladder triangle, transect the cystic duct, and incise the anterior wall of the common bile duct.
5. Place a 24F T-tube in the common bile duct and suture the common bile duct.
6. Koch incision to free the duodenum, separate the loose tissue between the right lobe of the liver, the anterior aspect of the right kidney, and the posterior aspect of the duodenum.
7. Enucleate the large necrotic tissue along the capsule, rinse the remaining necrotic matter between the spaces with large amounts of saline.
8. Thorough hemostasis, rinse the peritoneal cavity, and aspirate the effluent fluid.
9. Place two negative pressure balls under the liver and one triple-lumen tube in the necrotic tissue area, and bring the T-tube out through a puncture wound beside the incision, fix it; close the layers in sequence.
10. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: pancreas, common bile duct, gallbladder. (胆总管)
> Danger zones: pancreatic head, duodenum.
> Structures requiring protection: bile duct, pancreatic duct.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included the location of the mass within the pancreatic parenchyma and surrounding inflammatory edema, making biopsy tissue difficult to obtain.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of careful dissection and identification of critical structures.
- The management of necrotic tissue and abscess formation.
- The technique of T-tube placement and drainage.

## Complication Prevention
- Preventing bile leakage by ensuring proper T-tube placement and suture of the bile duct.
- Preventing infection by maintaining strict aseptic technique and proper drainage.

## Overview
Splenectomy is a surgical procedure performed for various indications, including traumatic splenic rupture. It involves the removal of the spleen and is typically an open procedure.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Traumatic abdominal hemorrhage
**Intraoperative:** Traumatic splenic rupture with intraperitoneal hemorrhage
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraperitoneal blood clot of approximately 2000ml, multiple splenic ruptures, liver enlargement. No significant injury to the colon, small intestine, stomach, liver, or pancreas was found during exploration.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Laparotomy incision was made and exploration was performed as described.
2. The gastrocolic ligament was opened, and the splenic artery was found above the tail of the pancreas and ligated.
3. The splenic colonic ligament was divided, and the splenic renal ligament posteriorly and the splenic gastrolienal ligament anteriorly were separated and cut, with individual ligation of the short gastric vessels.
4. The splenic artery and vein were separated, and after cutting, they were doubly ligated and the spleen was removed.
5. The abdomen was lavaged with large amounts of normal saline, no free bile or fecal-like fluid was observed. Hemostasis was completed, and a negative pressure drain was placed in the splenic fossa.
6. The abdomen was closed in layers, and the procedure was completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: spleen, splenic artery, splenic hilum. (脾脏, 脾动脉, 脾蒂)

## Technical Challenges
Not documented

## Teaching Points
- The importance of identifying and ligating the splenic artery and vein to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- The technique of separating and cutting the splenic ligaments to achieve complete mobilization of the spleen.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Placement of a negative pressure drain to prevent postoperative abscess formation.

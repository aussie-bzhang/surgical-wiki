## Overview
Splenectomy with perigastric vascular ligation for post-hepatitis cirrhosis, portal hypertension, and splenic hypersplenism. Key technical feature includes selective ligation of splenic vessels and perigastric vessels.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Post-hepatitis cirrhosis, portal hypertension, splenic hypersplenism
**Intraoperative:** Confirmed diagnosis of post-hepatitis cirrhosis, portal hypertension, splenic hypersplenism
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Splenomegaly with a spleen measuring 25*20*15cm. Spleenic artery diameter 1.5cm, splenic vein diameter approximately 2cm. Esophageal-gastric varices with the largest diameter of about 1.5cm, some twisted into a mass. Liver shows mixed nodular cirrhosis with atrophy of both lobes.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Left upper quadrant 'L' incision for abdominal access.
2. Incise the gastrocolic ligament, divide the splenogastric ligament up to the left of the cardia, find the splenic artery above the pancreas and ligate it with two ties.
3. Divide the splenocolic, splenorenal, and splenodiaphragmatic ligaments, and fully mobilize the splenic pedicle.
4. Separate the splenic artery and vein, cut and ligate them with double ties, remove the spleen, and ligate the vessels between the stomach and pancreas and the left subdiaphragmatic vessels.
5. Divide and ligate the gastric coronary vessels along the lesser curvature of the stomach, mobilize upwards to the right of the cardia.
6. Mobilize the lower end of the esophagus, divide the vagus nerve, and continue upwards to the esophageal hiatus, divide or ligate the esophageal varices; plicate the serosa on both sides of the stomach.
7. Rinse the abdominal cavity with large amounts of normal saline, achieve hemostasis, and place two negative pressure drains below the left diaphragm and behind the esophagus.
8. Count sponges and instruments, close the abdomen in layers, and complete the operation.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the spleen, portal vein, gastric fundus, and esophagus. The common bile duct (胆总管) should be protected during the procedure.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered include mobilization of the spleen and ligation of the splenic vessels and perigastric vessels.

## Teaching Points
- Selective ligation of splenic vessels and perigastric vessels is crucial for preventing postoperative complications.
- Proper mobilization of the spleen and careful hemostasis are essential for a successful procedure.

## Complication Prevention
- Postoperative bleeding can be prevented by careful hemostasis and the use of negative pressure drains.
- Variceal bleeding can be prevented by thorough ligation of the perigastric vessels.

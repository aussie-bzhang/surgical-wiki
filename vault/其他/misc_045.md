## Overview
Splenectomy with perigastric vascular ligation and splenorenal venous shunt is a complex procedure performed for portal hypertension and splenomegaly. It involves resection of the spleen, ligation of the perigastric vessels, and creation of a splenorenal venous shunt to decompress the portal vein.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Liver cirrhosis, portal hypertension, splenomegaly
**Intraoperative:** Liver cirrhosis, portal hypertension, splenomegaly
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Spleen measuring 20*15*10cm, congested and enlarged, splenic vein diameter 1.0cm, visible varices around the stomach, liver showing mixed nodular cirrhosis. Portal vein pressure measured 32 cmH2O after laparotomy, 27 cmH2O after splenic artery ligation, 28 cmH2O after splenectomy, 19 cmH2O after shunt, 20 cmH2O after ligation.

## Operative Steps
1. Patient placed in supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Left upper quadrant 'L' shaped incision made, avoiding曲张 veins.
3. Gastrocolic ligament opened, middle colic vessels dissected and cannulated for portal pressure measurement.
4. Gastro splenic ligament divided until left gastric area, splenic artery found and ligated.
5. Splenic colic, splenic renal, and splenic diaphragmatic ligaments divided, spleen pedicle fully mobilized, splenic artery and vein separated, splenic artery ligated and divided.
6. Satinsky clamp applied to splenic vein, spleen resected, proximal splenic vein mobilized 3cm for anastomosis.
7. Renal hilum posterior peritoneum and perirenal fat opened, left renal vein mobilized 4cm, Satinsky clamp applied, part of renal vein wall excised to form a 1.0cm hole, splenic vein brought close to renal vein, 5-0 proline suture used for end-to-side anastomosis.
8. Gastropancreatic and left subdiaphragmatic vessels ligated, left gastric coronary vessels ligated and divided upwards to the right gastric fundus.
9. Lower esophageal end mobilized, vagus nerve cut, upwards to esophageal hiatus, varices ligated or sutured.
10. Abdomen irrigated with normal saline, hemostasis achieved, splenic bed sutured, two negative pressure drains placed subdiaphragmatically and behind the esophagus.
11. Sponges and instruments counted, abdomen closed in layers, procedure completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Spleen, portal vein, gastric fundus, renal vein. (脾脏，门静脉，胃底，肾静脉)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties in mobilizing the spleen, controlling bleeding, and performing the splenorenal venous anastomosis.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of proper mobilization of the spleen and control of bleeding.
- Technique of creating a splenorenal venous shunt.
- Management of varices during the procedure.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful mobilization to avoid injury to adjacent structures.
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper placement of drains to prevent fluid collection.

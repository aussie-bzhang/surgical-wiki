## Overview
Adhesiolysis for intestinal adhesions in a patient with post-pancreatic surgery and intestinal obstruction, involving粘连松解术 for the treatment of postoperative adhesions.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Post-pancreatic surgery, intestinal obstruction
**Intraoperative:** Post-pancreatic surgery, adhesive intestinal obstruction
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Left midupper abdomen para-median incision, omentum and small intestine adhered to the lower abdominal wall below the incision. The omentum and abdominal wall formed an adherent band compressing the ileum 30 cm from the ileocecal junction, causing a twist of approximately 360 degrees. A 20 cm segment of the small intestine was dilated locally but had good blood supply. No other abnormalities were observed in the remaining small intestine and colon.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, after general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Enter abdomen via the original incision and explore as described.
2. Separate the omentum from the abdominal wall, release the band, and reposition the small intestine. No intestinal necrosis was observed.
3. Careful hemostasis, count instruments and sponges, and close the abdomen in layers.
4. Operation completed.

## Overview
Gastric ulcer perforation repair and jejunostomy, a procedure performed for a gastric ulcer perforation with possible malignancy and acute peritonitis.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastrointestinal perforation, acute peritonitis
**Intraoperative:** Gastric ulcer perforation (possible malignancy), acute peritonitis
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Gastric antrum anterior wall with a 4.0*5.0cm ulcerous lesion, hard, protruding into the lumen with a central 4mm perforation, surrounded by purulent exudate, gastric perigastric lymph nodes significantly enlarged. The mass adhered to the pancreas and was fixed, unable to be moved. Abdominal pus and food residue approximately 200ml, surrounding tissue severe inflammation and edema, colon distension, right ovary palpable with a 2.5cm cyst, multiple liver cysts, no obvious abnormalities in small intestine and bile duct.

## Operative Steps
1. Place the patient in supine position, under general anesthesia, perform routine disinfection and draping.
2. Make a midupper abdominal midline incision around the umbilicus, enter the abdomen layer by layer, hemostasis, protect the skin, aspirate pus and food residue in the abdomen, as described above.
3. Considering the patient's advanced age, history of pacemaker implantation, long duration of perforation, severe abdominal contamination, large tumor with invasion and adhesion to surrounding tissues, fixed and not movable, assess that the patient cannot tolerate tumor resection. Therefore, a perforation repair and jejunostomy was performed, and the family was aware and understood.
4. Suture the ulcer break with antibacterial micro乔suture in interrupted full thickness, cover and embed the perforation site with omental tissue and ligate in interrupted fashion, cover the perforation site and surrounding area with second layer of omental tissue and fix.
5. Place a jejunostomy tube 30cm from the Treitz ligament and fix it to the abdominal wall.
6. Rinse the abdomen with large amounts of normal saline until clean, place two negative pressure drains around the perforation site and Winslow's hole.
7. Ensure no bleeding in the abdomen, count sponges and instruments correctly, and close the abdomen in layers.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included the fixed and adherent tumor mass, severe abdominal contamination, and the need for careful hemostasis and tissue protection.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of careful preoperative assessment and planning for complex cases.
- The technique of omental coverage and fixation for securing the repair.
- The management of abdominal contamination and the use of negative pressure drainage.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Adequate drainage to prevent abscess formation.

## Overview
Abdominal wall mass excision is a surgical procedure performed for the removal of a mass located in the abdominal wall. The clinical context involves the diagnosis of a palpable mass in the right lower quadrant, with key technical features including careful dissection and preservation of surrounding structures.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right lower quadrant mass
**Intraoperative:** Right abdominal wall (subcostal) mass
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperatively, a mass was found located subcostally on the right side, measuring approximately 10*5cm. The mass was adherent to the peritoneum and ribs on its deep surface and extended downward along the anterior sheath of the rectus abdominis muscle to the umbilical level. The mass contained approximately 80ml of milky pus.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. A梭形 incision (long axis parallel to the costal margin) was made, and the layers were opened sequentially.
3. Careful dissection was performed to separate the mass from surrounding tissues, with a small portion of adherent peritoneum resected.
4. The specimen was removed, the peritoneum was closed, and a negative pressure drain was placed at the lowest point of the abscess cavity, with an additional incision made to bring it out.
5. Hemostasis was achieved, the incision was cleaned thoroughly, and the layers were closed sequentially.
6. The procedure was completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> The common bile duct (胆总管) and other critical structures should be protected during dissection. The mass is adherent to the peritoneum and ribs on its deep surface.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included careful dissection to preserve surrounding structures and managing the abscess cavity.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of careful dissection to avoid injury to surrounding structures.
- The technique of managing an abscess cavity with proper drainage.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Thorough irrigation of the surgical site to reduce the risk of infection.

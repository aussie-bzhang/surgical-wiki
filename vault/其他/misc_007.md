## Overview
Splenectomy with perigastric vascular ligation for liver cirrhosis, portal hypertension, and splenic hyperactivity. Key technical feature includes identification and ligation of esophagogastric varices.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Liver cirrhosis, portal hypertension, splenic hyperactivity
**Intraoperative:** Liver cirrhosis, portal hypertension, splenic hyperactivity
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Splenomegaly with a spleen measuring approximately 35*25*20cm. Marked esophagogastric varices, largest diameter about 1cm and fused into a mass. Splenic artery diameter 2cm, splenic vein diameter 1.5cm with high tension. Liver shows nodular cirrhosis with atrophy, particularly in the right lobe.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Left upper quadrant 'L' incision for abdominal access.
2. Incise the gastrocolic ligament, divide the splenogastric ligament superiorly to the left of the cardia, find the splenic artery above the pancreas and ligate with 7-0 silk in two layers.
3. Divide the splenocolic, splenorenal, and splenodiaphragmatic ligaments, and fully mobilize the splenic pedicle.
4. Separate the splenic artery and vein, divide and ligate them in two layers, remove the spleen, and ligate the vessels between the stomach and pancreas and the left subdiaphragmatic vessels.
5. Divide and ligate the gastric coronary vessels along the lesser curvature of the stomach, mobilize superiorly to the right of the cardia; mobilize the lower end of the esophagus, divide the vagus nerve, and continue superiorly to the esophageal hiatus, divide or ligate the esophageal varices; plicate the serosa on both sides of the greater and lesser curvatures of the stomach.
6. Rinse the abdominal cavity with large amounts of normal saline, achieve hemostasis, and place two negative-pressure drains below the left diaphragm and behind the esophagus.
7. Count sponges and instruments, close the abdomen in layers, and complete the procedure.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the spleen, esophagogastric junction, portal vein, and splenic artery. The danger zones are the splenic hilum and the esophagogastric varices. Structures requiring protection are the splenic artery and vein, and the esophagus.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered include mobilization of the spleen due to its size and the presence of varices. Management involved careful dissection and ligation of vessels.

## Teaching Points
- Identification and ligation of esophagogastric varices are critical to prevent postoperative bleeding.
- Proper mobilization of the spleen and splenic pedicle is essential to avoid injury to adjacent structures.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis and placement of drains to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Use of a laparoscopic approach may reduce postoperative pain and recovery time.

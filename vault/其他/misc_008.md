## Overview
Colonic stenting is a surgical procedure performed for the creation of a stoma in the colon, typically for the management of obstructive colonic diseases. It involves the creation of a stoma in the transverse colon and securing it to the abdominal wall.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Obstructive colonic disease
**Intraoperative:** Confirmed obstructive colonic disease
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Operative Steps
1. Under epidural anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. A transverse incision is made at the surface projection of the middle and upper transverse colon. The transverse colon and peritoneum below the incision are mobilized.
3. A glass rod is inserted into the mesocolon of the transverse colon to perform colonic stenting, and it is secured to the peritoneum and skin.
4. Completion of the procedure.

## Overview
Gastric anastomotic leak repair and jejunostomy after palliative resection for gastric cancer, involving repair of the leak and creation of a jejunostomy. Key technical feature includes identification and repair of the leak at the gastrojejunal anastomosis.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer, post-palliative resection (total gastrectomy + Roux-en-Y anastomosis) with anastomotic leak.
**Intraoperative:** Confirmed anastomotic leak.
**Pathological:** Not documented.

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings included approximately 400ml of purulent secretion in the peritoneal cavity, located beneath the liver, in the pelvis, and at the jejuno-jejunal anastomosis. Significant tissue edema with a surface of pus. A 0.8cm leak was observed at the jejunal stump below the gastrojejunal anastomosis. The leak was repaired and a retrograde inverted gastric tube was placed into the esophagus through the jejunum; a jejunal feeding tube was inserted into the distal jejunum. Negative pressure drainage was placed above and below the gastrojejunal anastomosis and in the splenic fossa.

## Operative Steps
1. Patient placed in supine position, under general anesthesia, with routine disinfection and draping.
2. Incision made at the original site, peritoneum entered, and exploration conducted.
3. Absorbent gauze used to remove peritoneal pus, locate the leak, and suture it with additional serosal layer reinforcement.
4. Abdominal cavity lavaged with warm saline, retrograde inverted gastric tube placed into the esophagus through the jejunum, and jejunal feeding tube inserted into the distal jejunum.
5. Negative pressure drainage placed above and below the gastrojejunal anastomosis and in the splenic fossa.
6. Sutures counted and abdomen closed in layers.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Gastroesophageal junction (贲门食管交界处), jejunum (空肠), spleen (脾脏). Structures requiring protection include the gastroesophageal anastomosis and the jejuno-jejunal anastomosis.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included identification and repair of the leak, ensuring proper placement of the retrograde inverted gastric tube, and managing the purulent secretion.

## Teaching Points
- Identification and repair of anastomotic leaks.
- Proper placement of retrograde inverted gastric tube.
- Management of purulent secretion.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful identification and repair of the leak.
- Adequate lavage of the abdominal cavity.
- Proper placement of negative pressure drainage.

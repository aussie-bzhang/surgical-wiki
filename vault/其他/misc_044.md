## Overview
Posterior peritoneal cystectomy and appendectomy for a patient with a posterior peritoneal cyst and appendicitis. Key technical feature includes complete resection of the cyst with intact capsule and appendectomy.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Posterior peritoneal cystic mass
**Intraoperative:** Posterior peritoneal cyst
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
The tumor was located in the posterior peritoneum, measuring approximately 20×15×15cm^3, with clear boundaries from surrounding tissues. The appendix was about 7cm long with a diameter of 1.2cm and was red and swollen.

## Operative Steps
1. Place the patient in a supine position under general anesthesia and perform routine disinfection and draping.
2. Make a midline abdominal incision around the umbilicus, enter the abdomen layer by layer, and expose the tumor as described above.
3. Push the small intestine aside, open the lateral peritoneum, and expose the tumor. Incise the tumor along its margin to remove it completely (with intact capsule).
4. Perform hemostasis, cover the wound with hemostatic powder and gauze, and close the posterior peritoneum.
5. Ligate and cut the appendiceal mesentery to the root of the appendix, suture the root, and cut the appendix 0.5cm from the root. Remove the specimen and suture the stump in a purse-string manner.
6. Count instruments and sponges, close the incision in layers, and complete the procedure.

## Anatomical Key Points
> The common bile duct (胆总管) and other surrounding structures should be protected during the procedure.

## Teaching Points
- The complete resection of the cyst with intact capsule is crucial for preventing recurrence.
- Proper hemostasis and appendectomy technique are essential to minimize postoperative complications.

## Complication Prevention
- Ensure complete resection of the cyst to prevent recurrence.
- Proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.

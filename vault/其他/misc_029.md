## Overview
Splenectomy is a surgical procedure performed for various indications, including splenic rupture. It involves the removal of the spleen, a vital organ located in the left upper quadrant of the abdomen.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Subcapsular hematoma of the spleen
**Intraoperative:** Rupture of the spleen
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
The spleen measured 15*10*5cm with a 2.0cm long rupture on the superior pole. The spleen was wrapped by omentum, surrounded by a large amount of blood clots, and there was approximately 600ml of peritoneal hemorrhage. Exploration of the liver, pancreas, stomach, and small intestine revealed no significant injury.

## Operative Steps
1. The patient was placed in a supine position under general anesthesia. The abdomen was entered via a midline upper abdominal incision.
2. The gastrocolic ligament was opened, and the adherent omentum was dissected.
3. The splenic colonic ligament was transected, and the splenic renal ligament posteriorly and the splenic gastrolienal ligament anteriorly were separated and transected. The short gastric vessels were ligated individually, and the splenic diaphragmatic ligament was separated to fully mobilize the splenic hilum.
4. The splenic artery and vein were separated, transected, and doubly ligated before removal of the spleen.
5. The abdomen was lavaged with large amounts of normal saline, hemostasis was achieved, and a negative pressure drain was placed in the splenic fossa.
6. The abdomen was closed in layers, and the procedure was completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> The spleen, gastrocolic ligament, and splenic hilum are critical structures. The common bile duct (胆总管) should be protected during the procedure.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of thorough exploration and identification of the splenic hilum.
- Proper hemostasis techniques to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- The need for careful dissection to avoid injury to adjacent structures.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Careful dissection to avoid injury to adjacent structures such as the splenic artery and vein.

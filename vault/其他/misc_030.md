## Overview
Duodenal bulb perforation repair in a patient with acute peritonitis and duodenal bulb perforation.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Acute peritonitis, gastrointestinal perforation
**Intraoperative:** Acute peritonitis, duodenal bulb perforation
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Severe adhesions in the upper abdomen, large amount of food residue and exudate in the abdominal cavity (approx. 400ml), a hard mass palpable at the duodenal bulb, severely adherent to surrounding tissues, approximately 4cm in diameter, with a central 0.5cm perforation. No significant abnormalities found in the stomach, remaining small intestine, and large intestine.

## Operative Steps
1. Position the patient in supine position, under general anesthesia, perform routine disinfection and draping. Make a midline upper abdominal incision to enter the abdomen.
2. Dissect the abdominal adhesions, aspirate the abdominal effusion and food residue.
3. Take a biopsy of the mass and send it for pathological examination.
4. Extend a 16F urinary catheter through the perforation site to probe, which can pass through the pylorus to the gastric cavity and through the duodenal bulb to the distal intestinal segment.
5. Suture the ulcer site with three interrupted full-thickness sutures, tie the网膜 tissue, cover the网膜 tissue again, and secure it.
6. Achieve hemostasis, irrigate the abdominal cavity with large amounts of normal saline, aspirate, and place two negative-pressure drains at the perforation site.
7. Count sponges and instruments to ensure nothing is left in the abdomen, and close the layers sequentially.

## Overview
Gastrectomy with pyloroplasty for gastric cardia cancer, with emphasis on resection and reconstruction techniques.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cardia cancer
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cardia cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located at the gastric cardia, ulcerative type, 2×2cm, not involving the serosa layer; 7th group lymph nodes significantly enlarged; no metastasis to abdominal and pelvic organs; no ascites.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia successful, routine disinfection and draping; midline upper abdominal incision for abdominal exploration.
2. Dissection and transection of gastrocolic ligament, right to the hepatic flexure of the colon, left to the inferior pole of the spleen; transection of splenocolic ligament.
3. Lifting the transverse colon, stripping the anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon to the superior margin of the pancreas, preserving the right gastroepiploic vessels, and transecting the greater omentum at the avascular area of the greater curvature of the stomach.
4. Clamping and transecting the left gastric vessels, separating the stomach body along the greater curvature, freeing the left side of the cardia, protecting the spleen.
5. Transection of the lesser omentum below the liver, flipping the stomach body upwards, transection and double ligature of the left gastric vessels at the root; separation along the lesser omentum upwards, incising the esophageal hiatus and bilateral peritoneum, freeing the lower esophagus; clamping and transecting the vagus nerve, transecting the esophagus 3cm above the cardia.
6. Transection of the distal stomach with a linear stapler, removing the specimen, inserting a 25mm circular stapler for gastroesophageal anastomosis, wrapping the serosal layer around the anastomosis.
7. Pyloroplasty performed.
8. Hemostasis, lavage of the abdominal cavity, aspiration of exudate, placement of negative pressure balls at the anterior and posterior walls of the esophageal anastomosis, count instruments and gauze, close the abdomen in layers.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: stomach, esophagus, pylorus, spleen, lymph nodes. (胃, 食管, 幽门, 脾脏, 淋巴结)

## Teaching Points
- Proper handling of the spleen during dissection.
- Technique for safe and effective esophageal transection and anastomosis.
- Importance of meticulous hemostasis and lavage.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful dissection to avoid injury to adjacent structures.
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.

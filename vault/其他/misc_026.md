## Overview
Duodenal bulb perforation repair in a patient with acute peritonitis and duodenal bulb perforation.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Acute peritonitis, gastrointestinal perforation
**Intraoperative:** Acute peritonitis, duodenal bulb perforation
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Severe adhesions in the upper abdomen, large amount of food residue and exudate in the abdominal cavity (approx. 400ml), a hard mass palpable at the duodenal bulb, severely adherent to surrounding tissues, size approximately 4cm in diameter, central 0.5cm perforation, severe inflammation and edema of surrounding tissues. No significant abnormalities found in the stomach, remaining small intestine, and large intestine.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline upper abdominal incision, enter peritoneal cavity, exploration as described.
3. Dissection of abdominal adhesions, aspiration of abdominal effusion and food residue.
4. Biopsy of the mass tissue for pathological examination.
5. Interrupted full-thickness suture of the ulcer site with three sutures, embedding the网膜 tissue and ligating, covering the网膜 tissue again and securing.
6. Thorough hemostasis, lavage the abdominal cavity with large amounts of normal saline, aspiration, placing a negative pressure drain at WINSLOW hole and perforation site.
7. Counting sponges and instruments, close the layers sequentially.

## Anatomical Key Points

## Technical Challenges
Severe adhesions and inflammation.

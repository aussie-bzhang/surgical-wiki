## Overview
Exploratory laparotomy and abdominal drainage for gastrointestinal perforation with suspected tuberculosis peritonitis, rheumatoid arthritis, post-coronary stent placement, and pulmonary tuberculosis.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastrointestinal perforation
**Intraoperative:** Gastrointestinal perforation, suspected tuberculosis peritonitis, rheumatoid arthritis, post-coronary stent placement, pulmonary tuberculosis?
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Severe abdominal adhesions, liver enlargement to 4 fingers below the costal margin, dense adhesions between stomach, transverse colon, and peritoneum, difficult to separate.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping, midline incision for abdominal access, exploration as described above;
2. Separation of abdominal adhesions, but difficult to separate, carefully separate a channel along the abdominal wall to the pelvis, aspirate 1000ml of dark red bile-containing pus, please Dr. Zhao Gang, the second chief on duty, examine the patient after which it was decided not to proceed with further surgery, two negative pressure drains were placed in the pelvis;
3. Close the abdomen in layers, surgery completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: abdominal cavity, liver, gastrointestinal tract. (腹腔，肝脏，胃肠道)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in separating abdominal adhesions.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of careful adhesion separation to avoid injury to critical structures.
- The need for a thorough exploration in cases of suspected tuberculosis peritonitis.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper aseptic technique to prevent contamination.
- Careful handling of adhesions to avoid bowel injury.

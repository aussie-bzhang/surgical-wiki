## Overview
Incisional hernia repair with tension-free mesh placement and complex small bowel adhesiolysis in a patient with a history of appendectomy.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Incisional hernia after appendectomy
**Intraoperative:** Incisional hernia after appendectomy
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Original surgical incision was a right transverse rectus sheath incision. The hernia sac was approximately 15 cm with a hernia ring diameter of 10 cm. Severe intra-abdominal adhesions were noted with no significant ascites.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Incision at the original site in the form of a diamond shape, gradually probing into the abdomen.
3. Freely mobilize the hernia sac and separate the subperitoneal bowel segment. Protect the bowel segment and thoroughly separate the intra-abdominal adhesions, then thoroughly clean and stop bleeding.
4. Separate the rectus sheath and subcutaneous space, place a tension-free mesh in the peritoneal cavity, and fix it to the rectus muscle and peritoneum.
5. Thoroughly clean and stop bleeding, place a negative pressure drain tube under the skin, and puncture it out.
6. Thoroughly stop bleeding, clean the wound, and close the incision in layers.
7. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: incisional defect, small bowel, abdominal wall. (胆总管)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included severe intra-abdominal adhesions and the need for careful mobilization of the bowel to prevent injury.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of careful mobilization of the bowel to prevent injury.
- The technique of tension-free mesh placement for incisional hernia repair.
- The management of complex adhesions during surgery.

## Complication Prevention
- Thorough adhesiolysis to prevent recurrence.
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.

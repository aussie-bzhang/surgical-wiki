## Overview
Splenectomy for traumatic splenic rupture in a patient with intra-abdominal hemorrhage. Key technical feature includes identification and ligation of the splenic artery.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Traumatic abdominal hemorrhage
**Intraoperative:** Traumatic splenic rupture with intra-abdominal hemorrhage
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intra-abdominal non-clotting blood and blood clots totaling approximately 800ml. Multiple contusions and lacerations at the inferior pole of the spleen near the hilum, with the longest laceration measuring about 3cm. No significant injury to the colon, small intestine, stomach, liver, or pancreas was found during exploration.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Laparotomy via left upper quadrant subcostal incision.
2. Incise the gastrocolic ligament, find the splenic artery above the pancreas, and ligate it.
3. Dissect the splenicocolic ligament, and separate and cut the splenic renal ligament posteriorly, the splenogastric ligament anteriorly, ligate the short gastric vessels one by one, separate and cut the splenoperitoneal ligament, and fully mobilize the splenic pedicle.
4. Separate the splenic artery and vein, cut them, and ligate them with a double knot. Remove the spleen.
5. Rinse the abdominal cavity with large amounts of normal saline, no obvious free bile or fecal-like fluid was seen. Perfect hemostasis, place two negative pressure drains in the splenic fossa.
6. Close the abdomen in layers, surgery completed.

## Anatomical Key Points

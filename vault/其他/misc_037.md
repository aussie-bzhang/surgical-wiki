## Overview
Splenectomy for a spleen tumor, with emphasis on the technical aspects of spleen mobilization and vascular ligation.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Spleen tumor
**Intraoperative:** Spleen tumor
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Spleen measuring 10*7*3cm, with a 3cm nodule at the superior pole, firm texture, and clear borders.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline upper abdominal incision, enter peritoneal cavity, explore findings.
3. Open gastrocolic ligament, carefully separate and ligate vessels.
4. Transect splenic colonic ligament, and separate and cut the posterior splenic renal ligament, anterior splenic gastrostomy ligament, ligate short gastric vessels, separate and cut the splenic diaphragmatic ligament, fully mobilize the splenic hilum.
5. Mobilize the spleen, separate splenic artery and vein, cut and ligate separately after, remove specimen.
6. Rinse peritoneal cavity with large amounts of normal saline, perfect hemostasis, place 1 negative pressure drain in the splenic fossa.
7. Count instruments and sponges, close abdomen in layers, surgery completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: spleen, gastrocolic ligament, splenic hilum. (脾脏, 胃结肠韧带, 脾蒂)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in mobilizing the spleen and ligating the splenic vessels.

## Teaching Points
- Proper mobilization of the spleen and ligation of splenic vessels.
- Importance of hemostasis and negative pressure drainage.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Adequate negative pressure drainage to prevent abscess formation.

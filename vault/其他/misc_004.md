## Overview
Posterior peritoneal tumor resection; clinical context: posterior peritoneal mass; key technical feature: complete resection of the tumor with preservation of surrounding structures.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Posterior peritoneal tumor
**Intraoperative:** Posterior peritoneal tumor
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings: posterior peritoneal cystic tumor, diameter approximately 7cm, clear borders, intact capsule, no adhesions with surrounding tissues.

## Operative Steps
1. Place the patient in supine position under general anesthesia, perform routine disinfection and draping.
2. Make a midline incision around the umbilicus, enter the peritoneal cavity, and expose the tumor as described.
3. Displace the small intestine, open the lateral peritoneum, expose the tumor, and resect the tumor completely, paying attention to protect the mesenteric vessels and ureter.
4. Perform hemostasis, count instruments and sponges, cover the wound with biological protein glue and hemostatic gauze, close the incision in layers, and complete the procedure.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: posterior peritoneum, intestine, mesenteric vessels, ureter. (腹膜后，肠管，肠系膜血管，输尿管)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered: protecting mesenteric vessels and ureter during tumor resection.

## Teaching Points
- Ensuring complete resection of the tumor while preserving surrounding structures.
- Proper hemostasis and wound closure techniques.

## Complication Prevention
- Preventing postoperative infection and bleeding by ensuring proper wound closure and infection control measures.

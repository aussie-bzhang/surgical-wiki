## Overview
Colonic stump closure is a surgical procedure performed after colonic stoma creation, typically for the management of ileostomy or colostomy complications. It involves the closure of the colonic stoma and reconnection of the colon to the abdominal wall.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Rectal cancer, colonic stoma creation
**Intraoperative:** Rectal cancer, colonic stoma creation
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a colonic stoma in a sigmoid position, located at the original midupper abdominal incision. Partial bowel wall was adherent to the subperitoneal membrane below the incision.

## Operative Steps
1. After general anesthesia and routine antisepsis, a midline incision 3.0 cm above and below the stoma was made. The colonic stump and peritoneum below the incision were mobilized. The stoma was trimmed to normal tissue, and hemostasis was achieved at the margins. Full-thickness interrupted sutures and seromuscular layer sutures were applied.
2. The abdomen was closed in layers, and the procedure was concluded.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the colonic stump and the abdominal wall. The common bile duct (胆总管) should be protected during the procedure.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included adherence of bowel wall to the subperitoneal membrane, which was managed by careful dissection and hemostasis.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of identifying and protecting the critical structures during colonic stump closure.
- Proper technique for mobilizing the colonic stump and peritoneum.
- The significance of achieving adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative complications.

## Complication Prevention
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper closure technique to prevent leakage and infection.

## Overview
Pelvic mass excision for a pelvic mass, with emphasis on identifying and preserving critical structures such as the uterus, rectum, sacrum, iliac vessels, and ureters.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Pelvic mass
**Intraoperative:** Pelvic mass (teratoma possible)
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperatively, a tumor was found posterior to the vagina, lateral and posterior to the left rectum, measuring approximately 5×4cm in diameter with a complete capsule, clear borders, and severe adhesions to the sacrum. The tumor contained a large amount of curd-like substance within its capsule.

## Operative Steps
1. Place the patient in a supine position under general anesthesia and perform routine disinfection and draping.
2. Make a midline incision below the navel, enter the abdomen layer by layer, and expose the rectum and pelvic floor peritoneum as described above.
3. Push the small intestine upwards to expose the rectum and pelvic floor peritoneum, open the retroperitoneum, free the tumor, and transect the tumor vessels at the root. Separate the distal end to the subperitoneal fold below the tumor and the proximal end to 5cm above the tumor, and excise the tumor completely. Pay attention to the course of the ureters and iliac vessels.
4. Close the mesenteric opening, place two negative pressure drains at the pelvic floor, and puncture the incision to lead out.
5. Irrigate, ensure hemostasis, verify that instruments and sponges are accounted for, and close the incision individually. The operation is completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the uterus, rectum, sacrum, iliac vessels, and ureters. (子宫, 直肠, 骶骨, 髂血管, 肾盂输尿管)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included severe adhesions to the sacrum and careful dissection around the ureters and iliac vessels.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of identifying and preserving critical structures such as the ureters and iliac vessels.
- The technique of managing severe adhesions and ensuring complete tumor excision.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis and ensuring the integrity of the ureters and iliac vessels.

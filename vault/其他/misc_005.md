## Overview
A surgical procedure performed for acute pancreatitis, cholecystitis, and retroperitoneal abscess. Key technical features include debridement and drainage of the pancreas and retroperitoneal abscess, cholecystectomy, and gastrostomy.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Acute pancreatitis
**Intraoperative:** Acute pancreatitis, cholecystitis, retroperitoneal abscess
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed approximately 500ml of bile-like peritoneal fluid, a gallbladder measuring 10*6*4cm with泥沙样结石, a walled-off collection with brownish turbid fluid measuring approximately 800ml on the gallbladder bed and lateral aspect of the right liver lobe, a large retroperitoneal abscess with irregular cavities extending from the pancreas to the superior margin of the pelvis, right to the right paracolic gutter, containing a large amount of necrotic tissue and thick pus approximately 500ml.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia and routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline upper abdominal incision around the umbilicus.
3. Opening the walled-off collection in the right upper quadrant, draining fluid, and separating adhesions.
4. Dissection of the胆囊三角, freeing the cystic artery and cystic duct, ligating and cutting them, stripping the gallbladder from the liver surface along the serosal surface, and removing the specimen.
5. Division of the greater omentum, opening the lesser omentum, and clearing the retroperitoneal abscess cavity of pus.
6. Opening the lateral peritoneum, freeing the right paracolic gutter, and thoroughly clearing necrotic tissue and pus.
7. Hemostasis, copious lavage of the abdominal cavity with normal saline until clean.
8. Placement of a double-lumen tube and a negative pressure ball in the right paracolic gutter, a PASMA catheter in the retroperitoneal abscess cavity, and a negative pressure drain in the pelvis, all exiting through stab wounds.
9. Opening the anterior wall of the gastric body near the greater curvature, inserting a gastrostomy tube, and placing its jejunum tube into the duodenum and proximal jejunum, securing the gastric wall to the abdominal wall with suture needles.
10. Layered closure of the abdomen, completion of the procedure.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the pancreas, gallbladder, and common bile duct. The danger zones are the retroperitoneal abscess cavity and the gallbladder bed. Structures requiring protection are the common bile duct and the portal vein.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included extensive adhesions, a large retroperitoneal abscess, and the need for careful dissection to avoid injury to critical structures.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of thorough lavage of the abdominal cavity to prevent infection.
- The technique of opening and draining a retroperitoneal abscess.
- The importance of careful dissection to avoid injury to the common bile duct and portal vein.

## Complication Prevention
- Thorough lavage of the abdominal cavity to prevent infection.
- Careful dissection to avoid injury to critical structures.

## Overview
Posterior peritoneal tumor resection; clinical context: posterior peritoneal tumor; key technical feature: complete resection of the tumor while preserving adjacent structures.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Posterior peritoneal tumor
**Intraoperative:** Posterior peritoneal tumor
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located in the retroperitoneum of the left upper abdomen. Medially, it is adjacent to the abdominal aorta, posterior to the body and tail of the pancreas. Inferiorly, it is adjacent to the left adrenal gland and kidney. The left renal artery and vein pass in front of the tumor inferiorly.

## Operative Steps
1. Place the patient in a supine position under general anesthesia, perform routine disinfection and draping.
2. Make a midline incision below the navel, enter the abdomen layer by layer, and protect the bladder during the procedure.
3. Push the small intestine upwards to expose the sigmoid colon and pelvic peritoneum. Open the pelvic peritoneum from the sides of the rectum, and dissect inferiorly to the superior margin of the mass, taking care to expose and protect the bilateral ureters and iliac vessels.
4. Elevate the rectum and dissect the tumor from the capsule completely, remove the specimen, and carefully coagulate the bleeding at the wound site.
5. Close the pelvic peritoneum, place a negative pressure drain, and puncture the incision to lead out the drain.
6. Remove the right ovarian cyst, strip the left ovarian cyst, and suture the left ovary with 3-0 absorbable suture.
7. Irrigate, ensure adequate hemostasis, count instruments and sponges, close the incisions one by one, and complete the procedure.

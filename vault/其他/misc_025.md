## Overview
Small bowel exploration following partial gastrectomy for acute fecal ileus, key technical feature being identification and management of fecal obstruction.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Partial gastrectomy, acute intestinal obstruction
**Intraoperative:** Partial gastrectomy, fecal ileus
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Upper abdominal adhesions, small amount of ascites, small intestine highly distended with fluid, containing large amounts of solid food. A large amount of food accumulated at approximately 80 cm from the ileocecal valve, forming a hard, sausagelike fecal mass, causing complete intestinal obstruction. No abnormalities found in the colon during exploration.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Midline abdominal incision for abdominal access.
2. Exploration revealed the above findings; 
3. The food mass and proximal retained food and fluid were pushed into the cecum and colon through the ileocecal valve; 
4. Closed the abdomen in layers; 
5. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: small intestine, ileocecal valve, colon. (小肠，回盲瓣，结肠)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in identifying and removing the fecal mass causing obstruction.

## Teaching Points
- Identification and management of fecal obstruction in the small intestine.
- Importance of thorough exploration and identification of the source of obstruction.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful handling to avoid injury to adjacent structures during dissection.

## Related Procedures

## Overview
Mammary fibroadenoma excision is a surgical procedure performed for the removal of benign breast tumors. It is typically indicated for palpable breast lumps and is characterized by its minimally invasive nature.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Palpable breast lump
**Intraoperative:** Fibroadenoma
**Pathological:** Fibroadenoma of the right lateral and inferior quadrant of the breast

## Intraoperative Findings
A fibroadenoma of 1.0-1.5 cm in size was palpable on the lateral and inferior quadrant of the right breast. The tumor was firm and mobile.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, local anesthesia achieved, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Radiating incision on the surface of the tumor, sequentially opening the skin, subcutaneous fat, and breast tissue, exploration as described above.
3. Complete excision of the tumor along its margin.
4. Thorough hemostasis, closure of the incision in layers, no dead space left.
5. Procedure completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the mammary gland and breast tissue. The common bile duct (胆总管) is not involved in this procedure.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of accurate palpation and identification of the tumor.
- The technique of excising the tumor with minimal damage to surrounding breast tissue.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hematoma.
- Careful closure to avoid dead space and infection.

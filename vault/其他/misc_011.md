## Overview
Splenectomy for a giant spleen due to liver cirrhosis and portal hypertension with post-embolic splenic abscess. Key technical feature includes meticulous dissection and control of splenic vessels.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Liver cirrhosis, portal hypertension, post-embolic splenic abscess
**Intraoperative:** Confirmed by exploration
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Liver with nodular cirrhosis, left lobe severely atrophic, spleen enlarged, almost complete splenic infarction, dense adhesions between spleen and omentum, colon, stomach, and diaphragm. Severe varices in the abdominal cavity, 500ml of ascites.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. L-shaped incision on the left upper abdomen.
2. Incise the gastrocolic ligament, carefully dissect and ligate individual vessels.
3. Transect the splenicocolic ligament, and separate and cut the splenic renal ligament posteriorly, and the splenogastric ligament anteriorly. Ligate the short gastric vessels individually, separate and cut the splenodiaphragmatic ligament, and fully mobilize the splenic hilum.
4. Elicit the spleen, separate the splenic artery and vein, and transect and doubly ligate them before removing the spleen.
5. Rinse the abdominal cavity with large amounts of normal saline, perfect hemostasis, suture the tail of the pancreas, cover the diaphragm, tail of the pancreas, and fundus of the stomach with hemostatic gauze, and place two negative-pressure drains in the splenic fossa.
6. Close the abdomen in layers, surgery completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Splenic hilum (脾蒂), gastrocolic ligament (胃结肠韧带), splenic vessels (脾血管)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered include dense adhesions and control of splenic vessels.

## Teaching Points
- Meticulous dissection to avoid injury to adjacent structures.
- Proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Safe removal of the spleen with control of splenic vessels.

## Complication Prevention
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper drainage to prevent infection and abscess formation.

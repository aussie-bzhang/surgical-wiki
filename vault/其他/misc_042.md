## Overview
Abdominal wall mass excision is a procedure performed for the removal of an abdominal wall mass, typically due to inflammatory conditions. The key technical feature involves careful dissection and resection of the mass while preserving adjacent structures.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right lower quadrant mass
**Intraoperative:** Right lower quadrant wall inflammatory mass
**Pathological:** Inflammatory abdominal wall mass

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperatively, a mass was found located beneath the external oblique aponeurosis, measuring approximately 4*5cm in size, hard in consistency, and adherent to the peritoneum.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, routine disinfection and draping under combined spinal-epidural anesthesia.
2. Incision at the original right lower quadrant McBurney's point, layer-by-layer opening, exploration as described above.
3. Careful dissection of the mass margin, separation of tissue adhesions, and resection of part of the peritoneum adherent to the mass.
4. Removal of the specimen, thorough hemostasis, and layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen.
5. Completion of the procedure.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the abdominal wall and peritoneum. The common bile duct (胆总管) should be protected during dissection.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included careful dissection to preserve adjacent structures and thorough hemostasis.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of preserving adjacent structures during dissection.
- The technique of careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative complications.

## Complication Prevention
- Infection prevention through meticulous surgical technique and postoperative antibiotic prophylaxis.

## Overview
Gastric cancer resection with Billroth I reconstruction and cholecystectomy for gallstones. Intraoperative findings include a small gastric ulcer and lymph node metastasis.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer, gallstones
**Intraoperative:** Gastric cancer, gallstones confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Gastric ulcer located on the posterior wall of the lesser curvature, approximately 0.8 cm in diameter, invasive to the submucosa with a small ulcerative focus. Lymph nodes in the 1st and 8th groups were enlarged. No obvious metastasis was found in the liver, pancreas, mesentery, or pelvis. No ascites. Multiple gallstones were found in the gallbladder.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Abdominal exploration via an upper midline incision.
2. Dissection of the gallbladder and omentum adhesions, careful dissection of the胆囊三角, ligation and transection of the cystic duct 0.5 cm from the common bile duct, transection and ligation of the gallbladder vessels, dissection of the gallbladder from the gallbladder bed to the胆囊三角, transection of the cystic duct 0.5 cm from the common bile duct, ligation and suture of the residual end, removal of the specimen.
3. Mobilization of the transverse colon, dissection of the omentum, transverse mesocolon, and pancreatic capsule; clamping, transecting, and ligating the gastrocolic ligament from the right to the liver flexure of the transverse colon and from the left to the spleen flexure of the transverse colon.
4. Clamping, transecting, and ligating 2-3 branches of the short gastric vessels along the greater curvature of the stomach, protecting the spleen.
5. Transection of the lesser omentum below the liver, dissection of the anterior leaf of the hepatoduodenal ligament and its lymphatic tissue.
6. Clamping, transecting, and doubly ligating the right gastric and right gastroepiploic vessels at the vascular root; transection of the duodenum 3 cm distal to the pylorus, the distal end placed into the anvil of a 28F tube-shaped anastomosis stapler and fixed with purse-string suture.
7. Mobilization of the stomach body, clamping, transecting, and doubly ligating the left gastric vessel at the vascular root; removal of the gastric coronary vessels and their lymphatic tissue until below the cardia; transection of the stomach body at the middle-third of the stomach body, removal of the specimen.
8. Incision of the anterior wall of the residual stomach, anastomosis of the distal end of the greater curvature with the duodenum, continuous suture closure after withdrawal of the long tube of the stapler, reinforcement with seromuscular layer, and additional reinforcement with seromuscular layer sutures.
9. Hemostasis, lavage of the abdominal cavity, aspiration of exudate, placement of a negative pressure ball under the liver, drainage from the right upper abdomen, and layer-by-layer closure.

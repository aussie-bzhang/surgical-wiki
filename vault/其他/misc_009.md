## Overview
Laparoscopic exploration and hemostasis for patient with liver cirrhosis and portal hypertension, post-splenectomy and shunt operation, with intra-abdominal hemorrhage.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Liver cirrhosis, portal hypertension, post-splenectomy and shunt operation, intra-abdominal hemorrhage.
**Intraoperative:** Liver cirrhosis, portal hypertension, post-splenectomy and shunt operation, intra-abdominal hemorrhage.
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a large amount of blood and blood clots in the abdominal cavity, approximately 1500ml. The tail of the pancreas showed oozing bleeding without pulsatile hemorrhage.

## Operative Steps
1. Place the patient in a supine position under general anesthesia, perform routine disinfection and draping; enter the abdomen through the original incision.
2. Rapidly aspirate the blood and blood clots in the abdominal cavity, explore as described above.
3. Suture the tail of the pancreas with 8-0 absorbable suture.
4. Irrigate the abdominal cavity, achieve hemostasis, and ensure no bleeding is present. Place two negative pressure tubes in the splenic fossa for drainage, puncture and extract through the incision, and close the abdomen in layers.
5. Operation completed.

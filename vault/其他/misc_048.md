## Overview
Duodenal bulb perforation repair in a patient with acute peritonitis and duodenal bulb ulcer perforation.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Acute peritonitis, gastrointestinal perforation
**Intraoperative:** Acute peritonitis, duodenal bulb ulcer perforation
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Duodenal bulb with a 2cm ulcer and a 0.3cm perforation. Local gastric wall is congested and edematous with a purulent coating. The surrounding gastric wall has normal peristalsis and a smooth surface without obvious mass. Minimal pus in the abdominal cavity. During surgery, a 2cm smooth, well-defined mass in the liver's 6th segment was found, recommended for follow-up. No other significant abnormalities were found in the stomach and small intestine.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Midline upper abdominal incision for abdominal access.
2. Dissect abdominal adhesions, aspirate abdominal fluid and food debris.
3. Suture the ulcer site with three interrupted full-thickness sutures, incorporating omentum tissue and ligating, then covering with omentum tissue and securing.
4. Thorough hemostasis, lavage the abdominal cavity with large amounts of normal saline, aspirate, and place a negative pressure drain at the perforation site and in the pelvis.
5. Count sponges and instruments, close the abdomen in layers, and complete the procedure.

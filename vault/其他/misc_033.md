## Overview
Colonic stump closure is a surgical procedure performed to close a colostomy, typically following a resection of the colon. The key technical feature involves meticulous dissection and closure of the colonic stump to prevent leakage and ensure proper healing.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Colostomy
**Intraoperative:** Colostomy confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a colonic stoma located in the original midupper abdominal midline incision. The tissue and bowel were adherent to the incision.

## Operative Steps
1. After general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping were performed.
2. A midline incision 3.0 cm above and below the stoma was made. The colonic stump and peritoneum below the incision were mobilized. The stoma was trimmed to normal tissue, and the margins were sutured with interrupted sutures and reinforced with a seromuscular layer.
3. Due to the tissue's toughness and edema, tension sutures were used to close the abdominal wall. The procedure was completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the colonic stump and the abdominal wall. The common bile duct (胆总管) should be protected during dissection.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included the toughness and edema of the tissue, which required careful dissection and tension sutures for closure.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of meticulous dissection to prevent injury to adjacent structures.
- The technique of tension suturing for abdominal wall closure in the presence of edema and tissue toughness.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative bleeding.
- Ensuring proper closure of the colonic stump to prevent leakage.

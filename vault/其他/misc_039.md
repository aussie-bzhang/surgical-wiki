## Overview
Splenectomy is a surgical procedure performed for various indications, including traumatic spleen rupture. It involves the removal of the spleen, a vital organ located in the left upper quadrant of the abdomen.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Traumatic abdominal injury, intra-abdominal hemorrhage
**Intraoperative:** Intra-abdominal hemorrhage, splenic rupture
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intra-abdominal non-clotting blood and blood clots totaling approximately 1500ml. The inferior pole of the spleen had a laceration measuring 2cm in length and 0.5cm in depth.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Midline upper abdominal incision for abdominal access.
2. Incise the gastrocolic ligament, locate the splenic artery above the pancreas, and ligate it.
3. Dissect and transect the splenicocolic ligament, the splenic renal ligament posteriorly, and the splenogastric ligament anteriorly. Ligate the short gastric vessels individually. Dissect and transect the splenoperitoneal ligament, fully mobilizing the splenic pedicle.
4. Dissect the splenic artery and vein, transect and ligate them in two layers, and remove the spleen.
5. Rinse the abdominal cavity with large amounts of normal saline, achieve hemostasis, and place a negative pressure drain in the splenic fossa and a PASMA tube. Place another negative pressure drain in the pelvic cavity.
6. Close the abdomen in layers, and the procedure is completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the spleen, splenic artery, and splenic vein. The danger zone is the inferior pole of the spleen. Structures requiring protection are the short gastric vessels and the splenic hilum (脾门).

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered include hemostasis at the splenic hilum and mobilization of the spleen.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of identifying and ligating the splenic artery and vein before splenic mobilization.
- The technique of splenic mobilization and the importance of hemostasis.
- The placement of drains in the splenic fossa and pelvic cavity.

## Complication Prevention
- Intraoperative hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper placement of drains to prevent postoperative abscess formation.

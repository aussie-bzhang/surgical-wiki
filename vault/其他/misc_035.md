## Overview
Splenectomy with gastroesophageal variceal ligation is a procedure performed for the treatment of liver cirrhosis, portal hypertension, and splenic hypertrophy. It involves the removal of the spleen and ligation of varices at the gastroesophageal junction.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Liver cirrhosis, portal hypertension, splenic hypertrophy
**Intraoperative:** Liver cirrhosis, portal hypertension, splenic hypertrophy
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Spleen measuring 20*15*10cm, marked varices at the gastroesophageal junction, liver showing mixed nodular cirrhosis.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Laparotomy via a left upper quadrant 'L' incision.
2. Incise the gastrocolic ligament, divide the splenogastric ligament superiorly to the left of the cardia, identify the splenic artery above the pancreas and ligate it.
3. Divide the splenocolic, splenorenal, and splenodiaphragmatic ligaments, and mobilize the splenic pedicle.
4. Separate the splenic artery and vein, divide and ligate them in two places, remove the spleen, ligate the vessels between the stomach and pancreas, and the left subdiaphragmatic vessels.
5. Divide and ligate the gastroepiploic vessels along the lesser curvature of the stomach, mobilize superiorly to the right of the cardia.
6. Mobilize the lower esophagus, divide the vagus nerve, and continue superiorly to the esophageal hiatus, divide or ligate the esophageal varices.
7. Rinse the abdominal cavity with normal saline, achieve hemostasis, place two negative pressure drains below the left diaphragm and behind the esophagus.
8. Count sponges and instruments, close the abdomen in layers, and complete the procedure.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Spleen, gastroesophageal varices, portal vein, splenic artery. (脾脏, 胃食管静脉曲张, 门静脉, 脾动脉)

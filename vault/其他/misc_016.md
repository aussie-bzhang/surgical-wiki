## Overview
Splenectomy with gastroesophageal vein ligation for liver cirrhosis, portal hypertension, and splenic hyperactivity. Key technical feature includes ligation of gastroesophageal veins and splenectomy.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Liver cirrhosis, portal hypertension, splenic hyperactivity
**Intraoperative:** Liver cirrhosis, portal hypertension, splenic hyperactivity
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Spleen measuring 20*15cm, marked varices at the lower esophagus and fundus, forming a variceal ball, variceal diameter up to 1.0cm, splenic vein tortuous, liver showing small nodular sclerosis, mild atrophy, ascites approximately 300ml.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Left upper quadrant 'L' shaped incision for abdominal access.
2. Incise the gastrocolic ligament, divide the splenogastric ligament superiorly to the left of the cardia, identify and ligate the splenic artery above the pancreas.
3. Divide the splenocolic, splenorenal, and splenodiaphragmatic ligaments, fully mobilize the splenic pedicle.
4. Separate the splenic artery and vein, ligate and tie them separately, remove the spleen, ligate and tie the gastric pancreas and left subdiaphragmatic vessels.
5. Divide and ligate the gastric coronary vessels along the lesser curvature of the stomach, mobilize superiorly to the right of the cardia, suture the seromuscular layer.
6. Mobilize the lower esophagus, divide the vagus nerve, and continue superiorly to the esophageal hiatus, divide or ligate the esophageal varices.
7. Rinse the abdominal cavity with normal saline, ensure hemostasis, place two negative pressure drains below the left diaphragm and behind the esophagus.
8. Count sponges and instruments, close the abdomen in layers, surgery completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the spleen, gastroesophageal veins, and surrounding vasculature. The splenic hilum and the gastroesophageal veins are particularly important. (脾脏，贲门血管，周围血管。脾蒂和贲门血管是特别重要的。)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties in mobilizing the splenic hilum and ligation of the gastroesophageal veins.

## Teaching Points
- Proper mobilization of the splenic hilum is crucial to minimize blood loss.
- Careful ligation of the gastroesophageal veins is essential to prevent variceal bleeding.

## Complication Prevention
- Thorough hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Placement of drains to manage postoperative ascites.

- [[Liver Transplantation]] - Indicated for end-stage liver disease.

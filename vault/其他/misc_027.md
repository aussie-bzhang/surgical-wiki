## Overview
Gastric cardia and esophagus malignancy resection, a complex procedure involving exploration, biopsy, and stoma creation due to tumor involvement of adjacent structures.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cardia and esophagus malignancy
**Intraoperative:** Confirmed malignancy with involvement of pancreatic neck, body, tail, and spleen, and abdominal aorta
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Gastric cardia, esophagus, and a large mass approximately 10*8 cm2 in size, hard consistency. Tumor fused with pancreatic neck, body, tail, and spleen, and invaded abdominal aorta. Tumor unresectable.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Midline upper abdominal incision.
2. Dissection of the gastrocolic ligament from the attachment of the transverse colon, further exploration.
3. Biopsy of the tumor at the lesser curvature of the stomach body, hemostasis.
4. At 15 cm below the Treitz ligament, pull out a segment of jejunum, insert a 12F urinary catheter for jejunal stoma, suture the serosal layer of the bowel wall around the stoma tube for about 5 cm, and suture and fix it to the left lateral abdominal wall. The stoma tube is brought out from the upper left abdominal wall.
5. Hemostasis, lavage of the peritoneal cavity, aspiration of effluent, placement of a drain under the liver, brought out from the upper right abdominal wall and connected to a negative pressure ball. Layer-by-layer closure.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: gastric cardia, esophagus, pancreatic neck, abdominal aorta. (胆总管)
> Danger zones: areas around the abdominal aorta and pancreatic neck.
> Structures requiring protection: spleen, splenic vessels.

## Technical Challenges
Tumor involvement of adjacent structures, making resection difficult.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of careful exploration and biopsy in malignancy.
- The technique of jejunal stoma creation in unresectable cases.
- The management of complex anatomical situations.

## Complication Prevention
- Thorough hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper placement of drains to prevent postoperative infection.

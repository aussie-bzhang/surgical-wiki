## Overview
Laparotomy for abdominal penetrating injury, exploring for internal injuries and fracture of the left iliac bone.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Abdominal penetrating injury
**Intraoperative:** Abdominal penetrating injury, left iliac bone fracture
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a penetrating wound in the left iliac abdomen, an abdominal wound of approximately 2 cm, and a back wound of about 1 cm. No obvious active bleeding was observed. The penetrating wound passed through the extraperitoneal side of the lateral peritoneum, with partial muscle laceration. The peritoneum was intact. The left iliac bone was fractured. The colon, rectum, small intestine, liver, spleen, and stomach were explored, and no significant abnormalities were found. Orthopedic consultation was requested intraoperatively, considering initial debridement and subsequent emergency fixation based on CT three-dimensional reconstruction for fracture confirmation.

## Operative Steps
1. Positioning the patient in supine position, under general anesthesia, and performing routine disinfection and draping.
2. Incision along the midline of the abdomen and layer-by-layer entering the abdominal cavity, exploration as described.
3. Orthopedic consultation requested, details in consultation records.
4. Thorough exploration revealed no active bleeding or abdominal perforation.
5. Hemostasis was achieved, and the abdomen was closed in layers. Debridement and suture of the left lower abdominal and back wounds were performed.
6. Completion of the procedure.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: left iliac bone, abdominal wall, peritoneum. (髂骨, 腹壁, 腹膜)

## Technical Challenges
- Management of the penetrating wound and fracture of the left iliac bone.
- Hemostasis and closure of the abdominal wall and wounds.

## Teaching Points

- Management of associated fractures and hemostasis.

## Complication Prevention
- Prevention of infection and wound healing complications.

## Overview
Laparoscopic exploration for liver cirrhosis, biliary surgery, post-splenectomy, and biliary calculi. Key technical feature:粘连分离与止血。

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Liver cirrhosis, biliary surgery, post-splenectomy, biliary calculi.
**Intraoperative:** Liver cirrhosis, biliary surgery, post-splenectomy, biliary calculi.
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings: dense adhesions under the original surgical incision, extensive varices, prone to bleeding. Dense adhesions between transverse colon and abdominal wall, separation of adhesions resulted in rupture of transverse colon, dense adhesions between the liver and gastrointestinal tract, prone to bleeding during separation, unable to further dissect, surgery terminated. Intraoperative blood loss of 500ml, transfusion of RBC 600ml, plasma 600ml.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Incision at the original right upper quadrant midline.
2. Careful separation of adhesions, difficult separation, separation of transverse colon during the process, suture repair performed; extensive bleeding at the liver and gastrointestinal tract, unable to ligate or suture for hemostasis, unable to further dissect the porta hepatis, hemostatic powder, hemostatic gauze, and gelatin sponge packing and compression for hemostasis.
3. Due to dense adhesions and excessive bleeding, unable to further surgery, communicated with family and terminated surgery; thorough hemostasis, negative pressure tube placed under the incision for drainage, punctured out, closed in layers.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: liver, gastrointestinal tract, spleen, common bile duct. (胆总管)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered: dense adhesions, extensive bleeding, unable to further dissect.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of careful adhesion separation and hemostasis.
- Management of extensive bleeding in the presence of dense adhesions.

## Complication Prevention
- Not documented

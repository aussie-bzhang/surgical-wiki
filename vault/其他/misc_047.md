## Overview
Sacral cystectomy for a sacral tail mass, a common clinical scenario with key technical steps involving the sacrum and coccyx.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Sacral tail mass
**Intraoperative:** Sacral tail mass
**Pathological:** Sacral tail mass excision

## Intraoperative Findings
Sacral tail mass located, approximately 2*3*3cm3 in size, adherent to the sacral coccygeal membrane. No necrotic tissue seen within the mass.

## Operative Steps
1. After successful lumbar anesthesia, the patient is placed in prone position, and the surgical site is prepared with routine antisepsis and draping.
2. A梭形纵切口 is made to excise the mass from the surface of the sacrum.
3. Hemostasis is achieved, and the incision is closed without dead space.
4. Procedure completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the sacrum and coccyx. The common bile duct (胆总管) is not involved in this procedure.

## Technical Challenges
Not documented

## Teaching Points
- The importance of identifying and preserving the sacral coccygeal membrane during resection.
- Proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative bleeding.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hematoma.

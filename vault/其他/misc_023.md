## Overview
Splenectomy with perigastric vascular ligation for liver cirrhosis, portal hypertension, upper gastrointestinal bleeding, and splenic hyperactivity. Key technical feature includes selective ligation of the splenic artery and portal vein.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Liver cirrhosis, portal hypertension, upper gastrointestinal bleeding, splenic hyperactivity
**Intraoperative:** Liver cirrhosis, portal hypertension, upper gastrointestinal bleeding, splenic hyperactivity
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Spleen 20*15*10cm, gastric fundus and esophagus distal end varices with the largest diameter of 0.6cm, twisted into a mass. Splenic artery diameter 1cm, splenic vein diameter 1.5cm. Liver shows mixed nodular cirrhosis, pale yellow ascites about 1000ml. Intraoperatively, a 15cm segment of small intestine in the middle segment was dark in color, with edematous bowel wall and a deep black thrombus in the corresponding mesentery. After warm saline irrigation, the color turned red, peristalsis returned to normal, and pulsation of the vessels in the mesentery was palpable.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Left upper quadrant 'L' shaped incision for abdominal access.
2. Drainage of ascites, opening of gastrocolic ligament, ligation of splenogastric ligament up to the left side of the cardia, finding the splenic artery above the pancreas and ligating with 7号线.
3. Dissection of splenocolic, splenorenal, and splenoperitoneal ligaments, complete mobilization of the splenic pedicle.
4. Separation and ligation of splenic artery and vein, removal of the spleen, ligation of gastric pancreas and left subphrenic vessels.
5. Ligation of gastric coronary vessels along the lesser curvature, mobilization up to the right side of the cardia.
6. Mobilization of the lower esophagus, transection of the vagus nerve, up to the esophageal hiatus, transection or ligation of esophageal varices; serosalization of the greater and lesser curvature of the stomach.
7. Abdominal lavage with large amounts of saline, hemostasis, placement of two negative pressure drains in the left subphrenic space and behind the esophagus.
8. Counting sponges and instruments, closure of the abdomen in layers.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: spleen, gastric fundus, esophagus, portal vein, splenic artery. (胆总管)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in mobilizing the spleen and controlling bleeding during dissection.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of selective ligation of the splenic artery and portal vein
- Proper handling of esophageal varices to prevent postoperative bleeding
- Technique of serosalization to prevent adhesions

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis during dissection
- Adequate lavage of the abdominal cavity to reduce the risk of infection

## Related Procedures
- [[Gastrojejunostomy]] - for alternative drainage in case of difficulty with the primary procedure

## Overview
Posterior peritoneal tumor resection; diagnosis of retroperitoneal tumor; key technical feature: complete resection of tumor with preservation of adjacent structures.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Retroperitoneal tumor
**Intraoperative:** Retroperitoneal tumor
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located in the posterior peritoneum, 15cm in diameter, adherent to the inferior mesenteric vein, closely related to the left renal perinephric fat.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline abdominal incision around the umbilicus, layer-by-layer abdominal entry, findings as described above.
3. Displace the small intestine, open the lateral peritoneum, expose the tumor, and completely resect the tumor (intact capsule), left renal perinephric fat, and part of the inferior mesenteric vein, paying attention to protect the descending colon, mesenteric vessels, and ureter.
4. Perfect hemostasis, count instruments and gauze, cover the wound with hemostatic powder and hemostatic gauze, close the posterior peritoneum, close the incision in layers, end of operation.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: posterior peritoneum, inferior mesenteric vein, left renal perinephric fat, descending colon, mesenteric vessels, ureter. (胆总管)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in tumor resection and protection of adjacent structures.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of complete tumor resection while preserving adjacent structures.
- Technique of hemostasis and wound closure.

## Complication Prevention
- Prevention of postoperative complications through meticulous hemostasis and wound closure.

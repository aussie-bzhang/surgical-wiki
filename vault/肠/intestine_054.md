## Overview
Left hemicolectomy for colon cancer, involving resection of the left colon and spleen flexure due to obstruction caused by a 5cm colon cancer.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Intestinal obstruction, colon脾曲癌
**Intraoperative:** Confirmed intestinal obstruction, colon脾曲癌
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located at the colon spleen flexure, diameter about 5cm, hard, involving the serosa, tumor infiltration causing complete obstruction of the intestinal lumen, marked distension of the proximal colon, edema of the intestinal wall, multiple longitudinal ruptures of the serosa of the ascending colon, longest about 2.5cm, mucosal bulging. No obvious abnormalities found in the liver, omentum, stomach, small intestine, other colon, and pelvic cavity.

## Operative Steps
1. After successful induction of general anesthesia, the patient is placed in a supine position, and the abdomen is disinfected and draped.
2. A midupper midline incision is made, the abdomen is entered in layers, hemostasis is performed, and the skin is protected.
3. The gastrocolic ligament is separated, the anterior leaf of the mesocolon is excised, the left part of the greater omentum is excised, the transverse mesocolon is mobilized, the lateral peritoneum of the left colon is opened, the splenocolic ligament is transected, and the colon spleen flexure is mobilized.
4. The mesocolon is separated and transected one by one, the left branch of the middle colic vessels is transected and ligated, the distal part is separated to 7-8cm below the tumor, and the proximal part is mobilized to 10cm above the tumor.
5. The proximal and distal ends of the colon are transected, the specimen is removed, and the proximal and distal ends of the colon are anastomosed end-to-side using a 31# tubular anastomosis stapler. The anastomosis is reinforced with 3-0 absorbable suture in an interrupted full-thickness manner, followed by seromuscular layer suture.
6. The mesenteric defect is closed, and the serosal rupture of the ascending colon is sutured in an interrupted manner.
7. Irrigation, hemostasis is completed, instruments and sponges are counted and confirmed to be correct, and two negative pressure drainage tubes are placed in the left colonic sulcus through puncture incisions.
8. The incision is closed layer by layer, and the operation is completed.

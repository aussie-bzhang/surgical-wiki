## Overview
Rectal tumor resection using the DIXON technique in a patient with rectal adenocarcinoma.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Rectal tumor
**Intraoperative:** Rectal tumor
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located at the middle to lower segment of the rectum near the peritoneal reflection, diameter 4.0cm, soft, pedunculated, broad-based, not penetrating the serosa, no intestinal obstruction.

## Operative Steps
1. Position: lithotomy, general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline incision in the lower abdomen, laparotomy, findings as described above.
3. Push the small intestine upwards, expose the rectum and pelvic peritoneum, open the lateral peritoneum, mobilize the rectal mesentery, transect the rectal vessels at the root, transect the rectal mesentery, mobilize the distal end to the level below the peritoneal reflection, and the proximal end to the ileocecal junction, paying attention to the course of the ureter and iliac vessels.
4. Transect the proximal and distal ends of the colon, remove the specimen, dilate the anal canal, and place a 28F tube-shaped anastomosis stapler through the anus for colonic end-to-end anastomosis.
5. Close the mesenteric defect, place two negative pressure drains in the pelvic floor, and puncture the incision.
6. Irrigation, hemostasis, count instruments and sponges, close the incision in order, and the operation is completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Rectum, ileocecal junction, sigmoid colon. (直肠，回盲部，乙状结肠)

- Attention to the course of the ureter and iliac vessels during mobilization
- Technique of colonic end-to-end anastomosis using a stapler

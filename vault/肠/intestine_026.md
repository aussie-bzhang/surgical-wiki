## Overview
Right hemicolectomy for right colon cancer, involving resection of the ascending colon and ileocecal junction. Key technical feature is the anastomosis of the ileum to the remaining colon.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right colon cancer
**Intraoperative:** Right colon cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Ascending colon mass, approximately 8cm in diameter, circumferential, with slight distension of the proximal small intestine. No abnormalities found in liver, spleen, or pelvis. No urinary tract injury.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline incision, enter peritoneum, findings as described.
3. Dissection of gastrocolic ligament, transection of the right omentum, mobilization of the transverse mesocolon, transection of the hepatocolic ligament, separation of colon from duodenum, mobilization of the hepatic flexure of the colon.
4. Incision of the right paracolic fascia, mobilization of the ascending colon mesocolon, attention to the course of the ureter, mobilization of the ileocecal junction and terminal ileum.
5. Transection of the right colic artery, ileocolic artery, and right colic vein, ligation at the root, transection of the mesocolon, ileum transected 20cm from the ileocecal junction, transverse colon transected at the middle, specimen removed.
6. Placement of the anvil of the stapler in the terminal ileum, opening of the colon on the transverse mesocolon, 28.5mm circular stapler for ileocolic end-to-end anastomosis, closing the colon mesentery defect.
7. Irrigation, hemostasis, count instruments and sponges, placement of two negative pressure drainage balls in the right paracolic gutter, closing the incision, operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: ascending colon, ileocecal junction, sigmoid colon. (升结肠，回盲部，乙状结肠)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in mobilization of the ascending colon and ileocecal junction, careful hemostasis, and precise anastomosis.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of careful mobilization around the ileocecal junction to avoid injury to the ileocolic vessels.
- Proper hemostasis techniques to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- The technique of using a circular stapler for ileocolic anastomosis.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful mobilization to avoid injury to adjacent structures.
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper irrigation and drainage to prevent infection.

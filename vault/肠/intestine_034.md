## Overview
Rectal cancer resection with Hartmann procedure for a patient with complex intestinal adhesions and acute intestinal obstruction.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Acute intestinal obstruction, post-gastric cancer surgery.
**Intraoperative:** Sigmoid colon cancer.
**Pathological:** Not documented.

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings: Mass located in the sigmoid colon, diameter 4cm, annular narrowing causing complete obstruction of the intestinal lumen. Distension of proximal colon and part of the small intestine with fluid and gas. No liver-pelvic adhesions. Dense adhesions in the upper abdomen preventing exploration.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline lower abdominal incision, enter peritoneum as described.
3. Bluntly dissect abdominal adhesions, push the small intestine upwards, expose the rectum and pelvic peritoneum, open the lateral peritoneum, mobilize the sigmoid-rectal mesentery, transect the mesocolon, mobilize the proximal colon to the transection of the sigmoid colon, mobilize the distal colon to 10cm below the mass, transect the distal rectum, suture the distal rectum, pay attention to the course of the ureter and iliac vessels.
4. Reconstruct the pelvic peritoneum, make a circular incision in the left lower abdomen, incise to the peritoneum, pull out the proximal sigmoid colon for colostomy.
5. Close the mesenteric defect, place a negative pressure drain in the pelvic floor, puncture and lead out.
6. Irrigation, hemostasis, count instruments and sponges, close the incision, operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: rectum, sigmoid colon. Danger zones: ureter and iliac vessels. Structures requiring protection: ureter and iliac vessels (输尿管及髂血管).

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered: Dense adhesions in the upper abdomen, careful mobilization to avoid injury to adjacent structures.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of careful adhesion dissection to avoid injury to adjacent structures.
- Technique of sigmoid colon mobilization and rectal transection.
- Management of potential complications such as ureteral injury.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful dissection to avoid injury to adjacent structures.
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.

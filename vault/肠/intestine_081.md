## Overview
Rectal tumor resection using the DIXON technique, left ovarian cyst excision, and preventive colostomy for a patient with rectal cancer and ovarian cyst.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Rectal tumor
**Intraoperative:** Rectal cancer, ovarian cyst
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located at 8cm and 20cm from the anal margin (double lesions), 5.0cm in diameter, hard, infiltrative ulcerative type, extending to the serosa, infiltrating the posterior wall of the uterus, posterior vaginal wall, and pelvic floor with dense adhesions. Two ovarian cysts on the left, approximately 3.0cm in diameter. The surgery was extremely difficult.

## Operative Steps
1. Position: lithotomy, general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Mid-lower midline incision around the umbilicus, layer by layer entering the abdomen, findings as described above.
3. Push the small intestine upwards, expose the rectum and pelvic peritoneum, open the lateral peritoneum, carefully separate the mass from surrounding adhesions, mobilize the rectal mesentery, transect the rectal vessels at the root, transect the rectal mesentery, distally separate to 3cm below the mass, proximally mobilize to the sigmoid colon, 5cm above the mass, paying attention to the course of the ureter and iliac vessels.
4. Strip the left ovarian membrane cyst, suture the stripping surface.
5. Transect the proximal and distal colons, remove the specimen, dilate the anal canal and clean thoroughly, place a 28.5mm tube-shaped anastomosis stapler through the anus for colonic end-to-end anastomosis.
6. Irrigation, hemostasis, close the mesenteric orifice, place two negative pressure drains in the pelvic floor, puncture and lead out.
7. Pull out the middle segment of the transverse colon through the abdominal wall, perform preventive colostomy, suspend with a pasma tube.
8. Hemostasis, count instruments and gauze, close the incision.
9. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: rectum, ovary, colon, uterus, vagina, pelvic floor. (胆总管)
> Danger zones: ureter, iliac vessels.
> Structures requiring protection: ureter, iliac vessels.

## Technical Challenges
Extremely difficult due to dense adhesions and infiltrative growth of the tumor.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of careful dissection and mobilization to avoid injury to critical structures.
- The technique of using a stapler for colonic anastomosis.
- The management of multiple lesions and extensive adhesions.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper placement of drains to prevent postoperative infection and abscess formation.

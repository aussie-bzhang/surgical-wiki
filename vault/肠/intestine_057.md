## Overview
Duodenal bulb perforation repair in a patient with clinical presentation of gastrointestinal perforation, involving the duodenal bulb with a central perforation and surrounding contamination.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastrointestinal perforation
**Intraoperative:** Duodenal bulb perforation
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a 1.0 cm ulcerous lesion on the anterior wall of the duodenal bulb with a central 0.5 cm perforation. Surrounding tissue was contaminated with pus and slough. Approximately 100 ml of purulent exudate was present in the peritoneal cavity.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia administered, routine disinfection and draping performed.
2. Midline upper abdominal incision made, findings as described.
3. Perforation sutured, covered with omentum tissue.
4. Abdominal cavity lavaged with large volumes of normal saline until clean, exudate aspirated.
5. Negative pressure tubes placed under the liver and in the pelvis, punctured wounds brought out.
6. Closed in layers.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: Duodenal bulb (十二指肠球), Perforation site (穿孔部位). Structures requiring protection: Adjacent bowel, blood vessels.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included managing the contaminated tissue and ensuring adequate closure of the perforation without causing obstruction.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of thorough lavage of the abdominal cavity to prevent infection.
- Proper technique for suturing the perforation to avoid leakage and obstruction.

## Complication Prevention
- Adequate lavage and drainage to prevent infection.
- Careful suturing to avoid bowel obstruction.

## Related Procedures
- [[Gastrojejunostomy]] - Used if there is significant bowel damage.

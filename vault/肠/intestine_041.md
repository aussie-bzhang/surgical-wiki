## Overview
Sigmoid colon cancer resection is a procedure performed for the treatment of sigmoid colon cancer. It involves the removal of the affected sigmoid colon and surrounding structures, followed by anastomosis of the remaining colon. The clinical context includes the diagnosis of sigmoid colon cancer and the need for curative resection.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Sigmoid colon cancer
**Intraoperative:** Sigmoid colon cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
A 4*4cm tumor was found at approximately 30cm from the anal margin on the sigmoid colon, involving the entire circumference of the bowel wall in an infiltrative ulcerative pattern. The tumor had infiltrated the serosa. Lymph nodes in the mesentery root were enlarged and were excised for pathological examination. No metastatic lesions were found in the abdominal cavity or bowel lumen. No ascites was present.

## Operative Steps
1. Position the patient in the lithotomy position under general anesthesia and perform routine disinfection and draping.
2. Make a midline lower abdominal incision, enter the abdomen, and proceed as described above.
3. Push the small intestine upwards to expose the sigmoid colon and peritoneum of the pelvis.
4. Open the lateral peritoneum, mobilize the mesosigmoid, and transect the ileocolic vessels at their root. Divide the mesosigmoid, separating the distal end to 7cm below the tumor at the level of the peritoneal reflexion and the proximal end to 7cm above the tumor. Pay attention to the course of the ureter and iliac vessels.
5. Transect the proximal and distal ends of the colon, remove the specimen, and perform an end-to-end anastomosis with a 28.5# anastomosis device, reinforcing with seromuscular suture. Close the mesenteric defect.
6. Place two negative-pressure drains in the pelvis and exit through the stab wounds.
7. Irrigate, ensure hemostasis, count instruments and sponges, and close the incision in layers. The procedure is completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> The common bile duct (胆总管), ileocolic vessels, ureter, and iliac vessels are critical structures that require protection during the procedure.

## Overview
Rectal perforation repair and adhesion release in a patient with acute peritonitis and gastrointestinal perforation at the rectosigmoid junction.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Acute peritonitis, gastrointestinal perforation
**Intraoperative:** Rectosigmoid junction perforation
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed multiple abdominal adhesions. A longitudinal perforation was observed in the anterior wall of the rectum at the rectosigmoid junction, approximately 5 cm in length. Multiple serosal ruptures were found in the sigmoid and descending colon. Approximately 300 ml of cloudy effusion was present in the abdominal cavity. No abnormalities were found in the exploration of the remaining intestinal segments.

## Operative Steps
1. Place the patient in a supine position under general anesthesia, perform routine disinfection and draping.
2. Make a midline incision, enter the abdominal cavity layer by layer, and aspirate the effusion in the abdominal cavity.
3. Release adhesions of the colon and small intestine, and explore the abdominal cavity as described above.
4. Suture the rectal perforation with antibacterial micro乔sutures in a running full-thickness manner, adding an intraperitoneal inversion suture of the serosa; and suture the multiple serosal ruptures in the colon with running sutures.
5. Irrigate the abdominal cavity with 5000 ml of normal saline and 300 ml of metronidazole.
6. Perform hemostasis, place two negative-pressure drains in the Douglas' pouch, and close the abdomen.
7. Operation completed.

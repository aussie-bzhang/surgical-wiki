## Overview
Low anterior resection (LAR) for rectal cancer, a common colorectal procedure, involves resecting the rectum and sigmoid colon with lymph node dissection. Key technical feature is the anastomosis of the remaining rectum to the descending colon.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Rectal cancer
**Intraoperative:** Rectal cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings: A 5.0 cm mass 6 cm from the anal margin, presenting as an ulcerative infiltrative type, encircling the lumen for 1/3 of its circumference. No liver, gallbladder, small intestine, remaining colon, or pelvic organs involved.

## Operative Steps
1. Position the patient in supine position under general anesthesia, perform routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline incision below the navel, enter peritoneum layer by layer, intraoperative findings as described.
3. Push the small intestine upwards, expose the rectum and pelvic peritoneum, open the sigmoid colon lateral peritoneum and peritoneal reflex, free the sigmoid colon mesentery, and cut the sigmoid colon vessels at the root. Continue to the preperitoneal space, cut the sigmoid colon, and dissect the lymphatic fatty tissue, protecting the left and right ureters.
4. Open the rectouterine peritoneum, free along the rectovaginal space, cut the rectal side ligament, free the posterior wall of the rectum, and separate 3.0 cm below the mass.
5. Cut the rectum 3 cm below the mass, remove the specimen, dilate the anal canal, and place a 31F tube anastomosis stapler for end-to-end anastomosis.
6. Close the mesenteric defect, place two negative pressure drains in the pelvic peritoneal extraperitoneal space, and puncture the wound for drainage.
7. Irrigate, ensure hemostasis, count instruments and sponges, close the incision in layers, and the procedure is completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: rectum, sigmoid colon, ileum, ileocecal valve, sigmoid colon vessels, sigmoid colon mesentery, rectal vessels, rectal side ligament, uterus, vagina, ureters. (胆总管)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered: protecting the ureters, ensuring proper anastomosis, and managing blood loss.

## Teaching Points
- Protecting the ureters during dissection.
- Ensuring proper anastomosis technique.
- Managing blood loss effectively.

## Complication Prevention
- Preventing anastomotic leakage.
- Managing postoperative ileus.

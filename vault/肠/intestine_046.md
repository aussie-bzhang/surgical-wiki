## Overview
Right hemicolectomy for right colon cancer, with emphasis on the resection of the ascending colon and ileocecal junction.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right colon cancer
**Intraoperative:** Right colon cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
A tumor was found in the middle segment of the ascending colon, approximately 5cm in diameter, with ulcerative infiltration, involving the serosa, encircling the bowel for 3/4 of its circumference, and causing incomplete bowel obstruction. Liver (-), ascites (-).

## Operative Steps
1. Place the patient in a supine position under general anesthesia and perform routine disinfection and draping.
2. Make a midline incision, enter the abdomen layer by layer, and observe the findings as described.
3. Dissect the gastrocolic ligament, divide the right omentum, mobilize the transverse mesocolon, divide the hepatocolic ligament, and separate the colon from the duodenum, mobilizing the hepatic flexure of the colon.
4. Open the right paracolic fascia, mobilize the mesocolon of the ascending colon, and pay attention to the course of the ureter. Mobilize the ileocecal junction and the distal ileum.
5. Divide the right branch of the middle colonic artery, ileocolic artery, and right colic artery, ligate them at the root, divide the mesocolon, and transect the ileum 20cm from the ileocecal junction and the sigmoid colon at the middle of the transverse colon to remove the specimen.
6. Place the anvil of the stapler in the distal ileum, make an incision on the transverse colon mesocolon, use a 28mm circular stapler for ileocolic end-to-end anastomosis, and close the residual ends with a 55mm clamp. Close the mesenteric defect.
7. Irrigate, ensure hemostasis, count instruments and sponges, place two negative pressure balls in the right paracolic gutter of the pelvis, place them one above the other, close the incision one by one, and complete the operation.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: ascending colon, ileocecal junction, ileum, sigmoid colon. (升结肠，回盲部，回肠，乙状结肠)

## Teaching Points
- Proper mobilization of the ascending colon and ileocecal junction is crucial for complete resection.
- Attention to the course of the ureter during mobilization is essential to avoid injury.
- Accurate anastomosis technique is vital for preventing anastomotic leakage.

## Complication Prevention
- Ensure complete resection of the tumor and adjacent structures.
- Perform meticulous hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Use a circular stapler for anastomosis to reduce the risk of leakage.

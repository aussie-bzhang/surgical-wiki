## Overview
Rectal cancer; pre-operative diagnosis; low anterior resection (Dixon) technique.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Rectal cancer.
**Intraoperative:** Rectal cancer.
**Pathological:** Not documented.

## Intraoperative Findings
Rectal mass located below the peritoneal reflex, 8 cm from the anal margin, ulcerative infiltrative type, occupying half of the intestinal lumen. No obvious metastasis observed in the stomach, liver, omentum, small intestine, and other colon.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline lower abdominal incision, enter peritoneum layer by layer, findings as described above.
3. Push the small intestine upwards, expose the rectum and pelvic peritoneum, open the sigmoid colon lateral peritoneum, free the mesentery, cut off the inferior mesenteric vessels at the root, dissect the lymph nodes at the root, open the peritoneal reflex, dissect along the pre-sacral plane, cut off the lateral rectal ligaments, free the anterior wall over the seminal vesicle and prostate, distal dissection to 2-3 cm below the mass, proximal dissection to 10 cm above the mass, protect the ureter and iliac vessels.
4. Cut off the sigmoid colon, place the proximal sigmoid colon into a 29* tube anastomosis stapler, ready for use.
5. The distal rectum 3 cm from the tumor is blocked with a large-angle clamp, after dilating the anal canal, flush the anal canal and distal rectum with 1000 ml of normal saline, block the distal rectum with a large-angle clamp, cut off, remove the specimen, place the tube anastomosis stapler through the anus for rectosigmoid anastomosis, reinforce the serosa of the anastomosis for one week.
6. Close the mesenteric opening, place two negative pressure drains through the peritoneal extraperitoneal at the pelvic floor, puncture and lead out.
7. Flush, perfect hemostasis, count instruments and gauze, close the incision layer by layer, operation closed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Rectum, sigmoid colon, ileum, ureter, iliac vessels. (直肠，乙状结肠，回肠，输尿管，髂血管)

## Technical Challenges
Not documented.

## Teaching Points
- Proper dissection and mobilization of the rectum and sigmoid colon.
- Protection of the ureter and iliac vessels during dissection.
- Proper use of staplers for anastomosis.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Adequate drainage to prevent pelvic abscess.

## Overview
Right hemicolectomy for ileocecal cancer; clinical context: patient with right-sided colon cancer; key technical feature: ileocolic anastomosis.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right-sided colon cancer
**Intraoperative:** Ileocecal cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings: tumor located at the ileocecal junction, approximately 1.5 cm in diameter, ulcerative type; exploration of liver, stomach, and pelvis negative.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline incision, layer-by-layer abdominal entry, findings as described above.
3. Bluntly separate adhesions in the upper right abdomen, separate gastrocolic ligament, transect the right omentum, mobilize the transverse mesocolon, transect the hepatocolic ligament, separate the colon from the duodenum, mobilize the hepatic flexure of the colon.
4. Open the right parietal peritoneum, mobilize the ascending colon mesocolon, pay attention to the course of the ureter, mobilize the ileocecal junction and distal ileum.
5. Mobilize the ileocolic vessels and right colic vessels, and transect and ligate them, transect the mesocolon, 20 cm from the ileocecal junction, transect the ileum, place the anvil of the anastomosis stapler in the distal ileum, make an opening 10 cm above the tumor margin, use a 28.5 mm tubular stapler for ileocolic end-to-side anastomosis, use a 60 mm clamp to transect the colon and remove the specimen, reinforce the anastomosis and residual colon with gastrointestinal suture for one week, close the mesenteric defect.
6. Irrigation, hemostasis, count instruments and gauze, close the incision layer by layer, operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: ileocecal junction, ascending colon, ileum; danger zones: vessels and ureter; structures requiring protection: mesenteric defect.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered: mobilization of the ileocecal junction and distal ileum, anastomosis technique.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of identifying and protecting the ileocolic vessels and ureter.
- Technique of ileocolic anastomosis with stapler.
- Proper handling of mesenteric defects.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper hemostasis and anastomotic technique to prevent anastomotic leakage.
- Careful mobilization to prevent injury to the ureter.

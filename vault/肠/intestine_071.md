## Overview
Rectal cancer resection using the DIXON technique in a patient with locally advanced rectal cancer.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Rectal cancer
**Intraoperative:** Rectal cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located at the upper rectum, approximately 12 cm from the anal margin, infiltrative ulcerative type, encircling the bowel for 1/2 circumference, involving the serosa layer. Lymph nodes in the mesorectum are palpable. No metastasis to liver, gallbladder, biliary tract, stomach, small intestine, or remaining colon, or in the pelvis.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, successful general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline incision below the navel, enter peritoneum layer by layer, findings as described above.
3. Push the small intestine upwards, expose the rectum and pelvic peritoneum, open the lateral peritoneum of the sigmoid colon, free the mesentery, and transect the inferior mesenteric vessels at the root, and dissect the mesenteric lymph nodes.
4. Open the peritoneal reflex, dissect along the pre-sacral plane, transect the lateral rectal ligaments on both sides and ligate them, free the anterior wall while avoiding injury to the uterus and vagina, distal dissection to 3 cm below the tumor, proximal dissection to 10 cm above the tumor, expose and protect the ureters and iliac vessels.
5. Transect the sigmoid colon, place the proximal sigmoid colon into a 29* anastomotic stapler, ready for anastomosis.
6. Block the distal rectum 3 cm below the tumor with a large-angle clamp, dilate the anal canal, flush the anal canal and distal rectum with 1000 ml of normal saline, block the upper end of the large-angle clamp with a purse-string clamp, transect the bowel wall, remove the specimen, and place a tube-shaped stapler through the anus for coloanal anastomosis, reinforce the anastomosis with interrupted serosal layer for one week.
7. Suture the mesenteric defect, place one negative pressure drain at the anterior and posterior aspects of the anastomosis in the pelvis, and puncture the incision for drainage.
8. Flush until clean, perfect hemostasis, count instruments and gauze, close the incision in layers, and the operation is completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: rectum, ileocecal junction, sigmoid colon, mesorectum, ureters, iliac vessels. (直肠，回盲瓣，乙状结肠，直肠系膜，输尿管，髂血管)

## Teaching Points
- Proper dissection of the mesorectum to avoid injury to adjacent structures.
- Accurate identification and protection of the ureters and iliac vessels during dissection.
- Proper handling of the anastomosis to ensure a secure and leak-proof connection.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Adequate drainage to prevent pelvic abscess.

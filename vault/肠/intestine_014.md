## Overview
Left hemicolectomy for colon cancer with adhesion release; diagnosis of colon脾曲 cancer; significant adhesion in upper abdomen.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Colon cancer
**Intraoperative:** Colon脾曲 cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located at the splenic flexure of the colon, diameter about 4cm, infiltrative ulcerative type, hard, involving the serosa layer. The tumor encircles the bowel for 3/4 of its circumference, causing incomplete bowel obstruction. Severe adhesions in the upper abdomen, especially in the right upper quadrant. No significant abnormalities found in the remaining colon and pelvis.

## Operative Steps
1. After general anesthesia, the patient is placed in a supine position, and the abdomen is entered through a midline upper abdominal incision.
2. The gastrocolic ligament is separated, the anterior leaf of the mesocolon is excised, the left part of the omentum is excised, the transverse mesocolon is mobilized, the lateral peritoneum of the left colon is opened, the splenocolic ligament is transected, and the splenic flexure of the colon is mobilized.
3. The mesocolon is separated and transected one by one, the left branch of the middle colic artery is transected and ligated, the distal part is separated to 7-8cm below the tumor, and the proximal part is mobilized to 10cm above the tumor.
4. The proximal and distal ends of the colon are transected, the specimen is removed, and the posterior wall is sutured with 3-0 absorbable suture in a running manner, the anterior wall is sutured in a running inversion manner, and the serosal layer is sutured to reinforce.
5. Irrigation, hemostasis, and confirmation of no instruments or sponges left, two negative pressure drainage tubes are placed in the left colonic sulcus and the incision at the anastomosis.
6. Counting sponges and instruments, close the abdomen in layers, and the operation is completed.

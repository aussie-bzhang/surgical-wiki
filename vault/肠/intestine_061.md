## Overview
Rectal resection (DIXON) for rectal cancer, involving resection of the rectum and ileocecal junction with anastomosis, with emphasis on the DIXON technique.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Rectal cancer
**Intraoperative:** Rectal cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Rectal mass 10cm from anal margin, approximately 3.5cm in diameter, infiltrative ulcerative type, encircling the lumen for 4/5 of the circumference, penetrating the serosa. No significant abnormalities found in the remaining colon, liver, gallbladder, small intestine, and peritoneal cavity.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline lower abdominal incision, enter peritoneum layer by layer, findings as described above.
3. Push the small intestine upwards, expose the rectum and pelvic peritoneum, open the lateral peritoneum, transect the rectal vessels at the root, transect the rectum at the ileocecal junction, transect the rectal mesentery, separate the distal end to the peritoneal reflection below, along the pre-sacral space down to the pelvic floor, transect the lateral ligaments, pay attention to the course of the ureter and iliac vessels, and separate forward, free the rectum.
4. Transect the distal rectum 3.0cm below the mass, remove the specimen, dilate the anal canal, and place a 28.5mm circular stapler through the anus for anastomosis of the colonic and rectal ends.
5. Close the mesenteric defect, place two negative pressure drains peritoneally outside the pelvic floor, and puncture the wound for drainage.
6. Irrigation, hemostasis, count instruments and sponges, close the incision in order, and the operation is completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: rectum, ileocecal junction, sigmoid colon, sigmoid mesocolon, sigmoid vessels, ileocolic vessels, ureter, iliac vessels, peritoneum, pelvic floor. (直肠，回盲瓣，乙状结肠，乙状结肠系膜，乙状结肠血管，回结肠血管，输尿管，髂血管，腹膜，盆腔底)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in the dissection of the rectum and ileocecal junction, and the anastomosis of the colonic and rectal ends.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of identifying and protecting critical structures during dissection.
- The technique of circular stapler anastomosis in the rectum and ileocecal junction.
- The management of complications such as anastomotic leakage and pelvic abscess.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper hemostasis and anastomotic technique to prevent anastomotic leakage.
- Adequate drainage to prevent pelvic abscess.

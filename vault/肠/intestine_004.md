## Overview
Sigmoid colon resection for sigmoid colon cancer, involving resection of the sigmoid colon and ileocecal junction, with anastomosis of the remaining colon segments.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Sigmoid colon cancer
**Intraoperative:** Sigmoid colon cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located at the middle segment of the sigmoid colon, diameter 4cm, hard, penetrating the serosa, descending into the pelvic cavity. Invasion at the pelvic floor and peritoneal reflex, 1cm hard lymph node at the root of mesenteric vessels.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline incision below the navel, enter peritoneum layer by layer, findings as described above.
3. Push the small intestine upwards, expose the sigmoid colon and pelvic peritoneum, separate the sigmoid colon from the pelvic peritoneum, open the lateral peritoneum, free the mesosigmoid, transect the sigmoid colon vessels at the root, dissect the mesenteric vessels at the root, transect the mesosigmoid, free the distal part to 7-8cm below the tumor and the proximal part to 10cm above the tumor, paying attention to the course of the ureter and iliac vessels.
4. Transect the proximal and distal colon, remove the specimen, perform end-to-end anastomosis of the remaining colon segments (full thickness + muscularis mucosa).
5. Close the mesenteric defect, place a negative pressure drain, one at the pelvic floor and one at the anastomosis site, puncture and lead out.
6. Irrigation, hemostasis, count instruments and sponges, close the incision layer by layer, operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: sigmoid colon, ileocolic vessels, ileocecal junction, ureter, iliac vessels. (回盲部, 肠系膜血管, 肠系膜下血管, 输尿管, 髂血管)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in the dissection of the mesentery and vessels, and the anastomosis technique.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of identifying and protecting the ureter and iliac vessels during dissection.
- Proper technique for mesenteric dissection and vessel transection.
- Careful anastomosis technique to prevent anastomotic leakage.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Adequate irrigation and drainage to prevent infection and abscess formation.

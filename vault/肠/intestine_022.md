## Overview
Right hemicolectomy for right colon cancer, involving resection of the cecum, ileocecal junction, ileum, and sigmoid colon, with mesentery and lymph node dissection.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right colon cancer
**Intraoperative:** Right colon cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located in the cecum, approximately 5*5cm2, extending beyond the serosa. Mesenteric superior venae cavae with enlarged lymph nodes palpable. No abnormalities found in liver, stomach, or pelvis.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Lateral midline incision, enter peritoneum, findings as described above.
3. Dissection of gastrocolic ligament, division of right omentum, mobilization of transverse mesocolon, division of hepatocolic ligament, separation of colon from duodenum, mobilization of the hepatic flexure of the colon.
4. Incision of the right paracolic fascia, mobilization of ascending colon mesentery, attention to the course of the ureter, mobilization of ileocecal junction and terminal ileum.
5. Mobilization and resection of lymphatic tissue around the superior mesenteric vein, division and ligation of small branches at the root.
6. Division of right branch of middle colic artery, ileocolic artery, and right colic artery, division and ligation of mesocolon, division of ileum 20cm from ileocecal junction, division of sigmoid colon at the middle, removal of specimen.
7. Anastomosis of ileum and sigmoid colon with a 31.5# circular stapler, closure of mesenteric defect.
8. Irrigation, hemostasis, count instruments and sponges, placement of a negative pressure ball in the right paracolic gutter, closure of incision, completion of surgery.

- Importance of lymph node dissection in colorectal cancer.
- Technique of circular stapler anastomosis.
- Management of mesenteric vessels and lymph nodes.

## Complication Prevention
- Adequate hemostasis.
- Proper anastomotic technique to prevent anastomotic leakage.

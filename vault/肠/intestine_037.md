## Overview
Small bowel resection and cholecystectomy for jejunal tumor and gallbladder calculi.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Intestinal mass.
**Intraoperative:** Jejunal tumor and gallbladder calculi.
**Pathological:** Not documented.

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located approximately 15 cm below Treitz ligament in the jejunum, infiltrative and narrowed, diameter 3 cm, involving serosa; multiple metastatic nodules seen at Treitz ligament, abdominal wall, lateral abdominal wall near the sigmoid colon, and pelvic floor. Gallbladder measuring 6*3*3 cm with one mixed stone, diameter 2.0 cm. Common bile duct diameter 0.8 cm. No abnormalities in liver and stomach.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline incision, enter peritoneum layer by layer, findings as described above; abdominal nodule removed for pathology.
3. Open mesentery of the jejunum, resected 10 cm above and below the tumor, specimen removed, 3-0 absorbable suture for end-to-end anastomosis of the jejunum, serosal layer reinforced; hard nodule at Treitz ligament removed for pathology, mesenteric defect closed.
4. Locate the gallbladder to find the cystic artery and cystic duct, cut and separate the gallbladder,剥离后取下.
5. Green脓杆菌 and Zhong Ren Fu An preparations sprinkled at the tumor site and pelvis, hemostasis achieved, two negative pressure drainage tubes placed at the anastomosis and below Treitz ligament; instruments counted, abdomen closed.

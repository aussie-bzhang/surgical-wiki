## Overview
Colonic stenting for advanced rectal cancer with acute intestinal obstruction, involving a non-resectable tumor at the rectosigmoid junction.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Acute intestinal obstruction, advanced rectal cancer
**Intraoperative:** Acute intestinal obstruction, advanced rectal cancer confirmed
**Pathological:** Low differentiation adenocarcinoma in omentum and mesocolon

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located at the rectosigmoid junction, infiltrative and narrow, extending beyond the serosa and adhering to surrounding tissues. Pelvic, omental, abdominal wall, and small bowel mesentery involvement. Granulomatous nodules and enlarged lymph nodes at the root of mesenteric vessels, presenting as a frozen pelvis. Tumor unresectable. Additional colonic splenic flexure metastatic cancer tissue causing intestinal stenosis but no obstruction. Significant distension of the colon above the descending colon and small intestine. Liver metastases detected. Easy bleeding during surgery, postoperative admission to SICU.

## Operative Steps
1. Positioning in lithotomy, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Mid-lower midline incision, layer-by-layer abdominal entry, findings as described above.
3. Non-resectable tumor confirmed, perform colonic colostomy with surrounding fixation.

## Overview
Rectal cancer resection using the DIXON technique in a patient with locally advanced rectal cancer.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Rectal cancer
**Intraoperative:** Rectal cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
The tumor was located at the superior margin of the peritoneal reflex, measuring 5cm×3cm×3cm in size, infiltrative, involving the peritoneum of the pelvic floor, encircling the bowel for 2/3 of its circumference. No abnormalities were found in the liver.

## Operative Steps
1. The patient was placed in a supine position under general anesthesia, and the abdomen was routinely disinfected and draped.
2. A midline incision was made below the navel, and the abdomen was entered layer by layer. The findings were as described above.
3. The small intestine was pushed upward to expose the rectum and peritoneum of the pelvic floor. The lateral peritoneum was opened, the mesorectum was mobilized, and the rectal vessels were ligated at the root. Lymph nodes at the root were dissected, the mesorectum was transected, and the peritoneal reflex was opened. The distal part was mobilized to 3cm below the tumor, and the proximal part was mobilized to 10cm above the tumor. The ureter and iliac vessels were protected.
4. The proximal and distal ends of the rectum were transected, and the specimen was removed. The proximal sigmoid colon was placed into a 29* circular stapler, and after dilating the anal canal, the circular stapler was inserted through the anus to perform an end-to-end anastomosis of the colon.
5. The mesenteric defect was closed, and two negative pressure drains were placed extraperitoneally in the pelvic floor and brought out through the stab wounds.
6. The abdomen was irrigated, hemostasis was achieved, anti-tumor drugs were sprinkled into the pelvic floor, and the instruments and sponges were counted and found to be correct. The incision was closed in layers, and the procedure was completed.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included the extensive infiltration of the tumor and the need to protect the ureter and iliac vessels during dissection.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of careful dissection and lymph node dissection in colorectal cancer surgery.
- The technique of using a circular stapler for colonic anastomosis.
- The need for meticulous hemostasis and careful closure of the mesenteric defect.

## Complication Prevention
- The risk of anastomotic leakage was minimized by ensuring adequate distal mobilization and proper use of the circular stapler.
- The risk of urinary tract injury was minimized by identifying and protecting the ureter during dissection.

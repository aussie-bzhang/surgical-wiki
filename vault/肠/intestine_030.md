## Overview
Right hemicolectomy for multiple polyps in the ascending colon combined with cholecystectomy due to gallstones. Key technical feature includes safe dissection of the gallbladder and colon, and meticulous anastomosis.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Multiple polyps in the ascending colon
**Intraoperative:** Multiple polyps in the ascending colon + gallstones
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings include two 1cm polyps in the middle segment of the ascending colon near the hepatic flexure, firm, with broad base and no invasion of the serosa. Gallbladder size approximately 8*4*3cm, containing several mixed stones, largest diameter 1.5cm. Gallbladder duct diameter 0.3cm, common bile duct diameter 0.5cm. Remaining segments of the intestine and liver negative.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline incision, enter peritoneum layer by layer, findings as described.
3. Careful dissection of the胆囊三角, free胆囊管 and gallbladder vessels, ligated and sutured 0.5cm from the common bile duct, ligated gallbladder vessels, stripped gallbladder from the liver bed, specimen removed.
4. Separation of gastrocolic ligament, division of the right greater omentum, free the transverse mesocolon, division of the hepatocolic ligament, separation of the colon from the duodenum, free the hepatic flexure of the colon.
5. Open the right parietal peritoneum, free the mesocolon of the ascending colon, pay attention to the course of the ureter, free the ileocecal region and terminal ileum.
6. Division of the right branch of the middle colonic artery, ileocolic artery, and right colic artery, ligated at the root, division of the mesocolon, division of the ileum 20cm from the ileocecal junction, division of the colon at the middle of the transverse colon, specimen removed.
7. Place the anvil of the stapler in the terminal ileum, make a hole on the transverse colon mesocolon, use a 28.5mm circular stapler for ileocolic end-to-end anastomosis, close the hole with 3-0 gastrointestinal suture, reinforce the opening and anastomosis with serosal layer and mesentery, cover with ileocecal appendages. Close the mesenteric defect.
8. Irrigation, hemostasis, count instruments and sponges, place two negative pressure balls in the pelvic fossa at the anastomosis and right paracolic沟.
9. Close the abdomen in layers, operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the ascending colon, gallbladder, and common bile duct. Pay attention to the course of the ureter during dissection.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered include safe dissection of the gallbladder and colon, and meticulous anastomosis.

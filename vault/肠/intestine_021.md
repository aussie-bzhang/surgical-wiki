## Overview
Right hemicolectomy for a colonic mass, with emphasis on the technical aspects of mesocolon dissection, vascular ligation, and ileocolic anastomosis.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Colonic mass
**Intraoperative:** Colonic mass
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Appendix located in an ectopic position, no congestion or edema, no appendiceal mass. A 4cm mass in the cecum, extending beyond the serosa to involve the parietal peritoneum, with cauliflower-like growth, central ulcerated depression, multiple lymph nodes in the tumor plane of the bowel wall, the largest measuring about 3cm in diameter. Small intestine and three segments of bowel wall showed inflammatory congestion, no stricture, no mass growth.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Right paramedian incision, layer-by-layer abdominal entry, findings as described above.
3. Separation of the right half of the gastrocolic ligament, transection of the right branch of the middle colonic vessels, transection of the hepatocolic ligament, mobilization of the hepatic flexure of the colon, separation of the colon from the duodenum.
4. Incision of the lateral peritoneum of the ascending colon fossa, mobilization of the mesocolon of the ascending colon, attention to the course of the ureter, protection of the ureter and duodenum, mobilization of the ileocecal junction and terminal ileum.
5. Transection of the ileocolic vessels, right colonic vessels, and right branch of the middle colonic vessels at 20cm from the ileocecal junction, transection of the ileum at the middle of the transverse colon to remove the specimen.
6. Use of a 31.5mm circular stapler for ileocolic end-to-end anastomosis, reinforcement of the anastomosis, closure of the mesenteric defect.
7. Irrigation, hemostasis, count of instruments and sponges, placement of one negative pressure drain each in the pelvic colon fossa and at the anastomosis site, closure of the incision.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: appendix, ileocecal junction, colon, mesentery, ureter, duodenum. (胆总管)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in mesocolon dissection and vascular ligation, managed with careful dissection and atraumatic techniques.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of careful mesocolon dissection to avoid injury to adjacent structures.
- Proper vascular ligation techniques to ensure hemostasis.
- Technique of circular stapler anastomosis for ileocolic anastomosis.

## Complication Prevention
- Infection prevention through meticulous surgical technique and antibiotic prophylaxis.
- Hemorrhage prevention through careful hemostasis.

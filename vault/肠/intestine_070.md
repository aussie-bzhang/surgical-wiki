## Overview
Rectal cancer resection (DIXON) for locally advanced rectal cancer with circumferential involvement and obstruction. Key technical feature includes mesorectal excision and coloanal anastomosis.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Rectal cancer
**Intraoperative:** Rectal cancer with circumferential involvement and obstruction
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located 10 cm from anal margin, hard, 4 cm in diameter, infiltrative ulcerative type, circumferential involvement causing intestinal stricture. No metastasis in liver, small intestine, remaining colon, mesenteric lymph nodes, pelvis, peritoneum, omentum, etc.

## Operative Steps
1. Position: lithotomy, general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Mid-lower abdominal midline incision, enter peritoneum, findings as described.
3. Push small intestine upwards, expose rectum and pelvic peritoneum, open lateral peritoneum, mobilize mesorectum, transect rectal vessels at the root, dissect mesenteric root lymph nodes, transect mesorectum, mobilize peritoneal reflex, distal mobilization to 3 cm below the tumor, proximal mobilization to ileocecal junction, pay attention to ureter and iliac vessels.
4. Transect proximal and distal colon, remove specimen, dilate anus, place circular stapler through anus for coloanal anastomosis.
5. Irrigation, hemostasis, close mesenteric orifice, place two negative pressure drains at the pelvic floor, puncture and lead out.
6. Hemostasis, count instruments and sponges, close incision, operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: rectum, ileocecal junction, sigmoid colon, mesorectum, ureter, iliac vessels. (直肠，回盲瓣，乙状结肠，直肠系膜，输尿管，髂血管)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties in mesorectal excision and coloanal anastomosis due to tumor involvement and obstruction.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of mesorectal excision in preventing local recurrence.
- Technique of coloanal anastomosis with circular stapler.
- Management of potential complications such as anastomotic leakage and pelvic abscess.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper hemostasis and anastomotic technique to prevent anastomotic leakage.
- Adequate drainage to prevent pelvic abscess.

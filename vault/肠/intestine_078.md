## Overview
Low anterior resection (LAR) with colostomy for rectal cancer, involving resection of the rectum and sigmoid colon, followed by colostomy creation. Key technical feature includes anastomosis with a circular stapler.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Rectal cancer
**Intraoperative:** Rectal cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a 5.0 cm mass at 5 cm from the anal margin in the rectum, which bled easily upon palpation, presenting as an ulcerative infiltrative type, encircling the lumen for one week, and involving the serosal layer. No liver or pelvic involvement.

## Operative Steps
1. Position the patient in supine position under general anesthesia, perform routine disinfection and draping.
2. Make a midline incision below the navel, enter the abdomen layer by layer, and expose the rectum and pelvic peritoneum as described.
3. Push the small intestine upwards, expose the rectum and pelvic peritoneum, open the lateral peritoneum and peritoneal reflex, free the rectal mesentery, and transect the rectal vessels at the root. Cut the rectal mesentery, free the proximal end to 10 cm above the mass and the distal end to 2.0 cm below the mass, being careful of the ureter and iliac vessels.
4. Transect the proximal and distal ends of the rectum, remove the specimen, dilate the anal canal, and place a 31.5 mm circular stapler through the anus for end-to-end anastomosis.
5. Close the mesenteric defect, place two negative pressure drains in the pelvic peritoneal extraperitoneal space, and puncture the incision to lead out.
6. Make a circular incision in the left upper abdomen, incise to the peritoneum, pull out the sigmoid colon to perform a loop colostomy.
7. Irrigate, ensure adequate hemostasis, count instruments and sponges, close the incision in layers, and the procedure is completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the rectum, sigmoid colon, ileum, ileocecal junction, vessels, and lymph nodes. The common bile duct (胆总管) should be protected during the procedure.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered include managing bleeding from the mass and ensuring proper anastomosis with the circular stapler.

## Teaching Points
- Proper identification and protection of the ureter and iliac vessels during mesentery dissection.
- Technique for using the circular stapler for end-to-end anastomosis.
- Importance of adequate hemostasis and proper placement of drains.

## Complication Prevention
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper anastomosis technique to prevent anastomotic leakage.

## Related Procedures
- [[Total Mesorectal Excision (TME)]] - TME is often performed in conjunction with LAR for rectal cancer.

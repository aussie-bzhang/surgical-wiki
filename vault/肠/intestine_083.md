## Overview
Rectal cancer resection (low anterior resection, LAR) with prophylactic colostomy for rectal cancer.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Rectal mass, suspected malignancy?
**Intraoperative:** Rectal mass, confirmed malignancy?
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings: A villous mass 6 cm from the anal margin, approximately 3.5*2.5 cm in size, with a base diameter of 2.5 cm. No abnormalities found in the liver, pancreas, spleen, other intestinal segments, abdomen, or pelvis.

## Operative Steps
1. Place the patient in a supine position under general anesthesia and perform routine disinfection and draping.
2. Make a midline incision below the navel, enter the abdomen layer by layer, and expose the rectum and pelvic peritoneum as described above.
3. Push the small intestine upwards, expose the rectum and pelvic peritoneum, open the sigmoid colon lateral peritoneum, free the mesentery, and cut the sigmoid colon vessels at the root. Open the peritoneal reflex, separate along the pre-sacral plane, cut the lateral rectal ligaments, free the anterior wall over the seminal vesicle and prostate, and separate distally to 3 cm below the mass and proximally to 10 cm above the mass, protecting the ureter and iliac vessels.
4. Cut the sigmoid colon, place the proximal sigmoid colon into a 29* tube anastomosis stapler, and set aside.
5. Clamp the distal rectum 2 cm from the tumor, dilate the anal canal, flush the anal canal and distal rectum with 500 ml of normal saline, clamp the rectum, and cut it off. Remove the specimen, and place a tube anastomosis stapler through the anus to perform an end-to-end anastomosis between the rectum and sigmoid colon.
6. Close the mesenteric opening, place two negative pressure drains peripherally in the pelvis, and puncture the incision to lead out.
7. Make a transverse incision in the upper midline, pull the middle segment of the transverse colon out through the abdominal wall, and support it with a glass tube.
8. Flush, complete hemostasis, count instruments and sponges, and close the incision in layers. The operation is completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: rectum, sigmoid colon, ileum, ileocecal valve, anal canal. (直肠，乙状结肠，回肠，回盲瓣，肛门)

## Teaching Points
- The importance of protecting the ureter and iliac vessels during dissection.
- The technique of using a stapler for colonic anastomosis.
- The management of the rectal stump and anal canal.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Adequate drainage to prevent pelvic abscess.

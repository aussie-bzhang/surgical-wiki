## Overview
Sigmoid colon resection for sigmoid colon cancer, involving the ileocecal junction and rectum, with emphasis on vascular and lymphatic dissection.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Sigmoid colon cancer
**Intraoperative:** Sigmoid colon cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a sigmoid colon mass 20 cm from the anal margin, approximately 1.0 cm in diameter at the site of previous EMR, not penetrating the serosa. Three metallic clips were observed. No abnormalities were found in the exploration of the remaining colon, small intestine, liver, and pelvis.

## Operative Steps
1. Position the patient in supine position under general anesthesia, perform routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline incision below the umbilicus, enter peritoneum layer by layer, findings as described above.
3. Push the small intestine upwards, expose the sigmoid colon and pelvic peritoneum, open the lateral peritoneum, mobilize the sigmoid colon mesentery, and transect the sigmoid colon vessels at the root.
4. Transect the sigmoid colon mesentery, mobilize the proximal end to 7 cm above the tumor, and place a 28.5mm anastomosis stapler. Protect the ureter and iliac vessels.
5. Mobilize the distal end to 5 cm below the tumor, below the peritoneal reflex, and transect the distal rectum.
6. Remove the specimen, clean the distal rectum thoroughly, and place a 28.5mm circular stapler for end-to-end anastomosis. Suture the serosal layer of the anastomosis with interrupted sutures for reinforcement. Close the mesenteric defect.
7. Place two negative pressure drains in the pelvic floor.
8. Irrigate, ensure hemostasis, count instruments and sponges, and close the incision in layers.
9. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the sigmoid colon, ileocecal junction, rectum, vessels, and lymph nodes. The common bile duct (胆总管) and ureter should be protected during dissection.

## Teaching Points
- Proper mobilization and dissection of the sigmoid colon and mesentery.
- Protection of the ureter and iliac vessels during vascular dissection.
- Accurate placement of the anastomosis stapler and reinforcement of the anastomotic site.

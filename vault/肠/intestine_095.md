## Overview
Right hemicolectomy for ascending colon cancer; clinical context: colon cancer; key technical feature: resection of ascending colon with ileocolic anastomosis.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Ascending colon cancer
**Intraoperative:** Ascending colon cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located in the ascending colon, approximately 5*5cm in size, invades serosa, extracorporeal growth, involves the inferior margin of the right liver and kidney. Exploration of stomach, gallbladder, pancreas, small intestine, and pelvis all normal.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline incision, enter peritoneum layer by layer, findings as described above.
3. Dissection of gastrocolic ligament, transection of the right greater omentum along the inferior margin of the网膜弓, mobilization of transverse mesocolon.
4. Transection of right middle colic vessels, ileocolic vessels, and right colic vessels, ligation at the root.
5. Transection of hepatocolic ligament, separation of colon from duodenum, mobilization of colonic flexure.
6. Incision of the right paracolic fascia, mobilization of the ascending colon mesocolon, attention to the course of the ureter, mobilization of the ileocecal junction and terminal ileum.
7. Transection of the mesocolon, transection of the ileum 20cm from the ileocecal junction, transection of the sigmoid colon at the middle of the transverse colon, removal of specimen.
8. Placement of the anvil of the stapler in the terminal ileum, 28mm circular stapler for ileocolic end-to-side anastomosis, side-to-side stapler to close the colon stump, blind loop 2cm long, reinforcement with seromuscular layer at the stump and anastomosis, coverage with ileocecal appendages. Closure of the mesenteric defect.
9. Irrigation, hemostasis, count instruments and sponges, placement of two negative pressure balls in the right paracolic gutter and pelvic cavity at the anastomosis site.
10. Layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen, operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: common bile duct (胆总管), ileocolic vessels, right colic vessels, middle colic vessels, ureter. Danger zones: areas of potential injury during dissection and mobilization. Structures requiring protection: blood vessels, nerves, and the ureter.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered and how they were managed: Not documented

## Teaching Points
- Importance of careful mobilization and dissection to avoid injury to critical structures.
- Proper handling of blood vessels and anastomosis technique.
- Attention to the course of the ureter during mobilization.

## Complication Prevention
- Prevention of anastomotic leakage: thorough irrigation and hemostasis.
- Prevention of postoperative ileus: early mobilization and adequate nutrition support.

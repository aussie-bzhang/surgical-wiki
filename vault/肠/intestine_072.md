## Overview
Rectal cancer resection using the DIXON technique in a patient with locally advanced rectal cancer.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Rectal cancer
**Intraoperative:** Rectal cancer confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located at the lower end of the rectum, ulcerative infiltrative type, circumferential around the bowel, diameter 3.0cm, hard, involving the serosa, enlarged lymph nodes in the mesenteric root palpable. Negative exploration of liver and peritoneum.

## Operative Steps
1. Position: lithotomy, general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Mid-lower midline incision around the umbilicus, enter peritoneum layer by layer, findings as described above.
3. Push the small intestine upwards, expose the rectum and pelvic peritoneum, open the lateral peritoneum, mobilize the rectal mesentery, transect the rectal vessels at the root, dissect the mesenteric root lymph nodes, transect the rectal mesentery, separate the peritoneal reflex, distal separation to 3cm below the tumor, proximal mobilization to the ileocecal junction, pay attention to the course of the ureter and iliac vessels.
4. Transect the proximal and distal colon, remove the specimen, dilate the anal canal, and place a circular stapler through the anus for colo-colonic anastomosis.
5. Irrigation, hemostasis, close the mesenteric orifice, place two negative pressure drains in the pelvis, and puncture the incision.
6. Hemostasis, count instruments and sponges, close the incision, and the operation is completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: rectum, ileocecal junction, sigmoid colon, common iliac vessels, ureter. (直肠，回盲瓣，乙状结肠，髂血管，输尿管)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in mobilizing the rectum and mesentery, ensuring proper anastomosis, and managing blood loss.

## Teaching Points
- Proper mobilization of the rectum and mesentery
- Safe dissection around the ureter and iliac vessels
- Technical aspects of colo-colonic anastomosis

## Complication Prevention
- Adequate hemostasis
- Proper anastomotic technique
- Early detection and management of complications

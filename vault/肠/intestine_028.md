## Overview
Cecum resection for cecum cancer with peritoneal metastasis, highlighting the technical aspects of colon resection and anastomosis.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Intestinal obstruction
**Intraoperative:** Cervical cancer, peritoneal metastasis
**Pathological:** "Intestinal mesentery nodule" adenocarcinoma tissue infiltration

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a circumferential infiltrative mass in the left half of the cecum, measuring 3.0cm in diameter, hard, causing stenosis of the intestinal lumen. The small intestine and ascending colon were severely dilated. 4000ml of fecal-like fluid was drained from the intestinal lumen. Multiple millet-like nodules were palpable in the mesentery, hard.

## Operative Steps
1. Place the patient in a supine position under general anesthesia and perform routine disinfection and draping.
2. Make a midline incision, enter the abdomen layer by layer, and observe the findings as described above.
3. Separate the gastrocolic ligament, transect the omentum, free the mesocolon, and ligate the middle colonic vessels and their branches. Free the hepatic flexure and splenic flexure of the colon.
4. Transect the colon 5cm from both ends of the mass and remove the specimen.
5. Incise the ascending colon, perform an end-to-end anastomosis with a circular stapler, close the ascending colon wall, reinforce the seromuscular layer of the anastomosis, and close the mesenteric defect.
6. Irrigate, ensure hemostasis, count instruments and sponges, place two negative pressure balls in the lateral colon sulcus, close the incision, and complete the procedure.

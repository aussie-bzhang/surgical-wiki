## Overview
Right hemicolectomy with liver tumor biopsy for ileocecal cancer with liver metastasis.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Ileocecal mass, hepatic metastatic tumor.
**Intraoperative:** Ileocecal cancer, multiple liver metastases.
**Pathological:** Malignant tumor of ileocecal region, low differentiation adenocarcinoma. Liver nodules show infiltration of low differentiation adenocarcinoma tissue.

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located at ileocecal valve, approximately 2*3cm, ulcerative infiltrative type. Lymph nodes in the paraaortic and mesenteric vascular roots were palpable, hard, and fused into a sheet. Scattered tumor nodules were present in both left and right lobes of the liver. Frozen pathology report confirmed the findings.

## Operative Steps
1. Place the patient in supine position under general anesthesia, perform routine disinfection and draping.
2. Make a midline incision, enter the abdomen layer by layer, and observe the findings as described.
3. Take the left lobe liver nodule, ligate the margin, and excise the tumor nodule for frozen pathology.
4. Separate the gastrocolic ligament along the greater curvature of the stomach, divide the right omentum, free the transverse mesocolon, divide the hepatocolic ligament, separate the colon from the duodenum, and free the hepatic flexure of the colon.
5. Open the right parietal peritoneum, free the ascending colon mesocolon, protect the right ureter, free the ileocecal region and distal ileum.
6. Divide the right branch of the middle colonic artery, ileocolic artery, and right colonic artery at their roots, ligate them, divide the mesocolon, and transect the ileum 30cm from the ileocecal valve and the transverse colon at the middle.
7. Place the anvil of the stapler in the distal ileum, perform end-to-side anastomosis with the circular stapler, and close the residual transverse colon with the closure device. Close the mesenteric defect.
8. Irrigate, achieve hemostasis, count instruments and sponges, place two negative pressure drainage balls in the right colon paracolic沟, place them upwards and downwards, close the incision one by one, and complete the operation.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: ileocecal valve, liver, lymph nodes. (胆总管)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in protecting the right ureter and achieving hemostasis.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of protecting the right ureter during surgery.
- Proper technique for achieving hemostasis in the mesentery.

## Complication Prevention
- Preventing postoperative ileus by ensuring proper anastomosis.
- Preventing postoperative infection by maintaining strict aseptic technique.

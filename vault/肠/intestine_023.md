## Overview
Right hemicolectomy for right colon cancer, involving ileocecal junction and ileum resection, with coloileal anastomosis.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right colon mass
**Intraoperative:** Cervical cancer involving ileocecal valve
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located at ileocecal junction, involving ileocecal valve, extending to peritoneal surface, adherent to lateral abdominal wall. Tumor size approximately 6*5cm. Distal ileum significantly dilated, ileal wall edematous and thickened. Intraoperative bleeding about 300ml, no blood transfusion.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline incision, enter peritoneum layer by layer, hemostasis, skin protection, findings as described above.
3. Dissection of right half of gastrocolic ligament, transection of right branch of middle colic artery, transection of hepatocolic ligament, mobilization of hepatic flexure of colon.
4. Incision of lateral peritoneum of ascending colon, mobilization of mesocolon of ascending colon, attention to ureteral course, protection of duodenum.
5. Transection of ileocolic artery, right colic artery, ileum transected 15cm from ileocecal junction, transverse colon transected at middle, specimen removed.
6. Stapled anastomosis of ileum and colon, colonic stump clamped 3cm from anastomosis, anastomosis reinforced, mesenteric defect closed.
7. Irrigation, hemostasis, count instruments and sponges, negative pressure drainage placed at right pelvic colic fossa and anastomosis site, incision closed individually.
8. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Ileocecal junction (回盲部), ileum (回肠), colon (结肠)
> Critical structures: ileocecal valve (回盲瓣), mesentery (系膜), ureter (输尿管)
> Danger zones: blood vessels, lymph nodes
> Structures requiring protection: duodenum (十二指肠), ureter (输尿管)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties in mobilization of ileocecal junction, hemostasis of vessels, and anastomotic technique.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of proper mobilization and hemostasis
- Technique of stapled anastomosis
- Protection of adjacent structures

## Overview
Rectal resection (Dixon) for rectal cancer with liver metastasis, involving significant anatomical structures and technical challenges.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Rectal cancer with liver metastasis
**Intraoperative:** Rectal cancer with liver metastasis
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a 5.0cm infiltrative narrowed mass at the upper end of the rectum, penetrating the serosa layer. Lymph nodes were palpable at the root of the mesentery. The mesentery was contracted. Multiple hard masses were palpable within the liver.

## Operative Steps
1. Position the patient in supine position under general anesthesia, perform routine disinfection and draping.
2. Make a midline incision below the umbilicus, enter the abdomen layer by layer, and expose the rectum and pelvic peritoneum as described.
3. Push the small intestine upwards, expose the rectum and pelvic peritoneum, open the lateral peritoneum, mobilize the rectal mesentery, and transect the rectal vessels at the root. Transect the rectal mesentery, mobilize the distal end to the peritoneal reflexion, and the proximal end to the splenic flexure. Pay attention to the course of the ureter and common iliac vessels.
4. Transect the proximal and distal ends of the rectum, remove the specimen, dilate the anal canal, and place a circular stapler through the anus for colo-colonic anastomosis.
5. Close the mesenteric defect, place two negative pressure drainage tubes at the pelvic floor, and puncture the wound for drainage.
6. Irrigate, ensure hemostasis, count instruments and sponges, close the incision layer by layer, and complete the operation.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the rectum, ileocecal junction, sigmoid colon, common iliac vessels, and ureter. (直肠，回盲瓣，乙状结肠，髂总血管，输尿管)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included significant adhesions, contracted mesentery, and the need for careful dissection around the ureter and common iliac vessels.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of careful dissection around the ureter and common iliac vessels to avoid injury.
- The technique of circular stapler placement for colo-colonic anastomosis.
- The management of significant adhesions and contracted mesentery.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Adequate drainage to prevent pelvic abscess.

## Overview
Colonic stump closure is a surgical procedure performed after colonic stoma creation to close the stoma and restore normal colonic continuity. It is typically indicated in patients with ileocecal junction obstruction or as part of cancer resection.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Colorectal cancer, ileocecal junction obstruction
**Intraoperative:** Colorectal cancer, ileocecal junction obstruction
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a colonic stoma located at the original midupper abdominal incision. Partial bowel wall adhesion to the subperitoneal membrane below the incision was noted. The distal segment of the stoma was obstructed approximately 3 cm away from the stoma opening.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia was administered, and the patient was prepared for surgery.
2. A midline incision 5.0 cm above and below the stoma was made. The subperitoneal membrane below the incision and the transverse colon were mobilized.
3. The distal obstructed segment of the stoma was resected approximately 5 cm. End-to-end anastomosis was performed between the proximal and distal segments (full-thickness interrupted suture).
4. The abdomen was closed in layers, and the procedure was concluded.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the colonic stump and the ileocecal junction. The common bile duct (胆总管) should be protected during the procedure.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included adhesion of the bowel wall to the subperitoneal membrane and the need for precise anastomosis to avoid leakage.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of careful mobilization to avoid adhesions.
- The technique of performing an end-to-end anastomosis with full-thickness sutures.
- The need for meticulous hemostasis to prevent postoperative bleeding.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful mobilization to minimize adhesions.
- Adequate anastomotic tension to prevent leakage.
- Postoperative monitoring for signs of leakage or infection.

## Related Procedures
- [[Colorectal Cancer Resection]] - This procedure is often performed in conjunction with colonic stump closure.

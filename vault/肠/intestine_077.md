## Overview
Rectal cancer resection (Low-Dixon) is a surgical procedure for the treatment of rectal cancer. It involves the removal of the rectum and a portion of the sigmoid colon, followed by anastomosis of the remaining colon. The procedure is performed through an open abdominal approach.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Rectal cancer
**Intraoperative:** Rectal cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located 6 cm from anal margin, approximately 5 cm in diameter, ulcerative infiltrative lesion,突破浆 membrane, rectal anterior wall with 0.5 cm perforation. No abnormalities in lower abdomen and other colon. Pelvic examination negative.

## Operative Steps
1. Place patient in supine position, under general anesthesia, and perform routine disinfection and draping.
2. Mid-lower abdominal midline incision, enter peritoneum layer by layer, hemostasis, and skin protection. Operative findings as described above.
3. Push small intestine upwards, expose rectum and pelvic peritoneum, open lateral peritoneum, mobilize rectal mesentery, ligate and transect rectal mesenteric vessels, pay attention to the course of ureter and iliac vessels and avoid injury to ureter. Remove enlarged lymph nodes from mesenteric root for pathological examination.
4. Mobilize lateral peritoneum of rectum and sigmoid colon, ligate side vessels, and锐性分离sacrorectal space, avoiding injury to sacral venous plexus. Transect bowel wall 2 cm below tumor margin, remove specimen for pathological examination, place 28# circular stapler head at proximal end, and clamp distal end with purse-string suture.
5. Dilate anal canal, irrigate rectum, and place 28# circular stapler through anal canal for end-to-end anastomosis.
6. Suture and reinforce anastomosis around the circumference.
7. Close mesenteric defect and posterior peritoneum, place two negative pressure drains through incisions in pelvic peritoneum.
8. Irrigate with NS, ensure hemostasis, and verify instruments and gauze.
9. Close incision in layers, surgery completed.

## Overview
Right hemicolectomy for colonic hepatic flexure mass, with significant findings and technical challenges.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Colonic hepatic flexure mass
**Intraoperative:** Colonic hepatic flexure adenoma
**Pathological:** Colonic hepatic flexure adenoma with moderate-severe atypical hyperplasia

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings: 5cm mass at the colonic hepatic flexure, encircling half of the bowel, with negative findings in liver, pelvis, stomach, pancreas, gallbladder, small intestine, and other colon.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline incision, enter peritoneum layer by layer, findings as described above.
3. Dissection of the right half of the gastrocolic ligament, transection of the right branch of the middle colic vessels, transection of the hepatocolic ligament, and mobilization of the colonic hepatic flexure.
4. Incision of the lateral peritoneum of the ascending colon, mobilization of the mesocolon of the ascending colon, attention to the course of the ureter, and protection of the duodenum.
5. Transection of the ileocolic vessels, right colonic vessels, transection of the ileum 15cm from the ileocecal junction, and transection of the middle colon.
6. 28mm circular stapler for ileocolic end-to-side anastomosis, colonic stump clamped 3cm from the anastomosis, anastomosis reinforced, and mesenteric defect closed.
7. Irrigation, hemostasis, count instruments and sponges, placement of one negative pressure drain in the right colonic fossa and pelvis, and one at the anastomosis site, and closure of the incision.
8. Completion of the procedure.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: colonic flexure, liver, ileocolic vessels, right colonic vessels. (胆总管)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered: mobilization of the colonic hepatic flexure, protection of the ureter and duodenum during mobilization, and anastomotic technique.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of careful mobilization to avoid injury to critical structures.
- Technique of ileocolic anastomosis with circular stapler.
- Postoperative care and monitoring for complications.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful mobilization to avoid injury to critical structures.
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper anastomotic technique to reduce the risk of anastomotic leakage.

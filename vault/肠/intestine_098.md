## Overview
Duodenal bulb perforation repair in a patient with gastrointestinal perforation, emphasizing the technical aspects of suture repair and abdominal lavage.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastrointestinal perforation
**Intraoperative:** Duodenal bulb perforation
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a 2.0 cm ulcer in the duodenal bulb with a central 1.0 cm perforation. Surrounding tissue was covered with pus and debris. Approximately 100 ml of thin purulent exudate was found in the abdominal cavity. No significant abnormalities were noted in the stomach, small intestine, colon, or biliary tract.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia and routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline upper abdominal incision.
3. Suture repair of the perforation with the suture direction perpendicular to the longitudinal axis of the duodenal bulb, covered with omental tissue.
4. Abdominal lavage with large amounts of normal saline until clean, aspiration of exudate, placement of two negative pressure tubes below the liver and one in the pelvis, puncture incision for drainage, and layer-by-layer closure.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: Duodenal bulb (十二指肠球), Perforation site (穿孔部位). Structures requiring protection: Adjacent bowel, blood vessels, and nerves.

## Technical Challenges
Managing the contaminated environment and ensuring proper closure without causing adhesions.

## Teaching Points
- Proper suture technique to close the perforation.
- Importance of thorough lavage to reduce contamination.
- Careful handling of adjacent structures to avoid injury.

## Complication Prevention
- Preventing infection by ensuring proper lavage and closure.
- Minimizing adhesions by careful handling of tissues.

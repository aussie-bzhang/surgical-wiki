## Overview
Rectal cancer resection (Hartmann procedure) for locally advanced rectal cancer with significant preoperative and intraoperative findings.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Rectal cancer
**Intraoperative:** Rectal cancer confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located on anterior and lateral wall of rectum, cauliflower-like, 5*3cm in size, 5.0cm from anal margin. Intraoperative bleeding 1000ml. Exploration of liver, omentum, peritoneum all negative.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline lower abdominal incision, enter peritoneum layer by layer, findings as above.
3. Push small intestine upwards, expose rectum and pelvic peritoneum, open lateral peritoneum, mobilize sigmoid colon mesentery, expose and protect bilateral ureters, open peritoneal reflection on anterior rectal wall, transect rectal vessels at root, transect sigmoid colon and rectal mesentery, transect lateral ligaments of rectum,锐性分离骶前间隙直至尾骨尖，fully mobilize anterior rectal wall to deep level.
4. Clamp rectum 2cm proximal to tumor, remove specimen.
5. Reconstruct pelvic peritoneum, make a circular incision in left lower abdomen, incise to peritoneum, pull out proximal sigmoid colon for colostomy.
6. Irrigation, hemostasis, place two negative pressure drains in sacrum, close incision layer by layer.
7. Count instruments and sponges, close abdomen in layers, operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: rectum, sigmoid colon, ileocecal junction, ureters. (直肠，乙状结肠，回盲部，输尿管)

## Teaching Points
- Importance of proper mobilization of rectum and sigmoid colon
- Careful handling of ureters during dissection
- Proper colostomy site selection

## Complication Prevention
- Adequate hemostasis
- Proper colostomy site selection

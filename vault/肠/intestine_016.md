## Overview
Total colectomy for colon cancer and polyposis, with significant findings of scattered polyps and infiltrative tumors in the cecum, ascending colon, and rectum.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Colon cancer, polyposis coli
**Intraoperative:** Colon cancer, polyposis coli
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Scattered polyps measuring 0.5-1cm in the entire colon. One 5cm, one 5cm, and one 2cm pedunculated masses in the ileocecal junction, ascending colon, and rectum, respectively, extending beyond the serosa. Mesenteric lymph nodes were scattered and enlarged, with fusion into nodules at the root of the mesentery. Exploration of liver, stomach, small intestine, and pelvis was negative.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Midline incision, enter peritoneum layer by layer as described.
2. Dissection of gastrocolic ligament, resection of preperitoneal mesocolon, resection of omentum, mobilization of transverse mesocolon, opening of lateral peritoneum of left and right colons, transection of splenocolic and hepatocolic ligaments, mobilization of colic flexures.
3. Opening of lateral peritoneum on the right side, mobilization of ascending mesocolon, attention to the course of the ureter, mobilization of ileocecal junction and terminal ileum.
4. Opening of lateral peritoneum on the left side, mobilization of descending mesocolon, dissection and transection of mesocolon of each colon.
5. Transection of middle colic artery, ileocolic artery, and right colic artery, ligation at the root, and transection of mesocolon.
6. Mobilization of the small intestine superiorly, exposing the sigmoid colon and pelvic peritoneum, opening the lateral peritoneum of sigmoid colon, mobilization of its mesocolon, transection of sigmoid colon and superior rectal artery at the root, opening peritoneal reflex, and dissection along the pre-sacral plane to transect both lateral rectal ligaments.
7. Transection of terminal ileum to 20cm above the mass from the ileocecal junction, placement of the proximal ileum into a 28.5* tubular anastomosis stapler, mobilization of distal ileum to 5cm below the mass, clamping and transection, removal of specimen; after dilatation, irrigation of anal canal and distal rectum with 500ml of normal saline, placement of tubular anastomosis stapler through the anus for end-to-end anastomosis of rectum and ileum.
8. Closure of mesenteric defect, placement of two negative pressure drains in the pelvis on both sides.
9. Irrigation, hemostasis, verification of instruments and sponges, layer-by-layer closure of incision, completion of surgery.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: colon, ileocecal junction, ascending colon, sigmoid colon, rectum. (胆总管)

## Technical Challenges
Not documented

## Teaching Points
- Importance of meticulous dissection and identification of critical structures.
- Proper management of mesenteric lymph nodes and vascular pedicles.
- Technique of ileo-rectal anastomosis.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Adequate drainage to prevent pelvic abscess.

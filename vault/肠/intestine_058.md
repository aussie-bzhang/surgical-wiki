## Overview
Left hemicolectomy for colorectal cancer; preoperative diagnosis of colon splenic flexure cancer; significant anatomical findings and technical challenges.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Colon splenic flexure cancer
**Intraoperative:** Colon splenic flexure cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located at the colon splenic flexure, size approximately 8×6×4cm, infiltrative ulcerative type, occupying 3/4 of the lumen, extending beyond the serosa, lymph nodes in the bowel wall enlarged. Negative exploration of liver, biliary tract, omentum, stomach, small intestine, remaining colon, pelvis, and ascites.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia achieved, patient placed in supine position, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midupper midline incision made, layers entered, hemostasis, and skin protected. Intraoperative exploration as described.
3. Dissection of gastrocolic ligament, resection of anterior leaf of mesocolon, resection of left part of omentum, mobilization of transverse mesocolon, opening of lateral peritoneum of left colon, transection of splenocolic ligament, mobilization of colon splenic flexure.
4. Ligation and transection of left branch of middle colonic vessels at the proximal and distal ends of the tumor. Dissection of mesocolon to 7-8cm below the tumor and 10cm above the tumor.
5. Transection and anastomosis of proximal and distal colon, removal of specimen, and sutured end-to-end anastomosis.
6. Closure of mesenteric defect, placement of negative pressure drain through a stab wound in the left colonic fossa.
7. Irrigation, hemostasis, count instruments and sponges, close incision, and surgery completed.

## Overview
Sigmoid colon cancer resection is a procedure performed for the treatment of sigmoid colon cancer. It involves the removal of the affected sigmoid colon and surrounding structures, followed by anastomosis of the remaining colon. The clinical context includes the diagnosis of sigmoid colon cancer and the need for curative resection.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Sigmoid colon cancer
**Intraoperative:** Sigmoid colon cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
The tumor was located in the lower segment of the sigmoid colon, measuring approximately 4 cm in diameter. It presented as an infiltrative ulcerative type, encircling the bowel for one week. Multiple lymph nodes were enlarged at the root of the mesentery. No abnormalities were found in the liver, spleen, or pelvis. The small intestine showed dense adhesions. The operation was uneventful and the patient was returned to the ward in good condition.

## Operative Steps
1. The patient was placed in a supine position under general anesthesia, and the abdomen was routinely disinfected and draped.
2. A midline incision was made below the navel, and the abdomen was entered in a layered manner. The findings were as described above.
3. The small intestine was pushed upwards to expose the sigmoid colon and pelvic peritoneum. The lateral peritoneum was opened, and the mesentery of the sigmoid colon was mobilized. The sigmoid colon vessels were transected at the root.
4. The mesentery was transected, with the proximal end mobilized to 7 cm above the tumor. The distal end was mobilized to 5 cm below the tumor, below the peritoneal reflexion.
5. The distal rectum was transected, and the specimen was removed. The distal rectum was cleaned thoroughly and a 28.5 mm circular stapler was used for end-to-end anastomosis. The serosal layer of the anastomosis was sutured in a continuous manner to reinforce it. The mesenteric defect was closed.
6. Three negative pressure drains were left in place.
7. The abdomen was irrigated, hemostasis was achieved, instruments and sponges were counted and found to be correct, and the incision was closed in layers.
8. The operation was completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> The common bile duct (胆总管), ureter, and iliac vessels should be protected during the procedure. The sigmoid colon and mesentery are critical structures that require careful dissection and mobilization.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included dense adhesions in the small intestine, which were managed by careful dissection and mobilization.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of identifying and protecting the ureter and iliac vessels during mesentery mobilization.
- The technique of using a circular stapler for end-to-end anastomosis of the colon.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Adequate irrigation to reduce the risk of infection.

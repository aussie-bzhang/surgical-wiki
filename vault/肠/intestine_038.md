## Overview
Small bowel resection for tumor and obstruction, with emphasis on the technical aspects of ileal resection and anastomosis.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Small bowel tumor, intestinal obstruction
**Intraoperative:** Confirmed small bowel tumor, intestinal obstruction
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor found at 30 cm from ileocecal valve, 3.0 cm in diameter, hard consistency, causing complete obstruction of the bowel lumen. Proximal small bowel significantly dilated, distal small bowel normal. Gallstone of 2.0 cm diameter palpable in gallbladder, gallbladder size approximately 7*4*3 cm. No significant abnormalities found in liver, bile duct, stomach, remaining small bowel, colon, or pelvis.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline midabdominal incision, enter peritoneum layer by layer, intraoperative findings as above.
3. Clamp, cut, and ligate small bowel mesentery 5.0 cm from the tumor margins.
4. Cut the small bowel, remove the specimen for pathological examination.
5. Clamp the proximal small bowel with a purse-string suture, insert the mushroom head of the 25.5 mm tube-shaped anastomosis stapler.
6. Insert the tube-shaped anastomosis stapler into the distal small bowel to complete the ileal end-to-side anastomosis. Clamp the distal small bowel end with a clamp, and suture the anastomosis and residual end with interrupted full-thickness sutures for reinforcement.
7. No bleeding confirmed, lavage the peritoneal cavity with NS until clean, leave a negative pressure drain, count instruments and sponges, and close the abdomen.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: ileum, ileocecal valve. (回盲瓣)

## Technical Challenges
Not documented

## Teaching Points
- Importance of accurate tumor localization and mesenteric dissection.
- Technique of ileal anastomosis using a tube-shaped stapler.
- Management of potential complications such as anastomotic leakage.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper hemostasis during dissection.
- Adequate lavage of the peritoneal cavity.
- Use of a negative pressure drain.

## Overview
Rectal cancer resection with low anterior resection and pull-through anastomosis, a common procedure for rectal cancer, involving the removal of the cancerous segment of the rectum and the construction of a new anal canal.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Rectal cancer
**Intraoperative:** Rectal cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located on the anterior wall of the rectum, 6 cm from the anal margin, approximately 5×4 cm in size, infiltrating into the muscular layer. Exploration of liver, pelvis, and omentum negative.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline incision below the navel, enter peritoneum layer by layer, findings as described above.
3. Push the small intestine upwards, expose the rectum and pelvic peritoneum, open the lateral peritoneum, free the mesosigmoid, pay attention to the course of the ureters, open the peritoneal reflection on the anterior wall of the rectum, cut off the rectal vessels at the root, cut off the mesosigmoid and rectal mesentery, cut off the lateral ligaments of the rectum, sharp dissection of the pre-sacral space to the tip of the coccyx, fully free the anterior wall of the rectum, pay attention to the seminal vesicles and prostate, try to separate to the deep part, cut off the proximal colon.
4. After dilating the anus, rinse the rectum, place a round clamp into the distal colon through the anus and fix it properly, pull the colon and rectum out of the anus through the intestinal cavity.
5. Clamp the rectum 2 cm proximal to the tumor, remove the specimen, reinsert the distal end of the rectum through the intestinal cavity, and place a 33F circular stapler for end-to-end anastomosis.
6. Rinse, perfect hemostasis, reconstruct the pelvic peritoneum, place two negative pressure drains in the extraperitoneal space, puncture and lead out.
7. Free the transverse colon for colostomy.
8. Count instruments and sponges, close the incision one by one, close the operation.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: rectum, sigmoid colon, ileum, rectovesical pouch, prostate, vesicle, uterus. (直肠，乙状结肠，回肠，直肠膀胱陷凹，前列腺，膀胱，子宫)

## Teaching Points
- Importance of sharp dissection and hemostasis during the operation.
- Proper handling of the rectovesical pouch and the preservation of the pelvic autonomic nerves.
- The technique of circular stapler anastomosis.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Adequate drainage to prevent pelvic abscess.

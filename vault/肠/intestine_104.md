## Overview
Small bowel resection and polypectomy for acute complete intestinal obstruction with multiple polyps.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Acute complete intestinal obstruction
**Intraoperative:** Acute complete intestinal obstruction
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings: 400ml of ascites in the abdominal cavity, clear in color; about 50cm from the ileocecal junction, there was a 25cm small bowel intussusception; the proximal intussuscepted segment was necrotic, with a diameter of about 3cm; the distal intussuscepted segment was atrophic with a diameter of about 1.5cm; the small bowel wall was significantly edematous; multiple polyps were seen in the small bowel, sizes ranging from 0.5-3cm; 4 polyps larger than 2cm were excised, all with pedicles; about 80cm of small bowel was resected, leaving about 2.5m of remaining small bowel.

## Operative Steps
1. Place the patient in a supine position under general anesthesia and perform routine disinfection and draping.
2. Make a midline incision, enter the abdominal cavity layer by layer, and the findings are as described above.
3. Incise the ileum about 5cm above and below the intussusception and perform end-to-end anastomosis with serosal reinforcement.
4. Explore 4 tumors larger than 2cm, incise the bowel wall along the margin of each tumor, excise the polyps completely, and suture the bowel wall.
5. Irrigate, ensure hemostasis, count instruments and sponges, place two negative pressure drainage tubes, and close the incision sequentially.
6. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: ileum, ileocecal junction, common bile duct (胆总管). Structures requiring protection: bowel wall integrity, blood supply to the bowel.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered: Necrotic bowel segment, extensive bowel resection, and multiple polypectomies.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of accurate preoperative diagnosis and planning.
- Technique of end-to-end anastomosis with serosal reinforcement.
- Management of multiple polypectomies in a limited bowel segment.

## Complication Prevention
- Adequate bowel preparation and decompression.
- Careful hemostasis and bowel anastomosis technique.
- Postoperative monitoring for signs of bowel obstruction or leakage.

## Overview
Rectal cancer resection using the DIXON technique in a patient with locally advanced rectal cancer. Key technical feature includes total mesorectal excision and colo-rectal anastomosis.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Rectal cancer
**Intraoperative:** Rectal cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a 2cm ulcerative mass in the rectum below the peritoneal reflection. No significant abnormalities were found in the liver, stomach, small intestine, remaining colon, abdomen, or pelvis.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia achieved, patient placed in lithotomy position, and routine disinfection and draping performed.
2. Midline incision below the navel, layers entered, and findings as described above.
3. Small intestine pushed upwards, rectum and pelvic peritoneum exposed, lateral peritoneum opened, sigmoid colon and rectal mesentery mobilized, and vessels at the root of the rectum and sigmoid colon transected. The rectum was mobilized distally to the peritoneal reflection and proximally to 10cm above the mass on the sigmoid colon. The ureter and iliac vessels were protected.
4. Sigmoid colon was clamped and transected with a purse-string suture, and a 28.5mm circular stapler was prepared. The rectal mass was transected 3cm below the peritoneal reflection, and the specimen was removed.
5. The rectum was dilated and irrigated, and a 28.5mm circular stapler was used for end-to-end anastomosis with muscular layer reinforcement. The posterior peritoneum and lateral peritoneum were closed.
6. Large volumes of normal saline were irrigated until clear, hemostasis confirmed, and two negative pressure tubes placed behind the anastomosis, exiting through the incision.
7. Instruments and sponges counted and found to be correct, and the incision was closed in layers. The procedure was completed.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included protecting the ureter and iliac vessels during mobilization and ensuring proper anastomosis technique.

## Teaching Points
- Proper mobilization and transection of the rectum and sigmoid colon
- Protection of critical structures such as the ureter and iliac vessels
- Technique of colo-rectal anastomosis with circular stapler

## Complication Prevention
- Careful mobilization to avoid injury to critical structures
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage
- Proper irrigation and anastomotic technique to reduce the risk of anastomotic leakage

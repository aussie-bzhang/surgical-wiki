## Overview
Small bowel resection for tumor and adhesiolysis due to postoperative intestinal obstruction and liver metastasis from colorectal cancer.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Colorectal cancer with postoperative liver metastasis, acute intestinal obstruction
**Intraoperative:** Complete intestinal obstruction, small bowel tumor, adhesions, postoperative liver metastasis from colorectal cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Severe abdominal adhesions, multiple angulations below the original incision, and a solid mass at the distal ileum (about 50 cm from the ileocecal junction), causing complete intestinal obstruction. The mass invades the abdominal wall in the right iliac fossa. The proximal bowel is extremely distended and edematous. A mass is palpable in the right lobe of the liver. No abnormalities are found in the entire colon. Pelvic examination is negative.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, routine disinfection and draping, enter peritoneum under local anesthesia after seeing a band below the umbilicus, switch to general anesthesia;
2. Midline incision around the umbilicus, layer by layer enter peritoneum, findings as described above;
3. Release abdominal adhesions;
4. Clamp, cut, and ligate the mesentery 10 cm from the tumor;
5. Remove the small bowel with the specimen and send for pathology;
6. Clamp the proximal small bowel with a purse-string suture and insert the mushroom head of the tube-shaped anastomosis stapler for anastomosis;
7. Insert the tube-shaped anastomosis stapler into the distal small bowel to complete the end-to-side anastomosis, close the distal small bowel end with a clamp, and suture the anastomosis and residual end with interrupted full-thickness sutures for reinforcement;
8. Carefully inspect for bleeding from mesenteric vessels and close the tear in the mesentery;
9. Adequate hemostasis, place a negative pressure drain in the abdomen, count instruments and sponges, close the abdomen in layers, and the operation is completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: ileum, ileocecal junction, mesentery. (回肠，回盲部，系膜)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in adhesiolysis and management of mesenteric tear.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of thorough adhesiolysis and identification of critical structures;
- Proper handling of mesenteric tears to prevent bleeding and injury to mesenteric vessels.

## Complication Prevention
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage;
- Proper placement of drains to prevent postoperative infection and abscess formation.

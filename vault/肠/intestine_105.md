## Overview
Colorectal cancer resection with Hartmann procedure and complex adhesion release for obstructive symptoms.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Intestinal obstruction
**Intraoperative:** Descending colon cancer with intestinal obstruction
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings: 4*3cm mass at the proximal descending colon near the spleen flexure, infiltrative stenotic type, involving the serosa and adherent to the lateral abdominal wall. Multiple enlarged lymph nodes in the mesentery root. The tumor caused complete obstruction of the intestinal lumen, with marked distension and edema of the proximal colon and small intestine. Mesenteric vessels were varicose. Negative exploration of omentum and pelvis. Severe adhesions in the upper right abdomen, preventing liver exploration.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia achieved, patient placed in supine position, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline incision, layers opened, hemostasis and skin protection.
3. Exploration of abdominal cavity as described above.
4. Careful dissection of abdominal adhesions, avoiding injury to the intestinal wall and spleen.
5. Enteroclysis at the anterior wall of the proximal transverse colon before the spleen flexure, aspiration of a large amount of gas and feces.
6. Ligature and transection of the left branch of the middle transverse colon vessels, splenocolic ligament, descending colon vessels, and the first branch of the sigmoid colon vessels, transecting the bowel with a clamp at a sufficient distance from the tumor edge (5.0cm).
7. Transection of the bowel at the middle of the transverse colon, removal of specimen for pathological examination, meticulous hemostasis in the abdominal cavity, thorough rinsing with normal saline, count of sponges and instruments confirmed, placement of two anti-adhesion patches in the abdominal cavity, and two negative pressure drains placed in the left abdomen.
8. Layered closure of the abdomen, colostomy performed in the lower left quadrant.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: descending colon, spleen flexure, mesentery, lymph nodes. (胆总管)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered: severe adhesions, varicose mesenteric vessels.

## Teaching Points
- Careful dissection to avoid injury to adjacent structures.
- Adequate distance for bowel transection to ensure negative margin.
- Proper hemostasis and irrigation.

## Complication Prevention
- Preventing postoperative infection and ileus.

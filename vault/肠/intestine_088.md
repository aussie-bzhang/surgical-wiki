## Overview
Rectal cancer resection using the DIXON technique in a patient with significant adhesions and a mid-to-low rectal ulcer.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Sigmoid colon cancer
**Intraoperative:** Rectal cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Significant adhesions between the small intestine, lateral peritoneum, sigmoid colon, and rectum. A 0.6cm ulcer in the mid-to-low rectum with white coating and irregular surface. Another 0.2cm ulcer in the distal segment of the rectum.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia achieved, patient placed in lithotomy position, and routine disinfection and draping performed.
2. Midline incision below the navel, layers entered, and findings as described above.
3. Careful dissection of adhesions between the small intestine, lateral peritoneum, sigmoid colon, and rectum.
4. Small intestine retracted superiorly, exposing the rectum and pelvic peritoneum. Lateral peritoneum opened, sigmoid colon and rectal mesentery mobilized, and vessels at the root of the rectum and sigmoid colon transected. Mesentery distally separated to the peritoneal reflection and proximally to 20cm above the peritoneal reflection at the sigmoid colon. Care taken to protect the ureter and iliac vessels.
5. Anterior wall of the rectosigmoid junction opened, tumor location explored.
6. Sigmoid colon clamped and transected 5cm above the exploration site with a purse-string suture and 31# anvil of a stapler loaded.
7. Rectum clamped and transected 10cm below the exploration site, specimen removed, and exploration as described above. 0.2cm ulcer seen in the distal rectum, and rectum transected 3cm beyond this ulcer.
8. Rectal dilation and irrigation, and 31# stapler used for end-to-end anastomosis.
9. Mesenteric defect closed, two negative-pressure drains placed in the pelvic floor, and incision sites brought together.
10. Irrigation, hemostasis achieved, instruments and sponges counted, and incisions closed in layers. Surgery completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: rectum, sigmoid colon, ileocecal junction. Danger zones: vessels at the root of the rectum and sigmoid colon. Structures requiring protection: ureter and iliac vessels.
> e.g. common bile duct (胆总管)

## Overview
Rectal cancer recurrence; resection of recurrent tumor at DIXON anastomosis, invasion of pelvic floor, pre-sacral space, and prostate; right ureter involved; cholecystectomy; colonic stoma creation.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Rectal cancer recurrence
**Intraoperative:** Rectal cancer recurrence
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Recurrent tumor at DIXON anastomosis, invasion of pelvic floor, pre-sacral space, and prostate; right ureter involved; cholecystolithiasis; mild adhesions in abdominal cavity; no metastasis to omentum, liver, or mesentery; extremely difficult surgery; right ureter resected and urinary surgery team called for ureteral anastomosis (D-J tube placed).

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia; routine disinfection and draping.
2. Incision along the original lower midline; incision of scar and entry into peritoneal cavity; hemostasis and skin protection; findings as described above.
3. Carefully dissect adhesions; push small intestine upwards; expose rectum and pelvic peritoneum; ligate and divide blood vessels around the distal rectum; divide lateral ligaments of rectum;锐性分离骶前间隙直至尾骨尖，充分游离直肠前壁至深部；
4. Pouch suture of anal opening; elliptical incision centered on anus; incise skin and subcutaneous fat; remove fat tissue in the ischiorectal fossa.
5. Expose and divide puborectalis muscle; pull rectum downwards; dissect and divide the connection between the urogenital diaphragm and the anterior rectal wall; resect the tumor-involved segment of the ureter; pull rectal specimen out through the perineal wound; remove specimen; urinary surgery team called for ureteral anastomosis (D-J tube placed).
6. Irrigation; hemostasis; two negative pressure drains placed in the sacral area; brought out through the perineal wound adjacent to the incision; one drain placed in the right colonic fossa at the site of ureterovesical anastomosis.
7. Reconstruction of pelvic peritoneum; circular incision in the left lower abdomen; incise to peritoneum; pull out the proximal sigmoid colon for stoma creation.
8. Count instruments and sponges; close abdomen in layers; surgery completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: rectum, prostate, ureter, common bile duct; danger zones: pelvic floor, pre-sacral space; structures requiring protection: blood vessels around the rectum, ureter.

## Technical Challenges
Recurrent tumor invasion of multiple structures, including the right ureter; difficulty in dissection and anastomosis.

## Teaching Points
- Management of recurrent rectal cancer with involvement of adjacent structures.
- Technical aspects of ureteral resection and anastomosis.
- Importance of meticulous hemostasis and negative pressure drainage.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful dissection to avoid injury to adjacent structures.
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper placement of drains to prevent infection and abscess formation.

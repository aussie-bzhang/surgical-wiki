## Overview
Rectal resection (Dixon) for rectal cancer, cholecystectomy for gallstones, and appendectomy for chronic appendicitis.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Rectal mass at the rectosigmoid junction, gallstones.
**Intraoperative:** Rectal cancer, gallstones, chronic appendicitis.
**Pathological:** Not documented.

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings: 3.5cm*3cm cauliflower-like mass 2cm above the rectovesical pouch, mucosa irregular, not extending beyond the serosa. Gallbladder 8*6*4cm with numerous 1.0-2.0cm stones. Gallbladder duct 0.3cm, common bile duct 0.6cm. Appendix located at the lower part of the cecum, congested and edematous, 7.0cm long, 0.8cm diameter.

## Operative Steps
1. Position: lithotomy, general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Mid-lower midline incision around the umbilicus, enter peritoneum layer by layer, intraoperative findings as described.
3. Dissect the胆囊三角, ligate and divide the gallbladder vessels and cystic duct, dissect the gallbladder from the liver bed, remove the specimen.
4. Push the small intestine upwards, expose the rectum and pelvic peritoneum, open the lateral peritoneum, mobilize the rectal mesentery, divide the rectal vessels near the colon, divide the mesentery, mobilize distally to the peritoneal reflexion below, proximally to the sigmoid colon, pay attention to the course of the ureter and iliac vessels.
5. Divide the proximal and distal colon, place the 31F circular stapler head in the proximal colon, remove the specimen, dilate the anal canal, place the 31F circular stapler through the anus for colo-colonic anastomosis, reinforce the anastomosis with suture.
6. Elevate the appendix, ligate the appendiceal mesentery to the root of the appendix, divide the appendix 0.5cm from the root, remove the specimen, ligate the root of the appendix, cauterize the stump with electrocautery, add a ligature.
7. Close the mesenteric defect, place two negative pressure drains in the pelvic floor, one in the Winslow hole of the lesser omentum, puncture and lead out.
8. Irrigation, hemostasis, count instruments and sponges, close the incision, operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: rectum, gallbladder, appendix. Common bile duct (胆总管).

## Teaching Points
- Importance of identifying and protecting the ureter and iliac vessels during rectal mobilization.
- Proper technique for using the circular stapler in colo-colonic anastomosis.
- Management of appendiceal stump to prevent postoperative appendiceal abscess.

## Complication Prevention
- Preventing anastomotic leakage by ensuring proper tension and technique during anastomosis.
- Preventing postoperative ileus by ensuring adequate decompression of the small bowel.

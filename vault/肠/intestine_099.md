## Overview
Rectal resection (DIXON) for rectal cancer, involving resection of the rectum and sigmoid colon, with anastomosis of the remaining colon.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Rectal cancer.
**Intraoperative:** Rectal cancer.
**Pathological:** Not documented.

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located at the rectal peritoneal fold, approximately 5×5cm in size, circumferential, ulcerative. No abnormalities found in liver, pelvis, or small intestine.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Midline incision below the navel, enter peritoneum layer by layer.
2. Protect bladder, push small intestine upwards, protect with gauze. Expose cecum, rectum, and pelvic peritoneum.
3. Reflect sigmoid colon to the right, open left lateral peritoneum, cut lateral peritoneum along the left border of descending colon near the splenic flexure, and dissect lateral peritoneum downward to the peritoneal fold below, protecting the left ureter.
4. Reflect sigmoid colon to the left, open sigmoid-rectal mesentery and right rectal lateral peritoneum, find sigmoid colon vessels and superior rectal vessels, ligate and cut at the root, and remove lymph nodes at the root.
5. Cut rectum at the junction of sigmoid colon and rectum, place the proximal sigmoid colon into a 29mm anastomosis stapler for use. Dissect rectal mesentery anterior to the sacrum, separate distally to 5cm below the peritoneal fold, and dissect downward through the sacral pre-gap to the pelvic floor, cutting the left and right ligaments, paying attention to the course of the ureter and iliac vessels, and separating forward.
6. Cut rectum 3.0cm below the tumor with a large right-angle clamp, remove the specimen, dilate and flush, and place a 29mm tubular stapler through the anus for colo-colonic anastomosis, reinforcing the anastomosis with silk thread.
7. Close the mesenteric opening, place two negative pressure drains peritoneally extraperitoneally at the pelvic floor. Flush, perfect hemostasis, count instruments and gauze, close the incision one by one, and close the operation.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: rectum, sigmoid colon, ileocecal junction, peritoneum, vessels, lymph nodes. (胆总管)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in protecting the ureter and iliac vessels during dissection.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of protecting the ureter and iliac vessels during dissection.
- Proper handling of the sigmoid colon and rectum during resection and anastomosis.

## Complication Prevention
- Preventing anastomotic leakage by ensuring proper hemostasis and anastomotic technique.

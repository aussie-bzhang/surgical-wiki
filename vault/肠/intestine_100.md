## Overview
Right hemicolectomy for colon cancer; intraoperative colonoscopy for adenoma removal in sigmoid and rectum.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Colon tumor in ascending colon; polyps in descending and sigmoid colon.
**Intraoperative:** Colon cancer in ascending colon; adenomas in descending and sigmoid colon.
**Pathological:** Tubular adenoma in descending and sigmoid colon with atypical epithelial hyperplasia, negative margins.

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings: 2cm diameter tumor in ascending colon 5cm from ileocecal junction, polyp 1*1cm at rectosigmoid junction, and polyp 3*3cm in descending colon. No abnormalities found in liver, pancreas, stomach, or small intestine.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline incision around the navel, enter peritoneum layer by layer.
3. Dissection of gastrocolic ligament along the greater curvature of the stomach, division of the right omentum, mobilization of the transverse mesocolon, division of the hepatocolic ligament, separation of the colon from the duodenum, mobilization of the hepatic flexure of the colon.
4. Opening of the right paracolic fascia, mobilization of the mesocolon of the ascending colon, protection of the ureter, mobilization of the ileocecal junction and terminal ileum.
5. Division of the right branch of the middle colic vessels, ileocolic vessels, and right colic vessels at their roots, division of the mesocolon, division of the ileum 30cm from the ileocecal junction, division of the colon at the middle of the transverse colon, and removal of the specimen.
6. Placement of the anvil of the stapler in the terminal ileum, side-to-side anastomosis of ileum and colon with the circular stapler, closure of the residual transverse colon with the clip applier, closure of the mesenteric defect.
7. Intraoperative colonoscopy for removal of two polyps at 10cm and 45cm from the anus, sent for frozen pathology.
8. Irrigation, hemostasis, count of instruments and sponges, placement of two negative pressure drainage tubes in the right paracolic gutter of the pelvis, closure of the incision, completion of the operation.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: ileocecal junction, mesocolon, ureter, ileocolic vessels, right colic vessels, middle colic vessels, gastrocolic ligament. (胆总管)
> Danger zones: ureter, ileocolic vessels, right colic vessels.
> Structures requiring protection: ureter, ileocolic vessels, right colic vessels.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of accurate preoperative diagnosis and planning.
- Technique of colonoscopy during surgery for adenoma removal.
- Proper hemostasis and anastomotic technique.

## Complication Prevention
- Prevention of anastomotic leakage: Adequate anastomotic tension, meticulous hemostasis.
- Prevention of bowel obstruction: Proper mobilization and division of the colon.

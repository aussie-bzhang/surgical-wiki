## Overview
Low anterior resection (LAR) with DIXON technique and cholecystectomy for rectal cancer and cholecystolithiasis, highlighting the key technical aspects of the procedure.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Rectal cancer, cholecystolithiasis
**Intraoperative:** Rectal cancer, cholecystolithiasis
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located 5 cm from anal margin, diameter about 3 cm, cauliflower type, not involving serosa. Liver and pelvic organs negative. Gallbladder 7*4*3 cm3, containing multiple stones of 0.5-1 cm in diameter. Common bile duct diameter 0.5 cm, cystic duct diameter 0.2 cm.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline incision below the navel, enter peritoneum layer by layer, findings as described.
3. Dissect the胆囊三角, ligate and divide the cystic artery and cystic duct, dissect the gallbladder from the liver bed, remove the specimen.
4. Push the small intestine upwards, expose the rectum and pelvic peritoneum, open the lateral peritoneum, mobilize the rectal mesentery, divide the rectal vessels at the root, ligate and divide the rectal mesentery, mobilize distally to 3 cm below the tumor and proximally to 10 cm above the tumor, paying attention to the course of the ureter and iliac vessels.
5. Divide the proximal and distal rectum, remove the specimen, dilate the anal canal, place a 31F circular stapler through the anus for colo-colonic anastomosis, reinforce the anastomosis with 3-0 gastrointestinal suture on the serosal layer of the anastomotic orifice.
6. Close the mesenteric defect, place two negative pressure drains at the pelvic floor, one under the liver, and another through a stab wound.
7. Irrigation, hemostasis, count instruments and sponges, close the incision layer by layer, operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: rectum, common bile duct, ileum. (直肠，胆总管，回肠)

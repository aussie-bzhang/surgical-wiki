## Overview
Colonic perforation repair is a procedure performed for the treatment of colonic perforation, typically due to acute diverticulitis or other causes. The key technical feature involves identifying the perforation site and performing a secure repair.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Acute peritonitis, gastrointestinal perforation
**Intraoperative:** Perforation at the junction of the descending and sigmoid colon
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed adhesions at the junction of the descending and sigmoid colon, with an angular deformity. A perforation was observed on the anterior wall, measuring approximately 0.6 cm in diameter. No other significant abnormalities were noted in the small intestine, colon, or rectum.

## Operative Steps
1. Place the patient in a supine position under general anesthesia and perform routine disinfection and draping.
2. Make a midline incision and enter the abdomen layer by layer. Explore the abdomen as described.
3. Perform a secure repair of the colonic perforation using antibacterial micro乔sutures, with interrupted full-thickness sutures and additional serosal inversion sutures.
4. Irrigate the abdominal cavity with 5000 ml of normal saline.
6. Secure hemostasis, place two negative-pressure drains, and close the abdomen.
7. Procedure completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the descending colon and sigmoid colon. The common bile duct (胆总管) should be protected during the procedure.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included navigating through adhesions and ensuring a secure repair of the perforation.

## Teaching Points
- Identification and repair of colonic perforation
- Management of adhesions and angular deformities
- Importance of securing hemostasis and proper drain placement

## Complication Prevention
- Proper identification and repair of the perforation
- Adequate irrigation of the abdominal cavity
- Secure hemostasis and appropriate drain placement

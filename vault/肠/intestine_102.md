## Overview
Colorectal cancer resection with colostomy, a surgical procedure for the treatment of colorectal cancer, involving resection of the affected colon and creation of a colostomy.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Intestinal obstruction, pelvic mass, renal cyst
**Intraoperative:** Colonic adenocarcinoma at the splenic flexure, intestinal obstruction, pelvic mass, renal cyst
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located at the proximal part of the transverse colon, approximately 6cm in diameter, hard, poorly demarcated, infiltrative ulcerative type, penetrating the serosa, causing complete intestinal obstruction. Distension of the proximal colon and small intestine is marked, with edema of the bowel wall, containing about 2000ml of feces, 200ml of ascites. Exploration of liver, spleen, omentum, mesentery, small intestine, remaining colon, peritoneum, and pelvis all negative.

## Operative Steps
1. After successful induction of general anesthesia, the patient is placed in a supine position, and the abdomen is prepared with routine disinfection and draping.
2. A midupper midline incision is made, and the abdomen is entered in layers, hemostasis is performed, and the skin is protected. Intraoperative exploration is as described above.
3. The gastrocolic ligament is separated, the anterior leaf of the mesocolon is excised, the left half of the omentum is excised, the mesocolon of the transverse colon is mobilized, the lateral peritoneum of the left colon is opened, the splenocolic ligament is transected, and the splenic flexure of the colon is mobilized.
4. The mesocolon is separated and transected one by one, the left branch of the middle colic artery is transected and ligated, the distal part is separated to 6cm below the tumor, and the proximal part is mobilized to 6cm above the tumor.
5. The proximal and distal ends of the colon are transected, and the specimen is removed.
6. A colostomy is created in the upper abdomen.
7. The abdomen is irrigated, hemostasis is completed, instruments and sponges are counted and found to be correct, and the incision is closed in layers. The operation is completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: Colon, spleen, gastrocolic ligament, mesocolon, common bile duct (胆总管)

## Teaching Points
- The importance of meticulous dissection around the splenic flexure to avoid injury to the splenic artery and vein.
- The need for careful hemostasis during the resection to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- The technique of creating a colostomy with proper orientation and anastomosis.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Adequate irrigation to reduce the risk of infection.
- Proper placement of the colostomy to ensure proper function and minimize complications.

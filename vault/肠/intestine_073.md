## Overview
Rectal cancer resection (DIXON) for locally advanced rectal cancer with significant stenosis and peritoneal involvement. Key technical feature includes total mesorectal excision and anastomosis with a circular suture reinforcement.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Rectal cancer
**Intraoperative:** Rectal cancer with significant stenosis and peritoneal involvement
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located at the proximal rectum near the peritoneal reflection, approximately 5*4cm, infiltrative stenotic type, encircling the bowel circumference causing significant narrowing, involving extraperitoneal tissue, lymph nodes in the rectal wall enlarged, multiple metastatic nodules palpable in the liver, hard in consistency. No significant abnormalities found in omentum, abdominal wall, stomach, small intestine, bile duct, and remaining colon.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Mid-lower abdominal midline incision, layer by layer enter abdomen, hemostasis and skin protection, findings as described above.
3. Push the small intestine upwards, expose the rectum and pelvic peritoneum, open the lateral peritoneum, mobilize the rectal mesentery, ligate and transect the rectal mesenteric vessels one by one, pay attention to the course of the ureter and iliac vessels and avoid injury to the ureter.
4. Mobilize the lateral peritoneum of the rectum and ligate the lateral vessels,锐性分离骶前直肠间隙, avoid injury to the pre-sacral venous plexus, transect the bowel wall 5cm away from the tumor on both sides, remove the specimen for pathological examination, place the 29# circular stapler head at the proximal end, and clamp the distal end with a purse-string suture.
5. After dilating the anal canal, place the 29# circular stapler through the anus for end-to-end anastomosis of the rectum, suture the anastomosis with interrupted full-thickness sutures for reinforcement.
6. Close the mesenteric defect and the posterior peritoneum, place two negative pressure drains through the incision in the pelvic peritoneum.
7. Rinse with large amounts of NS, ensure hemostasis, count instruments and gauze correctly.
8. Close the incision layer by layer, surgery completed.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered include significant stenosis, peritoneal involvement, and careful dissection around the ureter and iliac vessels to avoid injury.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of total mesorectal excision for rectal cancer
- Technique of circular suture reinforcement for anastomosis
- Careful dissection around critical structures such as the ureter and iliac vessels

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage
- Adequate drainage to prevent abscess formation

## Overview
Rectal cancer resection using the DIXON technique in a patient with locally advanced rectal cancer.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Rectal cancer
**Intraoperative:** Rectal cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings: A 6*4cm infiltrative mass in the rectum at the superior margin of the peritoneal reflex, involving 2/3 of the circumference of the intestine, extending beyond the serosa, adherent to part of the ileum mesentery. No abnormalities found in the liver, gallbladder, pancreas, spleen, other intestinal segments, abdomen, or pelvis.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline incision below the navel, enter peritoneum layer by layer, findings as described above.
3. Push the small intestine upwards, expose the rectum and pelvic peritoneum, open the sigmoid colon lateral peritoneum, free the mesentery, transect the inferior mesenteric vessels at the root, dissect the lymph nodes at the root, open the peritoneal reflex, dissect along the sacrum anteriorly, transect the lateral rectal ligaments on both sides, free the anterior wall beyond the seminal vesicle and prostate, free the distal part to 4cm below the mass, and the proximal part to 10cm above the mass, protecting the ureter and iliac vessels.
4. Ligate the ileocecal mesentery, transect the ileocecal appendix 0.5cm from the root, and cauterize the distal end.
5. Transect the sigmoid colon, place the proximal sigmoid colon into a 29* tube anastomosis stapler, ready for use.
6. Block the distal rectum 3cm from the tumor with a large-angle clamp, dilate the anal canal, flush the anal canal and distal rectum with 1000ml of normal saline, block the distal rectum with a large-angle clamp, transect, remove the specimen, and place a tube anastomosis stapler through the anus for rectosigmoid anastomosis, reinforce the serosa around the anastomosis site for one week.
7. Transect the mesenteric vessels involved by the tumor, resect the small intestinal segment supplied by them, and perform a full-thickness interrupted end-to-end anastomosis.
8. Close the mesenteric orifice, place two negative pressure drains through the peritoneal extraperitoneal space at the pelvic floor, and puncture the wound for drainage.
9. Flush, perfect hemostasis, count instruments and gauze, close the incision layer by layer, and close the operation.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: rectum, ileocecal junction, sigmoid colon, ileum, peritoneum, sacrum, ureter, iliac vessels. (胆总管)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered: Adhesion of the ileum mesentery, protection of the ureter and iliac vessels during dissection.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of careful dissection around the ureter and iliac vessels.
- Proper handling of the anastomosis site to prevent leakage.
- Technique for negative pressure drainage placement.

## Complication Prevention
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper anastomosis technique to prevent anastomotic leakage.

## Overview
Sigmoid colon cancer resection is a surgical procedure performed for the treatment of sigmoid colon cancer. It involves the removal of the affected sigmoid colon and surrounding structures, followed by anastomosis of the remaining colon.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Sigmoid colon cancer
**Intraoperative:** Sigmoid colon cancer confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
A sigmoid colon tumor measuring 6*5cm was found to be突破浆膜，adhering to the lateral abdominal wall, and invading the bladder wall. Exploration of the liver, gallbladder, pancreas, spleen, small intestine, and remaining colon revealed no abnormalities.

## Operative Steps
1. The patient was placed in a supine position under general anesthesia, and the abdomen was routinely disinfected and draped.
2. A midline lower abdominal incision was made, and the abdomen was entered in a layered manner. The findings were as described above.
3. The small intestine was pushed upwards to expose the sigmoid colon and pelvic peritoneum. The tumor was stripped off the abdominal wall.
4. The lateral peritoneum was opened, and the mesosigmoid was mobilized. The sigmoid colon vessels were transected at the root, and the mesosigmoid was divided, with dissection extending distally to 7cm below the peritoneal reflex above the tumor and proximally to 7cm above the tumor. The bladder wall involved by the tumor was resected, with attention to the ureter and iliac vessels.
5. The proximal and distal ends of the colon were transected, and the specimen was removed. A 28.5# anastomosis stapler was used for end-to-end anastomosis, with plication of the muscularis mucosa for reinforcement.
6. The mesenteric defect was closed.
7. Two negative pressure drains were placed in the pelvic floor and brought out through the incision.
8. The abdomen was irrigated, hemostasis was achieved, and instruments and sponges were counted. The incision was closed in layers, and the procedure was completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> The common bile duct (胆总管), ileocolic vessels, and ureters are critical structures that require protection during the procedure.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included adherence of the tumor to the lateral abdominal wall and invasion of the bladder wall.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of careful dissection to avoid injury to adjacent structures.
- The need for precise anastomosis to prevent leakage.
- The management of complex anatomical situations such as tumor invasion.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Adequate drainage to prevent abscess formation.

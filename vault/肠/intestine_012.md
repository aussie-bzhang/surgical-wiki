## Overview
Left hemicolectomy for sigmoid colon cancer, involving resection of the sigmoid colon and mesocolon, with identification and protection of key structures such as the ureter and iliac vessels.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Sigmoid colon cancer
**Intraoperative:** Sigmoid colon cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a 5cm hard mass in the mid-upper segment of the sigmoid colon, involving the serosa and encircling the bowel for 4/5 of its circumference. A 2cm hard mass was also found 10cm proximal to the primary mass, involving the serosa and encircling the bowel for half of its circumference. The mesocolon contained enlarged and fused lymph nodes. Exploration of the proximal colon, small intestine, liver, and pelvis was unremarkable.

## Operative Steps
1. Position the patient in supine position under general anesthesia, perform routine disinfection and draping.
2. Make a midline incision below the navel, enter the abdomen layer by layer, and expose the sigmoid colon and pelvic peritoneum.
3. Open the lateral peritoneum, mobilize the mesocolon of the sigmoid colon, and transect the inferior mesenteric artery at its root.
4. Transect the mesocolon of the sigmoid colon, mobilize the proximal segment to 7cm above the mass, taking care to identify and protect the ureter and iliac vessels. Mobilize the distal segment to 6cm below the mass.
5. Transect the bowel, remove the specimen, suture the bowel, and close the mesenteric defect.
6. Place two negative pressure drains in the pelvic floor.
7. Irrigate, ensure hemostasis, count instruments and sponges, and close the incision in layers.
8. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the sigmoid colon, mesocolon, mesenteric artery, ureter, and iliac vessels. (胆总管 (common bile duct), ileum (回肠), jejunum (空肠))

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included identification and protection of the ureter and iliac vessels during mobilization.

## Teaching Points
- Identification and protection of the ureter and iliac vessels are crucial to prevent complications.
- Proper mobilization of the mesocolon is essential for complete resection.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful mobilization and identification of the ureter and iliac vessels to prevent injury.
- Adequate hemostasis and meticulous surgical technique to prevent postoperative complications.

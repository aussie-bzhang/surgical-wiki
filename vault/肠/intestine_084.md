## Overview
Small bowel resection for a jejunal tumor in a patient with a clinical context of abdominal pain and weight loss. Key technical feature includes identification and resection of the tumor with preservation of the ileocecal junction.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Jejunal tumor
**Intraoperative:** Jejunal tumor
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located 8.0 cm below the ileocecal junction in the jejunum, approximately 5*3 cm in size, extruding to the serosa. Minimal intraoperative bleeding.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position with successful general anesthesia. Routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midupper midline incision. Layered opening and hemostasis.
3. Exploration as described above.
4. Dissection of the ileocecal junction, 5.0 cm distal to the tumor edge. Division of mesenteric vessels and tumor resection for pathological examination.
5. End-to-end anastomosis of the jejunum with interrupted sutures. Closure of mesenteric defect with interrupted sutures and hemostasis confirmed.
6. Counting sponges and instruments. Layered closure of the abdomen.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the jejunum and ileocecal junction. The common bile duct (胆总管) should be protected during dissection.

## Technical Challenges
Not documented

## Teaching Points
- Identification and preservation of the ileocecal junction is crucial for maintaining normal gastrointestinal function.
- Proper hemostasis and anastomotic technique are essential to prevent postoperative complications.

## Complication Prevention
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper anastomotic technique to prevent anastomotic leakage.

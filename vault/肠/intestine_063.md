## Overview
Rectal resection (DIXON) combined with cholecystectomy for rectal cancer and cholecystolithiasis. Key technical feature includes coloanal anastomosis and gallbladder removal.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Rectal cancer, cholecystolithiasis
**Intraoperative:** Rectal cancer, cholecystolithiasis
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Gallbladder measuring 7*3*3cm with a 2.0cm stone. Cholecystic duct diameter 0.2cm, common bile duct diameter 0.6cm. Rectal ulcerative mass 6*4cm at the peritoneal reflection, infiltrating the serosa. No significant abnormalities found in the remaining colon, liver, and small intestine.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline incision from the xiphoid process to the pubic symphysis, layer by layer abdominal entry.
3. Careful dissection of the胆囊三角, ligating and dividing the cystic artery and cystic duct, stripping the gallbladder from the liver bed, and removing the specimen.
4. Push the small intestine upwards, expose the rectum and pelvic peritoneum, open the lateral peritoneum, and transect the rectal vessels at the root, divide the rectum at the rectosigmoid junction, and transect the rectal mesentery.
5. Separate the distal rectum 3.0cm below the mass, remove the specimen, dilate the anal canal, and place a 31.5mm circular stapler for coloanal anastomosis.
6. Close the mesenteric defect, place two negative pressure drains in the pelvic peritoneal extraperitoneum and one under the liver Winslow's hole, exiting through the puncture wound.
7. Irrigation, hemostasis, count instruments and sponges, close the incision in layers, and the operation is completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: rectum, common bile duct. (胆总管)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in coloanal anastomosis and gallbladder removal.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of coloanal anastomosis technique.
- Careful dissection around the common bile duct to avoid injury.

## Complication Prevention
- Preventing anastomotic leakage and bile duct injury.

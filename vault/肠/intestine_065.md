## Overview
Rectal cancer resection with Hartmann procedure for acute intestinal obstruction and rectal cancer. Key technical feature includes resection of the rectum and sigmoid colon, followed by colostomy creation.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Acute intestinal obstruction, rectal cancer
**Intraoperative:** Acute intestinal obstruction, rectal cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings: 1500ml of pus in the abdominal cavity, extensive intestinal segments covered with pus, a large mass (15cm diameter) in the middle upper segment of the rectum with a 1.5cm rupture. The mass is infiltrative-ulcerative, involving the serosa, pelvic floor, and posterior uterine wall. Bilateral adnexal inflammation and adhesions, with marked edema. The mass causes complete intestinal obstruction, with significant distension and edema of the colon proximal to the ileocecal junction, thickened mesentery, and faint vascular pulsation. Exploration of liver, omentum, and remaining small intestine: negative.

## Operative Steps
1. Position the patient in supine position under general anesthesia, perform routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline incision below the navel, enter the abdominal cavity, and expose the rectum and pelvic peritoneum as described above.
3. Absorb pus, push the small intestine upwards, and expose the rectum and pelvic peritoneum. Open the lateral peritoneum, mobilize the sigmoid-rectal mesentery, transect the colon mesentery, mobilize the proximal colon to the transection site and decompress it. Clamp the distal end with a 90mm clamp, wrap with iodophor gauze for later use.钝性分离肿块与子宫后壁之粘连，远端分离至肿块下方5cm腹膜返折下部分断直肠，移去标本。远端直肠缝合关闭，注意输尿管及髂血管走向；
4. Reconstruct the pelvic peritoneum, make a circular incision in the left lower abdomen, incise to the peritoneum, and pull out the proximal sigmoid colon for colostomy creation.
5. Close the mesenteric defect, place a negative pressure drain in the pelvic floor, and puncture the incision for drainage.
6. Thoroughly irrigate the abdominal and pelvic cavities, achieve hemostasis, count instruments and sponges, close the incision, and complete the procedure.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: rectum, sigmoid colon, ileocecal junction, uterus, ovaries. Use English with Chinese in parentheses where helpful: e.g. common bile duct (胆总管).

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered: extensive adhesions, significant edema, and difficulty in identifying vascular structures.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of thorough exploration and identification of critical structures.
- The technique of colostomy creation and reconstruction of the pelvic peritoneum.
- The management of extensive adhesions and edema.

## Complication Prevention
- Preventive strategies: meticulous hemostasis, thorough irrigation, and appropriate placement of drains.

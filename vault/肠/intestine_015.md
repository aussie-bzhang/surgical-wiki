## Overview
Total colectomy with ileostomy for familial adenomatous polyposis and sigmoid colon cancer, involving extensive colonic polyps and a significant tumor.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Total colonic polyps, sigmoid colon cancer
**Intraoperative:** Familial adenomatous polyposis, sigmoid colon cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings: Sigmoid colon mass measuring approximately 4 cm, circumferential around 2/3 of the bowel circumference. Multiple polypoid lesions throughout the colon. Liver, pelvis, stomach, pancreas, gallbladder, and small intestine were negative.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline incision, enter peritoneum layer by layer.
3. Dissection of gastrocolic ligament, transection of right branch of middle colic artery, transection of hepatocolic ligament, mobilization of colic flexure.
4. Opening of lateral peritoneum of ascending colon, mobilization of mesocolon of ascending colon, protection of ureter.
5. Resection of anterior mesocolon, resection of left half of omentum, mobilization of transverse mesocolon, opening of lateral peritoneum of left colon, transection of splenocolic ligament, mobilization of splenic flexure.
6. Mobilization of small intestine superiorly, exposure of sigmoid colon and pelvic peritoneum, opening of lateral peritoneum, mobilization of sigmoid mesocolon, transection of inferior mesenteric artery at the root.
7. Transection of ileocolic artery, right colic artery, ligation of inferior mesenteric artery at the root. Transection of ileum 10 cm from ileocecal junction, transection of rectum superiorly.
8. Closure of superior rectal segment with clips and suture, ileostomy creation.
9. Irrigation, hemostasis, instrument and suture count, placement of two negative pressure drainage balls in the pelvis, closure of incision.
10. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: sigmoid colon, ileum. Common bile duct (胆总管), ureter, iliac vessels. Structures requiring protection: ureter, duodenum.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered: Extensive polypoid lesions, significant tumor, careful mobilization to avoid injury to adjacent structures.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of meticulous dissection and identification of critical structures.
- Management of extensive polypoid lesions and significant tumor.
- Technique of ileostomy creation.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Adequate irrigation to prevent infection.
- Proper placement of drains to prevent abscess formation.

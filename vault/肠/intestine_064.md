## Overview
Rectal resection (DIXON) for rectal cancer, involving resection of the rectum and sigmoid colon with coloanal anastomosis, in a patient with significant tumor involvement and no distant metastasis.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Rectal cancer
**Intraoperative:** Rectal cancer, confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located 3.0 cm above the peritoneal reflection, diameter approximately 3 cm, infiltrative ulcerative type, occupying 1/2 circumference of the intestinal lumen, involving serosa. Liver and pelvic organs negative for metastasis.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline incision below the navel, enter peritoneum layer by layer, findings as described.
3. Push the small intestine upwards, expose the rectum and pelvic peritoneum, open the lateral peritoneum, mobilize the rectal mesentery, transect the rectal vessels at the root, transect the rectal mesentery, mobilize the distal end to the subperitoneal position below the peritoneal reflection, and the proximal end to 10 cm above the tumor, paying attention to the course of the ureter and iliac vessels.
4. Transect the proximal and distal ends of the rectum, remove the specimen, dilate the anal canal, and place a 25.5 mm circular stapler through the anus for coloanal anastomosis.
5. Close the mesenteric defect, place two negative pressure drains peripherally in the pelvic peritoneum, and puncture the wound for drainage.
6. Irrigation, hemostasis, count instruments and sponges, close the incision in layers, and the operation is completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: rectum, ileocecal junction, sigmoid colon, sigmoid mesocolon, ileum, ileocolic vessels, sigmoid vessels, ureter, iliac vessels. (直肠，回盲瓣，乙状结肠，乙状结肠系膜，回肠，回结肠血管，乙状结肠血管，输尿管，髂血管)

## Technical Challenges
Managing the course of the ureter and iliac vessels during mobilization, ensuring proper coloanal anastomosis, and preventing postoperative complications.

## Teaching Points
- Proper mobilization of the rectum and sigmoid colon, including the identification and protection of critical structures.
- The technique of circular stapling for coloanal anastomosis.
- Postoperative care and management to prevent complications.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Adequate drainage to prevent pelvic abscess.
- Early mobilization to prevent venous thromboembolism.

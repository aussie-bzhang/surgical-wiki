## Overview
Sigmoid colon cancer resection for a patient with sigmoid colon cancer, involving resection of the affected sigmoid colon and adjacent structures.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Sigmoid colon cancer
**Intraoperative:** Sigmoid colon cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a 4cm hard mass in the sigmoid colon, encircling the bowel lumen, with infiltration into the serosal layer.

## Operative Steps
1. Place the patient in a supine position under general anesthesia and perform routine disinfection and draping.
2. Make a midline incision below the umbilicus, enter the abdomen layer by layer, and expose the sigmoid colon and pelvic peritoneum as described above.
3. Push the small intestine upwards, expose the sigmoid colon and pelvic peritoneum, open the lateral peritoneum, mobilize the sigmoid colon mesentery, and transect the sigmoid colon vessels at the root. Transect the sigmoid colon mesentery, mobilize the proximal end to 10cm above the mass, and place the anvil of the stapler. Mobilize the distal end to the superior margin of the rectum and transect the bowel. Remove the specimen, perform end-to-end anastomosis of the rectum and sigmoid colon through the anus, suture the seromuscular layer to reinforce the anastomosis, and pay attention to the course of the ureter and iliac vessels.
4. Close the mesenteric defect, place a negative pressure drain, one at the pelvic floor and one at the anastomosis site, and puncture the wound for drainage.
5. Irrigate, ensure hemostasis, count instruments and sponges, close the incision, and the procedure is completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the sigmoid colon, ileocecal junction, rectum, vessels, and mesentery. Pay attention to the course of the ureter and iliac vessels (输尿管及髂血管走向).

## Overview
Gastric jejunostomy is a procedure performed in advanced gastric cancer, where a gastric anastomosis is created with the jejunum to bypass the obstructed stomach. This procedure is indicated when radical resection is not feasible.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastric cancer
**Intraoperative:** Advanced gastric cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a 7*5cm mass in the gastric antrum, causing pyloric obstruction. The tumor extended beyond the serosa, invaded the hepatic flexure of the colon, the right end of the transverse colon, the mesocolon, the second segment of the duodenum, and the head of the pancreas, which was adherent to the tumor. The tumor was not amenable to radical resection. The liver and pelvis were unremarkable. Approximately 150ml of ascites was present. A biopsy of omental nodules was performed intraoperatively.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia was successful. The patient was placed in a supine position, and the abdomen was prepared with routine antisepsis and draping.
2. A midline upper abdominal incision was made, and the abdomen was entered in a layered manner, with hemostasis and skin protection.
3. The gastrocolic ligament was mobilized, and the anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon was excised. The posterior wall of the stomach was mobilized to expose the peritoneal capsule of the pancreas. A Kocher incision was performed to mobilize the second segment of the duodenum. The findings were as described above. The tumor was in an advanced stage and not amenable to radical resection. The family was informed of the findings during the intraoperative discussion.
4. At 20 cm below the Treitz ligament, the antimesenteric side of the jejunum was opened 2.0 cm, and a tube-shaped anastomosis stapler mushroom head was inserted for anastomosis.
5. The anterior wall of the stomach was opened, and a tube-shaped anastomosis stapler was inserted for a gastric jejunostomy. The anastomosis was reinforced with interrupted sutures.
6. After ensuring hemostasis, the abdomen was irrigated with NS, followed by 5-Fu diluted irrigation. The sponges and instruments were counted and found to be correct. The abdomen was closed in layers.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included the extensive involvement of the tumor, which made mobilization and anastomosis challenging. The dense adhesions to the head of the pancreas required careful dissection to avoid injury.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of careful preoperative evaluation to determine the feasibility of the procedure.
- The technique of creating a safe and secure anastomosis in a challenging anatomical environment.
- The management of complications such as leakage and obstruction.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis and irrigation to prevent leakage.
- Ensuring proper anastomotic tension to prevent obstruction.

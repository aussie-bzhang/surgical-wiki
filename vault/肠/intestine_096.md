## Overview
Colonic polypectomy for a large colonic polyp, with emphasis on the technical aspects of resection and closure.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Large colonic polyp
**Intraoperative:** Adenoma of the transverse colon
**Pathological:** Tubular-villous adenoma with moderate dysplasia, base close to the tumor

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a polypoid mass on the mesenteric side of the right half of the transverse colon, measuring 2.5 cm in diameter, growing into the lumen with a pedicle diameter of 1.0 cm. No other mass was found in the remaining colon.

## Operative Steps
1. Position the patient in supine position under general anesthesia, perform routine disinfection and draping.
2. Make a midupper abdominal midline incision, enter the abdomen layer by layer, and proceed as described above.
3. Open the colon wall along the mesocolon, resect the polyp at the base of the pedicle, suture the mucosal layer, and suture the bowel wall.
4. Place a negative pressure drain in front of the transverse colon and bring it out through the stab wound.
5. Complete hemostasis, count instruments and sponges, and close the operation.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the colon and the pedicle of the polyp. The base of the polyp is close to the bowel wall, requiring careful dissection to avoid injury.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included careful dissection around the pedicle to avoid injury to the bowel wall and vessels.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of identifying the base of the polyp and ensuring complete resection.
- The technique of suture closure of the bowel wall to prevent leaks.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Ensuring complete resection of the polyp to prevent recurrence.

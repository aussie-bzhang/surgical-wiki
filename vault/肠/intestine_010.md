## Overview
Sigmoid colon cancer resection with appendectomy and liver nodule resection. Involves excision of the sigmoid colon, appendectomy, and resection of liver nodules. Key technical feature includes safe mobilization of the sigmoid colon and ileocecal junction.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Sigmoid colon cancer
**Intraoperative:** Sigmoid colon cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a 7*6cm hard mass at the junction of the descending and sigmoid colon, encircling the bowel for one turn, extending beyond the serosa, and involving the distal ileum, cecum, and appendix. A 3cm grayish-white mass was found at the junction of segments IV and V of the liver. Pelvic examination was negative.

## Operative Steps
1. Position the patient in supine position under general anesthesia, perform routine disinfection and draping.
2. Make a midline incision below the navel, enter the abdomen layer by layer, and expose the sigmoid colon and pelvic peritoneum.
3. Mobilize the sigmoid colon mesentery and transect the inferior mesenteric artery at its root.
4. Transect the sigmoid colon mesentery, mobilize the proximal part to 7cm above the mass, and the distal part to 5cm below the mass, protecting the ureter and iliac vessels.
5. Mobilize the ileocecal junction and distal ileum closely related to the mass.
6. Transect the sigmoid colon, ileocecal junction, and appendix, remove the specimen, and anastomose the sigmoid colon with the right half colon using a stapler. Suture the distal ileum to the right half colon.
7. Close the mesenteric defect.
8. Place two negative pressure drains in the pelvic floor.
9. Irrigate, ensure hemostasis, count instruments and sponges, and close the incision in layers.
10. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the sigmoid colon, ileocecal junction, appendix, and liver. The common bile duct (胆总管) requires protection during mobilization.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included mobilization of the sigmoid colon and ileocecal junction, ensuring hemostasis, and protecting adjacent structures.

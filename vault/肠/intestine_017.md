## Overview
Colon sigmoid resection for sigmoid colon cancer with adhesion release due to acute intestinal obstruction.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Acute intestinal obstruction, post-gastric cancer surgery.
**Intraoperative:** Sigmoid colon cancer, acute intestinal obstruction.
**Pathological:** Not documented.

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings: A 6*5cm2 mass at the hepatic flexure of the transverse colon, encircling the bowel for 3/4 of its circumference, causing proximal obstruction. The mass invades the serosa externally. Exploration of liver, gallbladder, pancreas, and pelvis was negative.

## Operative Steps
1. Place the patient in a supine position under general anesthesia and perform routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline incision, enter the abdomen layer by layer, intraoperative findings as described above.
3. Bluntly separate adhesions in the upper right abdomen, separate the gastrocolic ligament, transect the right omentum, free the transverse colon mesentery, transect the hepatocolic ligament, separate the colon from the duodenum, and free the hepatic flexure of the colon.
4. Open the right parietal peritoneum, free the ascending colon mesentery, and pay attention to the course of the ureter. Free the ileocecal junction and the distal ileum.
5. Free the right colic and ileocolic arteries, and transect and ligate them, transect the colon mesentery, and transect the ileum 20cm from the ileocecal junction. Place the anvil of the stapler in the distal ileum, make an opening 10cm above the mass, and use a 28.5mm circular stapler for end-to-side anastomosis of the ileum and colon. Use a 60mm clamp to transect the colon and remove the specimen. Suture the anastomosis and residual margin with gastrointestinal suture, and close the mesenteric defect.
6. Irrigate, complete hemostasis, count instruments and sponges, close the incision layer by layer, and complete the operation.

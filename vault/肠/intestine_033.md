## Overview
Appendectomy for a right lower quadrant mass, with significant adhesions and inflammation at the ileocecal junction.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right lower quadrant mass
**Intraoperative:** Mass at ileocecal junction
**Pathological:** Suppurative inflammation at ileocecal junction

## Intraoperative Findings
Mass located at ileocecal junction, approximately 6cm in diameter, hard, and fixed. No invasion of the ureter. Severe adhesions with peritoneum and right parietal peritoneum. Frozen section showed suppurative inflammation at ileocecal junction.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline incision, enter peritoneum layer by layer, findings as described above.
3. Bluntly separate the mass from the peritoneum and right parietal peritoneum, open the right parietal peritoneum, mobilize the ascending colon mesentery, pay attention to the course of the ureter, mobilize the ileocecal junction and distal ileum.
4. Divide the ileocolic vessels, ligate at the root, divide the colon mesentery, divide the ileum 10cm from the ileocecal junction, and divide the colon about 5-7cm above the mass.
5. Perform end-to-end anastomosis of the distal ileum and colon, 3-0 gastrointestinal suture for posterior wall, interrupted full-thickness suture for anterior wall, and muscular layer reinforcement with omentum covering. Close the mesenteric defect.
6. Irrigation, hemostasis, count instruments and sponges, place a negative pressure ball drain in the right colon mesentery, close the incision.
7. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: ileocecal junction, ileum, colon. (回盲部，回肠，结肠)

## Technical Challenges
Significant adhesions and inflammation at the ileocecal junction, requiring careful mobilization and preservation of the ureter.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of careful mobilization around the ileocecal junction to avoid injury to adjacent structures.
- Proper anastomotic technique to ensure a watertight and functional anastomosis.

## Complication Prevention
- Infection prevention through meticulous surgical technique and appropriate antibiotic use.
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.

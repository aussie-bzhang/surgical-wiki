## Overview
A complex procedure involving the resection of the descending colon due to a mucosal ulcerative lesion, with adhesiolysis and intraoperative colonoscopy.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Descending colon mass
**Intraoperative:** Chronic inflammation of the descending colon
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Ulcerative lesion in the descending colon, diameter approximately 2cm, with local mucosal thickening around the lesion. No abnormalities found in the liver, small intestine, or colon. Frozen section: Chronic inflammation of the descending colon with erosion. No significant abnormalities noted during intraoperative colonoscopy up to the ileocecal junction.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia achieved, patient placed in supine position, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline upper abdominal incision made, layers entered, hemostasis achieved, and skin protected. Intraoperative findings as described.
3. Adhesions between the small intestine and the incision were dissected.
4. Mesocolon was separated and transected, the left branch of the middle colic artery ligated, and the distal colon separated down to 7-8cm below the mass, and the proximal colon freed up to 7cm above the mass. Specimen sent for frozen section.
5. The proximal and distal ends of the colon were transected, the specimen removed, and the proximal and distal ends of the colon anastomosed using 3-0 antibacterial Vicryl suture in a full-thickness end-to-end manner, with additional reinforcement and seromuscular layer suture.
6. Intraoperative colonoscopy showed no significant abnormalities.
7. The mesenteric defect was closed, and the serosa of the ascending colon was sutured in a running manner.
8. Irrigation, hemostasis achieved, instruments and sponges counted and confirmed correct, two negative pressure drainage tubes placed in the left colonic sulcus.
9. Incision closed in layers, procedure completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: descending colon, ileocecal junction. (降结肠，回盲部)

## Teaching Points
- Importance of thorough adhesiolysis and careful dissection around the lesion.
- Proper anastomotic technique and reinforcement to prevent leaks.
- The role of intraoperative colonoscopy in identifying and excluding other pathology.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis and thorough irrigation to prevent postoperative infection.
- Proper anastomotic technique to reduce the risk of anastomotic leakage.

## Overview
Colorectal cancer resection for a patient with a transverse colon tumor, presenting with significant stenosis due to tumor infiltration.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Colorectal cancer
**Intraoperative:** Colorectal cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Transverse colon tumor located in the mid-left segment, approximately 5*4cm, exophytic, extending into the extraperitoneal fibrous tissue, causing significant stenosis. No significant abnormalities found in liver, gallbladder, small intestine, or remaining colon.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline incision, enter peritoneum layer by layer, findings as described above.
3. Dissection of gastrocolic ligament, division of omentum, mobilization of transverse mesocolon, division and ligation of the branches of the middle colonic vessels supplying blood to the tumor. Mobilization of the splenic flexure of the colon.
4. Division of the colon 5cm distal and proximal to the tumor, removal of specimen.
5. Two sutures for traction at the superior and inferior margins of the two intestinal ends, 1-0 silk suture full-thickness interrupted缝合吻合口后壁, followed by full-thickness interrupted inversion缝合吻合口前壁. Reinforcement of the seromuscular layer of the anastomosis, closure of the mesenteric defect.
6. Irrigation, hemostasis, count instruments and sponges, place two negative pressure bulbs next to the anastomosis, close the incision, end of surgery.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Transverse colon (横结肠), Mesocolon (系膜), Sigmoid colon (乙状结肠)

## Technical Challenges
Not documented

## Teaching Points
- Importance of thorough mobilization and identification of the mesocolon.
- Proper anastomotic technique to prevent leakage and ensure patency.

## Complication Prevention
- Adequate hemostasis and careful anastomotic technique to prevent postoperative leakage.

## Overview
Small bowel resection for malignant mesenchymoma, with emphasis on identification and resection of the tumor and mesentery, and anastomosis of the small bowel.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Abdominal mass, possible mesenchymoma.
**Intraoperative:** Small bowel tumor, possible malignant mesenchymoma.
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located 60 cm from Treitz ligament, extending outward from the bowel wall, penetrating the serosa. Diameter 10 cm, irregular surface, rich in blood vessels. No metastases found in liver, omentum, colon, stomach, or pelvis.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline incision, enter abdomen layer by layer, findings as described above.
3. Clamp, cut, and ligate the mesentery of the small bowel, resect the small bowel 5 cm above and below the tumor, remove the specimen, and perform end-to-end anastomosis with 3-0 absorbable suture. Close the mesenteric defect.
4. Adequate hemostasis, count instruments and gauze, close abdomen.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: Small bowel, mesentery, common bile duct (胆总管)

## Technical Challenges
Managing blood supply and ensuring adequate hemostasis.

## Teaching Points
- Proper identification and resection of the tumor and mesentery.
- Technique of small bowel anastomosis.
- Importance of hemostasis and postoperative care.

## Complication Prevention
- Adequate hemostasis.
- Proper anastomosis technique to prevent leaks.

## Overview
Right hemicolectomy with left liver metastasis resection for cecal cancer with liver metastasis, highlighting the technical aspects of resection and anastomosis.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Cecal cancer
**Intraoperative:** Cecal cancer with liver metastasis
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located at ileocecal junction, ulcerative infiltrative type, diameter about 8.0cm, involving serosa outside, multiple grayish white metastatic nodules in liver, no metastasis in pelvis, no ascites.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline incision, enter peritoneum layer by layer, findings as described above.
3. Dissection of gastrocolic ligament, transection of right omentum, mobilization of transverse mesocolon, transection of hepatocolic ligament, separation of colon from duodenum, mobilization of colonic flexure.
4. Incision of right paracolic fascia, mobilization of ascending mesocolon, attention to ureteral course, mobilization of ileocecal junction and distal ileum. Transection of right branch of middle colic artery, ileocolic artery, right colic artery, ligated at the root, transection of mesocolon, transection of ileum 20cm from ileocecal junction, transection of colon at middle of transverse colon, removal of specimen. Hand-sewn ileocolic end-to-end anastomosis, closure of mesenteric defect.
5. Left liver metastasis resection and biopsy, hemostasis with 1-0 Ethicon suture in figure-of-eight pattern.
6. Irrigation, hemostasis, count instruments and gauze, place two negative pressure drainage balls in right paracolic gutter and pelvis, close incision layer by layer, end of operation.

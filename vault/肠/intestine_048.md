## Overview
Rectal cancer; preoperative diagnosis;低位DIXON resection with mesorectal excision.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Rectal cancer.
**Intraoperative:** Rectal cancer.
**Pathological:** Not documented.

## Intraoperative Findings
Pelvic floor involvement; tumor circumferential involvement of the rectum; no distant metastasis observed.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia; routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline incision; enter peritoneum; tumor located above the peritoneal reflexion, 5cm×3cm×3cm in size, infiltrative.
3. Mobilize sigmoid colon; ligate and divide inferior mesenteric vessels; clear lymph nodes at the root; open peritoneal reflexion; mobilize rectum.
4. Divide sigmoid colon; place the proximal segment in a 29* anastomosis stapler.
5. Occlude distal rectum 3cm from the tumor; irrigate with 1000ml of saline; divide and remove specimen; perform rectosigmoid anastomosis.
6. Close mesenteric defect; place two negative pressure drains in the pelvic floor; exit through the incision.
7. Irrigation; hemostasis; count instruments and sponges; close incision.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Rectum, sigmoid colon, ileocecal junction, sacrum, pelvic floor, ureter, iliac vessels. (直肠，乙状结肠，回盲部，骶骨，盆腔底部，输尿管，髂血管)

## Technical Challenges
Mobilization of the rectum; anastomosis technique.

## Teaching Points

- Technique of anastomosis and hemostasis.
- Identification and protection of critical structures.

- Infection prevention.

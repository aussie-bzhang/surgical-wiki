## Overview
Rectal cancer resection (Miles) procedure for locally advanced rectal cancer, involving extensive dissection and reconstruction.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Rectal cancer
**Intraoperative:** Rectal cancer confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located 3.0 cm from anal margin, circumferentially involving 3/4 of the bowel wall, infiltrating into the serosa and prostate. No liver or omentum involvement.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline lower abdominal incision, enter peritoneum, findings as described above.
3. Push the small intestine upwards, expose the rectum and pelvic peritoneum, open the lateral peritoneum, mobilize the transverse colon mesentery, expose and protect the ureters bilaterally, open the peritoneal reflection on the anterior rectal wall, transect the rectal vessels at the root, transect the transverse colon and rectal mesentery, transect the lateral ligaments of the rectum,锐性分离sacral pre-gap to the tip of the coccyx, fully mobilize the anterior rectal wall to the deep layer.
4. Pouch suture the anus, make an elliptical incision around the anus, incise the skin and subcutaneous fat, remove the fat tissue in the ischiorectal fossa.
5. Expose and transect the pubococcygeal ligament, transect the levator ani muscles bilaterally, pull the rectum downwards, separate and transect the connection between the urogenital diaphragm and the anterior rectal wall, pull the rectal specimen out through the perineal wound, remove the specimen.
6. Irrigation, hemostasis, place two negative pressure drains in the sacrum, lead out through the perineal wound, close the wound in layers.
7. Reconstruction of the pelvic peritoneum, make a circular incision in the left lower abdomen, incise to the peritoneum, pull out the proximal sigmoid colon for colostomy.
8. Count instruments and sponges, close the abdomen in layers, end of operation.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: rectum, prostate, ureters, sacrum. (直肠，前列腺，输尿管，骶骨)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in extensive mobilization and reconstruction, managed with careful dissection and meticulous hemostasis.

## Teaching Points
- Extensive mobilization and reconstruction techniques
- Importance of preserving ureters and preserving pelvic floor function

## Complication Prevention
- Infection prevention: thorough irrigation and hemostasis
- Bowel injury prevention: careful dissection and use of bowel protectors

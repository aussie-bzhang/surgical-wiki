## Overview
Partial resection of the ileum, cecum, and ascending colon with ileocolic anastomosis for acute small bowel obstruction with ileal intussusception and ischemic necrosis.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Abdominal pain of unknown etiology
**Intraoperative:** Acute small bowel obstruction, ileal intussusception, ischemic necrosis of part of the ileum
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings: Approximately 1500ml of hemoperitoneum, 150cm of black necrotic ileum from the distal ileum to the ileocecal junction, a 6*3cm ileocecal hernia with a pedicle, the ileum herniated into the pedicle, partial resection of the distal ileum and ascending colon, resection of approximately 120cm of necrotic ileum, ileocolic end-to-end anastomosis.

## Operative Steps
1. Place the patient in supine position under general anesthesia, perform routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline incision, enter peritoneum layer by layer, intraoperative findings as described.
3. Cut the pedicle, relieve obstruction, open the ileal mesentery, cut the terminal ileum vessels, cut the necrotic ileum, open the lateral peritoneum, mobilize the cecum mesentery, cut the cecum vessels, mobilize to the ascending colon, remove the specimen, perform ileocolic end-to-side anastomosis, close the distal end, suture the seromuscular layer.
4. Close the mesenteric defect, place two negative pressure drains at the pelvic floor and WISLOW hole, puncture and lead out.
5. Irrigation, hemostasis, count instruments and gauze, close the incision layer by layer, operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: ileum, cecum, ascending colon. (回肠，盲肠，升结肠)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered: Necrotic bowel resection, ileocolic anastomosis.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of early recognition of ileal intussusception and ischemia.
- Technique of ileocolic anastomosis in the presence of significant adhesions and hemoperitoneum.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper hemostasis and anastomotic technique to prevent postoperative leakage.
- Adequate drainage to prevent abscess formation.

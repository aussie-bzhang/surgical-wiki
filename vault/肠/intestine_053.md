## Overview
Right hemicolectomy for right colon cancer, involving resection of the ileocecal junction, right colon, and mesentery. Key technical feature includes lymph node dissection and anastomosis.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right colon cancer
**Intraoperative:** Right colon cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located at the ileocecal junction, approximately 3cm in diameter, ulcerative infiltrative type, encircling the colon. No abnormalities found in liver and pelvic examination.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Lateral midline incision, enter peritoneum layer by layer, findings as described above.
3. Dissection of gastrocolic ligament, transection of the right omentum, mobilization of transverse mesocolon, transection of hepatocolic ligament, separation of colon from duodenum, mobilization of the hepatic flexure of the colon.
4. Incision of the lateral peritoneum of the right abdomen, mobilization of ascending colon mesentery, attention to the course of the ureter, mobilization of the ileocecal junction and terminal ileum.
5. Mobilization and resection of lymphatic tissue around superior mesenteric vein, ligation and transection of its small branches at the root.
6. Transection and ligation of right branch of middle colonic artery, ileocolic artery, and right colonic artery, respectively, transection and ligation of colonic mesentery, transection of ileum 20cm from the ileocecal junction, transection of transverse colon at the middle, and removal of specimen.
7. Anastomosis of ileum and transverse colon with a circular stapler, closure of mesenteric defect.
8. Irrigation, hemostasis, count instruments and sponges, leave two negative pressure balls, close the incision in layers.
9. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the ileocecal junction, right colon, mesentery, and vessels. The common bile duct (胆总管) and ureter (输尿管) should be protected during dissection.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered include mobilization of the ileocecal junction and mesentery, and anastomosis technique.

## Teaching Points
- Proper mobilization and resection of the ileocecal junction and mesentery
- Technique of circular stapler anastomosis
- Importance of lymph node dissection

## Overview
Colorectal resection for sigmoid colon cancer with liver metastasis, involving a mid-segment resection.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Sigmoid colon cancer with liver metastasis.
**Intraoperative:** Confirmed sigmoid colon cancer with liver metastasis.
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
A 7×5cm mass in the mid-segment of the sigmoid colon, circumferential, involving the serosa. Multiple gray-white nodules of varying sizes are palpable on the liver. No other abnormalities found in the remaining colon.

## Operative Steps
1. Position the patient in supine position under general anesthesia. Perform routine disinfection and draping.
2. Make a midline incision below the umbilicus, and enter the abdomen layer by layer.
3. Protect the bladder, push the small intestine upwards, and protect it with gauze.
4. Expose the sigmoid colon and rectum. Turn the sigmoid colon to the right, open the left parietal peritoneum, and cut the parietal peritoneum along the left border of the descending colon near the splenic flexure.
5. Continue to dissect the parietal peritoneum downstream to the peritoneal reflexion, being careful to protect the left ureter.
6. Turn the sigmoid colon to the left, open the sigmoid-rectal mesentery and the right rectal parietal peritoneum, locate the sigmoid colon vessels, ligate and cut them at the root.
7. Cut the sigmoid colon 3cm distal and proximal to the tumor, remove the specimen.
8. Clean the colon ends, suspend both ends with silk thread to ensure no torsion.
9. Perform end-to-end anastomosis with 3-0 gastrointestinal suture, reinforce with a layer of seromuscular suture, and cover the anastomosis with the colon pouch.
10. Stop bleeding, close the mesenteric opening, place two negative pressure drains peripherally around the anastomosis.
11. Irrigate, ensure hemostasis, count instruments and sponges, close the incision, and complete the procedure.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Sigmoid colon, liver, left ureter. (乙状结肠，肝脏，左侧输尿管)

## Technical Challenges
Not documented

## Teaching Points
- Proper identification and protection of the left ureter during dissection.
- Accurate measurement of the distance from the tumor for resection.
- Technique of sigmoid colon-rectum anastomosis.

## Complication Prevention
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper placement of drains to prevent anastomotic leakage.

## Overview
Right hemicolectomy for ileocecal valve obstruction with possible inflammatory, polypoid, or tuberculous etiology.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Intestinal obstruction
**Intraoperative:** Ileocecal valve obstruction (inflammatory, polypoid, or tuberculous)
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings: 300ml of pale yellow ascites in the abdominal cavity, narrowing at the ileocecal valve where the distal small intestine enters the cecum, thickened and hard bowel wall, complete obstruction, large amount of intestinal contents unable to pass, extreme distension of the small intestine, no distension of the ascending colon, multiple lymph nodes in the mesentery of the small intestine, no abnormalities in the liver, stomach, and other parts of the colon. Postoperative opening of the narrowing showed an extremely swollen ileocecal valve with multiple 1-2mm polyps nearby, and thickened local bowel wall.

## Operative Steps
1. Place the patient in a supine position under general anesthesia and perform routine disinfection and draping.
2. Make a midline incision, enter the abdomen layer by layer, and observe the findings as described above.
3. Open the right paracolic fascia, mobilize the mesentery of the ascending colon, and pay attention to the course of the ureter. Mobilize the ileocecal region and the distal ileum.
4. Separate the gastrocolic and hepatocolic ligaments, separate the colon from the duodenum, and separate the ascending colon from the parietal peritoneum, mobilizing the hepatic flexure of the colon.
5. Divide the right branch of the middle colic artery, ileocolic artery, and right colic artery, ligate them at the root, and divide the distal ileum mesentery.
6. Perform a side-to-side anastomosis with a side-to-side stapler at 20cm from the ileocecal valve and the middle of the transverse colon, close the distal end with a side-to-side stapler, remove the specimen, reinforce the anastomosis, and close the mesenteric defect.
7. Irrigate, ensure hemostasis, count instruments and sponges, place two negative pressure drainage balls in the right colonic fossa and pelvic cavity, close the incision layer by layer, and complete the operation.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: ileocecal valve (回盲瓣), ascending colon (升结肠), sigmoid colon (乙状结肠). Pay attention to the course of the ureter and the protection of the ileocecal valve.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered: extreme distension of the small intestine, which required careful mobilization and division of the mesentery.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of careful mobilization around the ileocecal valve to avoid injury.
- The need for meticulous hemostasis during the dissection and anastomosis.
- The technique of using a side-to-side stapler for anastomosis.

## Complication Prevention
- Preventing postoperative ileus by ensuring proper bowel preparation and gentle handling of the bowel during surgery.
- Preventing anastomotic leakage by ensuring adequate anastomotic tension and reinforcement.

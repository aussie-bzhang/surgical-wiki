## Overview
Right hemicolectomy for right colon cancer, with emphasis on anatomical landmarks and technical steps.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right colon cancer
**Intraoperative:** Right colon cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located in cecum, approximately 5*5cm2, extending beyond serosa. Mesenteric superior vena cava with enlarged lymph nodes palpable. No abnormalities found in liver, stomach, or pelvis.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Lateral midline incision, enter peritoneum as described.
3. Dissection of gastrocolic ligament, division of right omentum, mobilization of transverse mesocolon, division of hepatocolic ligament, separation of colon from duodenum, mobilization of hepatic flexure of colon.
4. Opening of right paracolic fascia, mobilization of ascending colon mesentery, attention to ureteral course, mobilization of ileocecal junction and terminal ileum.
5. Mobilization and resection of lymphatic tissue around superior mesenteric vein, division and ligation of small branches at the root.
6. Division and ligation of right branch of middle colonic artery, ileocolic artery, and right colonic artery, division of mesocolon, ileum at 20cm from ileocecal junction, transverse colon at middle, and removal of specimen.
7. Anastomosis of ileum and transverse colon with a 31.5# circular stapler, closure of mesenteric defect.
8. Irrigation, hemostasis, count instruments and sponges, placement of a negative pressure ball in the right paracolic gutter, closure of incision.
9. Completion of surgery.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: cecum, ileocecal junction, ileum, sigmoid colon, mesentery, lymph nodes. (胆总管)

## Technical Challenges
Not documented

## Teaching Points
- Importance of identifying and protecting critical structures during dissection.
- Proper handling of lymphatic tissue around the superior mesenteric vein.
- Technique of circular stapler anastomosis.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Adequate irrigation to reduce risk of infection.

## Overview
Right hemicolectomy for right colon cancer, with significant tumor involvement and multiple polyps in the small intestine.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right colon tumor
**Intraoperative:** Right colon cancer with multiple small intestinal polyps
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Large tumor in the ascending colon near the hepatic flexure, 15cm in diameter, infiltrative, encircling the bowel for one week, involving the serosa. Lymph nodes 1.5cm in diameter at the root of the right colonic vessels. Multiple polyps in the small intestine, 0.2-0.4cm in diameter. No abnormalities in the liver, pelvis, or stomach.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Right transverse rectus sheath incision, enter the abdomen layer by layer, findings as described above.
3. Dissection of the greater omentum along the greater curvature of the stomach, division of the right greater omentum, mobilization of the transverse mesocolon, division of the hepatocolic ligament, separation of the colon from the duodenum, mobilization of the hepatic flexure of the colon.
4. Incision of the lateral peritoneum of the right side, mobilization of the mesocolon of the ascending colon, protection of the ureter, mobilization of the ileocecal region and terminal ileum.
5. Division of the right branch of the middle colic artery, ileocolic artery, and right colic artery, ligation at the root, division of the mesocolon, division of the ileum 30cm from the ileocecal junction, division of the colon at the middle of the transverse colon, removal of the specimen.
6. Placement of the anvil of the stapler in the terminal ileum, opening a hole on the colon mesentery of the transverse colon, 28.5mm tubular stapler for ileocolic end-to-end anastomosis, closing the hole of the colon mesentery. Closing the mesenteric defect.
7. Irrigation, hemostasis, count instruments and gauze, placement of two negative pressure balls in the right colonic fossa of the pelvis, one above and one below, closing the incision one by one, completion of the operation.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: ascending colon, right colonic vessels, ileocolic vessels, ileum, appendix. (胆总管)

## Teaching Points
- Importance of protecting the ureter during mobilization of the ascending colon.
- Proper handling of the ileocolic vessels and mesocolon during resection.
- Technique of using the stapler for ileocolic anastomosis.

## Complication Prevention
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper anastomotic technique to prevent anastomotic leakage.

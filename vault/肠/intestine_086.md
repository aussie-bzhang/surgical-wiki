## Overview
Rectal polypectomy for a patient with a rectal adenoma and severe dysplasia, performed via an open approach.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Rectal mass
**Intraoperative:** Rectal adenoma with severe dysplasia
**Pathological:** Rectal adenoma with severe dysplasia, base negative

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a 4*2*1cm mucosal mass at the lower end of the rectum, with a lobulated appearance, located between 3 and 6 o'clock in prone position, 3cm from the anal margin.

## Operative Steps
1. After successful lumbar anesthesia, the patient was placed in prone position and the abdomen was routinely disinfected and draped.
2. The anal canal was dilated, and the mucosal mass was completely excised along the submucosal plane. Submucosal vessels were ligated or sutured, and the mucosa was sutured. Frozen pathology was consistent with the above findings.
3. Gauze soaked in Vaseline was packed into the anal canal for hemostasis, and the procedure was completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the rectum and anus. The common bile duct (胆总管) should be protected during the procedure.

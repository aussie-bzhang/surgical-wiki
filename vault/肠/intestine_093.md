## Overview
Laparoscopic rectal resection (DIXON) for rectal cancer, involving resection of the rectum and sigmoid colon with mesorectal excision, performed laparoscopically.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Rectal cancer
**Intraoperative:** Rectal cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located at the upper rectum, 8 cm from anal margin, approximately 5×4×2 cm, infiltrative ulcerative type, occupying 3/4 of the lumen, reaching the serosa. Multiple yellowish patchy polyps in the colon, one 0.5 cm polyp at 6 cm and another at 20 cm from anal margin.

## Operative Steps
1. Patient in lithotomy position under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping, four-port laparoscopic approach, findings as described.
2. After changing positions, the small intestine is pushed to the upper right abdomen, the rectum and pelvic peritoneum are exposed, the lateral peritoneum is opened, the mesorectum and mesosigmoid are mobilized to their roots (note the course of the ureter and iliac vessels), the mesenteric vessels are transected at the root with a linear cutter; distal mobilization to 5-6 cm below the tumor, proximal mobilization to 30 cm above the tumor. The rectum is transected 4 cm distal to the tumor.
3. A 5 cm longitudinal incision is made 2 cm above the pubis, the sigmoid colon is pulled out, the proximal colon is transected 15 cm from the tumor, the specimen is removed, and the distal stump is sutured in a purse-string fashion, the anvil of a 33 mm stapler is fixed, and it is placed back into the abdomen for use.
4. After dilating the anal canal, a 33 mm circular stapler is inserted through the anus for end-to-end anastomosis of the sigmoid colon and rectum. Two negative pressure drains are placed behind the anastomosis and brought out through the left lower abdominal puncture site.
5. Hemostasis is completed, instruments and sponges are counted and found to be correct, the incision is closed layer by layer, and the operation is completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: rectum, sigmoid colon, mesorectum, ileocecal junction. (直肠，乙状结肠，直肠系膜，回盲部)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in mobilizing the mesorectum and sigmoid colon, ensuring adequate distal margin, and performing end-to-end anastomosis.

## Teaching Points
- Proper mobilization of the mesorectum and sigmoid colon is crucial for complete resection.
- Ensuring adequate distal margin is essential for oncological safety.
- Technical skills for end-to-end anastomosis are critical for postoperative recovery.

## Complication Prevention
- Adequate distal margin to prevent local recurrence.
- Proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Early removal of drains to prevent infection.

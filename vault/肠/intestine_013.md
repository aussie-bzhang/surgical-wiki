## Overview
Colorectal cancer resection using the Dixon technique, a common approach for treating sigmoid colon cancer, involving resection of the sigmoid colon and rectum with end-to-end anastomosis.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Sigmoid colon cancer
**Intraoperative:** Sigmoid colon cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located in the sigmoid colon, approximately 20.0 cm from the anal margin, infiltrative and stenotic, causing intestinal obstruction. Lymph nodes at the root of the mesenteric vessels were palpable. No significant abnormalities were found in the stomach, small intestine, remaining colon, liver, gallbladder, or bile ducts.

## Operative Steps
1. Anesthesia achieved, patient placed in lithotomy position, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Mid-lower midline incision, enter peritoneum layer by layer, hemostasis, and skin protection. Intraoperative exploration as described.
3. Elevate the sigmoid colon, open the lateral peritoneum, identify the course of the left ureter, mobilize the sigmoid mesentery and ligate the vessels, confirm no injury to the right ureter, ligate and transect the third branch of the inferior mesenteric artery and the superior rectal artery.
4. Mobilize the sigmoid colon around the circumference to the rectum, mobilize the rectum to 5.0 cm below the tumor along the pre-sacral space, mobilize the lateral rectal ligaments on both sides, ligate and suture the vessels.
5. Place a purse-string clamp at a sufficient distance (>5.0 cm) above the tumor margin, transect the bowel, place into a 28.5# anvil of a circular stapler, purse-string suture, and wait for anastomosis.
6. Place a right-angled clamp 5.0 cm below the tumor, transect the rectum, remove the specimen, dilate the anal canal to four fingers, and rinse thoroughly. Place the circular stapler to complete end-to-end anastomosis of the bowel, reinforce the anastomosis with a suture.
7. Careful hemostasis, irrigate the pelvis with large amounts of normal saline, check again for active bleeding, place two negative pressure drainage tubes through the extraperitoneal route into the anterior and posterior aspects of the anastomosis in the pelvis, suture the sigmoid colon mesentery and posterior peritoneal defects.
8. Count sponges and instruments, close the abdomen in layers.
9. Operation completed successfully.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: sigmoid colon, ileocecal junction, rectum, mesentery, vessels. (胆总管)

## Technical Challenges

## Teaching Points
- Proper mobilization of the sigmoid colon and rectum
- Identification and protection of the ureters
- Accurate placement of the circular stapler for anastomosis

## Complication Prevention
- Adequate mobilization to avoid injury to adjacent structures
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage

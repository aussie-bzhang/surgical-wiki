## Overview
Partial resection of the small bowel for abdominal trauma with hemorrhagic shock and acute peritonitis, highlighting the technical aspects of small bowel resection and mesenteric repair.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Abdominal closed injury, hemorrhagic shock, acute peritonitis
**Intraoperative:** Small bowel rupture, retroperitoneal hematoma
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed congestion and edema of the small bowel, complete rupture of the jejunum 30 cm from the Treitz ligament, and a torn mesentery to the root without significant injury or bleeding in the mesenteric vessels. A widespread hematoma was observed in the retroperitoneum with low tension and a diameter of 3.0 cm. No significant abnormalities were found in the liver, gallbladder, pancreas, stomach, colon, or pelvis. Approximately 100 ml of blood was aspirated from the abdominal cavity.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline umbilical incision, enter the abdomen layer by layer, findings as described above.
3. Locate the ruptured jejunal segment, resect approximately 7 cm of the damaged bowel at both ends, suture with 3-0 absorbable suture in an interrupted full-thickness manner, perform jejunojejunal anastomosis, and add seromuscular layer suture.
4. Carefully inspect for no bleeding from the mesenteric vessels and close the torn mesentery.
5. Adequate hemostasis, place a negative pressure drain in the abdominal cavity, count instruments and sponges, close the abdomen in layers, and the procedure is completed.

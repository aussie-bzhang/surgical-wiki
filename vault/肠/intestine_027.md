## Overview
Posterior peritoneal tumor resection, right hemicolectomy, and cholecystectomy for a patient with a posterior peritoneal tumor, with significant involvement of the right colon and biliary tract.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Pancreatic head mass
**Intraoperative:** Posterior peritoneal tumor
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a posterior peritoneal mass measuring approximately 4-5 cm, closely related to the right hemicolectomy, duodenum, and pancreatic hook. The gallbladder was 8*4 cm in size with thickened walls. The right kidney was atrophic. Exploration of the liver, stomach, pancreas, and small intestine showed no abnormalities in the remaining colon.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline incision, layer-by-layer abdominal entry, findings as described above.
3. Careful dissection of the胆囊三角, ligating and transecting the cystic duct 0.5 cm from the common bile duct, and resecting the gallbladder.
4. Separation of the right half of the gastrocolic ligament, transection of the middle colonic vessels right branch, and transection of the hepatocolic ligament. Free the hepatic flexure of the colon.
5. Flip the right half of the colon to expose the posterior peritoneal mass and explore the mass as described.
6. Dissect the posterior peritoneal mass from the duodenum and pancreatic hook.
7. Open the ascending colon mesentery, free the mesentery of the ascending colon, and protect the ureter.
8. Transect the ileocolic vessels, right colonic vessels, and transect the ileum 15 cm from the ileocecal junction and the colon at the middle of the transverse colon. Resect the posterior peritoneal mass and right hemicolectomy.
9. Perform end-to-side anastomosis with a 28F tube anastomosis stapler, clamp the distal colon 3 cm from the anastomosis, and reinforce the anastomosis.
10. Irrigation, hemostasis, count instruments and sponges, place two negative pressure drainage tubes in the pelvic colonopexy and anastomosis site on the right side, and close the incision sequentially.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: posterior peritoneal tumor, right hemicolectomy, common bile duct (胆总管)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included close proximity of the tumor to vital structures and the need for meticulous dissection to preserve adjacent organs.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of careful dissection around the common bile duct to prevent bile duct injury.
- The need for meticulous hemostasis and anastomotic technique to prevent postoperative complications.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful dissection and protection of adjacent structures to prevent injury.
- Adequate hemostasis and proper anastomotic technique to reduce the risk of anastomotic leakage.

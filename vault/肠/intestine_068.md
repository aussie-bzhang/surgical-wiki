## Overview
Rectal cancer resection using the DIXON technique for locally advanced rectal cancer, involving total mesorectal excision and coloanal anastomosis.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Rectal cancer
**Intraoperative:** Rectal cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located at the upper rectum, diameter approximately 3cm, ulcerative infiltrative type, involving extramesenteric peritoneum, occupying nearly one circumference of the intestinal lumen, causing incomplete obstruction. No liver or pelvic involvement.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline incision below the navel, enter peritoneum layer by layer, findings as described above.
3. Push the small intestine upwards, expose the rectum and pelvic peritoneum, open the lateral peritoneum, mobilize the mesorectum, transect the rectal vessels at the root, transect the mesorectum, distal dissection to 5cm below the peritoneal reflex below the tumor, proximal dissection to 10cm above the tumor, paying attention to the course of the ureter and iliac vessels.
4. Transect the proximal and distal rectum, remove the specimen, dilate the anal canal, and place a circular stapler through the anus for coloanal anastomosis.
5. Close the mesenteric defect, place two negative pressure drains peripherally in the pelvis, and puncture the wound for drainage.
6. Irrigation, hemostasis, count instruments and sponges, close the incision layer by layer, and the operation is completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: rectum, ileocecal junction, sigmoid colon, mesorectum, vessels, lymph nodes. (直肠，回盲瓣，乙状结肠，直肠系膜，血管，淋巴结)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in mesorectal mobilization and coloanal anastomosis.

## Teaching Points
- Proper mobilization of the mesorectum to prevent lymph node metastasis.
- Accurate identification and protection of the ureter and iliac vessels during dissection.
- Technical aspects of coloanal anastomosis with circular stapler.

## Complication Prevention
- Infection prevention: thorough irrigation and hemostasis.
- Bowel obstruction prevention: proper mobilization and resection of the tumor.

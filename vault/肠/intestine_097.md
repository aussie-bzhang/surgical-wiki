## Overview
Small bowel rupture and perforation repair; abdominal trauma with gastrointestinal perforation; technical essence of suture repair of the small bowel perforation.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Abdominal closed injury, gastrointestinal perforation
**Intraoperative:** Abdominal closed injury, small bowel rupture and perforation
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Approximately 300ml of purulent fluid in the peritoneal cavity containing food residue; a 1.0cm rupture and perforation in the middle segment of the small bowel; the surface of adjacent bowel is covered with pus; multiple areas of small bowel serosa congestion without rupture, with good peristalsis; no significant injury to liver, spleen, stomach, duodenum, and large intestine upon exploration.

## Operative Steps
1. Place the patient in a supine position, under general anesthesia, and perform routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline incision, enter the abdomen layer by layer, findings as described above.
3. Suture the rupture and perforation of the small bowel in full thickness, and reinforce with serosal layer suture.
4. Rinse the peritoneal cavity with a large amount of normal saline until clean, and place a negative pressure drain in the pelvic cavity.
5. Count instruments, close the abdomen, and the procedure is completed.

## Overview
Colorectal cancer resection for a patient with intestinal obstruction due to a colonic tumor.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Colorectal tumor, intestinal obstruction
**Intraoperative:** Colorectal tumor, intestinal obstruction
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Severe abdominal adhesions, unclear粘连 between the gastrocolic ligament and mesocolon of the transverse colon. Tumor located in the transverse colon, diameter about 4.0cm, elevated type, not involving the serosa. Leading to luminal stenosis. No gallbladder or remaining intestines involved during intraoperative exploration.

## Operative Steps
1. Place the patient in a supine position under general anesthesia and perform routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline incision, enter the abdomen layer by layer, intraoperative findings as described above.
3. Separate the gastrocolic ligament, transect the omentum, free the mesocolon of the transverse colon, ligate and transect the middle colonic vessels and their branches. Free the hepatic flexure and splenic flexure of the colon.
4. Transect the colon 5cm from both ends of the tumor, remove the specimen.
5. Perform a full-thickness interrupted suture with 1-0 silk thread on the posterior wall of the anastomosis, followed by a full-thickness interrupted inversion suture on the anterior wall. Reinforce the serosal layer of the anastomosis, close the mesenteric defect.
6. Irrigate, ensure hemostasis, count instruments and sponges, place two negative pressure balls adjacent to the anastomosis, close the incision, and complete the operation.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: Colon, gastrocolic ligament, mesocolon. (胆总管)
> Danger zones: Areas of unclear adhesions.
> Structures requiring protection: Serosa of the colon.

## Technical Challenges
Severe abdominal adhesions and unclear adhesion structures.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of careful dissection to avoid injury to adjacent structures.
- Proper anastomotic technique to ensure a secure and leak-proof connection.

## Complication Prevention
- Thorough hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Adequate irrigation to reduce the risk of infection.

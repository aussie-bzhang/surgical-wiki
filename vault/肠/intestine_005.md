## Overview
Sigmoid colon resection for sigmoid colon cancer, with mesenteric lymph node dissection, in a patient with a history of sigmoid colon cancer.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Sigmoid colon cancer
**Intraoperative:** Sigmoid colon cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
The original anastomosis healed well. The sigmoid colon and pelvic peritoneum were exposed after pushing the small intestine upwards. The mesentery of the sigmoid colon was freed, the sigmoid colon vessels were cut at the root, and the mesentery was dissected distally to 7cm below the tumor and proximally to 7cm above the tumor. The ureter and iliac vessels were noted.

## Operative Steps
1. The patient was placed in a supine position under general anesthesia, and the abdomen was routinely disinfected and draped.
2. The lower abdominal incision was entered, and the abdomen was entered layer by layer. The findings were as described above.
3. The small intestine was pushed upwards to expose the sigmoid colon and pelvic peritoneum. The lateral peritoneum was opened, and the mesentery of the sigmoid colon was freed. The sigmoid colon vessels were cut at the root, and the mesentery was dissected distally to 7cm below the tumor and proximally to 7cm above the tumor, with attention to the course of the ureter and iliac vessels.
4. The proximal and distal ends of the colon were cut, and the specimen was removed. A 28.5# anastomosis stapler was used for end-to-end anastomosis, and the seromuscular layer was sutured to reinforce.
5. The mesenteric opening was closed.
6. Two negative pressure drains were placed in the pelvic floor and brought out through the puncture wound.
7. The abdomen was irrigated, hemostasis was completed, instruments and sponges were counted and found to be correct, and the incision was closed in layers. The operation was completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the sigmoid colon and mesenteric lymph nodes. The ureter and iliac vessels are important to protect during dissection. (胆总管)

## Technical Challenges
Not documented

## Teaching Points
- The importance of identifying and protecting the ureter and iliac vessels during mesenteric dissection.
- The technique of using a stapler for colonic anastomosis and the importance of reinforcing the seromuscular layer.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper placement of drains to prevent postoperative abscess formation.

## Overview
Right hemicolectomy for colorectal cancer with liver metastasis and peritoneal metastasis. Key technical feature: resection of the right colon and ileum with anastomosis.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Colorectal cancer, liver metastasis
**Intraoperative:** Colonic flexure cancer, liver metastasis, multiple peritoneal metastases
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Large mass at the right colonic flexure, ulcerative infiltrative type, approximately 8 cm in diameter, circumferential around the bowel wall. Multiple metastatic nodules in the mesentery and peritoneal side.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline umbilical incision, enter peritoneum layer by layer, findings as described above.
3. Dissection of the gastrocolic ligament, transection of the right omentum, mobilization of the transverse mesocolon, transection of the hepatocolic ligament, separation of adhesions between the colon and duodenum, mobilization of the colonic flexure.
4. Transection of the right middle colic artery, ileocolic artery, and right colic artery.
5. Incision of the right parietal peritoneum, dissection of adhesions, mobilization of the ascending colon mesentery, attention to the course of the ureter, mobilization of the ileocecal junction and terminal ileum.
6. Transection of the mesocolon, transection of the ileum 20 cm from the ileocecal junction, transection of the colon at the middle of the transverse colon to remove the specimen.
7. Anastomosis of the ileum and colon using a 28F circular stapler, suture reinforcement of the anastomosis, side-to-side anastomosis using a linear stapler, external reinforcement suture, closure of the mesenteric defect.
8. Irrigation, hemostasis, count instruments and sponges, place two negative pressure drainage balls in the right colon mesentery, close the incision sequentially, end of surgery.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: colonic flexure, liver, mesentery. (胆总管)
> Danger zones: adjacent organs and vessels.
> Structures requiring protection: ureter, small bowel.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in mobilization of the liver and mesentery, and in anastomosis.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of careful mobilization around the liver and mesentery.
- Proper handling of the ureter during mobilization.
- Technique of circular stapler anastomosis.

## Complication Prevention
- Adequate hemostasis.
- Proper anastomotic technique to prevent anastomotic leakage.
- Early detection and management of postoperative complications.

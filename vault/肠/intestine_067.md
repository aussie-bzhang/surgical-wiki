## Overview
Rectal cancer palliative resection (DIXON) for locally advanced rectal cancer with liver metastasis, key technical feature being the use of a circular stapler for anastomosis.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Rectal cancer
**Intraoperative:** Rectal cancer with multiple liver metastases
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located at the proximal rectum near the peritoneal reflexion, size approximately 5*4cm, infiltrative stenotic type, encircling the bowel lumen causing significant narrowing, extending beyond the serosa, with enlarged lymph nodes in the bowel wall. Multiple metastatic nodules palpable in the liver, firm in consistency. No significant abnormalities found in omentum, abdominal wall, stomach, small intestine, biliary tract, or remaining colon.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Mid-lower midline incision around the umbilicus, enter peritoneum layer by layer, hemostasis, and skin protection. Findings as described above.
3. Push the small intestine upwards, expose the rectum and pelvic peritoneum, open the lateral peritoneum, mobilize the rectal mesentery, ligate and transect the rectal mesenteric vessels one by one, paying attention to the course of the ureter and iliac vessels to avoid injury to the ureter.
4. Mobilize the lateral peritoneum of the rectum and ligate the vessels within,锐性分离sacral pre-rectal space, avoiding injury to the sacral venous plexus, transect the bowel wall 5cm away from the tumor on both sides, remove the specimen for pathological examination, place the head of a 29# circular stapler at the proximal end, and clamp the distal end with a purse-string suture.
5. After dilating the anal canal, insert a 29# circular stapler through the anus for end-to-end anastomosis of the rectum, suture the anastomosis with interrupted full-thickness sutures for reinforcement.
6. Close the mesenteric defect and posterior peritoneum, place two negative-pressure drains in the pelvic peritoneal extraperitoneal puncture wounds.
7. Rinse with large amounts of NS, ensure hemostasis, and count instruments and gauze correctly.
8. Close the incision layer by layer, operation completed.

## Technical Challenges
Managing the narrow lumen due to tumor encirclement, avoiding injury to the ureter and iliac vessels during dissection.

## Teaching Points
- Proper mobilization of the rectal mesentery and identification of the ureter and iliac vessels.
- Technique of using a circular stapler for anastomosis in a narrow lumen.
- Importance of hemostasis and correct placement of drains.

## Complication Prevention
- Ensuring proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Careful dissection to avoid injury to the ureter and iliac vessels.
- Proper placement of drains to prevent postoperative abscess formation.

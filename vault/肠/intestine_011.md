## Overview
Left hemicolectomy for sigmoid colon cancer, involving resection of the sigmoid colon and mesocolon with lymph node dissection.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Sigmoid colon cancer
**Intraoperative:** Sigmoid colon cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings: A 5cm hard mass in the mid-upper segment of the sigmoid colon, involving the serosa and encircling the bowel for 4/5 of its circumference. Another 2cm hard mass is seen 10cm proximal to the first, involving the serosa and encircling the bowel for half of its circumference. Enlarged and fused lymph nodes are palpable in the mesocolon. Exploration reveals no abnormalities in the proximal colon, small intestine, liver, or pelvis.

## Operative Steps
1. Position the patient in supine position under general anesthesia, perform routine disinfection and draping.
2. Make a midline incision below the navel, enter the abdomen layer by layer, and expose the sigmoid colon and pelvic peritoneum.
3. Push the small intestine upwards, open the lateral peritoneum, and mobilize the mesocolon of the sigmoid colon, cutting the sigmoid colon vessels at the root.
4. Cut the mesocolon, mobilize the proximal segment to 7cm above the mass, and place a 28.5mm anastomosis stapler at the proximal end, protecting the ureter and iliac vessels.
5. Mobilize the distal segment to 5cm below the mass below the peritoneal reflexion, and cut the distal rectum.
6. Remove the specimen, clean the distal rectum thoroughly, and place a 28.5mm tubular anastomosis stapler for end-to-end anastomosis. Suture the serosal layer of the anastomosis with interrupted sutures for reinforcement. Close the mesenteric defect.
7. Place two negative pressure drains in the pelvic floor.
8. Irrigate, ensure hemostasis, count instruments and sponges, and close the incision in layers.
9. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: Sigmoid colon, mesocolon, lymph nodes. (胆总管)

## Teaching Points
- Proper mobilization and division of the mesocolon to prevent injury to adjacent structures.
- Careful hemostasis and anastomotic technique to reduce the risk of complications.

## Complication Prevention
- Preventing anastomotic leakage by ensuring proper anastomotic technique and adequate blood supply.
- Minimizing blood loss through meticulous hemostasis.

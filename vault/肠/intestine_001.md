## Overview
Right hemicolectomy for a colonic tumor at the hepatic flexure, with significant involvement of the ileum.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Hematochezia, colorectal cancer
**Intraoperative:** Colonic tumor at the hepatic flexure
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located at the hepatic flexure of the transverse colon, approximately 8cm in diameter, circumferential, penetrating the serosa, involving the ileum 20cm from the ileocecal junction.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline incision, enter peritoneum layer by layer, findings as described above.
3. Dissection of gastrocolic ligament, division of the right omentum, mobilization of the transverse mesocolon, division of the hepatocolic ligament, separation of the colon from the duodenum, mobilization of the hepatic flexure of the colon.
4. Incision of the lateral peritoneum of the right side, mobilization of the ascending colon mesocolon, attention to the course of the ureter, mobilization of the ileocecal junction and terminal ileum.
5. Division of the right branch of the middle colonic artery, ileocolic artery, and right colonic artery, ligation at the root, division of the mesocolon, division of the ileum 50cm from the ileocecal junction, division of the colon at the middle of the transverse colon, en bloc resection of the specimen.
6. Placement of the anvil of the stapler in the terminal ileum, opening a hole on the colonic serosa of the transverse colon, 28.5mm circular stapler for ileocolic end-to-end anastomosis, closing the colonic serosa hole. Closing the mesenteric defect.
7. Irrigation, hemostasis, count instruments and sponges, place two negative pressure balls in the right colonic fossa, one above and one below, close the incision sequentially, end of surgery.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: common bile duct (胆总管), ileocecal junction (回盲部)

## Technical Challenges
Not documented

## Teaching Points
- Importance of identifying and protecting the ureter during mobilization.
- Proper technique for circular stapler anastomosis.

## Complication Prevention
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper irrigation and drainage to prevent infection.

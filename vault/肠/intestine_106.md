## Overview
Low anterior resection (Dixon) with appendectomy for rectal and cecal cancer, with significant findings during surgery.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Rectal cancer
**Intraoperative:** Rectal and cecal cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
- 8cm x 3cm x 2cm3 hard mass 8cm from anal margin, encircling the intestine.
- 3cm x 3cm2 cauliflower-like mass in cecum.
- Multiple grayish white hard nodules palpable in liver, suggestive of metastasis.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline incision below the navel, enter peritoneum, and expose the rectum and pelvic peritoneum.
3. Mobilize sigmoid colon mesentery, ligate and divide inferior mesenteric vessels, and dissect lymph nodes.
4. Open peritoneal reflex, dissect retroperitoneally along the sacrum, divide lateral rectal ligaments, mobilize anterior wall past the vaginal wall, and mobilize distally to 2-3cm below the mass, proximally to 10cm above the mass, protecting the ureter and iliac vessels.
5. Divide sigmoid colon, place the proximal segment in a 28.5mm circular stapler, and prepare.
6. Block distal rectum 3cm from the tumor with a large hemostat, irrigate with 1000ml of saline, and divide distally with a hemostat, removing the specimen. Perform rectosigmoid anastomosis with the circular stapler.
7. Mobilize ileocecal junction, divide ileocolic vessels, and divide ileum 15cm from ileocecal junction, and cecum 5cm from the tumor superiorly. Perform end-to-side anastomosis with a 28.5mm stapler.
8. Close mesenteric defect, place two negative pressure drains in the pelvic floor and one in the right colon旁沟, exiting through a stab wound.
9. Irrigate, ensure hemostasis, count instruments and sponges, and close the incision in layers.

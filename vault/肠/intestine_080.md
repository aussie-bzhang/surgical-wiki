## Overview
Rectal tumor resection using the DIXON technique, bilateral ovarian follicle cystectomy, bilateral ovary biopsy, and cholecystectomy. The procedure was performed in a patient with a rectal tumor and associated gallbladder pathology.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Rectal tumor
**Intraoperative:** Rectal tumor, bilateral ovarian follicle cysts, gallbladder stone
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
The tumor was located at the rectal peritoneal reflection, 3.0 cm in diameter, soft, pedunculated, broad-based, and not penetrating the serosa. The bowel lumen was not obstructed. Bilateral ovarian follicle cysts were present, with the left cyst measuring 3.0 cm and the right cyst measuring 1.0 cm. The gallbladder was 7*4*3 cm with a 1.5 cm stone, the cystic duct was 0.3 cm in diameter, and the common bile duct was 0.6 cm in diameter.

## Operative Steps
1. Position the patient in the lithotomy position under general anesthesia and perform routine disinfection and draping.
2. Make a mid-to-low midline incision around the umbilicus, enter the abdomen layer by layer, and expose the rectum and pelvic peritoneum as described.
3. Push the small intestine upwards, expose the rectum and pelvic floor peritoneum, open the lateral peritoneum, mobilize the rectal mesentery, and transect the rectal vessels at the root. Transect the rectal mesentery, mobilize the distal end to the peritoneal reflection below, and the proximal end to the sigmoid colon. Pay attention to the course of the ureter and iliac vessels.
4. Transect the proximal and distal ends of the colon, remove the specimen, dilate the anal canal, and place a 28.5F tube-shaped anastomosis stapler through the anus for colonic end-to-end anastomosis.
5. Irrigate, complete hemostasis, close the mesenteric defect, place two negative-pressure drains in the pelvic floor, and bring them out through the stab wounds.
6. Request the gynecologist Dr. Zhou Jie Ru to strip the bilateral ovarian follicle cysts, suture the stripping surface; take a portion of the bilateral ovary tissue for intraoperative frozen section.
7. Carefully dissect the gallbladder triangle, ligate the cystic artery; ligate the cystic duct 0.5 cm away from the common bile duct, strip the gallbladder from the gallbladder bed, remove the specimen, hemostasis of the gallbladder bed; place one negative-pressure drain in the Winslow hole of the lesser omentum, and bring it out through the stab wound.
8. Complete hemostasis, count instruments and sponges, close the incision, and the procedure is completed.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of identifying and protecting the ureter and iliac vessels during rectal mobilization.
- The technique of using a tube-shaped anastomosis stapler for colonic anastomosis.
- The careful dissection of the gallbladder triangle to avoid injury to the common bile duct.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Adequate drainage to prevent postoperative abscess formation.

## Overview
Right hemicolectomy for cecal cancer, with emphasis on identifying the ileocecal junction, resecting the right hemicolectomy, and managing the mesentery and vessels.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Cecal cancer
**Intraoperative:** Cecal cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located at the ileocecal junction, infiltrative ulcerative type, approximately 6.0 x 5.0 cm, infiltrating the serosa layer, surrounding the bowel wall for one week. Lymph nodes in the right hemicolectomy mesentery and root are palpable. Exploration of the liver, stomach, small intestine, and pelvis did not reveal metastasis. No ascites.

## Operative Steps
1. Place the patient in a supine position under general anesthesia and perform routine disinfection and draping.
2. Make a right rectus abdominis incision and enter the abdominal cavity layer by layer.
3. Separate the gastrocolic ligament below the vascular arch, transect the right omentum, separate the colon from the duodenum, and find the superior mesenteric vein.
4. Cut off the right branch of the middle colic artery, the right colic artery, and the ileocolic artery along the right margin of the superior mesenteric vein.
5. Free the hepatic flexure of the colon, open the right lateral peritoneum, free the ascending colon mesentery, pay attention to the course of the ureter, free the ileocecal junction, and protect the duodenum.
6. Cut off the right half of the transverse colon, 15 cm from the ileocecal junction, cut off the small intestine, and perform a side-to-side anastomosis with a 31.5# tube anastomosis stapler. Clamp the residual colon end, close the mesenteric defect.
7. Irrigate, perfect hemostasis, count instruments and gauze correctly, place two negative pressure drainage tubes behind the anastomosis along the right lateral colon groove, close the incision in layers, and finish the operation.

## Overview
Right hemicolectomy for colon hepatic flexure tumor, involving resection of the right colon and adjacent structures, with emphasis on meticulous dissection and vascular control.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Colon hepatic flexure tumor
**Intraoperative:** Colon hepatic flexure tumor
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located at the colon hepatic flexure, approximately 3cm in diameter, ulcerative infiltrative type, infiltrating the serosa, surrounding the bowel for 3/4 of its circumference. No abnormalities found in liver or pelvis.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline incision, enter peritoneum layer by layer, findings as described.
3. Dissection of gastrocolic ligament, division of right greater omentum, mobilization of transverse mesocolon, division of hepatocolic ligament, separation of colon from duodenum, mobilization of colon hepatic flexure.
4. Incision of lateral peritoneum of right side, mobilization of ascending colon mesentery, attention to ureteral course, mobilization of ileocecal junction and terminal ileum.
5. Division of right middle colonic artery, ileocolic artery, and right colonic artery at their roots, ligation, division of mesocolon, division of ileum 20cm from ileocecal junction, division of colon at the middle of transverse colon, removal of specimen.
6. End-to-end anastomosis of ileum and colon using eight interrupted sutures, closure of mesenteric defect.
7. Irrigation, hemostasis, count instruments and sponges, placement of two negative pressure drainage balls in the right paracolic沟 of pelvis, closure of incision, completion of surgery.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: Colon hepatic flexure, mesentery, vascular pedicles. (胆总管)
> Danger zones: Areas around the vessels and bowel.
> Structures requiring protection: Ureter, mesenteric vessels.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in meticulous dissection around the vessels and bowel, ensuring adequate hemostasis and preventing complications.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of meticulous dissection and vascular control.
- Identification and protection of critical structures such as the ureter.
- Proper anastomotic technique for ileocolic anastomosis.

## Complication Prevention
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper anastomotic technique to prevent anastomotic leakage.

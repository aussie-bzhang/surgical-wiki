## Overview
Rectal resection with DIXON technique and cholecystectomy for rectal cancer and gallbladder multiple stones; key technical feature includes DIXON technique for rectal resection and laparoscopic cholecystectomy.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Rectal cancer, gallbladder multiple stones
**Intraoperative:** Rectal cancer, gallbladder multiple stones
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Gallbladder measuring 8*6*4cm with mixed stones, common bile duct diameter 0.7cm. Distal rectal mass 8cm from anal margin, not penetrating serosa. No abnormalities in liver, gallbladder, pancreas, spleen, other intestinal segments, peritoneum, or pelvis. No ascites.

## Operative Steps
1. Positioning, anesthesia, and routine disinfection.
2. Four-port laparoscopic setup, insufflation, and positioning.
3. Dissection of adhesions, anatomization of Calot triangle, and dissection of cystic duct and artery.
4. Dissection of gallbladder from liver bed, removal of specimen, and cautery of gallbladder bed.
5. Midline incision, laparotomy, and exposure of rectum and pelvic peritoneum.
6. Dissection and division of sigmoid mesentery, inferior mesenteric artery, and lymph node dissection.
7. Dissection of rectum, division of sigmoid colon, and preparation for anastomosis.
8. Distal rectal resection, lavage, and anastomosis with stapler.
9. Resection of involved small bowel mesentery and anastomosis.
10. Closure of mesenteric defect, placement of drains, and closure of incision.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Common bile duct (胆总管), rectum

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties in dissection of rectum and mesentery, and anastomosis.

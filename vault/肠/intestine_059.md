## Overview
Right hemicolectomy for right-sided colon cancer, nephrectomy for right kidney, and adnexectomy for right ovary. Involves extensive dissection and organ resection.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right-sided colon cancer
**Intraoperative:** Right-sided colon cancer confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings: Mass at ileocecal junction, 8*7*7cm in size, extending to peritoneal surface. Invades right ovary, posteriorly infiltrates right iliac artery bifurcation. Segment of 4.0cm ureter encircled by tumor.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline incision, enter peritoneum layer by layer.
3. Dissection of gastrocolic ligament, transection of right omentum, mobilization of transverse mesocolon, transection of hepatocolic ligament, separation of colon from duodenum, mobilization of colonic flexure.
4. Incision of right paracolic fascia, mobilization of ascending colon mesocolon, attention to ureteral course, mobilization of ileocecal junction and terminal ileum.
5. Transection of middle colic artery right branch, ileocolic artery, right colic artery, ligation at the root, transection of mesocolon, ileum transected 20cm from ileocecal junction, transverse transection of colon at middle, specimen removed.
6. Partial infiltration of abdominal wall by tumor, resection performed.
7. 28F tube anastomosis stapler for ileocolic end-to-side anastomosis, colon stump clamped, mesenteric defect closed.
8. Irrigation, hemostasis, instrument and suture count confirmed, two negative pressure drainage balls placed in right pelvic colonohydropexy, incision closed sequentially, procedure completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: ileocecal junction, right kidney, right adnexa. (回盲部, 右肾, 右附件)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered: extensive infiltration of tumor, careful dissection around vital structures.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of careful dissection around the ileocecal junction and right iliac artery.
- Management of extensive infiltration of abdominal wall.
- Technique of ileocolic anastomosis with stapler.

## Complication Prevention
- Prevention of anastomotic leakage: thorough irrigation and hemostasis.
- Prevention of postoperative ileus: early mobilization and bowel stimulation.

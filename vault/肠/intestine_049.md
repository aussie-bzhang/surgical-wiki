## Overview
Duodenal bulb perforation repair in a patient with gastrointestinal perforation, emphasizing the technical aspects of suture repair and abdominal lavage.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gastrointestinal perforation
**Intraoperative:** Duodenal bulb perforation
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a 2.5 cm ulcerous lesion on the anterior wall of the duodenal bulb with a central 0.5 cm perforation. Surrounding tissue was contaminated with pus and slough. Approximately 100 ml of purulent exudate was present in the abdominal cavity.

## Operative Steps
1. After general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping were performed. An upper midline incision was made to enter the abdomen.
2. The duodenal bulb perforation was sutured with 3-0 gastrointestinal suture, with the suture direction perpendicular to the longitudinal axis of the duodenal bulb, covered with omental tissue.
3. The abdominal cavity was lavaged with large amounts of normal saline until clean, and the exudate was aspirated. A negative pressure tube was placed under the liver and in the pelvis, and the incision was punctured to drain, then the abdomen was closed in layers.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the duodenal bulb and the perforation site. The common bile duct (胆总管) should be protected during the procedure.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of accurate identification and repair of the perforation site.
- Proper lavage of the abdominal cavity to reduce contamination.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper suture technique to prevent leakage.
- Adequate lavage to reduce the risk of postoperative infection.

## Overview
Small bowel perforation repair in a patient with abdominal trauma and peritoneal effusion, highlighting the technical aspects of identifying and repairing the perforation.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Trauma, abdominal closed injury, peritoneal effusion
**Intraoperative:** Trauma, abdominal closed injury, small bowel rupture and perforation, mesenteric hematoma of ascending colon
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Abdominal cavity contained approximately 2000ml of pale, bloody, cloudy fluid with a small amount of food residue. Two perforations were found in the small bowel at distances of 1.6m and 2.4m from the Treitz ligament, with diameters of 1.0cm and 1.5cm, respectively. The bowel lumen was dilated, and the bowel wall was congested. A hematoma measuring 3.0cm was found in the mesentery near the liver area of the ascending colon. No significant injury was observed in the liver, spleen, stomach, or pancreas. After lavage with saline, no blood, bile, or fecal fluid was seen.

## Operative Steps
1. Place the patient in a supine position under general anesthesia and perform routine disinfection and draping.
2. Make a midline incision, enter the abdomen layer by layer, and observe the findings as described above.
3. Suture the perforation site of the small bowel in full thickness, and reinforce with a serosal layer.
4. Lavage the abdominal cavity with a large amount of saline, achieve hemostasis, and place one negative-pressure drain in the right colonic sulcus and another in the pelvis.
5. Count instruments and ensure accuracy, close the abdomen, and complete the procedure.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the small bowel, mesentery, and common bile duct. The mesentery near the liver area of the ascending colon is a potential danger zone requiring careful handling to avoid injury. (胆总管)

## Technical Challenges
Managing the dilated bowel lumen and achieving hemostasis were the main technical challenges.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of thorough exploration of the abdominal cavity to identify multiple perforations.
- The technique of reinforcing the suture line with a serosal layer to prevent leakage.
- The need for meticulous hemostasis and proper placement of drains.

## Complication Prevention
- Preventing postoperative infection by ensuring thorough lavage and appropriate antibiotic prophylaxis.
- Minimizing the risk of bowel obstruction by ensuring proper closure and drain placement.

## Related Procedures
- [[Small Bowel Resection]] - For extensive damage or when perforation cannot be adequately repaired.

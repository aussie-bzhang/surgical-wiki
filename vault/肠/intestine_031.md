## Overview
Right hemicolectomy for right colon cancer, involving resection of the ascending colon and ileocecal junction, with anastomosis of the ileum to the transverse colon.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right colon cancer
**Intraoperative:** Right colon cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
A mass was found in the middle segment of the ascending colon, approximately 5cm in diameter, with ulcerative infiltration, involving the serosa, encircling the bowel for 1/2 of its circumference. The remaining bowel segments, liver, and ascites were normal.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline incision, enter peritoneum layer by layer, findings as described above.
3. Dissection of gastrocolic ligament, division of the right greater omentum, mobilization of the transverse mesocolon, division of the hepatocolic ligament, separation of the colon from the duodenum, mobilization of the hepatic flexure of the colon.
4. Incision of the lateral peritoneum of the right side, mobilization of the mesentery of the ascending colon, attention to the course of the ureter, mobilization of the ileocecal junction and terminal ileum.
5. Division of the right branch of the middle colonic vessels, ileocolic vessels, and right colic vessels, ligation at the root, division of the mesocolon, division of the ileum 20cm from the ileocecal junction, and division of the colon at the middle of the transverse colon to remove the specimen.
6. Placement of a 28.5mm anvil of the stapler in the terminal ileum, opening a hole on the colonoblast of the transverse colon, using the tube stapler for ileocolic end-to-side anastomosis, 60mm clamp to close the residual ends. 3-0 gastrointestinal suture to reinforce the anastomosis and residual ends, closure of the mesenteric defect.
7. Irrigation, hemostasis, count instruments and sponges, placement of a negative pressure ball in the right paracolic沟 and next to the anastomosis, closure of the incision, completion of the operation.

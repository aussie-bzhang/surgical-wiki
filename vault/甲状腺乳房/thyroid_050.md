## Overview
A surgical procedure involving subtotal resection of the right thyroid gland and isthmus resection for a right thyroid mass.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right thyroid mass
**Intraoperative:** Right thyroid mass
**Pathological:** Nodular goiter

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperatively, multiple heterogenous nodules were observed in the right thyroid gland, with the largest measuring approximately 3cm. Capsules were intact, soft, with clear boundaries and mobility. No obvious abnormalities were found in the left thyroid.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Arcuate incision made 2 cm above the sternal notch.
3. Layer-by-layer incision of skin, subcutaneous tissue, and platysma, flap elevation up to the thyroid cartilage and down to the sternal notch.
4. Incision along the pretracheal line, opening of the anterior muscle group, and exposure of the thyroid capsule.
5. Dissection of the middle and lower poles of the right thyroid gland, ligation of the middle and lower thyroid vessels, and complete dissection of the lateral loose adhesions.
6. Ligation of the isthmus, hemostasis, and attention to the course of the recurrent laryngeal nerve.
7. Dissection of the superior pole of the right thyroid gland, ligation of the superior thyroid vessels, and complete resection of the right thyroid gland and isthmus.
8. Thorough hemostasis, wound irrigation, placement of a T-tube drain, and layer-by-layer closure of the incision.
9. Completion of the procedure.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the right thyroid gland and isthmus. The recurrent laryngeal nerve is a key structure requiring protection (喉返神经).

## Overview
Right thyroidectomy with lobe resection for a right thyroid adenoma, with emphasis on the technique of lobe dissection and isthmus resection.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right thyroid nodule
**Intraoperative:** Right thyroid adenoma with isthmus involvement
**Pathological:** Right thyroid adenoma

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a 4cm hard mass at the inferior pole of the right thyroid near the isthmus, and a 0.5cm mass at the superior pole. The mass had a complete capsule. No abnormalities were found in the contralateral thyroid.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Arcuate incision 2 cm above the sternal notch.
3. Dissection of skin, subcutaneous tissue, and platysma, flap elevation to the thyroid cartilage superiorly and to the sternal notch inferiorly.
4. Incision along the cervical white line, opening the superficial muscle group, and exposing the thyroid capsule.
5. Dissection of the middle and inferior poles of the right thyroid, transection of thyroid vessels, and complete dissection of the lateral loose adhesions.
6. Transection of the isthmus, ligation and hemostasis, with attention to the course of the recurrent laryngeal nerve.
7. Dissection of the superior pole of the right thyroid, transection of thyroid vessels, and resection of the right and isthmus thyroid glands.
8. Thorough hemostasis, wound irrigation, placement of a T-tube drain in the residual cavity of the right thyroid, and layer-by-layer closure of the incision.
9. Completion of the procedure.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the right thyroid and isthmus. The recurrent laryngeal nerve is a key structure requiring protection. (胆总管)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included the need for careful dissection to avoid injury to the recurrent laryngeal nerve and maintaining hemostasis during the procedure.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of identifying and protecting the recurrent laryngeal nerve during thyroidectomy.
- The technique of lobe dissection and isthmus resection.
- The management of hemostasis to prevent postoperative complications.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful dissection to avoid injury to the recurrent laryngeal nerve.
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.

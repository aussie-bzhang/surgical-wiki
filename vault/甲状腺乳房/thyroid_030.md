## Overview
A modified radical mastectomy for left breast cancer, involving the removal of the affected breast, surrounding tissue, and axillary lymph nodes, with the preservation of the skin and nipple-areolar complex.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Left breast cancer
**Intraoperative:** Left breast cancer
**Pathological:** Left breast invasive carcinoma

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a 3.0cm mass in the upper inner quadrant of the left breast, subareolar, with palpable enlarged lymph nodes in the axilla.

## Operative Steps
1. The patient was placed in a supine position under general anesthesia, and the surgical site was prepared with routine antisepsis and draping.
2. An incision was made around the breast, extending from below the midpoint of the clavicle to the costal arch, with the skin flap elevated up to the clavicle and down to the costal arch, laterally to the anterior edge of the trapezius muscle, and medially to the sternal border.
3. The breast, surrounding fat, and the fascia and pectoralis major muscle were dissected down to the pectoralis minor muscle, and intermuscular lymph nodes were removed.
4. The axilla was opened, and the subclavian vessels, axillary vessels, and brachial plexus were exposed. Fat and lymphatic tissue in front of the axillary vein and around the subclavian vessels were removed.
5. The specimen was removed en bloc, irrigated, and three negative-pressure drains were placed in the chest wall and axilla, with the drains brought out through stab wounds.
6. Hemostasis was achieved, and instruments and sponges were counted and found to be correct. The incision was closed in layers, and the procedure was completed.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of careful dissection around the axillary lymph nodes to minimize complications.
- The technique of preserving the skin and nipple-areolar complex for aesthetic outcomes.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hematoma.
- Careful dissection to avoid injury to the brachial plexus.

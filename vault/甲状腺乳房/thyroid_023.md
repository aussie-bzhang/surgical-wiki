## Overview
Mammary fibroma excision is a surgical procedure performed for the removal of a benign fibrous tumor from the breast. It is typically indicated for symptomatic fibromas or those causing cosmetic concerns.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right breast mass
**Intraoperative:** Right breast fibroma
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperatively, a 2.5cm diameter nodule was observed in the upper lateral quadrant of the right breast, with moderate consistency and clear borders.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, routine disinfection and draping under local anesthesia;
2. Arcuate incision above the areola on the right breast, incising the skin, subcutaneous tissue, and breast tissue sequentially as observed;
3. Complete excision of the tumor, sent for rapid pathological examination;
4. Adequate hemostasis, count of instruments and sponges confirmed, and the incision was closed in layers; surgery completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the mammary gland and the fibroma. The common bile duct (胆总管) is not involved in this procedure.

## Technical Challenges
Not documented

## Teaching Points
- Understanding the anatomy of the breast and the potential for complications during fibroma excision.
- Proper technique for excising the fibroma while preserving surrounding structures.

## Complication Prevention
- Ensuring adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Minimizing tissue trauma to reduce the risk of infection and scarring.

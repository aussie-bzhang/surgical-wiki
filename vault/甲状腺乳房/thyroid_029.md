## Overview
A right thyroidectomy with isthmus resection was performed for a right thyroid adenoma with cystic degeneration in a patient presenting with a large cystic mass in the right thyroid gland.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right thyroid adenoma
**Intraoperative:** Right thyroid adenoma with cystic degeneration
**Pathological:** Right thyroid adenoma with cystic degeneration

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperatively, a large cystic mass measuring approximately 5.0 cm was found in the inferior pole of the right thyroid gland. The mass had a complete capsule, soft texture, clear borders, and was mobile. No significant abnormalities were noted in the left thyroid gland.

## Operative Steps
1. The patient was placed in a supine position under general anesthesia. The skin, subcutaneous tissue, and platysma were incised and elevated to the thyroid cartilage superiorly and to the sternal notch inferiorly.
2. The anterior muscle group was opened along the pretracheal line, and the perithyroid fascia was opened to expose the thyroid gland.
3. The middle and inferior poles of the right thyroid gland were mobilized, the thyroid vessels were ligated, and the lateral adhesions were freed. The isthmus was divided, and hemostasis was achieved while being cautious of the recurrent laryngeal nerve.
4. The superior pole of the right thyroid gland was mobilized, the thyroid vessels were ligated, and the right thyroid gland with the isthmus was resected en bloc.
5. Hemostasis was achieved, the wound was irrigated, a 14F T-tube was placed for drainage, and the incision was closed in layers.

## Anatomical Key Points
> The critical structures include the right thyroid gland, isthmus, and the recurrent laryngeal nerve. (甲状腺, 峡部, 喉返神经)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included careful dissection around the recurrent laryngeal nerve and achieving hemostasis without compromising the nerve.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of identifying and protecting the recurrent laryngeal nerve during thyroid surgery.
- The technique of en bloc resection of the thyroid gland with the isthmus.
- The management of hemostasis and postoperative care.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful dissection to avoid injury to the recurrent laryngeal nerve.
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.

## Related Procedures
- [[Total thyroidectomy]] - Indicated for bilateral thyroid disease or when the isthmus is involved in malignancy.

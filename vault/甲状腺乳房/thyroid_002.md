## Overview
Total thyroidectomy and isthmectomy with lateral lymph node dissection for a right thyroid adenoma with papillary thyroid microcarcinoma.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right thyroid adenoma
**Intraoperative:** Right thyroid adenoma with papillary thyroid microcarcinoma (0.2*0.2*0.1cm)
**Pathological:** Right and isthmus thyroid papillary microcarcinoma (0.2*0.2*0.1cm)

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a hard right thyroid gland with a nodule of approximately 1.2cm in diameter at the inferior pole. Frozen section indicated right and isthmus thyroid papillary microcarcinoma (0.2*0.2*0.1cm). Another nodule was also seen, and the patient returned to the operating room safely.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Arcuate incision 2 cm above the sternal notch.
3. Dissection of skin, subcutaneous tissue, and cervical platysma, flap elevation up to the thyroid cartilage and down to the sternal notch.
4. Incision along the cervical white line, opening the superficial cervical muscle group, and exposing the thyroid capsule.
5. Dissection of the right thyroid middle and lower poles, transection of thyroid vessels, and complete dissection of the lateral loose adhesions.
6. Dissection of the right thyroid superior pole, transection of thyroid vessels, and elevation of the posterior capsule to completely remove the right and isthmus thyroid glands, with attention to protecting the right recurrent laryngeal nerve.
7. Thorough hemostasis, wound irrigation, placement of a T-tube, and layered closure of the incision.
8. Operation completed.

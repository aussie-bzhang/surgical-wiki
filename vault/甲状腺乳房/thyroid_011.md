## Overview
Total bilateral thyroidectomy is a surgical procedure performed for the removal of most of the thyroid gland, typically due to thyroid nodules or thyroid cancer. The key technical feature involves careful dissection and preservation of the recurrent laryngeal nerve.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Bilateral thyroid nodules
**Intraoperative:** Bilateral thyroid nodules
**Pathological:** Cystic transformation of thyroid adenoma

## Intraoperative Findings
The left thyroid lobe superior pole had a 1.4cm x 0.9cm nodule, soft in consistency and well-defined. The right lobe inferior pole had a 1.2cm x 1.0cm cystic mass, soft in consistency and well-defined. Multiple small nodules were scattered in both lobes. No enlarged cervical lymph nodes were observed.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. An arc-shaped incision 2 cm above the sternal notch was made. The skin, subcutaneous tissue, and cervical platysma were sequentially opened. The skin flap was elevated up to the thyroid cartilage and down to the sternal notch.
3. The superficial cervical muscles were opened along the cervical white line, and the outer capsule of the thyroid was opened. The thyroid was explored as found during surgery; the right lobe was addressed first.
4. The middle pole of the right thyroid was freed, the middle thyroid vessels were ligated, and the lateral loose adhesions of the thyroid were freed. The isthmus was ligated and tied. The superior pole vessels were ligated and tied proximally. The thyroidectomy range was confirmed with hemostats, with attention to the course of the recurrent laryngeal nerve. The majority of the right thyroid lobe was resected in front of the laryngeal capsule. The same method was used to address the left lobe, retaining the superior pole.
5. Hemostasis was achieved, the wound was cleaned, one drain was left in place, the anterior cervical muscles were sutured, and the incision was closed in layers.
6. The procedure was completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> The critical structures include the thyroid gland, recurrent laryngeal nerve. (甲状腺, 喉返神经)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included careful dissection around the recurrent laryngeal nerve and maintaining hemostasis during the procedure.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of preserving the recurrent laryngeal nerve during thyroidectomy.
- The technique of identifying and ligating thyroid vessels.
- The management of cystic thyroid nodules.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Preservation of the recurrent laryngeal nerve to prevent vocal cord paralysis.

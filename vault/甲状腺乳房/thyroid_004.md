## Overview
Mastectomy for left breast cancer, with emphasis on the resection of the breast and axillary lymph nodes.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Left breast cancer
**Intraoperative:** Left breast cancer
**Pathological:** Invasive ductal carcinoma of the left breast

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a resected left breast mass, with palpable enlarged lymph nodes in the axilla.

## Operative Steps
1. Position the patient in supine position, under general anesthesia, and perform routine disinfection and draping.
2. Make an incision around the breast, with the upper end below the midpoint of the clavicle and the lower end reaching the costal arch.剥离皮瓣，上到锁骨，下达肋弓，外侧至背阔肌前缘，内侧为胸骨旁。
3. Dissect the breast, surrounding fat, and pectoralis major fascia and muscle to the pectoralis minor muscle, and clear the intermuscular lymph nodes.
4. Open the axilla to expose the subclavian vessels, axillary vessels, and brachial plexus, and clear the fat and lymphatic tissue in front and below the axillary vein, as well as the fat and lymphatic tissue around the subclavian vessels.
5. Remove the specimen en bloc, irrigate, and place a negative pressure drain in the chest wall and axilla, and puncture the incision to drain.
6. Perfect hemostasis, count instruments and sponges, and close the incision in layers. The operation is completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the breast, axillary lymph nodes, and the common bile duct (胆总管). The danger zones are the axillary vessels and nerves. Structures requiring protection are the brachial plexus and the subclavian vessels.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered include hemostasis and the removal of the specimen en bloc.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of proper hemostasis during the procedure.
- The technique of en bloc resection of the breast and axillary lymph nodes.
- The identification and protection of critical structures during the operation.

## Complication Prevention
- Infection prevention through proper disinfection and antibiotic prophylaxis.
- Hemorrhage prevention through meticulous hemostasis.

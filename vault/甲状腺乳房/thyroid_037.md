## Overview
Bilateral thyroidectomy with isthmus subtotal resection for bilateral thyroid adenomas, involving critical structures such as the thyroid gland, trachea, and recurrent laryngeal nerve.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Bilateral thyroid adenoma
**Intraoperative:** Bilateral thyroid adenoma
**Pathological:** Left, right, and isthmus thyroid adenoma

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a 8*5*5mm nodule at the inferior pole of the left thyroid, soft, well-defined, with a complete capsule. A 2.5cm nodule was found in the middle-lower part of the right thyroid, with a complete capsule and moderate texture. Frozen section pathology during surgery showed "bilateral thyroid adenoma."

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Arcuate incision 2 cm above the sternal notch; sequentially open the skin, subcutaneous tissue, and platysma; free the skin flap up to the thyroid cartilage and down to the sternal notch.
3. Open the superficial cervical fascia along the white line of the neck; open the perithyroidic fascia; explore the findings as discovered during surgery.
4. Free the inferior pole of the left thyroid; ligate and transect the inferior thyroid vessels; free the lateral thyroid from loose adhesions; ligate the middle thyroid vein; transect the isthmus and suture for hemostasis; use hemostat to determine the resection range of the thyroid; protect the recurrent laryngeal nerve; resect the left thyroid in its entirety in front of the trachea and send for frozen section pathology.
5. Perform subtotal resection of the right thyroid in the same manner.

## Overview
A surgical procedure for the treatment of right thyroid cancer and left thyroid adenoma, involving resection of the affected thyroid lobes and lymph node dissection.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Bilateral thyroid mass
**Intraoperative:** Right thyroid cancer + left thyroid adenoma
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Left thyroid: A 1cm cystic mass at the middle pole, with clear borders and mobility. Right thyroid: A 1cm hard mass at the middle superior pole, and a 1cm hard mass at the inferior and posterior poles. Frozen section pathology: "Right thyroid and isthmus papillary thyroid cancer (1.2*1.0*0.7cm), one lymph node in the right thyroid with papillary thyroid cancer metastasis. Left thyroid adenoma."

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Arc-shaped incision 2 cm above the sternal notch, sequentially opening the skin, subcutaneous tissue, and platysma, freeing the flap up to the thyroid cartilage and down to the sternal notch.
3. Open the superficial cervical muscle group along the white line of the neck, open the outer capsule of the thyroid, and explore as found during surgery.
4. Free the middle and lower poles of the right thyroid, transect the middle and lower thyroid vessels, fully free the lateral loose adhesions of the thyroid, transect the isthmus, ligate and stop bleeding, and pay attention to the course of the recurrent laryngeal nerve.
5. Free the superior pole of the right thyroid, transect the superior thyroid vessels, and completely resect the left and isthmus thyroid glands in front of the posterior capsule.
6. Free the middle pole of the left thyroid, transect the middle thyroid vessels, fully free the lateral loose adhesions of the thyroid, transect the isthmus, ligate and stop bleeding; ligate the superior pole near the upper pole with a double knot. Use hemostats to determine the resection range of the thyroid, pay attention to the course of the recurrent laryngeal nerve, and resect part of the left thyroid gland in front of the laryngeal capsule.
7. Dissect the lymph nodes in front of the trachea, above the isthmus of the thyroid, behind the trachea, around the recurrent laryngeal nerve, and in the tracheoesophageal groove and supraclavicular area.
8. Thorough hemostasis, clean the wound, leave a negative pressure ball, and close the incision in layers.
9. End of surgery.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of identifying and protecting the recurrent laryngeal nerve during surgery.
- The technique of lymph node dissection in the thyroid region.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Careful dissection to avoid injury to the recurrent laryngeal nerve and parathyroid glands.

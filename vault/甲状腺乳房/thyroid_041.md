## Overview
Total thyroidectomy with lateral lobe resection for papillary thyroid carcinoma, involving removal of the right thyroid lobe, isthmus, and partial left lobe.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right thyroid mass
**Intraoperative:** Papillary thyroid carcinoma
**Pathological:** Papillary carcinoma (10.80.4cm)

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a thyroid mass located on the right side of the isthmus and right thyroid lobe, approximately 1.0 x 0.5 cm in size, hard in consistency. No significant findings on the contralateral thyroid. No obvious cervical lymphadenopathy.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Arcuate incision 2 cm above the sternal notch.
3. Dissection of skin, subcutaneous tissue, and platysma, flap elevation up to the thyroid cartilage and down to the sternal notch.
4. Incision along the white line of the neck, opening the superficial muscle group, and exposing the thyroid capsule.
5. Dissection of the right thyroid middle and lower poles, transection of thyroid vessels, and complete dissection of the lateral thyroid.
6. Dissection of the right thyroid superior pole, transection of thyroid vessels, and elevation of the posterior capsule for complete resection of the right and isthmus with partial left lobe.
7. Attention to protecting the right recurrent laryngeal nerve.
8. Hemostasis, wound irrigation, placement of a T-tube, and closure of the incision.

## Anatomical Key Points

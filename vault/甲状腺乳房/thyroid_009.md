## Overview
A modified radical mastectomy for the treatment of right breast cancer. The procedure involves the removal of the entire breast, axillary lymph nodes, and parts of the chest wall musculature, with the aim of achieving local control and preventing recurrence.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right breast cancer
**Intraoperative:** Right breast cancer
**Pathological:** Invasive ductal carcinoma of the right breast

## Intraoperative Findings
The original surgical incision was located in the lower inner quadrant of the right breast, approximately 4 cm in length. During surgery, several lymph nodes in the right axilla were found to be enlarged, with the largest measuring 1.5 cm in diameter.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. A T-shaped incision was made centered on the breast, with the upper end below the midpoint of the clavicle and the lower end reaching the costal arch. The skin flap was elevated, extending superiorly to the clavicle, inferiorly to the anterior sheath of the rectus abdominis muscle, laterally to the anterior edge of the trapezius muscle, and medially to the sternocostal angle.
3. The entire breast, subcutaneous fat tissue, and pectoralis major fascia were dissected off to the lateral border of the pectoralis minor muscle, and the intermuscular lymph nodes were dissected.
4. The axilla was opened, and the subclavian vessels, axillary vessels, and brachial plexus were exposed. The fat and lymphatic tissue in front and below the axillary vein were dissected, and the fat and lymphatic tissue around the subclavian vessels were removed, with attention to protecting the long thoracic nerve and thoracodorsal nerve.
5. The entire specimen was removed en bloc, irrigated, and a negative pressure drain was placed in the chest wall and axilla, with the drain exiting through a stab incision.
6. Hemostasis was achieved, instruments and sponges were counted and found to be correct, and the incision was closed in layers. The procedure was completed.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of meticulous dissection to preserve critical structures.
- The technique of en bloc resection of the breast and axillary lymph nodes.
- The significance of lymphatic mapping and sentinel lymph node biopsy.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hematoma.
- Careful dissection to avoid injury to the brachial plexus and other critical structures.

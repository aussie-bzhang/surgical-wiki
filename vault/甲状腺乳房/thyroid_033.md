## Overview
Bilateral thyroid resection for nodules, with emphasis on preserving critical structures and minimizing complications.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Bilateral thyroid nodules
**Intraoperative:** Bilateral thyroid colloid nodules
**Pathological:** Frozen section: Thyroid colloid nodules with focal lymphocytic infiltration

## Intraoperative Findings
Bilateral thyroid nodules palpable, approximately 0.5-0.8 cm in diameter, firm. Intraoperative frozen pathology as documented.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Arcuate incision 2 cm above the sternal notch; sequentially open skin, subcutaneous tissue, and platysma; free the flap up to the thyroid cartilage and down to the sternal notch.
3. Open the superficial cervical fascia along the white line of the neck; open the perithyroid fascia; explore as found during surgery.
4. Free the right thyroid mid pole, ligate the right thyroid inferior vessels, and thoroughly mobilize the right thyroid lateral loose adhesions; ligate the left thyroid inferior vessels near the right pole. Use hemostats to define the resection range of the right thyroid; pay attention to the course of the right recurrent laryngeal nerve; resect most of the right thyroid gland and isthmus within the capsule; suture the remaining capsule.
5. Similarly, perform subtotal resection of the left thyroid.
6. Thorough hemostasis, clean the wound; place a T-tube in the thyroid fossa on both sides; suture the anterior cervical muscles; close the incision in layers.
7. Operation completed.

## Overview
Total thyroidectomy and isthmectomy for a left thyroid adenoma, with emphasis on the dissection and resection of the thyroid gland and isthmus.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Thyroid nodule
**Intraoperative:** Left thyroid adenoma
**Pathological:** Left thyroid adenoma

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed two cystic masses in the left thyroid gland, measuring 3.0cm and 2.5cm in diameter, occupying the entire left thyroid. The right thyroid was normal upon exploration.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Arcuate incision 2 cm above the sternal notch.
3. Dissection of skin, subcutaneous tissue, and cervical platysma, freeing the flap up to the thyroid cartilage and down to the sternal notch.
4. Incision along the cervical white line, opening the superficial cervical muscle group, and the outer capsule of the thyroid gland.
5. Dissection of the middle and lower poles of the left thyroid gland, transection of the middle and lower thyroid vessels, and complete dissection of the lateral loose adhesions of the thyroid gland.
6. Transection of the isthmus, hemostasis, and attention to the course of the recurrent laryngeal nerve.
7. Dissection of the superior pole of the left thyroid gland, transection of the superior thyroid vessels, and complete resection of the left thyroid gland.
8. Thorough hemostasis, wound irrigation, placement of a T-tube, and layered closure of the incision.
9. Completion of the procedure.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the thyroid gland and isthmus. The recurrent laryngeal nerve should be protected during dissection.

## Teaching Points
- Proper dissection and resection of the thyroid gland and isthmus.
- Identification and protection of the recurrent laryngeal nerve.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Protection of the recurrent laryngeal nerve to prevent vocal cord paralysis.

## Overview
A right thyroidectomy with thyroid lobectomy was performed for a patient with a right thyroid adenoma, confirmed intraoperatively as a cystic variant.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right thyroid adenoma
**Intraoperative:** Right thyroid adenoma with cystic change
**Pathological:** Right thyroid adenoma with cystic change

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a large cystic mass in the inferior pole of the right thyroid, measuring approximately 4 cm in diameter, with a complete capsule, soft texture, clear borders, and mobility. No significant abnormalities were found in the left thyroid.

## Operative Steps
1. The patient was placed in a supine position under general anesthesia. The skin, subcutaneous tissue, and platysma were incised and elevated up to the thyroid cartilage and down to the superior thyroid notch.
2. The anterior muscle group was opened along the pretracheal line, and the outer capsule of the thyroid was opened to explore the findings.
3. The middle and inferior poles of the right thyroid were freed, the middle and inferior thyroid vessels were ligated, and the lateral loose adhesions were freed. The isthmus was transected, ligated, and止血, with attention to the course of the recurrent laryngeal nerve.
4. The superior pole of the right thyroid was freed, the superior thyroid vessels were ligated, and the right thyroid and isthmus were completely excised.
5. Hemostasis was achieved, the wound was cleaned, and a 14F T-tube was left for drainage. The incision was closed in layers.
6. The procedure was completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> The critical structures include the right thyroid and isthmus. The danger zones are the recurrent laryngeal nerve and the inferior thyroid artery. Structures requiring protection are the trachea and esophagus (气管和食管).

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included hemostasis and protecting the recurrent laryngeal nerve.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of identifying and protecting the recurrent laryngeal nerve during thyroid surgery.
- Proper hemostasis techniques to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- The technique of complete excision of the thyroid and isthmus.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Identification and protection of the recurrent laryngeal nerve to prevent vocal cord paralysis.

## Overview
A surgical procedure for the removal of a near-total left thyroid lobe and isthmus, performed for a left thyroid adenoma and right thyroid nodule.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Left thyroid adenoma, right thyroid nodule
**Intraoperative:** Left thyroid adenoma, right thyroid nodule
**Pathological:** Left thyroid adenoma

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a mass located in the middle of the left thyroid gland, measuring approximately 2.0 cm in diameter, with a firm consistency and intact capsule. No abnormalities were found in the contralateral thyroid gland.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. An arc-shaped incision 2 cm above the sternal notch was made, and the skin, subcutaneous tissue, and platysma were sequentially opened. The flap was freed up to the thyroid cartilage and down to the sternal notch.
3. The superficial cervical muscles were opened along the cervical white line, and the outer capsule of the thyroid gland was opened to explore the findings as during surgery.
4. The middle and lower poles of the left thyroid gland were freed, the middle and lower thyroid vessels were ligated, and the lateral loose adhesions of the thyroid gland were freed up to the isthmus, which was then ligated and sutured for hemostasis.
5. The superior pole of the left thyroid gland was freed, and the superior pole was ligated close to the superior thyroid vessels and excised from the capsule.
6. Hemostasis was achieved, the wound was cleaned, a T-tube was left in place, the anterior cervical muscles were sutured, and the incision was closed in layers.
7. The procedure was completed.

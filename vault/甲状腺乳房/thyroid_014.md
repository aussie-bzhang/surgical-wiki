## Overview
Right breast abscess drainage; clinical context: localized abscess formation in the right breast; key technical feature: incision and drainage of the abscess cavity.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right breast abscess
**Intraoperative:** Right breast abscess
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings: Approximately 50ml of purulent fluid and necrotic tissue were drained from the right breast outer quadrant in a radiating manner. The abscess occupied approximately 6*3*3cm in the right breast outer quadrant.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, routine disinfection and draping under combined spinal-epidural anesthesia.
2. Take a radiating incision in the outer quadrant of the right breast, sequentially incise the skin, subcutaneous tissue, and open the abscess cavity as described above.
3. Irrigate the abscess cavity with 500ml of iodophor water, then rinse with normal saline.
4. Excise part of the surrounding tissue of the abscess, send for pathological examination.
5. Perfect hemostasis, fill the abscess cavity with iodophor gauze.
6. Count instruments and sponges, close the incision in layers.
7. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: right breast, abscess cavity. (乳房, 脓腔)

## Teaching Points
- The importance of proper incision and drainage technique for abscess management.
- The significance of ensuring adequate irrigation and debridement of the abscess cavity.
- The need for proper hemostasis and wound closure to prevent postoperative complications.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper incision and drainage technique to prevent recurrence.
- Adequate irrigation to reduce the risk of infection.
- Proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.

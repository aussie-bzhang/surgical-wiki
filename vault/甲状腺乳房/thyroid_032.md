## Overview
A modified radical mastectomy for the treatment of invasive ductal carcinoma of the right breast. The procedure involves the removal of the breast, surrounding tissue, and axillary lymph nodes, with preservation of the pectoralis major muscle.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right breast mass
**Intraoperative:** Invasive ductal carcinoma of the right breast
**Pathological:** Invasive ductal carcinoma of the right breast

## Intraoperative Findings
Right breast mass located in the upper outer quadrant, approximately 5*4*3cm in size, hard, poorly defined, rich in blood supply, with enlarged intermuscular lymph nodes.

## Operative Steps
1. Place the patient in a supine position, perform routine disinfection and draping, local anesthesia, and make a radiating incision over the mass. Incise the skin, subcutaneous tissue, and breast tissue, remove the mass, and send for frozen section.
2. Perform general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
3. Make an incision around the breast, with the upper end below the midpoint of the clavicle and the lower end reaching the costal arch.剥离皮瓣，上至锁骨，下达肋弓，外侧至背阔肌前缘，内侧为胸骨旁。
4. Dissect the breast, surrounding fat, and pectoralis major fascia to the pectoralis minor muscle, and clear the intermuscular lymph nodes.
5. Open the axilla, expose the subclavian vessels, axillary vessels, and brachial plexus, and clear the fat and lymphatic tissue in front of the axillary vein and below the axilla, as well as the fat and lymphatic tissue around the subclavian vessels.
6. Remove the specimen en bloc, irrigate, and place a negative pressure drain in the chest wall and axilla, and bring it out through a stab incision.
7. Perfect hemostasis, count instruments and sponges, close the incision in layers, and complete the operation.

## Anatomical Key Points

## Teaching Points
- The importance of proper incision and flap design.
- The meticulous dissection of the axillary lymph nodes.
- The preservation of the pectoralis major muscle.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hematoma.
- Adequate drainage to prevent seroma formation.

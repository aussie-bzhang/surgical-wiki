## Overview
Total thyroidectomy for a left thyroid adenoma, with emphasis on identifying and preserving critical structures such as the recurrent laryngeal nerve and parathyroid glands.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Left thyroid adenoma
**Intraoperative:** Left thyroid adenoma
**Pathological:** Left thyroid adenoma

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a 5*3*2cm soft, well-defined mass in the left thyroid gland.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Arcuate incision 2 cm above the sternal notch, sequentially opening the skin, subcutaneous tissue, and platysma. The flap is elevated up to the thyroid cartilage and down to the sternal notch.
3. Incise the superficial cervical fascia along the white line of the neck, open the perithyroid fascia, and explore the findings as observed during surgery.
4. Free the inferior pole of the left thyroid gland, ligate the inferior thyroid vessels, and thoroughly mobilize the lateral loose adhesions of the thyroid gland. Divide the isthmus, ligate and tie off the bleeding.
5. Use hemostatic forceps to determine the resection range of the thyroid gland, paying attention to the course of the recurrent laryngeal nerve, and completely resect the left thyroid gland in front of the thyroid sheath.
6. Thorough hemostasis, clean the wound, and place a T-tube. Close the incision in layers.
7. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the thyroid gland, parathyroid glands, and recurrent laryngeal nerve. (甲状腺, 胞腺, 喉返神经)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included identifying and preserving the recurrent laryngeal nerve and parathyroid glands.

## Teaching Points
- Identifying and preserving the recurrent laryngeal nerve and parathyroid glands is crucial for patient safety and function.
- Proper hemostasis and wound closure techniques are essential to prevent complications.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful dissection to avoid injury to the recurrent laryngeal nerve and parathyroid glands.
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.

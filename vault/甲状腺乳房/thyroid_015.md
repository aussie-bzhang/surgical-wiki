## Overview
Total thyroidectomy and isthmectomy for right thyroid nodule and Hashimoto's thyroiditis, with intraoperative diagnosis of papillary thyroid microcarcinoma.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right thyroid nodule, Hashimoto's thyroiditis
**Intraoperative:** Papillary thyroid microcarcinoma, Hashimoto's thyroiditis
**Pathological:** Right thyroid and isthmus papillary thyroid microcarcinoma (0.2*0.2*0.2cm), associated with chronic lymphocytic thyroiditis

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a 1.0cm nodule in the middle and lower lobe of the right thyroid gland, firm, well-defined. No obvious mass was found in the left thyroid gland. A 1cm enlarged lymph node was palpable adjacent to the right recurrent laryngeal nerve.

## Operative Steps

1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Arcuate incision 2 cm above the sternal notch; sequentially open the skin, subcutaneous tissue, and cervical platysma, free the skin flap up to the thyroid cartilage and down to the sternal notch.
3. Open the superficial cervical fascia along the white line of the neck, expose the thyroid capsule, and explore the findings as discovered during surgery.
4. Free the middle and lower poles of the right thyroid gland, ligate the middle and lower thyroid vessels, and thoroughly free the lateral loose adhesions of the thyroid gland.
5. Free the superior pole of the right thyroid gland, ligate the superior thyroid vessels, lift the posterior capsule of the gland, and completely remove the right thyroid and isthmus, protecting the right recurrent laryngeal nerve.
6. Thorough hemostasis, clean the wound, and leave a T-tube drain; close the incision in layers.
7. Operation completed.

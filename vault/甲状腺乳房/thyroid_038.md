## Overview
Total thyroidectomy with峡部 resection and partial thyroidectomy of the left lobe for bilateral thyroid adenoma, performed in a patient with significant clinical symptoms.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Bilateral thyroid adenoma
**Intraoperative:** Right thyroid and isthmus adenoma, left thyroid colloid nodule
**Pathological:** Right thyroid and isthmus adenoma, left thyroid colloid nodule

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a 3.0 cm mass in the right thyroid and a 1.0 cm cystic mass in the middle of the left thyroid.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Arcuate incision 2 cm above the sternal notch, sequentially opening the skin, subcutaneous tissue, and platysma, freeing the flap up to the thyroid cartilage and down to the sternal notch.
3. Incise the anterior muscle group along the white line of the neck, open the perithyroidic fascia, and explore the findings as discovered during surgery.
4. Free the middle and lower poles of the right thyroid, transect the middle and lower thyroid vessels, and thoroughly free the lateral loose adhesions of the thyroid. Transect the isthmus, ligate and stop bleeding, and pay attention to the course of the recurrent laryngeal nerve.
5. Free the superior pole of the right thyroid, transect the superior thyroid vessels, and completely resect the right and isthmus thyroid glands from the anterior perithyroidic fascia.
6. Free the lateral loose adhesions of the left thyroid, use hemostats to determine the resection range of the left thyroid mass, resect the mass, and suture the perithyroidic fascia.
7. Thoroughly ligate and stop bleeding, clean the wound, leave a T-tube drain, and close the incision in layers.
8. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the thyroid gland, trachea, and recurrent laryngeal nerve. Pay attention to the course of the recurrent laryngeal nerve during surgery (喉返神经走向).

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included the need for careful dissection to preserve the recurrent laryngeal nerve and the need for precise hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of identifying and protecting the recurrent laryngeal nerve during thyroid surgery.
- The technique of hemostasis to prevent postoperative complications.
- The importance of accurate preoperative planning for resection of thyroid nodules.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Identification and protection of the recurrent laryngeal nerve to prevent vocal cord paralysis.

## Overview
Total thyroidectomy for a left thyroid adenoma, with emphasis on the identification and preservation of the recurrent laryngeal nerve.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Left thyroid adenoma
**Intraoperative:** Left thyroid adenoma
**Pathological:** Left thyroid adenoma

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a 2.5cm soft, well-defined mass in the left thyroid.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Arcuate incision 2 cm above the sternal notch, sequentially opening the skin, subcutaneous tissue, and cervical platysma, freeing the flap up to the thyroid cartilage and down to the sternal notch.
3. Incise the superficial cervical muscles along the white line of the neck, open the perithyroidic fascia, and explore the findings as discovered during surgery.
4. Free the inferior pole of the left thyroid, transect the inferior thyroid vessels, and thoroughly free the lateral loose adhesions of the thyroid. Transect the isthmus, ligate and stop bleeding; use hemostatic forceps to determine the resection range of the thyroid, paying attention to the course of the recurrent laryngeal nerve, and completely resect the left thyroid gland in front of the laryngeal fascia.
5. Thorough hemostasis, clean the wound, place a T-tube, and close the incision in layers.
6. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the thyroid gland and the recurrent laryngeal nerve. (甲状腺，喉返神经)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included identification and preservation of the recurrent laryngeal nerve.

## Teaching Points
- Identification and preservation of the recurrent laryngeal nerve are crucial for preventing postoperative laryngeal nerve injury.
- Proper hemostasis and wound closure are essential for preventing postoperative complications.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis and proper wound closure are strategies to prevent postoperative complications.

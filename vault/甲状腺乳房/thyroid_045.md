## Overview
A right thyroidectomy with thyroid lobectomy was performed for a right thyroid adenoma. The procedure involved resection of the majority of the right thyroid gland and the isthmus, with careful attention to anatomical landmarks and nerve preservation.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right thyroid adenoma
**Intraoperative:** Right thyroid adenoma
**Pathological:** Right thyroid adenoma

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperatively, a solid mass was found in the right thyroid gland, measuring approximately 3.0 cm in diameter. The mass was firm, encapsulated, and no abnormalities were noted in the contralateral thyroid.

## Operative Steps
1. The patient was placed in a supine position under general anesthesia. The skin was disinfected and prepped.
2. An arc-shaped incision was made 2 cm above the sternal notch. The skin, subcutaneous tissue, and platysma were sequentially opened, and the flap was elevated up to the thyroid cartilage and down to the sternal notch.
3. The superficial cervical fascia was opened along the midline, and the capsule of the thyroid was exposed. The findings were consistent with the preoperative diagnosis.
4. The middle and lower poles of the right thyroid gland were mobilized, and the vessels were ligated. The lateral adhesions were freed, and the isthmus was transected and suture ligated.
5. The superior pole of the right thyroid gland was mobilized, and the thyroid tissue and vessels were ligated and transected close to the mass. The remaining thyroid tissue was sutured to the trachea, and hemostasis was achieved.
6. Hemostasis was confirmed, the wound was irrigated, and a T-tube was left in place. The cervical muscles were sutured, and the incision was closed in layers.
7. The procedure was completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> The common bile duct (胆总管) and the recurrent laryngeal nerve (喉返神经) are critical structures that require protection during the procedure.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included mobilizing the thyroid gland and preserving the recurrent laryngeal nerve.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of identifying and protecting the recurrent laryngeal nerve.
- The technique of thyroid gland mobilization and hemostasis.
- The use of a T-tube for postoperative drainage.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Identification and protection of the recurrent laryngeal nerve to prevent vocal cord paralysis.

## Overview
A surgical procedure involving the subtotal resection of the left thyroid gland and isthmus for a cystic change in a thyroid adenoma.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Bilateral thyroid adenoma
**Intraoperative:** Cystic change in left thyroid adenoma
**Pathological:** Left thyroid adenoma with cystic change

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a large cystic mass in the inferior pole of the left thyroid gland, measuring approximately 7 cm in diameter, with a complete capsule, soft texture, clear boundaries, and mobility. No obvious abnormalities were found in the left thyroid.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. An arc-shaped incision is made 2 cm above the sternal notch, and the skin, subcutaneous tissue, and platysma are sequentially opened. The flap is freed up to the thyroid cartilage and down to the sternal notch.
3. The pretracheal muscle group is opened along the white line of the neck, and the outer capsule of the thyroid gland is opened to explore the findings.
4. The middle and lower poles of the left thyroid gland are freed, the middle and lower thyroid vessels are ligated, and the lateral loose adhesions of the thyroid gland are freed up. The isthmus is transected, ligated, and止血, with attention to the course of the recurrent laryngeal nerve.
5. The superior pole of the left thyroid gland is freed, the superior thyroid vessels are ligated, and the left thyroid gland and isthmus are completely resected in front of the posterior capsule.
6. Hemostasis is achieved, the wound is cleaned, a 16F T-tube drain is left in place, and the incision is closed in layers.
7. The procedure is completed.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of identifying and protecting the recurrent laryngeal nerve during surgery.
- The technique of resecting the thyroid gland and isthmus while preserving the integrity of the capsule.
- The management of hemostasis and postoperative care.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper placement of the T-tube to prevent postoperative thyroid fistula.

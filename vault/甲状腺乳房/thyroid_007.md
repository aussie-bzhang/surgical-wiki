## Overview
A modified radical mastectomy for left breast cancer, involving the removal of the breast, surrounding tissue, and axillary lymph nodes, with a focus on preserving the pectoralis major muscle and submammary gland.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Left breast mass
**Intraoperative:** Left breast cancer
**Pathological:** Invasive ductal carcinoma of the left breast

## Intraoperative Findings
A 4*3cm mass in the outer upper quadrant of the left breast with unclear borders, approximately 4cm from the nipple. Multiple enlarged lymph nodes in the left axilla, the largest measuring 1.0cm in diameter.

## Operative Steps
1. Position the patient in supine position, perform routine disinfection and draping, local anesthesia, and make a radiating incision over the mass. Incise the skin, subcutaneous tissue, and breast tissue, and remove the mass for frozen section pathology.
2. Perform general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
3. Make an incision around the breast, with the upper end below the mid-clavicle and the lower end reaching the costal arch.剥离皮瓣，上至锁骨，下达肋弓，外侧至背阔肌前缘，内侧为胸骨旁。
4. Dissect the breast, surrounding fat, and pectoralis major fascia to the pectoralis minor muscle, and remove intermuscular lymph nodes.
5. Open the axilla, expose the infraclavicular vessels, axillary vessels, and brachial plexus, and remove fat and lymphatic tissue in front and below the axillary vein, and around the infraclavicular vessels.
6. Remove the specimen en bloc, irrigate, and place three negative pressure drains in the chest wall and axilla, and bring them out through puncture wounds.
7. Perfect hemostasis, count instruments and sponges, and close the incision in layers. The operation is completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> - Left breast
> - Axillary lymph nodes
> - Pectoralis major muscle
> - Submammary gland
> - Infraclavicular vessels (锁骨下血管)

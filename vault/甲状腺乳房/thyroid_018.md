## Overview
A right thyroidectomy with thyroid lobectomy and isthmus resection was performed for a right thyroid adenoma. The procedure involved resection of the right thyroid gland and isthmus, with careful attention to the preservation of the recurrent laryngeal nerve.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right thyroid adenoma
**Intraoperative:** Right thyroid adenoma
**Pathological:** Right thyroid adenoma

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperatively, a cystic and solid mass was found in the middle and lower pole of the right thyroid gland, measuring approximately 3.0 cm in diameter. The mass had a complete capsule, soft texture, clear borders, and was mobile. No significant abnormalities were found in the left thyroid.

## Operative Steps
1. The patient was placed in a supine position under general anesthesia. The skin, subcutaneous tissue, and platysma were incised and elevated to the thyroid cartilage superiorly and to the superior thyroid notch inferiorly.
2. The anterior muscle group was opened along the pretracheal line, and the perithyroid fascia was opened to expose the thyroid gland.
3. The middle and lower poles of the right thyroid gland were mobilized, the thyroid vessels were ligated, and the lateral adhesions were freed. The isthmus was resected, and hemostasis was achieved, with attention to the course of the recurrent laryngeal nerve.
4. The superior pole of the right thyroid gland was mobilized, the thyroid vessels were ligated, and the right thyroid gland and isthmus were resected en bloc.
5. Hemostasis was achieved, the wound was irrigated, and a 14F T-tube was left in place for drainage. The incision was closed in layers.

## Anatomical Key Points
> The recurrent laryngeal nerve (喉返神经) is a critical structure requiring protection during the procedure. The isthmus (峡部) is also a key structure that needs to be resected carefully.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included careful mobilization of the thyroid gland and isthmus while preserving the recurrent laryngeal nerve.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of identifying and protecting the recurrent laryngeal nerve.
- The technique of en bloc resection of the thyroid gland and isthmus.
- The need for meticulous hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful dissection to avoid injury to the recurrent laryngeal nerve.
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.

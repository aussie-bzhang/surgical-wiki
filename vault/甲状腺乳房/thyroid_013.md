## Overview
A modified radical mastectomy for the treatment of right breast cancer. The procedure involves the removal of the entire breast, surrounding skin, chest wall muscles, and regional lymph nodes, with the aim of achieving a high rate of local control and survival.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right breast cancer
**Intraoperative:** Right breast cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a 1.5 cm hard, ill-defined mass in the lower inner quadrant of the right breast. Multiple enlarged lymph nodes were observed in the axilla.

## Operative Steps
1. The patient was placed in a supine position under general anesthesia. The surgical site was prepared and draped in a standard manner.
2. An incision was made around the breast, extending from below the midpoint of the clavicle to the costal arch. The skin flaps were elevated, extending superiorly to the clavicle and inferiorly to the costal arch, laterally to the anterior edge of the trapezius muscle, and medially to the sternal border.
3. The breast, surrounding fat, and pectoralis major fascia were dissected free. Lymph nodes between the pectoralis muscles were removed.
4. The axilla was opened, and the subclavian vessels, axillary vessels, and brachial plexus were exposed. Fat and lymphatic tissue anterior to the axillary vein and around the subclavian vessels were removed. The long thoracic nerve, thoracodorsal nerve, and subscapular vessels were preserved.
5. The specimen was removed en bloc, irrigated, and the chest wall and axilla were drained with a single negative pressure drain. The drain was brought out through a stab incision.
6. Hemostasis was achieved, instruments and sponges were counted and accounted for, and the incision was closed in layers. The procedure was completed.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of preserving the long thoracic nerve, thoracodorsal nerve, and subscapular vessels during axillary dissection.
- The technique of en bloc resection of the breast and surrounding tissues.
- The proper placement and management of drains.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Adequate drainage to prevent seroma formation.

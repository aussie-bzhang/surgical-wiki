## Overview
Left thyroidectomy for a left thyroid adenoma, with emphasis on the technique of partial thyroid resection and the management of adjacent structures.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Left thyroid adenoma
**Intraoperative:** Left thyroid acidophilic adenoma
**Pathological:** Left thyroid acidophilic adenoma

## Intraoperative Findings
A mass was found in the upper pole of the left thyroid gland, approximately 3.0 cm in diameter, with medium consistency, a complete capsule, and cystic. No abnormalities were found in the contralateral thyroid gland.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. An arc-shaped incision 2 cm above the sternal notch was made, and the skin, subcutaneous tissue, and platysma were sequentially opened. The skin flap was freed up to the thyroid cartilage and down to the sternal notch.
3. The superficial cervical muscles were opened along the white line of the neck, and the outer capsule of the thyroid gland was opened, and the findings were explored as in surgery.
4. The middle and lower poles of the left thyroid gland were freed, and the lateral loose adhesions of the thyroid gland were fully freed.
5. The superior pole of the left thyroid gland was freed, the left thyroid suspensory ligament was cut, and the left thyroid vessels were cut and ligated. A partial resection of the right thyroid gland was performed in front of the posterior capsule.
6. Hemostasis was achieved, the wound was cleaned, a T-tube was left in place, the anterior neck muscles were sutured, and the incision was closed in layers.
7. The operation was completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the left thyroid gland and thyroid lobule. The common bile duct (胆总管) should be protected during the procedure.

## Overview
Right thyroidectomy and isthmectomy for a right thyroid adenoma, with emphasis on the dissection of the thyroid gland and isthmus, and the protection of critical structures.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right thyroid adenoma
**Intraoperative:** Right thyroid adenoma
**Pathological:** Right thyroid adenoma

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a 3.5cm diameter mass in the right thyroid gland, with a firm consistency and intact capsule. No significant abnormalities were found in the contralateral thyroid gland.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Arcuate incision 2 cm above the sternal notch, sequentially opening the skin, subcutaneous tissue, and platysma, freeing the flap up to the thyroid cartilage and down to the sternal notch.
3. Open the superficial cervical fascia along the white line of the neck, open the perithyroidic fascia, and explore as found during surgery.
4. Free the middle and lower poles of the right thyroid gland, ligate and transect the middle and lower thyroid vessels, and thoroughly free the lateral loose adhesions of the thyroid gland. Transect the isthmus and ligate for hemostasis.
5. Free the superior pole of the right thyroid gland, closely adhering to the edge of the mass, ligate and transect the thyroid tissue and vessels, remove the mass, suture the remaining thyroid gland and tracheal anterior tissue for hemostasis, and pay attention to the protection of the recurrent laryngeal nerve.
6. Thorough hemostasis, clean the wound, place a T-tube, suture the anterior cervical muscles, and close the incision in layers.
7. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the right thyroid gland, isthmus of thyroid, and recurrent laryngeal nerve. (甲状腺, 胸腺峡部, 喉返神经)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included the need for careful dissection to preserve the recurrent laryngeal nerve and the need for precise hemostasis.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of identifying and protecting the recurrent laryngeal nerve.
- The technique of preserving the integrity of the thyroid capsule.
- The importance of thorough hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful dissection to avoid injury to the recurrent laryngeal nerve.
- Thorough hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.

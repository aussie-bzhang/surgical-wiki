## Overview
A radical resection of the right thyroid gland for the treatment of papillary thyroid microcarcinoma in a patient with a pre-operative diagnosis of a right thyroid nodule.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right thyroid nodule
**Intraoperative:** Right thyroid cancer
**Pathological:** Papillary thyroid microcarcinoma

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a 0.5 cm hard nodule in the middle of the right thyroid gland, with another 0.2 cm nodule adjacent to it. No abnormalities were found in the contralateral thyroid gland.

## Operative Steps
1. The patient was placed in a supine position under general anesthesia. The skin, subcutaneous tissue, and platysma were incised and elevated up to the thyroid cartilage and down to the superior thyroid notch.
2. The superficial cervical fascia was opened along the pretracheal line, and the thyroid capsule was opened to expose the thyroid gland.
3. The middle and lower poles of the right thyroid gland were mobilized, the thyroid vessels were ligated, and the lateral adhesions were freed. The isthmus was divided and suture ligated, with attention to the course of the right recurrent laryngeal nerve.
4. The superior pole of the right thyroid gland was mobilized, the thyroid vessels were ligated, and the gland was completely excised.
5. Lymph nodes in the right neck were dissected, including those around the common carotid artery and internal jugular vein, in front of the trachea, above the isthmus, posterior to the trachea, around the recurrent laryngeal nerve, and in the area of the tracheoesophageal groove and supraclavicular region.
6. Hemostasis was achieved, the wound was irrigated, a T-tube was left in place, and the incision was closed in layers.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the right thyroid gland, right recurrent laryngeal nerve. (甲状腺，喉返神经)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included careful dissection around the recurrent laryngeal nerve and lymph node dissection in a limited space.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of identifying and protecting the recurrent laryngeal nerve during thyroid surgery.
- The technique of lymph node dissection in the neck.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper placement of the T-tube to prevent postoperative thyroid fistula.

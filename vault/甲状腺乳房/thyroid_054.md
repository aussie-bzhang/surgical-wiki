## Overview
Total thyroidectomy and isthmus resection for a left thyroid nodule, with emphasis on the technical aspects of thyroid gland dissection and nerve protection.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Left thyroid nodule
**Intraoperative:** Left thyroid nodule confirmed
**Pathological:** Atypical adenoma with calcification in the left thyroid and isthmus

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a left thyroid mass measuring approximately 3 cm in diameter, irregular in shape. Frozen section indicated an atypical adenoma with calcification in the left thyroid and isthmus.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Arcuate incision made 2 cm above the sternal notch.
3. Dissection of skin, subcutaneous tissue, and platysma, flap elevation up to the thyroid cartilage and down to the sternal notch.
4. Incision along the cervical white line, opening the superficial cervical muscle group, and exposing the thyroid capsule.
5. Dissection of the middle and lower poles of the left thyroid, transection of the middle and lower thyroid vessels, and complete dissection of the lateral loose adhesions.
6. Dissection of the superior pole of the left thyroid, transection of the superior thyroid vessels, and elevation of the posterior capsule to remove the left thyroid and isthmus completely, with attention to protecting the left recurrent laryngeal nerve.
7. Thorough hemostasis, wound irrigation, placement of a T-tube, and layered closure of the incision.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the thyroid gland, isthmus, and the recurrent laryngeal nerve. (喉返神经)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included complete dissection of the thyroid gland and isthmus while protecting the recurrent laryngeal nerve.

## Teaching Points
- Proper dissection technique to avoid injury to the recurrent laryngeal nerve.
- Identification and protection of critical anatomical structures during thyroidectomy.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Routine use of a T-tube to prevent postoperative thyroid fistula.

## Related Procedures

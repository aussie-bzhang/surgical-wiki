## Overview
Mammary gland excision for a palpable mass in the left breast, with a focus on the technique of tumor resection and histopathological confirmation.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Palpable mass in the left breast.
**Intraoperative:** Chronic inflammation of fibroadipose tissue in the left breast.
**Pathological:** Not documented.

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a 2.5cm nodule in the upper outer quadrant of the left breast, with moderate consistency and clear margins.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, routine disinfection and draping under local anesthesia.
2. Incision along the superior arc of the areola on the left breast, sequentially incising the skin, subcutaneous tissue, and breast tissue.
3. Complete excision of the tumor, sent for rapid pathological examination.
4. Adequate hemostasis, count of instruments and sponges confirmed, and the incision was closed in layers.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the mammary gland and breast tissue. (乳腺, 胸腺组织)

## Technical Challenges
Not documented.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of accurate tumor localization and complete excision.
- The significance of histopathological confirmation for diagnosis.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hematoma.
- Careful incision and closure to minimize scarring.

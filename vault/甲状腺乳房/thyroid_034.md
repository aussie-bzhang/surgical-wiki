## Overview
Total bilateral thyroidectomy with峡部 resection for bilateral thyroid nodules, with emphasis on the technical aspects of thyroid gland dissection and nerve protection.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Bilateral thyroid nodules
**Intraoperative:** Right thyroid and isthmus adenoma, left thyroid colloid nodule
**Pathological:** Right thyroid and isthmus adenoma, left thyroid colloid nodule

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a right thyroid mass of approximately 1.5 cm in diameter, hard in consistency, with clear borders, and a left thyroid nodule with the largest diameter of 0.5 cm.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Arcuate incision 2 cm above the sternal notch, sequentially opening the skin, subcutaneous tissue, and platysma, freeing the flap up to the thyroid cartilage and down to the sternal notch.
3. Open the anterior muscle group along the cervical white line, open the perithyroidic fascia, and explore as found during surgery.
4. Free the left and right thyroid glands, ligate and transect thyroid tissue and vessels close to the mass edge, resect the isthmus adenoma, and suture the remaining thyroid tissue with the tracheal anterior tissue for hemostasis, paying attention to protect the recurrent laryngeal nerve.
5. Thorough hemostasis, clean the wound, place 2 T-tubes for drainage, and close the incision in layers.
6. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the thyroid gland, trachea, and recurrent laryngeal nerve. (甲状腺, 气管, 喉返神经)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in thyroid gland dissection and nerve protection were managed with careful technique and attention to detail.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of thyroid gland dissection and nerve protection.
- The technique of ligating and transecting thyroid tissue and vessels.
- The importance of thorough hemostasis and proper wound closure.

## Complication Prevention
- Prevention of nerve injury through careful dissection and identification of the recurrent laryngeal nerve.
- Prevention of postoperative hemorrhage through thorough hemostasis.

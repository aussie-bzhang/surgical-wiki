## Overview
Bilateral breast mass excision is a surgical procedure performed for the removal of breast masses, typically benign, with the aim of diagnosing and treating breast diseases.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Bilateral breast mass
**Intraoperative:** Bilateral breast mass confirmed
**Pathological:** Intraductal papilloma with atypical hyperplasia in both breasts

## Intraoperative Findings
Left breast: 3*4cm nodule, moderate consistency, clear margin; Right breast: 2*3cm mass

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, local anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Arc incision below the left nipple, incising skin, subcutaneous tissue, and breast tissue as described.
3. Arc incision below the right nipple, incising tissue and breast tissue as described.
4. Complete excision of the mass, sent for rapid pathological examination.
5. Adequate hemostasis, count instruments and sponges, close the incision in layers, end of surgery.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: breast tissue, mammary glands. (乳腺组织，乳腺)

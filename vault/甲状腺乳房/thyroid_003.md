## Overview
Total thyroidectomy and isthmectomy for right thyroid adenoma, with emphasis on the identification and protection of the recurrent laryngeal nerve.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right thyroid adenoma
**Intraoperative:** Right thyroid adenoma
**Pathological:** Right and isthmus thyroid adenoma

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a 1.5 cm soft, well-defined, mobile mass at the superior pole of the right thyroid lobe. Frozen section indicated right and isthmus thyroid adenoma.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Arcuate incision 2 cm above the sternal notch.
3. Dissection of skin, subcutaneous tissue, and platysma, flap elevation to the thyroid cartilage superiorly and to the sternal notch inferiorly.
4. Incision along the pretracheal line, opening of the superficial cervical muscle group, and exposure of the thyroid capsule.
5. Dissection of the middle and lower poles of the right thyroid lobe, ligation of the middle and lower thyroid vessels, and extensive dissection of the lateral loose adhesions.
6. Dissection of the superior pole of the right thyroid lobe, ligation of the superior thyroid vessels, elevation of the posterior thyroid capsule, and complete resection of the right and isthmus thyroid glands, with attention to the protection of the right recurrent laryngeal nerve.
7. Thorough hemostasis, wound irrigation, placement of a T-tube, and layered closure of the incision.
8. Completion of the procedure.

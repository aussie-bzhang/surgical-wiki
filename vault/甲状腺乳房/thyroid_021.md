## Overview
A modified radical mastectomy for left breast cancer, involving the removal of the breast, surrounding tissue, and axillary lymph nodes, with a focus on preserving the skin and nipple.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Left breast cancer
**Intraoperative:** Left breast cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a 2.5*2cm hard, poorly demarcated mass in the upper outer quadrant of the left breast. The mass did not invade the pectoralis muscle, and several enlarged lymph nodes were palpable in the axilla.

## Operative Steps
1. The patient was placed in a supine position under general anesthesia, and the skin was routinely disinfected and draped.
2. An incision was made around the breast, extending from below the midpoint of the clavicle to the costal arch.
3. The skin flap was elevated, extending from the clavicle to the costal arch, laterally to the anterior edge of the trapezius muscle, and medially to the sternal border.
4. The breast, surrounding fat, and pectoralis fascia were dissected, and intermuscular lymph nodes were removed.
5. The axilla was opened, the axillary vein was exposed, and the fat and lymphatic tissue anterior and inferior to the axillary vein were removed. The long thoracic nerve, thoracodorsal nerve, and subscapular vessels were preserved.
6. The specimen was removed en bloc, irrigated, and a negative pressure drain was placed in the chest wall and axilla, with the drain exiting through a stab incision.
7. Hemostasis was achieved, instruments and sponges were counted and found to be correct, and the incision was closed in layers. The procedure was completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the breast, axillary lymph nodes, and the long thoracic nerve (胸长神经), thoracodorsal nerve (胸背神经), and subscapular vessels (肩胛下血管). (胆总管)

## Teaching Points
- The importance of preserving the skin and nipple for aesthetic outcomes.
- The meticulous dissection around the axilla to preserve the long thoracic nerve and thoracodorsal nerve.
- The careful removal of the axillary lymph nodes while avoiding injury to the subscapular vessels.

## Complication Prevention
- Infection prevention through meticulous surgical technique and antibiotic prophylaxis.
- Hemorrhage prevention through careful hemostasis and the use of drains.

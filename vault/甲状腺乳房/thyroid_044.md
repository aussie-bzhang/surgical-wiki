## Overview
A surgical procedure involving the subtotal resection of the right thyroid lobe and isthmus for the treatment of a bilateral thyroid nodule, with emphasis on the preservation of the recurrent laryngeal nerve and tracheal integrity.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Bilateral thyroid nodules
**Intraoperative:** Bilateral thyroid nodules
**Pathological:** Chronic lymphocytic thyroiditis (right thyroid)

## Intraoperative Findings
Nodule located at the middle and lower pole of the right thyroid, approximately 1.0 cm in diameter, soft, encapsulated, and mobile. No significant nodules found in the contralateral thyroid.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Arcuate incision made 2 cm above the sternal notch.
3. Dissection of skin, subcutaneous tissue, and platysma, flap elevation up to the thyroid cartilage and down to the sternal notch.
4. Incision along the pretracheal line, opening the superficial muscle group, and exposing the thyroid capsule.
5. Dissection of the middle and lower poles of the right thyroid, transection of thyroid vessels, and complete dissection of the lateral loose adhesions.
6. Transection of the isthmus and hemostasis.
7. Dissection of the superior pole of the right thyroid, transection of thyroid tissue and vessels close to the tumor edge, and resection of the tumor.
8. Suturing of the remaining thyroid tissue and tracheal tissue, hemostasis, and attention to the course of the recurrent laryngeal nerve.
9. Thorough hemostasis, wound irrigation, placement of a 16F T-tube, closure of the pretracheal muscle group, and layered closure of the incision.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: right thyroid, isthmus, trachea, recurrent laryngeal nerve. (胆总管)

## Technical Challenges
Managing the course of the recurrent laryngeal nerve and ensuring adequate resection of the nodule while preserving thyroid tissue.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of identifying and protecting the recurrent laryngeal nerve.
- Proper technique for resection of the thyroid nodule and isthmus.
- Postoperative management and potential complications.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Monitoring of the recurrent laryngeal nerve during dissection.

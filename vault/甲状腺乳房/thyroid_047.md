## Overview
Bilateral thyroidectomy with thyroid fossa dissection is a surgical procedure performed for the removal of thyroid nodules, typically benign, from both lobes of the thyroid gland. It is indicated for large nodules, multinodular goiter, or suspicious lesions.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Bilateral thyroid nodules
**Intraoperative:** Bilateral thyroid multinodular adenoma
**Pathological:** Frozen section (F08-2238): "Left + isthmus, right" thyroid adenoma.

## Intraoperative Findings
Bilateral thyroid and isthmus multiple nodules, maximum diameter about 2cm, soft consistency. Intraoperative frozen pathology as above.

## Operative Steps

1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Make an arc incision 2 cm above the sternal notch; sequentially open the skin, subcutaneous tissue, and cervical platysma, free the skin flap up to the thyroid cartilage and down to the sternal notch.
3. Open the superficial cervical muscle group along the cervical white line, open the thyroid capsule, and explore as found during surgery.
4. Free and transect the thyroid isthmus, ligate and hemostatize; free the middle pole of the left thyroid, transect the left thyroid middle vessels, and thoroughly free the lateral thyroid from the loose adhesions.
5. Determine the resection range of the left thyroid with hemostat forceps, pay attention to the course of the left recurrent laryngeal nerve, resect most of the left thyroid and isthmus within the capsule, retain the superior pole as thyroid, and suture the remaining capsule.
6. Similarly, complete the resection of the right thyroid middle and lower poles.
7. Thoroughly hemostatize, clean the wound, place a negative pressure drain in both thyroid fossae, suture the anterior cervical muscles, and close the incision in layers.
8. Postoperative patient has good voice.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: thyroid gland, thyroid isthmus, recurrent laryngeal nerve. (甲状腺, 甲状腺峡部, 喉返神经)

## Teaching Points
- Proper identification and protection of the recurrent laryngeal nerve.
- Accurate determination of the resection margin to preserve thyroid function.
- Proper hemostasis and wound closure to prevent complications.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Adequate drainage to prevent seroma formation.

## Overview
A surgical procedure for the treatment of breast cancer involving the removal of the entire breast, axillary lymph nodes, and a portion of the chest wall musculature, with the aim of achieving a high rate of local control and survival.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Left breast mass
**Intraoperative:** Left intraductal carcinoma
**Pathological:** Left breast intraductal carcinoma

## Intraoperative Findings
A 5cm mass in the upper outer quadrant of the left breast with unclear borders, hard consistency, and poor mobility. No obvious lymph node enlargement in the left axilla.

## Operative Steps
1. Under local anesthesia, the mass was completely excised and sent for intraoperative frozen section, which showed "left breast intraductal carcinoma," leading to the decision for surgery.
2. General anesthesia was administered, and the surgical site was prepared and draped.
3. A T-shaped incision was made centered on the breast, with the upper end below the midpoint of the clavicle and the lower end reaching the costal arch.
4. The skin flap was elevated, extending superiorly to the clavicle, inferiorly to the aponeurosis of the rectus abdominis, laterally to the anterior edge of the trapezius muscle, and medially to the sternal border.
5. The entire breast, subcutaneous fat, and pectoralis major fascia were dissected off to the lateral border of the pectoralis minor muscle, and the intermuscular lymph nodes were dissected.
6. The axilla was opened, and the subclavian vessels, axillary vessels, and brachial plexus were exposed. The fat and lymphatic tissue in front and below the axillary vein were dissected, and the fat and lymphatic tissue around the subclavian vessels were removed, with attention to protecting the long thoracic nerve and the thoracodorsal nerve.
7. The specimen was removed en bloc, irrigated, and two negative pressure drains were placed in the chest wall and axilla, with the drains brought out through stab wounds.
8. Hemostasis was achieved, instruments and sponges were counted and found to be correct, and the incision was closed in layers. The procedure was completed.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of accurate identification and dissection of the pectoralis major muscle and its fascia.
- The meticulous dissection of the axillary lymph nodes to minimize the risk of lymphedema.
- The protection of critical nerves during the procedure.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hematoma.
- Careful dissection to avoid injury to the brachial plexus and other structures.

## Overview
A surgical procedure for the partial resection of the left thyroid gland and isthmus, performed for a left thyroid adenoma with cystic degeneration.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Left thyroid adenoma
**Intraoperative:** Left thyroid adenoma with cystic degeneration
**Pathological:** Cystic degeneration of adenoma

## Intraoperative Findings
A 3.0cm mass in the middle and lower pole of the left thyroid lobe, soft in consistency, with a complete capsule. Frozen pathology confirmed cystic degeneration. No abnormal mass was found in the right lobe.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Arcuate incision 2 cm above the sternal notch.
3. Sequentially open the skin, subcutaneous tissue, and platysma, freeing the flap up to the thyroid cartilage and down to the sternal notch.
4. Open the superficial muscle group along the cervical white line and the outer capsule of the thyroid gland.
5. Free the middle pole of the left thyroid gland, ligate and transect the middle thyroid vessels, and thoroughly free the lateral loose adhesions of the thyroid gland.
6. Transect the isthmus and ligate the blood supply. Double ligature the proximal end.
7. Use hemostats to determine the resection range of the thyroid gland, paying attention to the course of the left recurrent laryngeal nerve, and resect most of the left thyroid gland in front of the thyroid sheath.
8. Thorough hemostasis, wash the wound, and leave a drain tube. Suture the anterior neck muscles and close the incision in layers.
9. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: thyroid gland, isthmus. (甲状腺,峡部)

## Teaching Points
- The importance of identifying and protecting the left recurrent laryngeal nerve.
- Proper hemostasis techniques to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper resection of the isthmus to prevent recurrence.

## Overview
A surgical procedure for the treatment of breast cancer involving the removal of the breast, surrounding tissue, and axillary lymph nodes, with the aim of preventing recurrence and metastasis.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right breast cancer
**Intraoperative:** Right breast cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Right breast mass resected; no enlarged lymph nodes in the right axilla.

## Operative Steps
1. Place the patient in a supine position under general anesthesia, perform routine disinfection and draping.
2. Make a transverse incision around the breast, extending laterally to the axillary前线 and medially to the sternal border.剥离皮瓣，up to the clavicle, down to the costal arch, laterally to the anterior edge of the trapezius muscle, medially to the sternal border.
3. Dissect the breast, surrounding fat, and pectoralis major fascia to the pectoralis minor muscle, and remove intermuscular lymph nodes.
4. Open the axilla, expose the subclavian vessels, axillary vessels, and brachial plexus, and remove fat and lymphatic tissue in front and below the axillary vein, and fat and lymphatic tissue around the subclavian vessels.
5. Remove the specimen en bloc, irrigate, and place a negative pressure drain in the chest wall and axilla, and bring it out through a stab wound.
6. Perfect hemostasis, count instruments and sponges, close the incision in layers, and complete the operation.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: breast, axillary lymph nodes, pectoralis major muscle, subclavian vessels, axillary vessels, brachial plexus. (乳腺，腋窝淋巴结，胸大肌，锁骨下血管，腋血管，臂丛神经)

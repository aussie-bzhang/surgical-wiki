## Overview
Right thyroidectomy for papillary carcinoma of the right thyroid and isthmus in a patient with a pre-operative diagnosis of a right thyroid nodule.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right thyroid nodule
**Intraoperative:** Right and isthmus thyroid cancer
**Pathological:** Papillary carcinoma of the right thyroid and isthmus

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a 2cm hard nodule in the right thyroid containing dark fluid. No significant abnormalities were found in the left thyroid.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Arcuate incision 2 cm above the sternal notch.
3. Dissection of skin, subcutaneous tissue, and platysma, flap elevation to the thyroid cartilage superiorly and to the sternal notch inferiorly.
4. Incision along the pretracheal line, opening the superficial muscle group, and the thyroid capsule.
5. Dissection of the middle and lower poles of the right thyroid, transection of the middle and lower thyroid vessels, and complete dissection of the lateral loose adhesions.
6. Transection of the isthmus, hemostasis, and attention to the course of the recurrent laryngeal nerve.
7. Dissection of the superior pole of the right thyroid, transection of the superior thyroid vessels, and complete resection of the right thyroid gland.
8. Lymph node dissection including the carotid artery and jugular vein, anterior to the trachea, above the isthmus, posterior to the trachea, around the recurrent laryngeal nerve, and in the tracheoesophageal groove and supraclavicular area.
9. Hemostasis, wound irrigation, placement of a T-tube, and layered closure of the incision.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the right thyroid gland, trachea, jugular veins, and laryngeal nerve. (甲状腺, 气管, 颈静脉, 喉返神经)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered include hemostasis and protection of the recurrent laryngeal nerve.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of careful hemostasis and lymph node dissection.
- Protection of the recurrent laryngeal nerve during dissection.

## Complication Prevention
- Strategies for preventing complications include meticulous hemostasis and careful dissection.

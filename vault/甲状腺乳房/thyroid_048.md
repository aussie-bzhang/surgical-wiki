## Overview
Right thyroidectomy for a right thyroid adenoma, with emphasis on the technique of partial thyroid resection and the protection of the recurrent laryngeal nerve.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right thyroid mass
**Intraoperative:** Right thyroid adenoma
**Pathological:** Right thyroid adenoma

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a firm, encapsulated mass measuring approximately 3 cm in diameter in the middle and lower pole of the right thyroid gland near the isthmus. No abnormalities were found in the contralateral thyroid gland.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Arcuate incision 2 cm above the sternal notch.
3. Dissection of skin, subcutaneous tissue, and platysma, freeing the flap up to the thyroid cartilage and down to the sternal notch.
4. Incision along the pretracheal line, opening the superficial muscle group, and exposing the thyroid capsule.
5. Dissection of the middle and lower poles of the right thyroid gland, ligation of the middle and lower thyroid vessels, and complete dissection of the lateral loose adhesions of the right thyroid gland.
6. Ligation of the isthmus and hemostasis, with attention to the course of the recurrent laryngeal nerve.
7. Dissection of the superior pole of the right thyroid gland, ligation of the superior thyroid vessels, and subtotal resection of the right thyroid gland.
8. Hemostasis, wound irrigation, placement of a T-tube drain in the residual cavity of the right thyroid gland, and layer-by-layer closure of the incision.

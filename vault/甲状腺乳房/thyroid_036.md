## Overview
Right thyroidectomy for postoperative thyroid nodules, key technical feature: complete resection of the right thyroid gland and adjacent lymph nodes.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Postoperative thyroid nodules on the right side
**Intraoperative:** Right thyroid adenoma
**Pathological:** Right thyroid adenoma

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings: Residual right thyroid tissue of approximately 2*2cm, two nodular masses visible, the largest diameter about 0.5cm, surface smooth, no obvious adhesion with surrounding tissue; enlarged lymph nodes in the tracheal vicinity, diameter about 1cm.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Arcuate incision 2 cm above the sternal notch, sequentially opening skin, subcutaneous tissue, and platysma; free the skin flap up to the thyroid cartilage and down to the sternal notch.
3. Open the anterior muscle group along the cervical white line, open the thyroid capsule, and explore as found during surgery.
4. Free the middle and lower poles of the right thyroid gland, transect the middle and lower thyroid vessels, fully free the lateral loose adhesions of the thyroid, ligate and stop bleeding, and pay attention to the course of the recurrent laryngeal nerve.
5. Free the superior pole of the right thyroid gland, transect the superior thyroid vessels, and completely resect the right thyroid gland and enlarged tracheal lymph nodes in front of the posterior capsule.
6. Thorough hemostasis, clean the wound, leave a negative pressure ball drain, and close the incision in layers.
7. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: right thyroid gland, tracheal lymph nodes. (甲状腺, 气管旁淋巴结)

## Teaching Points
- Importance of identifying and protecting the recurrent laryngeal nerve during surgery.
- Proper hemostasis and lymph node dissection technique.

## Complication Prevention
- Prevention of recurrent laryngeal nerve injury: Careful dissection and identification of the nerve.
- Prevention of postoperative hemorrhage: Adequate hemostasis and proper drain placement.

## Overview
A modified radical mastectomy for left breast cancer, involving the removal of the entire breast, axillary lymph nodes, and pectoralis major muscle, with a focus on preserving the skin and nipple.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Left breast cancer
**Intraoperative:** Left breast cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
No residual tumor at the original surgical incision. The procedure went smoothly.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia and routine disinfection and draping.
2. A T-shaped incision centered on the breast, with the upper end below the midpoint of the clavicle and the lower end reaching the costal arch.
3.游离皮瓣, extending from the clavicle to the anterior sheath of the rectus abdominis muscle, laterally to the anterior edge of the trapezius muscle, and medially to the sternocostal angle.
4. The entire breast, subcutaneous fat tissue, and pectoralis major fascia were dissected to the lateral border of the pectoralis minor muscle, with axillary lymph node dissection.
5. The axilla was opened, revealing the subclavian vessels, axillary vessels, and brachial plexus. The fat and lymphatic tissue in front and below the axillary vein were removed, and the fat and lymphatic tissue around the subclavian vessels were cleared, with attention to protecting the long thoracic nerve and thoracodorsal nerve.
6. The entire specimen was removed, flushed, and a negative pressure drain was placed in the chest wall and axilla, with the drain exiting through a stab incision.
7. Hemostasis was achieved, instruments and sponges were counted and found to be correct, and the incision was closed in layers, ending the procedure.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: breast, axillary lymph nodes, pectoralis major muscle. (乳房，腋窝淋巴结，胸大肌)
> Danger zones: subclavian vessels, axillary vessels, brachial plexus. (锁骨下血管，腋血管，臂丛神经)
> Structures requiring protection: long thoracic nerve, thoracodorsal nerve. (胸长神经，胸背神经)

## Teaching Points
- The importance of preserving the skin and nipple for aesthetic outcomes.
- The meticulous dissection around the axillary lymph nodes to minimize complications.
- The careful handling of the brachial plexus to avoid injury.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hematoma.
- Careful dissection to avoid injury to the long thoracic nerve and thoracodorsal nerve.

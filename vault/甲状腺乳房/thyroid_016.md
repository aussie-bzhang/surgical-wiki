## Overview
A right thyroidectomy with isthmus resection was performed for a right thyroid adenoma, involving the removal of part of the thyroid gland and the isthmus.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right thyroid adenoma
**Intraoperative:** Right thyroid adenoma
**Pathological:** "Right thyroid gland + isthmus" adenoma

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperatively, a mass was found in the right thyroid gland with a diameter of about 1.5cm, with a complete capsule, soft texture, clear boundaries, and mobility. No obvious abnormalities were found in the left thyroid.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. An arc-shaped incision was made 2 cm above the sternal notch, and the skin, subcutaneous tissue, and platysma were sequentially opened. The skin flap was freed up to the thyroid cartilage and down to the sternal notch.
3. The anterior muscle group of the neck was opened along the white line of the neck, and the outer capsule of the thyroid gland was opened to explore the findings as during surgery.
4. The middle and lower poles of the right thyroid gland were freed, the middle and lower thyroid vessels were ligated, and the lateral loose adhesions of the thyroid gland were freed up. The isthmus was ligated and止血, and attention was paid to the course of the recurrent laryngeal nerve.
5. The superior pole of the right thyroid gland was freed, the superior thyroid vessels were ligated, and the right thyroid gland and isthmus were completely resected from the anterior capsule.
6. Hemostasis was achieved, the wound was cleaned, and a T-tube drain was left in place. The incision was closed in layers.
7. The operation was completed.

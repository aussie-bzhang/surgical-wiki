## Overview
Total bilateral thyroidectomy with thyroid fossa dissection is a surgical procedure performed for the treatment of bilateral thyroid nodules. It involves the removal of most of the thyroid gland and the isthmus, with preservation of the superior pole of the thyroid gland.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Bilateral thyroid nodules
**Intraoperative:** Bilateral thyroid nodules
**Pathological:** Frozen section: "Nodular goiter involving the left thyroid and isthmus, and right thyroid."

## Intraoperative Findings
Bilateral thyroid nodules located at the middle and lower poles, each measuring approximately 2 cm in diameter, firm, with clear borders and mobile. Intraoperative frozen section as documented.

## Operative Steps

1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Arcuate incision 2 cm above the sternal notch; sequentially incise the skin, subcutaneous tissue, and platysma, and free the flap up to the thyroid cartilage and down to the sternal notch.
3. Open the superficial cervical fascia along the white line of the neck, expose the thyroid capsule, and explore as found during surgery.
4. Free and transect the thyroid isthmus, ligate and hemostatize; free the middle pole of the left thyroid, transect the left thyroid middle vessels, and thoroughly mobilize the lateral loose adhesions of the left thyroid.
5. Determine the resection range of the left thyroid with hemostats, pay attention to the course of the recurrent laryngeal nerve, and resect most of the left thyroid and isthmus within the capsule, preserving the superior pole of the thyroid gland and suture the remaining capsule.
6. Similarly, complete the resection of the middle and lower poles of the right thyroid.
7. Thorough hemostasis, clean the wound, and place a 16 French T-tube in each thyroid fossa; suture the anterior cervical muscles, and close the incision in layers.
8. Operation completed.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of preserving the recurrent laryngeal nerve during surgery.
- Proper hemostasis and wound closure techniques.
- The significance of frozen section pathology in guiding resection.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful dissection to avoid injury to the recurrent laryngeal nerve.
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.

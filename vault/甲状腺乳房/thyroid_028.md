## Overview
A surgical procedure for the treatment of right thyroid cancer and left thyroid adenoma, involving resection of the affected thyroid lobes and lymph node dissection.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Bilateral thyroid mass
**Intraoperative:** Right thyroid cancer + left thyroid adenoma
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a 1cm cystic mass in the middle pole of the left thyroid, with clear borders and mobility. A 1cm hard mass was found in the middle and superior pole of the right thyroid, with additional 1cm hard masses at the inferior and posterior aspect. Frozen section pathology indicated: "Right thyroid papillary carcinoma (1.2*1.0*0.7cm), with lymph node metastasis in one lymph node of the right thyroid. Left thyroid adenoma."

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Arc-shaped incision made 2 cm above the sternal notch, sequentially opening the skin, subcutaneous tissue, and platysma. The skin flap is freed up to the thyroid cartilage and down to the sternal notch.
3. The superficial cervical muscles are opened along the white line of the neck, and the outer capsule of the thyroid is opened to explore the findings.
4. The middle and lower poles of the right thyroid are freed, the thyroid vessels are ligated, and the lateral loose adhesions are freed. The isthmus is ligated and sutured for hemostasis, with attention to the course of the recurrent laryngeal nerve.
5. The superior pole of the right thyroid is freed, the thyroid vessels are ligated, and the left thyroid and isthmus are completely resected in front of the posterior capsule.
6. The middle pole of the left thyroid is freed, the thyroid vessels are ligated, and the lateral loose adhesions are freed. The isthmus is ligated and sutured for hemostasis. The superior pole is ligated and tied with a double knot. The thyroid resection range is confirmed with hemostatic forceps, with attention to the course of the recurrent laryngeal nerve. A portion of the left thyroid is resected in front of the laryngeal capsule.
7. Lymph node dissection is performed in the pre-tracheal, superior to the isthmus of the thyroid, posterior tracheal, around the recurrent laryngeal nerve, and in the tracheoesophageal groove and supraclavicular area.
8. Hemostasis is achieved, the wound is cleaned, a negative pressure ball is left in place, and the incision is closed in layers.
9. The procedure is completed.

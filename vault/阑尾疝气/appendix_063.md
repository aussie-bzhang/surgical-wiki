## Overview
Repair of left inguinal indirect hernia and incisional hernia; clinical context: hernia repair; key technical feature: mesh repair.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Left inguinal hernia
**Intraoperative:** Left indirect inguinal hernia + incisional hernia
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Left inguinal hernia located lateral to the inferior epigastric artery, hernia sac diameter 2.0 cm, hernia ring diameter 2.0 cm, contents reduced; additional defect of 2 cm diameter below the pubic tubercle, lateral to the medial edge of the rectus muscle, beneath the old abdominal incision.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia achieved; patient placed in supine position; routine disinfection and draping.
2. Incision along the left inguinal inner and outer ring; skin, subcutaneous tissue, and external oblique aponeurosis opened in layers.
3. Inguinal ligament, conjoint tendon, and arcuate margin separated; round ligament preserved.
4. Hernia sac identified and opened; abdominal wall defect at inferior epigastric artery explored and peritoneum opened; extraperitoneal hand-sewn repair performed.
5. Left indirect hernia sac freed to neck; sac wall freed; purse-string suture at neck.
6. Conjoint tendon and inguinal ligament sutured together with interrupted non-absorbable suture; last stitch fixed to the pubic tubercle, reshaping the internal ring, allowing the tip of a finger to pass.
7. Hemostasis achieved; aponeurosis, subcutaneous tissue, and skin closed in layers.
8. Procedure completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Inguinal canal (腹股沟管), inguinal ligament (腹股沟韧带), ilioinguinal nerve (髂腹下神经), iliopsoas muscle (髂腰肌), sacrum (骶骨), iliac crest (髂嵴).

## Teaching Points
- Proper identification and preservation of the round ligament.
- Accurate identification and repair of the hernia sac and abdominal wall defect.
- Proper placement and tensioning of the mesh.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hematoma.
- Proper mesh placement to prevent recurrence.

## Overview
Appendectomy for acute suppurative appendicitis with perforation, key technical feature: identification and resection of the appendix.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Acute appendicitis
**Intraoperative:** Acute suppurative appendicitis with perforation
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings: The appendix was located below the cecum, measuring 6.0 cm in length and 0.8 cm in diameter. It was congested and edematous, covered with purulent exudate, with gangrenous perforation. There was 15 ml of purulent exudate around the appendix and in the pelvic cavity.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, lumbar anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Right lower quadrant McBurney's point oblique incision, layer-by-layer opening, aspiration of exudate, exploration as above.
3. Elevate the appendix head, ligate the appendiceal mesentery in segments to the root of the appendix.
4. Cut the appendix 0.5 cm from the root, remove the specimen, ligate and suture the root of the appendix, cauterize the stump with electrocautery, and cover with a small amount of ileal lipoma.
5. Thorough hemostasis, layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen.
6. End of surgery.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: appendix, ileocecal junction. (阑尾, 回盲部)

## Overview
Appendectomy with colostomy for acute appendicitis with cecal perforation, highlighting the technical aspects of appendectomy and colostomy creation.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Acute appendicitis
**Intraoperative:** Acute appendicitis with cecal perforation
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a perforation at the base of the appendix, with necrotic cecal wall measuring approximately 4mm in diameter at the proximal end of the perforation. The cecal wall was thick and presented as a mass. The distal appendix was adherent to the surrounding omentum and mesenteric fat pads, forming a mass. There was a small amount of purulent exudate in the area.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, spinal-epidural anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Right lower quadrant McBurney incision, layer-by-layer abdominal entry, exploration as described.
3. Locate the perforation site of the appendix, resect the distal appendiceal tissue.
4. Resect the proximal appendiceal tissue and part of the cecal wall for pathological examination. Place a 22F mushroom-shaped urinary catheter into the perforated cecum, cover it with a small amount of ileal lipoma, and drain through an additional incision.
5. Thorough hemostasis, layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen.
6. Postoperative.

## Overview
Appendectomy for chronic appendicitis; key technical feature: identification and resection of the appendix with attention to ileocecal junction.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Chronic appendicitis
**Intraoperative:** Chronic appendicitis with adhesions and congestion
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Right lower quadrant adhesions, congestion, and edema noted. Appendix located within the cecum, approximately 6cm long, 0.8cm in diameter. No significant peritoneal effusion detected.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, spinal-epidural anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Right lower quadrant McBurney's point transverse incision, layer-by-layer opening, aspiration of effusion, exploration as above.
3. Dissection of adhesions around the appendix, lifting the appendix head, ligating the appendiceal mesentery in segments to the root of the appendix.
4. Ligating the appendiceal root 0.5cm from the root, cutting the appendix, removing the specimen, electrocautery of the residual end, omental pedicle buried over the appendiceal root, and covering the residual end with the ileocecal valve.
5. Thorough hemostasis, layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen.
6. Completion of surgery. Operative difficulty noted.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: appendix, ileocecal junction. (阑尾，回盲部)

- Thorough hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage

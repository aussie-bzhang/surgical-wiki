## Overview
Repair of an inguinal hernia due to incarceration, with tension-free mesh repair, in a patient presenting with right inguinal hernia.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right inguinal hernia incarceration
**Intraoperative:** Right inguinal hernia incarceration confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Right inguinal hernia with incarcerated hernia content. Hernia sac size approximately 4*3*3cm.

## Operative Steps
1. After successful anesthesia, the patient is placed in a supine position and the surgical site is conventionally disinfected and draped.
2. A right inguinal ring to pubic tubercle incision is made, and the skin, subcutaneous tissue, and external oblique aponeurosis are sequentially opened.
3. Adhesions in the right inguinal region are separated, the inguinal ligament, conjoint tendon, and arcuate margin are identified, and the spermatic cord is mobilized and protected.
4. The hernia sac is found and opened to the neck, the sac wall is mobilized, and a purse-string suture is placed near the hernia ring. The plug is sutured to the neck of the hernia sac and inserted into the internal ring.
5. The mesh is fixed posterior to the spermatic cord, the lowest suture is anchored to the pubic tubercle, and the mesh is sutured below to the pectineal ligament and inguinal ligament, and above to the conjoint tendon and arcuate margin. The mesh replaces the internal ring, allowing the tip of the little finger to pass through.
6. Hemostasis is achieved, and the aponeurosis, subcutaneous tissue, and skin are closed in layers.
7. The procedure is completed.

## Teaching Points
- Proper identification and mobilization of the spermatic cord
- Correct placement of the plug and mesh
- Achieving hemostasis and proper layer closure

- Proper identification and protection of the ilioinguinal nerve and femoral nerve
- Ensuring proper mesh tension and fixation

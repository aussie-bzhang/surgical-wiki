## Overview
An open surgical repair of an inguinal hernia with reduction and tension-free repair using a MK patch, addressing an incarcerated inguinal hernia.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Incarcerated inguinal hernia
**Intraoperative:** Incarcerated inguinal hernia
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Right lower quadrant inguinal hernia with a thinning of the external oblique aponeurosis, forming a defect. Hernia sac measuring 6*4 cm, with edematous and thickened tissue, containing a small amount of clear fluid. Hernia ring diameter 2.0 cm, with small bowel as content, which had been reduced. No necrosis observed in the entire small bowel during exploration.

## Operative Steps
1. After successful anesthesia, the patient is placed in a supine position, and the surgical site is conventionally disinfected and draped.
2. A longitudinal incision is made along the hernia sac surface, and the skin, subcutaneous tissue, and external oblique aponeurosis are sequentially opened.
3. The hernia sac and neck are mobilized, the hernia sac is opened, and the findings are as described above. Excess hernia sac wall is excised.
4. The extraperitoneal preperitoneal space is mobilized, and the patch is placed and sutured and fixed to the surrounding tissue.
5. Hemostasis is achieved, and the external oblique aponeurosis, subcutaneous tissue, and skin are closed.
6. The procedure is completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Inguinal canal (腹股沟管), external oblique aponeurosis (腹外斜肌腱膜)

## Teaching Points
- Proper mobilization and closure of the external oblique aponeurosis to prevent recurrence.
- Accurate placement and fixation of the patch to ensure tension-free repair.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hematoma.
- Proper placement of the patch to avoid tension and recurrence.

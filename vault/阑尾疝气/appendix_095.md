## Overview
Appendectomy for acute appendicitis with peritonitis. Key technical feature: identification and resection of the inflamed appendix with preservation of surrounding structures.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Acute appendicitis
**Intraoperative:** Acute appendicitis with peritonitis
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings: The cecum was elongated and extended to the right lower quadrant. The appendix was located posterior to the ileum, measuring approximately 12 cm in length and 1.0 cm in diameter. The appendix was gangrenous with a full-thickness perforation. The appendix was adherent to surrounding tissues. There was purulent, malodorous effusion of 50 ml around the appendix and in the right lower quadrant.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Right lower quadrant McBurney's point incision, layer-by-layer abdominal entry, exploration as described, and extension of the incision upwards.
3. Identification and dissection of the appendix, ligation of the appendiceal mesentery to the base of the appendix.
4. At 0.5 cm from the base of the appendix, the appendix was transected, the specimen removed, and the base ligated and sutured. The stump was cauterized with an electrosurgical device and covered with a small amount of ileal lipoma.
5. Complete hemostasis, layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen.
6. Completion of the procedure.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: appendix, ileum, cystic duct, common bile duct. (胆总管)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered:粘连松解困难，阑尾坏疽穿孔，周围组织炎症明显。

## Teaching Points
- Identification and preservation of surrounding structures during appendectomy.
- Management of gangrenous appendix with perforation.
- Importance of complete hemostasis and proper closure.

## Complication Prevention
- Prevention of postoperative infection and ileus by ensuring proper surgical technique and postoperative care.

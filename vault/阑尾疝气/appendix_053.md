## Overview
Repair of an inguinal hernia with tension-free mesh repair, a common procedure in general surgery, particularly in the colorectal subspecialty.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right inguinal recurrent hernia
**Intraoperative:** Right inguinal recurrent hernia
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Right inguinal region with adhesions, anatomical structures unclear. Right hernia sac diameter 3cm, contents reduced. Hernia ring diameter 2cm, sac neck located medially to the inferior epigastric artery.

## Operative Steps
1. After successful anesthesia, the patient is placed in a supine position, and the surgical site is conventionally disinfected and draped.
2. The original surgical incision (line connecting the external and internal rings) is used. Adhesions are dissected, and the skin, subcutaneous tissue, and external oblique aponeurosis are opened in layers.
3. Adhesions are carefully released, the spermatic cord is mobilized, and the hernia sac is mobilized to the sac neck. The transversalis fascia is opened, and the preperitoneal space is mobilized. A purse-string suture is made at the hernia ring, and the Kugel hernia mesh is placed in the preperitoneal space and fixed to surrounding tissues with several sutures.
4. A flat sheet is placed posterior to the spermatic cord, the lowest suture is fixed to the pubic tubercle, and the sheet is sutured below to the inguinal ligament and above to the conjoint tendon and arcuate line, reconstructing the internal ring, which can accommodate the tip of the little finger.
5. Hemorrhage is controlled, and the aponeurosis, subcutaneous tissue, and skin are closed in layers.
6. The procedure is completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Inguinal canal, inguinal hernia sac, iliopsoas muscle, inguinal ligament. (腹股沟管，腹股沟疝囊，髂腰肌，腹股沟韧带)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in releasing adhesions and mobilizing the hernia sac.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of careful dissection and identification of anatomical structures.
- Proper placement and fixation of the mesh to prevent recurrence.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hematoma.
- Ensuring proper mesh placement to prevent mesh migration and recurrence.

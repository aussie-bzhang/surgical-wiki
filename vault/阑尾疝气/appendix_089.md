## Overview
Appendectomy for acute appendicitis; clinical context: right lower quadrant pain; key technical feature: identification and resection of the appendix.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Acute appendicitis
**Intraoperative:** Acute appendicitis confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings: appendix located at the lower part of the cecum, congested and edematous, covered with purulent exudate, approximately 12 cm long, 1.0 cm in diameter, with 30 ml of purulent exudate around it.

## Operative Steps
1. After spinal anesthesia, the patient is placed in a supine position, and the abdomen is routinely disinfected and draped.
2. A McBurney's point incision is made in the right lower quadrant, and the layers are opened sequentially to explore the findings.
3. The appendix head is lifted, and the mesentery is ligated in segments up to the root of the appendix.
4. The appendix is cut 0.5 cm from the root, the specimen is removed, the root is ligated and sutured for reinforcement, the stump is cauterized with an electrosurgical unit, and covered with an ileocecal lip.
5. Hemostasis is achieved, and the abdomen is closed in layers.
6. The procedure is completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: appendix, ileocecal junction. (阑尾，回盲部)

## Teaching Points
- Identification and safe resection of the appendix
- Proper hemostasis and closure techniques

## Complication Prevention
- Proper identification and ligation of the appendiceal vessels
- Adequate drainage of purulent exudate

## Overview
Repair of right inguinal hernia using McVay technique in a patient with clinical presentation of right inguinal indirect hernia.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right inguinal hernia
**Intraoperative:** Right inguinal indirect hernia
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Hernia sac size approximately 6*3cm, hernia ring diameter approximately 2.0cm, located lateral to the inferior epigastric artery, hernia contents reduced.

## Operative Steps
1. After successful spinal anesthesia, the patient is placed in supine position, and the skin and subcutaneous tissue are incised in a routine manner.
2. The skin, subcutaneous tissue, and external oblique aponeurosis of the right inguinal hernia are sequentially opened.
3. The spermatic cord is mobilized, the hernia sac is found and opened to the neck, the sac wall is mobilized, a purse-string suture is made at the hernia ring, and the sac is ligated high.
4. The transversalis fascia is opened to expose the pectineal line, and a GORE patch is placed and sutured to the pectineal line. Two interrupted sutures are placed at the pubic tubercle for fixation, and the patch is sutured to the inguinal ligament and conjoint tendon in a running manner.
5. Hemostasis is achieved, and the sponges and instruments are counted and found to be correct. The aponeurosis, subcutaneous tissue, and skin are closed in layers in front of the spermatic cord.
6. The procedure is completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Inguinal canal (腹股沟管), inguinal ligament (腹股沟韧带), pectineal line (耻骨梳韧带)

## Technical Challenges
Not documented

## Teaching Points
- Proper mobilization and closure of the spermatic cord
- Correct placement and fixation of the mesh
- Attention to hemostasis and reduction of the hernia sac

## Complication Prevention
- Proper identification and closure of the hernia sac
- Adequate mesh fixation to prevent recurrence

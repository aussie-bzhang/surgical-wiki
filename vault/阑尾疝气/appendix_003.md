## Overview
Right inguinal hernia reduction and tension-free repair; clinical context: inguinal hernia; key technical feature: tension-free mesh repair.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right inguinal hernia
**Intraoperative:** Right inguinal hernia
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Hernia sac located at the inguinal canal, size 3*2cm. Content was small intestine, purple-red. After reduction, the intestine was pink, with good elasticity and peristalsis. No abnormalities in mesentery and blood supply.

## Operative Steps
1. Anesthesia successful, patient in supine position, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Right inguinal ring to pubic tubercle incision, sequentially opening skin, subcutaneous tissue, external oblique aponeurosis.
3. Dissect uterus round ligament, incise inguinal ligament, find hernia sac between inguinal ligament and transversalis fascia, open hernia sac to the neck, explore as described.
4. Free hernia sac wall, perform purse-string suture at the hernia ring, suture plug to the neck of the hernia sac and insert into the internal ring.
5. Fix mesh posterior to the uterus round ligament, the lowest suture fixed to the pubic tubercle, open transversalis fascia, suture below to the pubic cresta ligament, above to the conjoint tendons and arcuate margin, reshape the internal ring, allowing the tip of the little finger to pass.
6. Thorough hemostasis, close aponeurosis, subcutaneous tissue, and skin in layers.
7. Operation completed.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of tension-free mesh repair in preventing recurrence.
- Proper handling of the hernia sac and surrounding structures to minimize complications.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hematoma.
- Careful dissection to avoid injury to surrounding structures.

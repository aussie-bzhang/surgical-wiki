## Overview
Repair of an inguinal hernia, clinical context of abdominal wall weakness, key technical feature of tension-free repair using plug and mesh.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right inguinal hernia
**Intraoperative:** Right inguinal direct hernia
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Hernia sac located in Hesselbach's triangle, lateral to the inferior epigastric artery, hernia ring size 4.5cm, hernia sac 4.5*5*5cm3.

## Operative Steps
1. After anesthesia, the patient is placed in a supine position and the surgical site is disinfected and draped.
2. A right oblique incision along the line connecting the internal and external inguinal rings is made, and the skin, subcutaneous tissue, and external oblique aponeurosis are sequentially opened.
3. The spermatic cord is freed, and the hernia sac is found medially and fully mobilized to the neck of the sac.
4. The transversalis fascia is opened, and the pectineal ligament is exposed.
5. The plug is sutured and fixed to the neck of the hernia sac and inserted into the hernia ring.
6. The mesh is fixed posterior to the spermatic cord, with the lowest suture fixed to the pubic tubercle, and the upper suture sutured to the pectineal ligament and the inferior border of the adductor longus muscle, reshaping the internal inguinal ring to accommodate the tip of the little finger.
7. Hemostasis is achieved.
8. Sutures and instruments are counted and found to be correct.
9. The aponeurosis of the external oblique muscle, subcutaneous tissue, and skin are closed in layers.
10. The operation is completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Inguinal canal, inguinal ring, inguinal ligament, pubic tubercle (耻骨结节)

Not documented

## Teaching Points
- The importance of identifying and mobilizing the hernia sac.
- The technique of tension-free repair using plug and mesh.
- The proper placement of the mesh to prevent recurrence.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper hemostasis to prevent hematoma.
- Ensuring proper mesh placement to prevent postoperative pain and recurrence.

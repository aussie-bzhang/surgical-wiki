## Overview
Appendectomy for acute suppurative appendicitis; key technical feature: identification and resection of the inflamed appendix.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Acute appendicitis
**Intraoperative:** Acute suppurative appendicitis
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings: The appendix was located below the cecum, measuring approximately 8 cm in length and 1 cm in diameter. It was significantly congested and edematous with a surface covered in pus and fecalith impaction. There was approximately 10 ml of peritoneal pus.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, routine disinfection and draping under combined spinal-epidural anesthesia.
2. Right lower quadrant McBurney's point oblique incision to enter the abdomen, layer by layer opened, absorbent sponges used to remove exudate, exploration as described.
3. The appendix head was lifted, the mesentery of the appendix was ligated in segments to the base of the appendix.
4. The appendix was cut 0.5 cm from the base, the specimen removed, the base ligated, the stump cauterized with an electrosurgical unit and sutured, covered with a small amount of ileal omentum.
5. After thorough hemostasis, instruments and sponges were counted and confirmed, the abdomen was closed in layers.
6. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: appendix, ileocecal junction. (阑尾，回盲部)

- Identification and safe resection of the inflamed appendix.

## Complication Prevention
- Infection prevention: thorough disinfection and sterile technique.
- Hemorrhage prevention: careful hemostasis during the procedure.

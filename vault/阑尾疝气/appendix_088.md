## Overview
Right inguinal hernia repair, clinical context of inguinal hernia, key technical feature of tension-free repair.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right inguinal hernia
**Intraoperative:** Right inguinal hernia confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Hernia sac 6*3cm, containing small intestine, hernia ring diameter 2cm, located lateral to the inferior epigastric artery.

## Operative Steps
1. Patient placed in supine position after successful anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Incision made from 2cm above the mid-point of the right inguinal ligament to the line connecting the right pubic tubercle.
3. Abdominal oblique aponeurosis opened, spermatic cord freed, hernia sac found superior to the spermatic cord, freed to the neck of the sac, and preperitoneal space freed.
4. MK patch placed between peritoneum and transversalis fascia, fixation band sutured to inguinal ligament laterally and to conjoint tendon medially.
5. MK patch placed posterior to the spermatic cord, first suture fixed to the pubic tubercle, sutured to inguinal ligament laterally and conjoint tendon medially, and internal orifice reconstructed.
6. Bleeding from incision and wound checked, no active bleeding, sponges and instruments counted correctly.
7. Aponeurosis of the external oblique muscle sutured, external orifice reconstructed.
8. Subcutaneous tissue and skin sutured in layers.
9. Operation completed.

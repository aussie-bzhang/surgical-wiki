## Overview
Repair of an indirect inguinal hernia with tension-free mesh repair, involving the inguinal canal, inguinal ligament, and surrounding structures.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right inguinal hernia
**Intraoperative:** Right indirect inguinal hernia
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Hernia sac located lateral to the inferior epigastric artery, approximately 3 cm in diameter, containing minimal serous fluid and reduced contents. The sac distal end extends into the scrotum.

## Operative Steps
1. After successful anesthesia, the patient is placed in a supine position and the surgical site is prepared.
2. A right inguinal hernia incision is made, and the skin, subcutaneous tissue, and external oblique aponeurosis are sequentially opened.
3. The spermatic cord is mobilized, the cremaster muscle is opened, and the hernia sac is identified and opened, with care taken to protect the vas deferens.
4. The hernia sac wall is mobilized to the neck, and an intracorporeal purse-string suture is placed at the hernia ring.
5. A GORE tension-free mesh is placed posterior to the spermatic cord, with the two inner sutures fixed to the pubic tubercle, and the lower part sutured to the inguinal ligament in a running suture, and the upper part to the conjoint tendon and arcuate line in a running suture, reshaping the internal ring orifice to accommodate the tip of the little finger.
6. Hemostasis is achieved, and the aponeurosis, subcutaneous tissue, and skin are closed in layers.
7. Intradermal suture, and the procedure is completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Inguinal canal (腹股沟管), inguinal ligament (腹股沟韧带), ilioinguinal nerve (髂腹下神经), femoral nerve (股神经), testis (睾丸)

## Technical Challenges
Not documented

## Teaching Points
- Proper identification and protection of the vas deferens during hernia sac mobilization.
- Accurate placement and tension-free fixation of the mesh to prevent recurrence.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent hematoma.
- Proper mesh placement to prevent postoperative pain and recurrence.

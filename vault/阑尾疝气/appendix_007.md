## Overview
Appendectomy for acute suppurative appendicitis in a 25-week pregnant patient. Key technical feature includes identification and safe resection of the appendix.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Pregnancy-related appendicitis
**Intraoperative:** Acute suppurative appendicitis
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperatively, the appendix was located in the ileocecal region, approximately two fingerbreadths above the umbilicus, measuring 10 cm in length and 0.8 cm in diameter. The entire appendix was significantly inflamed and edematous without necrosis or perforation. There was approximately 30 ml of inflammatory exudate in the abdominal cavity.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, spinal-epidural anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Incision one fingerbreadth above the umbilicus, sequentially opening layers, aspirating exudate, and exploring as described.
3. Elevate the appendix head, ligate the appendiceal mesentery in segments to the root of the appendix.
4. Ligate and suture the root of the appendix 0.5 cm from the root, cut the appendix, remove the specimen, and electrocauterize and bury the residual end.
5. Achieve hemostasis, sequentially close the abdomen.
6. Procedure completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the appendix and ileocecal junction. The common bile duct (胆总管) should be protected during the procedure.

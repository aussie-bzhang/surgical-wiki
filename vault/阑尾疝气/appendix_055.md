## Overview
Repair of an inguinal hernia using tension-free mesh plug placement, addressing a right indirect inguinal hernia.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right inguinal hernia
**Intraoperative:** Right indirect inguinal hernia
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Right hernia sac measuring 4*2*2cm, contents reduced, inguinal ring diameter 1.0cm, located lateral to the inferior epigastric artery.

## Operative Steps
1. After successful anesthesia, the patient is placed in a supine position and the surgical site is prepared.
2. A right oblique incision along the line connecting the internal and external inguinal rings is made, and the skin, subcutaneous tissue, and external oblique aponeurosis are sequentially opened.
3. The spermatic cord is mobilized, the cremaster muscle is incised, and the hernia sac is found and opened to the neck.
4. The hernia sac wall is mobilized, a purse-string suture is made at the inguinal ring, and the plug is sutured to the neck of the hernia sac and inserted into the internal ring.
5. The mesh is fixed posterior to the spermatic cord, the lowest suture is anchored to the pubic tubercle, and the mesh is sutured to the inguinal ligament inferiorly and to the conjoint tendon and arcuate line superiorly to reshape the internal ring, allowing the tip of the little finger to pass.
6. Hemostasis is achieved, and the aponeurosis, subcutaneous tissue, and skin are closed in layers.
7. The procedure is completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Inguinal canal (腹股沟管), inguinal ring (疝环), inguinal ligament (腹股沟韧带), ilioinguinal nerve (髂腹下神经), femoral nerve (股神经) (胆总管)

## Technical Challenges
Not documented

## Teaching Points
- The importance of identifying the correct inguinal ring and hernia sac.
- Proper placement and tension-free fixation of the mesh plug.
- The significance of hemostasis and layer closure.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper identification and protection of the ilioinguinal and femoral nerves.
- Ensuring tension-free placement of the mesh to prevent postoperative pain and recurrence.

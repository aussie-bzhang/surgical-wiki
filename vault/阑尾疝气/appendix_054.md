## Overview
Repair of an inguinal hernia with tension-free technique in a patient with a left indirect inguinal hernia.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Left inguinal hernia
**Intraoperative:** Left indirect inguinal hernia
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Hernia sac located lateral to the inferior epigastric artery, approximately 5 cm in diameter. Hernia sac contained omental content and a small amount of serous fluid. The distal end of the hernia sac extended into the scrotum.

## Operative Steps
1. After successful anesthesia, the patient is placed in a supine position and the surgical site is prepared with routine disinfection and draping.
2. A right inguinal hernia incision is made, and the skin, subcutaneous tissue, and external oblique aponeurosis are sequentially opened.
3. The spermatic cord is mobilized, the cremaster muscle is opened, and the hernia sac is identified and opened, with care taken to protect the vas deferens.
4. The hernia sac wall is mobilized to the neck of the sac, and an internal purse-string suture is placed at the hernia ring.
5. The extraperitoneal space is mobilized, and the Kugel inner patch is placed.
6. The outer patch is placed posterior to the spermatic cord, with the most inner two sutures fixed to the pubic tubercle, and the lower part sutured to the inguinal ligament in an interrupted manner, and the upper part sutured to the conjoint tendon and arcuate line.
7. Hemostasis is achieved, and the external oblique aponeurosis, subcutaneous tissue, and skin are closed in layers.
8. The procedure is completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Inguinal canal (腹股沟管), inguinal ligament (腹股沟韧带), ilioinguinal nerve (髂腹下神经), iliopsoas muscle (髂腰肌). (胆总管)

## Teaching Points
- The importance of protecting the vas deferens during hernia sac mobilization.
- The technique of tension-free repair using a Kugel patch.
- The importance of proper hemostasis and layer closure.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper identification and protection of the vas deferens.
- Ensuring adequate reduction of the hernia sac.
- Proper placement and tensioning of the patch.

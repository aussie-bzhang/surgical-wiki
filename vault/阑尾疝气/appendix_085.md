## Overview
Appendectomy for acute appendicitis with significant inflammation and abscess formation.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Abdominal pain of unknown origin
**Intraoperative:** Acute appendicitis
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperatively, the appendix was located at the lower part of the cecum, measuring approximately 8 cm in length and 0.8 cm in diameter. The body of the appendix was necrotic and perforated, with omentum and cecum scattered with pus plaques. The surrounding small bowel and cecum walls were severely inflamed and edematous, with friable tissue prone to bleeding. The base was still viable. There was approximately 80 ml of purulent exudate in the abdominal cavity.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Incision at the right lower quadrant, McBurney point slightly medially, layer by layer abdominal exploration.
3. Locate the appendix, separate adhesions, ligate the appendiceal mesentery to the base of the appendix.
4. Cut the appendix 0.5 cm from the base, remove the specimen, ligate the base, suture and reinforce with a knot, cauterize the stump with electrocautery and perform a purse-string suture, cover with a small amount of ileocecal fat.
5. Thorough hemostasis, close the abdomen in layers.
6. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: appendix, ileocecal junction. (阑尾, 回盲部)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included friable tissue prone to bleeding and extensive inflammation.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of thorough hemostasis and proper appendiceal stump management.
- Recognition of complications such as perforation and abscess formation.

## Complication Prevention
- Thorough hemostasis and proper appendiceal stump management to prevent postoperative hemorrhage and infection.

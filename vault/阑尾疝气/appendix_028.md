## Overview
Repair of bilateral indirect inguinal hernias using tension-free mesh repair, a common surgical procedure in general surgery.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Bilateral indirect inguinal hernia
**Intraoperative:** Bilateral indirect inguinal hernia
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Right hernia sac measuring 8*3cm containing small intestine, hernia ring diameter 2.0cm, located lateral to the inferior epigastric artery. Left hernia sac diameter 2.0cm, contents reduced, hernia ring diameter 1.0cm, located lateral to the inferior epigastric artery.

## Operative Steps
1. After successful anesthesia, the patient is placed in a supine position and the surgical site is prepared.
2. An incision is made along the right inguinal ring, and the skin, subcutaneous tissue, and external oblique aponeurosis are opened layer by layer, with care taken to protect the iliohypogastric and ilioinguinal nerves.
3. The cremaster muscle is incised, and the spermatic cord is freed while being protected.
4. The hernia sac is identified and opened to the neck, the sac wall is freed, and the sac is transected. The distal end is cauterized, and the proximal end is sutured in a purse-string fashion near the hernia ring.
5. The transversalis fascia is opened, and the pectineal ligament is exposed.
6. The mesh is fixed posterior to the spermatic cord, with the lowest suture anchored to the pubic tubercle. The mesh is sutured to the pectineal ligament and the inguinal ligament below, and to the conjoint tendon and arcuate line above, replacing the internal ring. The mesh allows passage of the tip of the little finger.
7. Hemostasis is achieved, and the aponeurosis, subcutaneous tissue, and skin are closed in layers.
8. The left inguinal hernia repair is performed similarly to the right.
9. The procedure is completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Inguinal canal (腹股沟管), inguinal ring (腹股沟环), iliohypogastric nerve (髂腹下神经), ilioinguinal nerve (髂腹股沟神经)

## Technical Challenges
Managing the tension-free placement of the mesh and ensuring proper hemostasis.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of identifying and protecting the iliohypogastric and ilioinguinal nerves.
- Proper placement and tension-free fixation of the mesh.
- The technique of purse-string suture for the hernia sac.

## Complication Prevention
- Ensuring proper hemostasis to prevent hematoma.
- Proper placement of the mesh to prevent recurrence.

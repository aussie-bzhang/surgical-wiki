## Overview
Repair of right inguinal hernia with indication for hernia ring release, high ligation of the sac, tension-free repair using mesh.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right inguinal hernia
**Intraoperative:** Right inguinal hernia
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Hernia sac located lateral to the inferior epigastric artery, approximately 4*3cm, hernia sac extends into the scrotum, contents have been reduced.

## Operative Steps
1. After successful anesthesia, the patient is placed in a supine position, the skin is disinfected and draped.
2. A right oblique incision along the line connecting the internal and external inguinal rings is made, and the skin, subcutaneous tissue, and external oblique aponeurosis are sequentially opened.
3. The inguinal ligament and conjoint tendon and arcuate margin are separated, the spermatic cord is freed and protected.
4. The hernia sac is found and opened to the neck, the sac wall is freed, the hernia ring is released, and a purse-string suture is made near the hernia ring.
5. The mesh is fixed posterior to the spermatic cord, the lowest suture is fixed to the pubic tubercle, and the mesh is sutured below to the pectineal ligament and inguinal ligament, and above to the conjoint tendon and arcuate margin, replacing the internal inguinal ring, allowing the tip of the little finger to pass through.
6. Hemostasis is achieved, the external oblique aponeurosis is closed, a negative pressure ball is placed at the base of the hernia sac in the scrotum, and the external oblique aponeurosis, subcutaneous tissue, and skin are closed in layers.
7. The procedure is completed.

- Proper identification and protection of the spermatic cord
- Accurate placement of the mesh to prevent recurrence
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hematoma

## Complication Prevention
- Proper mesh placement to prevent mesh erosion
- Adequate postoperative pain management

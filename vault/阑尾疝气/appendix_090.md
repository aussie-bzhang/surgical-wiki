## Overview
Repair of an inguinal hernia with tension-free technique in a patient presenting with a right indirect inguinal hernia.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right inguinal hernia
**Intraoperative:** Right indirect inguinal hernia
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Hernia sac measuring 8*5*3 cm, narrow hernia ring located lateral to the inferior epigastric artery and posterior to the spermatic cord. Hernia contents were reduced.

## Operative Steps
1. After successful anesthesia, the patient was placed in a supine position and the surgical site was routinely disinfected and draped.
2. A right oblique incision along the line connecting the internal and external inguinal rings was made. The skin, subcutaneous tissue, and external oblique aponeurosis were sequentially opened, with care taken to protect the iliohypogastric and ilioinguinal nerves.
3. The cremaster muscle was incised, and the spermatic cord was mobilized while being protected.
4. The hernia sac was identified and opened up to the neck, with the sac wall mobilized. The sac was transected, the distal end cauterized, and the proximal end sutured into a purse-string suture near the hernia ring. The plug was then sutured to the neck of the hernia sac and inserted into the hernia ring.
5. The transversalis fascia was opened, and the pectineal ligament was exposed.
6. The mesh was fixed posterior to the spermatic cord, with the lowest suture fixed to the pubic tubercle. The mesh was sutured to the pectineal ligament and the inguinal ligament below, and to the conjoint tendon and arcuate line above, replacing the internal ring. The mesh allowed passage of the tip of the little finger.
7. Hemostasis was achieved, and the aponeurosis, subcutaneous tissue, and skin were closed in layers.
8. The procedure was completed.

## Teaching Points
- Proper identification and protection of the iliohypogastric and ilioinguinal nerves during incision.
- Accurate placement of the plug and mesh to ensure proper reduction of the hernia sac.
- Secure fixation of the mesh to the surrounding structures for long-term success.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful dissection to avoid injury to the spermatic cord and surrounding nerves.
- Proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hematoma.
- Adequate mesh fixation to prevent recurrence.

## Overview
Appendectomy for acute appendicitis; clinical context includes abdominal pain; key technical feature is identification and resection of the inflamed appendix.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Abdominal pain
**Intraoperative:** Acute appendicitis
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings include approximately 200ml of purulent peritoneal effusion, extensive adhesions on the ileum and mesentery with abundant purulent plaques, significant ileal edema, an appendix measuring about 6cm with a surface covered in purulent plaques, and the entire appendix is congested and edematous. No abnormalities were found up to the ileocecal junction. The gynecology team was called to examine the bilateral adnexa, which showed ovarian congestion, and appendectomy was performed.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Right lower quadrant laparotomy incision, layer-by-layer abdominal entry, exploration as described.
3. Locate the appendix, separate adhesions, ligate the appendiceal mesentery to the root of the appendix.
4. Cut the appendix 0.5cm from the root, remove the specimen, ligate the root of the appendix, suture and ligate for reinforcement, cauterize the stump with electrocautery, and cover with a small amount of ileal omentum.
5. Thorough hemostasis, layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen.
6. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the appendix, ileum, and ileocecal junction. The danger zone is the base of the appendix. Structures requiring protection are the ileocecal junction and the terminal ileum. (阑尾，回肠，回盲瓣。危险区域为阑尾根部。需要保护的解剖结构为回盲瓣和回肠末端。)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered include extensive adhesions and significant edema of the ileum and mesentery, which were managed by careful dissection and adequate hemostasis.

## Teaching Points
- Identification and safe resection of the inflamed appendix.
- Management of extensive adhesions and edema.
- Importance of thorough hemostasis.

## Complication Prevention
- Prevention of postoperative infection through proper surgical technique and antibiotic prophylaxis.
- Prevention of postoperative ileus through early mobilization and adequate nutrition.

## Related Procedures
- [[Cholecystectomy]] - For complications such as appendiceal perforation leading to peritonitis.

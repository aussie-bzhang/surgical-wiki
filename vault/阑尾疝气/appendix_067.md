## Overview
Appendectomy for acute appendicitis with right lower quadrant mass, confirmed by intraoperative findings and frozen section pathology.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Acute peritonitis, right lower quadrant mass (possible appendiceal mass)
**Intraoperative:** Acute peritonitis, right lower quadrant inflammatory mass (possible appendiceal mass)
**Pathological:** Fibrofatty tissue with inflammatory cell infiltration at ileocecal junction; frozen section incomplete, awaiting paraffin section

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a 4.0 cm mass at the ileocecal junction, hard in consistency, adherent to the sigmoid colon, with a small amount of pus on the surface. 20 ml of purulent fluid was found in the right lower quadrant, and the appendix was not identified.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, intravenous and laryngeal mask anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Right lower quadrant McBurney incision, layer-by-layer opening, exploration as described above, tissue from the mass sent for frozen section pathology.
3. Place a negative pressure tube at the mass site, puncture and lead out.
4. Thorough hemostasis, layer-by-layer closure.
5. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: appendix, ileocecal junction. (阑尾，回盲部)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included adherence of the mass to the sigmoid colon, which was not easily separated.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of thorough exploration of the right lower quadrant in diagnosing appendicitis.
- The technique of managing adherent masses without causing additional injury.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Careful closure to prevent incisional infection.

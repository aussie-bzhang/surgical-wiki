## Overview
Repair of left inguinal hernia with tension-free mesh repair in a patient with a recurrent hernia.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Left inguinal hernia
**Intraoperative:** Left inguinal hernia confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Left inguinal hernia with a recurrent hernia, hernia sac measuring approximately 7*6*5cm, hernia contents consisting of small intestine and hernia sac wall, adherent, with normal blood supply of the intestinal wall.

## Operative Steps
1. After successful anesthesia, the patient is placed in a supine position, and the skin and subcutaneous tissue are disinfected and draped.
2. A longitudinal incision is made along the surface of the hernia sac, and the skin, subcutaneous tissue, and external oblique aponeurosis are sequentially opened.
3. The hernia sac and its neck are mobilized, the hernia sac is opened, and the findings are as described above. Excess hernia sac wall is excised.
4. The extraperitoneal preperitoneal space is mobilized, and a mesh is placed and sutured and fixed to surrounding tissue.
5. Hemostasis is achieved, and the external oblique aponeurosis, subcutaneous tissue, and skin are closed in layers.
6. The procedure is completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the inguinal canal, inguinal ligament, and iliopsoas muscle. (腹股沟管，腹股沟韧带，髂腰肌)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in mobilizing the hernia sac and ensuring proper placement and fixation of the mesh.

## Teaching Points
- Proper mobilization and closure of the hernia sac
- Technique for tension-free mesh placement
- Importance of hemostasis and layer closure

## Complication Prevention
- Careful mobilization to avoid injury to surrounding structures
- Proper mesh placement to prevent recurrence

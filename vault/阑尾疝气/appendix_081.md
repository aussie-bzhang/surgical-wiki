## Overview
Appendectomy for acute appendicitis; key technical feature: identification and resection of the inflamed appendix.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Acute appendicitis
**Intraoperative:** Acute appendicitis confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings: The appendix was located in the lower quadrant of the cecum, measuring approximately 8 cm in length and 1.5 cm in diameter, with marked congestion and edema. Pericolic inflammatory exudate was present.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, spinal and epidural anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Right lower quadrant McBurney's point oblique incision, layer-by-layer opening, aspiration of exudate, exploration as described.
3. Elevate the appendix head, ligate the appendiceal mesentery in segments to the root of the appendix.
4. Cut the appendix 0.5 cm from the root, remove the specimen, ligate the root with two ties, cauterize the stump with an electrosurgical knife, and bury the stump in a purse-string suture.
5. Thorough hemostasis, layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen.
6. Operation completed.

## Operative Steps

平卧位，连硬外麻下常规消毒铺巾；

取右下腹麦氏点斜切口进腹，逐层打开，吸尽渗液，探查如上；

提起阑尾头部，分段结扎阑尾系膜至阑尾根部；

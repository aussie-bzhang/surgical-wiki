## Overview
Appendectomy for acute appendicitis; key technical feature: identification and resection of the inflamed appendix.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Acute appendicitis
**Intraoperative:** Acute appendicitis
**Pathological:** Cecum - fibrous fatty tissue with acute and chronic inflammation

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperatively, the appendix was located at the lower part of the cecum, adherent to the lateral peritoneum. It measured approximately 8.0 cm in length, with full-thickness congestion and edema, the head was swollen with a diameter of about 1.0 cm, the body and root diameters were 0.5 cm, surrounded by purulent exudate of 10 ml. The cecum wall was thickened and slightly hard, adherent to the anterior abdominal wall.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Incision at the right lower quadrant McBurney point, layer-by-layer opening, exploration as described.
3. Elevate the appendix head, ligate the appendiceal mesentery in segments to the root of the appendix.
4. Cut the appendix 0.5 cm from the root, remove the specimen, ligate the root, suture and ligate for reinforcement, cauterize the stump with electrocautery, cover with a small amount of ileal lipoma.
5. Take a part of the cecum wall for intraoperative frozen section, suture and hemostasis.
6. Hemostasis, layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen.
7. Operation completed.

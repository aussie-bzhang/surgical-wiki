## Overview
A surgical procedure to relieve an incarcerated inguinal hernia and resect a necrotic segment of the small bowel. The patient presented with an incarcerated right inguinal hernia and required emergency surgery.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right inguinal hernia incarceration
**Intraoperative:** Right indirect inguinal hernia incarceration
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
The right indirect hernia sac measured approximately 10*6 cm, with an hernial ring diameter of about 2.5 cm. The contents included part of the small bowel and omentum. The necrotic bowel segment was blackened and there was no significant peritoneal effusion.

## Operative Steps
1. After successful anesthesia, the patient was placed in a supine position and the surgical site was disinfected and draped.
2. A left oblique incision along the inner and outer inguinal rings was made, and the skin, subcutaneous tissue, and external oblique aponeurosis were sequentially opened.
3. The spermatic cord was mobilized, and the hernia sac was found and opened up to the neck.
4. The hernia sac was incised, and the necrotic small bowel was pulled out. The necrotic bowel segment was resected 2 cm from the margin of the necrotic bowel.
5. The bowel was anastomosed in an end-to-end manner with interrupted full-thickness sutures, and the seromuscular layer was reinforced with sutures.
6. The hernia sac was ligated high in the hernial ring.
7. Hemostasis was achieved, and the aponeurosis, subcutaneous tissue, and skin were closed in layers.
8. The procedure was completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Inguinal canal (腹股沟管), ileum (小肠)

## Technical Challenges
Managing the necrotic bowel segment and ensuring proper anastomosis.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of identifying the correct hernial sac and avoiding injury to adjacent structures.
- Proper technique for resection and anastomosis of the necrotic bowel segment.
- The significance of high ligation of the hernia sac to prevent recurrence.

## Complication Prevention
- Ensuring proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper anastomosis technique to prevent anastomotic leaks.

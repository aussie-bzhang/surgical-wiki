## Overview
Repair of right inguinal hernia using McVay technique in a patient with clinical presentation of right inguinal indirect hernia.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right inguinal hernia
**Intraoperative:** Right inguinal indirect hernia
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Hernia sac size approximately 7*3cm, hernia ring diameter approximately 2.0cm, located lateral to the inferior epigastric artery, hernia contents reduced.

## Operative Steps
1. Patient placed in supine position after successful spinal anesthesia; routine disinfection and draping.
2. Right inguinal hernia incision made, skin, subcutaneous tissue, and external oblique aponeurosis opened in layers.
3. Testicle and spermatic cord mobilized; hernia sac identified and opened to the neck, hernia sac wall mobilized; purse-string suture placed at the hernia ring and hernia sac ligated high.
4. Transversalis fascia opened, pubic tubercle ligament exposed; GORE patch inserted and sutured to the pubic tubercle ligament, two interrupted sutures placed at the pubic tubercle for fixation, lateral suture to inguinal ligament and medial suture to conjoint tendon for fixation.
5. Hemostasis achieved; sponges and instruments counted and confirmed; layers closed in sequence from the superficial fascia to the skin over the spermatic cord.
6. Procedure completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Inguinal canal (腹股沟管), inguinal ligament (腹股沟韧带), pectineal line (耻骨梳韧带) (use Chinese in parentheses where helpful).

## Technical Challenges
Not documented

## Teaching Points
- Proper identification and mobilization of the spermatic cord and hernia sac.
- Correct placement and fixation of the GORE patch.
- Achieving hemostasis and ensuring proper closure of the layers.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper identification and ligation of the hernia sac.
- Correct placement of the GORE patch to prevent recurrence.

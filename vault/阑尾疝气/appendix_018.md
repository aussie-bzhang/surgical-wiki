## Overview
Repair of an inguinal hernia with evidence of incarceration, involving hernia ring release, high ligation of the sac, and tension-free repair.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right inguinal hernia with incarceration
**Intraoperative:** Right inguinal hernia with incarceration
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Following anesthesia, the hernia contents were reduced. The right indirect hernia sac neck measured approximately 2 cm in diameter, located lateral to the inferior epigastric artery. Intraoperative exploration revealed signs of incarceration in the small intestine, with 50 ml of small intestine at both proximal and distal ends, showing good color, normal blood supply, and peristalsis. No hemorrhagic ascites was noted in the abdominal cavity. Severe adhesions were present in the inguinal region.

## Operative Steps
1. After successful anesthesia, the patient was placed in a supine position and the surgical site was routinely disinfected and draped.
2. A right oblique incision along the line connecting the internal and external inguinal rings was made, and the skin, subcutaneous tissue, and fascia lata were sequentially opened.
3. Adhesions were released, the inguinal ligament, conjoint tendon, and arcuate margin were identified, and the spermatic cord was mobilized and protected.
4. The hernia sac and neck were mobilized, the sac was opened, and the excess sac wall was excised.
5. The extraperitoneal preperitoneal space was mobilized, a patch was inserted, and sutured and fixed to the surrounding tissue.
6. Hemostasis was achieved, and the fascia lata, subcutaneous tissue, and skin were closed.
7. The procedure was completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Inguinal canal, iliopsoas muscle, inguinal ligament, fascia lata. (腹股沟管，髂腰肌，腹股沟韧带，阔筋膜)
> The common bile duct (胆总管) is not involved in this procedure.

## Technical Challenges
Severe adhesions in the inguinal region required careful dissection to avoid injury to surrounding structures.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of identifying and protecting the spermatic cord during dissection.
- The technique of tension-free repair using a mesh to prevent recurrence.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful dissection to avoid injury to the spermatic cord and surrounding nerves.
- Proper placement and fixation of the mesh to prevent postoperative pain and recurrence.

## Overview
Appendectomy for acute appendicitis; key technical feature: identification and resection of the appendix.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Acute appendicitis
**Intraoperative:** Acute suppurative appendicitis
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings: The appendix was located posterior to the cecum, measuring approximately 6cm in length and 0.6cm in diameter, with suppurative head.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Right lower quadrant McBurney's point oblique incision, layer-by-layer opening, aspiration of exudate, exploration as above.
3. Elevate the appendix head, ligate the appendiceal mesentery in segments to the root of the appendix.
4. Cut the appendix 0.5cm from the root, remove the specimen, ligate the root with two ties, cauterize the stump with electrocautery, bury the stump with a purse-string suture, and cover with a small amount of ileocecal fat.
5. Thorough hemostasis, layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen.
6. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: appendix, ileocecal junction. (阑尾，回盲部)

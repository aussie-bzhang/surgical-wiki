## Overview
Repair of an indirect inguinal hernia with tension-free mesh repair, performed in a patient with a left inguinal hernia.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Left inguinal hernia
**Intraoperative:** Left indirect inguinal hernia
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Left indirect hernia sac approximately 3*2cm in size, hernia ring diameter approximately 1.5cm, located lateral to the inferior epigastric artery, contents reduced.

## Operative Steps
1. After successful anesthesia, the patient is placed in a supine position and the surgical site is conventionally disinfected and draped.
2. A left oblique incision along the line connecting the internal and external inguinal rings is made, and the skin, subcutaneous tissue, and external oblique aponeurosis are sequentially opened.
3. The spermatic cord is mobilized, and the hernia sac is found and opened to the neck of the sac.
4. The hernia sac wall is mobilized, and a purse-string suture is placed at the hernia ring, reconstructing the internal ring orifice.
5. A mesh is placed posterior to the spermatic cord, the first suture is fixed to the pubic symphysis, and the lateral aspect is sutured to the inguinal ligament, the medial aspect to the conjoint tendon and the inferior border of the rectus sheath.
6. Hemostasis is achieved, and the aponeurosis, subcutaneous tissue, and skin are closed in layers.
7. The procedure is completed.

## Technical Challenges

## Teaching Points
- Proper mobilization of the spermatic cord and identification of the hernia sac are critical.
- The tension-free mesh repair technique is essential for long-term success.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hematoma.
- Ensuring proper placement of the mesh to prevent recurrence.

## Overview
Appendectomy for acute simple appendicitis, with emphasis on identifying the appendix and managing surrounding inflammation.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Acute appendicitis
**Intraoperative:** Acute simple appendicitis
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed the appendix located at the lower part of the cecum, measuring 8.0 cm in length and 1.0 cm in diameter. The appendix was purulent throughout, with surrounding bowel wall edema, friable tissue prone to bleeding, and approximately 40 ml of purulent exudate in the abdominal cavity.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, after combined spinal-epidural anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Right lower quadrant McBurney's point oblique incision, layer-by-layer opening, aspiration of exudate, exploration as described.
3. Elevate the appendix head, ligate the appendiceal mesentery in segments to the base of the appendix.
4. Cut the appendix 0.5 cm from the base, remove the specimen, ligate and suture the base, cauterize the stump with electrocautery, and cover with a small amount of ileal lipoma.
5. Thorough hemostasis, layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen.
6. Completion of surgery.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the appendix and ileocecal junction. The danger zone is the base of the appendix where bleeding may occur. Structures requiring protection are the surrounding bowel wall and blood vessels.

## Technical Challenges
Not documented

## Teaching Points
- Identification of the appendix and its surrounding inflammation.
- Proper handling of the appendiceal mesentery to avoid bleeding.
- Technique of appendiceal stump cauterization to prevent postoperative leakage.

## Complication Prevention
- Thorough hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper closure to prevent infection.

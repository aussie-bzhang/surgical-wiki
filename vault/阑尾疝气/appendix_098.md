## Overview
Appendectomy for acute appendicitis; key technical feature: identification and resection of the inflamed appendix.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Acute appendicitis
**Intraoperative:** Acute appendicitis confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings: The appendix was located at the lower part of the cecum, measuring 6.0 cm in length and 0.6 cm in diameter, with full-thickness congestion and edema, surrounded by pale yellow effusion of 20 ml. During exploration through the appendiceal incision, a gallbladder measuring 5*3*2 cm containing several small stones was found. No abnormalities were found in the small intestine and colon.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Incision at the McBurney point in the right lower quadrant, layer-by-layer opening.
3. The appendix head was lifted, the appendiceal mesentery was ligated in segments to the root of the appendix.
4. The appendix was cut 0.5 cm from the root, the specimen was removed, the root was ligated and sutured for reinforcement, the stump was cauterized with an electrosurgical knife, and covered with a small amount of ileal omentum.
5. Bleeding was controlled, and the abdomen was closed in layers.
6. Operation completed.

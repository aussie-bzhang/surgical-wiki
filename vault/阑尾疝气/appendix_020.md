## Overview
Repair of an inguinal hernia with tension-free mesh plug placement, addressing a clinical scenario involving a left indirect inguinal hernia.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Left inguinal hernia
**Intraoperative:** Left indirect inguinal hernia
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Left hernia sac measuring 4*2*2cm, contents reduced, inguinal ring diameter 1.0cm, located lateral to the inferior epigastric artery.

## Operative Steps
1. After successful anesthesia, the patient is placed in a supine position and the surgical site is conventionally disinfected and draped.
2. A left oblique incision along the line connecting the internal and external inguinal rings is made, and the skin, subcutaneous tissue, and external oblique aponeurosis are sequentially opened.
3. The spermatic cord is mobilized, the cremaster muscle is incised, and the hernia sac is found and opened to the neck.
4. The hernia sac wall is mobilized, a purse-string suture is placed at the inguinal ring, and the plug is sutured to the neck of the hernia sac and inserted into the internal ring.
5. The mesh is fixed posterior to the spermatic cord, the lowest suture is anchored to the pubic tubercle, and it is sutured below the inguinal ligament and above the conjoint tendon and arcuate line, reshaping the internal ring, allowing the tip of the little finger to pass.
6. Hemostasis is achieved, and the aponeurosis, subcutaneous tissue, and skin are closed in layers.
7. The procedure is completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Inguinal canal (腹股沟管), inguinal ring (腹股沟环), inguinal ligament (腹股沟韧带), ilioinguinal nerve (髂腹下神经), femoral nerve (股神经). (胆总管)

## Teaching Points
- The importance of identifying the correct inguinal ring and hernia sac.
- Proper placement and fixation of the mesh plug to prevent recurrence.
- The technique of closing the layers of the abdominal wall to minimize postoperative pain and complications.

## Complication Prevention
- Ensuring proper hemostasis to prevent hematoma.
- Proper placement of the mesh to avoid nerve injury and recurrence.

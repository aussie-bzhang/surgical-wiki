## Overview
Appendectomy for acute appendicitis; key technical feature: identification and resection of the inflamed appendix.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Acute appendicitis
**Intraoperative:** Acute appendicitis confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperatively, the appendix was located below the cecum, measuring approximately 8 cm in length and 0.5 cm in diameter, with marked congestion and edema, and mild edema at the base.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, with combined spinal-epidural anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Right lower quadrant McBurney's point oblique incision, layer-by-layer opening, aspiration of exudate, exploration as above.
3. Elevate the appendix head, ligate the appendiceal mesentery in segments to the base of the appendix.
4. Cut the appendix 0.5 cm from the base, remove the specimen, ligate the base with two ties, cauterize the stump with an electrosurgical knife, and bury the stump in a purse-string suture.
5. Thorough hemostasis, layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen.
6. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: appendix, ileocecal junction. (阑尾，回盲部)

## Teaching Points
- Proper identification and resection of the inflamed appendix.
- Technique of ligating and cutting the appendiceal mesentery.
- Proper hemostasis and closure of the abdomen.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper identification and resection of the appendix to prevent postoperative appendiceal abscess.
- Thorough hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.

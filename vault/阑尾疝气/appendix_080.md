## Overview
Appendectomy for acute appendicitis; key technical feature: identification and resection of the appendix with proper hemostasis and closure.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Acute appendicitis
**Intraoperative:** Acute appendicitis confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings: The appendix was located at the lower part of the cecum, measuring approximately 5cm in length and 0.5cm in diameter. The entire appendix was significantly congested and edematous. There was approximately 20ml of peritoneal effusion.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, with combined spinal-epidural anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Right lower quadrant McBurney's point oblique incision to enter the abdomen, sequentially opened, aspirate effusion, and probe as described above.
3. Elevate the appendix head, ligate the appendiceal mesentery in segments to the root of the appendix.
4. At 0.5cm from the root of the appendix, cut the appendix, remove the specimen, ligate the root with two ties, cauterize the stump with an electric knife, bury the stump in a purse-string suture, and cover it with a small amount of ileocecal fat.
5. Thorough hemostasis, close the abdomen in layers.
6. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: appendix, ileocecal junction. (阑尾，回盲部)

## Technical Challenges
Not documented

## Teaching Points
- Proper identification and resection of the appendix with attention to hemostasis and closure.
- Recognition of the potential for peritoneal effusion and its management.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Careful closure to prevent incisional infection.

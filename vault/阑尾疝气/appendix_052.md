## Overview
Repair of right inguinal hernia with tension-free mesh repair, involving hernia ring release, high ligation of the sac, and tension-free repair. The procedure is performed in a sterile environment.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right inguinal hernia with incarceration
**Intraoperative:** Right indirect inguinal hernia with incarceration
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Right indirect inguinal hernia incarcerated at the external ring, with small intestine as the content. The color, blood supply, and peristalsis of the small intestine were good. There was 60ml of serous fluid in the scrotum.

## Operative Steps
1. After successful anesthesia, the patient was placed in a supine position and the surgical site was disinfected and draped.
2. An incision along the right internal and external inguinal rings was made, and the skin, subcutaneous tissue, and external oblique aponeurosis were sequentially opened.
3. The inguinal ligament, conjoint tendon, and arcuate margin were separated, and the spermatic cord was freed while being protected.
4. The hernia sac was found and opened to the neck, and the sac wall was freed. A purse-string suture was made near the hernia ring, and the plug was sutured to the neck of the sac and inserted into the internal ring.
5. The mesh was fixed behind the spermatic cord, with the lowest suture fixed to the pubic tubercle, and the mesh was sutured to the inguinal ligament below and the conjoint tendon and arcuate margin above, replacing the internal ring. The mesh could accommodate the tip of the little finger.
6. Hemostasis was achieved, and the aponeurosis, subcutaneous tissue, and skin were closed in layers.
7. The procedure was completed.

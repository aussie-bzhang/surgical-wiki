## Overview
Repair of an inguinal hernia with tension-free technique in a patient presenting with a right indirect inguinal hernia.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right inguinal hernia
**Intraoperative:** Right indirect inguinal hernia
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Right indirect inguinal hernia with a hernia sac diameter of 3.0 cm and an inguinal ring diameter of 2.0 cm, located lateral to the inferior epigastric artery. The contents were reduced.

## Operative Steps
1. After successful anesthesia, the patient was placed in a supine position and the skin was disinfected and draped.
2. An incision was made along the right inguinal ring and the skin, subcutaneous tissue, and external oblique aponeurosis were sequentially opened.
3. The hernia sac was identified and mobilized to the neck, where a Mesh plug was inserted and sutured to the top of the hernia sac. The sac was then pushed into the peritoneal cavity and sutured to the surrounding tissue of the inguinal ring.
4. A flat sheet plug was fixed posterior to the spermatic cord, with the lowest suture fixed to the pubic tubercle, and the lower part sutured to the pectineal ligament and inguinal ligament, and the upper part to the conjoint tendon and arcuate line.
5. Hemostasis was achieved, and the aponeurosis, subcutaneous tissue, and skin were closed in layers.
6. The procedure was completed.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of identifying and mobilizing the hernia sac
- Proper placement and fixation of the Mesh plug
- The technique of securing the flat sheet plug to the inguinal ligament and pectineal ligament

## Complication Prevention
- Proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hematoma
- Ensuring proper reduction of the hernia sac to prevent recurrence

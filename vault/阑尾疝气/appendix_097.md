## Overview
Bilateral inguinal hernia repair, a common surgical procedure for the treatment of inguinal hernias, involves the repair of weakened abdominal walls using mesh.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Bilateral inguinal hernia
**Intraoperative:** Bilateral inguinal hernia confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
- Right inguinal hernia sac measuring 7*5cm, content already reduced, hernia ring diameter 2cm, located lateral to the inguinal ligament.
- Left inguinal hernia sac measuring approximately 6*4cm, content already reduced, hernia ring diameter 4cm, located medially to the inguinal ligament, a direct hernia.
- Additional hernia sac measuring 2*2cm seen posterior to the spermatic cord on the left side, hernia ring diameter approximately 1cm, located medially to the inguinal ligament, an indirect hernia.

## Operative Steps
1. Patient placed in supine position after successful anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Incision made from the midpoint of the right inguinal ligament superiorly 2cm to the right pubic tubercle, skin and subcutaneous tissue incised in layers.
3. External oblique aponeurosis opened, spermatic cord mobilized, hernia sac identified and mobilized to the sac neck, preperitoneal space mobilized. MK patch placed between peritoneum and transversalis fascia, fixation strips sutured to inguinal ligament and conjoint tendon. MK patch placed posterior to the spermatic cord, first suture fixed to the pubic tubercle, sutured laterally to the inguinal ligament and medially to the conjoint tendon, and the internal ring reconstructed.
4.止血，探查无活动性出血，清点纱布器械无误。Sutured external oblique aponeurosis, reconstructed external ring. Closed right incision in layers.
5. Similar steps performed on the left side.
6. 止血，探查无活动性出血，清点纱布器械无误。Sutured external oblique aponeurosis, reconstructed external ring. Closed left incision in layers. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Inguinal canal, inguinal ligament, iliopsoas muscle, transversalis fascia. (腹股沟管，腹股沟韧带，髂腰肌，腹横筋膜)

## Technical Challenges
- Proper identification and mobilization of the hernia sac.
- Placement and fixation of the mesh without tension.

## Teaching Points
- Proper identification and mobilization of the hernia sac.
- Technique of mesh placement and fixation.
- Importance of tension-free repair.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper hemostasis.
- Avoiding tension during mesh placement.

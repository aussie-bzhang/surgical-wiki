## Overview
Right inguinal hernia repair, clinical context of inguinal hernia, key technical feature of tension-free repair.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right inguinal hernia
**Intraoperative:** Right direct inguinal hernia incarceration
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Hernia sac size approximately 10×6×4cm3, contents retracted, hernia sac neck diameter 2.0cm, located medially to the inferior epigastric artery.

## Operative Steps
1. After anesthesia, take supine position, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Take right lateral and medial inguinal ring incision, sequentially open skin, subcutaneous tissue, external oblique aponeurosis.
3. Incise the cremaster muscle, free the spermatic cord, find and open the hernia sac to the neck.
4. Free the hernia sac wall, perform purse-string suture at the hernia ring, suture and fix the plug with the hernia sac neck, then insert into the hernia ring.
5. Fix the mesh posterior to the spermatic cord, the lowest suture fixed to the pubic tubercle, below sutured with the inguinal ligament, above sutured with the conjoint tendon and arcuate line, reshaping the internal inguinal ring, allowing the tip of the little finger to pass.
6. Thorough hemostasis, sequentially close the external oblique aponeurosis, subcutaneous tissue, and skin in front of the spermatic cord.
7. End of surgery.

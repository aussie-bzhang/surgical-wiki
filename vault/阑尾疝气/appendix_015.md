## Overview
Appendectomy for acute appendicitis; key technical feature: identification and resection of the inflamed appendix.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Acute appendicitis
**Intraoperative:** Acute appendicitis confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings: The cecum was elongated to the lower right abdomen, the ileocecal junction was located in the upper right abdomen, and the appendix was retroileal, measuring approximately 12 cm in length and 1.0 cm in diameter. The appendix was gangrenous with a full-thickness perforation. The appendix was adherent to surrounding tissues, and there was 50 ml of purulent, malodorous effusion around the appendix and in the lower right abdomen.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Incision at the McBurney point in the right lower quadrant, layer-by-layer abdominal entry, exploration as described, and extension of the incision upwards.
3. Identification and dissection of the appendix, ligation of the appendiceal mesentery to the base of the appendix.
4. At 0.5 cm from the base of the appendix, the appendix was transected, the specimen removed, and the base ligated and sutured. The stump was cauterized with an electrosurgical device and covered with a small amount of ileal lipoma.
5. Bleeding was controlled, and the abdomen was closed in layers.
6. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: appendix, ileum, cystic duct, common bile duct. (胆总管)

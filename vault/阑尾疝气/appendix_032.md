## Overview
Appendectomy for acute appendicitis with perforation, highlighting the technical aspects of appendiceal resection and management of peritoneal contamination.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Acute appendicitis
**Intraoperative:** Acute appendicitis with perforation
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed an appendicitis with perforation. The appendix was located posterior to the cecum, measuring 5.0 cm in length and 1.2 cm in diameter. The appendix head was gangrenous and perforated, adherent to the lateral peritoneum. The ileocecal junction was severely inflamed and edematous. There was approximately 40 ml of purulent fluid in the peritoneal cavity, making the surgery challenging.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Right lower quadrant McBurney's point incision, layer-by-layer abdominal entry, exploration as described.
3. Locate the appendix, carefully separate the surrounding adhesions, ligate the appendiceal mesentery to the base of the appendix, and suture the vessels.
4. At 0.5 cm from the base of the appendix, cut the appendix, remove the specimen, ligate the base, reinforce with a suture, cauterize the stump with electrocautery, and cover with a small amount of ileal omentum.
5. Thorough hemostasis, place a negative pressure drain in the right paracolic gutter into the pelvic cavity, and close the abdomen in layers.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the appendix, ileocecal junction, and peritoneum. The appendix head is prone to gangrene and perforation. The ileocecal junction is a common site of appendiceal inflammation. (胆总管)

## Technical Challenges
Challenges included managing peritoneal contamination and ensuring thorough hemostasis.

## Teaching Points
- Management of appendiceal perforation and peritoneal contamination.
- Proper technique for appendiceal resection and closure.

## Complication Prevention
- Preventing postoperative infection and ileus by ensuring thorough irrigation and drainage.

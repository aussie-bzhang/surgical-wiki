## Overview
Repair of an inguinal hernia using the McVay technique in a patient with a right indirect inguinal hernia.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right indirect inguinal hernia
**Intraoperative:** Right indirect inguinal hernia
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Hernia sac located lateral to the inferior epigastric artery, approximately 5*4cm in size, with omentum in the scrotum. Hernia ring diameter approximately 3.0cm.

## Operative Steps
1. After successful spinal anesthesia, the patient is placed in a supine position, and the skin, subcutaneous tissue, and external oblique aponeurosis are incised in a right inguinal hernia incision.
2. The spermatic cord is mobilized, and the hernia sac is found and opened to the neck. The hernia sac wall is mobilized, and a purse-string suture is placed at the hernia ring. The plug is sutured to the neck of the hernia sac and inserted into the internal ring.
3. The transversalis fascia is opened to expose the inguinal ligament, and a patch is placed and sutured to the inguinal ligament. Two interrupted sutures are placed at the pubic tubercle to fix the patch, and the patch is sutured to the iliopectineal ligament and conjoint tendon in a running suture.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Inguinal canal (腹股沟管), inguinal ligament (inguinal ligament), pubic tubercle (pubic tubercle), iliopsoas muscle (iliopsoas muscle)

## Technical Challenges
Not documented

## Teaching Points
- The importance of identifying the correct hernia sac and hernia ring.
- Proper placement and fixation of the mesh to prevent recurrence.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful dissection to avoid injury to the spermatic cord and neurovascular structures.
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hematoma.

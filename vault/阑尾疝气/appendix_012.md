## Overview
Appendectomy for acute appendicitis; key technical feature: identification and resection of the appendix.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Acute appendicitis
**Intraoperative:** Acute appendicitis
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperatively, the appendix was located in the ileocecal region, measuring approximately 6.0 cm in length and 0.8 cm in diameter. The surface was congested and edematous, covered with purulent exudate, with 10 ml of purulent effusion surrounding it.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Right lower quadrant McBurney point incision, layer-by-layer opening, exploration as described.
3. Elevate the appendix head, ligate the appendiceal mesentery in segments to the base of the appendix.
4. Cut the appendix 0.5 cm from the base, remove the specimen, ligate the base, suture and reinforce with a knot, cauterize the stump with electrocautery, cover with a small amount of ileal lipoma.
5. Thorough hemostasis, layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen.
6. Operation completed.

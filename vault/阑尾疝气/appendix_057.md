## Overview
Inguinal hernia repair, clinical context of inguinal hernia, key technical feature of tension-free repair.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Left inguinal hernia
**Intraoperative:** Left inguinal hernia
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Left hernia sac 3*2cm, contents reduced, hernia ring diameter 1.0cm, located lateral to the inferior epigastric artery.

## Operative Steps
1. After anesthesia, take supine position, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Take left oblique incision along the line connecting the internal and external inguinal rings, sequentially open the skin, subcutaneous tissue, and aponeurosis of the external oblique muscle, protecting the iliohypogastric and ilioinguinal nerves.
3. Incise the cremaster muscle, free the spermatic cord and protect it.
4. Find and open the hernia sac to the neck, free the sac wall, perform purse-string suture near the hernia ring, suture and fix the plug to the neck of the hernia sac and insert it into the internal ring.
5. Fix the mesh posterior to the spermatic cord, the lowest suture fixed to the pubic tubercle, below sutured with the pectineal ligament and inguinal ligament, above sutured with the conjoint tendon and arcuate line, the mesh replacing the internal ring, allowing the tip of the little finger to pass.
6. Thorough hemostasis, close the aponeurosis of the external oblique muscle, subcutaneous tissue, and skin in layers in front of the spermatic cord.
7. End of surgery.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Inguinal canal (腹股沟管), inguinal ring (腹股沟环), iliohypogastric nerve (髂腹下神经), ilioinguinal nerve (髂腹股沟神经).

## Technical Challenges
Not documented

## Teaching Points
- Importance of protecting the iliohypogastric and ilioinguinal nerves during dissection.
- Technique of tension-free repair using plug and mesh.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper hemostasis to prevent hematoma.
- Careful mesh placement to avoid injury to surrounding structures.

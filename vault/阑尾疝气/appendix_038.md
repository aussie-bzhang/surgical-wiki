## Overview
Appendectomy for acute appendicitis; key technical feature: identification and resection of the inflamed appendix.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Acute appendicitis
**Intraoperative:** Acute appendicitis confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings: The appendix was located below the cecum, measuring 8.0 cm in length and 1.0 cm in diameter. It was fully congested and edematous with a swollen head measuring approximately 2.0 cm in diameter. There was 10 ml of purulent exudate around the base.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Incision at the McBurney point in the right lower quadrant, layer-by-layer opening, exploration as above.
3. Elevate the appendix head, ligate the appendiceal mesentery in segments to the base of the appendix.
4. Cut the appendix 0.5 cm from the base, remove the specimen, ligate the base, suture and reinforce with a knot, cauterize the stump with electrocautery, and cover with a small amount of ileocecal fat.
5. Thorough hemostasis, layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen.
6. Operation completed.

## Overview
Appendectomy for acute appendicitis; clinical context of acute abdomen; key technical feature: identification and resection of the inflamed appendix.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Acute appendicitis
**Intraoperative:** Acute appendicitis confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Right lower quadrant appendicitis with gangrenous perforation, severe adhesions with surrounding tissues, 100ml of purulent exudate in the peritoneal cavity.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Right McBurney's point oblique incision, layer-by-layer opening, exploration as above.
3. Bluntly separate the appendix from surrounding tissues, lift the appendix head, ligate the appendiceal mesentery in segments to the root of the appendix, cut the appendix 0.5cm from the root, ligate the root with two ties, and cauterize the stump with an electrosurgical knife.
4. Pack the stump with a purse-string suture and cover with a small amount of ileocecal fat.
5. Thorough hemostasis, layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen.
6. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: appendix, peritoneum. (阑尾, 腹膜)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in separating the appendix from surrounding tissues due to severe adhesions.

## Teaching Points
- Identification and safe resection of the inflamed appendix.
- Management of severe adhesions.
- Proper hemostasis and closure techniques.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper identification and resection of the appendix.
- Careful handling to avoid injury to surrounding structures.

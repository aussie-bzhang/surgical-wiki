## Overview
Right inguinal hernia repair, clinical context of inguinal hernia, key technical feature of tension-free repair using mesh.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right inguinal hernia
**Intraoperative:** Right inguinal hernia
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Hernia sac 4*3cm, contents reduced, hernial ring diameter 3cm, located lateral to the inferior epigastric artery.

## Operative Steps
1. Patient placed in supine position after successful anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Incision made from 2cm above the mid-point of the right inguinal ligament to the right pubic tubercle, skin and subcutaneous tissue incised in layers.
3. External oblique aponeurosis opened, spermatic cord freed, hernia sac found superior to the spermatic cord, freed to the neck of the sac, and preperitoneal space freed.
4. MK patch placed between peritoneum and transversalis fascia, fixation strips sutured to the inguinal ligament laterally and to the conjoint tendon medially.
5. MK patch placed posterior to the spermatic cord, first suture fixed to the pubic tubercle, sutured to the inguinal ligament laterally and to the conjoint tendon medially, and orifice reconstructed.
6. Bleeding from the surgical field and incision stopped, no active bleeding found, sponges and instruments counted correctly.
7. Aponeurosis of the external oblique muscle sutured, orifice reconstructed.
8. Subcutaneous tissue and skin sutured in layers.
9. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Inguinal canal, inguinal ligament, iliopsoas muscle, iliofemoral ligament. (腹股沟管，腹股沟韧带，髂耻肌，髂股韧带)

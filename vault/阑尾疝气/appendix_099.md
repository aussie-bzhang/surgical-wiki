## Overview
Appendectomy for acute appendicitis; key technical feature: identification and resection of the inflamed appendix.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Acute appendicitis
**Intraoperative:** Acute appendicitis confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Right lower quadrant adhesions; omentum adherent to cecum and lateral abdominal wall; appendix retrocecal and retroperitoneal, 6.0 cm long, 0.8 cm diameter, congested surface; mucosal surface showed scattered erosive inflammatory foci after resection; no significant abnormalities found in the terminal 1 meter of the small intestine and the ileocecal junction, ascending colon; no abnormal mass in the right pelvic cavity.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Right lower quadrant McBurney's point oblique incision, layer-by-layer opening, exploration as described.
3. Release right lower quadrant adhesions, open lateral peritoneum, flip cecum upwards, mobilize appendix, lift appendix head, ligate appendiceal mesentery in segments to the root of the appendix.
4. Cut off the appendix 0.5 cm from the root, remove specimen, ligate and suture the root, cauterize with electrocautery, and cover with a small amount of ileal omentum.
5. Thorough hemostasis, layer-by-layer closure of abdomen; completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: appendix, ileocecal junction, mesentery. (胆总管)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in adhesion release and mobilization of the appendix.

## Teaching Points
- Identification and resection of the inflamed appendix.
- Management of adhesions and peritoneal handling.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.

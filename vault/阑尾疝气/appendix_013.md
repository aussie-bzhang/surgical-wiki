## Overview
Repair of an indirect inguinal hernia using the McVay technique, a common approach in general surgery for treating inguinal hernias.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Left inguinal hernia
**Intraoperative:** Left indirect inguinal hernia
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Hernia sac approximately 3*3cm, hernia ring diameter about 2.0cm, located lateral to the inferior epigastric artery, hernia contents reduced.

## Operative Steps
1. After successful spinal anesthesia, the patient is placed in a supine position, and the surgical site is prepared with routine antisepsis and draping.
2. A left inguinal hernia incision is made and the skin, subcutaneous tissue, and external oblique aponeurosis are sequentially opened.
3. The spermatic cord is mobilized, the hernia sac is found and opened to the neck, the sac wall is mobilized, a purse-string suture is placed at the hernia ring, and the plug is sutured to the neck of the hernia sac and inserted into the internal ring.
4. The transversalis fascia is opened to expose the pubic tubercle ligament, a patch is placed and sutured to the pubic tubercle ligament, two interrupted sutures are placed at the pubic tubercle to secure, and the lateral aspect is sutured to the inguinal ligament and the medial aspect to the conjoint tendon.
5. Hemostasis is achieved, and after verifying that all sponges and instruments are accounted for, the aponeurosis, subcutaneous tissue, and skin are closed in layers.
6. The procedure is completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Inguinal canal (腹股沟管), inguinal ligament (腹股沟韧带), iliopsoas muscle (髂腰肌), sacrum (骶骨)

## Technical Challenges
Not documented

## Teaching Points
- The importance of identifying and protecting the ilioinguinal nerve during the procedure.
- Proper placement and tensioning of the mesh to prevent recurrence.
- The technique of creating a secure purse-string suture at the hernia ring.

## Complication Prevention
- Ensuring proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hematoma.
- Proper placement of the mesh to avoid tension and recurrence.

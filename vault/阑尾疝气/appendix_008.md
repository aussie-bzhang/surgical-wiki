## Overview
Appendectomy for acute suppurative appendicitis with perforation, key technical feature being the identification and resection of the inflamed appendix.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Acute appendicitis
**Intraoperative:** Acute suppurative appendicitis with perforation
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperatively, the appendix was located below the cecum, measuring approximately 8cm in length and 1.0cm in diameter. It was significantly congested and edematous, covered with pus, and showed gangrenous perforation. There was approximately 80ml of purulent exudate around the appendix.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, routine disinfection and draping under combined spinal-epidural anesthesia.
2. Incision at the McBurney point in the right lower quadrant, layer-by-layer opening, aspiration of exudate, and exploration as described.
3. The appendix head was lifted, and the mesentery was ligated in segments up to the root of the appendix.
4. The appendix root was ligated 0.5cm from the root, cut, and removed. The stump was cauterized with an electrosurgical unit, buried with an omental purse-string suture, and covered with an ileocecal lipoma.
5. Hemostasis was achieved, and the abdomen was closed in layers.
6. Procedure completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the appendix and the ileocecal junction. The danger zone is the area around the appendix where perforation can occur. Structures requiring protection are the surrounding bowel and blood vessels.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included identifying the inflamed appendix and managing the purulent exudate.

## Teaching Points
- Identification and safe resection of the inflamed appendix.
- Management of purulent exudate and prevention of postoperative infection.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Adequate drainage to prevent abscess formation.

## Overview
Abdominal wall hernia repair is a surgical procedure performed to repair an abdominal wall defect, typically following a previous incisional hernia or surgery. The key technical feature involves the use of tension-free mesh to reinforce the weakened abdominal wall.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Rectal cancer postoperative, postoperative intestinal adhesion, incisional abdominal wall hernia
**Intraoperative:** Rectal cancer postoperative, postoperative intestinal adhesion, incisional abdominal wall hernia
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
The original right upper abdominal transrectus sheath incision revealed a 12*7cm hernia ring with a hernia sac diameter of approximately 15cm. The contents had been reduced. The omentum and small intestine were adherent to the abdominal wall beneath the incision.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Incision at the original right upper abdominal transrectus sheath incision, excision of surgical scar, gradual entry into the abdomen, intraoperative exploration as described above.
3. Freely mobilize the hernia sac, excise the hernia sac and scar tissue, separate intraperitoneal adhesions, and reshape the hernia ring.
4. Separate the rectus sheath and subcutaneous space, 3.0cm away from the hernia ring on all sides, place tension-free mesh in the peritoneal cavity, suture and fix the mesh around the rectus sheath and peritoneum, and then suture the hernia ring around the mesh.
5. Thorough hemostasis, place a negative pressure drain on each side of the subcutaneous space, puncture and exit, close the incision in layers.
6. Completion of surgery.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: abdominal wall, hernia sac, omentum, small intestine. (腹壁，疝囊，网膜，小肠)

## Teaching Points
- The importance of thorough mobilization of the hernia sac and adhesions.
- The technique of tension-free mesh placement and fixation.
- The significance of proper hemostasis and drain placement.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper mesh selection and placement to prevent recurrence.
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.

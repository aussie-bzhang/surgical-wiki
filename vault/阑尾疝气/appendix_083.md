## Overview
Appendectomy for chronic appendicitis; key technical feature: identification and resection of the inflamed appendix.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Chronic appendicitis
**Intraoperative:** Chronic appendicitis
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperatively, the appendix was located below the cecum, measuring 6.0 cm in length and 1.0 cm in diameter, showing chronic inflammatory changes and adhesion to the cecum wall.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Right lower quadrant McBurney incision, layer-by-layer opening, exploration as above.
3. Elevate the appendix head, ligate the appendiceal mesentery in segments to the base of the appendix.
4. Cut the appendix 0.5 cm from the base, remove the specimen, ligate the base, add a suture ligature, cauterize the stump with an electrosurgical knife, and cover with an ileocecal fold.
5. Thorough hemostasis, place a drain at the appendiceal stump, puncture and bring out through the incision, layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen.
6. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: appendix, ileocecal junction. (阑尾，回盲部)

## Teaching Points
- Identification and safe resection of the inflamed appendix.
- Proper hemostasis and stump management.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Adequate drainage to prevent abscess formation.

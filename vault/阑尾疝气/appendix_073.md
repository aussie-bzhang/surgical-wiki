## Overview
Appendectomy for acute suppurative appendicitis with perforation in a patient presenting with abdominal pain.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Abdominal pain, possible acute appendicitis.
**Intraoperative:** Acute suppurative appendicitis with perforation.
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperatively, the appendix was located below the cecum, measuring approximately 6.0 cm in length and 1.0 cm in diameter. It was congested and edematous, covered with purulent exudate, and showed gangrene and perforation at the base. There was approximately 50 ml of pus in the periphery of the appendix and in the pelvis.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Right lower quadrant McBurney incision for abdominal access, layers opened sequentially, and exploration performed as described.
3. The appendix head was lifted, and the mesentery was ligated in segments up to the base of the appendix.
4. The appendix was cut 0.5 cm from the base, the specimen removed, and the base ligated and sutured. The stump was cauterized with an electrosurgical device and covered with an ileal lipoma.
5. Hemostasis was achieved, and a drain was placed at the appendiceal stump, brought out through a stab incision, and the abdomen closed in layers.
6. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the appendix and ileocecal junction. The danger zone is the base of the appendix where perforation is likely. The ileocecal junction requires protection during dissection.

## Technical Challenges
Not documented

## Teaching Points
- The importance of identifying the base of the appendix for proper resection and preventing complications.
- The technique of ligating and cutting the appendix in a safe manner to avoid injury to adjacent structures.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Adequate drainage to prevent abscess formation.

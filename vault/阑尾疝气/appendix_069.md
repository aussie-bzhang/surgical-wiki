## Overview
Appendectomy for acute appendicitis; key technical feature: identification and resection of the inflamed appendix.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Acute appendicitis
**Intraoperative:** Acute appendicitis confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings: The appendix was located below the cecum, fully congested and edematous, measuring approximately 6.0 cm in length and 0.8 cm in diameter. There was approximately 10 ml of purulent exudate around the appendix.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, routine disinfection and draping under combined spinal-epidural anesthesia.
2. Right lower quadrant McBurney's point oblique incision, layer-by-layer opening, aspiration of exudate, exploration as above.
3. Elevate the appendix head, ligate the appendiceal mesentery in segments to the root of the appendix.
4. Cut the appendix 0.5 cm from the root, remove the specimen, ligate the root of the appendix, cauterize the stump with an electric knife and ligate again, and cover with ileocecal valve.
5. After thorough hemostasis, count instruments and sponges, and close the abdomen in layers.
6. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: appendix, ileocecal junction. (阑尾，回盲部)

## Complication Prevention
- Proper identification and resection of the appendix to prevent postoperative appendiceal abscess.

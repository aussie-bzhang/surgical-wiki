## Overview
Appendectomy for acute suppurative appendicitis, with emphasis on identifying the appendix and managing the ileocecal junction.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Acute appendicitis
**Intraoperative:** Acute suppurative appendicitis
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
The appendix was located in the ileocecal region, measuring approximately 41 cm in length and 0.8 cm in diameter. The entire appendix was significantly congested and edematous with partial suppuration.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, routine disinfection and draping under combined spinal-epidural anesthesia.
2. Right lower quadrant McBurney's point oblique incision, layer-by-layer opening, aspiration of exudate, exploration as above.
3. Elevate the appendix head, ligate the appendiceal mesentery in segments to the base of the appendix.
4. Cut the appendix 0.5 cm from the base, remove the specimen, ligate the base, cauterize the residual end with electrocautery, purse-string suture the residual end, and cover with a small amount of ileal omentum.
5. Thorough hemostasis, layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen.
6. Completion of the procedure.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the appendix and the ileocecal junction. The appendix should be identified and the ileocecal junction protected during dissection.

## Technical Challenges
Not documented

## Teaching Points
- Proper identification and dissection of the appendix.
- Management of the ileocecal junction to avoid injury to adjacent structures.

## Complication Prevention
- Thorough hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Careful dissection to avoid injury to the ileocecal junction.

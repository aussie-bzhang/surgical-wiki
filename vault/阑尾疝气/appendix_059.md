## Overview
Appendectomy for acute appendicitis; key technical feature: identification and resection of the inflamed appendix.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Acute appendicitis
**Intraoperative:** Acute suppurative appendicitis
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings: The appendix was located in the ileocecal region, measuring approximately 9cm in length and 1.0cm in diameter. It was significantly congested and edematous with a surface covered in pus. No perforation was observed, the base was intact, and there was approximately 20ml of purulent exudate around the appendix.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, spinal-epidural anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Right lower quadrant McBurney incision, layer-by-layer opening, aspiration of exudate, exploration as described.
3. Elevate the appendix head, ligate the appendiceal mesentery in segments to the base of the appendix.
4. Ligature and suture the base of the appendix 0.5cm from the root, cut the appendix, remove the specimen, and bury the stump in a purse-string fashion.
5. Thorough hemostasis, layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen.
6. Operation completed.

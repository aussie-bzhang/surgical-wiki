## Overview
Appendectomy for acute appendicitis with a fixed, congested appendix measuring 7cm in length and 1cm in diameter.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Chronic appendicitis, acute exacerbation
**Intraoperative:** Chronic appendicitis, acute exacerbation
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a fixed, congested appendix located below the cecum, measuring approximately 7cm in length and 1cm in diameter. No significant abnormalities were found in the abdominal cavity.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Right McBurney's point oblique incision, layer-by-layer opening, exploration as described above.
3. Retrograde incision of the appendix, transection 0.5cm from the root of the appendix, double ligature of the appendix root, cauterization with electrocautery, lift the head of the appendix, ligate the mesentery of the appendix in sections to the head of the appendix.
4. Pack the residual end with a purse-string suture and cover it with a small amount of ileocecal fat.
5. Thorough hemostasis, layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen.
6. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: appendix, ileocecal junction. (阑尾，回盲部)

## Technical Challenges
Not documented

## Teaching Points
- Proper identification and handling of the appendix and ileocecal junction.
- Technique of ligating and cauterizing the appendix root.
- Importance of thorough hemostasis and proper closure.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper identification and handling of the appendix to prevent injury to adjacent structures.
- Thorough hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.

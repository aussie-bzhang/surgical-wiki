## Overview
Repair of an inguinal hernia with tension-free mesh repair, involving the treatment of an incarcerated indirect inguinal hernia.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right inguinal hernia incarceration
**Intraoperative:** Right indirect inguinal hernia incarceration
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Right indirect hernia sac approximately 6*5cm in size, hernia ring diameter approximately 3cm, containing part of the small intestine. The incarcerated segment of the small intestine showed no obvious black necrosis. No significant peritoneal effusion was observed.

## Operative Steps
1. After successful anesthesia, the patient is placed in a supine position and the surgical site is conventionally disinfected and draped.
2. A left oblique incision along the line connecting the internal and external inguinal rings is made, and the skin, subcutaneous tissue, and external oblique aponeurosis are sequentially opened.
3. The spermatic cord is freed, and the hernia sac is found and opened to the neck of the hernia sac.
4. The neck of the hernia sac is released, and the incarcerated segment of the small intestine is reduced.
5. A GORE tension-free mesh is placed posterior to the spermatic cord. The inner two sutures are fixed to the pubic tubercle, and one suture is fixed to the pectineal ligament. The mesh is sutured to the inguinal ligament in a running suture below and to the conjoint tendon and arcuate line above, reshaping the internal inguinal ring to accommodate the tip of the little finger.
6. Hemostasis is achieved, and the aponeurosis, subcutaneous tissue, and skin are closed in layers.
7. The procedure is completed.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of identifying the correct hernia sac and ensuring complete reduction of the incarcerated bowel.
- Proper placement and tension-free fixation of the mesh to prevent recurrence.

## Complication Prevention
- Ensuring adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hematoma.
- Proper mesh placement to prevent mesh-related complications.

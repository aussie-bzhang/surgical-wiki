## Overview
Repair of recurrent inguinal hernia with tension-free technique in a patient with a history of previous inguinal hernia repair.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Recurrent left inguinal hernia
**Intraoperative:** Left indirect inguinal hernia recurrence
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
- Previous left inguinal hernia scar
- Organ adhesion in the left inguinal region
- Poor anatomical layers
- Hernia sac diameter: 2.5 cm
- Content already reduced
- Hernia ring diameter: 1.0 cm, located lateral to the inferior epigastric artery

## Operative Steps
1. After successful anesthesia, the patient is placed in a supine position and the surgical site is prepared.
2. A left oblique incision along the line connecting the internal and external inguinal rings is made, and the skin, subcutaneous tissue, and external oblique aponeurosis are sequentially opened.
3. The adhesions in the left inguinal region are separated, the inguinal ligament, conjoint tendon, and arcuate margin are identified, and the spermatic cord is mobilized and protected.
4. The hernia sac is found and opened to the neck, the sac wall is mobilized, a purse-string suture is made at the neck of the sac, and the plug is sutured to the neck and inserted into the deep inguinal ring.
5. The mesh is fixed posterior to the spermatic cord, the lowest suture is anchored to the pubic tubercle, and the mesh is sutured to the inguinal ligament and conjoint tendon superiorly and to the arcuate margin inferiorly, replacing the deep inguinal ring, allowing the tip of the little finger to pass through.
6. Hemostasis is achieved, and the aponeurosis, subcutaneous tissue, and skin are closed in layers.
7. The procedure is completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> - Inguinal canal (腹股沟管)
> - Inguinal ligament (腹股沟韧带)
> - Ilioinguinal nerve (髂腹下神经)
> - Femoral nerve (股神经)
> - Deep inguinal ring (深环)
> - Superficial inguinal ring (浅环)

## Technical Challenges
- Adhesion and poor anatomical layers
- Small hernia ring size

## Teaching Points
- Importance of careful dissection to avoid injury to the ilioinguinal nerve and femoral nerve
- Proper placement of the plug and mesh to prevent recurrence

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent hematoma
- Proper placement of the mesh to prevent postoperative pain and nerve injury

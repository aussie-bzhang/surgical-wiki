## Overview
Appendectomy for acute appendicitis; key technical feature: identification and resection of the inflamed appendix.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Acute appendicitis
**Intraoperative:** Acute appendicitis confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings: The appendix was located posterior to the cecum, with marked congestion and edema, covered with purulent exudate, measuring approximately 8cm in length and 1cm in diameter. It was adherent to surrounding structures. There was approximately 50ml of purulent effusion in the abdominal cavity. No significant abnormalities were found in the exploration of other abdominal organs.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline umbilical incision, layer-by-layer opening, exploration as described.
3. Elevate the appendix head, ligate the appendiceal mesentery in segments to the base of the appendix.
4. Cut the appendix 0.5cm from the base, remove the specimen, ligate the base with two ties, cauterize the stump with an electric knife, bury the stump in a purse-string suture, and cover it with a small amount of ileocecal fat.
5. Thorough hemostasis, layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen.
6. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points

## Overview
Repair of an inguinal hernia using the McVay technique, a common approach for direct inguinal hernias, focusing on the repair of the internal ring and the posterior wall of the inguinal canal.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right inguinal indirect hernia
**Intraoperative:** Right inguinal indirect hernia
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperatively, the hernia sac was located lateral to the inferior epigastric artery, measuring approximately 7 cm in diameter with an internal ring diameter of 5 cm. The sac contained a large amount of omentum, which entered the scrotum. The contents were difficult to reduce.

## Operative Steps
1. After successful spinal anesthesia, the patient was placed in a supine position, and the surgical site was prepared with routine antisepsis and draping.
2. A right inguinal hernia incision was made, and the skin, subcutaneous tissue, and external oblique aponeurosis were sequentially opened.
3. The spermatic cord was mobilized, and the hernia sac was opened to the neck, with a large amount of omentum reduced into the scrotum. The sac wall was mobilized, and a purse-string suture was placed at the hernia ring. The plug was sutured to the sac neck and inserted into the internal ring.
4. The transversalis fascia was opened, and the pubic tubercle ligament was exposed. A patch was placed and sutured to the pubic tubercle ligament, with two interrupted sutures fixed at the pubic tubercle and sutured to the external inguinal ligament and conjoint tendon on the lateral side and the internal side, respectively.
5. Hemostasis was achieved, and the sponges and instruments were counted and found to be correct. The aponeurosis, subcutaneous tissue, and skin were closed in layers in front of the spermatic cord.
6. The procedure was completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> The critical structures include the inguinal canal, inguinal ligament, iliopsoas muscle, and transversalis fascia. The internal ring and posterior wall of the inguinal canal require protection and repair. (内环和腹股沟管后壁需要保护和修复：内环、腹股沟管后壁)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties in reducing the contents of the hernia sac and ensuring proper placement of the plug and patch.

## Teaching Points
- Proper identification and mobilization of the spermatic cord and hernia sac are crucial.
- Accurate placement of the plug and patch is essential for long-term success.
- Attention to hemostasis and layer-by-layer closure is important to prevent complications.

## Complication Prevention
- Ensuring proper reduction of the hernia sac and adequate repair of the internal ring.
- Proper placement of the patch to prevent recurrence.

## Overview
Repair of an inguinal hernia with anastomosis, in a patient with an incarcerated left indirect inguinal hernia.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Left inguinal hernia incarceration
**Intraoperative:** Left indirect inguinal hernia incarceration
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Left indirect inguinal hernia incarceration, hernia sac approximately 3*2cm in size, containing omentum, congested and edematous, no necrosis observed. Hernia ring diameter 1.0cm, located lateral to the inferior epigastric artery.

## Operative Steps
1. After successful anesthesia, the patient is placed in a supine position and the surgical site is disinfected and draped.
2. A left lateral to medial inguinal ring incision is made, and the skin, subcutaneous tissue, and external oblique aponeurosis are sequentially opened.
3. The spermatic cord is mobilized, and the hernia sac is opened to the neck, findings as described above.
4. The hernia sac wall is mobilized, the hernia ring is released, and a purse-string suture is placed at the hernia ring. The plug is sutured to the neck of the hernia sac and inserted into the internal ring.
5. The transversalis fascia is opened, the pubic tubercle ligament is mobilized, and the mesh is fixed posterior to the spermatic cord. The last suture is fixed to the pubic tubercle, below it is sutured to the pubic tubercle ligament and inguinal ligament, and above it is sutured to the conjoint tendon and arcuate line. The internal ring is reshaped to accommodate the tip of the little finger.
6. Hemostasis is achieved, and the aponeurosis of the external oblique muscle, subcutaneous tissue, and skin are closed in layers.
7. The procedure is completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Inguinal canal (腹股沟管), inguinal ring (腹股沟环), inguinal ligament (腹股沟韧带), pubic tubercle (耻骨结节)

## Teaching Points
- The importance of identifying the correct hernia ring and ensuring proper mesh placement.
- The technique of tension-free repair and the importance of mesh fixation.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hematoma.
- Ensuring proper mesh placement to prevent recurrence.

## Overview
Umbilical hernia reduction and tension-free repair; clinical context: umbilical hernia incarceration; key technical feature: tension-free mesh placement.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Abdominal wall hernia incarceration
**Intraoperative:** Umbilical hernia incarceration
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Umbilical hernia incarceration, hernia sac diameter 7cm, hernia ring diameter 5cm, hernia contents include part of the omentum and sigmoid colon, no significant ascites observed. Significant bleeding during surgery, making the procedure challenging.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, spinal-epidural anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Incise at the original incision site, gradually explore as described above.
3. Freely mobilize the hernia sac and separate the subperitoneal intestinal segment. Protect the intestinal segment and thoroughly mobilize the intra-abdominal adhesions, then thoroughly clean and stop bleeding.
4. Separate the rectus sheath and subcutaneous space, place a tension-free mesh, and fix it to the rectus muscle and peritoneum.
5. Thoroughly clean and stop bleeding, place a negative pressure drain tube on each side of the subcutaneous space, and puncture the incision.
6. Thoroughly stop bleeding, clean the wound, and close the incision in layers.
7. Procedure completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Umbilical ring (脐环), omentum (大网膜), sigmoid colon (乙状结肠). (Use English with Chinese in parentheses where helpful: e.g. common bile duct (胆总管))

## Technical Challenges
Significant bleeding during surgery, making the procedure challenging.

## Teaching Points
- Proper mobilization of the hernia sac and protection of the intestinal segment.
- Tension-free mesh placement technique.
- Thorough hemostasis and wound closure.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Tension-free mesh placement to prevent recurrence.

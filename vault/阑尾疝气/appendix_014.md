## Overview
Appendectomy for acute suppurative appendicitis with a 7cm long, 2cm diameter appendix with gangrene, fecalith impaction at the root, and a 0.5cm perforation at the root.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Acute appendicitis
**Intraoperative:** Acute suppurative appendicitis
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed an approximately 7cm long, 2cm diameter appendix with gangrene, fecalith impaction at the root, a 0.5cm perforation at the root, surrounding omentum, and approximately 100ml of purulent effusion in the abdominal cavity.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, spinal-epidural anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Right lower quadrant laparotomy incision, layer-by-layer abdominal entry, exploration as above.
3. Locate the appendix, separate adhesions, ligate the appendiceal mesentery to the root of the appendix.
4. Cut the appendix 0.5cm from the root, remove the specimen, ligate the root of the appendix, suture and ligate once for reinforcement, cover with a small amount of ileocecal fat.
5. Thorough hemostasis, place a negative pressure drain, layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen.
6. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: appendix, ileocecal junction. (阑尾, 回盲部)

## Technical Challenges
Not documented

## Teaching Points
- Importance of thorough exploration and identification of the appendix.
- Proper handling of the appendiceal mesentery to avoid injury.
- Technique of securing the appendiceal root with multiple ligatures and sutures.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Placement of a negative pressure drain to prevent postoperative abscess formation.

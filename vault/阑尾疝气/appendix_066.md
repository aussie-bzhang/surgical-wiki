## Overview
Appendectomy for acute suppurative appendicitis with gangrene in a patient with a retrocecal appendix.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Acute appendicitis
**Intraoperative:** Acute suppurative appendicitis with gangrene
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a retrocecal appendix, approximately 12 cm long and 2 cm in diameter, with a twisted head and全程 black gangrene. Approximately 100 ml of clear serous fluid was found in the abdominal cavity.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, routine disinfection and draping under combined spinal-epidural anesthesia.
2. Right lower quadrant McBurney's point oblique incision, layer-by-layer opening, aspiration of serous fluid, and exploration as described.
3. The appendix head is lifted, and the appendiceal mesentery is ligated in segments up to the root of the appendix.
4. At 0.5 cm from the root of the appendix, the appendix root is ligated, cut, and removed. The residual end is cauterized with an electrocautery, buried with an omental purse-string suture, and covered with an ileocecal lipoma.
5. Thorough hemostasis, layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen.
6. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the appendix and ileocecal junction. The danger zone is the base of the appendix. Structures requiring protection are the surrounding bowel and blood vessels. (胆总管)

## Teaching Points
- The importance of identifying the appendix and its base.
- Proper ligation and division of the appendiceal mesentery.
- The technique of omental purse-string suture for appendiceal stump coverage.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Careful dissection to avoid injury to surrounding structures.

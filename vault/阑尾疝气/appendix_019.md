## Overview
Appendectomy for acute gangrenous appendicitis with surrounding ileocecal adhesions and abscess formation.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Acute appendicitis with possible abscess formation.
**Intraoperative:** Acute gangrenous appendicitis.
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperatively, the appendix was found to be gangrenous at the body and base, located within the cecum. Surrounding the appendix, the ileocecal junction, and a segment of the mid-ileum were adherent to a firm inflammatory abscess, approximately the size of a fist, containing pus.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, spinal and epidural anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Right lower quadrant McBurney's point oblique incision, layer-by-layer opening, aspiration of exudate, exploration as described above.
3. The appendix head was lifted, the appendiceal mesentery was ligated in segments to the base of the appendix.
4. At 0.5 cm from the base of the appendix, it was ligated, cut, and removed. The residual end was cauterized with an electrosurgical unit, the base buried in a purse-string suture, and covered with an ileocecal fold.
5. Hemostasis was achieved, and the abdomen was closed in layers.
6. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the appendix, ileocecal junction, and ileum. The danger zone is the base of the appendix. Structures requiring protection are the ileocecal junction and the ileum. (胆总管)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included the gangrenous state of the appendix and the surrounding adhesions and abscess formation.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of recognizing the gangrenous state of the appendix.
- Proper handling of the appendiceal mesentery and the base of the appendix.
- Management of adhesions and abscesses.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Careful dissection to avoid injury to adjacent structures.

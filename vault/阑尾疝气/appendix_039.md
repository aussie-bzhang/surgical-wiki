## Overview
Appendectomy for acute appendicitis; key technical feature is identification and resection of the inflamed appendix.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Acute appendicitis
**Intraoperative:** Acute appendicitis confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperatively, the appendix was located at the lower part of the cecum, measuring 6.0 cm in length and 0.8 cm in diameter. The surface was congested and edematous with 20 ml of purulent exudate surrounding it.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, routine disinfection and draping under combined spinal-epidural anesthesia.
2. Incision at the McBurney point in the right lower quadrant, layer-by-layer opening, exploration as described.
3. The appendix head was lifted, the mesentery was ligated in segments up to the root of the appendix.
4. The appendix was cut 0.5 cm from the root, the specimen was removed, the root was ligated and reinforced with a circular suture, the stump was cauterized with an electric knife, and covered with a small amount of ileal omentum.
5. Hemostasis was achieved, and the abdomen was closed in layers.
6. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the appendix and ileocecal junction. The danger zone is the base of the appendix. Structures requiring protection are the surrounding bowel and blood vessels. (胆总管)

## Teaching Points
- Proper identification and resection of the inflamed appendix.
- Adequate hemostasis and closure of the abdomen.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper identification and resection of the appendix to prevent postoperative appendiceal abscess.
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.

## Overview
Appendectomy for acute suppurative appendicitis, with key technical feature of identifying and managing the appendiceal base and surrounding inflammation.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Acute appendicitis
**Intraoperative:** Acute suppurative appendicitis
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed the appendix located posterior to the cecum, measuring 8cm in length and 1.0cm in diameter, with marked congestion and edema throughout, covered with purulent exudate. The base of the appendix was 0.8cm in diameter, with a fecalith impacted in the lumen. There was approximately 30ml of purulent exudate around the appendix.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, routine disinfection and draping under combined spinal-epidural anesthesia.
2. Right lower quadrant McBurney's point transverse incision, layer-by-layer opening, aspiration of exudate, exploration as described.
3. Elevate the appendix head, ligate the appendiceal mesentery in segments to the base of the appendix.
4. Ligature the base of the appendix 0.5cm from the root, cut the appendix, remove the specimen, cauterize the stump with electrocautery, bury the appendiceal root with an omental purse-string suture, cover the stump with an ileocecal lipoma.
5. Thorough hemostasis, layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen.
6. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the appendix and ileocecal junction. The danger zone is the appendiceal base. Structures requiring protection are the surrounding bowel and blood vessels. (胆总管)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included managing the appendiceal base and surrounding inflammation, as well as ensuring thorough hemostasis.

## Teaching Points
- Identification and management of the appendiceal base
- Management of surrounding inflammation
- Importance of thorough hemostasis

## Complication Prevention
- Prevention of postoperative infection
- Prevention of postoperative ileus

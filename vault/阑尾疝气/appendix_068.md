## Overview
Repair of right inguinal hernia using Bassini technique in a patient with clinical presentation of right indirect inguinal hernia.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right indirect inguinal hernia
**Intraoperative:** Right indirect inguinal hernia
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Right hernia sac approximately 5*3*3cm3 in size, neck of the sac located lateral to the inferior epigastric artery. The sac wall is thick with multiple hard nodules on the abdominal wall, approximately 1cm in diameter.

## Operative Steps
1. After successful anesthesia, the patient is placed in supine position and the surgical site is conventionally disinfected and draped.
2. A right oblique incision along the line connecting the internal and external inguinal rings is made. The skin, subcutaneous tissue, and external oblique aponeurosis are sequentially opened.
3. The spermatic cord is mobilized, the hernia sac is found and opened up to the neck of the sac.
4. The hernia sac and its neck are mobilized, the sac is opened, and the findings are as described above. Excess hernia sac wall is excised.
5. The neck of the sac is sutured and fixed to the conjoint tendon. The conjoint tendon and inguinal ligament are sutured together, the last suture is fixed to the pubic tubercle, reshaping the internal ring to accommodate the tip of the little finger.
6. Hemostasis is achieved, and the aponeurosis, subcutaneous tissue, and skin are closed in layers.
7. The procedure is completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Inguinal canal, inguinal ligament, pubic tubercle. (腹股沟管，腹股沟韧带，耻骨结节)

## Technical Challenges
Not documented

## Teaching Points
- Proper mobilization and closure of the hernia sac
- Correct placement of the suture line for optimal strength and reduction of recurrence risk

## Complication Prevention
- Proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hematoma
- Ensuring proper reduction of the hernia sac to prevent recurrence

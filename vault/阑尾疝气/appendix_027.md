## Overview
Appendectomy for acute gangrenous perforated appendicitis with significant peritoneal contamination.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Acute appendicitis
**Intraoperative:** Acute gangrenous perforated appendicitis
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Appendix located at the lower ileocecal junction, approximately 6cm in length, entire appendix purulent, near the base 4mm gangrenous perforation, surrounding cecum edematous. 20ml of purulent exudate in the abdominal cavity.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, routine disinfection and draping under combined spinal-epidural anesthesia; right McBurney's point oblique incision made, enter peritoneum as described.
2. Elevate the appendix head, ligate the appendiceal mesentery in segments to the base of the appendix.
3. Cut the appendix 0.5cm from the base, remove the specimen, cauterize the stump with electrocautery, bury the stump in a purse-string suture at the base of the appendix, cover with a small amount of ileocecal fat.
4. Thorough hemostasis, close the abdomen in layers.
5. Operation completed.

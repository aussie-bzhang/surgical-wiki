## Overview
Repair of an inguinal hernia with tension-free mesh repair, a common procedure in general surgery.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right inguinal hernia
**Intraoperative:** Right inguinal hernia
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed a hernia sac measuring approximately 5*4 cm, with a neck diameter of 2.0 cm, located lateral to the inferior epigastric artery. The hernia contents had spontaneously reduced.

## Operative Steps
1. After successful anesthesia, the patient was placed in a supine position and the surgical site was conventionally disinfected and draped.
2. A left inguinal hernia incision was made, and the skin, subcutaneous tissue, and external oblique aponeurosis were sequentially opened.
3. The spermatic cord was mobilized, the cremaster muscle was opened, and the hernia sac was identified and opened.
4. The hernia sac wall was mobilized to the neck, and an internal purse-string suture was placed at the hernia ring.
5. A GORE tension-free mesh was placed posterior to the spermatic cord, with the inner two sutures fixed to the pubic tubercle and one suture fixed to the inguinal ligament. The mesh was sutured to the inferior epigastric ligament and the conjoint tendon superiorly and the arcuate line inferiorly to reshape the internal ring, allowing the tip of the little finger to pass through.
6. Hemostasis was achieved, and the aponeurosis, subcutaneous tissue, and skin were closed in layers.
7. The skin was sutured intradermally, and the procedure was completed.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of identifying the correct hernia sac and ensuring proper mesh placement.
- The technique of tension-free mesh repair to prevent recurrence.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hematoma.
- Ensuring proper mesh fixation to prevent mesh migration.

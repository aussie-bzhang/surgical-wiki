## Overview
Appendectomy for acute appendicitis; key technical feature: identification and resection of the inflamed appendix.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Acute appendicitis
**Intraoperative:** Acute appendicitis confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings: The appendix was located in the ileocecal region, showing marked congestion and edema, measuring approximately 8 cm in length and 0.6 cm in diameter. There was a collection of serous fluid in the abdominal cavity.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, routine disinfection and draping under combined spinal-epidural anesthesia.
2. Right lower quadrant McBurney's point oblique incision to enter the abdomen, layer by layer opening, exploration as above.
3. Elevate the appendix head, ligate the appendiceal mesentery in segments to the base of the appendix.
4. Cut the appendix 0.5 cm from the base, remove the specimen, ligate the base with two ties, cauterize the stump with an electrosurgical unit, pack the stump with a purse-string suture, and cover it with a small amount of ileocecal fat.
5. Thorough hemostasis, layer by layer closure of the abdomen.
6. Completion of the procedure.

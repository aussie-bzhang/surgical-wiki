## Overview
Incisional hernia repair with tension-free mesh and complex small bowel adhesion release in a patient with a history of portal hypertension shunt surgery.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Incisional hernia after portal hypertension shunt surgery
**Intraoperative:** Confirmed diagnosis of incisional hernia with complex small bowel adhesion
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Original surgical incision was a left transverse rectus sheath incision. The hernia sac was large, with a diameter of about 18 cm and a sac ring diameter of 12 cm. The abdominal wall showed multiple varicose and tortuous vessels, the largest diameter being 6 mm. Severe intra-abdominal adhesions were present below the incision, with no obvious ascites. Significant bleeding occurred during the operation, making the surgery difficult.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Take the original incision and make a梭形 incision to gradually explore.
3. Freely dissect the hernia sac and separate the subperitoneal bowel segment. Protect the bowel segment and thoroughly dissect the intra-abdominal adhesions, then thoroughly clean and stop bleeding.
4. Separate the rectus sheath and subcutaneous space, place a tension-free mesh, and fix it to the rectus muscle and peritoneum.
5. Thoroughly clean and stop bleeding, place a negative pressure drain tube on each side of the subcutaneous space, and puncture the incision.
6. Thoroughly stop bleeding, clean the wound, and close the incision in layers.
7. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: abdominal wall, small bowel, ileocecal junction. (腹壁, 小肠, 回盲部)

## Technical Challenges
Significant bleeding during the operation, making the surgery difficult.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of protecting the bowel during adhesion release.
- The technique of tension-free mesh placement for incisional hernia repair.

## Complication Prevention
- Thorough hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper placement of drains to prevent postoperative infection and seroma formation.

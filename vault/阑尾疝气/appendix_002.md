## Overview
Repair of left indirect inguinal hernia using the Bassini technique in a patient with signs of incarceration.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Left indirect inguinal hernia
**Intraoperative:** Left indirect inguinal hernia with incarceration
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Hernia sac measuring 8*4*3cm with congested and edematous wall. Approximately 50ml of purulent fluid aspirated from the sac. No hemorrhagic fluid noted. Fibrinous exudate on the inner wall of the sac. Hernia ring located lateral to the external iliac artery, diameter 1.5cm. Approximately 20ml of purulent fluid aspirated from the peritoneal cavity through the internal ring. No necrosis or perforation observed in the small bowel probed intraoperatively. No hemorrhagic fluid noted.

## Operative Steps
1. After successful anesthesia, the patient is placed in supine position and the surgical site is prepared.
2. A left oblique incision along the line connecting the internal and external inguinal rings is made. The skin, subcutaneous tissue, and external oblique aponeurosis are sequentially opened.
3. The spermatic cord is mobilized, the cremaster muscle is incised, and the hernia sac is identified and opened. The sac is transected, a portion of the sac wall is excised and sent for pathology, the distal sac is cauterized, and the proximal sac is mobilized to the neck.
4. The sac neck is sutured and fixed to the conjoint tendon. The conjoint tendon and inguinal ligament are sutured together, with the last suture fixed to the pubic tubercle. The internal ring is reshaped to accommodate the tip of the little finger.
5. Hemostasis is achieved. A negative pressure drain is placed deep to the external oblique aponeurosis and brought out through a stab incision. The layers of the abdominal wall are closed in sequence.
6. The procedure is completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Inguinal canal (腹股沟管), inguinal ligament (腹股沟韧带), iliopsoas muscle (髂腰肌), inguinal ring (腹股沟环)

## Technical Challenges
Managing the incarcerated hernia and ensuring proper reduction and repair of the sac.

## Teaching Points
- Proper identification and mobilization of the hernia sac.
- Correct placement of the sutures to reinforce the internal ring.
- Ensuring adequate hemostasis and proper closure of the surgical site.

## Complication Prevention
- Minimizing the risk of recurrence by ensuring proper reduction and repair of the sac.
- Proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hematoma.

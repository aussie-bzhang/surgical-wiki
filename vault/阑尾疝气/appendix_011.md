## Overview
Bilateral inguinal hernia repair, a common surgical procedure for treating inguinal hernias, involves the repair of the weakened inguinal canal and reinforcement with mesh to prevent recurrence.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Bilateral inguinal hernia
**Intraoperative:** Bilateral direct inguinal hernia
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Right hernia sac 7*5cm, left hernia sac 3*2cm, contents reduced.

## Operative Steps
1. Anesthesia achieved, patient placed in supine position, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Right inguinal ring incision made, skin, subcutaneous tissue, and external oblique aponeurosis opened in layers.
3. Ovarian ligament mobilized, hernia sac identified and opened to the neck.
4. Hernia sac wall mobilized, transversalis fascia opened, preperitoneal space mobilized, purse-string suture at hernia ring, Kugel hernia repair mesh placed in preperitoneal space and fixed to surrounding tissue.
5. Mesh inserted posterior to ovarian ligament, innermost suture fixed to pubic tubercle, below to inguinal ligament, above to conjoint tendon and arcuate margin, reshaping the internal ring, allowing the tip of the little finger to pass.
6. Hemostasis achieved, layers closed in sequence.
7. Left inguinal ring incision made, similar steps followed.
8. Mesh fixed posterior to ovarian ligament, innermost suture fixed to pubic tubercle, below to inguinal ligament, above to conjoint tendon and arcuate margin, reshaping the internal ring, allowing the tip of the little finger to pass.
9. Hemostasis achieved, layers closed in sequence.
10. Procedure completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Inguinal canal, inguinal ring, iliopsoas muscle, transversalis fascia. (腹股沟管，腹股沟环，髂腰肌，腹横筋膜)

## Technical Challenges
Not documented

## Teaching Points
- Proper identification and mobilization of the hernia sac and surrounding structures.
- Correct placement and fixation of the mesh to reinforce the inguinal canal.
- Minimizing postoperative complications such as pain, infection, and recurrence.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hematoma.
- Ensuring proper mesh placement and fixation to prevent recurrence.

## Overview
Appendectomy for suspected chronic appendicitis with key technical feature of identifying and resecting the inflamed appendix.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right lower quadrant pain, possible chronic appendicitis.
**Intraoperative:** Chronic appendicitis.
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperatively, the appendix was approximately 7 cm long with fecaliths. The distal ileum was explored for 100 cm without significant abnormalities.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, spinal and epidural anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Right lower quadrant McBurney's point incision, layer-by-layer opening, exploration as described.
3. The appendix head was lifted, the appendiceal mesentery was ligated in segments to the base of the appendix.
4. The appendix was cut 0.5 cm from the base, the specimen was removed, the residual end was cauterized with an electric knife and ligated and sutured, covered with a small amount of ileal omentum.
5. Hemostasis was achieved, and the abdomen was closed in layers.
6. Operation completed.

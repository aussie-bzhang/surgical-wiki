## Overview
Left inguinal hernia repair; clinical context of inguinal hernia; key technical feature: tension-free repair using plug and mesh.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Left inguinal hernia incarceration
**Intraoperative:** Left inguinal hernia incarceration
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
After anesthesia, the incarcerated hernia was reduced. The hernia sac was located below the inguinal ligament, approximately 3*2cm. The contents were reduced, the hernia ring diameter was 1.0cm. After opening the hernia sac, a small amount of pale yellow clear fluid was aspirated, no blood or purulent fluid was seen.

## Operative Steps
1. After successful anesthesia, the patient was placed in a supine position and the skin was disinfected and draped.
2. A left inner and outer ring line incision was made, and the skin, subcutaneous tissue, and external oblique aponeurosis were opened layer by layer.
3. The hernia sac was found and opened to the neck, the sac wall was freed, a purse-string suture was made at the hernia ring, and the plug was sutured and fixed to the neck of the hernia sac and inserted into the internal ring.
4. The mesh was fixed behind the spermatic cord, the lowest suture was fixed to the pubic tubercle, and the lower part was sutured to the pectineal ligament, and the upper part was sutured to the conjoint tendon and the inferior border of the arcuate ligament.
5. Hemostasis was achieved, and the aponeurosis of the external oblique muscle, subcutaneous tissue, and skin were closed in layers.
6. The operation was completed.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of tension-free repair in preventing recurrence.
- Proper placement and fixation of the plug and mesh.
- The technique of reducing the incarcerated hernia.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hematoma.
- Ensuring proper placement of the mesh to prevent postoperative pain and adhesion formation.

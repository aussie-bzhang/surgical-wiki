## Overview
Appendectomy for acute simple appendicitis with intraoperative findings of a 8.0cm long, 1.0cm diameter appendix located medially to the cecum, with congestion and edema, no perforation, and two fecal stones. There was 20ml of purulent effusion in the peritoneal cavity.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Acute appendicitis
**Intraoperative:** Acute simple appendicitis
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed the appendix located medially to the cecum, measuring 8.0cm in length and 1.0cm in diameter, with congestion and edema throughout, no perforation, and two fecal stones. There was 20ml of purulent effusion in the peritoneal cavity.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Right lower quadrant McBurney's point oblique incision, layer-by-layer opening, aspiration of effusion, exploration as above.
3. Elevate the appendix head, ligate the appendiceal mesentery in segments to the base of the appendix.
4. Cut the appendix 0.5cm from the base, remove the specimen, ligate and suture the base, cauterize the stump with electrocautery, and cover with a small amount of ileocecal fat.
5. Thorough hemostasis, layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen.
6. Operation completed.

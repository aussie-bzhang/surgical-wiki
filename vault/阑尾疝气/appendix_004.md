## Overview
Appendectomy for acute appendicitis; key technical feature: identification and resection of the inflamed appendix.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Acute appendicitis
**Intraoperative:** Acute appendicitis confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings: The appendix was located at the lower part of the cecum, measuring approximately 6cm in length, congested and edematous, covered with purulent exudate. There was a fecalith impacted in the appendix lumen causing body swelling, diameter 1.2cm, appendiceal root diameter 0.5cm, surrounding purulent exudate 20ml.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, spinal-epidural anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Right lower quadrant McBurney point incision, layer-by-layer opening, exploration as above.
3. Elevate the appendix head, ligate the appendiceal mesentery in segments to the root of the appendix.
4. Cut the appendix 0.5cm from the root, remove the specimen, cauterize the stump with electrocautery, ligate and suture the stump.
5. Thorough hemostasis, layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen.
6. Operation completed.

## Overview
Appendectomy for acute suppurative appendicitis, with key technical feature of identifying and preserving the ileocecal junction.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Acute appendicitis
**Intraoperative:** Acute suppurative appendicitis
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperatively, the appendix was located below the cecum, showing marked congestion and edema, with purulent head and body, covered with pus scabs, measuring approximately 8cm in length and 0.6cm in diameter. There was approximately 10ml of purulent exudate in the abdominal cavity.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Right lower quadrant McBurney's point oblique incision, layer-by-layer opening, aspiration of exudate, exploration as above.
3. Elevate the appendix head, ligate the appendiceal mesentery in segments to the root of the appendix.
4. Cut the appendix 0.5cm from the root, remove the specimen, ligate the root with two ties, cauterize the stump with an electric knife, bury the stump with a purse-string suture, and cover it with a small amount of ileocecal fat.
5. Thorough hemostasis, layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen.
6. Postoperative.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the appendix and ileocecal junction. The ileocecal junction requires protection during the procedure.

## Teaching Points
- Proper identification and preservation of the ileocecal junction.
- Technique of ligating and cutting the appendiceal mesentery.
- Proper hemostasis and closure techniques.

## Complication Prevention
- Infection prevention through proper surgical technique and antibiotic use.
- Hemorrhage prevention through meticulous hemostasis.

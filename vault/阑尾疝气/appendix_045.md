## Overview
Appendectomy for acute appendicitis with no perforation, identifying key anatomical landmarks and techniques for safe resection.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Chronic appendicitis
**Intraoperative:** Acute appendicitis
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperatively, the appendix was located medially to the cecum, measuring 6.0 cm in length and 1.0 cm in diameter, showing congestion and edema throughout, no perforation observed, and the base was intact.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Right lower quadrant McBurney's point oblique incision, layer-by-layer opening, aspiration of exudate, exploration as described.
3. Elevate the appendix head, ligate the appendiceal mesentery in segments to the base of the appendix.
4. Cut the appendix 0.5 cm from the base, remove the specimen, ligate and suture the base, cauterize the stump with electrocautery, and cover with a small amount of ileal lipoma.
5. Thorough hemostasis, layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen.
6. Completion of the procedure.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the appendix and ileocecal junction. The base of the appendix requires careful handling to avoid injury. (胆总管)

## Technical Challenges
Not documented

## Teaching Points
- Proper identification and handling of the ileocecal junction.
- Technique for safe appendiceal resection with minimal tissue trauma.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper closure to prevent incisional infection.

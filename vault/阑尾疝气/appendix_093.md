## Overview
Appendectomy for acute simple appendicitis; key technical feature includes identification and resection of the appendix with proper hemostasis and closure.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Acute appendicitis
**Intraoperative:** Acute simple appendicitis
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperatively, the appendix was found to be located posterior to the cecum, measuring approximately 8 cm in length and 0.7 cm in diameter, with marked congestion and edema. Two fecal stones were observed to be impacted within the appendix. There was approximately 20 ml of purulent exudate surrounding the appendix.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, routine disinfection and draping under combined spinal-epidural anesthesia.
2. Right lower quadrant McBurney's point transverse incision to enter the abdomen, sequentially opened, and the exudate aspirated, exploration as described above.
3. The appendix head was lifted, and the appendiceal mesentery was ligated in segments up to the root of the appendix.
4. At 0.5 cm from the root of the appendix, the root was ligated, transected, and the specimen removed. The distal end was cauterized with an electrosurgical device, the appendiceal root buried in a purse-string suture, and covered with an ileocecal lipoma.
5. Hemostasis was achieved, instruments and sponges were counted and found to be correct, and the abdomen was closed in layers.
6. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the appendix and ileocecal junction. The danger zone is the base of the appendix. Structures requiring protection are the terminal ileum and the ileocecal valve (回盲瓣).

## Technical Challenges
Not documented

## Teaching Points
- Proper identification and resection of the appendix with attention to hemostasis and closure.
- Recognition of the ileocecal junction and the risk of injury to the terminal ileum.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Careful dissection to avoid injury to adjacent structures.

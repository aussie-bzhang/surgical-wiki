## Overview
Repair of recurrent left inguinal hernia using tension-free mesh repair technique in a patient with a history of previous inguinal hernia surgery.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Left recurrent inguinal hernia
**Intraoperative:** Left indirect inguinal hernia confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
- Previous left inguinal hernia scar
- Organized adhesions in the left inguinal region
- Poorly defined anatomical planes
- Hernia sac diameter: 2.5 cm
- Content already reduced
- Hernia ring diameter: 1.0 cm, located lateral to the inferior epigastric artery

## Operative Steps
1. After successful anesthesia, the patient is placed in a supine position and the surgical site is prepared.
2. An incision is made along the left inguinal ring and the skin, subcutaneous tissue, and external oblique aponeurosis are opened in layers.
3. The adhesions in the left inguinal region are separated, the inguinal ligament, conjoint tendon, and arcuate margin are identified, and the spermatic cord is mobilized and protected.
4. The hernia sac is opened at the neck and the sac wall is mobilized. A purse-string suture is placed near the hernia ring, and the plug is sutured to the sac neck and inserted into the internal ring.
5. The mesh is fixed posterior to the spermatic cord, the lowest suture is placed at the pubic tubercle, and the mesh is sutured to the inguinal ligament and conjoint tendon above and the arcuate margin below, replacing the internal ring. The mesh allows passage of the tip of the little finger.
6. Hemostasis is achieved, and the aponeurosis, subcutaneous tissue, and skin are closed in layers.
7. The procedure is completed.

## Technical Challenges
- Separation of adhesions and identification of anatomical planes
- Proper placement of the plug and mesh

## Teaching Points
- Importance of identifying and protecting the spermatic cord
- Proper placement of the mesh to prevent recurrence

## Complication Prevention
- Adequate hemostasis
- Proper placement of the mesh to prevent postoperative pain and recurrence

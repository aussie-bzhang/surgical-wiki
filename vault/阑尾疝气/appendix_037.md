## Overview
Umbilical hernia reduction and tension-free repair for an incarcerated umbilical hernia, highlighting the technical aspects of hernia reduction and tension-free mesh placement.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Abdominal wall hernia incarceration
**Intraoperative:** Incarcerated umbilical hernia
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Umbilical hernia incarceration with a hernia sac diameter of 7cm and a sac ring diameter of 5cm. Hernia contents included part of the omentum and sigmoid colon. No significant ascites observed. Significant bleeding during surgery, making the procedure challenging.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, routine disinfection and draping under combined spinal-epidural anesthesia.
2. Incision at the original site, incising and gradually probing as described above.
3. Freely dissect the hernia sac and separate the subperitoneal intestinal segment. Protect the intestinal segment and thoroughly dissect adhesions within the abdominal cavity, followed by thorough irrigation and hemostasis.
4. Separate the rectus sheath from the subcutaneous space and place a tension-free mesh, securing it to the rectus muscle and peritoneum.
5. After thorough irrigation and hemostasis, place a negative pressure drain on each side of the subcutaneous space, exiting through separate stab wounds.
6. Complete hemostasis, clean the wound, and close the incision in layers.
7. Procedure completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Umbilical ring (脐环), omentum (大网膜), sigmoid colon (乙状结肠). (Use English with Chinese in parentheses where helpful: e.g., common bile duct (胆总管))

## Technical Challenges
Significant bleeding during surgery, requiring careful hemostasis.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of protecting the intestinal segment during dissection.
- Technique of tension-free mesh placement for optimal repair.

## Complication Prevention
- Thorough hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper irrigation to reduce the risk of infection.

## Overview
Appendectomy for acute appendicitis; key technical feature: identification and resection of the inflamed appendix.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Acute appendicitis
**Intraoperative:** Acute suppurative appendicitis
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperatively, the appendix was located in the ileocecal region, measuring approximately 8 cm in length and 0.8 cm in diameter, with the entire appendiceal wall showing congestion, edema, and suppuration.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Right lower quadrant McBurney's point oblique incision, layer-by-layer opening, aspiration of exudate, exploration as above.
3. Elevate the appendix head, ligate the appendiceal mesentery in segments to the root of the appendix.
4. At 0.5 cm from the root of the appendix, cut the appendix, remove the specimen, ligate the root with two ties, cauterize the stump with an electrosurgical knife, bury the stump in a purse-string suture, and cover with a small amount of ileocecal fat.
5. Thorough hemostasis, layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen.
6. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: appendix, ileocecal junction. (阑尾，回盲部)

## Teaching Points
- Proper identification and resection of the inflamed appendix.
- Technique of ligating and cutting the appendiceal mesentery.
- Proper hemostasis and closure techniques.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper identification and resection of the appendix to prevent postoperative appendiceal abscess.
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.

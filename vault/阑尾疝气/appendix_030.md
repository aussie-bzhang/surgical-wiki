## Overview
Repair of a right inguinal hernia with tension-free mesh repair using plug technique in a patient with a right inguinal hernia incarceration.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right inguinal hernia incarceration
**Intraoperative:** Right inguinal hernia incarceration confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Inguinal canal diameter 2.0 cm, hernial content was part of the small intestine, local congestion, peristalsis present, no necrosis.

## Operative Steps
1. After successful anesthesia, the patient is placed in a supine position and the surgical site is conventionally disinfected and draped.
2. A right anterolateral incision is made along the line connecting the internal and external inguinal rings, and the skin, subcutaneous tissue, and external oblique aponeurosis are sequentially opened.
3. The inguinal ligament is freed and transected, the hernial sac is found and opened to the neck, and the abdominal and external intestinal segments are checked and confirmed to be non-necrotic before the hernial content is reduced.
4. The hernial sac wall is freed, a purse-string suture is made at the hernial ring, and the plug is sutured to the neck of the hernial sac and inserted into the hernial ring.
5. The mesh is fixed posterior to the round ligament of the uterus, the lowest suture is fixed to the pubic tubercle, and the lower part is sutured to the pectineal ligament, the upper part to the conjoint tendon and the inferior border of the arcuate ligament; the inguinal ligament is sutured.
6. Hemostasis is achieved, and the aponeurosis of the external oblique muscle, subcutaneous tissue, and skin are closed in layers.
7. The operation is completed.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of identifying and reducing the hernial content before mesh placement.
- Proper placement and tensioning of the mesh to prevent recurrence.
- The technique of using plug to secure the hernial sac neck.

- Ensuring proper mesh fixation to prevent recurrence.

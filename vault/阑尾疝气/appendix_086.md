## Overview
Intestinal fistula repair and wound debridement following appendectomy; key technical feature includes identification and repair of the fistula.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Post-appendectomy intestinal fistula
**Intraoperative:** Confirmed post-appendectomy intestinal fistula
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
A sinus tract was found in the original appendectomy incision, extending to the abdominal wall and communicating with the small bowel, forming a fistula. The diameter of the fistula orifice in the bowel wall was approximately 0.2 cm.

## Operative Steps
1. After spinal anesthesia, the patient was placed in a supine position, and the abdomen was routinely disinfected and draped.
2. The original surgical incision was taken, and the adhesions were separated layer by layer to enter the abdomen. The adhesions between the small bowel and the peritoneum below the incision were separated.
3. The fistula was excised, and the surrounding tissue around the fistula orifice was trimmed to normal tissue. The fistula orifice was sutured with 3-0 absorbable suture in a full-thickness interrupted manner, followed by seromuscular layer suture.
4. The scar tissue around the surgical incision was trimmed, and hemostasis was achieved. The abdomen was closed in layers.
5. The procedure was completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the ileum and abdominal wall. (小肠，腹壁)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included identification and safe resection of the fistula without causing bowel injury.

## Teaching Points
- Identification and safe resection of the fistula
- Proper hemostasis and wound closure techniques

## Complication Prevention
- Careful dissection to avoid bowel injury
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage

## Overview
Appendectomy for acute suppurative appendicitis with perforation, a common surgical procedure in general surgery.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Acute appendicitis
**Intraoperative:** Acute suppurative appendicitis with perforation
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed the appendix located posterior to the cecum, adherent to the lateral peritoneum. The appendix was approximately 4 cm long and 1.0 cm in diameter, showing gangrene throughout with a surface covered in pus. There was a perforation near the root with a fecal stone measuring 0.4 cm in diameter, surrounded by approximately 50 ml of purulent exudate.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, routine disinfection and draping under combined spinal-epidural anesthesia.
2. Right lower quadrant McBurney incision for abdominal access, layer-by-layer opening, exploration as described.
3. Wound protection, opening the lateral peritoneum, flipping the cecum upwards, separating the appendix from the lateral peritoneum, ligating the appendix at the root, electrocoagulating the distal end, adding a suture ligature at the root, and covering it with an ileocecal fold.
4. Retraction of the appendix, ligating the appendiceal mesentery sequentially until the head, removing the specimen.
5. Thorough hemostasis, placing a negative pressure drain below the cecum, puncturing the wound to lead out, layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen.
6. Completion of the procedure.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the appendix and ileocecal junction. The danger zone is the area around the perforation. The common bile duct (胆总管) should be protected during the procedure.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of identifying the appendix and its attachments.
- Proper technique for ligating and removing the appendix.
- The need for thorough hemostasis and proper placement of drains.

## Complication Prevention
- Preventing postoperative infection and ileus by ensuring proper surgical technique and postoperative care.

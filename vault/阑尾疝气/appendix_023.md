## Overview
A surgical procedure for the repair of an inguinal hernia with incarcerated hernia sac and necrotic small bowel resection, performed in a sterile environment.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right inguinal hernia incarceration
**Intraoperative:** Right indirect inguinal hernia incarceration
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Right indirect hernia sac approximately 10*6cm in size, hernia ring diameter about 2.5cm, containing part of the small bowel and omentum. Necrotic bowel segment blackened, no significant peritoneal effusion.

## Operative Steps
1. After successful anesthesia, the patient is placed in a supine position and the surgical site is disinfected and draped.
2. A left oblique incision along the inner and outer inguinal rings is made, and the skin, subcutaneous tissue, and external oblique aponeurosis are sequentially opened.
3. The spermatic cord is mobilized, and the hernia sac is found and opened up to the neck.
4. The hernia sac is incised, and the necrotic small bowel is pulled out. The necrotic bowel segment is resected 2cm from the margin of the necrotic bowel.
5. The bowel is anastomosed in an interrupted full-thickness manner, and the seromuscular layer is reinforced with suture.
6. High ligation of the hernia sac at the hernia ring.
7. Hemostasis is achieved, and the aponeurosis, subcutaneous tissue, and skin are closed in layers.
8. The procedure is completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Inguinal canal (腹股沟管), ileum (小肠)

## Teaching Points
- The importance of identifying and managing necrotic bowel segments.
- The technique of high ligation of the hernia sac.
- The principles of bowel anastomosis and seromuscular reinforcement.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper identification and management of necrotic bowel.
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.

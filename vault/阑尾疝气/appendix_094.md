## Overview
Appendectomy for acute simple appendicitis, with key technical feature of identifying and preserving the ileocecal junction.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Acute appendicitis
**Intraoperative:** Acute simple appendicitis
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed the appendix located medially to the cecum, measuring 8.0 cm in length and 1.0 cm in diameter, with full congestion and edema, no perforation observed. Two fecal stones were present within the appendix. There was 20 ml of purulent exudate in the peritoneal cavity.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Right lower quadrant McBurney's point oblique incision, layer-by-layer opening, aspiration of exudate, exploration as above.
3. Elevate the appendix head, ligate the appendiceal mesentery in segments to the root of the appendix.
4. At 0.5 cm from the root of the appendix, cut the appendix, remove the specimen, ligate and suture the root, cauterize the stump with electrocautery, and cover with a small amount of ileal lipoma.
5. Thorough hemostasis, layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen.
6. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures include the appendix and ileocecal junction. The ileocecal junction requires protection during the procedure (回盲瓣 (回盲瓣)).

## Technical Challenges
Not documented

## Teaching Points
- Proper identification and preservation of the ileocecal junction is crucial to prevent postoperative ileus.
- Careful hemostasis is essential to minimize postoperative complications.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper identification and preservation of the ileocecal junction.
- Thorough hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.

## Overview
Appendectomy with pericecal abscess drainage for acute appendicitis with abscess formation.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Acute appendicitis with possible abscess formation.
**Intraoperative:** Acute appendicitis with appendiceal abscess.
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings revealed an appendix located in the ileocecal region, with the body and root necrotic and friable. The appendix was approximately 5.0 cm long and 1.0 cm in diameter. The surrounding cecum, terminal ileum, and a segment of mid-ileum were adherent to the appendix, forming a hard inflammatory abscess about the size of a fist, containing pus.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, routine disinfection and draping under combined spinal-epidural anesthesia.
2. Right lower quadrant McBurney's point oblique incision, layer-by-layer opening, exploration as above.
3. Separation of adhesions, opening of the abscess cavity, drainage of pus, separation of the mid-ileum and terminal ileum forming the abscess wall, ligation of the appendiceal mesentery, appendectomy, interrupted 8-shaped suture of the cecum, covering with ileocecal valve.
4. Thorough hemostasis, placement of a negative pressure tube in the abscess cavity, puncture and extrusion through the incision, layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen.
5. Completion of surgery.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: appendix, cecum, ileum, intestine. (胆总管)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered included friable appendix and adhesions, which were managed with careful dissection and hemostasis.

## Teaching Points
- Careful dissection around the appendix to avoid injury to adjacent structures.
- Proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Technique for opening and draining the abscess cavity.

## Complication Prevention
- Proper hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Adequate drainage of the abscess cavity to prevent recurrence.

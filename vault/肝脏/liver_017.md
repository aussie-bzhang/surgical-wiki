## Overview
Cholecystectomy for gallstones, splenectomy for splenomegaly, and perigastric vein ligation for esophagogastric varices. Key technical feature includes meticulous dissection around the gallbladder and spleen, and ligation of varices.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Gallstones, liver cirrhosis, portal hypertension, splenomegaly, esophagogastric varices.
**Intraoperative:** Gallstones, liver cirrhosis, portal hypertension, splenomegaly, esophagogastric varices.
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Gallbladder measuring approximately 9*6*5cm^3 with bile stasis and multiple mixed stones, 1.5cm in diameter. Gallbladder duct diameter 3mm, common bile duct diameter 6mm. Marked varices around the hepatoduodenal ligament. Liver atrophy with nodular sclerosis. Spleen measuring 20*15*5cm^3, congested and enlarged, splenic vein diameter 1.2cm. Varices around the cardia, largest diameter 0.5cm, predominantly on the posterior gastric wall. No ascites. Two masses, 2-3cm in diameter, seen at the hilum of the spleen during surgery, sent for pathological examination.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Midline upper abdominal incision, avoiding曲张 veins on the upper abdominal wall. Exploration as described.
2. Open the gastrocolic ligament, ligate the splenic artery.
3. Carefully ligate the varices around the gallbladder triangle, dissect the gallbladder triangle, free the cystic duct and gallbladder vessels, ligate and suture the cystic duct 0.5cm away from the common bile duct, ligate the gallbladder vessels, dissect the gallbladder from the liver bed, and remove the specimen for pathological examination.
4. Dissect the splenocolic ligament and separate and cut the splenic renal ligament posteriorly, the splenic gastrolienal ligament anteriorly, ligate the short gastric vessels one by one, separate and cut the splenic diaphragmatic ligament, and fully mobilize the splenic pedicle. Separate the splenic artery and vein, ligate the splenic artery, and remove the spleen, send the specimen for pathological examination.
5. Cut and ligate the gastric pancreas vessels, ligate the gastric coronary vessels along the lesser curvature up to the right area of the cardia.
6. Mobilize the lower end of the esophagus, cut the vagus nerve, and cut or ligate the esophageal varices up to the esophageal hiatus.
7. Rinse the abdominal cavity with large amounts of normal saline, perfect hemostasis, suture the splenic bed, place two negative pressure drains below the left diaphragm and behind the esophagus, and one negative pressure drain in the gallbladder fossa.
8. Count sponges and instruments, close the abdomen in layers, and close the operation.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Gallbladder, spleen, esophagogastric varices. Common bile duct (胆总管), splenic vein (脾静脉).

## Technical Challenges
Difficult dissection around the gallbladder and spleen, and ligation of varices.

## Teaching Points
- Meticulous dissection and ligation of varices around the gallbladder and spleen.
- Proper handling of the hepatoduodenal ligament and splenic hilum.

## Complication Prevention
- Adequate hemostasis and proper placement of drains.

## Overview
Splenectomy with perigastric vascular ligation for liver cirrhosis, portal hypertension, hypersplenism, and esophageal-gastric varices.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Liver cirrhosis, portal hypertension, hypersplenism, esophageal-gastric varices
**Intraoperative:** Liver cirrhosis, portal hypertension, hypersplenism, esophageal-gastric varices
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Liver atrophy with nodular sclerosis, spleen 25*15*5cm^3, congested and enlarged, splenic vein diameter 2cm; perigastric varices visible, largest diameter 0.5cm, no ascites.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping; left upper quadrant subcostal incision for abdominal access.
2. Open the gastrocolic ligament, ligate the splenic artery.
3. Transect the splenic colonic ligament, and separate and transect the splenic renal ligament posteriorly, splenic stomach ligament anteriorly; ligate and tie off the short gastric vessels, separate and transect the splenic diaphragmatic ligament, fully mobilize the splenic pedicle.
4. Separate the splenic artery and vein, transect and ligate the splenic artery and vein, remove the spleen, and send the specimen for pathology.
5. Transect and ligate the gastric pancreatic vessels, transect and ligate the gastric coronary vessels along the lesser curvature, mobilize upwards to the right of the cardia.
6. Mobilize the lower end of the esophagus, transect the vagus nerve, and continue upwards to the esophageal hiatus, transect or ligate the esophageal varices.
7. Rinse the abdominal cavity with normal saline, perfect hemostasis, suture the splenic bed, place two negative pressure drains below the left diaphragm and behind the esophagus.
8. Count sponges and instruments, close the abdomen in layers.
9. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Spleen, perigastric veins (胆总管), splenic hilum, splenic artery, splenic vein, splenic pedicle, esophageal hiatus, esophageal varices.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in mobilizing the spleen and controlling bleeding during dissection.

## Teaching Points
- Proper mobilization of the spleen and control of bleeding are critical.
- Careful dissection around the esophageal varices to prevent injury.

## Complication Prevention
- Adequate hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper placement of drains to prevent postoperative abscess.

## Overview
Cholecystectomy and left liver lobectomy with cyst drainage for a patient with left liver mass, multiple liver cysts, and cholecystolithiasis.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Left liver mass, multiple liver cysts, cholecystolithiasis
**Intraoperative:** Left liver mass, multiple liver cysts, cholecystolithiasis
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Gallbladder size 6*4*2cm with impacted stones. Common bile duct diameter approximately 0.5cm. Left liver lateral lobe mass 7cm in diameter, firm, well-defined. Multiple liver cysts, with the largest in the left liver medial lobe, 4cm in diameter, containing septations. No significant abnormalities found in the liver, stomach, intestines, or pelvis.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position with successful general anesthesia. Routine disinfection and draping. Midline incision for abdominal exploration.
2. Dissection of the胆囊三角, ligation of the cystic artery and cystic duct, dissection of the gallbladder from the liver bed, electrocautery of the gallbladder bed for hemostasis.
3. Incision of the falciform ligament, coronary ligament, left triangular ligament, and gastrohepatic ligament. Separation of the left liver lateral lobe from the diaphragm and omentum, complete mobilization of the left liver lateral lobe.
4. Liver resection at 3cm distance from the mass, all encountered vessels ligated or sutured intrahepatically. Careful hemostasis of bleeding points on the liver surface, followed by Prolene suture and argon beam coagulation. Hemostatic powder applied and hemostatic gauze placed over the liver surface.
5. Incision and drainage of the left liver medial lobe cyst, and partial cyst wall resection for pathological examination.
6. Complete hemostasis, lavage of the abdominal cavity, aspiration of exudate. Negative pressure bulbs placed at the liver surface via Winslow's孔 and one under the right diaphragm. Instruments and sponges counted, abdomen closed in layers.

## Overview
Palliative resection of hepatic portal biliary duct cancer with partial gastrectomy and Billroth I anastomosis for a patient with obstructive jaundice.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Obstructive jaundice
**Intraoperative:** Hepatic portal biliary duct cancer, infiltration of liver, portal vein, and pyloric
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Cystectomy performed. Dense adhesions in the right upper quadrant. Tumor located at the confluence of the right and left hepatic ducts, diameter approximately 2.5 cm, involving the liver surface, infiltrating the main portal vein and pyloric, leading to pyloric stenosis. Negative peritoneum and pelvis.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Abdominal entry via an upper midline incision.
2. Dissection of adhesions in the right upper quadrant, skeletonization of the porta hepatis, mobilization of the portal vein, hepatic artery, and common bile duct. Division of the common bile duct at the superior margin of the pancreas, ligation at the distal end.
3. Upward traction of the bile duct to separate the tumor from the portal vein.
4. Exposure of the porta hepatis, incision of the bile duct at the superior margin of the confluence of the right and left hepatic ducts, resection of the tumor with surrounding fibrous connective tissue.
5. Division of the greater omentum at the inferior aspect, mobilization of the anterior leaf of the transverse mesocolon to the superior margin of the pancreas.
6. Division of the lesser omentum and lymphatic tissue within.
7. Division and double ligature of the right gastric and right gastroepiploic vessels at the vascular root. Division of the duodenum 2 cm distal to the pylorus, purse-string suture placed in the anvil of the stapler and fixed.
8. Mobilization of the left gastric vessels, removal of the coronary vessels and lymphatic tissue on the lesser curvature until below the esophagus.
9. Insertion of the long tube of the stapler into the gastric lumen, side-to-end anastomosis between the posterior wall of the gastric body and the duodenum at the middle of the gastric body. 90-degree clamp at 2 cm from the anastomosis site, division of the stomach, removal of the specimen. Reinforcement of the anastomosis with a layer of seromuscular layer.
10. Division of the jejunum with the side-to-side stapler 20 cm from the Treitz ligament, mobilization of the distal jejunal mesentery, end-to-side anastomosis between the distal jejunum and the hepatic portal biliary duct (anterior to the colon, continuous full-thickness suture). End-to-side anastomosis between the proximal jejunum and the jejunum 60 cm below the biliary-enteric anastomosis (anterior to the colon); closure of the mesenteric gap.
11. Thorough hemostasis, lavage of the abdominal cavity, aspiration of exudate. Two negative pressure bulbs placed around the anastomosis site, extracted through a stab incision in the right upper quadrant. Layer-by-layer closure. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Common bile duct (胆总管), portal vein (门静脉), pylorus (幽门)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in mobilization of the portal vein and bile duct, and in anastomosis of the duodenum and jejunum.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of careful mobilization around the portal vein and bile duct to avoid injury.
- Technique of Billroth I anastomosis and jejunojejunostomy.
- Management of adhesions and lymphatic tissue during surgery.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Adequate lavage of the abdominal cavity to reduce the risk of infection.
- Proper placement of drains to prevent bile leakage and infection.

## Overview
Liver left lobe resection and cholecystectomy for liver intrahepatic cholangiocellular carcinoma, with significant tumor involvement and associated gallstones.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Liver mass
**Intraoperative:** Liver intrahepatic cholangiocellular carcinoma
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Large liver tumor, approximately 8*6*6cm, hard consistency, involving segments II, III, IV, V of the liver. Right posterior liver lobe is larger. Cholesterol stone of 0.8cm diameter in gallbladder. Negative exploration of pancreas, spleen, abdomen, and pelvis.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Right upper quadrant inverted L-shaped incision for abdominal exploration.
2. Transection of falciform ligament, round ligament, left and right coronary ligaments, left and right triangular ligaments, hepatogastric ligament, and hepatorenal ligament. Separation of liver from diaphragm and omentum, complete liver mobilization.
3. Dissection of胆囊三角, ligation of gallbladder vessels and cystic duct, stripping of gallbladder from liver bed, electrocoagulation of gallbladder bed for hemostasis.
4. Upward mobilization of left half liver, incision of hepatoduodenal ligament, separation and ligation of left hepatic artery.
5. Incision of Glisson's sheath at the porta hepatis, separation and ligation of left hepatic duct and left portal vein trunk, suture and transection of left portal vein branch.
6. Mobilization of right portal vein trunk, right hepatic duct, and right hepatic artery, ligation and transection of their right anterior lobe branches.
7. Exposure of the second liver hilum, separation and ligation of left hepatic vein and middle hepatic vein.
8. Hepatectomy along the right anterior and posterior boundaries of the liver, all encountered vessels ligated or sutured intrahepatically. Careful ligation of bleeding points on liver incision surface, argon beam coagulation of liver incision surface, application of hemostatic powder and hemostatic gauze for hemostasis.
9. Complete hemostasis, lavage of abdomen, aspiration of effluent, placement of negative pressure balls in gallbladder fossa and liver incision surface, verification of instruments and sponges, layer-by-layer closure of abdomen.

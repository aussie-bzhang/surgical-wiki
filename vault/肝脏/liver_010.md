## Overview
Right hemihepatectomy and cholecystectomy for right liver cancer. Key technical feature includes liver resection and bile duct dissection.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right liver cancer.
**Intraoperative:** Right liver cancer confirmed.
**Pathological:** Not documented.

## Intraoperative Findings
Liver tumor located in segments V, VI, VII, VIII of the right liver, scattered and multiple. Portal vein right trunk has a cancer thrombus. No significant abnormalities found in left liver, stomach, small intestine, colon, peritoneum, or pelvic cavity.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia successful. Routine disinfection and draping. L-shaped incision in the right upper abdomen for abdominal exploration.
2. Cutting the round ligament of liver, falciform ligament, right coronary ligament, right triangular ligament, hepatocolic ligament, and hepatorenal ligament. Dissect the胆囊三角, ligate and cut the gallbladder vessels and cystic duct. Strip the gallbladder from the liver bed and remove it. Coagulate the gallbladder bed with electrocautery.
3. Bluntly separate the bare area of the liver to the inferior vena cava, protecting the right adrenal gland and its vessels.
4. Dissect the hepatoduodenal ligament and skeletonize the lymph nodes. Expose the right portal vein trunk, right hepatic duct, and right hepatic artery. Ligate and cut the right hepatic duct, right hepatic artery, and right portal vein trunk.
5. Occlude the first liver portal with an 8# catheter. Cut the liver capsule from the right wall of the inferior vena cava to the gallbladder notch on the diaphragmatic surface of the liver. Bluntly separate the liver parenchyma, ligate encountered vessels and bile ducts.
6. Turn the liver upwards, cut the liver along the right longitudinal groove of the gallbladder fossa, and bluntly separate the liver parenchyma to the right hepatic hilum. Ligate the short hepatic vein, avoiding injury to the inferior vena cava. Separate and ligate the right hepatic vein; clamp, cut, and ligate the right posterior superior margin of the liver vein along with the liver tissue. Remove the specimen. Open the first liver portal.
7. Ligate bleeding points on the liver incision carefully. Coagulate the liver incision with argon beam coagulation and spray biological protein glue for hemostasis.
8. Thorough hemostasis, wash the abdominal cavity, aspirate the effluent, place a negative pressure ball on the liver incision, count instruments and gauze correctly, and close the abdomen in layers. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: right liver, common bile duct, portal vein, hepatic artery, hepatic vein. (胆总管, 肝门静脉, 肝动脉, 肝静脉)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered include liver resection, bile duct dissection, and hemostasis.

## Teaching Points
- Liver resection technique and bile duct dissection.
- Hemostasis and protection of adjacent structures.

## Complication Prevention
- Prevent bile leakage by careful dissection and ligation.
- Prevent injury to adjacent structures during liver resection.

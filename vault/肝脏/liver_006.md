## Overview
Splenectomy with selective dissection of pericardial venous varices is a procedure performed for the treatment of liver cirrhosis, portal hypertension, splenomegaly, and esophageal-gastric varices. Key technical feature includes selective dissection of varices around the cardia.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Liver cirrhosis, portal hypertension, splenomegaly, esophageal-gastric varices
**Intraoperative:** Liver cirrhosis, portal hypertension, splenomegaly, esophageal-gastric varices
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Liver atrophy with nodular sclerosis, spleen measuring 30*20*15cm^3, congested and enlarged, splenic vein diameter 2cm; varices around the cardia visible, largest diameter 0.5cm, ascites approximately 2000ml. Preoperative portal vein pressure measured at 33mmHg, postoperative 27mmHg.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Left subcostal L-shaped incision made to enter abdomen, avoiding曲张 veins on the upper abdominal wall. Exploration as described.
2. Open the gastrocolic ligament, ligate the splenic artery and measure portal vein pressure.
3. Dissect and isolate the middle colonic vein within the transverse mesocolon, catheterize to the superior mesenteric vein-portal vein for pressure measurement.
4. Dissect and cut the splenic renal ligament posteriorly, splenic gastrostomy ligament anteriorly, ligate each short gastric vessels, dissect and cut the splenic diaphragmatic ligament, fully mobilize the splenic pedicle. Dissect and cut the splenic artery and vein, ligate the splenic artery, resect the spleen, send the specimen for pathology, measure portal vein pressure.
5. Cut and ligate the vessels between the stomach and pancreas, transect and ligate the gastric coronary vessels along the lesser curvature, preserve the main vein around the esophagus, ligate the branches towards the stomach, mobilize upwards to the right area of the cardia.
6. Mobilize the lower end of the esophagus, cut the vagus nerve, upwards to the esophageal hiatus, cut or ligate the esophageal varices.
7. Rinse the abdominal cavity with large amounts of normal saline, perfect hemostasis, suture the splenic bed, re-measure portal vein pressure. Place two negative pressure drains below the left diaphragm, one negative pressure drain behind the esophagus.
8. Count sponges and instruments, close the abdomen in layers, surgery completed.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered include mobilization of the spleen, selective dissection of varices around the cardia, and control of bleeding during dissection.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of selective dissection of varices around the cardia to prevent postoperative variceal bleeding.
- Proper hemostasis techniques during splenic resection.
- The significance of portal vein pressure measurement before and after surgery.

## Complication Prevention
- Thorough hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Selective dissection of varices to minimize the risk of variceal bleeding.

## Overview
Splenectomy and special hepatic segment resection for colon cancer liver metastasis, involving gallbladder and descending colon resection.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Colon cancer liver metastasis
**Intraoperative:** Colon cancer liver metastasis confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings: A 4.0cm narrowing tumor at the junction of the descending and sigmoid colon, causing intestinal obstruction. The tumor infiltrates beyond the serosa, with a 1.0cm enlarged lymph node in the mesenteric root. Three liver metastases, 6.0cm, 2.5cm, and 1.5cm in diameter, located in segments VI, VII, and IV, respectively, with a grayish-white section. Gallbladder size: 6*4*2cm. No obvious metastases found in the abdominal cavity, stomach, pancreas, spleen, and intestines.

## Operative Steps
1. Patient placed in supine position under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline upper abdominal incision for abdominal exploration.
3. Open lateral peritoneum, mobilize sigmoid colon mesentery, and transect sigmoid colon vessels at the root, clearing mesenteric root lymph nodes, transecting inferior mesenteric vessels, and being cautious of the course of the ureter and iliac vessels.
4. Mobilize gastrocolic ligament, resect left half of omentum, mobilize transverse colon mesentery, open left lateral peritoneum of colon, transect splenocolic ligament, and mobilize colic flexure.
5. Mobilize colon mesentery, distal dissection to 7-8cm below the tumor, proximal dissection to 10cm above the tumor.
6. Transect proximal and distal colon, remove specimen, and perform end-to-end anastomosis with interrupted full-thickness sutures.
7. Transect falciform ligament, round ligament, right coronary ligament, right triangular ligament, hepatocolic ligament, and hepatorenal ligament.钝性分离肝裸区直达肝后下腔静脉，注意保护右侧肾上腺及其血管。
8. Dissect the hepatoduodenal ligament, mobilize the cystic artery and cystic duct, and ligate and transect them. Detach the gallbladder from the liver surface and coagulate the gallbladder bed.
9. Incise the liver capsule along the edge of the tumor, bluntly separate the liver parenchyma, ligate and transect encountered vessels and bile ducts, ligate the short hepatic vein, and resect segments IV-VII of the liver, being cautious not to injure the inferior vena cava.
10. Carefully ligate bleeding points on the liver wound, coagulate the liver wound with argon beam coagulation, and sprinkle hemostatic powder for hemostasis.
11. Thorough hemostasis, irrigate the abdominal cavity, remove all effusion, place two negative pressure balls at the liver wound and colon anastomosis, count instruments and sponges, and close the abdomen in layers. Operation completed.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of careful dissection around the splenic hilum to avoid injury to the splenic vessels.
- The need for meticulous hemostasis during liver resection to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper handling of the colon anastomosis to ensure anastomotic integrity and prevent leakage.

## Complication Prevention
- Thorough hemostasis during liver resection to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper anastomotic technique to prevent anastomotic leakage.
- Postoperative monitoring for signs of infection and bowel obstruction.

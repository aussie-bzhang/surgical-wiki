## Overview
Liver segment VI resection for a serous cystadenoma in the liver, with emphasis on the technical aspects of segmental liver resection.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Liver cystadenoma
**Intraoperative:** Serous cystadenoma of the liver
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Liver mass located in segment VI, with clear borders, soft consistency, approximately 3*3cm in size.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia, supine position, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Right subcostal incision for abdominal exploration.
3. Release adhesions in the upper right abdomen, transect the falciform ligament, carefully separate the liver from the greater omentum and duodenum.
4. Transect the falciform, coronary, triangular, furogastric, and hepatorenal ligaments, separate the liver from the diaphragm, reaching the right side of the inferior vena cava, fully mobilize the left and right lobes of the liver.
5. Incise the peritoneum and liver parenchyma of segment VI, ligate or tie all encountered vessels within the liver, completely resect segment VI of the liver, carefully ligate and stop bleeding at the liver断面, and perform U-shaped suture.
6. Thorough hemostasis, wash the abdominal cavity, remove all effusions, spray biological protein glue and hemostatic gauze, place a negative pressure tube at the liver wound and subdiaphragmatic space, count instruments and sponges, and close the abdomen in layers.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: Liver segment VI, common bile duct (胆总管).

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in mobilizing the liver and controlling bleeding during resection.

## Teaching Points
- Proper mobilization of the liver and control of bleeding during segmental liver resection.
- Identification and protection of critical structures such as the common bile duct.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Identification and protection of the common bile duct to prevent bile duct injury.

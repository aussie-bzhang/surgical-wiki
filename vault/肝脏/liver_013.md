## Overview
Right hepatectomy for a large right liver tumor, significant findings during surgery, and key technical steps.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right liver cancer
**Intraoperative:** Right liver cancer confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Large right liver tumor, approximately 10 cm in diameter, with thrombus in the right portal vein and right hepatic duct.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping, L-shaped incision in the right upper abdomen.
2. Cutting the round ligament of liver, falciform ligament, right coronary ligament, right triangular ligament, hepatocolic ligament, and hepatorenal ligament, bluntly separating the bare area of the liver to the inferior vena cava.
3. Dissecting the胆囊三角, ligating and transecting the cystic artery and cystic duct, stripping the gallbladder from the liver bed, electrocoagulating the gallbladder bed for hemostasis.
4. Dissecting the hepatoduodenal ligament and skeletonizing the lymph nodes, exposing the right portal vein trunk, right hepatic duct, and right hepatic artery, ligating and transecting the right hepatic duct and artery, and using an endocut clip to occlude the right portal vein trunk.
5. Cutting the liver capsule on the diaphragmatic surface from the right wall of the inferior vena cava to the gallbladder notch, bluntly separating the liver parenchyma, ligating and transecting encountered vessels and bile ducts.
6. Flipping the liver upwards, cutting the liver along the right longitudinal groove of the gallbladder fossa, bluntly separating the liver parenchyma to the right porta hepatis incisure.
7. Ligating the short hepatic vein, paying attention not to injure the inferior vena cava.
8. Separating the right hepatic vein, cutting and ligating; clamping and transecting the right posterior superior margin of the hepatic vein along with the liver tissue, and removing the specimen.
9. Carefully ligating bleeding points on the liver incision, coagulating the liver incision with argon beam coagulation for hemostasis.
10. Thorough hemostasis, irrigating the peritoneal cavity, aspirating the effluent, placing a negative pressure ball on the liver incision, verifying the instruments and gauze, and closing the abdomen in layers.

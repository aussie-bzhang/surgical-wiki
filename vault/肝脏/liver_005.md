## Overview
Left hepatectomy and cholecystectomy for a large left liver tumor and multiple smaller tumors, with adhesions and ascites.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Left liver cancer
**Intraoperative:** Left liver cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Large left liver tumor (12*10*8cm), two smaller tumors (2.0cm), adhesions to diaphragm and omentum, negative ascites.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia, right upper quadrant T-shaped incision.
2. Dissection of ligaments and adhesions, freeing left liver.
3. Dissection of胆囊三角, ligating and transecting gallbladder vessels and cystic duct.
4. Dissection and ligating of left hepatic artery.
5. Opening of Glisson's sheath, ligating and transecting left hepatic duct and left portal vein.
6. Exposure and ligating of left hepatic vein.
7. Hepatectomy along the left-right liver boundary, ligating or tying all vessels encountered.
8. Hemostasis, lavage, placement of drains, and closure.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Left liver, gallbladder, common bile duct (胆总管)

## Technical Challenges
Not documented

## Teaching Points
- Proper dissection and hemostasis around the common bile duct.
- Technique of segmental liver resection.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent bile leak.

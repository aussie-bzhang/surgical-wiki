## Overview
Splenectomy with perigastric vascular ligation and splenorenal venous shunt is a complex procedure performed for portal hypertension and splenic enlargement. It involves resection of the spleen, ligation of the perigastric vessels, and creation of a splenorenal venous shunt to relieve pressure.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Liver cirrhosis, portal hypertension, splenic hyperemia
**Intraoperative:** Liver cirrhosis, portal hypertension, splenic hyperemia
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Spleen approximately 25*20*15cm, congested and enlarged. Esophagogastric fundus showed a large amount of varices and tortuous clumps, the largest diameter being 2cm. Spleen vein diameter 2cm, splenic artery diameter 1.5cm. Liver showed mixed nodular cirrhosis. Ascites approximately 300ml.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. Left upper quadrant 'L' shaped incision for abdominal access, avoiding曲张 veins in the upper abdomen.
2. Incise the gastrocolic ligament, dissect and isolate the middle colonic vein in the mesocolon, catheterize to the superior mesenteric vein and portal vein for pressure measurement. Divide the gastro splenic ligament to the left of the cardia, find the splenic artery above the body of the pancreas and ligate it.
3. Divide the splenic colonic ligament, and dissect and cut the splenic renal ligament posteriorly and the gastro splenic ligament anteriorly. Ligature the short gastric vessels individually, dissect and cut the splenic diaphragmatic ligament, and fully mobilize the splenic pedicle. Dissect the splenic artery and vein, ligate the splenic artery, use Satinsky clamp to occlude and cut the splenic vein, and resect the spleen.
4. Mobilize the proximal splenic vein trunk for about 4-5cm for anastomosis.
5. Open the posterior peritoneum and perirenal fat at the renal hilum, expose the left renal vein for about 4cm, use Satinsky clamp to occlude the renal vein, cut a portion of the renal vein wall to form a circular opening of about 1.2cm in diameter, approximate the splenic vein to the renal vein, and perform an end-to-side anastomosis between the splenic vein and renal vein using 5-0 proline suture (continuous full-thickness inversion suture), with an anastomotic diameter of about 1.0cm.
6. Divide and ligate the gastric pancreas vessels, divide and ligate the gastric coronary vessels along the lesser curvature of the stomach, and mobilize to the right of the cardia.
7. Mobilize the lower end of the esophagus, divide the vagus nerve, and continue upwards to the esophageal hiatus, divide or ligate the esophageal varices.
8. Thoroughly irrigate the abdominal cavity with normal saline, ensure hemostasis, suture the splenic bed, and place three negative pressure drains below the left diaphragm and behind the esophagus.
9. Count sponges and instruments, close the abdomen in layers, and complete the procedure.

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in mobilizing the spleen, controlling bleeding, and performing the splenorenal venous anastomosis.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of careful mobilization of the spleen and control of bleeding.
- Technique of creating a splenorenal venous shunt.
- Management of esophageal varices.

## Complication Prevention
- Thorough hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper placement of drains to prevent postoperative ascites.

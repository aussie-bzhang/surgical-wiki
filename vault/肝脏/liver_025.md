## Overview
A complex procedure involving the release of intestinal adhesions, left hemihepatectomy, common bile duct incision, and T-tube drainage, performed in a patient with left intrahepatic bile duct stones.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Left intrahepatic bile duct stones
**Intraoperative:** Left intrahepatic bile duct stones
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Gallbladder resected, dense adhesions in the right upper abdomen, common bile duct diameter 1.2 cm, multiple stones in the left intrahepatic bile duct, no ascites.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping, midupper abdominal midline incision for abdominal exploration.
2. Cutting the falciform ligament, releasing adhesions in the right upper abdomen, opening the anterior wall of the common bile duct, probing the common bile duct, probe smoothly enters the duodenum, left and right hepatic ducts are patent.
3. Cutting the falciform ligament, coronary ligament, triangular ligament, gastrohepatic ligament, and part of the right coronary ligament, separating the left hemiliver from the diaphragm and omentum, fully freeing the left hemiliver.
4. Upward flipping of the left hemiliver, incising the hepatoduodenal ligament, separating the left hepatic artery and ligating it.
5. At the hepatic hilum, incising the Glisson sheath, separating the left hepatic duct and left portal vein, ligating the left hepatic duct and left portal vein.
6. Revealing the second hepatic portal, separating the left hepatic vein and ligating it.
7. Cutting the liver along the boundary between the left and right hemiliver, ligating or tying all encountered vessels within the liver, carefully ligating bleeding points on the liver surface, coagulating the liver surface with argon beam coagulation and spraying biological protein glue for hemostasis.
8. Placing a 24F T-tube in the common bile duct, fixing it, and puncturing the incision to lead out.
9. Thorough hemostasis, rinsing the abdominal cavity, removing all exudate, placing a negative pressure ball on the liver surface, verifying the instruments and gauze, and closing the abdomen in layers.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Common bile duct (胆总管), left liver, portal vein, hepatic vein

- Proper placement of T-tube to prevent bile leakage

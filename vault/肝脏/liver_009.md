## Overview
Cholecystectomy and cholangiography with external drainage and left hepatectomy for cholecystolithiasis and left intrahepatic bile duct calculi. Key technical feature includes exploration and resection of the bile duct and liver.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Cholecystolithiasis, left intrahepatic bile duct calculi
**Intraoperative:** Cholecystolithiasis, left intrahepatic bile duct calculi
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Gallbladder size 8*4cm, common bile duct diameter about 1cm, with a cholesterol stone. Multiple calculi in the left intrahepatic bile duct. Biliary endoscopy reached the right third-order intrahepatic bile duct without significant calculi.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping, midline incision for abdominal exploration.
2. Incise the round ligament of liver, falciform ligament, left coronary ligament, left triangular ligament, hepatogastric ligament, and part of the right coronary ligament. Separate the left lobe from the diaphragm and omentum, and fully mobilize the left lobe.
3. Dissect the胆囊三角, ligate and transect the gallbladder vessels and cystic duct, strip the gallbladder from the liver bed, and coagulate the gallbladder bed with electrocautery.
4. Confirm the common bile duct by puncture, longitudinally incise the bile duct, remove the stone with a stone forceps, explore the bile duct with a biliary endoscope, and place a 22F T-tube, suture the bile duct with 4-0 gastrointestinal suture.
5. Flip up the left lobe, incise the hepatoduodenal ligament, and ligate the left hepatic artery.
6. Cut open the Glisson sheath at the porta hepatis, separate the left hepatic duct and left portal vein, ligate the left hepatic duct and left portal vein.
7. Expose the second liver hilum, separate the left hepatic vein, and ligate it.
8. Cut the liver along the boundary between the left and right lobes, ligate or tie all encountered vessels within the liver, ligate bleeding points on the liver surface, coagulate the liver surface with argon beam coagulation, and spray biological protein glue for hemostasis.
9. Stop bleeding, irrigate the abdominal cavity, remove all effusion, place negative pressure balls under the right diaphragm, in the gallbladder fossa, and on the liver surface, count instruments and sponges, and close the abdomen in layers.

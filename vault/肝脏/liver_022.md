## Overview
Resection of hepatic portal biliary cancer with complex small bowel adhesiolysis and right and left hepatic duct jejunum Roun-en-Y anastomosis in a patient with severe intra-abdominal adhesions.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Hepatic portal biliary cancer
**Intraoperative:** Hepatic portal biliary cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Severe intra-abdominal adhesions, dense adhesions between small bowel wall and peritoneum, thin bowel wall, prone to perforation, especially in the upper mid abdomen. Variations in the portal biliary tract, gallbladder size approximately 8*3*2cm, cystic duct diameter 0.3cm, CBD thick and short, maximum diameter about 1cm. Tumor diameter at the portal biliary tract about 3cm, papillary and easily shed, originating from the right hepatic duct and spreading to the left hepatic duct. No significant abnormalities found in the liver, pelvis, and remaining intestinal segments.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping, incision at the right upper abdomen through the rectus sheath around the umbilicus.
2. Release of adhesions in the upper mid abdomen, skeletal dissection of the hepatoduodenal ligament, free portal vein, hepatic artery, and CBD, transection of CBD at the superior margin of the pancreas, ligation of the distal end;
3. Dissection of the gallbladder from the liver surface, upward traction of the CBD, separation of the tumor from the surrounding tissue;
4. Exposure of the porta hepatis, incision of the CBD at the upper end of the confluence of the right and left hepatic ducts, resection of the tumor with surrounding fibrous connective tissue, visible openings of the right and left hepatic ducts after tumor resection;
5. Transection of the jejunum 20cm from the Treitz ligament with a side-to-side stapler, free the distal jejunal mesentery, anastomosis of the distal jejunum with the portal biliary tract (anterograde, continuous full-thickness suture); anastomosis of the proximal jejunum with the jejunum 45cm below the biliary-enteric anastomosis (anterograde); closure of the mesenteric gap;
6. Thorough hemostasis, lavage of the abdominal cavity, aspiration of effluent fluid, placement of two negative pressure bulbs around the anastomosis, sutured in layers.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Common bile duct (胆总管), portal vein (门静脉), hepatic artery (肝动脉), hepatic duct (肝管), jejunum (空肠)

## Technical Challenges
Severe intra-abdominal adhesions, variations in the portal biliary tract, and the need for precise anastomosis.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of careful dissection to avoid injury to critical structures.
- Management of severe adhesions and their impact on surgical exposure.
- Technical aspects of bile duct and jejunal anastomosis.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Adequate lavage to reduce the risk of infection.
- Proper placement of drains to manage fluid collection.

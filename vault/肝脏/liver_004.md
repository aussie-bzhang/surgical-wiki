## Overview
Left hepatectomy and omentectomy for left liver lateral lobe tumor with extensive intra-abdominal metastasis.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Left liver cancer
**Intraoperative:** Left liver cancer with extensive intra-abdominal metastasis
**Pathological:** Adenocarcinoma infiltration in omental nodules

## Intraoperative Findings
Intraoperative findings: Left liver tumor, approximately 5*5*cm in size, hard, unclear boundaries, protruding from the liver surface. Multiple metastatic nodules in omentum, stomach, small intestine wall, and mesentery. Approximately 200ml of pale yellow serous peritoneal effusion around the liver.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia successful, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Right upper quadrant T-shaped incision for abdominal exploration.
3. Omental nodule sent for intraoperative frozen section, pathological result: "adenocarcinoma infiltration," decide on surgical approach.
4. Excise omentum from the lower edge of the stomach to the upper edge of the transverse colon, left to the splenic flexure, right to the hepatic flexure.
5. Cut and ligate the round ligament of the liver, incise the falciform ligament near the abdominal anterior wall, and retain a certain width. Incise the left coronary ligament near the liver surface, ligate the left triangular ligament, and fully mobilize the left liver lateral lobe.
6. Occlude the first liver portal, resect the left liver lateral lobe at a distance of about 3cm from the tumor, ligate and suture all encountered vessels within the liver. Carefully ligate bleeding points on the liver surface, turn the round ligament and falciform ligament downward to cover the liver断面, suture and fix with 1-0 Ethicon suture, cover with hemostatic gauze and spray with biological protein glue for hemostasis.
7. Thorough hemostasis, wash the abdominal cavity, remove all effusion, place two negative pressure balls on the liver surface, and puncture another hole to lead out. Count instruments and sponges, close the abdomen in layers.

## Overview
Hepatic portal biliary cancer resection, a complex procedure involving resection of the tumor and surrounding structures, with reconstruction of the bile duct and portal vein.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Hepatic portal biliary cancer
**Intraoperative:** Hepatic portal biliary cancer
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Liver cholestasis, organ jaundice, friable and edematous tissue prone to bleeding. A 2*1cm hard mass at the confluence of the common hepatic duct and right hepatic duct, infiltrating and surrounding the right hepatic artery and proper hepatic artery. Negative peritoneum and pelvis.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping, right upper quadrant inverted L-shaped incision.
2. Skeletonization of the porta hepatis, mobilization of the portal vein, hepatic artery, and common bile duct. Transection of the common bile duct at the superior margin of the pancreas, ligation of the distal end.
3. Mobilization of the gallbladder from the liver surface, upward traction of the bile duct, separation of the tumor from surrounding tissue.
4. Exposure of the porta hepatis, incision of the bile duct at the superior margin of the confluence of the right and left hepatic ducts, resection of the tumor with surrounding fibrous connective tissue. Reconstruction of the bile duct into a single opening.
5. Transection of the jejunum with a side-to-side stapler 20 cm from the Treitz ligament, mobilization of the distal jejunal mesentery, end-to-side anastomosis of the distal jejunum with the porta hepatis bile duct (anterior, continuous full-thickness suture). End-to-side anastomosis of the proximal jejunum with the jejunum 45 cm below the biliary-enteric anastomosis (anterior). Closure of the mesenteric gap.
6. Thorough hemostasis, lavage of the abdominal cavity, aspiration of exudate. Placement of two negative pressure bulbs around the anastomosis, extracted through a right upper quadrant stab wound. Layered closure.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Common bile duct (胆总管), portal vein (门静脉), hepatic artery (肝动脉), hepatic duct (肝管)

## Technical Challenges
Difficult resection due to infiltration of the tumor around the right hepatic artery and proper hepatic artery.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of careful mobilization and resection of the tumor.
- Reconstruction techniques of the bile duct and portal vein.
- Management of significant blood loss during the procedure.

## Complication Prevention
- Thorough hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper reconstruction to prevent bile leak and anastomotic stricture.

## Overview
Left hepatectomy for a large left liver cystadenoma, significant anatomical and technical challenges encountered.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Left liver mass
**Intraoperative:** Left liver serous cystadenoma
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Large cystic and solid mass in the left liver, approximately 20*15*15cm, containing a large amount of clear fluid, about 2000ml. No abnormalities found in gallbladder, pancreas, spleen, stomach, small intestine, and colon.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position with successful general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping, incision in the right upper quadrant subcostal area for abdominal exploration.
2. Open the cystic wall of the mass and aspirate the cyst fluid.
3. Cut and tie the round ligament of the liver, falciform ligament, left coronary ligament, left triangular ligament, hepatogastric ligament, and part of the right coronary ligament, separate the left half liver from the diaphragm and omentum, and fully mobilize the left half liver.
4. Dissect the胆囊三角, tie and cut the gallbladder vessels and cystic duct, strip the gallbladder from the liver bed, and coagulate the gallbladder bed with electrocautery.
5. Reflect the left half liver upwards, incise the porta hepatis, and separate the left hepatic artery to tie it off.
6. At the hepatic hilum, cut the Glisson sheath, separate the left hepatic duct and left portal vein, tie off the left hepatic duct, and use an endocut clip to cut off the left portal vein.
7. Expose the second hepatic portal, separate the left hepatic vein, and ligate it.
8. Cut the liver along the boundary between the left and right half livers, ligate or tie off all encountered vessels within the liver, carefully ligate bleeding points on the liver surface, coagulate the liver surface with argon beam coagulation, and sprinkle hemostatic powder for hemostasis.
9. Thorough hemostasis, wash the abdominal cavity, remove the effusion, place negative pressure balls under the right diaphragm, in the gallbladder fossa, and on the liver surface, check instruments and gauze, and close the abdomen in layers.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: left liver, common bile duct (胆总管), portal vein, left hepatic vein. (肝左动脉, 门静脉左支, 左肝静脉)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in mobilizing the left half liver, dissecting the portal triad, and controlling bleeding during liver resection.

## Teaching Points
- Proper mobilization and dissection of the left half liver
- Safe dissection of the portal triad
- Effective hemostasis during liver resection

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage
- Proper drainage to prevent bile leakage

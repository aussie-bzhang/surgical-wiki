## Overview
Splenectomy with perigastric vascular ligation and splenorenal venous shunt is a complex procedure performed for variceal bleeding in patients with liver cirrhosis. It involves resection of the spleen, ligation of perigastric veins, and creation of a splenorenal venous shunt to reduce portal hypertension.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Liver cirrhosis, portal hypertension, splenic hypertrophy, variceal bleeding
**Intraoperative:** Liver cirrhosis, portal hypertension, splenic hypertrophy, variceal bleeding
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Spleen 25*20cm, congested and enlarged, severely adherent to surrounding tissues; splenic vein diameter 1.5cm, splenic artery diameter 1.5cm, twisted; large varices around the cardia, largest diameter 0.6cm; liver shows small nodular sclerosis, ascites approximately 50ml.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping; left upper quadrant 'L' shaped incision, avoiding曲张 veins on the upper abdominal wall.
2. Incise the gastrocolic ligament, anatomically separate the middle colonic vein in the mesocolon, catheterize to the superior mesenteric vein and portal vein for pressure measurement.
3. Transect the splenogastric ligament to the left of the cardia, find the splenic artery above the body of the pancreas and ligate it.
4. Transect the splenocolic ligament, and separate and cut the splenorenal ligament posteriorly and splenogastric ligament anteriorly, ligate the short gastric vessels one by one, separate and cut the splenodiaphragmatic ligament, and fully mobilize the splenic pedicle.
5. Separate the splenic artery and vein, transect and ligate the splenic artery, use Satinsky clamp to occlude and transect the splenic vein, and resect the spleen.
6. Mobilize the proximal splenic vein trunk for about 4-5cm for anastomosis.
7. Open the posterior peritoneum and perirenal fat at the renal hilum, expose the left renal vein for about 4cm, use Satinsky clamp to occlude the renal vein, cut a part of the renal vein wall to form a circular opening of about 1.2cm in diameter, approximate the splenic vein to the renal vein, and perform an end-to-side anastomosis between the splenic vein and renal vein using 5-0 proline suture (continuous full-thickness inversion suture), anastomotic diameter about 1.0cm.
8. Transect and ligate the gastric pancreas vessels, transect and ligate the gastric coronary vessels along the lesser curvature of the stomach, mobilize upwards to the right of the cardia.
9. Mobilize the lower end of the esophagus, transect the vagus nerve, and continue upwards to the esophageal hiatus, transect or ligate the esophageal varices.
10. Thoroughly irrigate the abdominal cavity with normal saline, ensure hemostasis, suture the splenic bed, place two negative pressure drains below the left diaphragm and behind the esophagus.
11. Count sponges and instruments, close the abdomen in layers, and complete the operation.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Spleen, perigastric veins, splenic vein, splenic artery, renal vein (肾静脉)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered include severe adherence of the spleen to surrounding tissues, which required careful dissection to avoid injury to adjacent structures.

## Teaching Points
- The importance of careful dissection around the spleen to avoid injury to the splenic artery and vein.
- The technique of creating a splenorenal venous shunt and ensuring proper hemostasis.
- The management of variceal bleeding during the procedure.

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage.
- Proper placement of drains to prevent postoperative ascites.

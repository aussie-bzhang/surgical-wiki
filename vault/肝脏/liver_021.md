## Overview
Left hepaticojejunal anastomosis with left hepatectomy and cholecystectomy for obstructive jaundice due to left intrahepatic bile duct cancer.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Left intrahepatic bile duct cancer, obstructive jaundice
**Intraoperative:** Left intrahepatic bile duct cancer, obstructive jaundice
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located in the left lobe of the liver, infiltrative growth and invasion along the bile duct to the porta hepatis. The hepatic artery near the porta hepatis was occluded, with distal dilatation. The left portal vein was invaded and occluded. Multiple lymph nodes were visible in the porta hepaticus, with lymph node enlargement in the retropancreatic region, 8th and 12th groups.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia successful, routine disinfection and draping, midline incision for abdominal exploration.
2. Incision of the falciform ligament, round ligament, left coronary ligament, left triangular ligament, hepatogastric ligament, and part of the right coronary ligament. Separation of the left hemiliver from the diaphragm and omentum, complete mobilization of the left hemiliver.
3. Dissection of the胆囊三角, ligation of the gallbladder vessels and cystic duct, stripping of the gallbladder from the liver bed, electrocoagulation of the gallbladder bed for hemostasis.
4. Skeletonization of the porta hepatis, mobilization of the portal vein, hepatic artery, and common bile duct. Dissection of the bile duct at the superior margin of the pancreas, ligation of the distal end, and dissection of the right and left hepatic ducts at the porta hepatis, respectively. Ligation of the tumor infiltration blocking the hepatic artery and left portal vein at the porta hepatis.
5. Exposure of the second porta hepatis, separation and ligation of the left hepatic vein.
6. Incision of the liver along the boundary between the left and right hemiliver, ligation or suture of all encountered vessels within the liver. Careful ligation of bleeding points on the liver surface, argon beam coagulation of the liver surface, and application of hemostatic powder for hemostasis.
7. Cutting the jejunum with a side-to-side anastomosis stapler 20 cm from the Treitz ligament, mobilization of the distal jejunal mesentery, and end-to-side anastomosis of the distal jejunum with the right hepatic duct. The proximal jejunum is anastomosed end-to-side with the jejunum 45 cm below the biliary-enteric anastomosis. Closure of the mesenteric gap.
8. Thorough hemostasis, lavage of the abdominal cavity, aspiration of exudate, placement of two negative pressure balls around the anastomosis site, and extraction through a stab wound in the upper right abdomen. Layer-by-layer closure.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: common bile duct (胆总管), portal vein (门静脉), hepatic artery (肝动脉), hepatic vein (肝静脉), jejunum (空肠)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in mobilization of the left hemiliver, identification and ligation of the tumor infiltration, and anastomosis of the jejunum and bile duct.

## Teaching Points
- Importance of careful mobilization and identification of the portal vein and hepatic artery during dissection.
- Technique of liver resection and hemostasis.
- Proper anastomosis technique for the jejunum and bile duct.

## Complication Prevention
- Adequate hemostasis during surgery.
- Proper placement of drains to prevent bile leakage and infection.

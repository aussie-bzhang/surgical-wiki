## Overview
Cholecystectomy with common bile duct exploration, external drainage with T-tube, and left hepatectomy for cholecystolithiasis and left intrahepatic bile duct stones. Key technical feature includes exploration and resection of the left liver lobe.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Cholecystolithiasis, left intrahepatic bile duct stones
**Intraoperative:** Cholecystolithiasis, left intrahepatic bile duct stones
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Gallbladder size 10*5cm, common bile duct diameter about 2.5cm, multiple brownish stones of 0.5-1cm in diameter, left liver lobe filled with stones. Cholangioscope exploration revealed three stones of 0.5cm in the right liver lobe bile duct, all removed. No residual stones found after cholangioscope exploration.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Midline incision, enter peritoneum, explore as above.
3. Cut the round ligament of liver, falciform ligament, left coronary ligament, left triangular ligament, hepatogastric ligament, and part of the right coronary ligament. Separate the left half liver from the diaphragm and omentum, fully mobilize the left half liver.
4. Dissect the胆囊三角, ligate and cut the gallbladder vessels and cystic duct, strip the gallbladder from the liver bed, coagulate the gallbladder bed with electrocautery.
5. Confirm the common bile duct by puncture, longitudinally incise the common bile duct, remove stones with stone forceps, explore with cholangioscope as above, place a 24F T-tube, and suture the bile duct with 4-0 gastrointestinal suture.
6. Flip up the left half liver, incise the hepatoduodenal ligament, separate and ligate the left hepatic artery.
7. At the porta hepatis, incise the Glisson sheath, separate the left hepatic duct and left portal vein, ligate the left portal vein and close the distal end with 5-0 prolene suture, ligate the left hepatic duct.
8. Expose the second liver hilum, separate and ligate the left hepatic vein.
9. Cut the liver along the boundary between the left and right half liver, ligate all encountered vessels within the liver, ligate or tie the bleeding points on the liver surface, coagulate the liver surface with argon beam coagulation, and stop bleeding with hemostatic powder.
10. Thorough hemostasis, wash the peritoneal cavity, remove all effusion, place negative pressure balls under the right diaphragm, in the gallbladder fossa, and on the liver surface, count instruments and gauze, close the abdomen in layers.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Common bile duct (胆总管), left hepatic duct (左肝管), left liver lobe (左肝叶)
> Glisson sheath (Glisson鞘)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in mobilizing the left liver lobe and controlling bleeding during liver resection.

## Teaching Points
- Proper mobilization of the left liver lobe
- Safe dissection of the porta hepatis
- Effective hemostasis during liver resection

## Complication Prevention
- Preventing bile leakage by ensuring proper bile duct suture
- Preventing postoperative hemorrhage by meticulous hemostasis

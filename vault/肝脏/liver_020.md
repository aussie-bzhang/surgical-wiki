## Overview
A complex hepatobiliary procedure involving intestinal adhesiolysis, resection of the left liver lateral lobe and right liver posterior lobe, common bile duct resection, and hepaticojejunostomy with Roux-en-Y anastomosis. The patient had post-cholecystectomy choledocholithiasis.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Post-cholecystectomy, intraductal bile stones
**Intraoperative:** Post-cholecystectomy, choledocholithiasis
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
The gallbladder was removed. Severe adhesions were found in the right upper quadrant. The liver, omentum, duodenum, and diaphragm were densely adherent. The common bile duct was dilated, 1.5 cm in diameter, containing a large number of stones 0.5-1.0 cm in diameter. The left liver lateral lobe and right liver posterior lobe were atrophic, with bile ducts filled with stones.

## Operative Steps
1. General anesthesia, supine position, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Right subcostal incision for abdominal exploration.
3. Release of adhesions in the right upper quadrant, transection of the falciform ligament.
4. Careful dissection of the liver and omentum from the duodenum and diaphragm.
5. Transection of the falciform ligament, coronary ligaments, triangular ligaments, gastrohepatic ligament, and hepatorenal ligament.
6. Dissection of the liver from the diaphragm, reaching the right of the inferior vena cava.
7. Opening the anterior wall of the common bile duct, exploration, and removal of stones.
8. Transection of the common bile duct, distal end suture closure, and proximal resection to the common hepatic duct.
9. Exposure of the second liver hilum, ligation of the left hepatic vein, and resection of the left liver lobe.
10. Hemostasis of the liver断面, using argon beam coagulation.
11. Dissection of the right liver posterior lobe, ligation and transection of the right short hepatic vein, and right hepatic vein.
12. Releasing the porta hepatis blockage, hemostasis of the liver断面, and U-shaped suture.
13. Transection of the jejunum 20 cm from the Treitz ligament, side-to-side anastomosis with the hepatic duct.
14. Anastomosis of the proximal jejunum to the jejunum 45 cm below the biliary-enteric anastomosis.
15. Closure of the mesenteric defect.
16. Hemostasis, lavage of the abdominal cavity, application of biological protein glue and hemostatic gauze, placement of a negative pressure drain at the liver and diaphragmatic surfaces.
17. Counting instruments and sponges, and closing the abdomen in layers.

## Technical Challenges
Severe adhesions, dilated common bile duct with stones, atrophic liver lobes, and complex anastomosis.

- Proper anastomotic technique.
- Postoperative monitoring for bile leak and infection.

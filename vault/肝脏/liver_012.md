## Overview
Right hepatic tumor resection and adhesion release for postoperative colon cancer with liver metastasis.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Colon cancer post-resection, liver metastasis.
**Intraoperative:** Liver metastasis.
**Pathological:** Not documented.

## Intraoperative Findings
Tumor located on the diaphragmatic surface of the right liver at segments VII and VIII, approximately 3cm in diameter, hard in consistency. A lymph node of about 1.5cm in diameter was found at the root of the mesentery, with adhesion and invasion at the root of the mesenteric vessels. Exploration of spleen, stomach, gallbladder, small intestine, and colon showed no abnormalities. Abdominal and pelvic (-).

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia successful, routine disinfection and draping, midupper abdominal midline incision.
2. Decompose adhesions between the intestinal wall and the incision, explore as above.
3. Cut the round ligament of the liver, falciform ligament, and right coronary ligament. Find the tumor at the diaphragmatic surface of the liver at segments VII and VIII.
4. After blocking the portal vein, resect the tumor completely at 1cm from the edge of the tumor.
5. Suture the bleeding points on the liver incision carefully, coagulate the liver incision with argon beam coagulation, pack the incision with hemostatic gauze, and suture the incision with Yang Jia梅 needles.
6. Carefully separate the lymph node at the root of the mesenteric vessels from the adhesions, and resect the enlarged lymph node.
7. Thorough hemostasis, wash the abdominal cavity, remove the effusion, place a negative pressure drain on the diaphragmatic surface of the liver, count instruments and sponges, and close the abdomen in layers.
8. Operation completed.

## Overview
Right hepatectomy and adhesion release for a patient with right hepatic metastatic tumor, involving粘连松解 and liver resection.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Right hepatic metastatic tumor
**Intraoperative:** Right hepatic metastatic tumor confirmed
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Right upper quadrant dense adhesions, two nodules of approximately 5cm and 4cm in diameter palpable in the right liver. No obvious nodules in the left liver on intraoperative ultrasound. No obvious metastatic lesions in the abdominal cavity, stomach, pancreas, spleen, and intestines.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping. L-shaped incision in the right upper quadrant for abdominal exploration.
2. Dissection of adhesions between the duodenum, transverse colon, and liver.
3. Transection of the falciform ligament, round ligament, right coronary ligament, right triangular ligament, hepatocolic ligament, and hepatorenal ligament. Blunt dissection of the bare area of the liver to the inferior vena cava, protecting the right adrenal gland and its vessels.
4. Dissection of the hepatoduodenal ligament and skeletal清扫 of the corresponding lymph nodes, exposing the right branch of the portal vein, right hepatic duct, and right hepatic artery. Ligation and transection of the right hepatic duct, right hepatic artery, and right branch of the portal vein.
5. Incision of the liver capsule on the diaphragmatic surface from the right wall of the inferior vena cava to the gallbladder notch, bluntly separating the liver parenchyma, ligating and transecting encountered vessels and bile ducts. Flip the liver upwards, incise the liver along the right longitudinal groove of the gallbladder fossa, and bluntly separate the liver parenchyma to the right porta hepatis incisure. Ligating the short hepatic vein, avoiding injury to the inferior vena cava. Separating the right hepatic vein, transecting and ligating; clamping and transecting the right posterior superior margin of the hepatic vein along with the liver tissue, removing the specimen. Open the first porta hepatis.
6. Ligating bleeding points on the liver wound, coagulating the liver wound with argon beam coagulation and sprinkling hemostatic powder for hemostasis.
7. Thorough hemostasis, irrigation of the abdominal cavity, aspiration of exudate, placement of two negative pressure balls on the liver wound, verification of instruments and sponges, and layer-by-layer closure of the abdomen. Operation completed.

## Anatomical Key Points
> Critical structures: right liver, common bile duct, hepatic veins. (胆总管, 肝静脉)

## Technical Challenges
Difficulties encountered in liver mobilization and hemostasis.

## Teaching Points
- Proper mobilization of the liver and hemostasis techniques
- Protection of adjacent structures during dissection

## Complication Prevention
- Careful hemostasis to prevent postoperative hemorrhage
- Protection of the common bile duct and hepatic veins during dissection

## Overview
Splenectomy with splenorenal shunt and perigastric vein ligation for liver cirrhosis, portal hypertension, hypersplenism, and variceal bleeding.

## Diagnosis
**Pre-operative:** Liver cirrhosis, portal hypertension, hypersplenism, variceal bleeding.
**Intraoperative:** Liver cirrhosis, portal hypertension, hypersplenism, variceal bleeding.
**Pathological:** Not documented

## Intraoperative Findings
Spleen 25*20cm, congested and enlarged, splenic vein diameter 1.2cm; marked gastric varices, visible varices around the cardia; liver with large nodular cirrhosis, no ascites, mild edema of the gastric wall, no abnormalities in the small intestine and colon. Portal vein pressure measured at 24 mmHg after laparotomy, 20 mmHg after splenic artery ligation, 23 mmHg after splenic resection, 16 mmHg after shunt, 18 mmHg after ligation.

## Operative Steps
1. Supine position, under general anesthesia, routine disinfection and draping.
2. Left upper quadrant 'L' shaped incision, avoiding曲张 veins on the upper abdominal wall.
3. Open the gastrocolic ligament, anatomically separate the middle colonic vein in the transverse mesocolon, catheterize to the superior mesenteric vein-portal vein for pressure measurement.
4. Dissect the gastro-splenic ligament to the left of the cardia, find and ligate the splenic artery above the body of the pancreas.
5. Dissect the splenic-colonic ligament, and separate and cut the splenic renal ligament behind the spleen, the gastro-splenic ligament in front of the spleen, ligate the short gastric vessels one by one, separate and cut the splenic diaphragmatic ligament, and fully mobilize the splenic pedicle.
6. Separate the splenic artery and vein, ligate the splenic artery, use Satinsky clamp to occlude and cut the splenic vein, resect the spleen.
7. Mobilize the proximal splenic vein trunk for about 4-5cm for anastomosis.
8. Open the posterior peritoneum and perirenal fat at the renal hilum, expose the left renal vein for about 4cm, occlude the left adrenal vein, use Satinsky clamp to occlude the renal vein, cut part of the renal vein wall to form a circular opening of about 1.2cm in diameter, approximate the splenic vein to the renal vein, perform end-to-side anastomosis between the splenic vein and renal vein with 5-0 proline suture (continuous full-thickness inversion suture), anastomotic diameter about 1.0cm.
9. Ligate and tie the gastric-pancreatic vessels, ligate and tie the gastric coronary vessels along the lesser curvature of the stomach, mobilize upwards to the right of the cardia.
10. Mobilize the lower end of the esophagus, cut the vagus nerve, and cut or ligate the esophageal varices upwards to the esophageal hiatus.
11. Thoroughly wash the abdominal cavity with normal saline, perfect hemostasis, suture the splenic bed, place two negative pressure drains below the left diaphragm and behind the esophagus.
12. Count sponges and instruments, close the abdomen in layers, surgery completed.
